1. a patient renal failure​


Answer:

Sometimes the kidneys are no longer able to filter and cleanse the blood. This can cause the formation of unsafe levels of waste products. This is known as kidney (or kidney) failure. Unless it is treated, it can cause DEATH.


2. renal failure cause and effect​


Answer:

Signs and symptoms of acute kidney failure may include: Decreased urine output, although occasionally urine output remains normal. Fluid retention, causing swelling in your legs, ankles or feet. Shortness of breath.

Answer:

Effect:

When your kidneys are damaged, waste products and fluid can build up in your body. That can cause swelling in your ankles, nausea, weakness, poor sleep, and shortness of breath. Without treatment, the damage can get worse and your kidneys may eventually stop working.

Cause:

Kidneys can become damaged from a physical injury or a disease like diabetes, high blood pressure, or other disorders. High blood pressure and diabetes are the two most common causes of kidney failure. Kidney failure does not happen overnight. It is the end result of a gradual loss of kidney function.


3. what is the meaning of renal failure​


Answer:

Listen to pronunciation. (REE-nul FAYL-yer) A condition in which the kidneys stop working and are not able to remove waste and extra water from the blood or keep body chemicals in balance. Acute or severe renal failure happens suddenly (for example, after an injury) and may be treated and cured.

5. what are the cause and effects of diabetes arthritis and renal failure​


Patient Care & Health Information

Diseases & Conditions

Acute kidney failure

SECTIONSFOR ACUTE KIDNEY FAILURE

Kidney cross section

Kidney cross sectionOpen pop-up dialog box

Acute kidney failure occurs when your kidneys suddenly become unable to filter waste products from your blood. When your kidneys lose their filtering ability, dangerous levels of wastes may accumulate, and your blood's chemical makeup may get out of balance.

Acute kidney failure — also called acute renal failure or acute kidney injury — develops rapidly, usually in less than a few days. Acute kidney failure is most common in people who are already hospitalized, particularly in critically ill people who need intensive care.

Acute kidney failure can be fatal and requires intensive treatment. However, acute kidney failure may be reversible. If you're otherwise in good health, you may recover normal or nearly normal kidney function.
