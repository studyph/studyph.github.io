18. SAQ3: What is the importance of analyzing functions of communities in terms of structures, dynamics, and processes?​


Answer:

Understanding community behaviour: By analyzing community structures, dynamics, and processes, we can gain insights into how communities behave, how they interact with one another, and how they respond to different environmental stimuli.

Identifying patterns and trends: By analyzing data related to community structures, dynamics, and processes, we can identify patterns and trends that can help us predict future behavior and make more informed decisions.

Improving community management: By understanding the structures, dynamics, and processes of communities, we can develop more effective strategies for managing them. This includes identifying potential problems, developing solutions, and evaluating the effectiveness of different approaches.

Supporting sustainable development: Understanding the functions of communities can also help us develop more sustainable practices and policies that support long-term social, economic, and environmental well-being.

23. why is communication described as a two way continuous, and dynamic process?​


Answer:

Communication requires a sender and a receiver, and as long as they both have inputs, the communication between the two may go longer or change.

Explanation:

The process of communication starts by the sender encoding a message and the receiver decoding said message and then returning their feedback. This process goes on and on until one of them stops responding or both of them have agreed to stop. Communication can also be interpersonal or intrapersonal, interpersonal communication is communicating with other people, while intrapersonal is communicating with one's self. In both types of communication there is a sender and receiver (meaning it is two way), will not stop until agreed to (continuous), and might change depending on the sender or receiver (dynamic).
