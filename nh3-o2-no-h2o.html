8. 4NH3+3O2----> 2N2+6H2O 1. How many moles of NH3 are needed to react with 6 moles of O2? 2. How many grams of N2 are needed to react with 12 moles of H2O? 3. How many grams of O2 are needed to react with 250g of NH3? 4. How many grams of H2O are needed tp react with 100g of N2? With solution po sana. Thanks


Answer:

1) n NH3 = 8 mol

2) mass N2 = 112 g

3) mass O2 = 353 g

4) mass H2O = 193 g

Explanation:

1. How many moles of NH3 are needed to react with 6 moles of O2?

n NH3 = 6 mol O2 × (4 mol NH3 / 3 mol O2)

n NH3 = 8 mol

2. How many grams of N2 are needed to react with 12 moles of H2O?

mass N2 = 12 mol H2O × (2 mol N2 / 6 mol H2O) × (28 g N2 / 1 mol N2)

mass N2 = 112 g

3. How many grams of O2 are needed to react with 250g of NH3?

mass O2 = 250 g NH3 × (1 mol NH3 / 17 g NH3) × (3 mol O2 / 4 mol NH3) × (32 g O2 / 1 mol O2)

mass O2 = 353 g

4. How many grams of H2O are needed tp react with 100g of N2?

mass H2O = 100 g N2 × (1 mol N2 / 28 g N2) × (6 mol H2O / 2 mol N2) × (18 g H2O / 1 mol H2O)

mass H2O = 193 g

11. In reaction NH3 + O2 —> NO + H2O, what is the total number of moles of NO produced when 1 mole of O2 is completely consumed?


Answer:

In the reaction NH3 + O2 -> NO + H2O, the mole ratio of NO to O2 is 1:1. This means that for every 1 mole of O2 that is consumed, 1 mole of NO is produced. Therefore, when 1 mole of O2 is completely consumed, the total number of moles of NO produced is also 1 mole.

17. What are the missing coefficients for the skeleton equation below? NH3 (g) + O2(g) → N2(g) + H2O(l)​


The balanced equation :

4NH₃ (g) + 3O₂(g) → 2N₂(g) + 6H₂O(l)​

Further explanation

A chemical reaction can be expressed in terms of:  

words equation the skeleton equation  balanced equation

The skeleton equation is still in unbalanced form  

so only chemical formulas of reactants and products so that the relative amounts of the reactants and products are not included

Reaction

NH₃ (g) + O₂(g) → N₂(g) + H₂O(l)​

Give a coefficient to balance equation :

NH₃ (g) + aO₂(g) → bN₂(g) + cH₂O(l)​

Make a equation :

N, left=1, right=2b⇒2b=1⇒b=1/2

H, left=3, right=2c⇒2c=3⇒c=3/2

O, left=2a, right=c⇒2a=c⇒2a=3/2⇒a=3/4

The equation becomes :

NH₃ (g) + 3/4O₂(g) → 1/2N₂(g) + 3/2H₂O(l) ​x4

4NH₃ (g) + 3O₂(g) → 2N₂(g) + 6H₂O(l)​

28. 2 NH3(g) + 1 half O2(g) → N2H4(g) + H2O(l) calculate the value of ΔH


Explanation:

To calculate the value of ΔH (the enthalpy change) for the given reaction:

2 NH3(g) + 1/2 O2(g) → N2H4(g) + H2O(l)

We need to know the enthalpy of formation of each species involved in the reaction, as well as the stoichiometric coefficients of the reactants and products.

Using standard enthalpies of formation, we have:

ΔH = [ΔHf(N2H4) + ΔHf(H2O)] - [2ΔHf(NH3) + 1/2 ΔHf(O2)]

Where ΔHf represents the enthalpy of formation.

The values for the enthalpies of formation are:

ΔHf(N2H4) = 50.6 kJ/mol

ΔHf(H2O) = -285.8 kJ/mol

ΔHf(NH3) = -46.1 kJ/mol

ΔHf(O2) = 0 kJ/mol

Substituting these values into the equation, we get:

ΔH = [(50.6 kJ/mol) + (-285.8 kJ/mol)] - [2(-46.1 kJ/mol) + 1/2(0 kJ/mol)]

ΔH = -235.6 kJ/mol
