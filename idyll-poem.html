10. a period which focuses on idyllic sceneries and secular forms of art​


A period that focuses on idyllic sceneries and secular forms of art​ is the American era. American art shifted its focus from the idyllic scenery of the Hudson River School to more secular forms of art. This was a departure from the previous era, which focused on religious themes.

The American era was one that focused on idyllic sceneries and secular forms of art. This was a time when the country was growing and expanding, and many people were looking for ways to escape the hustle and bustle of city life. The American landscape was filled with beautiful scenery, and artists began to capture this in their work. Landscapes became popular subjects for paintings and photographs, and people began to appreciate the beauty of nature.

Idyllic sceneries are those that evoke a sense of peace, beauty, and serenity. They may include natural settings like mountains, forests, or bodies of water. Secular forms of art are those that don't explicitly reference religious themes or icons. This includes much of the world's great literature, music, and visual art.
