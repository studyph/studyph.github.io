6. In how many ways can a committee of 5 be chosen out of 9 people?


PROBLEM

In how many ways can a committee of 5 be chosen out of 9 people?

ANSWER

The word "chosen" is synonymous to a word selecting. We can identify a combination when it is selecting something.

[tex] \sf Using \: The \: Rule \: Of \: Combination[/tex]

[tex] \tt C(n,r) = \frac{n!}{r!(n - r)!} [/tex]

[tex] \sf Given: \tt n = 9 || r = 5[/tex]

[tex] \sf Subtitute \: The \: Values : [/tex]

[tex] \tt C(9,5) = \frac{9!}{5!(9 - 5)!} [/tex]

[tex] \tt C(9,5) = \frac{9 \times 8 \times 7 \times 6 \times \cancel{5! }}{ \cancel{5!} \times 4! }[/tex]

[tex] \tt C(9,5) = \frac{9 \times 8 \times 6 \times 5}{4!} [/tex]

[tex] \tt C(9,5) = \frac{3024}{24} [/tex]

[tex] \tt C(9,5) = 126 \: ways[/tex]

Therefore, there are 126 ways can a committee of 5 be chosen out of 9 people.

21. in how many ways can a committee of 7 students be chosen from 9 juniors and nine seniors if there must be for seniors in the committee​


✏️COMBINATIONS

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM:}}[/tex]

In how many ways a committee of 7 students be chosen from 9 juniors and 9 seniors if there must be 4 seniors in the committee?

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER:}}[/tex]

[tex] \qquad\Large\rm» \:\: \green{4\text,536\:ways} [/tex]

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION:}}[/tex]

» First, find the number of ways in which 7 students are chosen out of 9 of them.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \bold{\color{lightblue}Formula:} \\ & \boxed{\: \rm _nC_r = \frac{n!}{r!(n-r)!} \:} \end{aligned} [/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned} \rm _9C_7 = \frac{9!}{7!(9 - 7)!} \end{aligned} [/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned} \rm _9C_7 = \frac{9!}{7! \ 2!} \end{aligned} [/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned} \rm _9C_7 = \frac{9 \cdot 8 \cdot \cancel{7!}}{ \cancel{7!} \cdot 2} \end{aligned} [/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned} \rm _9C_7 = \frac{9 \cdot \cancel8^{ \ 4} }{ \cancel2} \end{aligned} [/tex]

[tex] \rm _9C_7 = 9 \cdot 4[/tex]

[tex] \rm _9C_7 = 36[/tex]

» Then, find the number of ways in which 4 seniors are chosen out of 9 of them.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \rm _9C_4 = \frac{9!}{4!(9 - 4)!} \end{aligned} [/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned} \rm _9C_4 = \frac{9!}{4! \ 5!} \end{aligned} [/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned} \rm _9C_4 = \frac{9 \cdot 8 \cdot 7 \cdot 6 \cdot \cancel{5!}}{4 \cdot 3 \cdot 2 \cdot \cancel{ 5!}} \end{aligned} [/tex]

[tex]\begin{aligned} \rm _9C_4 = \frac{9 \cdot \cancel8^{ \: 2} \cdot 7 \cdot \cancel6}{ \cancel4 \cdot \cancel3 \cdot \cancel2} \end{aligned} [/tex]

[tex] \rm _9C_4 = 9 \cdot 2 \cdot 7[/tex]

[tex] \rm _9C_4 = 126[/tex]

» Apply the Fundamental Counting Principle.

[tex] \rm FCP = \: _9C_7 \: \cdot \: _9C_4 [/tex]

[tex] \rm FCP = \: 36 \: \cdot \: 126 [/tex]

[tex] \rm FCP = 4\text,536 [/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] There are 4,536 ways to choose 7 students from 9 juniors and 4 seniors from 9 seniors.
