23. Articles that mention sidney walton from usa today


Answer:

Sidney Walton (born 11 February 1919) is a United States Army World War II veteran and a centenarian.[1]

Born on the Lower East Side of Manhattan, New York, on 11 February 1919, Walton spent his early life there, joining the Army in March 1941.[1][2] He served during World War II, being discharged in 1946 at the rank of corporal.[1]

After the war, he taught geology at Duke University and pursued graduate studies at Yale. He married school teacher Rena Bell in 1954.[1][2] He later had a civilian job at the United States Air Force base in Maine, and one at the NAS North Island in San Diego after having moved there in 1960.[1]

He lived in Clairemont, San Diego, until the death of his wife in 1982, and by the late 1980s had moved to Rancho Bernardo.[1]

In April 2018, he set out on a "No Regrets" tour with the goal of meeting the President of the United States and the Governors of all 50 states[1] By July 2019, he had met 23 governors and President Donald Trump.[3]

On February 2, 2020, Cpl. Walton was one of four surviving World War II veterans who participated in the coin flip for Super Bowl LIV.
