13. which of the following animals can reproduce by means of external fertilization a. frog b. lizard c. eagle d. tarsier​


Frog one of animal which can reproduce by means of external fertilization

Definition external and internal fertilization External fertilization is the process of fertilizing a female egg by a male egg (gamete fusion) that occurs outside the female's body. While internal fertilization is the process of fertilizing a female egg by a male egg (gamete fusion) that occurs inside the female's body.

Process of fertilization

External fertilization is carried out by the female and male both releasing egg cells and sperm cells into the environment.

Egg cells and sperm cells can then move in the environment and some of them meet each other so that fertilization occurs. After fertilization occurs, the development of the embryo will occur in that environment.

Internal fertilization is carried out by the male inserting his sperm cells into the female's body.

Unlike internal fertilization, the egg is not released into the environment but waits for the arrival of sperm cells in the body. Internal fertilization occurs in three ways namely oviparous, viviparous and ovoviviparous.

After internal fertilization occurs, the development process occurs, the development process takes place inside the female body.
