Simple and Maturity Value

Simple interest is calculated on the principal portion of a loan or the original contribution to a savings account. Simple Interest is calculated using the following formula: SI = P × R × T. Maturity value is the amount due and payable to the holder of a financial obligation as of the maturity date of the obligation. Refers to the remaining principal balance on a loan or bond. Maturity value is the same as par value.
