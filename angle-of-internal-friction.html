9. Solve for the force p to obtain equilibrium. angle of friction is 25° between block and the inclined plane.


Frictional Force

If the block is at rest, it is in equilibrium and the net force acting on it is zero. This means that the sum of the forces acting on the block must be zero. The forces acting on the block include the gravitational force (mg), the normal force (N), and the frictional force (f).

The gravitational force is equal to the mass of the block times the acceleration due to gravity (mg). The normal force is the force exerted by the surface on the block, perpendicular to the surface. The frictional force is the force that acts to oppose motion between the block and the surface.

• In this case, we can set up the following equation to solve for the force p:

mg sin 25° - N - f = 0

To solve for p, we need to know the values of the mass of the block (m), the acceleration due to gravity (g), the coefficient of friction between the block and the surface (μ), and the angle of the inclined plane (θ).

• Once we have these values, we can rearrange the equation to solve for p:

p = mg sin 25° - (μ * N)

• Note that the normal force (N) can be calculated using the following equation:

N = mg cos θ

• Substituting this equation into the equation for p gives us:

p = mg sin 25° - (μ * mg cos θ)

Solving for p gives us the force required to achieve equilibrium.

According to Newton's first law, a body in motion tends to remain in motion at a constant velocity. However, when you slide an object across a surface, the object eventually slows down and stops. Why? The object experiences a frictional force exerted by the surface, which opposes its motion.

The force of friction is always equal and opposite to the applied force.

Friction is the resistance to motion of one object moving relative to another. It is not a fundamental force, like gravity or electromagnetism. Instead, scientists believe it is the result of the electromagnetic attraction between charged particles in two touching surfaces

Sliding Friction

Sliding friction (also called kinetic friction) is a contact force that resists the sliding motion of two objects or an object and a surface.

Static Friction

Static friction is the force of friction on an object that is not moving. If you push on a stationary block and it doesn't move, it is being held by static friction which is equal and opposite to your push.

Fluid Friction​​

Fluid friction describes the friction between layers of a viscous fluid that are moving relative to each other.
