Answer:

A classic example of the materiality concept is a company expensing a $20 wastebasket in the year it is acquired instead of depreciating it over its useful life of 10 years. 15. Give three principles, out of the 10 principles discussed, on how you can be an effective and active listener. You may give specific examples or situations, whether in school, in the office, or in day to day life, when you can practice these principles. What is empathy and how does it relate to being a good listener ?​


Answer:

Three principles for effective and active listening are:

Pay attention and minimize distractions: Focus on the speaker and avoid multitasking or being distracted by external factors. For example, in a classroom setting, actively listen to the teacher by taking notes and avoiding the temptation to check your phone or engage in side conversations.

Show interest and ask questions: Demonstrate interest in the speaker's message by asking relevant and open-ended questions. For instance, in a job interview, actively listen to the interviewer's questions and provide thoughtful responses that show your knowledge and enthusiasm for the position.

Paraphrase and summarize: Repeat the speaker's message in your own words to confirm your understanding and show that you are actively listening. In a team meeting, for example, summarize the main points of the discussion to ensure that everyone is on the same page and can move forward with a clear understanding of the objectives.

Empathy is the ability to understand and share the feelings of others. It relates to being a good listener because it allows you to not only hear the speaker's words but also to understand their emotions and perspective. By practicing empathy, you can create a deeper connection with the speaker and demonstrate that you value their thoughts and feelings. For example, if a friend is going through a difficult time, actively listen to their concerns and offer support by showing empathy and compassion.

27. Describe the anthropometric design principles, give examples from everyday life to support your arguments.


Describe the anthropometric design principles: Anthropometric design principles are design guidelines that take into account the measurements and proportions of the human body. These principles are important in creating products, spaces, and environments that are comfortable, safe, and accessible for people of different sizes and abilities.

Give examples from everyday life to support your arguments:

ErgonomicsAccessibilityProportion and scaleAnthropometry

Ergonomics: This principle involves designing products and spaces that fit the human body and its movements. For example, chairs and desks that are adjustable to different heights can accommodate people of different sizes, and ergonomic computer mice and keyboards can reduce the risk of repetitive strain injuries.

Accessibility: This principle involves designing spaces and products that are accessible to people with disabilities. For example, wheelchair ramps, tactile signage, and adjustable height counters can make public spaces and buildings more inclusive for people with mobility, vision, or hearing impairments.

Proportion and scale: This principle involves using the human body as a reference for determining the size and layout of objects and spaces. For example, the height of shelves and cabinets in a kitchen or bathroom can be designed based on the average height of the users to make them easy to reach and use.

Anthropometry: This principle involves using data on human body measurements and proportions to inform design decisions. For example, car manufacturers use anthropometric data to design car seats that are comfortable and safe for drivers and passengers of different sizes, and clothing companies use anthropometric data to design clothes that fit well and flatter different body types.

30. Explain the principle of sincerity. - example of the principle of sincerity? - How are you going to apply such a principle of sincerity in your student life? -


The principle of sincerity is a moral and ethical principle that requires an individual to be truthful, honest, and authentic in their actions and communications with others. This principle emphasizes the importance of being genuine and transparent in one's intentions and behaviors and avoiding deceit, hypocrisy, and manipulation.

One example of the principle of sincerity is when a person is honest with themselves and others about their feelings and opinions, even if it may be uncomfortable or unpopular. For instance, if someone is asked for feedback on a project or performance, they should provide honest and constructive criticism rather than sugarcoating their thoughts or holding back to avoid hurting someone's feelings.

As a student, applying the principle of sincerity can be beneficial in various ways. For instance, being honest with oneself about one's strengths and weaknesses can help set realistic goals and avoid over-committing to activities that may not be achievable. Similarly, being truthful and authentic in interactions with peers and teachers can help build trust and foster positive relationships, leading to a better academic and social experience.

To apply the principle of sincerity in student life, one can start by reflecting on their values and intentions and being mindful of how they communicate with others. One can also practice active listening and empathy, striving to understand others' perspectives and being open to constructive feedback. Additionally, one can seek out opportunities to engage in honest and authentic conversations, whether it be in the classroom, during extracurricular activities, or with friends and family.
