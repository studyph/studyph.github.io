28. A cylinder water tank has a diameter of 4 feet and height of 7 feet while a water tank shapped like a rectangular prism has a length of 1 meter, a width of a 2 meters and a height of 2 meter. Which of the two tanks can hold more water? By how many cubic metersnSolution plss​


Answer:

cylinder area= 3.14r²× h [diameter × 1/2= radius]

=3.14(2²)× 7 = 87.92 ft³ =(approximately) 2.49m³

rectangular prism area= l × w × h

=1×2×2= 4m³

4m³-2.49m³= 1.51m³

Therefore, the rectangular prism can hold 1.51m³ more than the cylinder.


29. A cylindral water tank has a diameter of 4 feet and height of 7 feet while a water tank shaped like a rectangular prism has a length of 1 m, a width of 2 meters and a height of 2 meters. Which of the two tanks can hold more water? By how many cubic meters?


Given:

for tank1

d=4ft
h=7ft

V=Bh where B is the area of the base and h is the height

B=pi*r^2=pi(d/2)^2=pi*d^2/4
B=[pi(4)^2]/4
B=4pi sq ft

V1=(4pi)(7)
V1=28pi or approximately 87.9646 cubic ft

for tank2

L=1m
W=2m
h=2m

V=Bh (base area times height)

B=L*W
B=1*2
B=2 sq m

V2=(2)(2)
V2=4 cu. m


V1=(87.9646 ft^3)(1 m^3/(3.2808)^3 ft^3) = (approx.) 2.491 cu. m

therefore V1<V2
