22. Kyrie IV Language Texture Vocal perform Performance Text setting style


Kyrie IV

Language

Kyries are performed in Greek rather than Latin since they originate in Greek culture. Sung during the mass, the Kyrie follows the chanted Introit. Catholics traditionally recite each verse of the Kyrie three times, a numerological representation of their faith in the triune essence of God.

Texture

The Kyrie possesses a beautiful polyphonic sound. Its six vocal components combine harmoniously despite their continual imitation of one another. The employment of six voices, rather than four, contributes to the music's richer tone than in Josquin's Ave Maria. The melodies' graceful arcs evoke gregorian chants.

A vocal perform

The first known documentation of the Kyrie Eleison sung in a Roman Catholic church dates back to the seventh century.

Performance

The Kyrie is the first prayer of the Mass Ordinary in the Tridentine Mass. Kyrie, Eleison" is what is being said over and over again (or "Lord, have mercy"). A musical setting of the mass will typically (but not always) include this. The musical structure of a Kyrie is frequently ternary (ABA), reflecting the symmetrical nature of the text. There are a variety of musical styles that can be used, from Gregorian chant to folk.

Nearly every one of the thousands of composers who have set the ordinaries of the mass to music over the centuries has included a movement of the Kyrie, as it is the first item in configurations of the mass ordinary and the second in the requiem mass (the only mass proper set regularly over the centuries).

Text Setting style

Kyrie musical passages typically have a ternary (ABA) form that mirrors the symmetrical nature of the text. Different musical styles, from Gregorian chant to folk, are used in different contexts.

23. language syntax and meaning of the graphic novel spring primarily through the relationship between images than words pasagot po ng tama explain po ​


The statement means that in a graphic novel, the syntax and meaning of the story are communicated primarily through the relationship between the images or illustrations, rather than the words or text. The images in a graphic novel are used to convey emotions, actions, and settings that are central to the story, while the text is used to provide additional information, such as dialogue or narration. Therefore, the relationship between the images is more important than the words, as the former provides the primary source of information and context for the story. The images and text work together to create a complete and cohesive narrative, but the images are the primary vehicle for telling the story in a graphic novel.
