Answer

As a Believer, your best respond to sympathize with them and tell them, "I know someone who can give you better help than I can."

Explanation

“I am no good!" "Nobody loves me." "I am hopeless."

These are the emotional responses of someone who has anxiety, loneliness, and depression. As Believers, this is an opportunity to lead anyone into having a relationship and true faith in Jesus Christ.

Loneliness is very hazardous for anyone's health. As Believers, we should sympathize with them and let them know that they are not alone.  In fact, being alone is an opportunity to talk with Jesus, because, we all have ourselves to God.

In Romans 5:6-8 you can read that Paul says that only a very few could die for a good person. And as sinners, we are evil in the eyes of the Lord. All of us have sinned(lying, lusting, stealing, etc.), but God gave his unconditional love to us by giving his Son, Jesus, for a chance at eternal life.

Romans 5:6-8, “For while we were still weak, at the right time Christ died for the ungodly. For one will scarcely die for a righteous person—though perhaps for a good person one would dare even to die—but God shows his love for us in that while we were still sinners, Christ died for us.”

We can teach them that God loves us so much that His son, Jesus, sacrificed for us all even before we could even love him.
