14. why was it necessary to put at least 40 ml of distilled water in the beaker before adding the computed amount of concentrated acid?


Answer:

It is necessary to put at least 40 mL of distilled water in a beaker before adding the computed amount of concentrated acid because it avoids violent boiling, and splashing out of the acid in the container.

Explanation:

In general chemistry, when strong acid or concentrated acid is being mixed with water, a large amount of heat is released. When you add more acid, more energy is also being released.

If you put the acid on the container first then pour water on it, you will form an extremely concentrated solution of an acid with too much heat. This will result to violent boiling, and splashing of the concentration solution you have made. This will cause laboratory accidents.

If you put acid to water, you'll form a very dilute solution with less heat.

That is why during laboratory experiments that uses mixture of acid and water, it is always advised to put "acid to water".

27. 1. Prepare 800 mL of 2M sodium chloride. How many grams of NaCl needs to be dissolve in a 800 mL distilled water?2. Prepare 100 mL of 1.0 M hydrochloric acid from concentrated (12.1 M) hydrochloric acid. How many mL of concentrated HCl is to be put in a 100 mL distilled water.​


1. mass of NaCl : = 93.6 g

2. volume of concentrated HCl : 8.26 ml

Further explanation

Dilution is the process of adding solvents (generally water) that aims to obtain a solution with a concentration that is smaller than the initial concentration of a more concentrated solution

So the principle of dilution is to get a lower concentration.  

In dilution, the number of mols dissolved before and after dilution is the same  

The dilution formula is as follows:  

M₁V₁=M₂V₂  

M₁ = Molarity of the solution before dilution  

V₁ = volume of the solution before dilution  

M₂ = Molarity of the solution after dilution  

V₂ = Molarity volume of the solution after dilution

Molarity shows the number of moles of solute in every 1 liter of solution or mmol in each ml of solution

M = n : V

1.

V = 800 ml = 0.8 L

M = 2 M

n = M x V

n = 2 mol/L x 0.8 L

n = 1.6 mol

mass NaCl(molar mass : 58.5) :

= mol x molar mass

= 1.6 mol x 58.5 g/mol

= 93.6 g

2.

M₂ = 1 M

V₂ = 100 ml = 0.1 L

M₁ = 12.1 M

Input the value in dilution formula :

M₁V₁=M₂V₂  

12.1 x V₁ = 1 x 0.1

V₁ = 0.00826 L = 8.26 ml
