14. how much the cost of light and heat in broiler production ​


➛Question:

how much the cost of light and heat in broiler production

Answer:

The cost of light and heat in broiler production can vary depending on several factors, such as the climate in the region where the broiler farm is located, the size of the operation, and the type of equipment used. However, in general, heating and lighting costs can account for a significant portion of the total production costs. For heating, the cost will depend on the fuel source used. Propane, natural gas, or electricity are commonly used to provide heat in broiler houses. The cost of these fuel sources will vary depending on the region and the time of year. Additionally, the cost of maintaining and operating the heating equipment, such as boilers or furnaces, will also factor into the overall cost. For lighting, the cost will depend on the type of bulbs used, the number of hours of light provided per day, and the cost of electricity in the region. LED bulbs are becoming increasingly popular for broiler production as they are more energy-efficient and have a longer lifespan than traditional incandescent bulbs.
