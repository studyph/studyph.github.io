Area = π r^2

That means pi times radius squared.

If you are given the diameter (as in #1a - #1j), you can use the diameter to find the radius and then use the radius to find the area:  

Step 1: Divide the diameter by 2 and that gives you the radius.

Step 2: Multiply the 3.14 by the radius squared

So, for 1.a.:

Step 1: 84 divided by 2 = 42, so radius = 42

(Note: The radius squared is the radius times the radius, so in this case, 42 times 42 = 1764)

Step 2: area = 3.14 x 1764 = 5538.96 mm

For 1.c.:  

Step 1: 14/3 divided by 2 = 14/6 cm

Step 2: area = 3.14 x 5.44444 = 17.095 cm

Etc.

For #2a - #2j, you are given the radius, so you don’t need to calculate the radius, so you can skip step 1.

So for 2.a.:

Skip Step 1.

Step 2: area = 3.14 x 1600 =  5024 mm
