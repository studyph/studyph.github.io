1. production function meaning?​


Answer:

In economics, a production function gives the technological relation between quantities of physical inputs and quantities of output of goods. The production function is one of the key concepts of mainstream neoclassical theories, used to define marginal product and to distinguish allocative efficiency, a key focus of economics. One important purpose of the production function is to address allocative efficiency in the use of factor inputs in production and the resulting distribution of income to those factors, while abstracting away from the technological problems of achieving technical efficiency, as an engineer or professional manager might understand it.

3. Research the different methods of enhancing or decorating finished products. Know their meanings and specify their role/function in innovating the products


Answer:

1. Laser Engraving: This form of decorating uses a laser beam to etch a design into the material, creating intricate patterns or text. Laser engraving is used on a variety of materials from wood to acrylics to metals, and can be used for both production and prototyping purposes. It produces an incredibly precise finish that is both permanent and long-lasting.

2. Silk Screen Printing: Silk screen printing is a popular decorating technique for large-scale orders, digital or otherwise. This process involves pressing a stencil through a mesh screen onto a desired object. The stencil is pressed against the object, and a specific ink is then imprinted onto the object to produce a unique and specifically designed artwork.

3. Embroidery: This decorative technique involves using a needle and thread to create a design on a fabric item. Embroidery adds a unique and personalized touch to any product and can be used to showcase logos and artwork on fabrics like t-shirts, caps, and bags.

4. Decals: Decals are a type of adhesive label used to decorate products. They are available in a range of shapes, sizes, and colors and can be printed or designed to feature any image or text.

5. Foil Stamping: Foil stamping is a type of printing process that uses a metallic or colorful foil to enhance the look of a product by imprinting a design onto the surface of the item. The final product is a foil-adhered logo or artwork that adds a touch of shine and glamor.

6. Hot Stamping: This is a decoration technique that involves the application of a heated metal die with a design to the surface of the product. This method of decorating is mostly used to imprint text onto leather, plastic, and other materials, as the heat melts the die into the substrate.

7. Pad Printing: Pad printing is a common method used to decorate products with printable ink. It involves transferring an image to a silicone pad, which is then pressed onto the product. This technique uses specific inks and covers a wide variety of materials, and is ideal for items that need to be printed on their sides or in awkward shapes.

8. Sand etching: Also known as sandblasting, this technique removes a thin layer of the top of the product's surface using sand particles propelled at high speeds. The overall effect is a dull etched level on the surface of the material, which can be customized to feature intricate designs.
