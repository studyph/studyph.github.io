6. GAS LAWS 1. WHAT WILL BE THE VOLUME OF 200ML OF ARGON AT 125 DEGREES CELSIUS WHEN THE TEMPERATURE RISES TO 250 DEGREES CELSIUS? 2. IF A SAMPLE OF NITROGEN OCCUPIES 505ML AT -23 DEGREES CELSIUS, WHAT VOLUME DOES IT OCCUPY AT 23 DEGREES CELSIUS? 3. IF A SAMPLE OF AMMONIA OCCUPIES A VOLUME OF 1.5L AT 50 DEGREES CELCIUS, TO WHAT TEMPERATURE MUST THE GAS BE LOWERED TO REDUCE ITS VOLUME TO 1.0 L?


Answer:

1. WHAT WILL BE THE VOLUME OF 200ML OF ARGON AT 125 DEGREES CELSIUS WHEN THE TEMPERATURE RISES TO 250 DEGREES CELSIUS?

V₁ = 200 mL

T₁ = 125⁰C

V₂ = ?

T₂ = 250⁰C

Charles’s law (formula):

V₁T₂ = V₂T₁

V₂ = V₁T₂/T₁

Charles’s law calculations require temperature on an absolute scale, such as the Kelvin scale.

Convert ⁰C to K

125⁰C + 273.15 = 398.15 K

250⁰C + 273.15 = 523.15 K

V₂ = V₁T₂/T₁

V₂ = (200 mL)(523.15 K)/398.15 K

V₂ = 262.79 mL ≈ 263 mL

2. IF A SAMPLE OF NITROGEN OCCUPIES 505ML AT -23 DEGREES CELSIUS, WHAT VOLUME DOES IT OCCUPY AT 23 DEGREES CELSIUS?

V₁ = 505 mL

T₁ = -23⁰C

V₂ = ?

T₂ = 23⁰C

Convert ⁰C to K

-23⁰C + 273.15 = 250.15 K

23⁰C + 273.15 = 296.15 K

V₂ = V₁T₂/T₁

V₂ = (505 mL)(296.15 K)/250.15 K

V₂ = 597.86 mL ≈ 598 mL

3. IF A SAMPLE OF AMMONIA OCCUPIES A VOLUME OF 1.5L AT 50 DEGREES CELSIUS, TO WHAT TEMPERATURE MUST THE GAS BE LOWERED TO REDUCE ITS VOLUME TO 1.0 L?

V₁ = 1.5 L

T₁ = 50⁰C

V₂ = 1.0 L

T₂ = ?

Convert ⁰C to K

50⁰C + 273.15 = 323.15 K

T₂ = V₂T₁/V₁

T₂ = (1.0 L)(323.15 K)/1.5 L

T₂ = 215.43 K

Answer:

10 degrees Celcius

Explanation:

The speed of sound decreases as the temperature decreases.

"In fact sound travels faster when the air is hotter." (Strong, 2019)

Answer:

10 degree Celcius

The higher the temperature, the more the gas particles wiggle and bounce. It takes less time for particles that are already moving quickly to push against the particles around them than it takes particles that are moving slowly. Sound, therefore, travels faster in high temperature than in low temperature.
