9. Examine how prose and poetry differ from one another: language use


Prose is a straight forward form of literature, wherein the author expresses his thoughts and feelings in a lucid way. On the other hand, poetry is a form of literature in which the poet uses a unique style and rhythm to express intense experience. In terms of language use, prose and poetry differ to each other.

Prose refers to a form of literature having ordinary language and sentence structure. Prose are straight forward and informative. On the other hand, poetry are expressive or decorated which is aesthetic by nature because it has sound, cadence, rhyme, metre that adds to its meaning.

30. 1. Compare and contrast the language of poetry and the language of science.​


In poetry, the author has creative control, which means he or she can write anything. Grammar can be forgotten as you choose to write about your feelings or any topic. Plus, you can be informal and write as if you're talking to a friend. Meanwhile, in science, the language must be more formal, which means you need to use proper grammar and punctuation. Also, you cannot use contractions and you have to write based on what happened to your experiment and whatever research topic you engaged in.
