2. A certain pycnometer weighs 20.00 g when empty, 22.00g when filled with water, and 21.76g when filled with benzene. Find the density of benzene


Answer:

0.88 g/mL

Explanation:

Water's density is 1 g/mL

Volume of water = 22 - 20 = 2 g

Volume of benzene = 21.76 - 20 = 1.76 g

Density of benzene = 1 g/mL × (1.76 g / 2 g)

= 1 g/mL × 0.88 g

= 0.88 g/mL

Answer:

In a bond, the more electronegative element will have a greater share of the electrons, and a partial negative charge to reflect this greater electron density. The less electronegative element will have a partial positive charge to reflect the lack of electron density.

18. · A solution with a density of 0.876 g/mL contains 5.0 g of toluene (C⁷H⁸)and 225 g of benzene. Calculate the molarity of the solution.​


Molarity (M)Discussion:

Molarity is one of the commonly used units in chemistry. Molarity is defined as the number of moles of solute per liter of solution --- that is,

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}}[/tex]

For example, a 1.46 [tex]M[/tex] glucose solution contains 1.46 mol of the solute (glucose) in 1 L of the solution. Of course, we do not always work with solution volumes of 1 L. Thus, a 500-mL solution containing 0.730 mol of glucose also has a concentration of 1.46 [tex]M.[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Problem:

A solution with a density of 0.876 g/mL contains 5.0 g of toluene (C⁷H⁸) and 225 g of benzene. Calculate the molarity of the solution.

Given:

[tex]\text{mass of solute = 5.0 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass of solvent = 225 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{density of solution = 0.876 g/mL}[/tex]

[tex]\text{solute = toluene}[/tex]

[tex]\text{solvent = benzene}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]\text{molarity}[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of solute.

molar mass of solute = (12.01 g/mol × 7) + (1.008 g/mol × 8)

molar mass of solute = 92.134 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{5.0 g}}{\text{92.134 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 0.05426878 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the mass of solution.

mass of solution = mass of solute + mass of solvent

mass of solution = 5.0 g + 225 g

mass of solution = 230 g

Step 4: Calculate the volume of solution.

[tex]\text{volume of solution} = \frac{\text{mass of solution}}{\text{density of solution}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solution} = \frac{\text{230 g}}{\text{0.876 g/mL}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solution = 262.5571 mL = 0.2625571 L}[/tex]

Step 5: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{0.05426878 mol}}{\text{0.2625571 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity} = 0.207 \: M}[/tex]

23. A solution of benzene and toluene was prepared by adding toluene to benzene until the total volume becomes 135mL. By volume, the solution is 30.0% benzene. How much benzene, in mL, is in the solution?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \%(v/v) = 30.0\% \\ & V_{\text{solution}} = \text{135 mL} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the volume of solute (benzene).

[tex]\begin{aligned} V_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{\%(v/v)}{100\%} \times V_{\text{solution}} \\ & = \frac{30.0\%}{100\%} \times \text{135 mL} \\ & = \boxed{\text{40.5 mL}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the volume of benzene in the solution is 40.5 mL.
