2. three angle of a quadrilateral are 68 degrees 78 degrees and 122 degrees what is the measure of the fourth angle​


[tex]\large\bold\blue{\tt{PROBLEM:}}[/tex]

Three angles of a quadrilateral are 68 degrees 78 degrees and 122 degrees what is the measure of the fourth angle.

[tex]\large\bold\blue{\tt{SOLUTION:}}[/tex]

The sum of the interior angles of a quadrilateral is 360°. Thus,

[tex]\large\tt m∠1 + m∠2 + m∠3 + m∠4 = 360° \\ \\ \large\tt68° + 78 °+ 122 °+ m∠4 = 360° \\ \\ \large\tt m∠4 = 360° - 68° - 78° - 122° \\ \\ \large \red{\boxed{\tt{ m∠4 = 92°}}}[/tex]

[tex]\large\bold\blue{\tt{CONCLUSION:}}[/tex]

∴ The measure of the fourth angle is 92°

7. the roof of a school canteen is triangular in shape what is the measure of the third angle if the other angles measure 78 degrees and 48° degrees,​


Answer:

Sum of interior angles of a triangle is equal to 180°.

Another way of understand this is, the 3 angles inside a triangle adds upto 180.

so we can say that,

∠1 + ∠2 + ∠3 = 180

since we already know the measurements of the 2 angles as,

∠1 = 78°

∠2 = 48°

so now let's solve for the measurement of the 3rd angle, ∠3

∠1 + ∠2 + ∠3 = 180

78 + 48 + ∠3 = 180

126 + ∠3 = 180

∠3 = 180 - 126

∠3 = 54°

Therefore the measurement of the third angle, ∠3, is 54°.

29. what are the complementary and supplementary angle of 78°?​


Answer:

The supplement of an angle of 78 is 102. Supplementary angles are angles whose sum is equal to 180. To get the measurement of a supplement of an angle subtract that angle from 180. Subtracting 78 from 180 will give you 102.

Answer:

Complementary Angle = 12°

Supplementary Angle = 102°

Step-by-step explanation:

Let the complementary and supplementary angle of 78° be c and s.

Complementary angles are two angles that have a sum of 90°

Thus,

[tex]\rm78^{\circ}+c = 90^{\circ}[/tex]

[tex]\rm{c = 90^{\circ} - 78^{\circ}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\rm{c=12^{\circ}}}[/tex]

Supplementary angles are two angles that have a sum of 180°

Thus,

[tex]\rm{78^{\circ} + s = 180^{\circ}}[/tex]

[tex]\rm{s = 180^{\circ} - 78^{\circ}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\rm{s = 102^{\circ}}}[/tex]
