At a pressure of 60 atm and a temperature of -100°C, carbon dioxide is in a solid state, also known as dry ice.At a pressure of 100 atm and a temperature of 20°C, carbon dioxide would be in a supercritical fluid state, which exhibits properties of both gases and liquids.The triple point of carbon dioxide occurs at a temperature of -56.6°C and a pressure of 5.2 atm, where solid, liquid, and gas phases can coexist in equilibrium.If the temperature of a bottle of CO2 at 1 atm pressure and 100°C is decreased to -120°C, the CO2 will undergo a phase change from a gas to a solid (dry ice).Carbon dioxide will sublime (transition from a solid directly to a gas) at a pressure of 1 atm and a temperature of -78.5°C.

Problem:

if a gas at 25.0 occupies 3.60 liters at a pressure of 1.00 atm,what will be its volume at apressure of 2.50 atm​

Given data:

V₁ (initial volume) = 3.60 liters             P₁ (initial pressure) = 1.00 atm

V₂ (final volume)  = ? unknown (atm)  P₂ (final pressure) = 2.50 atm

Solution:

In solving this problem, we can use the formula of

Boyle's law which is P₁V₁ = P₂V₂

                                           P₁V₁ = P₂V₂

                    1.00 atm ×  3.60 L = 2.50 atm ×  V₂

  1.00 atm ×  3.60 L / 2.50 atm =  V₂

             3.60  atm.L / 2.50 atm = V₂

                                          1.44 L = V₂
