3. a helium balloon has a volume of 5.00L at S.T.P. How many moles of helium will this balloon have?


Note: STP means 0°C or 273 K at 1 atm or 100 kPa.

So...

PV = nRT
where:
P = pressure
V = volume
n = amount of substance or moles
R = universal gas constant (8.3144598 L kPa K−1 mol−1)
T = temperature

So...
PV= nRT
(100 kPa)(5 L) = n(273.15 K)(8.3144598 L kPa/ K mol)
500 kPa.L = n(2270.96 kPa.L/ mol)
500 kPa.L / 2270.96 kPa.L/ mol = n
0.22 mol = n

8. There are 40 liters of helium in a balloon at 100 K. If the temperature of the balloon is increased to 200 K, what will the new volume of the balloon be?


Given:

[tex]V_{1} = \text{40 L}[/tex]

[tex]T_{1} = \text{100 K}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \text{200 K}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]V_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\frac{V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \frac{V_{2}}{T_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = V_{1} × \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{40 L} × \frac{\text{200 K}}{\text{100 K}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{V_{2} = \text{80 L}}[/tex]

19. A weather balloon contains 1.10x105 mol of helium and has a volume of 2.70x 106 L at 1.0 atm pressure. Calculate the temperature of the helium in the balloon in kelvins and in celsius​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P = \text{1.00 atm} \\ & V = 2.70 \times 10^6 \: \text{L} \\ & n = 1.10 \times 10^5 \: \text{mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the temperature of the gas in kelvin and in degree Celsius using the ideal gas equation.

[tex]\begin{aligned} T & = \frac{PV}{\text{nR}} \\ & = \frac{(\text{1.00 atm})(2.70 \times 10^6 \: \text{L})}{(1.10 \times 10^5 \: \text{mol})\left(0.082057 \: \dfrac{\text{L}\cdot\text{atm}}{\text{mol}\cdot\text{K}}\right)} \\ & = \boxed{\text{299 K}} \\ & = \boxed{26^{\circ}\text{C}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the temperature of the helium in kelvins and in degree Celsius are 299 K and 26°C, respectively.

28. A weather balloon filled with 2.0 mol Helium has a volume of 40.0 L. To what volume, in L, will the balloon expand if 4.0 mol of helium is added, to give a total of 6.0 mol of helium keeping the temperature and pressure the same?


Given:

[tex]V_{1} = \text{40.0 L}[/tex]

[tex]n_{1} = \text{2.0 mol}[/tex]

[tex]n_{2} = \text{6.0 mol}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]V_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\frac{V_{1}}{n_{1}} = \frac{V_{2}}{n_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = V_{1} × \frac{n_{2}}{n_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{40.0 L} × \frac{\text{6.0 mol}}{\text{2.0 mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{V_{2} = \text{120. L}}[/tex]
