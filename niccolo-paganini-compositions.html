8. 5 facts about the composer and their compositions 1. NICCOLO PAGANINI2. FREDERIC CHOPIN3. FRANZ LISZT4. ROBERT SCHUMANNPA HELP PLS​


Answer:

1. NICCOLO PAGANINI

- Niccolo Paganini's fame spread across Europe with a concert tour that started in Vienna in August 1828, stopping in every major European city in Germany, Poland, and Bohemia until February 1831 in Strasbourg.

- At the age of five, Paganini started learning the mandolin from his father, and moved to the violin by the age of seven.

- Paganini became a violinist for the Baciocchi court, while giving private lessons to Elisa’s husband, Felice.

- Paganini was part of the entourage, but, towards the end of 1809, he left Baciocchi to resume his freelance career.

- He was diagnosed with syphilis as early as 1822, and his remedy, which included mercury and opium, came with serious physical and psychological side effects.

2. FREDERIC CHOPIN

- Chopin was born Fryderyk Franciszek Chopin in the Duchy of Warsaw and grew up in Warsaw, which in 1815 became part of Congress Poland.

- Most of his compositions are for solo piano, though he also wrote two piano concertos, a few chamber pieces, and about 19 songs set to Polish lyrics.

- His keyboard style is esoteric and often technically demanding. His own performances were noted for their nuance and sensitivity.

- Chopin invented the concept of the instrumental ballade.

- His major piano works also include mazurkas, waltzes, nocturnes, polonaises, etudes, impromptus, scherzos, preludes and sonatas, some published only posthumously.

3. FRANZ LISZT

- In the field of music, he contributed to the development of thematic transformation concept and symphonic poem invention.

- Liszt decided to relocate to Paris after the death of his father in 1827. In the morning until the late night, he worked by giving lessons of composition and piano to get money after he left touring.

- The earlier musical interest of Liszt was developed after he listened to the piano playing of his father when he was six years old. He was interested to know more about the Romani and sacred music.

- Liszt left his legacy of musical works to anticipate the new coming of trends and ideas in 20th century. His works were also influential for the musical contemporaries.

- The birthplace of Liszt was located in Doborjan, Sopron County in the Hungarian Kingdom of Austrian Empire. His father was Adam Liszt, while his mother was Anna Liszt.

4. ROBERT SCHUMANN

- Neue Zeitschrift für Musik is a journal of music, which often published the musical writings of Schumann. He was one of the co-founders of this Leipzig-based publication.

- Until 1840, Schumann only published musical compositions for piano. Then he expanded the works into orchestra and piano. He also composed chamber works, choral, one opera, four symphonies and Lieder. The latter ones are the songs for voice and piano.

- In 1833, Schumann experienced a severe melancholic depressive episode. That was the first manifestation of his mental disorder. It occurred a couple times during his life. He had delusional ideas related to the metallic items, which might threaten or poison him.

- Schumann entered a metal asylum located in Endenich near Bonn after he attempted to commit suicide in 1854. In 1856, Schumann passed away two years after he was sent to the metal asylum. His mental disorder was never recovered.

- In 19th century, Schumann was considered as an influential musician and composer. After he married Clara, Schumann’s composition was defined as more conservative. After his death, he left many forms of musical compositions that the future generations liked to play and learn.


9. Niccolo Paganini essay​


Answer:

Niccolo Paganini was a prominent Italian violinist, guitarist, and composer of the 19th century. He was born in Genoa, Italy, in 1782, and was recognized as a child prodigy. Despite being physically weak, he was a virtuoso musician and revolutionized the playing of both the violin and the guitar. His innovative techniques and unique style of performance made him a sensation in Europe, and he gained immense popularity through his tours and performances. Paganini's influence on music in the 19th century was profound, and he is still remembered today as one of the most unique and enigmatic performers in history.

Paganini's music was inspired by various sources, including folk melodies, classical music, and the influence of his contemporaries. He is particularly known for the depth and intensity of his compositions, which he completed in a rapid-fire manner. Paganini was skilled in writing for both the violin and guitar and used his own instruments to create sounds that had never been heard before.

In terms of performance, Paganini was known for his showmanship, his technical skills, and his virtuosity. During his concerts, he would perform extraordinary stunts, such as playing with a single string, playing without music notes, or playing with his back to the audience. These stunts amazed his audiences, and it was this showmanship that made him so popular.

Paganini was also known for his association with the devilish legend. There were many rumors about his skill that suggested he had sold his soul to the devil in exchange for his musical abilities. Although most of these rumors were unfounded, they added to the legend of Paganini's persona and only contributed to his allure.

Paganini's impact on music was tremendous, and he is remembered as one of the greatest guitarists and violinists to have ever existed. His innovations and his technical prowess were so profound that they continued to inspire generations of musicians even after his death. Paganini's compositions and performances were profound, and he helped to create a renaissance in the composition and performance of music.

In conclusion, Niccolo Paganini was a remarkable musician who changed the course of music with his technical innovations and stunning performances. He revolutionized the performance of the guitar and the violin and his compositions have inspired generations of musicians. Despite his enigmatic persona, his immense contributions to music remain undeniable, and he remains one of the greatest musicians in history.
