26. a kite has pair of right angles and the measures of the two non congruent angles are in the ratio 12:33.find the measures​


SOLVING FOR THE UNKNOWN ANGLES USING RATIO

Problem:

A kite has pair of right angles and the measures of the two non congruent angles are in the ratio 12:33. Find their measures​.

Answers with Step-by-step explanation:

Since the kite is a kind of quadrilateral, the sum of all its interior angles equates to 360 degrees.

Given that the kite has a pair of right angles, then this means that it has two 90-degree angles, and added up to 180 degrees. This also means that the remaining sum of two non-congruent angles is also 180 degrees.

The problem tells us that the ratio of these remaining angles are 12:33. To solve for their measures, we will add first 12 and 33, which is 45. Then, we will divide 180 by 45. This will give us a quotient of 4.

The next step is to multiply 4 to 12 and 33.

12 x 4 = 48 degrees33 x 4 = 132 degrees

Therefore, the measure of the two angles are 48 and 132 degrees.
