17. 1.From the poem Desiderata, Get/Choose one stanza and explain it on a structuralist point of view.​


Answer:

Desiderata is a beautiful poem that offers a lot of wisdom and insight into how to live a fulfilling life. One of its stanzas is:

"Take kindly the counsel of the years,

gracefully surrendering the things of youth.

Nurture strength of spirit to shield you in sudden misfortune.

But do not distress yourself with dark imaginings.

Many fears are born of fatigue and loneliness."

From a structuralist point of view, this stanza can be analyzed in terms of binary oppositions, which are central to structuralist thought. The stanza presents several oppositions that create meaning and structure within the poem.

The first opposition is between youth and age, which is presented through the idea of "gracefully surrendering the things of youth." This suggests that there is a natural and necessary process of aging and letting go of youthful things, which is something that should be accepted and embraced.

The second opposition is between strength and vulnerability, which is presented through the idea of "nurturing strength of spirit to shield you in sudden misfortune." This suggests that while we should strive to be strong and resilient, we must also acknowledge our vulnerability and the fact that we cannot control everything that happens to us.

The third opposition is between light and dark, which is presented through the idea of "not distressing yourself with dark imaginings." This suggests that there is a tendency to imagine worst-case scenarios and to dwell on negative thoughts, which can be counterproductive and damaging.

Overall, this stanza presents a message of balance and acceptance, encouraging us to embrace the natural process of aging, to cultivate strength and resilience, and to avoid dwelling on negative thoughts and fears. By presenting these ideas through binary oppositions, the stanza creates structure and meaning within the poem, highlighting the importance of finding balance and harmony in life.
