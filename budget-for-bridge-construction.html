1. A government official's budget is represented by 6x^2+13x+12. Represent the amount of budget after 3x+29 was used for the construction of a bridge


Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

If the government official's budget is represented by the polynomial 6x^2+13x+12, then the amount of budget remaining after 3x+29 was used for the construction of a bridge can be represented by subtracting 3x+29 from the original budget. This gives us the following:

6x^2+13x+12 - (3x+29)

= 6x^2+13x+12 - 3x - 29

= 6x^2+10x-17

This is the polynomial that represents the amount of budget remaining after the construction of the bridge. It can be written in the form ax^2 + bx + c, where a = 6, b = 10, and c = -17.

12. What is the role of a salt bridge in the construction of a galvanic cell? ​


Answer:

A salt bridge or ion bridge, in electrochemistry, is a laboratory device used to connect the oxidation and reduction half-cells of a galvanic cell (voltaic cell), a type of electrochemical cell. It maintains electrical neutrality within the internal circuit.

Answer:

The purpose of the salt bridge is to act as a source of spectator ions that can migrate into each of the half cells to preserve neutrality. Any charge buildup in the solutions of the two half cells is known as a junction potential.

Completion of Panguil Bay Bridge unhampered, NEDA assures

CAGAYAN DE ORO CITY – The National Economic and Development Authority in Northern Mindanao (NEDA-10) on Friday assured that the PHP7.3-billion Panguil Bay Bridge project will be completed by 2023, regardless of the change in government administration.

NEDA-10 director Mylah Faye Aurora Cariño, in an online briefing, said the project is funded under official development plans, which means it is part of the national project with contracts signed between government agencies and infrastructure contractors that need to be fulfilled.

"We don't see negative sentiments, don't see (adverse) political intervention. In fact, there's vocal, explicit support in the province of Misamis Occidental and Lanao del Norte provinces," Cariño said.

The two provinces, she said, would ensure that the project will not be delayed.

Considered a major infrastructure development in Northern Mindanao, the project will connect Tangub City, Misamis Occidental and Tubod, Lanao del Norte within seven minutes.

Of the PHP7.37 billion total project cost, PHP2.16 billion was financed by the national government while PHP5.21 billion is a loan agreement between the Philippine government and Korean Export-Import Bank (Korea Eximbank).

Data from the Department of Public Works and Highways (DPWH) showed that as of March 2021, the preparation for the bored pile works at Tangub and Tubod side, earthworks, and embankment on the access road and approach road are already ongoing.

DPWH Secretary Mark Villar earlier said the bridge would soon become the longest bridge in the Philippines at 3.16 km., which would surpass the 2.16-km. San Juanico Bridge that connects Samar and Leyte provinces in the Eastern Visayas region.

Although the Covid-19 pandemic restriction on the movement of people and goods has affected the bridge construction, Villar said the contractor can now go full blast with the delivery of equipment and materials for its foundation works. (PNA)
