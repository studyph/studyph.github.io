[tex] \sf{}R = \dfrac{V}{I}[/tex]

R = Resistance

I = Current

V = Voltage

[tex] \\ \sf{}R = \frac{120}{40} [/tex]

[tex] \\ \sf{}R = 3[/tex]

The resistance of the 2 loads is 3Ω.

Now, find the resistance possesed by each loads.

[tex] \\ \sf{}2R = 3[/tex]

[tex] \\ \sf{}R = \frac{3}{2} [/tex]

[tex] \\ \huge \boxed{\sf{}R = 1.5} \\ \\ \\ \\ \\ [/tex]

15. In parallel circuit, what is the effect of the number of resistance or load to the strength of the current?


The total resistance in a parallel circuit is less than the smallest of the individual resistances. Each resistor in parallel has the same voltage of the source applied to it (voltage is constant in a parallel circuit).

As more resistors are added in parallel, the total current strength increases. The overall resistance of the circuit must therefore have decreased.

25. A 24-volt battery having an internal resistance of 0.2 ohm s connected by two 3-ohm conductors to a variable resistor. (a) For what value of load resistance RL will the load resistor be a maximum? (b) What will be the maximum load power and the corresponding efficiency?


Answer:

(a) The maximum load resistance will occur when the load resistance is equal to the total resistance of the circuit, which is equal to the resistance of the battery plus the resistance of the conductors. Therefore, the maximum load resistance is 24 volts / 0.2 ohms + 3 ohms + 3 ohms = 180 ohms.

(b) The maximum load power will be equal to the voltage across the load resistance multiplied by the current through the load resistance. The current through the load resistance is equal to the total current in the circuit, which is equal to the battery current divided by the total resistance of the circuit. Therefore, the maximum load power is 24 volts * (24 volts / (0.2 ohms + 3 ohms + 3 ohms)) = 2,160 watts.

The efficiency of the circuit is equal to the load power divided by the battery power, so the corresponding efficiency is 2,160 watts / (24 volts * 0.2 ohms) = 90%.
