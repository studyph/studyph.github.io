13. reflection about history of taekwondo​

History of Taekwondo

Explanation:

One of the earliest clues of Taekwondo’s existence is a mural painted on the wall of a tomb that was built in the Korean kingdom of Koguryo, between 37 BC and 66 AD. The drawing shows two unarmed figures facing each other in a Taekwondo style stance. Additional drawings in the tomb show figures performing blocks and wearing uniforms similar to those used in modern day Taekwondo training.

The advancement of Taekwondo and its techniques developed as the country of Korea developed. There are examples and history of Taekwondo training in virtually all the records of the different kingdoms that existed within the country throughout the centuries.

Following the Silla dynasty came the Koryo dynasty (935 AD – 1352 AD) from which Korea takes its name. Martial arts practice, known as Subak Do, became popular as an organized sport with detailed rules. The royal family sponsored competitions and demonstrations, and martial arts became deeply rooted in Korean culture.

15. reflection about history and structure of interpol​


Answer:

Interpol is a multinational law enforcement organization that was founded in 1923. It is headquartered in Lyon, France, and currently has 190 member countries. The organization's main goal is to facilitate international police cooperation and assistance in order to combat cross-border crimes, such as terrorism, drug trafficking, and human trafficking.

Interpol has a decentralized structure, with a General Secretariat at the head of the organization and a number of regional and functional bureaus responsible for specific areas of activity. Each member country has a National Central Bureau (NCB) that serves as a point of contact with Interpol and coordinates law enforcement activities within the country.

Throughout its history, Interpol has played a significant role in helping member countries cooperate and exchange information in order to combat cross-border crimes. It has also developed a range of tools and services to support international police cooperation, including a secure communication system and a database of criminal records and wanted persons.

Overall, Interpol's history and structure reflect its important role in facilitating international police cooperation and helping to make the world a safer place.

Answer:

Interpol, short for International Criminal Police Organization, is an international organization that facilitates cooperation among law enforcement agencies around the world. It was founded in 1923 in Vienna, Austria, as the International Criminal Police Commission (ICPC). At that time, it was a small organization composed of police officials from a few European countries who worked together to combat cross-border crime.

In 1956, the ICPC was renamed Interpol and its headquarters were moved to Lyon, France. Over the years, Interpol has grown in size and scope, and today it has member agencies in nearly every country in the world. Its mission is to promote cooperation and mutual assistance among law enforcement agencies and to provide a forum for them to exchange information and ideas.

Interpol has a number of specialized units that focus on specific areas of crime, such as terrorism, cybercrime, and organized crime. It also has a number of operational tools and resources that it makes available to its member agencies, including a secure communications system and a database of criminal records and other information.

One of the unique aspects of Interpol is that it is not a law enforcement agency itself, but rather a support organization that assists law enforcement agencies in their work. It does not have the authority to investigate crimes or make arrests, but rather relies on the cooperation of member agencies to carry out these activities.

Despite its important role in international law enforcement, Interpol has faced criticism over the years for its handling of sensitive political cases and allegations of corruption and abuse of power. In recent years, the organization has implemented a number of reforms in an effort to address these concerns and improve its transparency and accountability.
