5. A solution was prepared by dissolving some acetamide, CH2CONH2, in 65.0g of pure water. the boiling point of the solution at 1 atm was 100.208°C. How much acetamide, in grams, was dissolved to yield the solution. (0.512.°C - KB of H2O). Show solution​


Given:

mass of solvent = 65.0 g = 0.0650 kg

[tex]\text{BP}_{solution} = \text{100.208°C}[/tex]

[tex]K_{\text{b}} = \text{0.512°C/molal}[/tex]

solute: acetamide (CH₂CONH₂)

solvent: water (H₂O)

Required:

mass of solute

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the boiling point elevation.

[tex]\Delta T_{\text{b}} = \text{BP}_{solution} - \text{BP}_{solvent}[/tex]

[tex]\Delta T_{\text{b}} = \text{100.208°C} - \text{100°C}[/tex]

[tex]\Delta T_{\text{b}} = \text{0.208°C}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the molality of the solution.

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\Delta T_{\text{b}}}{K_{\text{b}}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{0.208°C}}{\text{0.512°C/molal}}[/tex]

molality = 0.40625 molal

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

Note: 1 molal = 1 mol/kg

moles of solute = molality × mass of solvent

moles of solute = 0.40625 mol/kg × 0.0650 kg

moles of solute = 0.02640625 mol

Step 4: Calculate the molar mass of solute.

molar mass of solute = (12.01 g/mol × 2) + (1.008 g/mol × 4) + (16.00 g/mol × 1) + (14.01 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of solute = 58.062 g/mol

Step 5: Calculate the mass of solute.

mass of solute = moles of solute × molar mass of solute

mass of solute = 0.02640625 mol × 58.062 g/mol

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of solute = 1.53 g}}[/tex]
