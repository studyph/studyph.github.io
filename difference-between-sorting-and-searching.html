27. What sort of relationship exists between the demand for goods and the price of complementary goods?​


Answer:

Complementary goods have a negative cross- price elasticity: as the price of one good increases, the demand for the second good decreases. Substitute goods have a positive cross-price elasticity: as the price of one good increases, the demand for the other good increases.

28. What are the difference between Sorting and Assembling?pls answer it correctly ​


Sorting and assembling are two different processes with distinct objectives and outcomes.

Sorting involves separating items based on specific criteria or attributes. For example, sorting a pile of books by author, title, or genre. The goal of sorting is to organize items into groups based on common characteristics, making them easier to find or use.

Assembling, on the other hand, involves putting together individual parts to create a finished product or system. For example, assembling the components of a computer or building a piece of furniture from a kit. The goal of assembling is to create a functional or usable item by combining individual parts or components.

In summary, sorting involves organizing items based on specific criteria, while assembling involves combining individual parts to create a finished product or system.
