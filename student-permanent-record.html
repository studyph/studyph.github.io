20. Compose an advice to a grade 9 student living near mayon volcano, will you advise him/her to permanently relocate to another place? Why?​


My advice to a grade 9 student living near Mayon Volcano, I will NOT advise him/her to permanently relocate to another place. But they need to pay attention to disaster mitigation:

1. Recognize and be aware of some signs of an eruption that appears.

2. Close doors and windows.

3. Turn off electrical equipment.

4. Prepare personal emergency kit

5. Follow the instructions from the authorities.

FURTHER EXPLANATION:

Before the volcanic eruption

The student needs to recognize and be aware of some of the eruption signs that appear. Some signs of an eruption to watch out for include an increase in the temperature of the mountain slopes, dry springs, earthquake tremors, lots of animals coming down the mountain, and frequent rumbling sounds.Close doors and windows. This is done to prevent ash rain from entering the house.Turn off electrical equipment.Prepare personal emergency equipment such as flashlights, food and drinks, first aid kits, warm clothes, and others.Follow the instructions from the authorities. If there is an appeal to leverage, then do it immediately.

During a volcanic eruption

Inside the house

At the Alert status level, residents are advised to immediately evacuate to an evacuation site. Avoid dangerous areas such as mountain slopes and valleys.You also need to cover your mouth and nose with a mask. If a mask is not available, use a wet cloth as a cover.Wear closed clothing that protects the body such as long-sleeved clothes, long pants, and protective glasses. If they wear contact lenses, it would be better if they are removed first.

Outside the house

They need to protect themselves from eruptive material. It would be best if they found a safe shelter and avoided dangerous areas such as mountain slopes and valleys.If it rains ash, cover your mouth with a mask. If a mask is not available, use a wet cloth instead.

After the volcanic eruption

Return home when the authorities say that conditions are safe and back to normal.Clean the roof of volcanic ash deposits.Keep protecting yourself from falling ash. One of them is by wearing a mask or wet cloth to cover your mouth and nose.If possible, avoid areas affected by ash fall.

23. What is example of Filing of permanent record?


Answer:

Work performance and evaluation: 201 file

Explanation:

When a person is employed, his employer has to have a permanent record about of that employee.

The 201 file is a comprehensive document. It contains information such as the employee's full name, date of birth , address , contact information , government numbers , educational background, Performance Assessments, work issues, and the like.

Your 201 file is a permanent record that attaches to your professional life. Whenever a prospective employer wants to know more about you, he or she can contact your previous employer and request for your 201 file.


24. The failure to capitalize a permanent asset, or the recording of an asset acquisition at the improper amount, affects the income statement:


Failure to capitalize a fixed asset at the correct amount would impact both the income statement and the balance sheet, until the company disposes of the asset.

In business, an asset is a valuable resource that a company owns or leases to help them run their operations. They include both tangible and intangible assets such as cash, investments, equipment, and even the company's reputation.

Fixed assets are long-term tangible assets used in business operations, these are assets that the company intends to keep and use for a period of time. They are also referred to as property, plant, and equipment and are recorded on a balance sheet.
