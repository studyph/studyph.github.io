26. Calculate the work (in J) associated with the expansion of a gas from 44 mL to 63 mL at a constant pressure of 14 atm.​


If you write the general formula of work from physics it becomes:

[tex]W=\int F \cdot dx[/tex]

where F is the force applied and dx is the change in direction.

If we simplify F:

[tex]P=\frac{F}{A_c} \\F=P\cdot A_c[/tex]

where A_c is the cross-sectional area of the object and P is the pressure applied.

Substituting it back to the simple work equation:

[tex]W=\int P\cdot d(x*A_c)[/tex]

We know that if x is the distance traveled by the cross-sectional area(let's say a piston laying on the floor) and when multiplied with the cross-section becomes Volume.

[tex]W=\int^{V_f}_{V_i} P \cdot V\\W=\int^{63}_{44} 14 \ atm \cdot dV\\W=14 \ atm \cdot (63-44)mL\\W=266 \ mL-atm \cdot \frac{1 \ L}{1000 \ mL} \cdot \frac{1 \ m^3}{1000 \ L} \cdot \frac{101325 \ Pa}{1 \ atm}\\1 \ Pa-m^3=1 \ J\\W\approx 26.95 \ J[/tex]
