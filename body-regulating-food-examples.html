"5 body regulating foods"

Vitamin A - RetinolVitamin B - Beta caroteneVitamin C - Ascorbic acidVitamin DVitamin E

MORE EXPLANATION:What are regulatory foods?For the most part, regulatory foods are of vegetable origin, ie fruits, vegetables, cereals and vegetables that contribute Essential vitamins and minerals For a healthy body.

Vitamin A - Retinol?Important for the development of healthy bones and firm skin. It also helps a good vision and intervenes in the good reproductive functioning.

It is found in carrots, eggs and beef liver, Cod liver oil And fish in general.

Also in milk, butter, cheeses and all green vegetables like spinach, chard, watercress, broccoli, cabbage and asparagus .

Vitamin B - Beta carotene?Intervenes in the regulation of almost all the systems of the human body: nervous, digestive, circulatory and Immunological .

Vitamin B is generally found in animal viscera (beef, chicken, pork), in eggs and in whole grains (that is, with its shell) such as wheat, oats And rice.

Also legumes are good contributors of vitamin B: lentils, peas and soy, as well as in nuts, peanuts and other nuts. They are vegetables rich in vitamin B all green, in addition to carrot and squash.

Vitamin C - Ascorbic acid?It is the main defender of the body against viruses and diseases like the flu and the common cold and is responsible for good healing (healthy tissues) and strong bones.

It is found in citrus fruits ( Oranges , Grapefruit, tangerines, limes and lemons) also in kiwi and strawberry (strawberry) and in vegetables such as pepper and cauliflower.Vitamin D?Set the calcium In the bones and teeth. It is present in dairy, fish, seafood and all kinds of fungi.

Vitamin EIt is important in the formation of tissues, strengthening muscles and a balanced bloodstream. It is present in corn and sunflower oils and in nuts.

They act directly in the control of the functions of very important organs, so they are also known as protective foods.
