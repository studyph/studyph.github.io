Bag is dropped from a hovering helicopter. Initial velocity is equal to 0 meter per secThe bag has fallen for 2 sec.

Step 2: Determine the formula/s that will be utilized

Since this is a free fall problem, then we can utilize the following equations

final velocity=initial velocity+(gravity)(time)change in distance (or height)= (initial velocity)(time)+(1/2)(gravity)(time)²(final velocity)²=(initial velocity)²+2(gravity)(change in distance or height)

Step 3: Find the velocity of bag the instant it hits the ground

final velocity is the velocity of the bag when it hits the groundthe initial velocity is equal to 0 since it is droppedUse the equation number 1 in step 2final velocity= 0 + (9.81 m per s²)(2 s)final velocity is equal to 19.62 meter per sec

Step 4: Find the height of the helicopter

Use the equation number 2 in step 2change in distance (or height)=(0 m/s)(2 s)+(1/2)(9.81 m/s²)(2 s)²change in distance (or height) is equal to 19.62 metersThe height of the helicopter when the bag is dropped is 19.62 meters.

Step 5: Find the time before the bag (dropped at 2 meter per sec) reach the ground

Use the equation number 3 in step 2final velocity²=initial velocity²+2(height)(gravity)Assume that the height of the helicopter at this problem is the same that of the previous conditionfinal velocity²=(2 m/s)²+(2)(9.81 m/s²)(19.62 m)The final velocity of the bag dropped at 2 m/s is equal to 19.7 meter per sec.

30. A package is dropped from a helicopter flying at a height of 7.5 m while travelling 15 m/s. How far does the package travel horizontally before it hits the ground?


Answer:

18.6 m

Step by step solution:

Step 1: Calculate the time it takes the package before it hits the ground or time of falling

Using formula: [tex]y=v_{0y}t+\frac{1}{2}gt^{2}[/tex]

[tex]v_{0y}=0[/tex] since there is no initial velocity of the package given

g=9.81 m/s^2 (gravitational constant)

[tex]7.5=0(t)+\frac{1}{2}(9.81)t^{2}[/tex]

[tex]7.5=\frac{1}{2}(9.81)t^{2}[/tex]

get the value of t

t= 1.24 s (fall time)

Step 2: Calculate the horizontal distance it travels:

Use formula: [tex]d=v_{0x}t[/tex]       (there is no gravity or acceleration that acts horizontally)

where [tex]v_{0x}=15 m/s[/tex] (horizontal velocity)

[tex]d=v_{0x}t[/tex]

[tex]d=(15 m/s)(1.24s)[/tex]

[tex]d=18.6 m[/tex]
