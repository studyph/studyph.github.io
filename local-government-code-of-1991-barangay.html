2. local government code 1991


Answer:

Local Government Code of 1991 is also known as Republic Act No. 7160.

Explanation:

What is Local Government Code or Republic Act No. 7160?

According to the law, this Code establishes the system and defines powers of provincial, city, municipal and barangay governments in the Philippines. It provides for a more responsive local government structure instituted through a system of decentralization whereby Local Government Units are delegated more powers, authority, responsibilities and resources.

The Father of Local Government Code

Our former Senate President Aquilino “Nene” Pimentel, Jr is the father of Local Government Code.

Levels of Local Government

Autonomous Regions

Autonomous regions have more powers than other local governments. It is is governed by the regional governor and a legislature.

Provinces

Provinces are organized into component cities and municipalities. A province is governed by the governor and a legislature known as the Sangguniang Panlalawigan.

Cities and municipalities

Cities and municipalities are governed by mayors and legislatures, known as Sangguniang Panlungsod in cities and the Sangguniang Bayan in municipalities

Barangays

This is the smallest of the Local Government Unit. A barangay's executive is the Punong Barangay or barangay captain and its legislature is the Sangguniang Barangay, composed of barangay captain, the Barangay Kagawads and the SK Chairman.

Responsibilities

Local government should provide the following:

facilities and research services for agriculture and fishery activities

health services

social welfare services

job placement information systems and a public library

a solid waste disposal system

municipal/city/provincial buildings, cultural centers, public parks, playgrounds, and sports facilities and equipment

public markets, slaughterhouses, and other local enterprises

public cemetery

tourism facilities and other tourist attractions

sites for police and fire stations and substations and municipal jail

3. Local government code of 1991


Answer:

This was Republic Act (No. 7160) originally introduced by Loren Legarda and enacted by the Congress in 1991 which gave authority and responsibility to Local Government Units or LGU.

Explanation:

What is the importance of having Local Government Units or LGUs?

The Philippines is an archipelago with the seat of power at its main island, Luzon, with the capital being at Manila. While the Philippines is a small country, it is made up 7,100++ islands with 81 provinces that are further divided into cities and municipalities.

Travelling and communication between all 81 provinces was no joke back then. If you can remember, low-cost air and sea travel and instant messaging only became a reality towards the mid-2000s.

To enable quick response to public health concerns and calamities, it would be best to have government-apppointed units at localities across the nation. They will be given a budget, authority, and the responsibility to oversee the needs of the local and to respond immediately without having to wait for a directive from the national government authorities.

To put this in perspective, let us use a recent event as an example. Let's say that we do not have any local government units and we are in the midst of the COVID-19 outbreak. Between 81 provinces and more than 41,000+ baranggays in the country, the President would have a hard time determining which places should be placed uner quarantine, which places need relief goods the most, and which areas lack medical facilities adn personnel.

With local government units at three different levels, the job is made easier because of delegation of work and authority. They no longer have to wait for the President to approve of suspension and quarantine, they no longer have to personally ask the President for funds to distribute food, or involve the President in other decision-making processes.
