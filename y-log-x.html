10. EXPAND THE LANS OF COGARITHM USING 3-) log₁ x ³√y​


Answer:

The logarithm of a product is the sum of the logarithms of the individual factors. Therefore,

log₁ x³√y = log₁ x + log₁ ∛y

This is known as the logarithm rule for products.

The logarithm of a quotient is the difference of the logarithms of the numerator and denominator. Therefore,

log₁ x/y = log₁ x - log₁ y

This is known as the logarithm rule for quotients.

The logarithm of a power is the product of the logarithm of the base and the exponent. Therefore,

log₁ x^n = nlog₁ x

This is known as the logarithm rule for powers.

It's worth noting that the logarithm base in the given example is 1, which is not a common base. Logarithms base 1 are known as the "natural logarithms" or the "Napierian logarithms" and the notation for them is typically ln(x) instead of log1(x).
