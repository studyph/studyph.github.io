9. A gas occupies 32.1 mL at 22 degrees Celcius and 1.5 atm. Find the new volume of this gas at 75 degrees Celcius and 5 atm.


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P_1 = \text{1.5 atm} \\ & V_1 = \text{32.1 mL} \\ & T_1 = 22^{\circ}\text{C} + 273 = \text{295 K} \\ & P_2 = \text{5 atm} \\ & T_2 = 75^{\circ}\text{C} + 273 = \text{348 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final volume using combined gas law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \frac{P_1V_1}{T_1} & = \frac{P_2V_2}{T_2} \\ V_2P_2T_1 & = V_1P_1T_2 \\ \frac{V_2P_2T_1}{P_2T_1} & = \frac{V_1P_1T_2}{P_2T_1} \\ V_2 & = \frac{V_1P_1T_2}{P_2T_1} \\ & = \frac{(\text{32.1 mL})(\text{1.5 atm})(\text{348 K})}{(\text{5 atm})(\text{295 K})} \\ & = \boxed{\text{11.4 mL}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the new volume of the gas at 75°C and 5 atm is 11.4 mL.

22. How To Write The Verbal Translation Of The Formula Of Converting Temperature From: Celcius To Farenheit From Farenheit To Celcius


Celsius to Farenheit:

° F = (9/5) ° C + 32

Verbal Translation:  Degree Farenheit is Nine/fifths of degree Celsius increased by thirty-two.

Farenheit to Celsius:

° C = (° F - 32) x 5/9

Verbal Translation:  Degree Celsius is the product of thirty-two less than degree Farenheit and five/ninths.
