15. Why does genetic drift have more of an effect on small population? ​


Answer:

Genetic drift is the reason why we worry about African cheetahs and other species that exist in small populations. Drift is more pronounced in such populations, because smaller populations have less variation and, therefore, a lower ability to respond favorably — that is, adapt — to changing conditions.

Answer:

A bottleneck can have a significant impact on the flow of manufacturing, and can sharply increase the time and expense of production. Companies are more at risk for bottlenecks when they start the production process for a new product because there may be flaws in the process that must be identified and corrected; this situation requires more scrutiny and fine-tuning.

21. how a population bottleneck can alter the genetic variation in the gene pool of a species.


A population bottleneck (or genetic bottleneck) is a sharp reduction in the size of a population due to environmental events (such as earthquakes, floods, fires, disease, or droughts) or human activities (such as genocide). Such events can reduce the variation in the gene pool of a population; thereafter, a smaller population, with a correspondingly smaller genetic diversity, remains to pass on genes to future generations of offspring through sexual reproduction. Genetic diversity remains lower, only slowly increasing with time as random mutations occur.

A population bottleneck is an event resulting to a drastic decrease in the genetic pool of a species. A population with lesser diversity has a more a chance of being extinct and has a lesser chance to evolve. When there is less genetic diversity, a given population would be vulnerable to environmental changes and diseases.

Let us take for example, a certain disease is being exposed to a population with a low genetic variation. A selection would occur for resistance to the disease. If a resistant genetic variant is not present, the entire population could be wiped out.

Another example would be in response to environmental conditions. If a resistant genetic variant is not present to adapt and survive to an extreme environmental condition, a species could be extinct.

Explanation:

Genetic engineering is a powerful technology that allows scientists to manipulate genes in living organisms, with the goal of enhancing or modifying their genetic makeup. While this technology has the potential to bring many benefits, there are also some potential risks associated with genetic engineering:

1. Negative impacts on biodiversity: Introducing genetically modified organisms (GMOs) into ecosystems can lead to unintended environmental consequences, such as disruptions to food webs, reduced biodiversity, and the spread of modified traits to other species.

2. Health risks: There is concern that consuming genetically modified foods or products could have long-term health effects, such as increased allergy risks or impacts on the gut microbiome.

3. Ethical concerns: There are also ethical concerns around the idea of “playing God” with the genetic makeup of organisms, and the potential consequences of manipulating the building blocks of life.

4. Development of antibiotic resistance: There is a risk that the use of genetic engineering to introduce antibiotic resistance genes into GMOs could lead to the development of drug-resistant bacteria, which could be dangerous for human and animal health.

It is important that genetic engineering is carefully regulated and monitored to ensure that the benefits outweigh the risks, and to prevent any negative impacts from this technology.
