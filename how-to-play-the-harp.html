To play a "doodle harp sketch," you will need a few materials: a sheet of paper, a pencil or pen, and something to use as a pick (such as a toothpick or small piece of plastic).

Here are the steps to play a doodle harp sketch:

1) Take your sheet of paper and draw a series of horizontal lines across it. The lines should be evenly spaced and close together.

2) Next, draw a series of vertical lines across the paper, intersecting the horizontal lines.

3) Now you should have a grid of squares on your paper. Use your pencil or pen to shade in some of the squares. You can shade them in randomly or create a pattern.

4) Take your pick and gently run it over the shaded squares on the paper. The pick should glide over the shaded areas and create a sound similar to a harp or guitar.

5) Experiment with different patterns and shading techniques to create different sounds and rhythms. You can also try using different materials for your pick to see how they affect the sound.

Overall, playing a doodle harp sketch is a fun and creative way to make music using only a few simple materials.
