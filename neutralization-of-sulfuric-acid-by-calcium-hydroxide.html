The molarity of the NaOH solution : Mb = 2.784 M

Further explanation  

Titration is a procedure for determining the concentration of a solution (analyte) by reacting with another solution whose known concentration (usually a standard solution) is called the titrant. Determination of the endpoint /equivalence point of the reaction can use indicators according to the appropriate pH range  

Titrations can be acid-base titration, depositional titration, and redox titration. An acid-base titration is the principle of neutralization of acids and bases  

Acid-base titration formula  

Ma Va. na = Mb. Vb. nb  

Ma, Mb = acid base concentration  

Va, Vb = acid base volume  

na, nb = acid base valence  

Given

Va = acid = 11.6 ml

Ma = 3 M

Vb = base = 25 ml

Required

Mb = the concentration of NaOH solution  

Solution

na = 2 (valence 2 because it releases 2 H⁺) ⇒H₂SO₄⇒2H⁺+SO₄²⁻

nb = 1 (valence 1 because it releases 1 OH⁻) ⇒NaOH⇒Na⁺+OH⁻

Input the value :

3 M x 11.6 ml x 2 = Mb x 25 ml x 1

Mb = 2.784 M

9. 1.Titration reveals that 11.6 mL of 3.0 M sulfuric acid are required to neutralize the sodium hydroxide in 25.00 mL of NaOH solution. What is the molarity of the NaOH solution?​


Note: The acid is sulfuric acid and the base is sodium hydroxide. Sulfuric acid has two replaceable hydrogen ions and sodium hydroxide has one replaceable hydroxide ion.

Given:

[tex]M_{\text{acid}} = \text{3.0 M}[/tex]

[tex]V_{\text{acid}} = \text{11.6 mL}[/tex]

[tex]f_{\text{acid}} = 2[/tex]

[tex]V_{\text{base}} = \text{25.00 mL}[/tex]

[tex]f_{\text{base}} = 1[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]M_{\text{base}}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]f_{\text{acid}}M_{\text{acid}}V_{\text{acid}} = f_{\text{base}}M_{\text{base}}V_{\text{base}}[/tex]

[tex]M_{\text{base}} = M_{\text{acid}} × \frac{V_{\text{acid}}}{V_{\text{base}}} × \frac{f_{\text{acid}}}{f_{\text{base}}}[/tex]

[tex]M_{\text{base}} = \text{3.0 M} × \frac{\text{11.6 mL}}{\text{25.00 mL}} × \frac{2}{1}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{M_{\text{base}} = \text{2.8 M}}[/tex]

13. Sulfuric acid reacts with aluminum hydroxide by double replacement. If 30.0 g of sulfuric acid reacts with 25.0 g of aluminum hydroxide, identify the limiting reactant.


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Write the balanced chemical equation.

3H₂SO₄ + 2Al(OH)₃ → Al₂(SO₄)₃ + 6H₂O

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of Al₂(SO₄)₃ formed by each reactant.

• Using H₂SO₄

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 3 moles of H₂SO₄ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mole of Al₂(SO₄)₃.

The molar mass of H₂SO₄ is 98.072 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of} \: \text{Al}_2(\text{SO}_4)_3 & = \text{30.0 g} \: \text{H}_2\text{SO}_4 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{H}_2\text{SO}_4}{\text{98.072 g} \: \text{H}_2\text{SO}_4} \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Al}_2(\text{SO}_4)_3}{\text{3 mol} \: \text{H}_2\text{SO}_4} \\ & = \text{0.102 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

• Using Al(OH)₃

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 2 moles of Al(OH)₃ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mole of Al₂(SO₄)₃.

The molar mass of Al(OH)₃ is 78.003 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of} \: \text{Al}_2(\text{SO}_4)_3 & = \text{25.0 g} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3}{\text{78.003 g} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3} \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Al}_2(\text{SO}_4)_3}{\text{2 mol} \: \text{Al(OH)}_3} \\ & = \text{0.160 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Determine the limiting reactant.

Since H₂SO₄ produced less amount of Al₂(SO₄)₃ than Al(OH)₃

[tex]\boxed{\text{H}_2\text{SO}_4 \: \text{is the limiting reactant.}}[/tex]

16. what volume (mL) of 0.70 M sodium hydroxide (NaOH) is needed to neutralize 270 mL of 0.40 M sulfuric acid (H2SO4)?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of H₂SO₄ by using dimensional analysis.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of} \: \text{H}_2\text{SO}_4 & = \frac{\text{0.40 mol} \: \text{H}_2\text{SO}_4}{1000 \: \cancel{\text{mL soln}}} \times 270 \: \cancel{\text{mL soln}} \\ & = \text{0.108 mol} \: \text{H}_2\text{SO}_4 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of NaOH by using dimensional analysis.

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 2 mol NaOH is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mol H₂SO₄.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of NaOH} & = 0.108 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{H}_2\text{SO}_4} \times \frac{\text{2 mol NaOH}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{H}_2\text{SO}_4}} \\ & = \text{0.216 mol NaOH} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Final Step: Calculate the volume of NaOH solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{volume of NaOH solution} & = 0.216 \: \cancel{\text{mol NaOH}} \times \frac{1 \: \cancel{\text{L}}}{0.70 \: \cancel{\text{mol NaOH}}} \times \frac{\text{1000 mL}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{L}}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{309 mL}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Therefore, the volume of NaOH solution is 309 mL.
