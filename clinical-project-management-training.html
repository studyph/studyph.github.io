24. Cite situations from least up to needed areas in clinical laboratory that organizing and staffing is one of the key areas of laboratory management. Specify and elaborate your answer.​


KEY AREAS OF LABORATORY MANAGEMENT

ORGANIZING

          Since a lab manager oversees several projects, maintains the finances and timetables for each, and stays up to date with relevant field research, organization is an essential part of the work.

Example situations:Working in a Clinical Laboratory can be messy and chaotic. To avoid accidents, the place should be organized. Which means there must be no spills or there is a readily available toolkit such as toilet paper or garbage bags. It is easier for Clinicians to work if they know where the tools or substances are instead of wasting time finding it. Organizing in Clinical laboratory is for avoiding accidents that might happen. Accidents are inevitable when working in the lab and organization minimizes those chances. Messy laboratories can make someone trip on something or hurt himself.Organizing helps to prevent contamination. When contaminants are unintentionally introduced into or onto an intermediate or beginning cell culture while sampling, holding, processing, storing, transferring, packing, and transportation, contamination occurs.

STAFFING

          The managers must find, develop, and retain competent employees as well as foster a culture of cooperation, dedication, and competence. Since good employees are difficult to discover and even more difficult to replace once they are hired, it is crucial to express gratitude for their efforts once they are on board. The development of a culture of open communication, cooperation, trust, and quality, which enables the continuing implementation of changes required for the survival of the laboratory, can only be accomplished via the use of management skills.

Example Situations:Proper work schedules and endorsements. Laboratories follow-up schedules and can easily relay a task.Staffing avoids overworking.Staffing avoids people from taking two or more responsibilities that are not in their description.
