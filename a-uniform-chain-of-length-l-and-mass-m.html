14. 1. A 3 m uniform rod with a mass of 1.5 kg acts as a physical pendulum. Calculate on what is the inertia of the rod about its axis of rotation and the period of the pendulum.​


Answer:
4.5 kg m^2
3.54 s.

Explanation:
I = (1/3) * m * L^2
I = (1/3) * 1.5 kg * (3 m)^2 = 4.5 kg m^2

T = 2 * pi * sqrt(I/m * g)

T = 2 * pi * sqrt(4.5 kg m^2 / 1.5 kg * 9.8 m/s^2)

= 2 * pi * sqrt(3)

= 3.54 s


15. A torque of 12 n • m is applied to a solid, uniform disk of radius 0.50 m, causing the disk to accelerate at 5.7 rad/s2. what is the mass of the disk?


Answer:

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The mass of the disk has approximately measure of 16.84 kg

Explanation:

To find the mass of the disk, we have to use the formula below:

[tex]\boxed{\bold{\tau = \frac{1}{2}mr^2 \cdot x}}[/tex]

Whereas,

[tex]\tau[/tex] - is the torque[tex]m[/tex] - mass of the object[tex]r[/tex] - is the radius of the object[tex]x[/tex] - radians of the object

Since we are solving for mass or [tex]m[/tex], we can derive the formula by solving for [tex]m[/tex].

[tex]\rm{\therefore \tau = \frac{1}{2}mr^2 \cdot x} \\\\ \rm{\frac{1}{2}mr^2x=\tau} \\\\ \rm{mr^2x=2\tau} \\\\ \rm{\frac{mr^2x}{r^2x}=\frac{2\tau}{r^2x}} \\\\ \therefore \boxed{\bold{m=\frac{2\tau}{r^2x}}}[/tex]

Substitute the following given to the formula.

[tex]\rm{m = \frac{2(12)}{(0.50)^2(5.7)}} \\\\ \rm{m=\frac{24}{1.425}} \\\\ \rm{m\approx 16.84 \ kg}[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The mass of the disk has approximately measure of 16.84 kg

22. In the center of mass, the point where mass distribution is not uniform in all direction. Do you agree why or why not?


Answer:

I disagree. The center of mass is a point where the mass distribution is uniform in all directions. It is a point where the net force from the mass distribution is zero. This means that the distribution of mass around the center of mass is symmetrical and balanced in all directions. When the mass distribution is not uniform, the center of mass will still be a single point at which the net force is zero, but it will not be the geometric center of the object.
