2. Suppose the mean gmat score is 550 with a standard deviation of 100. hayden takes the gmat and is told his z-score is 2.5. interpret the meaning of a z-score in this context.


Z-SCORE INTERPRETATION

If Hayden took the GMAT and was told that his z-score was 2.5 when the mean GMAT score is 550 and the standard deviation is 100, then he got 800 points on the test.

How did we know?

We can identify how much Hayden scored using the formula of retrieving the z-score: [tex]Z = \frac{x - \mu}{\sigma}[/tex].

Where:

Z is the z-score

x is the raw value

[tex]\mu[/tex] is the mean

[tex]\sigma[/tex] is the standard deviation

Finding the value of x:

x = Z[tex]\sigma[/tex] - [tex]\mu[/tex] = (2.5) (100) + 550 = 800

This only means that Hayden belongs to the 99th percentile. It means that he did a better job than 99% of the GMAT exam takers.
