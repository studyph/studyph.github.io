To solve this problem, we have to identify first the given numbers which are 50 cm (Venia's castle) and 24 cm (Paulus' castle).

Next, we have to determine which mathematical operation to use. Since the problem asks us to find the difference, we therefore have to subtract.

We now have the mathematical sentence: 50-24 = ?

We take away 24 from 50, we get 26.

Therefore, the difference of the height of the two castles is 26 cm.

30. Plate #4: A catapult launches a rock at a castle wall. The rock is launched at a 45 degree angle with a speed of 22 m/s. The castle wall is 45 m away and is 20 m high. Does the rock make it over the wall?​


No.

an initial velocity of 22m/s at 45 deg. will only have a velocity in Y direction of sin(45)22 or 15.55m/s.

the time when the projectile reached a range of 45m, is equal to 45÷cos(45)22 or 2.89 seconds. by this, we can calculate the current height of the projectile at a given time, which the time, in this case is 2.89 seconds. using the formula s=V_x0*t+0.5*a*t^2 where V_x0 is initial velocity at X direction, t is time, a is gravity, which is -9.8m/s².

plug in the numbers

s=cos(45)22*2.89+0.5*-9.8*2.89^2

s = 3.99m

Conclusion:

the rock did not make it over the wall. at a time when the rock is at a range of exactly 45m, the current height of the rock is at 3.99m. the wall is 20m height.
