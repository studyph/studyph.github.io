HAMILTON & JEFFERSON METHOD

The initial steps of Jefferson's approach are identical to those of Hamilton's. He uses the same divisor and quota and removes the decimal places, in the same manner, resulting in fewer representatives than required. The difference lies in how Jefferson approaches this issue.

The Hamilton/Vinton Method uses the proportion of the total population per house seat as the divisor. After dividing the population of each state by the divisor, the complete quotient is maintained, and the fraction is dropped.

Divide the population of each state by the new divisor to determine its modified quota. Each adjusted quota is rounded down to its lowest quota. Next, determine the sum of the reduced quotas. If the amount equals the number of seats to be divided, you are finished.
