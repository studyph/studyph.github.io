23. find the area of the given figure 6cm, 3cm, 3cm, 3cm, 3cm​


Answer:

The area of the given trapezoid is 13.5 square centimeters.

Solution:
To find the area of this figure, we need to determine its shape. Based on the given dimensions, it appears to be a trapezoid with a height of 3 cm, a longer base of 6 cm, and two shorter bases of 3 cm each.

The formula for the area of a trapezoid is:

A = (b1 + b2)h / 2

where b1 and b2 are the lengths of the parallel bases and h is the height.

Substituting the given values, we get:

A = (6 + 3) × 3 / 2

A = 9 × 3 / 2

A = 13.5

Therefore, the area of the given trapezoid is 13.5 square centimeters.

30. m-1000gL-5cmw-3cmt-2cmD?check theMassVolume ​


Answer:

Given,

m = 1000 g (mass)

L = 5 cm (length)

w = 3 cm (width)

t = 2 cm (thickness)

The volume of a rectangular object can be calculated as follows:

V = L x w x t

So, the volume of the rectangular object is:

V = 5 cm x 3 cm x 2 cm = 30 cm^3 = 30,000 mm^3

The density (D) of an object can be calculated as follows:

D = m / V

So, the density of the rectangular object is:

D = 1000 g / 30,000 mm^3 = 1000 / 30 g/mm^3 = 33.33 g/mm^3
