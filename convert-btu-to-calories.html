3. A seven tons are lifted 12 ft. Find the energy required in Btu to lift the 7 tons. a. 210 Btu b. 217 Btu C. 216 Btu d. 200 Btu ​


The amount of energy required in BTU to lift 7 tons at a distance of 12 feet is 216 BTU.

What we are going to do here is to convert the work done in Joules into BTU (British Thermal Unit). For us to get Joules, we have to convert the units of the given. Since N x m = J, we have to get the values in meters and Newton.

Converting 12 ft to meters:

Note: In 1 foot there are 0.3048 meters.

12 ft x [tex]\frac{0.3048 m}{1 ft}[/tex] = 3.6576 meters

Converting tons to Newton:

First, let us convert tons to kilograms.

Note: There are 907.185 kilograms in one ton.

7 tons x [tex]\frac{1000 kg}{1 ton}[/tex] = 6350.3 kg

Then, we have to convert kilograms into Newton. The simplest way to do this is to multiply it by the constant value of acceleration due to gravity: 9.81 m/[tex]s^{2}[/tex] because 1kg= 9.81 N

6350.3 kg x 9.81 m/[tex]s^{2}[/tex] = 62,296.39 kg-m/[tex]s^{2}[/tex]  = 62,296.39 N

Now, let us apply the work formula: W= Fd

W= 62,296.39 N (3.6576 meters)= 227,855.29 J

Finally, let's convert Joules into BTU.

1 J=0.000948 BTU

227,855.29 J x [tex]\frac{0.000948 BTU}{1J}[/tex] = 216 BTU

18. If the producer has 1000 calories, how much calories will the secondary consumer get? A. 100 calories B. 10 calories C. 1 calorie D. 0.1 calorie


Answer:

B. 10

Explanation:

The percentage of energy transferred from one trophic level to the next varies between 5% and 20% and

is called the ecological efficiency (efficiency of energy transfer). An average figure of 10% is often used.

The ten percent law states that the total energy content of a trophic level in an ecosystem is only about

one-tenth (or 10%) that of the preceding level

EXAMPLE: if the grasses produce 1000 kcal of energy, how many kcal are theoretically available to the frogs?

When energy is passed in an ecosystem from one trophic level to the next, only ten percent (10%) of the energy will be passed on. We can consider a typical food chain in the environment. It can be written as follows. Sun -----> Green Plants -----> Insects -----> Frogs -----> Snakes. The sun provides the energy that is used by the green plants. The green plants are the primary producers for the food chain. The energy from the sun is converted by the plants into food. The insects are the primary consumers. They are the first animals to eat the plants. When they eat the plants and obtain some of the energy (10%) they stored when making food. The rest of the energy is lost and dissipated as heat. Insects are eaten by frogs. The frogs are the secondary consumers in this food chain. They obtain part of the energy stored by the insects. The remaining energy stored by the insects is lost

25. Calculate the kinetic energy in joules of a 1200-kg automobile moving at 18 m/s. Convert the value into calories.​


Answer:

KE = 194400 J

KE = 46462.715 cal

Explanation:

Solve for the kinetic energy.

KE = ½mv²

KE = ½(1200 kg)(18 m/s)²

KE = 194400 J

Convert J to cal

KE = 194400 J × (1 cal / 4.184 J)

KE = 46462.715 cal
