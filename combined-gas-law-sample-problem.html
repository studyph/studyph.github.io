A sample of gas occupies a volume of 10 ml at 60°c and 567 mmHg. What volume would this gas occupy at STP?

Formula: P1V1/T1 = P2V2/T2

Given:

P1 = 567 mmHg

V1 = 10 ml

T1 = 60°c (333 k)

P2 = 760 mmHg

V2 = ?

T2 = 273 k

solution:

(273 k)(567 mmHg)(10 ml) / (333 k)(760 mmHg)

V2 = 6.116 ml


2. Give me 5 sample problem with solution of combined gas law​


Answer:

Sure, here are 5 sample problems with solutions for the combined gas law:

A balloon is filled with 1.00 L of helium at 1.00 atm and 20.0°C. If the balloon is released and rises to an altitude where the pressure is 0.750 atm and the temperature is -10.0°C, what is the new volume of the balloon?

Solution:

P1V1/T1 = P2V2/T2

(1.00 atm)(1.00 L)/(293 K) = (0.750 atm)(V2)/(263 K)

V2 = (1.00 atm)(1.00 L)/(293 K) × (263 K)/(0.750 atm)

V2 = 0.923 L

A scuba diver has a tank containing 2.50 L of air at 25.0°C and 1.20 atm. If the diver descends to a depth where the pressure is 3.50 atm and the temperature is 10.0°C, what is the new volume of the air in the tank?

Solution:

P1V1/T1 = P2V2/T2

(1.20 atm)(2.50 L)/(298 K) = (3.50 atm)(V2)/(283 K)

V2 = (1.20 atm)(2.50 L)/(298 K) × (283 K)/(3.50 atm)

V2 = 1.72 L

A gas sample has a volume of 1.50 L at 25.0°C and 1.00 atm. If the pressure is increased to 2.00 atm while keeping the temperature constant, what is the new volume of the gas?

Solution:

P1V1 = P2V2

(1.00 atm)(1.50 L) = (2.00 atm)(V2)

V2 = (1.00 atm)(1.50 L)/(2.00 atm)

V2 = 0.75 L

A gas sample has a volume of 2.00 L at 1.00 atm and -20.0°C. If the temperature is increased to 50.0°C while keeping the pressure constant, what is the new volume of the gas?

Solution:

V1/T1 = V2/T2

(2.00 L)/(253 K) = (V2)/(323 K)

V2 = (2.00 L)/(253 K) × (323 K)

V2 = 2.57 L

A gas sample has a volume of 5.00 L at 2.00 atm and 300 K. If the pressure is increased to 4.00 atm and the temperature is decreased to 250 K, what is the new volume of the gas?

Solution:

P1V1/T1 = P2V2/T2

(2.00 atm)(5.00 L)/(300 K) = (4.00 atm)(V2)/(250 K)

V2 = (2.00 atm)(5.00 L)/(300 K) × (250 K)/(4.00 atm)

V2 = 2.08 L

If the volume is expanded to 4.00L and the pressure reduced to 0.500 kPa, what must the temperature become? 2. Problem 1:Given:

[tex]P_{1} = \text{0.750 kPa}[/tex]

[tex]T_{1} = \text{25°C + 273 = 298 K}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{2.00 L}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{0.500 kPa}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{4.00 L}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]T_{2}[/tex]

Equation:

[tex]\frac{P_{1}V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \frac{P_{2}V_{2}}{T_{2}}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\frac{P_{1}V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \frac{P_{2}V_{2}}{T_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = T_{1} × \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} × \frac{V_{2}}{V_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \text{298 K} × \frac{\text{0.500 kPa}}{\text{0.750 kPa}} × \frac{\text{4.00 L}}{\text{2.00 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{T_{2} = \text{397 K}}[/tex]

Answer:

[tex]T_{2} = \text{397 K}[/tex]

Problem 2:Given:

[tex]P_{1} = \text{0.250 atm}[/tex]

[tex]T_{1} = \text{400.15 K}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{300.0 L}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{2.00 atm}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \text{200.15 K}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]V_{2}[/tex]

Equation:

[tex]\frac{P_{1}V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \frac{P_{2}V_{2}}{T_{2}}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\frac{P_{1}V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \frac{P_{2}V_{2}}{T_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = V_{1} × \frac{P_{1}}{P_{2}} × \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{300.0 L} × \frac{\text{0.250 atm}}{\text{2.00 atm}} × \frac{\text{200.15 K}}{\text{400.15 K}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{V_{2} = \text{18.76 L}}[/tex]

Answer:

[tex]V_{2} = \text{18.76 L}[/tex]
