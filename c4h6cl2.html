2. What is the molecular formula of a compound containing24.27% carbon, 4.07% hydrogen and 71.65% chlorine bymass? The molecular mass of the compound is 98.96amu.A. CH2CIC. C2H4Cl2B. CH2Cl2D. C2H4Cl​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Assume that the mass of a compound is 100 g.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & mass_{\text{C}} = \text{24.27 g} \\ & mass_{\text{H}} = \text{4.07 g} \\ & mass_{\text{Cl}} = \text{71.65 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of each element.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & n_{\text{C}} = \text{24.27 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{12.01 g}} = \text{2.021 mol} \\ & n_{\text{H}} = \text{4.07 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{1.008 g}} = \text{4.04 mol} \\ & n_{\text{Cl}} = \text{71.65 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{35.45 g}} = \text{2.0212 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Represent an empirical formula.

[tex]\text{empirical formula} = \text{C}_x\text{H}_y\text{Cl}_z[/tex]

Step 4: Divide the moles of each element by the least number of moles.

In this case, the least number of moles is 2.021 mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & x = \frac{\text{2.021 mol}}{\text{2.021 mol}} = 1 \\ & y = \frac{\text{4.04 mol}}{\text{2.021 mol}} \approx 2 \\ & z = \frac{\text{2.0212 mol}}{\text{2.021 mol}} \approx 1 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 5: Write the empirical formula.

[tex]\text{empirical formula} = \text{CH}_2\text{Cl}[/tex]

Step 6: Represent a molecular formula.

[tex]\text{molecular formula} = (\text{CH}_2\text{Cl})_n[/tex]

Step 7: Calculate the empirical mass.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{empirical mass} & = \text{12.01 amu + 2(1.008 amu) + 35.45 amu} \\ & = \text{49.48 amu} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 8: Divide the molecular mass by the empirical mass to find the value of n.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n & = \frac{\text{molecular mass}}{\text{empirical mass}} \\ & = \frac{\text{98.96 amu}}{\text{49.48 amu}} \\ & = 2 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 9: Multiply the subscripts by the value of n to obtain the molecular formula.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molecular formula} & = (\text{CH}_2\text{Cl})_2 \\ & = \boxed{\text{C}_2\text{H}_4\text{Cl}_2} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the answer is C. C₂H₄Cl₂.

21. The mole fraction of iodine, I2, dissolved in dichloromethane, CH2Cl2, is 0.115. What is the molal concentration, m, of iodine in this solution?​


Answer:

1.53 m

Explanation:

Molecular weights

I2 = 253.8 g/mol

CH2Cl2 = 84.93 g/mol

In this case, I2 is the solute and CH2Cl2 is the solvent. Since molality is defined as the number of moles of solute in a kilogram of solvent, we will assume that the mass of the solvent, which is CH2Cl2, is 1.00 kg or 1.000 × 10³ g.

For the number of moles of 1.000 × 10³ g CH2Cl2

n CH2Cl2 = 1.000 × 10³ g / 84.93 g/mol

n CH2Cl2 = 11.774 mol

For the number of moles of I2

X I2 = n I2 / (n I2 + n CH2Cl2)

0.115 = n I2 / (n I2 + 11.774 mol)

0.115n I2 + 1.354 mol = n I2

0.885n I2 = 1.354 mol

n I2 = 1.53 mol

For molality

molality = mol solute / kg solvent

molality = 1.53 mol / 1.000 kg

molality = 1.53 mol/kg

molality = 1.53 m
