14. Understanding the James - Lange Theory on Emotions. What is the correct sequence? A. Physiological changes, feel emotion, interpretation of emotion, observable behavior. B. Physiological changes, interpretation of physiological change, feel emotion, observable behavior. C. Feel emotion, physiological changes, interpretation of physiological change, observable behavior. D. Brain interpretation, physiological change, observable way. E. Interpretation of stimuli, brain interpretation, physiological changes, observable behavior. Discuss your answer, and then submit.


Answer:

B. Physiological changes, interpretation of physiological change, feel emotion, observable behavior.

Explanation:

The James-Lange Theory of Emotions states that events around us cause physiological changes or arousal. We then interpret these physiological changes or arousal and based on our interpretation, we feel the emotions associated with the interpretation. We then act in response to the felt emotion. These actions are the observable behaviors following the emotion. To illustrate the James-Lange Theory of Emotions, consider the following:

You see a man in a dark alley (stimulus)Your heart then suddenly beat faster and you feel sweaty (physiologic response)Your brain tried to interpret these emotions (interpretation)You feel fear based on the interpretation made by the brain (emotion)You turned around and ran back home (behavior)

26. what is physiological​


Answer:

It refers to the characteristic of or appropriate to an organism's healthy or normal functioning.

Explanation:

Answer:

Physiology is the science of life. It is the branch of biology that aims to understand the mechanisms of living things, from the basis of cell function at the ionic and molecular level to the integrated behaviour of the whole body and the influence of the external environment. Research in physiology helps us to understand how the body works in health and how it responds and adapts to the challenges of everyday life; it also helps us to determine what goes wrong in disease, facilitating the development of new treatments and guidelines for maintaining human and animal health. The emphasis on integrating molecular, cellular, systems and whole body function is what distinguishes physiology from the other life sciences.

29. 5 resfiratory disorder- define each disorder5 circulatory disorder- define each disorder ​


Answer:

5 Respiratory Disorders

1. Asthma

–evident blockage and inflamation of the airways

–recurring disease caused by irritants found in the environment.

2. Pneumonia

—lung infection caused by either Streptococcus pneumoniae or Mycoplasma pneumoniae.

—alveoli are filled with pus and fluid, which makes breathing painful and limits oxygen intake.

3. Pulmonary Fibrosis

—lung condition that causes lung scarring and stiffness.

4. Lung Cancer

—cancer that starts in the lungs.

—cells in the lungs divide uncontrollably, causing tumors to grow.

5. Emphysema

—lung condition in which the air sacs (alveoli) may be destroyed or either narrowed.

5 Circulatory Disorders

1. Myocardial Infarction (Heart Attack)

—block blood flow in the heart, resulting in starvation in oxygen supply.

2. Hypertension (High Blood Pressure)

—increase in volume of the blood in the body.

3. Cardiomyopathy

—disease of the heart muscle in which the heart loses its ability to pump blood effectively.

4. High cholesterol

—taken in too many fatty substances called cholesterol in your blood.

—can lead to stroke and heart disease.

5. Vasculitis

—inflammation of your blood vessels caused by an overactive immune system.
