5. Stores bile that is produced by the liver​


Stores bile that is produced by the liver​.

The gallbladder is a pear-shaped reservoir that absorbs and stores bile produced by the liver. It is placed directly under the liver. To aid in food digestion, the gallbladder sends this stored bile into the small intestine.

The gallbladder sits just beneath the liver. This pear-shaped sack has a thin wall and measures 7 to 10 centimeters (2.7 to 3.9 inches) long and up to 5 centimeters (2 inches) wide at its widest point.(bile )

Bile from the liver is stored and concentrated in the gallbladder. The bile is subsequently discharged into the duodenum, the first region of the small intestine, where it aids in the breakdown and absorption of lipids from diet.

3.How long can a liver store a supply of vitamin B12?

The liver stores vitamins and minerals for the times when they may be lacking in the diet. It can store enough vitamin A and vitamin B12 for four years, and enough vitamin D for four months.
