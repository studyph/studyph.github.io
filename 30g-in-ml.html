1. Calculate the molality of 30g of C6H8O7 (Citric acid) dissolved in 170 ml of C3H6O (Acetone). (Density of Acetone is 0.784 g/ml)


30 g of Citric Acid compute for the MM

MM (C₆H₈O₇) = 192.124 g/mol

n (C₆H₈O₇) = 30 g • 1 mol/192.124 g = 0.156 mol

m (C₃H₆O) = 170 mL • 0.784 g/mL = 133.28 g

Converting g to kg
133.28 g • 1 kg/10³ = 0.13328 kg


Molality = (0.156 mol)/(0.13328 kg)
= 1.17 m

29. A solution of ethyl alcohol is prepared by combining 30g of ethyl alcohol with 80g of water. What is the mass of the solute? 80g 10g 27g 30g​


Solution

Answer:

The mass of the solute is 30g.

Explanation:

A solution is a kind of homogeneous mixture that is made of solute and solvent. The solute is the substance that is dissolved and the solvent is the substance that does the dissolving. Solutions can be formed with many different types and forms of solutes and solvents. The dissolved substances or the solute can be solid, liquid, or gas. For fluid solutions, the solvent is in greater amount than the solute. Thus, in the solution that is made of 30 g ethyl alcohol and 80 g of water, the solute being in smaller amount is the solute while the water which is in greater amount is the solvent.
