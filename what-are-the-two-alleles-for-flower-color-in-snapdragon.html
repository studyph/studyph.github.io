Due to the flowers' alleged resemblance to a dragon's face that may open and close when pressed laterally, the plant genus Antirrhinum is also known as snapdragons, dog flowers, and dragon flowers. These species are native to rocky areas in North Africa, Europe, the US, and Canada. It is commonly used as a cut flower and makes a lovely border plant.

The genus Antirrhinum was utilized by Charles Darwin and Gregor Mendel for their earliest genetic study, and Erwin Baur used it as a model (Tolety 2011). Together with closely related species, it has developed into a model organism for the study of the genetic basis of plant growth, particularly floral development. The species serves as a prime example of the red allele's erroneous dominance over the anthocyanin pigment. A heterozygous phenotype with pink flowers that carries both the dominant and recessive alleles is produced by any hybrid between red-flowered and white-flowered snapdragons.

Snapdragons are short-lived perennials that thrive in cold locations despite being referred to as annual plants. They are usually transplanted each spring. Although they do require frequent watering, because their roots are prone to rotting, they flourish best in full or partial sunshine and in well-drained soil. They are categorized into three height groups for commercial purposes: tall (75-120 cm), medium (40-75 cm), and midget or dwarf (15–20 cm).

In order for plants to continue to produce beautiful flowers throughout their growing season, deadheading is an essential step. They are susceptible to ethylene gas harm, so avoiding ripe fruits and vegetables and removing any dead flowers can help them maintain their bloom. They grow most quickly in the Northern Hemisphere between April and June and August and October. Flowers can bloom in a variety of colors, including pink, orange, red, yellow, purple, white, and even patterns with many colors. They have a diverse range of biological species, particularly those from the New World (Saerorhinum).

Gametes

The gametes are the male and female sex or reproductive cells. In plants, the male gamete is the sperm cell in the form of pollen. The flower contains male sex organs called the stamens. Stamens contain the anther that contains pollen. The female gamete of plants, on the other hand, is the egg cell known as the ovule. The ovule is enclosed in an ovary which can be found on the pistil of the flower. The genetic material from the male and female gametes combines to produce offspring. This process is called fertilization. Pollination is the process of how flowering plants reproduce sexually.

Phenotype and Genotype

The genotype is the genetic material that is passed on from one generation to the next. It is made up of pair of alleles. Phenotype is the characteristic or trait that is expressed by the organism. This is based on the organism's genotype.

Alleles

Alleles are variants of genes. Each gene contains two alleles - one from the father and the other from the mother. When two alleles in a gene are the same, they are referred to as homozygous. However, when the alleles comprising the gene are different, they are considered heterozygous.
