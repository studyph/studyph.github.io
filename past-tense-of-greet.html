Explanation:

The action verb "greet" is a regular verb. Regular verbs do have the same form in its form in past tense and in past participle. That is, the past tense and past participle of a regular verb is formed by adding -d or -ed at the end of the word.

Answer:

24 STUDENTS

Step-by-step explanation:

C(n,r)=?

C(n,r)=C(24,2)

=24!/(2!(24−2)!)

=24!/2!×22!

= 276 GREETINGS
