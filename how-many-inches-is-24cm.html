26. Alex cuts circles with a diameter of 8cm from a piece of paperwith dimensions 40 cm by 24cm. how many circles can he cut out of the paper?


Answer:

15 circles

Step-by-step explanation:

There are many ways in solving this problem and that includes simple division and multiplication.

As stated in the problem, the paper is rectangular in form (24cm x 40cm).

There is a way we might think of to answer the question, that is by getting the AREA OF THE RECTANGLE and dividing by the AREA OF THE CIRCLE.

However, it would totally be a mistake. Because just by dividing the two areas, you are not getting the number of circles (complete circles) to be cut from the paper. Rather, you are getting just how many broken circles there is from the paper. See the illustration below for better understanding.

We take note that to maximize the number of circles to be made, we need to make sure that the first circle is at one of the edge of the paper and touches both dimensions. See illustration.

Then we identify whether the dimensions of the paper are divisible by the circle’s diameter.

So we divide

24cm / 8cm = 3

40cm / 8 cm = 5

Thus, there are 3 divisions we make out of the 24cm side. And there are 5 divisions we make from the 40cm side.

Getting the number of circles is just multiplying the divisions we got from each side

3 x 5 = 15 circles

Another simpler way to get the number of circles is by using areas.

We know that in order to cut a full circle, we need a SQUARE WHOSE SIDES ARE EQUAL TO THE CIRCLE’s diameter.

Thus, we divide the area of the rectangle

24cm x 40cm = 960 cm^2

by the area of the “square”

8cm x 8cm = 64 cm^2

Thus

960 cm^2 / 64cm^2

15 circles


Therefore, there are 15 circles that can be made from the paper.

27. A 12cm high object is placed in front of a concave mirror with a focal length of 24cm. if the object is placed 24cm away from the mirror (a) how far is the image from the mirror?(b) what is the height of the image formed​


Answer:

Sa kaso ng isang 12 cm na bagay na nakalagay sa harap ng isang konkabong salamin na may focal length na 24 cm, ang layo ng larawan mula sa salamin ay maaaring matukoy sa pamamagitan ng paggamit ng formula ng layo ng larawan sa konkabong salamin. Ang formula ay:

1 / f = 1 / do + 1 / di

kung saan:

f = focal length ng salamin

do = layo ng orihinal na bagay mula sa salamin

di = layo ng larawan mula sa salamin

Sa kaso ng tanong, do = 24 cm at f = 24 cm, kaya:

1 / 24 = 1 / 24 + 1 / di

1 / 24 = 1 / 24 + 1 / di

di = 48 cm

Ang layo ng larawan mula sa salamin ay 48 cm.

Upang matukoy ang taas ng larawan, maaari mong gamitin ang formula ng taas ng larawan sa konkabong salamin. Ang formula ay:

hi / ho = -di / do

kung saan:

hi = taas ng larawan

ho = taas ng orihinal na bagay

do = layo ng orihinal na bagay mula sa salamin

di = layo ng larawan mula sa salamin

Sa kaso ng tanong, ho = 12 cm, do = 24 cm, at di = 48 cm, kaya:

hi / 12 = -48 / 24

hi = -24 cm

Ang taas ng larawan ay -24 cm. Ito ay nangangahulugan na ang larawan ay nakalikha ng isang maliit na bagay na nakatungo sa ibaba.
