6. how many moles of silver nitrate are there in 80.0 grams of silver nitrate?


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of silver nitrate (AgNO₃).

molar mass = (107.9 g × 1) + (14.01 g × 1) + (16.00 g × 3)

molar mass = 169.91 g

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of AgNO₃.

[tex]\text{moles of AgNO₃ = 80.0 g AgNO₃} × \frac{\text{1 mol AgNO₃}}{\text{169.91 g AgNO₃}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{moles of AgNO₃ = 0.471 mol}}[/tex]

14. Silver (I) nitrate reacts with copper to form copper (II) nitrate and silver.​


Answer:

Silver nitrate (AgNO3) reacts with copper (Cu) to form copper(II) nitrate (Cu(NO3)2) and silver (Ag). This can be called a redox reaction because silver nitrate is reduced and copper is oxidized. This can also be called a single replacement reaction because copper replaces silver in the substance silver nitrate


15. What is nitration?. . .​


Answer:

Nitration is a general chemical process for the introduction of a nitro group in a chemical compound, like the conversation of glycerin to nitroglycerin and the conversion of toluene to trinitrotoluene, both conversions use nitric acid and sulfuric acid.
