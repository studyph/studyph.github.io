1. what is agri/agriculture or biology?


Agricultural science is a broad multidisciplinary field of biology that encompasses the parts of exact, natural, economic and social sciences that are used in the practice and understanding of agriculture. (Veterinary science, but not animal science, is often excluded from the definition.)


2. why it is important to study agricultural biology​


Agricultural biology is also concerned with improving chemical and biological methods for combating plant and animal diseases and pests and raising efficiency in the use of solar energy by agricultural plants during photosynthesis. For example, models for optimal planting are investigated.

3. Applying for a job in agriculture with biology degree


Agricultural biology is the study of plant and animal life that has been domesticated for production. A variety of jobs exist in this field, including natural science managers, scientific sales representatives and agricultural scientists. A bachelor's degree is the minimum level of education for all three of these careers.

15. 1. Give at least three (3) importance of these tools in genetic engineering in the advancement of cell and molecular biology in the following fields: a. Food b. Medicine c. Agriculture Hopefully someone can answer this. Thanks in advance <3


Genetic engineering is the bran of biological sciences that deals with the methods and applications of altering the genome sequence using modern tools and techniques.

Genetic engineering has proved to be a game-changer as it has a wide range of applications. The applications of genetic engineering can be expressed as follows:

Food - The food and beverage industry has benefitted tremendously with the advent of genetic engineering. The development of specific strains of species and active enzymes have proved to be beneficial for the fermented food, dairy, and beverage industry.

Medicine - Genetic engineering has contributed greatly to the medical sector. The manufacturing hormones like insulin, HGH, etc have been made possible due to genetic engineering. It has also helped in the development of monoclonal antibodies and recombinant vaccines.

Agriculture - The development of genetically modified crops has resulted in superior quality and increased quantity of crops. It is due to genetic engineering that we now have pest-resistant, disease-resistant crops. It has also contributed in increasing the meat and milk quality.

19. What is biology?What is the meaning of biology?Whose invented the biology? ​


What is biology?

Biology is the natural science that studies life and living organisms, including their physical structure, chemical processes, molecular interactions, physiological mechanisms, cognition, and even evolution.

What is the meaning of biology?

The concept that drives this field. Biology examines the cell as the basic unit of life, the gene as the basic unit of inheritance, and evolution as the engine that drives the reproduction and extinction of species. Living organisms are open systems that survive the transfer of energy and the reduction of their local entropy to maintain the slow and important condition called "homeostasis".

Whose invented the biology?

Aristotle was a Greek philosopher and polymath during the Classical period in Ancient Greece. Taught by Plato, he was the founder of the Lyceum, the Peripatetic school of philosophy, and the Aristotelian tradition and he called it History of Biology.
