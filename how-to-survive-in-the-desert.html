10. Explain how camels can survive in the desert, and give examples of adaptations that camels have.​


Answer:

Camels are able to survive in the desert due to a variety of adaptations that they have developed over time. Some examples include:

Humps: Camels have a hump or two on their back which stores fat, this fat can provide energy when food and water are scarce.

Thick fur: Camels have thick fur that protects them from the sun and hot temperatures.

Wide feet: Camels have wide feet that help them to walk on sand without sinking.

Long eyelashes and ear hair: Camels have long eyelashes and ear hair that protect their eyes and ears from sandstorms.

Adaptable nose: Camels have an adaptable nose that can close to keep out sand and sandstorms.

Water-storage: Camels can store water in their body, they can go without drinking for a long time, they can lose up to 30% of their body weight in water and still survive.

Sweat glands: Camels have specialized sweat glands that can conserve water by producing very little sweat.

Endurance: Camels can travel long distances in the desert and can withstand extreme temperatures, they can travel for days without water and food.

Overall, these adaptations allow camels to survive in the harsh desert environment by conserving water, protecting themselves from the sun and sandstorms, and being able to travel long distances without food or water.
