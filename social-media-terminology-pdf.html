9. Explain the meaning of the following terminology. Why did they affect in India? Terminologies -Rowlatt Act - Amritsar Massacre -Satyagraha -Salt March Effect ( explain each terminologies )


Answer:

1. Rowlatt Act: The Rowlatt Act was a law passed by the British in India in 1919 that authorized the government to imprison anyone without trial or conviction for up to two years. It also allowed the British to ban public meetings and censor the press. This act was highly controversial, and it led to widespread protests and demonstrations across India.

2. Amritsar Massacre: The Amritsar Massacre was a tragic event that occurred on April 13, 1919, in the city of Amritsar, Punjab. British troops opened fire on a peaceful gathering of thousands of unarmed Indians who were protesting against the Rowlatt Act. The massacre left hundreds of people dead and wounded. This event had a significant impact on the Indian independence movement, as it increased the demand for self-rule and inspired many Indians to join the struggle for independence.

3. Satyagraha: Satyagraha is a philosophy of nonviolent resistance developed by Mahatma Gandhi. The term means "insistence on truth" and Gandhi believed in using nonviolent means to resist injustice and oppression. Satyagraha played a significant role in the Indian independence movement as it helped to mobilize large numbers of people, and it inspired other nonviolent movements around the world.

4. Salt March: The Salt March was a nonviolent protest organized by Mahatma Gandhi in 1930. The march was a response to the British monopoly on salt, which prevented Indians from making their own salt. Gandhi and a group of followers walked more than 240 miles from Sabarmati Ashram to the Arabian Sea to collect salt. The march received extensive media coverage and inspired Indians to join the independence movement. This event marked a significant turning point in the Indian independence struggle as it demonstrated the power of nonviolent resistance.

Enumerate 10 political, 5 social, and 5 economic Spanish terminologies with their respective meanings.

POLITICAL:

Speech/Discourse [el discurso]Speech is a talk where we can express our feelings and thoughts. Politicians usually do this formal talk when they were delivering their announcement, actions, and thoughts to the audiences.

Power [el poder]Power is one's ability to create rules and regulations, and to give punishment to whoever disobeys it.

Government [el gobierno]Government is a group of politicians/group of responsible leaders who rules and administered a group, Country, and Society. They're the ones who are responsible for creating laws and enforce these.

President [el presidente, la presidente]Like a King, is the highest one who rules the whole country, society, or group. He/She is the Head of a Republic, or Government.

Politician [politico]One that has a political role, and part of a government. He/She involves making decisions in a Group/Society.

Senator [senador, senadora]Senators are also considered as politicians. They're also responsible for protecting the individual states and contributes in helping a Government to have a greater Power/Authority.

Vote [votar]Vote is the process of choosing which one would you like to rule the society, or which one would you like to be your leader. This usually done by normal people.

Unity [la unidad]Unity simply defines as being one/being together. Having one positive idea/ decision about a particular side/issue.

Tax [el impuesto]Tax is a required contribution of a person, group, business, or society to support the Government and its programs/expenses.

Secretary [secretaria, secretario]A government official who is responsible for taking care of the records and documents of the government. She/He also responsible for writing the President's letter/speech to His people.

SOCIAL:

Culture [la cultura]refers to the lifestyle, belief, and symbols of one's particular group/ethnicity.

Community [la comunidad]A group of people or any living things that has the same beliefs, culture, and characteristics.

World [mundo]The universe.

Civilization [civilización]A complex human society, usually comprised of different cities, with certain features of cultural and technological development.

Nation [nacion]A group of people who allocates the same culture, language, history, and ethnicity. A group of people living in the same country/society ruled by one whole government.

ECONOMIC:

Business [negocio]a particular work, job, or occupation. Source of living.

Industry [industria]economic activity associated with the processing of raw materials and tye production of goods in factories.

Merchandising [comercialización]Merchandising is anything that contributes to selling products to a retail consumer. Means displaying products that are on sale in a creative way that encourages customers to buy more items or products.

Dealing [relación comercial]A business transaction.

Exchange [intercambio]The act of exchanging (especially products/services) in a business.

What is environmental ethics/what does it mean?

It is the discipline in thinking that people study about the truthful relationship of human beings, and also the value/importance of the environment.

What is an example of environmental ethics?

Things like water and air pollution, the use of natural resources, loss of biodiversity, destruction of the community of living creatures, and global climate change are all part of the environmental ethics.

What is the purpose of environmental ethics?

The purpose of environmental ethics is that they maintain to provide moral grounds for social policies aimed at protecting the earth's environment and setting environmental humiliation/shame right.
