6. sue plans to mix pepper mints worth $1.20 per lb with chocolates worth $2.40 per lb to get a 40 lb mix that is worth $1.65 per lb. How much of each should she use?


Let 'x' be the amount of pepper mints ($1.20 per pound)
       'y' be the amount of chocolates ($2.40 per pound)
-------------------------
-The whole mixture amounts to 40 lbs.
x + y = 40    ----------equation 1
------------------------
-The mix is $1.65 per pound
1.2x + 2.4y = 40(1.65)
1.2x + 2.4y = 66   ----equation 2
-----------------------
From equation 1
x + y = 40
x = 40 - y   ----equation 3
---------------------
Substitute equation 3 to equation 2
1.2x + 2.4y = 66
1.2(40-y) + 2.4y = 66
48 - 1.2y + 2.4y = 66
-1.2y + 2.4y = 66 - 48
1.2y = 18
y = 15 lbs
--------------------
Substitute y=15 to equation 3
x = 40 - y
x = 40 - 15
x = 25 lbs
--------------------
Therefore 15 lbs of chocolates and 25 lbs of pepper mints will be used.

7. IF LA and LB are supplementary and mLA = 40 degree, what is mLB?


Supplementary Anglestwo angles whose sum is equal to 180°

Question

IF LA and LB are supplementary and m∠LA = 40 degree, what is m∠LB?

Answer

Since LA and LB are supplementary,

m∠LA + m∠LB = 180° ; transpose m∠LA

m∠LB = 180° - m∠LA ; substitute the given value of m∠LA

m∠LB = 180° - 40° ; solve

m∠LB = 140°

8. If a force of 60 lb. acts at an angle of 40 to the horizontal, what is its vertical component?


Answer:

The vertical component of force is 38.57 lb

Explanation:

In our case, the worded problem  is related to vector forces. Here, we are going to solve the vertical component of the force.

For the formula, we will use:

Fy = F sin θ

where

Fy     is the vertical component of the force, unit is in Newton (N) or pounds

        (lb)

F       is the given force, unit is in Newton (N) or pounds (lb)

θ       is the angle formed with the horizontal

For the given information

F = 60 lb

θ = 60°

Fy = ?

Solving the problem

Use the formula for the vertical component of force, we have:

Fy = F sin θ

Then, substitute the given information, we have:

Fy = 60 sin 40°

Fy = 38.57 lb

Therefore, the vertical component of the force is 38.57 lb.

21. a 40 lb block is on a horizontal surface. a force of 70 lb is applied to the block parallel to the surface. the coefficient of sliding friction is 0.2. what is the acceleration of the block?​


The acceleration of the block = 49.92 ft/s²

Further explanation  

The force acting on a system with static equilibrium is 0  

[tex]\large {\boxed {\bold {\sum F = 0}}[/tex]

(forces acting as translational motion only, not including rotational forces)  

[tex]\displaystyle \sum F_x = 0 \\\\\sum F_y = 0[/tex]

For objects undergoing rotation, the equilibrium must be met  

[tex]\large {\boxed {\bold {\sum \tau = 0}}[/tex]

The force acting on the surface between the two fields is called the normal force (N)

Newton's 2nd law explains that the acceleration produced by the resultant force on an object is proportional and in line with the resultant force and inversely proportional to the mass of the object  

∑F = m. a  

While the frictional force arises because of 2 objects that come into direct contact, especially when there is motion between two objects that are in direct contact.  

There are 2 friction forces  

static friction: fs = us.N  

kinetic friction: fk = uk .N  

us/uk = sliding friction (s=static, k=kinetic)

A 40 lb block is on a horizontal surface, then

[tex]\rm m=\dfrac{40~lb}{32.2~ft/s^2}=1.242[/tex]

A force of 70 lb is applied to the block parallel to the surface. the coefficient of sliding friction is 0.2, then

Parallel means the force moves in the x-axis direction

we describe the forces acting in the direction of the x and y axis :

[tex]\rm \sum F_y=0\\\\N(Normal force) - W = m.a\\\\N-40=1.242.\times 0(a_y=0)\\\\N-40=0\\\\N=40[/tex]

[tex]\rm \sum F_x=0\\\\F-f_k=m.a\\\\F-\mu k.N=m.a\\\\70-0.2.40=1.242.a\\\\62=1.242a\\\\a=\dfrac{62}{1.242}=\boxed{\bold{49.92~ft/s^2}}[/tex]
