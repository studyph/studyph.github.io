24. A weightlifter raise a 100kg barbel to a height of 2.2 m.what is the barbel of potential energy?


ANSWER:

G (Given) - m = 100 kg

g = 9.8 m/s^2

h = 2.2 m

R (Required) - Potential Energy

E (Equation) - PE = m × g × h

S (Solution) - PE = 100 kg × 9.8 m/s^2 × 2.2 m

PE = 2,156 J

A (Answer) - The potential energy of the barbel is 2,156 J.

30. a weight lifterlying on his back, raises a 60.0kg barbell from his chest a distance of 0.540m. above his chest. He then lowers it the same distance. assuming that he lowers and raises the barbell at constant speed, find the work done by the lifter. a. in lifting the barbell b. in lowering the barbell PLEASE ANSWER OT NOW! HUHU


#BrainlyFast

Note:

We will solve first for Force before we can solve Work

Given Data:

m ( mass ) = 60 kg

a ( acceleration ) = 9.8 m/s²

F ( Force ) = ? unknown

Solution:

We can use the formula F = m × a in order to solve for Force before we can compute for work done.

F = m × a

   = 60 kg × 9.8 m/s²

   = 588 kg·m/s²

  = 588 N

Solving for Work

F ( Force ) = 588 kg·m/s²

d ( distance ) = 0.54 m

W ( work ) = ? unknown

Solution:

We can use the formula W = F × d to solve for work.

W = F × d

   = 588 kg·m/s² × 0.54 m

   = 317.52 kg·m²/s²

   = 317.52 J

Answer:

317.52 J
