The concept that the giraffe would have developed a long neck so that it could adapt to grasping leaves on trees converges with Lamarck's Theory, involving the Law of Use and Disuse (answer letter B).

Lamarck and Evolution

Lamarck is represented as one of the main naturalist theorists, who had as one of his works, the Law of Use and Disuse. This law recommended that animals adapt or modify their structures according to environmental pressures, as in the case of the giraffe, which supposedly developed a long neck to feed itself.
