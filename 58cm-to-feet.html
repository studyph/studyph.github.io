3. Find the volume a cylinder with a radius of 36cm and a height of 58cm​


Answer: 236,147.24 cm^3

The volume of a cylinder can be calculated using the formula:

volume = π * radius^2 * height

where radius is the distance from the center of the base to the edge of the base, and height is the distance from the top of the cylinder to the bottom of the cylinder.

So, if the radius of the cylinder is 36 cm and the height is 58 cm, we can plug these values into the formula:

volume = π * (36 cm)^2 * 58 cm

V= π * 1296 cm^2 * 58 cm

The radius of the cylinder is given in cm, so the area (radius*radius) is in square cm and the volume will be in cubic cm

So the final volume will be:

V= π * 1296 * 58 cm^3

V= 236147.24 cm^3

Therefore, the volume of the cylinder is approximately  236,147.24 cm^3.


4. a concave mirror has a focal length of 18cm. where will an image form if an object is placed 58cm from the image​


Answer:

the image will form 30.86 cm away from the concave mirror.

Explanation:

Using the mirror formula:

1/f = 1/do + 1/di

where:

f = focal length = 18 cm

do = object distance = 58 cm

di = image distance (unknown)

Plugging in the given values, we have:

1/18 = 1/58 + 1/di

Solving for di, we get:

di = 30.86 cm

Therefore, the image will form 30.86 cm away from the concave mirror.

7. the length of a rectangle is 5 more than twice the width. if the perimeter is 58cm. what is the area of the rectangle?


Given:

P = 58 cm
Length = 5 more than twice the width ( L = 2W +5 )

Solution:

Let (L) be the length
Let (W) be the width
L = 2W + 5

P = 2L + 2W
58 = 2(2W +5) + 2W
58 = 4W + 10 +2W
48 = 6W
W = 8 cm

Substitute the value of W to get L:

L = 2W + 5
L = 2(8) + 5
L = 16 + 5
L = 21 cm

Get the area:

A = LW
A = ( 21 cm )( 8 cm )
A = 168 cm²

Answer:

The dimensions are 21 cm by 8 cm and the area is 168 cm².
