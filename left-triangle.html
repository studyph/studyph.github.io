10. amy cut a triangle from a rectangular paper.the base of triangle is 0.8m and it's height 1m as shown below. what is the area of the bigger piece of paper left?​


Step-by-step explanation:

Since Amy cut a triangle from the rectangular paper, the remaining shape is also a rectangle.

The area of the rectangle is equal to the area of the original rectangle minus the area of the cut-out triangle.

The area of the triangle can be calculated using the formula:

Area = (1/2) x base x height

Plugging in the values, we get:

Area of triangle = (1/2) x 0.8m x 1m

Area of triangle = 0.4m²

The area of the original rectangle is not given, so we cannot calculate the exact area of the remaining piece. However, we can say that the area of the remaining piece is equal to the area of the original rectangle minus the area of the cut-out triangle.

Let's use "A" to represent the area of the original rectangle. Then:

Area of remaining piece = A - 0.4m²

Therefore, we cannot find the exact area of the remaining piece without knowing the area of the original rectangle.
