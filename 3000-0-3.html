20. How many 4-digit numbers with no repeated digit can be formed from the numerals 0, 1, 2, 3, 4, 5 & 6? How many of these are odd? How many of these are greater than 3000?


Answer:

To form a 4-digit number with no repeated digit, there are 7 choices for the first digit, 6 choices for the second digit (as one of them has already been used), 5 choices for the third digit, and 4 choices for the fourth digit. Hence, the total number of 4-digit numbers with no repeated digit that can be formed is 7 × 6 × 5 × 4 = 840.

Of these 840 numbers, 420 are odd and 420 are even.

Out of the 840 numbers, there are 840 - 420 = 420 numbers that are greater than 3000.
