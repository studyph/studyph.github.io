Climate can be compromised due to various factors, including greenhouse gas emissions, deforestation, ocean acidification, and air pollution. Greenhouse gases trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, leading to a rise in global temperatures and changing weather patterns. Deforestation reduces the number of trees that absorb carbon dioxide, while ocean acidification makes it more difficult for marine organisms to build their shells and skeletons. Air pollution can lead to respiratory problems and other health issues for humans and animals.Latitude is a geographic coordinate that measures a location's distance north or south of the Earth's equator. It is typically expressed in degrees, minutes, and seconds, and ranges from 0° at the equator to 90° at the North and South poles.The tropical zone is the region of the Earth that lies between the Tropic of Cancer (23.5° north of the equator) and the Tropic of Capricorn (23.5° south of the equator). This region is characterized by warm temperatures and high humidity throughout the year, and is home to a variety of tropical forests, savannas, and grasslandsThe Earth is tilted because of the way it formed and the forces acting on it. There are several different ways to classify climate zones, but one common system divides the Earth into five main types: tropical, dry, temperate, continental, and polar. Examples of countries in each of these zones include:

Tropical: Brazil, Thailand, Indonesia, and the Democratic Republic of CongoDry: Egypt, Saudi Arabia, Mongolia, and parts of AustraliaTemperate: Japan, France, Canada, and the United StatesContinental: Russia, Kazakhstan, and parts of ChinaPolar: Norway, Canada (Arctic), and Antarctica.
