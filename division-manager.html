30. The management of International Heal Medical Company is evaluating the performance of its three (3) divisions. The Booboo Division had an operating profit of ₱24,950 and, on average used assets with a book value of ₱311,900. The Splint Division had an operating profit of ₱17,500 and used average assets of ₱177,950. The Intensive Care Division had an operating profit of ₱28,500 and average assets of ₱475,000. The company plans to award the Intensive Care Division, relying on its high operating profit. Should the management continue with this decision? Justify your answer


International Heal Medical Company

a. Booboo Division

Php 311,900 - Php 24,950 = Php 286,950

Php 24,950 ÷ Php 286,950 x 100% = 8.7%

Booboo Division has 8.7% operating profit.

b. Splint Division

Php 177,950 - Php 17,500 = Php 160,450

Php 17,500 ÷ Php 160,450 x 100% = 10.9%

Splint Division has 10.9% operating profit.

c. Intensive Care Division

Php 475,000 - Php 28,500 = Php 446,500

Php 28,500 ÷ Php 446,500 x 100% = 6.38%

Intensive Care Division has 6.38% operating profit.

Because the Intensive Care Division's operating profit is so low in comparison to the other two divisions—6.38%—the management should not continue to award it. Booboo Division outperformed Intensive Care Division in terms of operating profit, gaining 8.7%. The largest operational profit was realized by the Splint Division at 10.9%. Due to its higher operating profit, the company must give Splint Division the award rather than Intensive Care Division.

What is profit?

Profit is the term used to describe the financial gain experienced when the revenue from a commercial activity exceeds the costs, costs, and taxes associated with maintaining that activity. Any gains made go back to the company's owners, who can decide whether to keep the money for themselves, give it to shareholders as dividends, or put it to use again in the company.
