[tex]\tt{\huge{\green{Solution:}}}[/tex]

The problem asks to calculate for the volume of the solute. First, we need to identify the given values. These are

percent by volume = 40%

volume of solution = 60 mL

Finally, we can now calculate the volume of the solute. Therefore, the volume of the solute is

[tex]\text{volume of solute} = \dfrac{\text{percent by volume}}{100\%} \times \text{volume of solution}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solute} = \dfrac{40\%}{100\%} \times \text{60 mL}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{volume of solute = 24 mL}}[/tex]
