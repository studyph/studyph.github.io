Let:

A = # of 5 cents

B = # of 10 cents

C = # of 25 cents

Therefore:

0.05A + 0.10B + 0.25C = 4.90 where:

B = 3A

C = B+2 = (3A+2)= 6A so:

0.05A + 0.10(3A) + 0.25(3A+2) = 4.90

0.05A + 0.30A + (0.75A + 0.50) = 4.90

1.10A = 4.90-0.50

1.10A = 4.40

A = 4.40/1.10

A = 4

B = 3A = 3(4) = 12

C = B+2 = 12+2 = 14

Checking:

0.05A + 0.10B + 0.25C = 4.90

0.05(4) + 0.10(12) + 0.25(14) = 4.90

0.20 + 1.20 + 3.5 = 4.90

19. Ann noticed in counting her money that for each 5 cent she had a 10 cent and for each 10 cent she had a 25 cent. When she counted her money, she found that she had Php 57.60. Find how many of each he had.​


Answer:

Ann has 28.8 5 cent coins, 28.8 10 cent coins, and 57.6 25 cent coins.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let's use algebra to solve this problem. Let:

x = the number of 5 cent coins

y = the number of 10 cent coins

z = the number of 25 cent coins

From the problem, we know that for each 5 cent coin, Ann has a corresponding 10 cent coin. This means that the number of 10 cent coins is equal to the number of 5 cent coins:

y = x

We also know that for each 10 cent coin, Ann has a corresponding 25 cent coin. This means that the number of 25 cent coins is equal to twice the number of 10 cent coins:

z = 2y

Finally, we know that Ann has a total of Php 57.60. This can be expressed as the sum of the value of all her coins in centavos:

5x + 10y + 25z = 5760

Now we can substitute the first two equations into the third equation and simplify:

5x + 10x + 25(2y) = 5760

15x + 50y = 5760

3x + 10y = 1152

We can solve this system of equations using substitution or elimination. Let's use elimination by multiplying the second equation by 3 and subtracting it from the first equation:

3x - 3x + 10y - 30y = 1152 - 1728

-20y = -576

y = 28.8

This means that Ann has 28.8 10 cent coins, which is not a whole number. Since she cannot have a fractional number of coins, we need to check our work. Let's go back to the original equations and use the value of y to find x and z:

y = x

28.8 = x

z = 2y

z = 57.6

This tells us that Ann has 28.8 5 cent coins, 28.8 10 cent coins, and 57.6 25 cent coins. We can check that these values satisfy all the conditions of the problem:

For each 5 cent coin, Ann has a corresponding 10 cent coin: 28.8 x 2 = 57.6

For each 10 cent coin, Ann has a corresponding 25 cent coin: 28.8 x 2 = 57.6

The total value of all her coins is Php 57.60: 28.8(5) + 28.8(10) + 57.6(25) = 5760

Therefore, Ann has 28.8 5 cent coins, 28.8 10 cent coins, and 57.6 25 cent coins.
