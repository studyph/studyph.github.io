INTERVAL

In music, an interval is the distance between two notes. It describes the position of the two notes on the musical staff.

1st staff

1. Third interval: The first note is F (first space) and the second note is A (second space). The distance from F to A includes F, G and A  (three notes)so it is a third interval.

2. Prime interval: The first note is F (first space) and the second note is also an F (first space). The two notes occupy the same position on the treble staff. The two notes are written on the same space, the first space, so it is a prime interval.

3. Octave interval: The first note is D (first space below the treble staff) and the second note is the higher D (fourth line). The two notes are eight apart, so it is an octave interval.

4. Fourth interval: The first note is G (second line) and the second note is the higher C (third space). The two notes are four notes apart. The distance of the two notes encompasses G, A, B and C (four notes) so it is an fourth interval.

5. Third interval: The first note is the middle C (first ledger line below the treble staff) and the second note is the E (first line). The two notes are three notes apart. The distance of the two notes encompasses C, D and E (three notes with three whole steps apart) so it is an third interval.

second staff

1. Second interval: The first note is E (first line) and the second note is the F (first space). The two notes are two notes apart. The distance of the two notes encompasses only E and F (two notes with a half step apart) so it is a second interval.

2. Fifth interval: The first note is F (first space) and the second note is the higher C (third space). The two notes are four notes apart. The distance of the two notes encompasses F, G, A, B and C (five notes with five half steps apart) so it is a fifth interval.

3. Sixth interval: The first note is E (first line) and the second note is the higher C (third space). The two notes are six notes apart. The distance of the two notes encompasses E, F, G, A, B and C (six notes with six half steps apart) so it is a sixth interval.

4. Prime  interval: The first note is higher C (third space) and the second note is also the higher C (third space). The two notes occupy the same position on the treble staff. The two notes are written on the same space, the third space, so it is a prime interval.

5. Fifth interval: The first note is B (third line) and the second note is the higher F (fifth line). The two notes are five notes apart. The distance of the two notes encompasses B, C, D, E and F (five notes) so it is a fifth interval.
