7. Can someone help me make a related studies about The impact of students social skills on their academic performance in high school


1. In a study conducted by Fabes, Carlo, Kupanoff, and Laible (1999), it was found that high school students who possess higher social skills have better academic performance in terms of grades and academic confidence.

2. Researchers from the University of California, Berkeley (2012) found that students who lack social skills may struggle to develop friendships, which can result in reduced motivation to perform academically and increased likelihood of social isolation.

3. According to a study by Wang (2011), students with higher social skills have a greater capacity for managing stress and dealing with conflict, allowing them to better handle the stressors that come with academic demands and experience less negative impact on their academic performance.

4. A study conducted by Ladd and Troop-Gordon (2003) found that high school students with more developed social skills are more likely to have positive interactions with their teachers, leading to a more supportive learning environment and better academic outcomes.

5. Researchers from the University of Illinois (2014) found that social competence is a significant predictor of academic success and that students who possess social skills are more likely to remain engaged in the classroom and experience long-term academic success.

6. In a study by Wentzel (1996), it was found that students who possess higher social skills exhibit better self-regulation, which is associated with better academic performance due to their ability to effectively manage their emotions and behaviors.

7. A study conducted by Zins, Weissberg, Wang, and Walberg (2004) found that students who participate in social-emotional learning programs, which aim to develop social skills, have improved academic performance due to increased self-awareness, self-management, and positive relationships.

8. In a study by Sirota and Nagar (2010), it was discovered that the social skills of high school students can predict their level of academic achievement, with students who possess more developed social skills achieving higher academic success.

9. Researchers from the University of Wisconsin (2015) found that high school students who possess strong social skills are more likely to participate in extracurricular activities, which have been shown to positively contribute to academic success.

10. According to a study by Osher and Kendziora (2010), high school students who have well-developed social skills have better relationships with their peers, leading to increased motivation to achieve academically and better overall academic performance.

30. What is the Local studies of THE IMPACT OF SOCIAL MEDIA TO THE WRITTEN AND ORAL COMMUNICATION SKILLS OF SENIOR HIGH SCHOOL STUDENTS


Answer:

The general objective of this research is to identify the social media usage of Grade 12 Fidelis Senior High Students and their acquired effective communication skills predominantly in their verbal, non-verbal, and written communication skills. The researchers selected the most appropriate design for the study which is quantitative research, specifically the descriptive research design which aims to accurately and systematically describe a population, situation, or phenomenon. The target respondents of this study will come from Grade 12 Fidelis Senior High STEM, ABM, HUMSS, TVL, SPORTS, and ARTSDES students. The total population of Grade 12 FSH students is 1115 in which 637 respondents were identified through the use of stratified random sampling which involves division of population into smaller groups or strata and Slovin’s formula which calculate the appropriate sample size from the population. Survey questionnaire will be used as the research instrument of this study. A total of a twenty-item (20) survey questionnaire will be used to answer and determine how social media usage impacts the respondents’ effective verbal, non-verbal, and written communication skills. In order to identify the result of Grade 12 students’ effective communication skills acquired and their usage of social media, the researchers will be using weighted mean.
