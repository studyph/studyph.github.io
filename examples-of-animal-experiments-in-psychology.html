1. Why does psychological experiments utilize animals for testing?​


Answer:

experiments. Here is a selection of common animal procedures:

Forced chemical exposure in toxicity testing, which can include oral force-feeding, forced inhalation, skin or injection into the abdomen, muscle, etc.

Exposure to drugs, chemicals or infectious disease at levels that cause illness, pain and distress, or death

Genetic manipulation, e.g., addition or “knocking out” of one or more genes

Ear-notching and tail-clipping for identification

Short periods of physical restraint for observation or examination

Prolonged periods of physical restraint

Food and water deprivation

Surgical procedures followed by recovery

Infliction of wounds, burns and other injuries to study healing

Infliction of pain to study its physiology and treatment

Behavioural experiments designed to cause distress, e.g., electric shock or forced swimming

Other manipulations to create “animal models” of human diseases ranging from cancer to stroke to depression

Killing by carbon dioxide asphyxiation, neck-breaking, decapitation, or other means

Examples of interactive relationships between psychological aspects and physical aspects.

1. Physical health affects mental health and vice versa, mental health affects physical health.

This is the most basic interactive relationship between the physical and psychological aspects of human beings. When we keep our bodies healthy, we also take care of the health of our minds. For example, such as eating healthy foods, getting enough rest and sleep, and exercising regularly. Because our bodies are fit, our minds will also be fresh. But if we don't take care of our bodies, our minds will not be fresh because of unfit body conditions.

And vice versa. By keeping our minds healthy, we also take care of our bodies. Because if our minds experience mental problems, then it can have an impact on the physical. We can feel tired easily and not have the energy to do activities. On a more severe level, this can also cause physical pain.

2. Loving yourself = accepting your own physic

We may often compare ourselves with others or feel less beautiful and perfect. This may make us want to change our physique to become more beautiful. But one of the ways we can do that without changing our physique is to accept and love ourselves. Realize that everyone has their own strengths and weaknesses, and beauty is not always about physical appearance.

When we practice self-love, we will also feel more accepting of our physique and feel beautiful just the way we are. We will also feel more confident and comfortable with our own bodies.

3. Our mindset determines our physical health

The relationship between mindset and physical health is when we have the mindset that exercising, eating healthy foods, and adopting a healthy lifestyle are investments in body health. Then we will start living with a healthier and fitter body.

When psychosocial hazards are not effectively managed, they can negatively impact on organisational measures including productivity, absenteeism and turnover.
