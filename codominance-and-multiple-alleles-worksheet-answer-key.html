3. define and give examples of incomplete dominancecodominancemultiple allelles​


Answer:

In Incomplete Dominance, a heterozygote shows a phenotype that is intermediate between the two homozygous phenotypes. Neither allele is dominant over the other. For example, a pure blue-flowered plant is crossed with a pure yellow-flowered plan, the offspring will produce neither blue nor yellow flowers. Instead, all flowers will be green.

Codominance results when one allele is not dominant over the other. The resulting heterozygotes exhibit the traits of both parents. In codominance, both traits or characteristics of parents were shown. An example, red cows crossed with white cows will have offspring that are roan cows (roan refers to cows with red hair and white blotches).

Multiple Alleles is when more than two alleles control the inheritance of a character. This can also lead to more than two phenotypes expressed. For example, the ABO system of blood groups is controlled by three alleles, only two of which are present in an individual.

a type of inheritance in which two versions (alleles) of the same gene are expressed separately to yield different traits in an individual.

is a form of Gene interaction in which both alleles of a gene at a locus are partially expressed, often resulting in an intermediate or different phenotype. It is also known as partial dominance.

22. Define the following: Heredity Inheritance Variation Incomplete dominance Codominance Multiple Alleles Sex determination​


Explanation:

The genetic heritage that our biological parents have left behind is referred to as heredity. That's why we resemble them. In more detail, it refers to the passing down of traits from one generation to the following. These characteristics can be behavioral or physical, such as eye color, blood type, or an illness.

The foundation of heredity is inheritance, which is the process by which traits are passed on from parents to offspring. The degree to which offspring diverge from their parents is known as variation.

A cross in which each genetically distinct parental contribution results in progeny with an intermediate phenotype causes incomplete dominance.

In terms of genetics, codominance refers to a type of inheritance in which two different expressions (alleles) of the same gene result in distinct features in a person.

As different expressions of the same gene, multiple alleles affect the same trait. The wild-type allele predominates over the mutant alleles in most cases. All other alleles are viewed as variations on the wild type, which is the accepted norm.

Sex chromosomes determine sex in humans (XX females, XY males). Although they have drastically different numbers and sets of genes (approximately 1,000 on the X and only a few dozen on the Y), the X and Y chromosomes are thought to have developed from common autosomes during the early evolution of mammals.


23. 5. What type of inheritance produced when both alleles are fully expressed in anindividual?A. codominanceC. recessive allelesB. multiple allelesD.incomplete dominance​


Answer:

A. Codominance

Step-by-step explanation:

Codominance is a relationship between two versions of a gene. Individuals receive one version of a gene, called an allele, from each parent. If the alleles are different, the dominant allele usually will be expressed, while the effect of the other allele, called recessive, is masked. In codominance, however, neither allele is recessive and the phenotypes of both alleles are expressed.

Codominance is closely related to incomplete dominance. Codominance, in which both alleles are simultaneously expressed in the heterozygote.
