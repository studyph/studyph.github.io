2. In guinea pigs, black hair (B) is dominant to brown hair (b) and short hair (H) is dominant to long hair (h). A heterozygous black, long-haired guinea pig is crossed with a brown, heterozygous short-haired guinea pig. What percentage of the offspring will be black with long hair?​


Answer:

[tex]\large\sf\orange{ANSWER:}[/tex]

[tex]\orange{————————————————}[/tex]

25%

This cross is a dihybrid cross involving two genes

in the guinea pig. One gene is coding for hair

color while the other is coding for hair length.

The allele for black hair (B) is dominant over that

of brown hair (b) while the allele for short hair (H)

is dominant over that of long hair (h).

In a cross involving the parents with genotypes

Bbhh (black long hair) and bbHh (brown short

hair), both parents will undergo meiosis to

produce gametes as follows:

Bbhh: Bh, Bh, bh, bh

bbHh: bH, bH, bh, bh

Using punnet square (attached picture) for this

dihybrid cross, 16 offsprings will be produced.

According to the question, an offspring with

black and long hair phenotype will either have

BBhh or Bbhh genotype.

Note that, in the heterozygous state of a gene,

the dominant allele will express itself.

Checking the drawn punnet square attached,

one will notice that the four circled offsprings

have the above genotypes, hence they are

phenotypically black and long haired.

Since there are 16offsprings and only four are

black & long haired, we then calculate the

percentage by saying:

4/16 x 100 = 25%

Therefore, 25% of the offsprings will be black

and long haired.

30. In guinea pigs, rough coat (R) is dominant over smooth (r) coat; and short hair (S) is dominant over long (s) hair. After doing a cross between two guinea pigs, heterozygous for both traits, write the expected phenotypic and genotypic ratio of the offspring.


Answer:

Let's represent the dominant rough coat allele as R and the recessive smooth coat allele as r. Similarly, let's represent the dominant short hair allele as S and the recessive long hair allele as s.

The parental genotypes can be represented as RrSs and RrSs, since both parents are heterozygous for both traits.

To predict the genotypic and phenotypic ratios of their offspring, we can use a Punnett square, which is a visual tool used to calculate the possible combinations of gametes and the resulting offspring.

Here's the Punnett square for this cross:

| R r

-----|-----------

S | RS RS

s | Rs rs

From this Punnett square, we can see that the possible genotypes of the offspring are:

1/4 (25%) RRSS

1/4 (25%) RrSS

1/4 (25%) RRSs

1/4 (25%) RrSs

And the possible phenotypes of the offspring are:

1/4 (25%) rough coat, short hair

1/4 (25%) rough coat, long hair

1/4 (25%) smooth coat, short hair

1/4 (25%) smooth coat, long hair

Therefore, the expected genotypic ratio of the offspring is 1:2:1:2:4:2:2:4, or simply 1:2:1 for each genotype. And the expected phenotypic ratio is 1:1:1:1, or simply 1:1 for each phenotype.
