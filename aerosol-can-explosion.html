Problem:

What gas best explains the explosion of the heated aerosol container?

Answer

GayLussac's Law:

States that when temperature increases, pressure also increases when volume and the amount of gas are constant. Pressure is directly proportional to kelvin temperature of gas.

When heat up the aerosol container, liquefied propellant turns into gas and these gases increases its kinetic energy and move faster which results to increase in pressure. As the pressure increases and the container cannot contained the pressure inside, it will explode.

6. The gas in an aerosol can is under a pressure of 3. 00 atm at a temperature of 25°C. It is dangerous to dispose of an aerosol can by incineration. What would the pressure in the aerosol can be at a temperature of 845°.


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

To convert the temperature from degree Celsius to kelvin, add 273.15 to the temperature expressed in degree Celsius.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_1 & = \text{3.00 atm} \\ T_1 & = 25^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{298.15 K} \\ T_2 & = 845^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{1,118.15 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final pressure by using Gay-Lussac's law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \frac{P_1}{T_1} & = \frac{P_2}{T_2} \\ P_2T_1 & = P_1T_2 \\ \frac{P_2T_1}{T_1} & = \frac{P_1T_2}{T_1} \\ P_2 & = \frac{P_1T_2}{T_1} \\ & = \frac{(\text{3.00 atm})(\text{1,118.15 K})}{\text{298.15 K}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{11.25 atm}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the pressure in the aerosol would be 11.25 atm at a temperature of 845°C.
