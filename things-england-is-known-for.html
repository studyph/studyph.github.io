14. Take on the role of a New England Puritan. Write a letter to a cousin in England explaining a typical day.


Answer:

Greetings Alfred,

Life in the new colony is good. It is wonderful that, as Puritans, we are free to worship as we please without threat of persecution. It is truly nice to worship and not be distracted by the unnecessary rituals and dress of Catholicism.

As you know, God has chosen those who will be saved and only patience and self-reflection will tell us whether or not we are chosen. In this new colony that we have established, it is much easier to listen to God and live a morally just life.

Here, religion touches on almost all aspects of life. Our government is comprised of a General Court made up of free men who make our laws. But everyone follows the laws of the Bible as well as those created by the Court. Our town hall meetings double as church services. In this way, God guides us through our decisions. Part of me does wish that women could be included in the politics of this colony, but I know that it is best to leave the voting to men. It is also unfortunate that, although we came here to escape religious intolerance, this new community is also intolerant to any form of worship other than strict Puritanism.

I, of course, am satisfied because I know that our way is morally right in God's eyes. However, I know some community members who feel that they would like to worship in other ways.

Signed,

Samuel Dunn Alcott

27th, July of 1631
