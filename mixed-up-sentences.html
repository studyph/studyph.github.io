7. A common grammar mistake is to mix up ‘who’s’ and ‘whose’. Which of these sentences is incorrect? a)Can you tell me who's got my jacket? b)Whose clothes are these? c)Who's going to the party tonight? d?Who's books do these belong to?


Incorrect sentences:

a)Can you tell me who's got my jacket?

d)Who's books do these belong to?


Whose: Use 'whose' to provide information about someone using their possessions.

Example:

Sarah is a businesswoman whose company is very famous.

The family whose house was flooded lost many of their possessions.


Who’s (Who is or Who has): Use 'who' to provide more information about someone.

Example:

Look at the man who is holding up a glass of beer.

He's the murderer who has been on the news recently.
