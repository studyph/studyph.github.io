13. sally has 3:4 as many beads as Kelly.Kelly. has 18 more beads than Sally.Find the average number of beads the girl have.​


Answer:

The average number of beads the girls have is 63.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let's use algebra to solve the problem.

Let's assume that Sally has x beads. According to the problem, Kelly has 3/4 times as many beads as Sally. Therefore, Kelly has:

3/4x beads

We also know that Kelly has 18 more beads than Sally. Therefore:

3/4x = x + 18

Solving for x, we get:

x = 72

This means that Sally has 72 beads and Kelly has:

3/4x = 3/4 * 72 = 54

The total number of beads that the two girls have is:

72 + 54 = 126

The average number of beads that the girls have is:

126 / 2 = 63

Therefore, the average number of beads the girls have is 63.

20. Anna is making bead necklaces. She has 90 green beads and 108 blue beads. What is the greatest number of identical necklaces she can make if she wants to use all of the beads?​


Anna is making bead necklaces. She has 90 green beads and 108 blue beads. The greatest number of identical necklaces she can make if she wants to use all of the beads is 18 necklaces.

To solve this problem, you can use the rule of the greatest common divisor (gcd).

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

Amount of green beads: 90

Amount of blue beads: 108

Question:

What is the greatest number of identical necklaces she can make if she wants to use all of the beads?​

Solution:

Factor of 90 is 1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 9, 10, 15, 18, 30, 45, 90.

Factor of 108 is 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 27, 36, 54, 108.

The greatest common divisor of 90 and 108 is 18.

So, the greatest number of identical necklaces she can make if she wants to use all of the beads is 18 necklaces.

Beadwork has played a central part in the life and culture of Oriental and Occidental Mindoro in the Philippines, particularly for the Mangyan Indigenous People who inhabit both provinces. In the early times, beaded bracelets, headbands, and necklaces were often used as familial identification and to symbolize community identity. Beadwork has played a central part in the life and culture of Oriental and Occidental Mindoro in the Philippines, particularly for the Mangyan Indigenous People who inhabit both provinces. In the early times, beaded bracelets, headbands, and necklaces were often used as familial identification and to symbolize community identity. Beadwork was therefore considered an important art and collective activity among the women who learned to create intricate patterns early in childhood along with manual loom and basket weaving.Unfortunately, the practices of beadwork and loom weaving have declined over the years. Today the art of beadwork and loom weaving can be found among the Mangyan Hanunuo and some Iraya tribes whose communities are often situated in remote and far flung areas of the Mindoro province. There are of course, some communities near town centers which have managed to preserve the art of beadmaking. The practice of wearing beaded accessories has also caught the imagination of townsfolk, who prefer beaded bracelets not only for the distinctive and artistic designs but also as a sign of their affinity with the Mangyan indigenous people

27. This defect occurs when the bead meanders and deviates from the weld line ?


Answer:

Bead meandering (bent/misaligned bead)

Explanation:

This defect occurs when the bead meanders and deviates from the weld line. Possible causes include the wave or curl in the automatically supplied weld wire not being corrected properly or the directions of the weld line and wire curl being at right angles to each other.
