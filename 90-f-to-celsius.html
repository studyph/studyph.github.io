Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

The formula to convert Fahrenheit to Celsius is:

°C = [°F - 32] × (5/9)

Given that, F = 90

C = [90 - 32] × (5/9)

C = 58 × (5/9)

C = 290/9

C = 32.22

and 90 degrees Fahrenheit is equal to 32.22 degrees Celsius.

Thus, 90 °F is equivalent to 32.22 °C.

PROBLEM

The relationship between temperatures in degree Celsius (°C) and in degree Fahrenheit (°F) is given by °C 5/9 (°F – 32). What is the corresponding value in degree Fahrenheit of 37.78°C?

ANSWERThe answer is 100°F

SOLUTION

°C = 5/9 (°F -32)

9/5 °C = °F -32

9/5 °C + 42 = °F

°F = [9(37.78)] / 5+ 32

°F = 68.004 + 32

°F = 100.004

1. The reading in Fahrenheit scale is in 48°F. What is the final reading in Celsius  scale?

SOLUTION:

[tex]C=\frac{^{\circ}F-32}{1.8}\\C=\frac{48-32}{1.8}\\C=\frac{16}{1.8}\\\boxed{\boldsymbol{C=8.9^{\circ}C}}[/tex]

2. What is the final reading in Celsius scale if the temperature is in 90°F?

SOLUTION:

[tex]C=\frac{^{\circ}F-32}{1.8}\\C=\frac{90-32}{1.8}\\C=\frac{58}{1.8}\\\boxed{\boldsymbol{C=32.2^{\circ}C}}[/tex]

3. The temperature of a melted iron is in 98.6°C. What is the temperature in  Fahrenheit scale?

SOLUTION:

[tex]F=1.8(^{\circ}C)+32\\F=1.8(98.6)+32\\F=177.4+32\\\boxed{\boldsymbol{F=209.48^{\circ}F}}[/tex]
