12. employees in the private sectors are allowed to go on strike but those in the public sectors are not. Explain and why? ​


The ability to strike is usually determined by the laws and regulations of a particular country. In many countries, private sector employees have the right to strike as a means of bargaining for better working conditions, wages, and benefits. This is because private sector employers are seen as operating in a competitive market where they have more flexibility in making decisions related to their business operations.

On the other hand, public sector employees, such as government workers, may be subject to laws that prohibit them from going on strike. This is because their employers, often the government, have a monopoly on certain essential services, such as healthcare, education, and law enforcement. A strike in these sectors could cause a disruption of essential services and could negatively impact the public.

However, public sector employees may have other avenues to voice their concerns and negotiate for better working conditions and pay. For example, they may be able to participate in collective bargaining, where they negotiate with their employers for better wages and benefits through a union or other representative body. Additionally, public sector employees may have other means to express their concerns, such as through petitions, rallies, and public demonstrations.

20. what is the difference of private sector and squatter.​


Answer:

Private sector is richernthan the squaters

Answer:

The main difference between private sector and squatter settlements is ownership. Private sector housing is owned by individuals or companies, while squatter settlements are typically unplanned and unauthorized settlements that are occupied by people who do not have legal title to the land. Private sector housing is usually built with the intention of providing a safe and secure environment for its occupants, while squatter settlements often lack basic services such as running water, sanitation, and electricity. Squatters may also be subject to eviction at any time due to their lack of legal title to the land. Private sector housing is usually more expensive than squatter settlements, as it requires more investment in infrastructure and services.
