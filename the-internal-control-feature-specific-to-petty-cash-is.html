18. Why reversing entry is need for unreplenished petty cash fund?


Answer:

Reversing entries are needed for unreplenished petty cash funds because they ensure that the petty cash account accurately reflects the actual amount of cash on hand. When cash is withdrawn from the petty cash fund, it is recorded as a debit in the petty cash account. However, if the cash is not replenished, the account will not accurately reflect the true amount of cash on hand. To correct this, a reversing entry is made to reverse the original debit, so that the account accurately reflects the true amount of cash on hand. This helps to keep the financial records accurate and up-to-date.
