2. Write a three (3) paragrph essay explaining your understanding of Global Culture and Tourism Geography.​


Answer:

Global culture refers to the shared beliefs, values, behaviors, and artifacts that characterize a group or society that spans across the globe. With the advancements in technology, communication, and transportation, the world has become more interconnected, and global culture has emerged. It encompasses various aspects of human life, such as language, art, music, food, fashion, and sports, among others. Global culture has also brought about cultural diffusion, whereby different cultures interact and share their practices, resulting in new hybrid cultures.

Tourism geography, on the other hand, refers to the study of the geographical distribution and patterns of tourism, including its social, economic, and environmental impacts. The tourism industry has grown significantly over the years, and it has become a crucial part of many countries' economies. It involves people traveling to different destinations for leisure, business, or other purposes. Tourism geography helps to understand the factors that influence tourist behavior, such as the natural attractions, cultural heritage, infrastructure, and transportation networks of different destinations.

Global culture and tourism geography are intertwined. Global culture has contributed to the growth of the tourism industry by creating a demand for travel and exploration of different cultures. People travel to experience new cultures, taste different foods, and witness diverse lifestyles. Tourism, in turn, has also influenced global culture by promoting the exchange of ideas, customs, and traditions between different societies. Global culture and tourism geography continue to evolve, driven by the changing preferences of tourists and the constant exchange of cultural practices.

13. What is Philippine Culture, Tourism and Geography?​


Answer:

What is Philippine Culture, Tourism and Geography?

The Philippines is a Southeast Asian country consisting of more than 7,000 islands. It is located in the Pacific Ocean, east of Vietnam and north of Indonesia. The country has a rich and diverse culture that is influenced by its history, geography, and various ethnic groups.

Philippine culture is a mix of indigenous, Spanish, American, and Asian influences. The country has a long history of colonization by various foreign powers, which has contributed to its unique cultural blend. The national language is Filipino, which is based on Tagalog. The country also has more than 100 other indigenous languages.

The Philippines is known for its natural beauty and diverse landscapes, which include beaches, mountains, forests, and islands. The country's geography is characterized by its many islands, which are located along the Pacific Ring of Fire. This makes the Philippines susceptible to earthquakes and volcanic eruptions.

Tourism is an important industry in the Philippines, with millions of visitors each year. The country is known for its beautiful beaches, world-class diving, and rich cultural heritage. Some of the top tourist destinations in the Philippines include Boracay, Palawan, Cebu, and Bohol.

30. Differentiate impact of world tourism on globalization from impact of globalization on world tourism.​


The impact of world tourism on globalization refers to the ways in which the growth of the global tourism industry has contributed to the process of globalization. Globalization is the process by which countries, economies, and societies become more interconnected and interdependent through the increasing exchange of goods, services, ideas, and cultural practices.

World tourism has played a significant role in the process of globalization by facilitating the movement of people across national borders and promoting the exchange of goods and services. The growth of the global tourism industry has led to the development of new transportation and communication technologies, such as low-cost airlines and online booking platforms, which have made it easier and more affordable for people to travel internationally. This has facilitated the growth of global tourism and increased the mobility of people, goods, and ideas.

At the same time, globalization has also had a significant impact on world tourism. The growth of global economic and political integration has led to an increase in the number of people with disposable income and the ability to travel internationally. This has created new opportunities for the global tourism industry, as more people are able to afford to travel and experience different cultures. Additionally, the growth of the global economy has led to the development of new destinations and attractions, such as theme parks, luxury resorts, and cultural festivals, which have increased the appeal of international travel.

Overall, the impact of world tourism on globalization and the impact of globalization on world tourism are intertwined and interconnected. The growth of the global tourism industry has facilitated the process of globalization, while globalization has also created new opportunities for the tourism industry. Both factors have contributed to the increasing interconnectedness of the world and the growth of international travel and exchange.

The growth and development of the tourism industry has a direct impact on the process of globalization. This can be seen in the economic effects of tourism, as well as the cultural and social impacts of increased travel and interaction between people from different countries. The ways in which globalization has affected the tourism industry include factors such as the growing ease of travel and communication, the expansion of international tourism markets, and the development of global tourism networks and destinations. The relationship between world tourism and globalization is complex and multifaceted, with the growth of the tourism industry both driving and being driven by globalization. Globalization has had both positive and negative impacts on the tourism industry and on the countries and communities that host tourists.
