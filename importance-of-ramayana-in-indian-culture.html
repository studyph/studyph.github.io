18. culture and heritage demonstrated in ramayana​


Answer:

Here are some examples of the culture and heritage demonstrated in the Ramayana:

Hindu mythology: The Ramayana is one of the most significant texts in Hindu mythology. It portrays the life of Lord Rama, who is considered to be an incarnation of Lord Vishnu, and his journey to save his wife Sita from the demon king Ravana.

Family values: The Ramayana emphasizes the importance of family values. Lord Rama is depicted as an ideal son, brother, husband, and king. He is always respectful towards his elders, caring towards his siblings, devoted to his wife, and just towards his subjects.

Dharma: The Ramayana emphasizes the concept of dharma, which is the moral and ethical code of conduct in Hinduism. Lord Rama is shown as a person who always follows dharma, even if it means going against his personal desires.

Music and dance: The Ramayana also features music and dance, which are an integral part of Indian culture. The epic includes several songs and hymns that are still sung today, and the dance form of Ramayana is performed in many parts of India.

Art and literature: The Ramayana has inspired a lot of art and literature in India. There are numerous paintings, sculptures, and other art forms that depict scenes from the epic, and several books and plays have been written based on its storyline.

Overall, the Ramayana is a valuable source of cultural and heritage values, and it continues to be an important part of Indian culture even today.

2. Family values: The Ramayana is a story of family values and relationships. It teaches us about the importance of respecting our elders, fulfilling our duties towards our family, and being loyal to our loved ones.
