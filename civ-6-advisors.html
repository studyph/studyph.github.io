2. they are the advisors of the president​


Answer:

Vice President: Constitutionally, the only official functions prescribed for the Office of the Vice President is his or her role as President of the Senate. 

Secretary of State: The Secretary of Stateserves as the head of the US Department of State.

Secretary of the Treasury: The Secretary of the Treasury is in charge of the US Department of the Treasury, which is the principal economic advisor to the President and plays a critical role in policy-making by bringing an economic and government financial policy perspective to issues facing the government.

Secretary of Defense: The Secretary of Defense is the leader and chief executive officer of the Department of Defense.

Attorney General: The Attorney General is the head of the US Department of Justice. They’re concerned with legal affairs, and serve as the chief law enforcement office and lawyer of the United States Government. 

Secretary of the Interior: The US Department of the Interior is responsible for managing and conserving most of the land and natural resources that are under the control of the Federal Government.

Secretary of Agriculture: The Department of Agriculture encompasses the United States Forest Service, the United States Food Safety and Inspection Service, the Food Stamp Program, and the US Cooperative State Research, Education, and Extension Service.

Secretary of Commerce: The Secretary of Commerce promotes American businesses and industries.

Secretary of Labor: The US Department of Labor heads the Department of Labor, enforces and suggests laws involving unions, the workplace, and any other issues involving any form of business-person controversies.

Secretary of Health and Human Services: The Secretary of HHS advises the President on matters of health, welfare, and income security programs. 

Secretary of Housing and Urban Development: The Department of Housing and Urban Development strives to create quality affordable homes for every American.

Secretary of Transportation: The Department of Transportation is in charge of creating and maintaining the United States’ transportation system. 

Secretary of Energy: The Energy Departmentdeals with the science of energy and how that relates to the environment and technology solutions.

The Secretary of Education:oversees the US Department of Education and deals with the Federal Government’s influence over education policy.

Secretary of Veterans Affairs: The US Department of Veterans Affairs deals with veterans’ benefits, health care, and national memorials and cemeteries.

Secretary of Homeland Security: The newest cabinet position is the Secretary of Homeland Security. They deal with anti-terrorism, border security, immigration and customs, cybersecurity, and disaster prevention and management.
