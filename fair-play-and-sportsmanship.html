5. how can you know sportsmanship in playing "tumbang preso"​


Answer:Tumbang Preso

Tumbang Preso is not just a game. It’s a fun outdoor physical activity that helps players develop strategic planning skills and agility.

Long before the internet and gadgets were introduced to them, Filipino kids were already playing this social game called Tumbang Preso.

Tumbang Preso or Tumba lata (Tagalog), or Bato Lata (Bisaya) literally means “Knock Down the Prisoner” or “Knock Down the Can” in English. This game is played in backyards, open fields, or parks. And, in places where vehicular traffic is minimal, kids bring their tumbang preso right in the middle of the road.

In addition, the game involves much physical activity like running. And, most importantly, Tumbang Preso also calls for skills in strategic planning and agility to avoid being tagged.

The game’s primary objective is to knock down the guarded “prisoner” or “can”. Players use a certain tool to hit the prisoner. And they retrieve the same tool without being caught by the guard.Equipment Needed for Tumbang Preso a small can or any small cylindrical object, and;a slipper or a small flat stone. This tool is called pamatoIdeally, this game involves a maximum of 9 players. Although kids usually adjust the number of participants. One of the players serves as the guard or “It”, and the rest are the hitters.Tips on Playing Tumbang PresoChoose a pamato that is right for you. Practice your aim.Study the movement of your opponentsAct fast.Develop quick thinking

Older kids and adults also love playing Tumbang Preso. It’s a fun game, especially during family gatherings. How about you? Try Tumbang Preso with your officemates or fellow homemakers one weekend.

25. How does playing games enhance and improve skills, confidence, cooperation, and sportsmanship? (5 sentence)


Playing games can enhance and improve skills, confidence, cooperation, and sportsmanship in a number of ways. Firstly, games can provide opportunities for individuals to develop and practice specific skills such as hand-eye coordination, strategic thinking, and problem-solving. These skills can then be transferred to other areas of life, such as school or work.

Secondly, games can help individuals build confidence by providing opportunities for success and accomplishment. When individuals see that they can succeed at a particular game, they may feel more confident in other areas of their life as well.

Thirdly, games often require cooperation and teamwork, which can help individuals develop their social skills and learn how to work effectively with others. Through playing games, individuals can learn to communicate, collaborate, and compromise with others.

Fourthly, games can teach individuals about sportsmanship and fair play. By learning to win and lose gracefully, individuals can develop a sense of respect for themselves and others, and learn to appreciate the value of fair competition.

Lastly, playing games can be a fun and enjoyable way to learn and develop new skills, while also providing opportunities to socialize and make new friends. All of these benefits can contribute to a more well-rounded and successful individual.
