18. ACTIVITY 4: LET'S PRACTICE! Direction: Illustrate and solve the following problems. 1. Quadrilateral MECQ is a trapezoid with ME II QC and AB as the median. If ME=13 and QC=24, what is AB? 2. One diagonal of an isosceles trapezoid measures 72 cm and one leg measures 40 cm. What is the length of the other diagonal?​


Answer: 1. Given: ME=13, QC=24

To find: AB

Since AB is the median, it is equal to the average of the two parallel sides, ME and QC.

AB = (ME + QC)/2

AB = (13 + 24)/2

AB = 37/2

AB = 18.5

Therefore, the length of AB is 18.5.

2. Given: One diagonal = 72 cm, leg = 40 cm

To find: Length of the other diagonal

Let the other diagonal be x.

Since the trapezoid is isosceles, the diagonals are equal in length.

x = 72 cm

The two legs and one diagonal form a right triangle.

Using the Pythagorean theorem,

leg^2 + (1/2)(x)^2 = diagonal^2

(40 cm)^2 + (1/2)(72 cm)^2 = diagonal^2

1600 cm^2 + 2592 cm^2/4 = diagonal^2

6400 cm^2 + 2592 cm^2 = 4(diagonal^2)

8992 cm^2 = 4(diagonal^2)

diagonal^2 = 8992 cm^2/4

diagonal^2 = 2248 cm^2

diagonal = sqrt(2248)

diagonal = 47.43

Therefore, the length of the other diagonal is approximately 47.43 cm.
