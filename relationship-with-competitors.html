1. Explain the needs and it's relationship to price, to competitors and manufacturers​


My Answer:

The needs of consumers are the underlying motivation for their purchase decisions. When a consumer needs a product or service, they are more likely to be willing to pay a higher price for it. Therefore, the price of a product or service is often closely related to the needs of the consumers.

Competition also plays a significant role in the price of a product. If there are many competitors offering similar products or services, the price tends to be lower due to the increased competition. On the other hand, if a product is unique or there are few competitors, the price tends to be higher.

Manufacturers also consider the needs of consumers when setting prices for their products. They may invest in research and development to create new products that fulfill the needs of consumers, or improve existing products to better meet those needs. In addition, the cost of producing a product is also a factor in determining its price. If the cost of producing a product is high, the price will also be high to cover the cost of production.

In summary, the needs of consumers are an important consideration in setting the price of a product or service, as it determines their willingness to pay for it. Competition also affects the price, with increased competition leading to lower prices. Manufacturers also take into account consumer needs when setting prices and may invest in research and development to improve products.
