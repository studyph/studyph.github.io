13. research introduction about effects of racial Discrimination​


Answer:

Many racial and ethnic groups in the United States, including blacks, Hispanics, Asians, American Indians, and others, have historically faced severe discrimination—pervasive and open denial of civil, social, political, educational, and economic opportunities. Today, large differences among racial and ethnic groups continue to exist in employment, income and wealth, housing, education, criminal justice, health, and other areas. While many factors may contribute to such differences, their size and extent suggest that various forms of discriminatory treatment persist in U.S. society and serve to undercut the achievement of equal opportunity.

Measuring Racial Discrimination considers the definition of race and racial discrimination, reviews the existing techniques used to measure racial discrimination, and identifies new tools and areas for future research. The book conducts a thorough evaluation of current methodologies for a wide range of circumstances in which racial discrimination may occur, and makes recommendations on how to better assess the presence and effects of discrimination.

15. Research about the current situation of prejudice, discrimination. I stereotype in the PHILIPPINES​


Answer:

Prejudice and discrimination are persistent social issues not only in the Philippines but also in many other parts of the world. Prejudice refers to a preconceived opinion or attitude towards a group of people, while discrimination refers to the unfair treatment of individuals or groups based on their membership in that group. Stereotyping, on the other hand, is a cognitive process that involves generalizing characteristics about a group of people, often based on incomplete or inaccurate information.

In the Philippines, prejudice and discrimination manifest in various ways, including:

Ethnic and religious discrimination: Discrimination based on ethnicity and religion is still prevalent in the Philippines. Muslims, for example, are often subjected to discrimination and stereotypes that portray them as violent or dangerous.

Gender discrimination: Gender-based discrimination is also a significant problem in the Philippines. Women face various forms of discrimination, including lower pay for the same work as men, limited opportunities for career advancement, and sexual harassment.

Age discrimination: Ageism is also a problem in the Philippines, particularly in the workplace. Older workers often face discrimination in hiring and promotion decisions, despite having the necessary skills and experience.

LGBTQ+ discrimination: Members of the LGBTQ+ community in the Philippines continue to face discrimination and prejudice. Many are denied job opportunities, subjected to bullying and harassment, and denied access to basic services.

Efforts to address prejudice and discrimination in the Philippines include laws such as the Anti-Discrimination Bill, which seeks to protect individuals from discrimination based on their sexual orientation, gender identity, and expression. However, despite these efforts, prejudice, and discrimination continue to be significant challenges that require ongoing attention and action. Education and awareness-raising campaigns can help address stereotypes and promote acceptance and inclusion of diverse groups.>-<

This discrimination can take many forms, including verbal harassment, physical violence, workplace discrimination, and denial of services. This discrimination is often rooted in prejudice, fear, and misinformation about LGBTQ individuals and their lifestyles. •Explore the historical origins of racial discrimination, including its roots in colonialism, slavery, segregation, and other historical events.

•Utilize primary sources such as historical documents, diaries, letters, and newspapers from the relevant time periods to understand the historical context and attitudes towards racial discrimination.

III. Social, Economic, and Political Impacts of Racial Discrimination

•Discuss the social, economic, and political impacts of racial discrimination on individuals, communities, and society as a whole.

•Utilize secondary sources such as academic journals, books, reports, and statistical data to provide evidence and analysis on the effects of racial discrimination in various domains, such as education, employment, healthcare, housing, and criminal justice.

IV. Efforts to Combat Racial Discrimination

•Explore the various efforts, movements, and policies aimed at combating racial discrimination, including civil rights movements, anti-discrimination laws, diversity and inclusion initiatives, and social justice advocacy.

•Utilize primary sources such as speeches, interviews, and testimonies from activists, policymakers, and individuals affected by racial discrimination to understand the perspectives and experiences of those involved in the fight against racial discrimination.

V. Conclusion

•Summarize the main findings and insights from the research.

•Discuss the current status and future prospects of efforts to combat racial discrimination.
