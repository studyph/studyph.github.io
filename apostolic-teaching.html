12. Apostolicity[1] Apostolicity is the mark by which the Church of today is recognized as identical with the Church founded by Jesus Christ upon the Apostles. It is of great importance because it is the surest indication of the true Church of Christ, it is most easily examined, and it virtually contains the other three marks, namely, Unity, Sanctity, and Catholicity. Either the word "Christian" or "Apostolic", might be used to express the identity between the Church of today and the primitive Church. The term "Apostolic" is preferred because it indicates a correlation between Christ and the Apostles, showing the relation of the Church both to Christ, the founder, and to the Apostles, upon whom He founded it. "Apostle" is one sent, sent by authority of Jesus Christ to continue His Mission upon earth, especially a member of the original band of teachers known as the Twelve Apostles. Therefore, the Church is called Apostolic, because it was founded by Jesus Christ upon the Apostles. Apostolicity of doctrine and mission is necessary. Apostolicity of doctrine requires that the deposit of faith committed to the Apostles shall remain unchanged. Since the Church is infallible in its teaching, it follows that if the Church of Christ still exists it must be teaching His doctrine. Why is apostolicity important particularly in the current time? What are the criteria to determine apostolicity? Elaborate.


Answer:

Apostolicity is important in the current time because it is a way for us to ensure that the teachings of Jesus remain intact and unchanged. Without Apostolicity, there is no assurance that what is being taught is true and accurate. It is a way to help determine which church or denomination has the most reliable teachings.

The criteria to determine apostolicity include:

1. Authority: The Church must have the same authority as the original church founded by Jesus Christ, in accordance with his teachings and the authority he gave to the Apostles.

2. Doctrine: The Church must follow and maintain the teachings of Jesus as taught by the Apostles. It should not deviate from those teachings.

3. Succession: The Church must have unbroken succession of bishops who can trace their authority back to the original Apostles.

4. Unity: The Church must have a unified and unified rule within the institution and have its own hierarchy in place.

5. Practices: The church must follow the same practices as those found in the early Church. These include baptism, communion, confirmation, penance and more.

6. Catholicity: The Church must be catholic, meaning it must have members from all walks of life, from all races, countries and cultures.

7. Holiness: The Church must be holy and separated from the world by its faith and practices.

Apostolicity is important in maintaining a true adherence to the teachings and authority of Jesus Christ in the beliefs of a Church or denomination. By holding true to the criteria of Apostolicity, the Church or denomination is able to maintain its legitimacy, its fullness and its integrity. Through the criteria of apostolicity, the Church ensures that it upholds the same authority, doctrine, succession, unity, practices, catholicity, and holiness upon which it was founded.

14. what books are in the category of apostolic letter?name the books and why are they called apostolic letter?​


Answer:

Pauline Epistles, Letter to Hebrews, and General Epistles.

Pauline Epistles are letters written by Apostle Paul namely:

Romans, 1st Corinthians, 2nd Corinthians, Galatians, Ephesians, Philippians, Colossians, 1st Thessalonians, 2nd Thessalonians, 1st Timothy, 2nd Timothy, Titus, and Philemon.

General Epistles are letters written by other Apostles such as:

James, 1st Peter, 2nd Peter, 1st John, 2nd John, 3rd John, and Jude.

The letter to Hebrews is also an epistle but the author is still unknown and widely debated.

These epistles are called Apostolic letters simply because they are letters written by the Apostles of Jesus Christ.
