13. What is the proper scientific notation for 0.000476?


the scientific notation of 0.000476 = 4.76 × 10^-4Hello!
Order of magnitude of a measure is an estimate of the base power 10 closest to a given measure.

The scientific notation (N) of 0.000476 (x), being  (number of decimal places) y, knowing that the scientific notation according to the rule of scientific notation, before the comma can only have the numbers 1 to 9, let's see:
[tex]N = x*10^y[/tex]

[tex]N = 0,000476*10^y[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{N = 4,76*10^{-4}}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

A line segment is a part of a line that is bounded by two end points, and contains every point on the line that is between its end points. A ray can be defined as a part of a line that has a fixed starting point but no end point. It can extend infinitely in one direction. Parallel lines are lines in a plane which do not meet; that is, two straight lines in a plane that do not intersect at any point are said to be parallel. When two or more lines cross each other in a plane, they are called intersecting lines. Types of Angles Based on Measurement

Angles are geometric figures formed by two intersecting rays. The intersection of two rays is called the vertex. Angles can be named using the vertex or the names of the rays. There are several types of angles based on measurement which include: acute, right, obtuse, and straight. There are also several types of angles based on their relationship with one another: complementary, vertical, adjacent, corresponding, and supplementary.
