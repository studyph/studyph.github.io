7. how do fraudsters use social media to steal information​


Answer:

Here are three ways that fraudsters use Social Media to gather information for phishing schemes and to commit fraud:

•web scraping

Social media presents an opportunity for fraudsters to harvest huge amounts of publicly available personal data. Once data is publicly shared, fraudsters can use bots to collect and compile it. This technique, called web scraping, allows fraudsters to efficiently build profiles of their targets.

•Fake Profiles

Fake profiles are another way fraudsters gather more data from unsuspecting users. To make their fake profiles look real, fraudsters will generally use stolen images and canned text to flesh out their account. Once a fraudsters has gained enough clout to pass as a real user, they will begin adding targets to their fake profiles. Once a fraudster has been added to a real user’s account, they can quickly scrape all of the data and even use that data to even create a duplicate account.

•Account Takeovers and Impersonators

According to Kaspersky Labs, 33% of all phishing sites attempted to impersonate social networks. When fraudsters takeover a social media account they scrape the data of friends and use it for phishing attempts. Once a social media account has been taken over, fraudsters can also use it for OAuth login

Identity Theft Through Social Networking Sites

Social networking sites are used by fraudsters to recognise victims and steal their personal details. Consider these common ways offenders can exploit social media before you post any sensitive details or even entertain an invitation to communicate with someone you don't know in real life.

Targeted ads focused on personal knowledge is how social media platforms make money. As a result, they allow people to have as much detail as possible while registering. Users are vulnerable to identity theft and fraud due to a lack of regulatory regulation, industry regulations, or incentives to inform users on authentication, privacy, and identity safety.

With the increased use of social media around the world, there are more ways than ever to hack identity or commit fraud online. Criminals may use status messages shared on social networking platforms, for example. You've opened yourself up to robbery if you announce that you'll be out of town on holiday.

Social networking sites, on the other hand, have the highest capacity for violence. 26. Carlos, one of the best basketball players in his team, makes an average of 20 points, 5 blocks, 10 rebounds, 15 assists and 5 steals per game. Find the ratio of the following: 1. Steals to rebound 2. Point to assist 3. Steals to block


Answer:

To find the ratio of steals to rebounds, divide the number of steals by the number of rebounds: 5 steals / 10 rebounds = 1:2

To find the ratio of points to assists, divide the number of points by the number of assists: 20 points / 15 assists = 4:3

To find the ratio of steals to blocks, divide the number of steals by the number of blocks: 5 steals / 5 blocks = 1:1
