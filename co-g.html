5. Consider a solution of aqueous ammonium carbonate prepared by mixing 12.00 g of (NH₄)₂CO₃ with 125 g of water(density = 1.00 g/mL) to make 127 mL of solution. Calculate (a) % by mass of (NH₄)₂CO₃ (b) mole fraction of (NH₄)₂CO₃ (c) molarity of the solution (d) molality of the solution (e) parts per million of (NH₄)₂CO₃.


Given:

mass of solute = 12.00 g

mass of solvent = 125 g = 0.125 kg

density of solvent = 1.00 g/mL

volume of solution = 127 mL = 0.127 L

solute: (NH₄)₂CO₃

solvent: water

Required:

percent by mass

mole fraction of solute

molarity

molality

parts per million

Solution: (a)

[tex]\text{percent by mass} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solute + mass of solvent}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\text{percent by mass} = \frac{\text{12.00 g}}{\text{12.00 g + 125.0 g}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{percent by mass} = 8.76\%}[/tex]

Solution: (b)

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of solute and solvent.

For solute

molar mass of solute = (14.01 g/mol × 2) + (1.008 g/mol × 8) + (12.01 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 3)

molar mass of solute = 96.094 g/mol

For solvent

molar mass of solvent = (1.008 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of solvent = 18.016 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute and solvent.

For solute

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{12.00 g}}{\text{96.094 g/mol}}[/tex]

moles of solute = 0.1248777 mol

For solvent

[tex]\text{moles of solvent} = \frac{\text{mass of solvent}}{\text{molar mass of solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solvent} = \frac{\text{125 g}}{\text{18.016 g/mol}}[/tex]

moles of solvent = 6.9382771 mol

Step 3: Calculate the mole fraction of solute.

[tex]\text{mole fraction} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{moles of solute + moles of solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mole fraction} = \frac{\text{0.1248777 mol}}{\text{0.1248777 mol + 6.9382771 mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mole fraction} = 0.0177}[/tex]

Solution: (c)

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{0.1248777 mol}}{\text{0.127 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity} = 0.983 \: M}[/tex]

Solution: (d)

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{mass of solvent (kg)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{0.1248777 mol}}{\text{0.125 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molality} = 0.999 \: m}[/tex]

Solution: (e)

[tex]\text{parts per million by mass} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solute + mass of solvent}} × 10^{6}[/tex]

[tex]\text{parts per million by mass} = \frac{\text{12.00 g}}{\text{12.00 g + 125 g}} × 10^{6}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{parts per million by mass} = 8.76 × 10^{4} \: \text{ppm}}[/tex]

6. What is the molality of a solution containing 7.78 g of urea [(NH₂)₂CO] in 203 g of water?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & mass_{\text{solute}} = \text{7.78 g} \\ & MM_{\text{solute}} = \text{60.055 g/mol} \\ & mass_{\text{solvent}} = \text{203 g = 0.203 kg} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{mass_{\text{solute}}}{MM_{\text{solute}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{7.78 g}}{\text{60.055 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.12955 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molality of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} m & = \frac{n_{\text{solute}}}{mass_{\text{solvent}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.12955 mol}}{\text{0.203 kg}} \\ & = \text{0.638 mol/kg} \\ & = \boxed{0.638 \: m} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molality of the solution is 0.638 m.

10. A gas micture contains for 4.0g of CO(g) 11.0 of CO(g) and 2.0 of CH(g) in a 50.0 L vessel, Calculate the partial pressure of each gas in the mixture.​


Answer:

The partial pressure of each gas in a mixture is proportional to its mole fraction.

n = m / M

M (CO) = 28 g/mol

M (CO2) = 44 g/mol

M (CH4) = 16.04 g/mol

n (CO) = 4 / 28 = 0.14 mol

n (CO2) = 11 / 44 = 0.25 mol

n (CH4) = 2 / 16.04 = 0.13 mol

Sum of n = 0.14 + 0.25 + 0.13 = 0.52 moles

CO mole fraction = 0.14 / 0.52 = 0.27

CO2 mole fraction = 0.25 / 0.52 = 0.48

CH4 mole fraction = 0.13 / 0.52 = 0.25

29. . CHEMISTRY A: CO(g) + O2(g) – CO2(g) AHA = -283 kJ B: N2(g) + O2(g) → 2NO(g) AHB = 180.6 kJ C: CO(g) + NO(g) – CO2(g) + N2(9) AHc = ? 6. Two gaseous pollutants that form in auto exhaust are CO and NO. An environmental chemistp by is studying ways to convert them to less harmful gases through equation C shown above. What is the value of AHcgiven the information in equations A and B?


Answer:

Explanation:

The value of AHc, the standard enthalpy change for reaction C, can be calculated by adding the standard enthalpy changes of reactions A and B:

AHc = AHf (products) - AHf (reactants)

For reaction C, the products are CO2(g) and N2(g), and the reactants are CO(g) and NO(g). We can use the standard enthalpy of formation values from reactions A and B to calculate AHc:

AHc = [AHf (CO2(g)) + AHf (N2(g))] - [AHf (CO(g)) + AHf (NO(g))]

AHc = [-283 kJ + 0 kJ] - [0 kJ + 180.6 kJ]

AHc = -283 kJ - 180.6 kJ = -463.6 kJ

So, the value of AHc, the standard enthalpy change for reaction C, is -463.6 kJ.
