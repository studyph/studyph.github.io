8. which shows a predator in relationship​


Answer: Predation

Predation is a ecological relationship that the predator east or kills the prey.

Predator -The one that eats another animal.

Prey -The animal that is getting eaten by the predator.

Example: Cat and Mouse (The cat is predator while the mouse is the prey)

Additional Info:Mutualism

Mutualism is a symbiotic relationship that two organisms benefits.

Example: Butterfly and Flower (The butter gets nectar from the flower and the flower pollinates.)

Parasitism

Parasitism is a symbiotic relationship which the other benefits while the other is harmed.

Example: Mosquito and Human (The mosquito drinks the blood of the human so the human is harmed.)

Competition

Competition is a symbiotic relationship which there are 2 or more organisms race to get something to eat.

Example: Deer, elk, and grass (The deer and elk competes to eat the grass)

Commensalism

Commensalism is a symbiotic relationship which the other organism benefits while the other is not harmed.

Example: Frog and Plant (The frog hides in the leaves of the plant to get protected but the plant did not benefit.)

20. WHAT IS PREDATIONpredation a relationship between anprey and predatora predator hunts prey for its food they gets benefited from the process but the prey is harmed or even eaten​


In a biological interaction known as predation, one organism—the predator—kills and consumes another—its prey. It belongs to a group of widespread feeding habits that also includes parasitism, micropredation, and parasitoidism.

One organism kills and eats another during predation. To the detriment of the organism being consumed, the prey, predation supplies energy to prolong life and encourage reproduction of the organism doing the killing, the predator. Organisms are affected by predation on two ecological scales. Because the prey organism will never reproduce again, there is a sudden fall in fitness at the level of the individual, as indicated by its lifetime reproductive success. Predation decreases the size of the prey population at the level of the community.

As an example, consider how wolves hunt moose, owls hunt mice, or shrews hunt worms and other insects. The consumption of a larger individual by numerous smaller ones occurs in less visible carnivorous relationships. Social carnivores like lions, hyenas, and wolves frequently engage in this type of group predation. Ants and social spiders both engage in group hunting. But this is only a portion of the story.

An ecological relationship is the relationship between an organism in its ecosystem. All organisms in an ecosystem are connected in one way or another. Each interaction depends on the one before it. Each population interacts with one another in a complex web of relations. Ecological relationships help better to describe how they are connected.

There are ecological relationships in which two are oppositional and four are symbiotic. The oppositional relationships are predationand competition. The symbioticrelationships are mutualism, commensalism, amensalism , neutralism , cooperation and parasitism.

The ecological relationship an organism has depends on the way the organism adapted to its environmental pressures on evolutionary basis.
