4. differentiate xylem and phloem in terms of their function ​


Answer:

The vascular system is comprised of two main types of tissue: the xylem and the phloem. The xylem distributes water and dissolved minerals upward through the plant, from the roots to the leaves. The phloem carries food downward from the leaves to the roots.


5. What is Pholeum and xylem???


Xylem carries water and mineral salts from the roots going to other parts of the plant. Phloem delivers sucrose and amino acids between leaves and other parts of the plant. The xylem and phloem are usually found together in vascular bundles and arranged in positions. ‘Sap’ is the substances transported around a plant by its xylem and phloem. Generally, xylem and phloem transport water, sugars, and other important substances to all plant parts.

Components of Xylem  and PhloemXylem is composed of various specialized cells: Tracheary elements Xylem parenchyma cells Xylem fiber cells

Phloem is composed of various specialized cells: Sieve tubesCompanion cells  Phloem fibers Phloem parenchyma cells

Xylem is the specialised tissue of vascular plants that transports water and nutrients from the plant–soil interface to stems and leaves, and provides mechanical support and storage. ... 7. what makes the xylem cell​


Mature xylem is made up of dead cells that do not have cell contents, while phloem contains living cells (albeit without nuclei). The structure of xylem and phloem is also different. While xylem is made up of tracheids and vessels, phloem is made up of sieve tubes which have many holes for transporting nutrients.

DIFFERENTIATE THE FUNCTION OF XYLEM AND PHLOEM

Answer:

The xylem is placed on the leaf's adaxial surface, while the phloem is located on the leaf's abaxial surface. Water, nutrients, and food are transported throughout the plant by xylem and phloem. Phloem, on the other hand, transports the food prepared by the leaves to various areas of the plant. A continuous chain of dead cells known as vessel elements makes up xylem cells. Organelles do not exist in the vessel elements. The components of xylem tissues are lignified and scalarified to a high degree. As a result, xylem cells are regarded dead, whereas the cells that make up phloem tissues must be alive in order to support active sucrose transport throughout the plant.
