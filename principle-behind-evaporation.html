What was left behind after all the water evaporated?ANSWER

When evaporation occurs, usually the water molecules gain kinetic energy because of heat, causing these molecules to expand and become less denser.

If water molecules are less denser and lighter, all of it will rise up and evaporate in the form of water vapor (gas water), and it will leave all the solid residue like sand or solute that is originally mixed or dissolved with that water.

11. Explain the principle behind FIFO.


The principle of the FIFO system, or First in, First out, is a system of goods storage that is carried out with a system of goods that enter first, which is also issued first. So, the release of this item is done sequentially or according to chronology. The FIFO system is typically used for goods that are less durable or, if stored for an extended period of time, will be damaged or of lower quality.

 

The advantages of the FIFO system are:

- The goods' quality is better maintained.

With the FIFO system, it is expected that the goods that enter first are also the first to leave. This means that the goods will not be stored in the warehouse for too long. So, the item with the earliest expiry date will also come out the earliest. That way, the quality of goods can be more assured and we can anticipate the occurrence of mass damage to goods.

- Greater price certainty

Besides being able to maintain the quality of goods, another advantage of the FIFO system is the stability of the prices of the goods stored. Please note that the price can not always be the same. Sometimes the price goes up, and sometimes it goes down.

30. The philosopher behind "context principle." 


Answer:GOTTLOB FREGE

The context principle is one of Gottlob Frege's "three fundamental principles" for philosophical analysis, first discussed in his Introduction to The Foundations of Arithmetic (Grundlagen der Arithmetik, 1884).

Frege argued that many philosophical errors, especially those related to psychologism in the philosophy of logic and philosophy of mathematics, could be avoided by adhering carefully to the context principle.
