18. Possible effects of Humss students with disabilities?​


Answer:

Intellectual DisabilitiesLearning DisabilitiesEmotional/Behavioral DisabilitiesPhysical DisabilitiesOther Health ImpairmentAutism Spectrum Disorder

Explanation:

Intellectual disability has an effect on gross motor abilities, language, and social interaction. Break down tasks, teach one idea at a time, or give several chances for achievement to ensure student success.

Learning Disabilities: Affects linguistic milestones and social relationships. Implement instructional cues or emphasize instructions to ensure student success. Concentrate on a single subject at a time to enhance student attention to teaching.

Disabilities of the emotional/behavioural type impair social interactions and connections with peers, as well as cognitive performance. Incorporate all team members, family members, instructors, and all faculty to ensure student achievement. Incorporate a holistic strategy that places an emphasis on the whole student and their team.

Physical disabilities have an effect on both gross and fine motor skills. Consider the classroom environment and its effect on the students' learning. Make use of adaptable technology, such as touch displays.

Other health impairments: impaired communication, cognition, and social interaction. Allow more time for assignment completion, establish daily and weekly plans, and define clear goals to ensure student success.

Autism Spectrum Disorder has an effect on gross motor, fine motor, linguistic, social, and cognitive abilities. Create a program that incorporates visual and instructional cues to ensure student success. Establish daily objectives and routines that provide various avenues for achievement.

25. Do you favor Inclusive for students with disabilities?​


Answer:

Yes

Explanation:

Because it can benefit children especially to those who have disabilities. Inclusive education will not only benefit the students with disabilities but also create an environment in which every student, including those who do not have disabilities, has the opportunity to flourish. One of the most important principles of inclusive education is that no two learners are alike, and so inclusive schools place great importance on creating opportunities for students to learn and be assessed in a variety of ways. Certainly this enhances the way in which educators provide supports and accommodations for students with disabilities, but it also diversifies the educational experience of all students.

Another important factor in effective inclusive education is the implementation of consistent behavioral supports throughout the learning environment.  This consistency is essential for the success of students with emotional or behavioral disabilities in the general education environment, but school-wide behavioral supports also help to establish high expectations throughout the school community as a whole.

Inclusive education for students with disabilities can only be successful when those students feel that they are truly a part of the school community.  This requires open and honest discussion about difference, and an institutional respect for people of all backgrounds and abilities

As such, effective models of inclusive education not only benefit students with disabilities, but also create an environment in which every student, including those who do not have disabilities, has the opportunity to flourish.
