30. 7. Which of the following is an improper fraction? a. 10/67 b. 42/8 c. 56/63 pahelp po​


Answer:

A proper fraction is one in which the numerator is less than the denominator. Since 8 is not less than 8, 8/8 is not a proper fraction. Since 8/8 is not proper, it is an improper fraction. 8/8 is equal to 1 because it means that the whole is divided into 8 parts and the fraction represents 8 of those parts.An improper fraction is a fraction whose numerator is greater than the denominator. Hence, here 72 and 53 are improper fractions.The numerator of all the other fractions are smaller than the denominator. Thus, they are Proper fractions. Option (c) has 7 as its numerator and 4 as its denominator. Since 7 > 4, thus it is an Improper fraction.

Step-by-step explanation:

Explanation :-

The numerator of all the other fractions are smaller than the denominator. Thus, they are Proper fractions. Option (c) has 7 as its numerator and 4 as its denominator. Since 7 > 4, thus it is an Improper fraction.

Extra Information :-

A fraction can be identified as proper or improper by comparing the numerator and the denominator.

Fractions that are less than one, i.e, the numerator is less than the denominator, are known as Proper fractions.

In the same way, fractions that are more than one, i.e, the numerator is greater than the denominator, are known as Improper fractions.

Also, an Improper fraction can be expressed as a mixed fraction.
