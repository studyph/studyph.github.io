     Dr. Jose N. Rodriguez Memorial Hospital (DJNRMH), formerly known as Tala Leprosarium, was founded in 1940 to provide care for Hansen's Disease patients throughout the entire Luzon region. When there was a high success rate of treatment of the initial Hansen patients from research and the progress of methods done within the current medical practice, the hospital started treating general medical cases (non-Disease Hansen's patients) in 1970. The hospital then contemplated admitting general cases due to the sharp decline in Hansen patients. Currently, the hospital serves as both the primary training and research facility for leprosy care and management in the Philippines as well as the primary referral hospital for leprosy patients. It also meets the demands of the locals of Tala and the surrounding areas in terms of public health.

    Dr Jose N Rodriguez Memorial Hospital hopes to develop into a Tertiary General Hospital and a Center for Leprosy Patients' Care. The Philippine Congress is now debating House Bill No. 1395, which would convert 200 of the 2,000 beds at DJNRMH to tertiary general healthcare. DJNRMH's 2,000 beds are now being used for custodial care (1,800 beds) and general care for non-leprosy clients while waiting for this bill to be approved (200 beds). Other services offered by the hospital include radiology, occupational therapy, physical therapy, laboratory testing, and medical-social support for the right patients.

Philippines begins legally rolling out first COVID-19 vaccines

The Philippine government starts giving the first shots of a Corona Virus vaccine on Monday, March 1, Nurse Chareluck Santos legally administers the first Covid-19 vaccine in the country to Dr. Gerardo Legaspi, director of the University of the Philippines- Philippine General Hospital.

Food and Drug Administration Director, General Eric Domingo, Government Medical Adviser Dr. Edsel Salvaña and vaccine czar Carlito Galvez Jr. were also vaccinated, Monday. Vaccinations also takes place among health workers at the Lung Center of the Philippines, Dr. Jose N. Rodriguez Memorial Medical Center and Sanitarium (Tala), Veterans Memorial Medical Center, Philippine National Police General Hospital, and Victoriano Luna Medical Center.

The vaccinations are part of the first batch of vaccines to arrive from China, Sunday. The 600, 000 doses of Sinovac's Corona vaccine are donated from the Chinese government. The Sinovac vaccine had an efficacy of 50.4% from trials in Brazil involving health workers. But phase 3 trials showed an efficacy rate of 65.3% to 91.2% among healthy individuals aged 18 to 59 years sold. The World Helath Organization (WHO) early said a 50% efficacy rating is acceptable for vaccines. Though the Duterte government targeted to start distributing vaccines by February, The Philippines is now the last country in Southeast Asia to start vaccinating its residents.

Meantime, Preside Duterte says he will go to China to personally thank Chines ident Jinping for their vaccine donation when the pandemic settles down. "I want to do it personally because China has been good and the way that they are helping us right now- I suppose the debt of gratitude should be paid by a personal visit." In, related news, the arrival of over half a million vaccine doses of the AstraZeneca vaccine from the global COVAX facility on Monday is postponed. problem. WHO said they encountered problems with supply, so it will be delayed for about a week.
