15. if the width of a rectangle is 54 cm and the perimeter of the rectangle is 2.94 m, then what is the ratio of the width of the rectangle to its length?


Answer:

18:31

Step-by-step explanation:

The perimeter of a rectangle is the sum of all its sides.

A rectangle has 4 sides; 2 equal widths and 2 equal lengths.

[tex]p = 2l + 2w[/tex]

We already know the total perimeter: 2.94m. Let's convert it into cm.

[tex]2.94m \times \frac{100cm}{1m} = 294cm \\ [/tex]

Now, let's find for the length.

[tex]294 = 2l + 2(54)[/tex]

[tex]294 = 2l + 108[/tex]

[tex]2l = 294 - 108[/tex]

[tex]2l = 186[/tex]

[tex] \frac{2l}{2} = \frac{186}{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex]l = 93[/tex]

Now, we know that the rectangle has a width of 54 and length of 93. Let's find it greatest common factor.

54 - 1, 2, 3, 6, 9, 18, 27, 54

93 - 1, 3, 31, 93

GCF is 3

Divide both the length and the width by 3, we get the ratio of 18cm width to 31cm length (18:31).
