22. You are visiting a museum and a replica of a circular dome is on display. Your friend challenges you to get the radius of the dome and you are not allowed to go inside the dome. To get the radius, your friend let you stand 5 feet from the dome and measure 10 feet to a point of tangency. What is the radius and diameter of the dome?


[tex] \begin{array}{l} \bold{SOLUTION\ 1:}\\ \\ \textsf{Let }r\textsf{ be the radius of the circular dome.} \\ \\ \textsf{Refer to the diagram drawn.} \\ \\ \textsf{By Pythagorean Theorem,} \\ \\ \begin{aligned} AB^2 + BC^2 &= AC^2, \textsf{where }AC = r + 5\\ \\ 10^2 + r^2 &= (r + 5)^2 \\ \\ 100 + r^2 &= r^2 + 10r + 25 \\ \\ 100 - 25 &= 10r \\ \\ 75 &= 10r \\ \\ r & = \boxed{7.5\textsf{ ft.}} \\ \\ d&=2(7.5) = \boxed{15\textsf{ ft.}} \end{aligned} \\ \\ \\ \bold{SOLUTION\ 2:} \\ \\ \textsf{Let }d\textsf{ be the diameter of the circular dome.} \\ \\ \textsf{By Tangent - Secant Theorem,} \\ \\ \begin{aligned} AB^2 &= AD(AE),\textsf{ where }AD = d \\ \\ 10^2 &=5(d + 5) \\ \\ 100 &= 5d + 25 \\ \\ 5d &= 100 - 25 \\ \\ 5d &=75 \\ \\ d & = \boxed{15\textsf{ ft.}} \\ \\ r & = \dfrac{15}{2} = \boxed{7.5\textsf{ ft.}} \end{aligned} \end{array} [/tex]
