2. What is the formula of the Quadratic equation for the parabolic arc? (


Answer:

The formula for the quadratic equation is:

y = ax^2 + bx + c

where:

- y represents the height or vertical coordinate

- x represents the distance or horizontal coordinate

- a, b, and c are constants that determine the shape and position of the parabolic arc.

In general, the quadratic equation represents a parabola, which is a geometric shape that has a symmetrical and curved appearance. The specific formula for a parabolic arc will depend on the context and the values of the constants a, b, and c. However, some common examples include:

- y = x^2: this represents a simple parabolic arc that opens upward and has its vertex at the origin.

- y = -x^2 + 4: this represents another upward-opening parabolic arc that has a vertex at (0, 4).

- y = 2x^2 - 3x + 1: this represents a more general parabolic arc that opens upward and can be moved and scaled by changing the values of a, b, and c.

12. find the length of the gray arc. Be sure to write the formula, work and answer with units.​


Solution:-

To find the arc length, use the following formula:

[tex] \boxed{s = rθ}[/tex]

Where,

s = arc length

r = radius

θ = angle in radians

Before you plug in any values, convert the angle from degrees to radians using the following formula, where x is the angle in degrees:

[tex]θ = \frac{x}{180} \pi \\ [/tex]

[tex]θ = \frac{144}{180} \pi \\ [/tex]

[tex]θ≈2.5[/tex]

Now plug in your values:

[tex]s = 4 × 2.5[/tex]

[tex]s = 10ft[/tex]

Answer:-

[tex] \large \boxed{s = 10ft}[/tex]
