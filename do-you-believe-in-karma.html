Karma is concerned not only with the relationship between actions and consequences, but also the moral reasons or intentions behind actions, according to a 1988 article in the journal Philosophy East and West. So if someone commits a good deed for the wrong reasons — making a charitable donation to impress a potential love interest, for example — the action could still be immoral and produce bad karma.

Importantly, karma is wrapped up with the concept of reincarnation or rebirth, in which a person is born in a new human (or nonhuman) body after death. The effects of an action can therefore be visited upon a person in a future life, and the good or bad fortune someone experiences may be the result of actions performed in past lives.

20. Read the following beliefs. Choose one and explain it briefly but substantially. (5 points) 1. Hindus believe that the soul is eternal but is bound by the law of Karma (action). 2. Suffering is caused by passionate desire, lusts, cravings. 3. Faith is to believe what you do not see.


Answer:

1. Hindus believe that the soul is eternal but is bound by the law of Karma (action).

ANSWER: Hindus believe that every living creature has an ATMAN (soul), and this atman is eternal in nature. When people die, only the body dies and cease to exist, but the soul remains and will be placed in a new vessel (body). What body or  creature will the Atman inhabit in its second life will solely depends upon its karma. Hinduism teaches its followers to live a good and moral life, to follow their dharma so that they will obtain a good karma in their next life. Thus, if I lived my life observing the teachings of Hinduism and has faithfully followed my dharma, then it safe to say that in my next life, I will receive a good karma. In other words, my Atman will inhabit a better body in my next life, that is, I will be a step higher in the "caste system". However, If I have lived my life in wickedness and has deviated from my dharma, I will surely receive a bad karma and my second life will be worse than my previous one.

2. Suffering is caused by passionate desire, lusts, cravings.

This teachings had its roots in Buddhism. Siddhartha Gautama, also known as the Buddha taught his disciples the 4 noble truths:

1. Life is full of suffering.  

2. Suffering is closely linked to desire/cravings and attachment to impermanent things.

3. Suffering can be dispelled by the abandonment of all desires.

4. Such abandonment of desire can be achieved by following the Noble Eightfold Path.

3. Faith is to believe what you do not see.

Faith is not simply believing what the eyes can't see, but believing the existence of a God through the eyes of our faith.
