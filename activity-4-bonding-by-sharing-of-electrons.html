A covalent bond forms when the difference between the electronegativities of two atoms is too small for an electron transfer to occur to form ions.

Non-metals

Non metals form covalent bonds but there are instances such as both metals and non-metals form bonds, which is called a polar covalent bond. On the opposite, it is not possible for a metal and non-metal to form a non-polar covalent bond.
