5. smallest snake in the world?


Answer:

Barbados threadsnake

It is the smallest snake found in Barbados, in forested areas. This species of snake was discovered in 2008, under a rocks in the forest of Barbados.

Don't worry Barbados threadsnakes can't swallow a human being it only eat ant larvae and other small insects. Because of its size it can only lay one egg unlike big snakes.

Female Barbados threadsnakes produce a single large and elongated egg. The estimated size of the hatchtling is one-half the size of a full-sized adult.
