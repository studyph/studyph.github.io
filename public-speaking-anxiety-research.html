20. Stage fright is public speaking anxiety. true false


The answer is TRUE. The statement "Stage fright is public speaking anxiety." is true. Stage fright is the situation wherein someone experiences a feeling of nervousness during a public speaking or a performance. The feeling of stage fright arises when a person has to stand and perform in front of a camera or an audience.

Meaning of Stage Fright Stage fright is the feeling of being nervous during a public speaking or a performance. Stage fright does not only involve public speaking, but also other types of performances such as acting, recitals, dancing, singing, etc. Hence, it is also called "performance anxiety". The feeling of stage fright arises when a person has to stand and perform in front of a camera or an audience. How to Overcome Stage Fright

Here are some ways on how to overcome stage fright:

Exercise before going to the venue. Do relaxation techniques to counter stress. Breathe deeply to decrease stress. Practice your performance to master it. Drink a lot of water so that your mouth won't get dry. Avoid caffeinated beverages to prevent palpitation.
