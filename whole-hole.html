Whole' and 'hole' are homonyms, meaning they sound alike but are spelled differently and have different meanings. They can also have different functions in a sentence, known as their part of speech.

'Hole' is a noun, a word that refers to a person, place, or thing, because it refers to a place: a hollow area in an otherwise solid body or surface, like the ground or a wall. 'Whole' can be a noun as well, referring to something that is complete. But it can also be an adjective, a word used to described a noun.
