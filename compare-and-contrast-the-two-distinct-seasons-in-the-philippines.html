TWO DISTINCT SEASONS IN THE PHILIPPINES

The Philippines is one of the countries known to have a tropical climate.

It has a distinct high temperature during the summer season and abundant rainfall during the wet season.

Based on the weather agency PAG ASA, the average yearly temperature of the country (excluding Baguio) is 26.6o C.

The month of  January is the most significant time that has the lowest temperature average of 25.5oC and the month of May is known to be the warmest time, with an annual average of 28.3oC.

The overall climate of the Philippines is divided into two major seasons; Dry Seasons - from December to May, ( these can be further subdivided into Cool Dry Season from December to February and Hot Dry season from March to May.)Rainy Seasons  - from the month of June to November, known also for the months that has most recorded number of typhoons.

CONTRAST: Both the climates are being affected by the humidity or the presence of moisture in the atmosphere. There are instances that even if is a dry season, scattered rainfall is still present.

COMPARISON: Either of the two types of climate, has greatly affected the conditions, agriculture, and infrastructure of the country whenever it strikes the area with unbearable conditions.
