26. Find the coefficient of the 5th term of the expansion of (x2 x-1)8


Answer:

56

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the coefficient of the 5th term of the expansion of (x^2 x-1)^8, we can use the binomial theorem. The binomial theorem states that for any real numbers a and b, and any non-negative integer n, the expansion of (a + b)^n is given by the sum of the binomial coefficients (n choose k) * a^(n-k) * b^k, where k ranges from 0 to n.

In this case, we have a = x^2, b = x-1, and n = 8, so the expansion of (x^2 x-1)^8 is given by the sum of the binomial coefficients (8 choose k) * (x^2)^(8-k) * (x-1)^k for k = 0 to 8.

To find the coefficient of the 5th term, we need to find (8 choose 5) = 8! / (5! * 3!) = 56.
