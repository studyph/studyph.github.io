Given the balanced equation below for the highly exothermic reaction of aluminum with oxygen to form aluminum oxide: 4Al(s) + 3O2(g)  2Al2O3(s) ∆H = - 1,670 kJ a. What is the value of ∆H when 2 moles of aluminum react? b. What is the value of ∆H when 10.8 g of aluminum reacts? c. A certain reaction releases 6,881 kJ of heat. From the given enthalpies of reaction; 4HCl(g) + O2(g)  2H2O(l) + 2Cl2(g) ∆Hrxn = -202.4kJ/mol 1⁄2 H2(g) + 1⁄2 F2(g) HF(l) ∆Hrxn = -600.0 kJ/mol H2(g) + 1⁄2 O2 (g)  H2O(l) ∆Hrxn = -285.8 kJ/mol Find: ∆Hrxn for 2HCl(g) + F2(g)  2HF(l) + Cl2(g)


Problem 1:Given:

[tex]w = -\text{8 J}[/tex]

[tex]q = \text{40 J}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]\Delta E[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\Delta E = q + w[/tex]

[tex]\Delta E = \text{40 J} + (-\text{8 J})[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\Delta E = \text{32 J}}[/tex]

Problem 2:Given:

thermochemical equation: 4Al(s) + 3O₂(g) → 2Al₂O₃(s) ∆H = - 1,670 kJ/mol

Required:

(a) ∆H when 2 mol of Al react

(b) ∆H when 10.8 g of Al react

(c) mass of Al₂O₃ when a certain reaction releases 6881 kJ of heat

Solution: (a)

Step 1: Determine the conversion factor to be used.

[tex]\text{conversion factor} = \frac{-\text{1670 kJ}}{\text{4 mol Al}}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the heat evolved.

[tex]\Delta H = \text{2 mol Al} \times \frac{-\text{1670 kJ}}{\text{4 mol Al}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\Delta H = -\text{835 kJ}}[/tex]

Solution: (b)

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of Al.

molar mass of Al = 26.98 g/mol × 1

molar mass of Al = 26.98 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the heat evolved.

[tex]\Delta H = \text{10.8 g Al} \times \frac{\text{1 mol Al}}{\text{26.98 g Al}} \times \frac{-\text{1670 kJ}}{\text{4 mol Al}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\Delta H = -\text{167 kJ}}[/tex]

Solution: (c)

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of Al₂O₃.

molar mass of Al₂O₃ = (26.98 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 3)

molar mass of Al₂O₃ = 101.96 g/mol

Step 2: Determine the conversion factor to be used.

[tex]\text{conversion factor} = \frac{-\text{1670 kJ}}{\text{2 mol Al}_{2}\text{O}_{3}}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the mass of Al₂O₃ produced.

[tex]\text{mass of Al}_{2}\text{O}_{3} = -\text{6881 kJ} \times \frac{\text{2 mol Al}_{2}\text{O}_{3}}{-\text{1670 kJ}} \times \frac{\text{101.96 g Al}_{2}\text{O}_{3}}{\text{1 mol Al}_{2}\text{O}_{3}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of Al}_{2}\text{O}_{3} = \text{840.2 g}}[/tex]

Problem 3:Solution:

We are going to use Hess's Law to solve for the enthalpy of reaction of 2HCl (g) + F₂ (g) → 2HF (l) + Cl₂ (g), which is the given overall reaction. The goal

Step 1:

Looking at the overall reaction, we need 2 moles of HCl. So we divide the first thermochemical equation by 2 to obtain

[tex]\begin{array}{cccccccc} & 2\text{HCl}(g) & + & \frac{1}{2}\text{O}_{2}(g) & \rightarrow & \text{H}_{2}\text{O}(l) & + & \text{Cl}_{2}(g) & \Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = \frac{1}{2}(-\text{202.4 kJ/mol}) \\ & & & & & & & & \Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = -\text{101.2 kJ/mol} \\ \end{array}[/tex]

Step 2:

Looking at the overall reaction, we need 1 mole of F₂. So we multiply the second thermochemical equation by 2 to obtain

[tex]\begin{array}{cccccccc} & \text{H}_{2}(g) & + & \text{F}_{2}(g) & \rightarrow & 2\text{HF}(l) & \Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = 2(-\text{600.0 kJ/mol}) \\ & & & & & & \Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = -\text{1200.0 kJ/mol} \\ \end{array}[/tex]

Step 3:

Looking at the overall reaction, there are no H₂, O₂, and H₂O involved. We need to reverse the third thermochemical equation to obtain

[tex]\begin{array}{cccccccc} & \text{H}_{2}\text{O}(l) & \rightarrow & \text{H}_{2}(g) & + & \frac{1}{2}\text{O}_{2}(g) & \Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = -(-\text{285.8 kJ/mol}) \\ & & & & & & \Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = \text{285.8 kJ/mol} \\ \end{array}[/tex]

Step 4:

We combine the three new thermochemical equations to obtain the overall enthalpy of reaction.

[tex]\begin{array}{cccccccc} & 2\text{HCl}(g) & + & \frac{1}{2}\text{O}_{2}(g) & \rightarrow & \text{H}_{2}\text{O}(l) & + & \text{Cl}_{2}(g) & \Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = -\text{101.2 kJ/mol} \\ & \text{H}_{2}(g) & + & \text{F}_{2}(g) & \rightarrow & 2\text{HF}(l) & & & \Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = -\text{1200.0 kJ/mol} \\ & & & \text{H}_{2}\text{O}(l) & \rightarrow & \text{H}_{2}(g) & + & \frac{1}{2}\text{O}_{2}(g) & \Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = \text{285.8 kJ/mol} \\ \hline & 2\text{HCl}(g) & + & \text{F}_{2}(g) & \rightarrow & \text{F}_{2}(g) & + & 2\text{HF}(l) & \Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = -\text{1015.4 kJ/mol} \\ \end{array}[/tex]

Answer:

[tex]\Delta H_{\text{rxn}} = -\text{1015.4 kJ/mol}[/tex]

TEMPERATURE CONVERSION AND HEAT ENERGY

Answers with Explanation:

1. Convert 56 degrees Fahrenheit to degrees Celsius to degrees Kelvin

Solution:

In order to convert degrees Fahrenheit to degrees Celsius, just simply use the formula:

°C = (°F - 32) x 5/9

By direct substitution:

°C = (56 - 32) x 5/9

°C = 24 x 5/9

°C = 120/9

°C = 40/3 or 13.33 degrees Celsius

On the other hand, in order to convert degrees Celsius to degrees Kelvin, just simply add the temperature in degree Celsius by 273.15. This can be expressed in a formula:

°K = °C + 273.15

And by direct substitution:

°K = 13.33 + 273.15

°K = 286.48 degrees Kelvin

2. How much heat energy is required to raise the  temperature of 758 g of aluminum from 20.0 °C to 80.0 °C,  given that the specific heat of aluminum is 0.903  J/g°C?

Solution:

In order to solve for the required heat energy to raise the temperature of the given element, we can use the formula:

Q = mcΔT

where: Q refers to the heat energy measured in Joules (J), m refers to the mass measured in grams (g), c refers to the specific heat measured in J/g°C which varies for every element and ΔT, or read as delta-T, refers to the change in temperature in degrees Celsius. If the temperature is increased, it is positive. Otherwise, it is negative.

In the given problem, Q is the unknown, m is the 758 grams, c is the 0.903  J/g°C and ΔT is the difference between of 80 and 20.

By direct substitution:

Q = mcΔT

Q = (758)(0.903)(80-20)

Q = (758)(0.903)(60)

Q = 41,068.44 J or 41.07 kJ
