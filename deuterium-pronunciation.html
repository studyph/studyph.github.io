1. Question Which particle would result in the collision and reaction between two deuterium nuclei? n 8 - deuterium deuterium helium-3 + ?proton neutron electron photon ​


Answer:

The collision and reaction between two deuterium nuclei (also known as deuteron-deuteron fusion) typically involves a proton and a neutron. During the fusion process, the two deuterium nuclei combine to form a helium-4 nucleus, which consists of two protons and two neutrons. The reaction also releases a considerable amount of energy in the form of gamma rays and high-energy particles. Therefore, the correct answer is Proton.

28. Ano ba ang Deuterium ​


Answer:

Deuterium is one of two stable isotopes of hydrogen. The nucleus of a deuterium atom, called a deuteron, contains one proton and one neutron, whereas the far more common protium has no neutrons in the nucleus. Deuterium has a natural abundance in Earth's oceans of about one atom in 6420 of hydrogen.

Answer:

Deuterium got its name from the Greek word deuterons, meaning “second”. It refers to the two particles i.e. a neutron and a proton that make up the nucleus of a deuterium atom. The term used for the deuterium nucleus is deuteron.
