20. what is this roman numeral as a number


Answer:

LXMMMCCV

60 3000 200 5

yan!!

Answer:

63205

Step-by-step explanation:

The given roman numeral is equivalent to

[tex]\rm \overline{LX}MMMCCV=\overline{LX}+MMM+CC+V[/tex]

In Arabic numerals, LX = 60, MMM = 3,000, CC = 200, V = 5. Additionally, the line on the top of LX means that we are going to multiply LX by 1000. Thus,

[tex]\rm \overline{LX}MMMCCV=60(1000)+3000+200+5=63205[/tex]


21. Outlining format that uses roman numerals, letters, and numbers​


Answer:

Begin with a Roman Numeral for the Topic. After each Roman numeral, letter, or number put a period. Begin all lines with an upper case letter. Line up every letter or numeral under the one above. The main points of your topic will look like an abbreviated topic sentence for each paragraph. The next Roman numeral, or “II” will be for first body paragraph. The third “III” for the second body, and so on. When the outline is complete, each Roman numeral should equal the number of paragraphs in the paper.

29. Explain the verse in Romans 1:22 "Professing themselves to be wise, they became fools."


Answer:

Don't act like you are God and start worshiping yourself. Instead worship God only.

Explanation:

Here, the verse highlights how those who only focuses on how amazing themselves are, are considered 'fools'. In other words, this verse is criticizing how we humans should not be selfish and glorify ourselves like we are God. As in reality we are not God. There is only one God, who is Jesus Christ. This means that the only being we should worship is Jesus and not anyone else, including ourselves.

The Verse before this verse, Romans 1:21 NIV states

" For although they knew God, they neither glorified him as God nor gave thanks to him, but their thinking became futile and their foolish hearts were darkened.""

This further emphasizes this idea, as those who keep flexing to others that they are wise become distant from God. Humans do not know everything, only God knows everything. So instead of boasting what knowledge we have, as humans. We should boast the terrific actions that God has done for us.

Also here is a tip, when trying to understand what a particular Bible Verse is trying to highlight. Try read a few verses prior to that verse and after that verse. This allows you to understand the concept of the verse, allowing you to understand it more.
