DISPLACEMENT

Displacement is the shortest distance covered by an object (between the starting point to the final point. The Phythagorean Theorem can be used to find the displacement. Its formula is c² = a² + b² The symbol for displacement is [tex]\overrightarrow{d}[/tex]. The positive directions that are given are North and East while the negative directions that are also given are West and South. The Linear Equation can be also used to find the displacement.

Now, let us find the displacement of the following.

1. Move 4 meters east | Move 2 meters west

[tex]\begin{aligned}\overrightarow{d} & = |4 \rm m + (-2m)|\\& = |4\rm m - 2m|\\& = |2\rm m|\\& = \boxed{2\rm m \ \rm East}\end{aligned}[/tex]

The displacement is 2m East.

2. Move 4 meters north | Move 2 meters south

[tex]\begin{aligned}\overrightarow{d} & = |4 \rm m + (-2m)|\\& = |4\rm m - 2m|\\& = |2\rm m|\\& = \boxed{2\rm m \ \rm North}\end{aligned}[/tex]

The displacement is 2m North.

3. Move 2 meters east | Move 4 meters west

[tex]\begin{aligned}\overrightarow{d} & = |2 \rm m + (-4m)|\\& = |2\rm m - 4m|\\& = |-2\rm m|\\& = \boxed{2\rm m \ \rm West}\end{aligned}[/tex]

The displacement is 2m West.

4. Move 5 meters east | Move 5 meters west

[tex]\begin{aligned}\overrightarow{d} & = |5 \rm m + (-5m)|\\& = |5\rm m - 5m|\\& = \boxed{0}\end{aligned}[/tex]

Since the final point went back to the starting point, there will be zero displacement.

5. Move 5 meters south | Move 2 meters north

[tex]\begin{aligned}\overrightarow{d} & = |-5 \rm m + 2m|\\& = |-3\rm m|\\& = \boxed{3\rm m \ \rm South}\end{aligned}[/tex]

The displacement is 3m South.

6. Move 10 meters west | Move 3 meters east

[tex]\begin{aligned}\overrightarow{d} & = |-10 \rm m + 3m|\\& = |-7\rm m|\\& = \boxed{7\rm m \ \rm West}\end{aligned}[/tex]

The displacement is 7m West.

In this activity, I used the "Linear Equation" formula to find their displacement.
