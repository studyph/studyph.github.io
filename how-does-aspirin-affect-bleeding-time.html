19. aspirin tablet generally contains 325 mg of aspirin. how many such tablets may be prepared for 5kg of aspirin. Give a detailed explanation​


Answer:

There are 15,384 aspirins tablets that can be made from 5 kilograms of aspirin.

Step-by-step explanation:

The firs step is to make all the unit of measurements of our given be the same with each other. We know that 325 mg is needed to make 1 tablet of aspirin. If there is a total of 5 kg of aspirin, we just need to get how any 325 mg are there in 5 kg. We cannot directly divide the two numbers because they differ in their unit of measurement. So we need to convert 5 kg to mg. We know that 1 kg is equal to 1,000 g. So we can multiply 1,000 by 5.

[tex](\frac{5 kg}{1})( \frac{1,000g}{1kg} ) = 5,000 g[/tex]

In doing so we get, 5,000 g.

We now then convert 5,000 g to mg. We know that 1 g is equal to 1,000 mg. So we can multiply 5,000 g by 1,000.

[tex](\frac{5,000g}{1})(\frac{1,000mg}{1g} ) = 5,000,000 mg[/tex]

In doing so, we get 5,000,000 mg.

We can now divide 5,000,000 by 325 mg.

5,000,000 mg ÷ 325 mg = 15,384.6154

Since we need 325 mg to make a tablet, we can disregard the decimal places and just get the whole number which is 15,384. Therefore, we can make 15,384 aspirin tablets from 5 kg of aspirin.

Bleeding, also called hemorrhage, is the name used to describe blood loss. It can refer to blood loss inside the body, called internal bleeding, or to blood loss outside of the body, called external bleeding.

Blood loss can occur in almost any area of the body. Internal bleeding occurs when blood leaks out through a damaged blood vessel or organ. External bleeding happens when blood exits through a break in the skin.
