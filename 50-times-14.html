not sure? here's the solution!

first, let's subtract 50 and 22 (to find the tails)

50 - 22 = 28

so the tail is 28 but that's not the final answer

let's turn them into a fraction

[tex] \frac{28}{50} [/tex]

next, find the lowest term of 28/50 but how? first, find the GCF (greatest common factor) of 28 and 50

28 - 1,2,4,7,14,28

50 - 1,2,5,10,25,50

so the GCF of 28 and 50 is 2, and let's divide them!

[tex] \frac{28}{50} \div \frac{2}{2} = \frac{14}{25} [/tex]

so, the lowest term of 28/50 is 14/25
