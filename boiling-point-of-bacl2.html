12. If 45.5 g of bacl2 is dissolved in water to produce 2.74 l of solution, what is the molarity of the solution? 0.219 m 0.599 m 0.263 m 0.0797 m 0.0962 m


Given
[tex]m = 45.5 \: g \\ volume = 2.74 \: L[/tex]

Use formulas
To find moles:
[tex]n = \frac{m}{MM}[/tex]

where n = moles
m = mass
MM = molar mass

To find molarity:
[tex]c = \frac{n}{v}[/tex]

where c = concentration
n = moles
v = volume in L

Solve
[tex]n = \frac{45.5 \: g}{208.23 \: g/mol} \\ n = 0.219 \: mol}[/tex]

[tex]c = \frac{0.219 \: mol}{2.74 \: L} \\ c = 0.0799[/tex]

The normality of a solution is the gram equivalent weight of a solute per liter of solution. It may also be called the equivalent concentration. It is indicated using the symbol N, eq/L, or meq/L (= 0.001 N) for units of concentration. For example, the concentration of a hydrochloric acid solution might be expressed as 0.1 N HCl. A gram equivalent weight or equivalent is a measure of the reactive capacity of a given chemical species (ion, molecule, etc.). n(Cl−) = n(AgCl) = 0.0032mol

The mass of barium and sodium chloride is 0.2356g. Shown as:

25. If 410.8 grams of barium nitrate, Ba(NO3)2, are produced how many grams of silver nitrate, AgNO3, were reacted? 2 AgNO3 + BaCl2 → 2 AgCl + Ba(NO3)2


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of Ba(NO₃)₂ and AgNO₃.

For Ba(NO₃)₂

molar mass = (137.3 g/mol × 1) + (14.01 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 6)

molar mass = 261.32 g/mol

For AgNO₃

molar mass = (107.9 g/mol × 1) + (14.01 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 3)

molar mass = 169.91 g/mol

Step 2: Determine the mole ratio needed.

mole ratio = 2 mol AgNO₃ : 1 mol Ba(NO₃)₂

Step 3: Calculate the mass of AgNO₃ reacted using the mole ratio.

[tex]\text{mass of AgNO₃ = 410.8 g Ba(NO₃)₂} × \frac{\text{1 mol Ba(NO₃)₂}}{\text{261.32 g Ba(NO₃)₂}} × \frac{\text{2 mol AgNO₃}}{\text{1 mol Ba(NO₃)₂}} × \frac{\text{169.91 g AgNO₃}}{\text{1 mol AgNO₃}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of AgNO₃ = 534.2 g}}[/tex]
