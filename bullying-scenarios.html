Answer:

Once upon a time, there was a girl named Sarah who attended a school where bullying was rampant. Sarah was different from the other students because she had superpowers. She could fly, become invisible, and had super strength. Despite her powers, Sarah was often the target of bullies. They would taunt her and make fun of her for being different.

One day, Sarah had had enough. She decided that she was going to use her powers to stand up to the bullies and put an end to their cruel behavior. The next time a group of bullies confronted her, Sarah flew up into the air and became invisible. The bullies were confused and didn't know what was happening. Suddenly, they felt a strong force push them back, and they fell to the ground.

Sarah revealed herself and stood in front of the bullies. She told them that their behavior was unacceptable and that they needed to stop bullying others. The bullies were shocked by Sarah's bravery and didn't know what to do. They apologized for their actions and promised to change their ways.

From that day forward, Sarah became known as a hero at her school. She used her powers to help others who were being bullied and stood up for those who couldn't stand up for themselves. The school became a safer place for everyone, and Sarah's bravery inspired others to speak out against bullying.

In the end, Sarah learned that being different was something to be proud of, and that standing up for what is right is always worth it.

A. Write a short fiction story with its complete elements about a school scenario a girl pupil who has superpowers who fought against bullying.

Fiction Story: "The Girl with Superpowers"

Alice was just an ordinary girl, or so she thought. She attended her local school, where she excelled in her classes and had a small group of friends. But there was one thing that made Alice stand out from the rest: her superpowers. She could control the elements - fire, water, earth, and air - with just a flick of her wrist.

At first, Alice tried to keep her powers hidden, afraid of what others might think or do if they found out. But one day, as she was walking home from school, she witnessed a group of boys bullying a younger girl. They were teasing her and pushing her around, and the poor girl looked helpless.

Alice knew she couldn't just stand by and watch. She stepped forward and, with a fierce look in her eye, summoned a gust of wind that knocked the boys off their feet. They stumbled and fell, bewildered by what had just happened.

The younger girl looked up at Alice with wonder and gratitude, and Alice knew she had done the right thing. From that day on, Alice decided to use her powers to fight against bullying at her school. Whenever she saw someone being mistreated, she would step in and put a stop to it, using her powers to protect the innocent.

The younger girl looked up at Alice with wonder and gratitude, and Alice knew she had done the right thing. From that day on, Alice decided to use her powers to fight against bullying at her school. Whenever she saw someone being mistreated, she would step in and put a stop to it, using her powers to protect the innocent.

Word of Alice's actions quickly spread throughout the school, and soon everyone knew about the girl with superpowers who fought against bullying. Her bravery and strength inspired others to stand up for themselves and each other, and bullying began to disappear from the school.

Alice was hailed as a hero, and her superpowers became a symbol of hope and justice for all. She continued to use her powers for good, and her school became a safe and welcoming place for all students.

B. Write a short non-fiction story with its complete elements about a school scenario a group of boys bullies a helpless girl.

Non-fiction Story: "Standing Up to Bullies"

Sarah was a shy and introverted girl who had just started at a new school. She didn't know anyone and was struggling to make friends. But things got worse when a group of boys started bullying her. They would call her names, push her in the hallways, and make fun of her in front of others.

At first, Sarah tried to ignore them and hoped they would eventually leave her alone. But the bullying continued, and it was taking a toll on her self-esteem and happiness. She felt alone and helpless, with no one to turn to.

One day, Sarah decided enough was enough. She couldn't let the bullies win. She took a deep breath and, with a newfound courage, confronted the boys. She told them she wouldn't stand for their bullying anymore and demanded they stop.

The boys were taken aback by Sarah's sudden assertiveness. They had never expected her to stand up to them. But instead of backing down, they began to laugh and taunt her even more.

Sarah felt her heart sink. Had she made a mistake? But then something incredible happened. A group of other students stepped forward and stood with her. They told the bullies they wouldn't tolerate their behavior and that Sarah was their friend.

The bullies looked shocked and intimidated by the sudden show of support. They mumbled some apologies and walked away, defeated.

Sarah felt a surge of gratitude and relief. She had finally found the courage to stand up to the bullies, and it had paid off. She had made new friends and had shown the bullies that their behavior wouldn't be tolerated.

From that day on, Sarah continued to stand up for herself and others who were being bullied. She became a beacon of strength and courage for her school, and her story inspired others to speak up and take action against bullying. In the end, Sarah's bravery had transformed her school into a kinder and more inclusive community.
