19. John has a farm with cows and chickens. There are 1284 legs all in all. John has 142 chickens. How many cows are there?Show the:EquationSolutionFinal Answer​


Let:

cows = a

chickens = b

Equation: (number of legs) 2a + 4b = 1284

Solution:

2a + 4b = 1284; a = 142

2(142) + 4b = 1284

284 + 4b = 1284

4b = 1284 - 284

4b = 1000

b = 250

There are 250 cows.

Answer:

250 cows

Computation:

Knowing that a cow has four legs while a chicken has two legs, The following equation can be formulated.

[tex]\boxed{4x+2y=1,284}\\\\where,\\x\to number\:of\:cows\\y\to number\:of\:chickens=142[/tex]

Solving for the number of cows in John's farm.

[tex]4x+2y=1,284\\4x+2(142)=1,284\\4x+284=1,284\\4x=1,284-284\\4x=1,000\\x=1,000/4=\boxed{250}[/tex]
