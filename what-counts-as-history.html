s = σ / sqrt(n)

where σ is the population standard deviation, n is the sample size, and sqrt represents the square root function.

Plugging in the given values, we get:

s = 2.75 / sqrt(40,000)

s = 0.01375

Therefore, the standard deviation for a sample of 40,000 observations is 0.01375.

s = σ / sqrt(n)

where σ is the population standard deviation, n is the sample size, and sqrt represents the square root function.

s = 16.88 / sqrt(4)

s = 8.44
