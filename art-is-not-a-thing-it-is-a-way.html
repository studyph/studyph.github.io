1. Art is not thing- it is a way definition


Answer

Art is not the artwork that an artist creates but it is a way of living. Art is the lifestyle of an artist. It is a strong desire and the method of an artist to create something beautiful or something meaningful.

Explanation

Elbert Hubbard defines Art as being a big part of an artist's entire being and daily life. It is an ongoing experience in life that affects an artist's personal growth. Eventually, the acquired experiences will influence and affect the artworks and style of that artist.

"Art is not a thing, — it is a way." - is a quote by Elbert Hubbard written on his book, Little Journeys to the Homes of Great Teachers: Thomas Arnold published in 1908.

5. What is an element of art that refers to the ways things feel or look as if they might feel it touch​


Answer:

>>> Art

The element of art that refers to the ways things feel or look as if they might feel it touch is called "texture". Texture refers to the surface quality or tactile characteristic of an artwork. It can be actual, where you can feel the texture physically, or implied, where an artist creates the illusion of texture using techniques such as shading, patterns, and brushstrokes.

12 March 2023Answer:The element of art that refers to the ways things feel or look as if they might feel it touch is called texture. Texture is a visual or tactile quality of a surface, object, or artwork that can be perceived by touch or sight. It can be rough, smooth, bumpy, soft, hard, shiny, or dull, and it can be created using a variety of techniques such as brush strokes, carving, or collage. Texture can add depth and interest to an artwork and can help convey its meaning or mood.

Answer:

1.Because of its flexibility and three-dimensionality, installation art is influenced by developments in computer art - such as software developments in video and film projection - as well as techniques used in avant-garde theatre and dance. Architectural and interior design are other influences.

2.Many works of installation art are done in mixed media, or multiple types of art methods. They often incorporate found objects, cast off items repurposed in art, and might also involve audio and video components, as well as technology.

3.The term installation art is used to describe large-scale, mixed-media constructions, often designed for a specific place or for a temporary period of time.

4.Installation art also overlaps with the Conceptual art movement, since they both prioritize the importance of ideas over the work's technical merit. However, Conceptual art tends to be more understated and minimalist, whereas Installation art is often bold and more object-based.

5.Environmental artists seek to investigate our human relationship with the environment through embedding their artistic practice within it. Evolving from Land Art, Environmental Art also rethinks the importance of the exhibition space and seeks other places where art can happen and where art can exist.
