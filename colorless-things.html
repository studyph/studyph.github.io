Reduction: MnO4- + 3 e- MnO2

We now try to balance either the number of atoms or the charge on both sides of the equation. Because the reaction is run in basic solution, we can add either OH- ions or H2O molecules to either side of the equation, as needed. The key to deciding which side of the equation gets each of these reagents is simple: The only way to balance the net charge of -4 on the left side of the equation is to add four OH- ions to the products.

Reduction: MnO4- + 3 e- MnO2 + 4 OH-

We can then balance the number of hydrogen and oxygen atoms by adding two H2O molecules to the reactants.

30. Karl boiled a colorless liquid in a beaker. He noticed that the temperature of the colorless liquid varies at a given time. What could be the colorless liquid?


Answer

The colorless liquid is a mixture.

Explanation

The boiling point is the temperature by which liquid changes to a gas phase. It is one of the physical properties of matter and is unique to substances. Every substance has different boiling points. Thus, we can distinguish substances through the boiling point. Also, we can distinguish a pure substance from a mixture through the boiling point. Pure substances such as elements and compounds have a sharp boiling point and are very specific. It means that there is one boiling temperature of a pure substance. A mixture, on the other hand, boils at a range of temperatures. For example, pure water will boil at 100 degrees Celsius but the salt solution will have a range of boiling temperatures.
