QUESTION:

A sardines can is 10 cm tall and has a diameter of 3 cm. What is the volume?  

ANSWER:

A.70.65 cm³

SOLUTION:

Formula of Volume of cylinder is given by:

V = πr²h

where

V = Volume = ?

π = 3.14

h = height = 10 cm

r = radius = d/2 = 3/2 = 1.5 cm *

* if length is given in diameter, simply divide diameter by 2 to get the radius

Solve for V,

V = πr²h

V = 3.14(1.5)²(10)

V = 3.14(2.25)(10)

V = 70.65 cm³ *

[tex]\therefore \large\boxed{\text{\sf The volume sardines can is 70.65 $cm^3$}}[/tex]

19. FIND THE AREA OF A SPHERICAL TRIANGLE WHOSE ANGLES ARE 123°, 84° AND 73°, THE RADIUS OF THE SPHERE IS 40 CM.


Answer:

Area of a spherical triangle = (R^2 * (A + B + C - π)) where R is the radius of the sphere and A, B and C are the angles of the triangle.

Therefore, Area = (40^2 * (123° + 84° + 73° - π))

= (1600 * (280° - π))

= 1600 * (280 - 3.14159)

= 1600 * 276.85841

= 437,387.45 sq cm
