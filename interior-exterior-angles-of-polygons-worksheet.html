Answer:

The sum of the measures of the exterior angles of a convex polygon is always equal to 360 degrees. Each exterior angle of a convex polygon is supplementary to its corresponding interior angle. This means that if we know the measure of either the interior or exterior angle, we can use this relationship to find the measure of the other angle.

If the interior angle measures 78°, then the corresponding exterior angle is:

Exterior angle + Interior angle = 180°

Exterior angle + 78° = 180°

Exterior angle = 180° - 78°

Exterior angle = 102°

Therefore, the measure of the corresponding exterior angle is 102°.

If the exterior angle measures 30°, then the corresponding interior angle is:

Exterior angle + Interior angle = 180°

30° + Interior angle = 180°

Interior angle = 180° - 30°

Interior angle = 150°

Therefore, the measure of the corresponding interior angle is 150°.

If the exterior angle measures 108°, then the corresponding interior angle is:

Exterior angle + Interior angle = 180°

108° + Interior angle = 180°

Interior angle = 180° - 108°

Interior angle = 72°

Therefore, the measure of the corresponding interior angle is 72°.

If the interior angle measures 125°, then the corresponding exterior angle is:

Exterior angle + Interior angle = 180°

Exterior angle + 125° = 180°

Exterior angle = 180° - 125°

10. If an interior angle is eight times the exterior angle of a regular polygon, find:a. The value of an exterior angleb. The number of sides of the regular polygon​


PROBLEM:

If an interior angle is eight times the exterior angle of a regular polygon, find:

a. The value of an exterior angle

b. The number of sides of the regular polygon

[tex]\blue {\overline{ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: }} [/tex]

ANSWER:

> Let's denote the exterior angle as x and the interior angle as 8x.

> Interior and exterior angles that are adjacent to each other and forms a linear pair are supplementary. Thus, we'll equate the sum of the measures of the exterior angles to 180°.

The value of an exterior angle.

[tex] \rm 8x + x = 180 \degree[/tex]

[tex] \rm 9x = 180 \degree[/tex]

[tex] \rm x = 180 \degree \div 9[/tex]

[tex] \large \boxed{ \: \rm x = 20 \degree}[/tex]

> Since x is our exterior angle, thus, it measures 20°. If the exterior angle measures 20°, then the interior angle next to it measures 160°, 8 × 20°.

The number of sides of the regular polygon.

> Given the measure of one of the sides of a regular polygon, we can easily solve for the number of sides using the formula for the sum of the interior angles divided by the number of terms which is equal to 160°.

[tex] \rm 160 \degree = \frac{(n - 2) \times 180 \degree}{n} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \rm 160 \degree n = (n - 2) \degree \times 180 \degree[/tex]

[tex] \rm 160 \degree n = 180 \degree n - 360 \degree[/tex]

[tex] \rm 360 \degree = 180 \degree n - 160 \degree n[/tex]

[tex] \rm 360 \degree = 20 \degree n[/tex]

[tex] \rm n = 360 \degree \div 20 \degree[/tex]

[tex] \large \boxed{ \rm n = 18}[/tex]

> The number of sides of the given regular polygon is 18.

18. How do the interior and exterior angles of a polygon relate?


Answer:

A special rule exists for regular polygons: because they are equiangular, the exterior angles are also congruent, so the measure of any given exterior angle is 360/n degrees. As a result, the interior angles of a regular polygon are all equal to 180 degrees minus the measure of the exterior angle(s).

Step-by-step explanation:

1. The exterior angle of a regular polygon is given. Find the number of sides for each:

a. 72°

The exterior angle of a regular polygon is equal to 360/n, where n is the number of sides of the polygon. Therefore, we can solve for n using the equation:

72 = 360/n

n = 360/72

n = 5

Therefore, a regular polygon with an exterior angle of 72° has 5 sides.

b. 40°

Using the same formula, we can solve for n:

40 = 360/n

n = 360/40

n = 9

Therefore, a regular polygon with an exterior angle of 40° has 9 sides.

2. Find the measure of an interior and an exterior angle of a regular 46-gon

The sum of the interior angles of any polygon can be found using the formula:

Sum of interior angles = (n - 2) x 180°

where n is the number of sides of the polygon.

For a regular polygon, all interior angles are congruent, so we can find the measure of an interior angle by dividing the sum of interior angles by the number of sides:

Interior angle = (Sum of interior angles) / n

Using this formula, we can find the measure of an interior angle for a regular 46-gon:

Sum of interior angles = (46 - 2) x 180° = 8,280°

Interior angle = 8,280° / 46 = 180°

The exterior angle of a regular polygon is always 360/n, where n is the number of sides.

Therefore, the exterior angle of a regular 46-gon is:

Exterior angle = 360/46 = 7.8261°

3. The measure of an exterior angle of a regular polygon is 2x, and the measure of an interior angle is 4x. Find the measure of the interior and exterior angle.

The sum of the interior and exterior angle of a regular polygon is always 180°:

Interior angle + Exterior angle = 180°

Substituting the given values:

4x + 2x = 180°

6x = 180°

x = 30°

Therefore, the measure of the interior angle of the regular polygon is:

4x = 4(30°) = 120°

The measure of the exterior angle is:

2x = 2(30°) = 60°
