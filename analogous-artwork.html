Some of the answer are basically are part of ELEMENTS OF ARTS that are building blocks of art. Every piece of art ever created includes one or more elements, the Elements of art are 7 elements that is Line, Color, Shape, Form, Value, Space and Texture.

LINE - A line is an identifiable path created by a point moving in space. It is one-dimensional and can vary in width, direction, and length. Lines can be horizontal, vertical, or diagonal, straight or curved, thick or thin.KINETIC SCULPTURE - Kinetic art/sculpture is art from any medium that contains movement perceivable by the viewer or depends on motion for its effect.examples of kinetic art. More pertinently speaking, kinetic art is a term that today most often refers to three-dimensional sculptures and figures such as mobiles that move naturally or are machine operated. The moving parts are generally powered by wind, a motor or the observer.ANALOGOUS - Analogous colours are colours that are adjacent or located next to each other on the colour wheel.TEXTURE - An element of art that refers to the way things feel or look as if they might feel if touched. it can be hard, soft, through or smooth. Texture can be tectual or visual,SHAPE - A shape is an enclosed space. A bounded two-dimensional form that has both length and width.ACTUAL TEXTURE - A actual texture are literal, physical sensation of touching an object.SATURATION - Saturation refers to the intensity of a color. Saturation is the strength of a surface color, its degree of visual difference from neutral grey.TETRADIC - Tetradic colour scheme is made up of 4 colours, which are two pairs of complementary colours. There are 2 variations: the rectangle and the square.HUE - The characteristic related to the light frequency that appears in the color, for instance red, yellow, green, cyan, blue or magenta.ATMOSPHERIC PERSPECTIVE - Are emphasizes spatial intervals, the distance between objects where as objects recede into the distance, they become less detailed, lower in contrast, and more blurry.
