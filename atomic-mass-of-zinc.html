20. how many numbers of atoms are there in 5 moles of zinc​


Explanation:

The concept of the "mole" is based on the number of atoms in a single isotope of carbon, Carbon-12. In 12g of Carbon-12 there is a mole of Carbon atoms, or #6.02xx10^23# atoms of carbon.

For the rest of the atoms on the periodic table, their molar mass tells you how many grams of each you need to have a mole of those atoms, or #6.02xx10^23# atoms of that element.

If zinc has 1 mole, it will have #6.02xx10^23#. In chemistry we love to cancel units, so to solve your problem, we just cancel some units.

#"zinc atoms"="0.00150 moles Zn"xx(6.02xx10^23 \ "atoms")/"1 mole" = 9.03xx10^20 \ "atoms"#

If you just look at the units, this works out to:

#("moles" xx "atoms")/"moles"#

and this reduces to ATOMS.

23. A metal is made from copper, zinc and lead in the ratio 31:6:1 the mass of the zinc is 90 kg. calculate the mass of the metal​


A metal is made from copper, zinc and lead in the ratio 31:6:1 the mass of the zinc is 90 kg. Calculate the mass of the metal.

To find the mass of the metal,

Let,

Copper be 31x

Zinc be 6x

Lead be 1x or x.

Since the mass of zinc is given which is 90 kg, let us find the value of x:

[tex]6x = 90 \\ x = \frac{90}{6} \\ x = 15[/tex]

Now that we know the value of x, substitute x to each of the term.

[tex]copper \\ 31(15) = 465kg[/tex]

[tex]lead \\ 1(15) = 15kg[/tex]

Add the mass of the three element to solve for the mass of the metal,

[tex]465kg + 90kg + 15kg = 570kg[/tex]

Therefore, the mass of the metal is 570 kg.
