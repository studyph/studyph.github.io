So you might think that the second most commonly spoken language in this region would be Chinese. But it is actually French, the language of France. France is in Western Europe. That is half a world away from Indochina. Why do you think so many people in the region speak French


Answer:

The reason why French is the second most commonly spoken language in the Indochina Peninsula can be traced back to the history of the region. In the late 19th century, France colonized and established French Indochina, which included Vietnam, Laos, and Cambodia.

During the French colonization, the French language was imposed as the official language in these countries, and the French established a comprehensive education system that taught French to the locals. French was also used in government, administration, and commerce, which helped to spread the language.

Even after the countries gained independence, French continued to be used as a second language in education and business, and it remains an important language in the region today. Many people in the region also see learning French as a way to improve their employment opportunities and to communicate with French-speaking countries and communities around the world.

Therefore, the prevalence of French in the Indochina Peninsula can be attributed to the region's colonial history, the establishment of the education system, and its ongoing relevance in the global economy.

13. in a group of 100 persons 72 people can speak english and 43 can speak french how many can speak english only ？how many can speak both english and french​


Answer:

57 persons can speak English only.

15 persons can speak both English and French.

Step-by-step explanation:

Word Problem

Let us analyze the given problem above.

What is asked?

The number of persons who can speak English only and the number of persons who can speak both English and French.

Given:

people who can speak English - 72

people who can speak French - 43

Equation:

"How many can speak english only?"

To find the number of persons who can speak English only, take away the number of persons who speak French from 100.

100 - 43 = N

"How many can speak both English and French?"

To find the number of persons who can speak both English and French, take away the number of persons who can speak English only from the number of persons who can speak English.

72 - 57 = N

Solution:

100 - 43 = 57

57 persons can speak English only.

72 - 57 = 15

15 persons can speak both English and French.

22. Three hundred twenty people are traveling in an airplane. Sixty seven of them speak only English. Seventy three of them speak only French. Twenty one speak both French and English. What percentage of passengers speak neither French nor English?


Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the percentage of passengers who speak neither French nor English, we need to subtract the total number of passengers who speak English, French, or both from the total number of passengers on the plane.

Let's start by finding the total number of passengers who speak English or French:

English only = 67

French only = 73

Both English and French = 21

Total = 67 + 73 - 21 = 119

So, out of the 320 passengers on the plane, 119 speak English or French.

To find the number of passengers who speak neither language, we can subtract the number of passengers who speak English or French from the total number of passengers on the plane:

320 - 119 = 201

Therefore, 201 passengers speak neither French nor English.

To find the percentage of passengers who speak neither language, we can divide the number of passengers who speak neither language by the total number of passengers on the plane, and multiply by 100:

(201/320) x 100 = 62.81%

So, approximately 62.81% of the passengers speak neither French nor English.
