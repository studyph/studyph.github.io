7. 1. what is the difference between the green house effect and enhance green house effect. how do they affect the earth average global temperature 2. what does climate change help earth 3. why it is important to be aware to environmental issues like global warming and climate change​


Answer:

1. The greenhouse effect is a natural process that occurs when certain gases, such as carbon dioxide, water vapor, and methane, trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, keeping the planet warm enough to sustain life. The enhanced greenhouse effect, on the other hand, is the additional warming effect that occurs when human activities, such as burning fossil fuels, release more greenhouse gases into the atmosphere, leading to an overall increase in the Earth's temperature. Both the greenhouse effect and the enhanced greenhouse effect can lead to an increase in the Earth's average global temperature, which can have significant impacts on the planet's climate and ecosystems.

2. Climate change can have both positive and negative effects on the Earth. Some of the benefits of climate change include longer growing seasons for crops, increased access to resources in some regions, and reduced winter heating costs. However, the negative impacts of climate change, such as rising sea levels, more frequent and intense weather events, and loss of biodiversity, far outweigh any potential benefits.

3. It is important to be aware of environmental issues like global warming and climate change because they pose a significant threat to the planet's ecosystems and human society. These issues can lead to more frequent and severe weather events, food and water shortages, and displacement of people from low-lying areas. By being aware of these issues, individuals and communities can take action to reduce their carbon footprint, conserve resources, and advocate for policies that address climate change.

25. 1. what is the difference between the green house effect and enhance green house effect. how do they affect the earth average global temperature 2. what does climate change help earth 3. why it is important to be aware to environmental issues like global warming and climate change​


Answer:

The greenhouse effect is a natural process that occurs when certain gases, such as carbon dioxide, water vapor, and methane, trap heat in the Earth's atmosphere, leading to a warmer planet. This process is essential for life on Earth, as it helps to regulate the planet's temperature and keeps it habitable. However, the enhanced greenhouse effect refers to the increase in the concentration of these greenhouse gases due to human activities, such as burning fossil fuels and deforestation. This has resulted in a significant warming of the planet, leading to climate change.

Climate change does not necessarily help the Earth, as it has negative effects on the environment, such as sea level rise, more frequent and intense natural disasters, loss of biodiversity, and changes in weather patterns that can affect agriculture and human health. However, climate change can help spur awareness and action on environmental issues, leading to more sustainable practices and technologies that can benefit the planet in the long term.

It is important to be aware of environmental issues like global warming and climate change because they have significant impacts on the Earth's ecosystems, human health, and economies. By understanding these issues, we can take actions to reduce our greenhouse gas emissions and mitigate the effects of climate change, such as by transitioning to renewable energy, promoting sustainable agriculture and transportation, and protecting natural habitats. Being aware of these issues can also help us make informed decisions as consumers and voters, and drive policies that support a more sustainable and resilient future.
