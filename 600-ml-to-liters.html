This problem can be solve using Charle's Law wherein the relationship of volume and kelvin temperature is directly proportional. So, when you reduce the volume by 1/2 the kelvin temperature will also reduce to 1/2. So, 30 degree celsius is equal to 303.15 kelvin divide it by 2 so the answer 151.58 kelvin.

Hello!

A gas sample measure 600 mL at 30° at what temperature will it's volume reduced to half ?

We have the following information:

V1 (initial volume) = 600 mL → 0.6 L

V2 (final volume) = 300 mL → 0.3 L (reduced to half)

T1 (initial temperature) = 30ºC (in Kelvin)

TK = TC + 273.15 → TK = 30 + 273.15 → TK = 303.15

T1 (initial temperature) = 303.15 K

T2 (final temperature) = ? (in Kelvin)

*** We have an isobaric transformation, that is, when a certain mass under pressure maintains its constant pressure, on the other hand, as we increase the temperature, the volume increases and if we lower the temperature, the volume decreases.

P1 = P2 (pressure remains constant)

We apply the data to the formula of isobaric transformation (Charles and Gay-Lussac), let us see:

[tex]\dfrac{V_1}{T_1} =\dfrac{V_2}{T_2}[/tex]

[tex]\dfrac{0.6}{303.15} =\dfrac{0.3}{T_2}[/tex]

multiply the means by the extremes

[tex]0.6*T_2 = 303.15*0.3[/tex]

[tex]0.6\:T_2 = 90.945[/tex]

[tex]T_2 = \dfrac{90.945}{0.6}[/tex]

[tex]T_2 = 151.575\:\to\:\boxed{\boxed{T_2 \approx 151.58\:K}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

Answer:

151.58 K

16. What mass of CaCl2 is needed to make 600 mL of a 0.380 M solution?​


Given:

molarity = 0.380 M

volume of solution = 600 mL = 0.600 L

Required:

mass of solute

Solution:

In this problem, the solute is CaCl₂. Getting the molar mass of solute

molar mass of solute = (40.08 g/mol × 1) + (35.45 g/mol × 2)

molar mass of solute = 110.98 g/mol

Solving for the mass of solute (CaCl₂)

mass of solute = molarity × molar mass of solute × volume of solution

mass of solute = 0.380 M × 110.98 g/mol × 0.600 L

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of solute = 25.3 g}}[/tex]

Therefore, 25.3 g of CaCl₂ is needed.

--------------------------------------------------------

Note: In solving molarity problems, the volume of the solution must be expressed in liters because a 1-molar solution contains 1 mole of solute in 1 liter of solution.

1 molar (M) = 1 mole per liter (mol/L)

17. If a 600 ml of a gas measured at 4.50 atm and 127°C is compresses to avolume of 100 mL at 0°C, calculate the final pressure?​


Given:

[tex]V_{1} = \text{600 mL}[/tex]

[tex]P_{1} = \text{4.50 atm}[/tex]

[tex]T_{1} = \text{127°C + 273 = 400 K}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{100 mL}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \text{0°C + 273 = 273 K}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]P_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\frac{P_{1}V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \frac{P_{2}V_{2}}{T_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = P_{1} × \frac{V_{1}}{V_{2}} × \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{4.50 atm} × \frac{\text{600 mL}}{\text{100 mL}} × \frac{\text{273 K}}{\text{400 K}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P_{2} = \text{18.43 atm}}[/tex]
