1. yes:

The GCF of two non-zero integers, x(24) and y(72), is the greatest positive integer m(24) that divides both x(24) and y(72) without any remainder.

answer: 24

2. yes:

Find the prime factorization of 32

32 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2

Find the prime factorization of 64

64 = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2

To find the GCF, multiply all the prime factors common to both numbers:

Therefore, GCF = 2 × 2 × 2 × 2 × 2

GCF = 32

answer: 32

3. yes:

GCF of 35 and 45 is the divisor that we get when the remainder becomes 0 after doing long division repeatedly.

Step 1: Divide 45 (larger number) by 35 (smaller number).

Step 2: Since the remainder ≠ 0, we will divide the divisor of step 1 (35) by the remainder (10).

Step 3: Repeat this process until the remainder = 0.

The corresponding divisor (5) is the GCF of 35 and 45.

answer: 5

4. yes:

20. gcf and lcm of 35 and 45​


Answer:

GCF = 5

LCM = 315

Step-by-step explanation:

Factors of 35 = 1, 5, 7, 35

Factors of 45 = 1, 3, 5, 9, 15, 45

∴ The GCF is 5.

Find the prime factorization of the numbers.

Prime factorization of 35 = 5 · 7

Prime factorization of 45 = 3² · 5

Multiply the terms

LCM = 3² · 5 · 7

        = 315

∴ The LCM is 315

30. find the GCF and LCM of 35 and 45​


Answer:

What is the LCM of 35 and 45?

Find the prime factorization of 35. 35 = 5 × 7.

Find the prime factorization of 45. 45 = 3 × 3 × 5.

Multiply each factor the greater number of times it occurs in steps i) or ii) above to find the LCM: LCM = 3 × 3 × 5 × 7.

LCM = 315.
