Proof that the square root of 32.2 is 5.67450438364444

The square root of 32.2 is defined as the only positive real number such that, multiplied by itself, it is equal to 32.2

The square root of 32.2 can be written as (32.2)1/2. So,

(32.2)1/2 = (5.67450438364444 × 5.67450438364444)1/2

(32.2)1/2 = [(5.67450438364444)2]1/2

(32.2)1/2 = (5.67450438364444)2/2

(32.2)1/2 = (5.67450438364444)1

Thus,

√32.2 = 5.67450438364444

Proof that the square root of 110 is 10.48808848170152

The square root of 110 is defined as the only positive real number such that, multiplied by itself, it is equal to 110.

The square root of 110 can be written as (110)1/2. So,

(110)1/2 = (10.48808848170152 × 10.48808848170152)1/2

(110)1/2 = [(10.48808848170152)2]1/2

(110)1/2 = (10.48808848170152)2/2

(110)1/2 = (10.48808848170152)1
