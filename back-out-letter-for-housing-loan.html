7. Mrs. Simon applied a loan of P50,000 at a yearly interest of 10%. If she paid back the credit union of P65,000, what is the time period of her loan?


First, we need to find the the Percentage. 
50 000 × .10 (10%) = 5 000

Then...
65 000 - 50 000 = 15 000

Then...
15 000 ÷ 5 000 = 3


Therefore, the answer to your question is 3 years. Simple Interest Formula:
Interest = Principal x Rate x Time
I = P × R × T

Given:
Interest = Gross Amount Paid - Principal Loan
             = 65,000 - 50,000
             = 15,000

Principal = 50,000
Rate = 10%  or  0.10
Time = ?

Equation:
I = P × R × T

15,000 = (50,000) (0.10) (T)

15,000 = 5,000 (T)

[tex] \frac{15,000}{5,000} = \frac{5,000(T)}{5,000} [/tex]

T = 3

The loan has a period of 3 years.

10. Glen received $2,250 loan from bank. After six months, he paid back $45 interest and closed the loan. Find the rate of interest.​


SIMPLE INTEREST

The rate of interest is 4%.

The formula for calculating simple interest is S.I. = P R T, where P stands for principal, R for the annual percentage rate of interest, and T for time, which is typically expressed as the number of years. The interest rate is expressed as r/100 and is expressed as a percentage or r%.

GIVEN

Glen received $2,250 loan from bank. After six months, he paid back $45 interest and closed the loan.  

Question

Find the rate of interest.​

Solution

Interest = Amount - PrincipalPrincipal = $2,250I = 45The time period is 6 months.

We use the time period in years for the simple interest formula. However, the amount of time specified in the query is in months.

So let's convert the time frame to years.

6 months = 6/12 year = 1/2 years

Thus, the time frame is six months.

The formula for simple interest :

I = Prt

Then, substitute I = 45, P = 2250, t = 1/2.

45 = 2250 ⋅ r ⋅ 1/245 = 1125 ⋅ r

Divide both sides by 1125.

45/1125 = r0.04 = r

The decimal 0.04 needs to be multiplied by 100 to become a percentage.

0.04 ⋅ 100% = r4% = r

Therefore, the rate of interest is 4%.

✏️SIMPLE INTEREST===============================

Problem: Jenny applied for a loan of P15,000 at 8% annual interest. If she paid back the credit union of P17,400. How many years was her loan?

Solution: Determine the given of the following.

Given:

[tex]S.I. = ₱17\text,400 - ₱15\text,000 = ₱2\text,400[/tex][tex]P = ₱15\text,000[/tex][tex]R = 8% = 0.08[/tex][tex]T = \:? [/tex]

- Using the. Simple Interest Formula to find the time period of Jenny's loan.

[tex] \begin{aligned}& \bold{ \color{lightblue}Formula:} \\& \boxed{ S.I. = P \cdot R \cdot T } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]2 \text,400 = 15 \text,000 \cdot 0.08 \cdot T [/tex]

[tex]2 \text,400 = 1 \text,200 \cdot T [/tex]

[tex]2 \text,400 = 1 \text,200T [/tex]

[tex] \frac{2 \text,400}{1 \text,200} = \frac{ \cancel{1 \text,200}T}{ \cancel{1 \text,200}} \\ [/tex]

[tex]T = 2[/tex]

- Therefore, the time period of the loan is:
