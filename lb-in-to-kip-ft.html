3. 1. A 5 lb monkey is attached to a 20 ft hanging rope that weigh 0.30 lb/ft. The monkey climbs the rope up the top. How much work (in ft-lb) has it done?


Answer:

The work done is 3413.2 ft-lb

Explanation:

In this problem, we will use the concept of the work-energy theorem. We will relate the potential energy to work to solve the work done.

Formula to use

W = PE = mgh

where

W     is the work done, the given unit is in ft-lb

PE    is the potential energy which is equivalent to work using the concept

m      is the mass, the unit is in pounds, lb

g       is the acceleration due to gravity, 32.2 ft/s²

h       is the height, the given unit is in feet, ft

Given information

m = 5 lb

h = 20 ft

Wt = ?

Solving the problem

Here, we have two (2) work done: work done by the monkey and the hanging rope. Let us solve for each work.

Work by the monkey:

PE = mgh

PE = (5 lb)(32.2 ft/s²)(20 ft)

PE = 3220 ft-lb

Work is equal to the potential energy in work-energy theorem, therefore:

W (monkey) = 3320 ft-lb

Work by the hanging rope:

PE = mgh

PE = (0.30 lb/ft)(32.2 ft/s²)(20 ft)

PE = 193.2 ft-lb

W(rope) = 193.2 ft-lb

Now, let us solve for the total work by adding the work done by the monkey and the rope.

W(total) = 3220 ft-lb + 193.2 ft-lb

W(total) = 3413.2 ft-lb

Therefore, the work done is 3413.2 ft-lb

6. A pile of sand is in the form of a right circular cone of altitude 7 ft and slant height of 25 ft. what is the weight of the sand in lbs if its density is 100 lb/cu. ft?


Answer:

[tex]\therefore[/tex]The weight of sand is 422016lbs

Explanation:

To know the weight of the sand, first is to find the radius of the circular cone by using Pythagorean theorem. Then, find the volume of the cone by using the Volume of the Cone formula. Afterwards, multiply the volume of the cone and the density. In this way, we can find out what is the weight of the sand.

Solve for radius of the cone by using the Pythagorean theorem. Let [tex]b[/tex] be the cone's radius.

[tex]\boxed{\bold{c^2=a^2+b^2}} \\\\ \rm{25^2=7^2+b^2} \\\\ \rm{b^2=576} \\\\ \rm{b=\sqrt{576} = 24}[/tex]

Now, solve for the volume of the cone.

[tex]\boxed{\bold{V = \frac{1}{3} \pi r^2h}} \\\\ \rm{V = \frac{1}{3}(3.14)(24)^2(7)} \\\\ \rm{ = \frac{(3.14)(576)(7)}{3}} \\\\ \rm{=4220.16}[/tex]

Thus, solving for weight of the sand

[tex]\boxed{\bold{W_{Weight \ of \ the \ Sand} = V_{Volume \ of \ the \ Cone} \times D_{Density \ of \ the \ sand}}} \\\\ \rm{W = (4220.16)(100)} \\\\ \rm{W = 422016\ lbs}[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex]The weight of sand is 422016lbs
