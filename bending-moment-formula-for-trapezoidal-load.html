15. Sami put two trapezoids with the same dimensions together to make a parallelogram. the formula for the area of a trapezoid is A= 1/2 (b1+b2) h. explain why the bases of a trapezoid need to be added in the formula.1


Step-by-step explanation:

The formula for the area of a trapezoid is A = 1/2 (b1 + b2) h, where b1 and b2 are the lengths of the two parallel bases of the trapezoid, and h is the height of the trapezoid.

The bases of a trapezoid need to be added in the formula because the area of a trapezoid is the average of the lengths of the parallel bases multiplied by the height of the trapezoid. The height is the perpendicular distance between the parallel bases.

If we just used the height of the trapezoid to calculate the area, we would be ignoring the fact that the trapezoid has two different lengths for its parallel sides. The bases determine the overall size of the trapezoid, and therefore are a crucial part of calculating its area.

In the case of Sami putting two trapezoids together to form a parallelogram, the bases of the trapezoids would become the opposite sides of the parallelogram. Adding the bases of the trapezoids in the formula would give the total area of the parallelogram, which is equal to the sum of the areas of the two trapezoids.
