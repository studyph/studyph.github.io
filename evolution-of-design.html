Convergent evolution is the process where two or different species develop similar traits, in spite having the different ancestor. An example is wings of birds, bats, insects. 26. what is evolutions? is evolution important?why​


Answer:

Evolution is the process by which living things change over time. Each time a creature reproduces there is a small chance that the genetic code of the offspring will be slightly altered. Very rarely that small change will improve the creatures prospects of having offspring and so that change starts to dominate within a population group. Over time these changes accumulate.

Evolution provides insight into our biological history and functions.It helps us to understand genetics and their mutations, even giving us a means for predicting chances of certain medical issues by looking at your ancestry.It aids in medical advancements, including strategies for fighting viruses, cancers, and all sorts of mutating causes of disease.It has allowed us to improve and extend our lives.
