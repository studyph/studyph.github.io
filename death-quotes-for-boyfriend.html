2. Explain this quote by Haruki Murakami according to your level of understanding: “Death is not the opposite of life but a part of it” ​


Answer:

I believe the full quote is:

"Death is not the opposite of life but an innate part of it. By living our lives, we nurture death."

Simply put, because every life comes with inevitable and inescapable death, no one can attain immortality and live forever. All living sentient beings on this earth have a limited and finite life span. Death is natural, part of every living beings' life cycle; and therefore, is common to us all.

Hence, we should not be afraid of, or fear death. Rather, we should take on death as an integral part - stage - of our natural human life. We know that every living thing must come to face death at some point; therefore, why not just come to recognize it, acknowledge it, and be at peace with our future death?

Death is an inherent part of being human. Death is part of every life cycle. From the time we are born up until the time we die, we all know that we will eventually face death head-on. So embrace death while we're still alive, because it is part of us and part of who we are as humans.

17. . Sandra and her boyfriend had a fight. She slapped her boyfriend at an angle of 400 and landed 0.31 m away from her. With what velocity did Sandra slap her boyfriend if the contact time is 0.99 seconds?


To solve for the Velocity or Initial Velocity that Sandra had to slap her boyfriend, first you must look at the given.  

The given is an angle of 40 degrees so that will be your theta, next, you have the range or the distance of Sandra's Boyfriend from Sandra after the slap which is 0.31m, after that, the contact time or total time of 0.99s.  

With all of this, what's missing to complete the information is the Velocity which is what's being asked.

To find what is asked, you must look at the given which is:

Range (R) = 0.31m

Total time or Contact Time (Tt) = 0.99s

Theta (θ) = 40°

In the formulas used in Projectile Motion at an Angle, the formula that has range is R = VxTt. So we have to use formula and derive from it. R = VxTt will become R/Tt = Vx because we are trying to find the Initial Velocity of the slap. So it is 0.31m/0.99s = Vx and the Vx is equal to 0.31m/s, but we are not done yet. That is the Horizontal Velocity but we are trying to find the Initial Velocity.

Formula:

[tex]Vx = \frac{R}{Tt}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]Vx =\frac{0.31}{0.99}[/tex]

[tex]Vx = 0.31[/tex]

Final Answer:

[tex]Vx = 0.31 m/s[/tex]

To find the Initial Velocity, we must use another formula. The formula that we need to use next is Vx = Vi cos(Theta) and derive the needed formula that we need. It will become Vx/cos(Theta) = Vi because Vi is missing and we have a given of Vx and Theta. The solution is now 0.31m/s divided by cosine 40. By doing that, the answer will become Vi = 0.4m/s.

Formula:

[tex]Vi = \frac{Vx}{cos}[/tex](_θ)

Solution:

[tex]Vi = \frac{0.31}{cos40}[/tex]

[tex]vi = 0.4[/tex]

Final Answer:

[tex]vi = 0.4 m/s[/tex]

*In using significant figures in problems like this, the number of significant figures that you will use in your final answer is the least number of significant figures in the given. In this problem, both given have one significant figure, therefore, we will also use one in the given.

The INITIAL VELOCITY of Sandra's slap to her boyfriend is 0.4m/s
