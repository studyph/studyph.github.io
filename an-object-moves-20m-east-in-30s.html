m=500kg

10. A body move at a rate of 20m/s in 5s calculate the distance​


Answer:

To calculate the distance travelled by the body, we can use the formula:

distance = rate x time

Where "rate" is the speed of the body and "time" is the duration for which it has been moving.

Given that the body moves at a rate of 20 m/s for 5 seconds, we can substitute these values into the formula:

distance = 20 m/s x 5 s

distance = 100 meters

Therefore, the distance travelled by the body is 100 meters.

15. An object moving with a speed of 20m/s. If it is then accelarated with 6 m/s², then find the displacement covered by the object in 5 seconds.​


Answer:

[tex]\qquad \qquad {\boxed{\orange{\pmb{\sf{Distance,\ (s)\ =\ 175\ m}}}}} [/tex]

Explanation:

Need to FinD :

We need to calculate the displacement covered by the object at the end of 5 seconds.

Given : We are given with the object moving with a speed of 20 m/s. It is accelerated with a velocity of 6 m/s².

We know that, we're given the initial velocity of the object, that is 20 m/s. Also, the object is accelerating with a velocity of 6 m/s².

We need to calculate the displacement covered by the object at the end of 5 s.

So, by using a required formula we will calculate the displacement covered.

[tex] \qquad \qquad \qquad {\boxed{\sf{\blacksquare\ {\red{s\ =\ ut\ +\ \dfrac{1}{2}at^2}}}}} [/tex]

By using the above formula, which is the second equation of motion that shows the "Position-Time Relation", we will calculate the displacement covered.

[tex] \red {\frak{Where}} \begin{cases} &\sf {s\ denotes\ the\ displacement.} \\ &\sf {u\ denotes\ the\ initial\ velocity.} \\ &\sf{t\ denotes\ the\ time.} \\ &\sf{a\ denotes\ the\ acceleration.} \end{cases}[/tex]

So, by applying the required values in the second equation of motion, we will calculate the displacement covered at the end of 5 s.

[tex] \bigstar {\underline{\underline{\red{\sf{Finding\ the\ displacement\ covered:-}}}}} [/tex]

[tex] \sf \dashrightarrow {s\ =\ ut\ +\ \dfrac{1}{2}at^2} \\ \\ \\ \sf \dashrightarrow {s\ =\ (20\ m/s \times 5\ s)\ +\ \dfrac{1}{2} \times 6\ m/s^2 \times (5\ s)^2} \\ \\ \\ \sf \dashrightarrow {s\ =\ 100\ m\ +\ 3\ m/s^2 \times 25\ s} \\ \\ \\ \sf \dashrightarrow {s\ =\ 100\ m\ +\ 75\ m} \\ \\ \\ \dashrightarrow {\underbrace{\boxed{\pink{\frak{Displacement,\ (s)\ =\ 175\ m}}}}_{\sf \blue {\tiny{Displacement\ Covered}}}} [/tex]

∴ Hence, the required displacement covered by the object at the end of 5 s is 175 m.
