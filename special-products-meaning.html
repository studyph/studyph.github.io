1. meaning of special product in math


There are a couple of special instances where there are easier ways to find the product of two binomials than multiplying each term in the first binomial.

2. What is the meaning of special product?


The special products are the results of the special cases in multiplying binomials with other binomials.

There are common special cases considered in Mathematics that give special products, these are the following:

1. Multiplying the binomial (a + b) by itself

-Instead of executing the FOIL method [(a + b)(a + b)] in getting the answer, we can directly use a^2 + 2ab + b^2.

2. Multiplying the binomial (a - b) by itself

-Instead of executing the FOIL method [(a - b)(a - b)] in getting the answer, we can directly use a^2 - 2ab + b^2.

3. Multiplying binomials with an opposite operations but with the same terms

-In getting the answer of [(a + b)(a - b)], we can directly use a^2 - b^2.
