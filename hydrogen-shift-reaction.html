1. The following reaction is exothermic. C2H4(g)+Br2(g)⇌C2H4Br2(g) Predict the effect (shift right, shift left, or no effect) of increasing the reaction temperature. 1.) Predict the effect (shift right, shift left, or no effect) of increasing the reaction temperature. a.) shift right b.) shift left c.) no effect 2.) Predict the effect of decreasing the reaction temperature. a.) shift right b.) shift left c.) no effect


Increasing the reaction temperature.b.) shift left

Decreasing the reaction temperature.a.) shift right

Further explanation

In the equilibrium system, according to Le Chatelier, if there is a reaction there will be action

Reaction = - action

The reaction taken by the system is to shift the reaction to the right or left.

The composition of chemical reactions in equilibrium can be influenced by temperature, pressure, and concentration.

If the system temperature is raised, then the equilibrium reaction will reduce the temperature by shifting the reaction in a direction that requires heat (endotherms). Conversely, if the temperature is lowered, then the equilibrium shifts to a reaction that releases heat (exothermic)

The following reaction is exothermic.

C₂H₄ (g) + Br₂ (g) ⇌C₂H₄Br₂ (g)

Because exothermic., Then the value of ΔH is negative (ΔH = -), so the reaction to the right releases heat

1. increasing the reaction temperature.

As the temperature rises, the equilibrium will shift towards the endothermic reaction, so the reaction shifts to the left towards C₂H₄ (g) + Br₂ (g)

2. decreasing the reaction temperature.

At a decrease in temperature, the equilibrium will shift towards the exothermic reaction, so the reaction shifts right toward C₂H₄Br₂

7. According to the following reaction, how many moles of hydrogen iodide will be formed upon the complete reaction of 0.771 moles hydrogen gas with excess iodine?​


Answer:

n HI = 1.542 mol

Explanation:

Write the balanced chemical equation.

H2 + I2 → 2HI

Solve for the number of moles of HI formed.

n HI = 0.771 mol H2 × (2 mol HI / 1 mol H2)

n HI = 1.542 mol

15. Which of the following shows a chemical reaction in which magnesium and hydrogen sulfide are the reactants and magnesium sulfide and hydrogen are the products? A. magnesium sulfide + hydrogen → magnesium + hydrogen sulfide B. magnesium + magnesium sulfide → hydrogen sulfide + hydrogen C. magnesium + hydrogen sulfide → magnesium sulfide + hydrogen D. magnesium + hydrogen → magnesium sulfide + hydrogen sulfide


Answer:  C. magnesium + hydrogen sulfide → magnesium sulfide + hydrogen

In chemical reactions, it always involve both reactants and products. Reactants are the substances that start a chemical reaction, and products are substances that are produced in the reaction. Reactants are usually at the left of the tail of the arrow (→ ) and products are at the right or tip of the arrow (→) if it is forward reaction. And if it is backward reaction, it is the reverse of the forward reaction.

Given: Reactants: magnesium, hydrogen sulfide

           Products: magnesium sulfide, hydrogen

Answer: magnesium + hydrogen sulfide → magnesium sulfide + hydrogen
