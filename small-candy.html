19. a box contains 25 small chocolates bars 50 pieces of sweet candies a 50 pieces of polvorow if an item is taken out of the box what is the probability A. a sweet candy? B. small chocolate bar? C.small chocolate bar or polvoron? D.not a polvoron​


Answer:

A. The probability of getting a sweet candy is:

P(sweet candy) = number of sweet candies / total number of items in the box

P(sweet candy) = 50 / (25 + 50 + 50) = 50/125 = 0.4

B. The probability of getting a small chocolate bar is:

P(small chocolate bar) = number of small chocolate bars / total number of items in the box

P(small chocolate bar) = 25 / (25 + 50 + 50) = 25/125 = 0.2

C. The probability of getting a small chocolate bar or polvoron is:

P(small chocolate bar or polvoron) = (number of small chocolate bars + number of polvoron) / total number of items in the box

P(small chocolate bar or polvoron) = (25 + 50) / (25 + 50 + 50) = 75/125 = 0.6

D. The probability of not getting a polvoron is:

P(not a polvoron) = 1 - P(polvoron) = 1 - (number of polvoron / total number of items in the box)

P(not a polvoron) = 1 - (50 / (25 + 50 + 50)) = 1 - 0.4 = 0.6

Therefore, the probability of getting a sweet candy is 0.4, the probability of getting a small chocolate bar is 0.2, the probability of getting a small chocolate bar or polvoron is 0.6, and the probability of not getting a polvoron is 0.6.

28. karen and holly took heir families out to the movie theater. karen bought three boxes of candy and two small bags of popcorn and paid $18.35.holly bought four boxes of candy and three small bags of popcorn and paid $26.05. find the cost for a box of candy.


Answer:

$2.95

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x be the cost of a box of candy while y be the cost of a small bag of popcorn.

Out of the given data, two equations is formulated.

[tex]3x+2y=18.35[/tex]  Equation 1

[tex]4x+3y=26.05[/tex]  Equation 2

Multiply 3 to both sides of Eq.1 to derive Eq.1'

[tex]3(3x+2y=18.35)\\9x+6y=55.05[/tex]

Multiply 2 to both sides of Eq.2 to derive Eq.2'

[tex]2(4x+3y=26.05)\\8x+6y=52.10[/tex]

Elimination using Eq.1' and Eq.2' to derive x

[tex]\left[\begin{array}{ccc}9x&+6y&=55.05\\8x&+6y&=52.10\\x&&=2.95\end{array}\right][/tex]

A box of candy costs $2.95
