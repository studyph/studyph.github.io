16. ambag ni justinian at constantine sa imperyong byzantine?


Si Emperor Constantine (reg. 306-337 CE) ay pinaniniwalaang nagtatag ng Byzantine Empire sa pamamagitan ng kanyang mga reporma at paglilipat ng kabisera ng Roman Empire sa Constantinople (nagngangalang Byzantium noon). Ipinakilala rin niya ang Christianity sa emperyo at nagtatag ng mga batas at patakaran upang itaguyod ang relihiyon na ito.

Si Emperor Justinian I (reg. 527-565 CE) ay nagsulong ng mga reporma sa Byzantine Empire upang mapalakas ang kanyang kapangyarihan at mapanatili ang pagkakaisa ng kanyang mga nasasakupan. Pinangunahan niya ang pagpapalawak ng teritoryo ng emperyo sa pamamagitan ng pakikipaglaban sa iba't ibang kalaban at pag-aangkin ng mga teritoryo. Isinulong din niya ang pagpapakalat ng Byzantine culture at nasimulan ang konstruksyon ng mga makasaysayang gusali tulad ng Hagia Sophia.

Kaya, ang kanilang mga ambag ay hindi lamang nagbigay ng mga pangunahing pangyayari sa kasaysayan ng Byzantine Empire, ngunit nagpabago rin ng kultura at paniniwala ng mga tao sa nasasakupan nila.

Answer:

Justinian was born in Tauresium, Dardania, around 482. A native speaker of Latin (possibly the last Roman emperor to be one),[11] he came from a peasant family believed to have been of Illyro-Roman or Thraco-Roman origins The cognomen Iustinianus, which he took later, is indicative of adoption by his uncle Justin. During his reign, he founded Justiniana Prima not far from his birthplace. His mother was Vigilantia, the sister of Justin. Justin, who was commander of one of the imperial guard units (the Excubitors) before he became emperor, adopted Justinian, brought him to Constantinople, and ensured the boy's education. As a result, Justinian was well educated in jurisprudence, theology and Roman history. Justinian served as a candidatus, one of 40 men selected from the scholae palatinae to serve as the emperor's personal bodyguard. The chronicler John Malalas, who lived during the reign of Justinian, describes his appearance as short, fair skinned, curly haired, round faced and handsome. Another contemporary historian, Procopius, compares Justinian's appearance to that of tyrannical Emperor Domitian, although this is probably slander.

When Emperor Anastasius died in 518, Justin was proclaimed the new emperor, with significant help from Justinian. During Justin's reign (518–527), Justinian was the emperor's close confidant. Justinian showed a lot of ambition, and it has been thought that he was functioning as virtual regent long before Justin made him associate emperor on 1 April 527, although there is no conclusive evidence of this. As Justin became senile near the end of his reign, Justinian became the de facto ruler. Following the general Vitalian's assassination presumed to be orchestrated by Justinian or Justin, Justinian was appointed consul in 521 and later commander of the army of the east. Upon Justin's death on 1 August 527, Justinian became the sole sovereign.

Answer:

This analysis of “Désirée’s Baby,” an 1893 short story by Kate Chopin, explores the narrative of miscegenation and motherhood in the antebellum American South. Chopin is best known for the groundbreaking classic novella The Awakening (1899).

Désirée, a young woman adopted as a child, is married to a plantation owner named Armaud. She is startled when she suddenly realizes that their baby is of a darker complexion than her own and her husband’s.

Chopin uses the setting of Louisiana to create a delicate yet hostile environment for a disillusioned young mother. Motherhood in this era can be deemed sensitive and complicated, since Désirée lives in a time when its treatment is based on ethnicity and social stratification.
