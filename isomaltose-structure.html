23. What determines the vulnerability of structural and non-structural elements of a physical structure?​


Answer:

Structural measures are any physical construction to reduce or avoid possible impacts of hazards, or the application of engineering techniques or technology to achieve hazard resistance and resilience in structures or systems. Non-structural measures are measures not involving physical construction which use knowledge, practice or agreement to reduce disaster risks and impacts, in particular through policies and laws, public awareness raising, training and education.

Annotation: Common structural measures for disaster risk reduction include dams, flood levies, ocean wave barriers, earthquake-resistant construction and evacuation shelters. Common non-structural measures include building codes, land-use planning laws and their enforcement, research and assessment, information resources and public awareness programmes. Note that in civil and structural engineering, the term “structural” is used in a more restricted sense to mean just the load-bearing structure, and other parts such as wall cladding and interior fittings are termed “non-structural”.

26. 2. Wings of butterfly and bat is an example of a genetic structure b. vestigial structure c. analogous structure d. homologous structure​


Answer:

The correct answer is the letter C

Explanation:

The wings of a butterfly and a bat are an example of analogous structures. Analogous structures are structures that have similar functions but have evolved independently in different organisms and do not share a common ancestor. In this case, the wings of a butterfly and a bat have both evolved for flight, but the structures themselves are very different in terms of their genetic and structural composition. Homologous structures, on the other hand, are structures that have similar anatomy and genetic basis because they are inherited from a common ancestor. Vestigial structures are structures that have lost their original function over time but are still present in the organism.
