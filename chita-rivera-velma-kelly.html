"Homestretch" by Velma Pollard is a short story that makes use of symbolism to convey its underlying theme. Here are some examples of symbolism used in the story:

The horse - The horse in the story represents the aspirations and dreams of the characters. The horse symbolizes the things that the characters are striving for and hoping to achieve.

The track - The race track is a symbol of life's journey. The characters are running a race, just as we all are in life. The track symbolizes the obstacles and challenges that we all face in our lives.

The jockey - The jockey represents the person in control of their life. The jockey must navigate the horse through the obstacles on the track, just as we must navigate our lives through the challenges that we face.

The finish line - The finish line represents the goal that the characters are trying to reach. It symbolizes the achievement that they are striving for, whether it be financial success, happiness, or some other form of fulfillment.

Overall, the story uses these symbols to convey the message that success in life requires determination, hard work, and a willingness to face obstacles head-on. The characters in the story are all striving for something, but they must first overcome the challenges and obstacles that stand in their way before they can achieve their goals.

17. Explain the role of Brenda in advancing the theme of identity in Homestretch by Velma Pollard.​


In Homestretch by Velma Pollard, Brenda plays a significant role in advancing the theme of identity. Brenda is a young Jamaican woman who moves to Canada with her husband and daughter in search of a better life. However, she struggles to adjust to life in a new country and feels like she has lost her sense of identity.

Throughout the novel, Brenda's experiences in Canada highlight the challenges of maintaining one's identity while living in a foreign country. For example, Brenda is forced to confront racial discrimination in Canada, which makes her feel like an outsider. She is also frustrated by the fact that her daughter is losing touch with her Jamaican heritage and is becoming more Canadian.

As Brenda tries to navigate these challenges, she begins to question her own identity and her sense of belonging. She feels torn between her Jamaican roots and her new life in Canada, and struggles to reconcile these two parts of her identity. This internal conflict is a central theme of the novel.

Brenda's experiences also highlight the importance of community and support networks in maintaining one's identity. She finds solace in her friendship with Miss Ruby, a Jamaican woman who has lived in Canada for many years. Miss Ruby becomes a mentor figure for Brenda, offering guidance and support as she tries to navigate her new life.

Overall, Brenda's character plays a vital role in advancing the theme of identity in Homestretch. Her experiences highlight the challenges of maintaining one's identity while living in a foreign country and the importance of community and support networks in this process.

29. KELLY,MARVIN AND INDHU SAVED $100 ALTOGETHER. KELLY SAVED $16 MORE THAN INDHU. MARVIN SAVE TWICE THE AMOUNT INDHU SAVED. HOW MUCH DID KELLY SAVED?


Answer:

Kelly saved $37.

Step-by-step explanation:

Indhu's savings: x

Kelly's savings: x + 16  (16 more than Indhu's)

Marvin's savings: 2x  (twice the amount Indhu saved)

Equation:

x + (x+16) + (2x) = 100

4x + 16 = 100

4x = 100-16

Solve for Indhu's savings, x:

4x/4 = 84/4

x = 21

Hindhu saved $21.

Find how much Marvin and Kelly saved:

Kelly: x+16  ⇒ 21+16=  $37

Marvin: 2x  ⇒  2(21) = $42

Kelly saved $37.

Check:

$21 + $37 + $42 = $100

$100 = $100 (True)
