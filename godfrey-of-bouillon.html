Godfrey of Bouillon (Pranses: Godefroy de Bouillon , Olandes: Godfried van Bouillon , Aleman: Gottfried von Bouillon , Latin: Godefridus Bullionensis ; 18 Setyembre 1060 - 18 Hulyo 1100) ay isang Frankish na kabalyero at isa sa mga pinuno ng Unang Krusada mula 1096 hanggang sa pagtatapos nito noong 1099. Siya ang Panginoon ni Bouillon, kung saan kinuha niya ang kanyang pangalan, mula 1076 at ang Duke of Lower Lorraine mula 1087. Pagkatapos ng matagumpay na pagkubkob ng Jerusalem noong 1099, si Godfrey ang naging unang pinuno ng Kaharian ng Jerusalem. Gayunman, tinanggihan niya ang pamagat ng Hari, na naniniwala siya na ang tunay na Hari ng Jerusalem ay si Cristo, na pinipili ang pamagat ng Tagapagtaguyod (ie tagapagtanggol o tagapagtanggol) ng Banal na Sepulcher (Latin: Advocatus Sancti Sepulchri ). Siya ay kilala rin bilang ang "Baron ng Banal na Sepulcher" at ang "Crusader King".

18. Which of the following is a clear soup?a. bisque b. bouillon c. cream d. puree


Answer:

B. Bouillon

Explanation:

Soup is a liquid soup mostly served as hot made by boiling meat, vegetables and fish.

Two Categories:

1. Clear Soup- a soup with no thickening agents made of clear broth.

Examples:

Bouillon and Broth- a soup made with sliced meat, fish, vegetable, herbs in water. It contains nutritional ingredients which makes it popular in hospitals.

Consomme- a soup made from richly flavored stock which includes egg whites to concentrate any solid in the soup.

2. Thick Soup- a soup made with thickening agents such as flour, cream, cornstarch and other ingredients.

Examples:

Bisque- a creamy soup made from extracting flavors of shellfish.

Cream- a roux thinned with cream or milk with broth and preferred ingredients.

Potage- a soup from northern France made from combined meat and vegetables with thick mush.

26. what is the importance of a holistic perspective as pointed out by the poet John Godfrey Saxe?​


Answer:

What is Holistic Perspective?

It means you are looking at a bigger picture of something or the situation. It doesn't focus on one angle or one part, but it studies the subject as a whole.

John Godfrey Saxe

- is the poet of the poem "The Blind Men and the Elephant".

What was the poem all about?

The poem was all about a group of 6 blind men who tried to conceptualize an Elephant, an animal they'd not known before. Due to their blindness, each of them touch only a part of the elephant's body and mistook it as another being or another object. One man touched the side of the elephant and assumed it was like a wall, another touched the tusk and assumed it was like a spear, another man touched the trunk and assumed it was like a snake, next man touched the leg and assumed it was like a tree, another man touched the ears and assumed it was like a big fan, and the last blind man touched the tail and assumed it was like a rope. They talked about their obversation, and argued because their observations differ from one another. They're all touching the same thing but observing from different angles.

What's the importance of a Holistic Perspective as pointed out by the poet John Godfrey Saxe?

John Godfrey Saxe in his poem is trying to imply that we need to look at something holistically for deeper understanding. And if everyone understands it holistically, arguments are no longer necessary because without understanding an argument has no right nor wrong, it's just fools arguing about their beliefs.

30. 3. What is the moral lesson of the poem Blind men and the Elephant .by John Godfrey Saxe ​


Answer:

The parable of the blind men and an elephant originated in the ancient Indian subcontinent, from where it has been widely diffused. It is a story of a group of blind men who have never come across an elephant before and who learn and conceptualize what the elephant is like by touching it. Each blind man feels a different part of the elephant's body, but only one part, such as the side or the tusk. They then describe the elephant based on their limited experience and their descriptions of the elephant are different from each other. In some versions, they come to suspect that the other person is dishonest and they come to blows. The moral of the parable is that humans have a tendency to claim absolute truth based on their limited, subjective experience as they ignore other people's limited, subjective experiences which may be equally true.
