Answer:

Everything on earth is made up of elements and compound so without them, we cannot live even in a single day.

We encounter compounds in our daily life: from the food we eat, the water we drink, and the air we breathe. These compounds become our needs for our growth and survival. A compound is a pure substance formed by the chemical union of two or more elements in a definite ratio, which is fixed by nature. An example of a compound used in our daily life is water with a chemical formula of H2O. A water molecule consists of one oxygen atom combined with two hydrogen atoms. We use water throughout the day: drinking, cooking, agriculture, and household chores. In the morning, the first thing that comes to our mind is brushing our teeth. There are many varieties of toothpaste; like an anti-cavity, extra whitening, sensitivity, stronger gums, and many more. Toothpaste doesn't only make our teeth white, it may also be used in cleaning some stuff. Hydrogen Peroxide is used in a wide range of cleaning and personal care products. We start our day after taking a bath, like before going to school, work, and to run some errands. Soap is a sodium salt or potassium salt of many combinations of fatty acids having cleansing action in the water. Nowadays, we used alcohol to protect us from bacterias and germs that is a risk to our lives. Alcohol is an organic compound that carries at least one hydroxyl functional group bound to a saturated carbon atom. The term alcohol originally referred to the primary alcohol ethanol, which is used as a drug and is the main alcohol present in alcoholic beverages. It is also used as an antiseptic in medical wipes and hand sanitizers. These compounds make our life survivable and easier.
