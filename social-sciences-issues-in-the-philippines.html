LEVEL 1: Main Topic

The main topic is the Current Issues in the Philippines.

LEVEL 2: Major Disciplines with Description

Politics - This involves the study of government systems and political behavior. It includes the analysis of power relations, decision-making processes, and the interaction between different groups and institutions within society.

Economics - This pertains to the study of the production, distribution, and consumption of goods and services. It includes the analysis of market trends, economic policies, and their impact on different sectors of society.

Social Sciences - This encompasses various fields of study such as sociology, anthropology, psychology, and history. It involves the examination of human behavior, cultural norms and practices, and social institutions.

LEVEL 3: Current Issues Found in the Philippines with Explanation

Political instability and corruption - The Philippines has long been plagued by political instability and corruption, with allegations of vote-buying, graft and corruption, and abuse of power among public officials. This has resulted in a lack of trust in government institutions and political leaders, as well as hindering the country's economic development and social progress.

Income inequality - Despite recent economic growth, income inequality in the Philippines remains a major concern. A significant portion of the population continues to live below the poverty line, while a small percentage of the population controls a disproportionate share of the country's wealth. This creates social tensions and inhibits the country's long-term economic growth potential.

Education - The Philippines faces significant challenges in providing quality education to its citizens, particularly in rural areas. Access to education remains a problem, with many students dropping out due to lack of resources and support. This limits their opportunities for upward mobility and negatively impacts the country's long-term human capital development.

LEVEL 4: Integration of the 3rd Level

The three major issues in the Philippines identified in the third level are interconnected and have significant implications for the country's overall development. Political instability and corruption can hinder efforts to address income inequality and improve access to education. Addressing income inequality can help reduce social tensions and promote more inclusive economic growth, which can, in turn, improve access to education and other social services. Improved education can help address income inequality and empower individuals and communities to engage more actively in the political process and demand greater accountability from their leaders.
