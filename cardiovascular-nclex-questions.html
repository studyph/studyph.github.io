8. Answer these questions in Tagalog Cardiovascular Endurance meaning __________ Muscular strenght meaning ______________ body compositon meaning________ flexibility meaning _________ Muscular Endurance meanign __________]


Explanation:

—Cardiovascular endurance is how efficiently your heart, blood vessels, and lungs to supply oxygen rich blood to working muscles during physical activity (aerobic activity like walking, running, cycling or playing a sport) for a prolonged period of time or for more than 90 seconds.

— Muscular strength is the amount of force you can put out or the amount of weight you can lift. Muscular endurance is how many times you can move that weight without getting exhausted (very tired).

—Flexibility is the ability of a joint or series of joints to move through an unrestricted, pain free range of motion. Although flexibility varies widely from person to person, minimum ranges are necessary for maintaining joint and total body health.

—Muscular endurance is the ability of a muscle or group of muscles to perform repetitive contractions against a force for an extended period of time. The greater your muscular endurance the higher number of repetitions you could complete.

29. Directions: Answer the question below: 1. Which of the two locomotor movements: hopping and jumping, highly develops cardiovascular endurance? Explain why.​


Answer:

Both

Explanation:

Both hopping and jumping are locomotor movements that can develop cardiovascular endurance, but jumping is considered to be more effective in doing so. Jumping involves a greater range of motion and requires more explosive power, which means that it places a greater demand on the cardiovascular system. This results in an increase in heart rate and breathing rate, leading to improved cardiovascular endurance over time.

Hopping, on the other hand, is a less intense movement that is often used as a warm-up or cool-down exercise. While it can still contribute to the development of cardiovascular endurance, it is not as effective as jumping in doing so.

Overall, both hopping and jumping are beneficial for physical fitness, but jumping is the more effective choice for developing cardiovascular endurance.

Answer:

Hopping and jumping are both physical activities that require the use of the lower body and can contribute to the development of cardiovascular endurance. However, jumping is typically considered more effective at promoting cardiovascular endurance.

This is because jumping involves a greater range of motion and uses more muscle groups than hopping. In particular, jumping requires the use of larger muscle groups like the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and calves, which work together to generate power and lift the body off the ground. This increased demand on the muscles also requires a greater supply of oxygen and nutrients, which results in an increased heart rate and breathing rate to meet the body's demands.

Furthermore, jumping can be performed continuously for longer periods of time compared to hopping, which typically involves shorter bursts of activity. This sustained activity further challenges the cardiovascular system and helps to improve cardiovascular endurance over time.

In summary, while both hopping and jumping can contribute to cardiovascular endurance, jumping is generally more effective due to its greater demand on the muscles and its ability to be performed for longer periods of time.
