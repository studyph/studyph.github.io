18. What is included in the civil liability of a person criminally liable and how it is made.


Answer:

Civil liability is a legal obligation that requires a party to pay for damages or to follow other court-enforcements in a lawsuit. Different from criminal liability, which is often brought by the state to redress a public wrong, civil liability is usually brought by a private party to sue for damages or injunctions.

Empathy: We can encourage individuals to put themselves in others' shoes and understand their perspectives, feelings, and experiences.

Respect: We can promote mutual respect for individuals from different backgrounds, cultures, religions, and beliefs.

Cooperation: We can encourage collaboration and teamwork across cultures, fostering a sense of shared purpose and mutual support.
