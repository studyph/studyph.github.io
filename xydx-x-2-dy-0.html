3. What is xydx-(x^2+3y^2) dy=0 in a homogeneous differential equation​


HOMOGENEOUS DIFFERENTIAL EQUATION

The solution of homogenous differential equation xydx - (x^2+3y^2) dy = 0 is  ﻿[tex]\frac{x^2+y^2}{3xy} - ln |x| = C[/tex]

FURTHER EXPLANATION

A homogeneous differential equation is one that has a homogeneous function as one of its components. If f(kx, ky) = k^n·f(x, y), for any non-zero constant, the function is said to be homogenous. The homogeneous differential equation has the form f in its general form f(x, y). dy + g(x, y) (x, y). dx = 0. The degree of the homogeneous differential equation is the same for the equation's variables x and y.

When a first-order differential equation can take the following form, it is homogeneous.

dy/dx = F(x, y)

Example:

dy/dx = (x + y)/(x - y)dy/dx = x(3x - 3y)/y^2dy/dx = (x^2 + y^2)/2xydy/dx = (5x + 2y)/(x - y)dy/dx = (x^3 + y^3)/(2xy2 + 5yx^2)

We can substitute x = kx and y = ky in the examples above to demonstrate the homogeneous differential equation. Additionally, we replace x/y = v or x = vy if the homogeneous differential equation of the form dx/dy = f(x, y) and f(x, y) is a homogeneous function. The general solution of the homogeneous differential equation is obtained by integrating this further and then substituting back the variables x and y.

How To Solve a Homogeneous Differential Equation?

By integrating the specified differential equation, the general solution of the homogeneous differential equation can be found. By first separating the variable and the derivative of the specific variable on each side and then integrating it with regard to the variable, it is possible to solve a homogeneous differential equation of the type dy/dx = f(x, y).

We substitute y = v.x in order to solve a homogeneous differential equation of the type dy/dx = f(x, y). With this solution, the issue is simple to incorporate and resolve. By further differentiating y = vx, we obtain dy/dx = v + x.dv/dx with regard to x. The following expression can be obtained by changing the value of dy/dx in the formula dy/dx = f(x, y) = g(y/x).

v + x dv/dx = g(v)x dv/dx = g(v) - v

With the variables x and v separated, we have:

dy/(g(v)-v) = dx/x

Integrating both sides of the equation.

After integration, v=y/x is replaced.

8. General solution of: (1-xy)^-2 dx + [y^2 + x^2 (1-xy)^-2]dy = 0​


[tex] \Large \bold{SOLUTION\ 1:} [/tex]

[tex] \small \begin{array}{l} \text{First, we need to check if the given differential} \\ \text{equation is exact.} \\ \\ (1-xy)^{-2} dx + \big[y^2 + x^2 (1-xy)^{-2}\big]dy = 0 \\ \\ \dfrac{dx}{(1-xy)^2} + \left[y^2 + \dfrac{x^2}{(1-xy)^2}\right]dy = 0 \\ \\ \quad M(x, y) dx + N(x, y) dy = 0 \end{array} [/tex]

[tex] \small \begin{array}{l l} M(x,y) = \dfrac{1}{(1 - xy)^2}, & N(x,y) = y^2 + \dfrac{x^2}{(1-xy)^2}\\ \\ \dfrac{\partial M}{\partial y} = \dfrac{-2(-x)}{(1 - xy)^3}, & \dfrac{\partial N}{\partial x} = \dfrac{2x}{(1 - xy)^2} + \dfrac{-2(-y)x^2}{(1 - xy)^3} \\ \\ \dfrac{\partial M}{\partial y} = \dfrac{2x}{(1 - xy)^3}, & \dfrac{\partial N}{\partial x} = \dfrac{2x(1 - xy)+2x^2y}{(1 - xy)^3} \\ \\ \: & \dfrac{\partial N}{\partial x} = \dfrac{2x}{(1 - xy)^3} \end{array} [/tex]

[tex] \small \begin{array}{l} \dfrac{\partial M}{\partial y} = \dfrac{\partial N}{\partial x} \implies \text{Differential equation is exact.} \\ \\ \dfrac{\partial F}{\partial x} = M(x, y) = \dfrac{1}{(1 - xy)^2} \\ \displaystyle F(x, y) = \int \dfrac{1}{(1 - xy)^2} \partial x = -\dfrac{1}{y} \int \dfrac{1}{(1 - xy)^2}(-y)\partial x \\ \\ F(x, y) = \dfrac{1}{y(1 - xy)} + h(y) \\ \\ \dfrac{\partial F}{\partial y} = N(x, y) = y^2 + \dfrac{x^2}{(1-xy)^2} \\ \\ \dfrac{\partial}{\partial y}\left[\dfrac{1}{y(1 - xy)} + h(y)\right] = y^2 + \dfrac{x^2}{(1-xy)^2} \\ \\ -\dfrac{1 - xy + y(-x)}{y^2(1 - xy)^2} + h'(y) = y^2 + \dfrac{x^2}{(1-xy)^2} \\ \\ -\dfrac{1 - 2xy}{y^2(1 - xy)^2} + h'(y) = y^2 + \dfrac{x^2}{(1-xy)^2} \\ \\ h'(y) = y^2 + \dfrac{x^2}{(1-xy)^2} + \dfrac{1 - 2xy}{y^2(1 - xy)^2} \\ \\ h'(y) = y^2 + \dfrac{x^2y^2 - 2xy + 1}{y^2(1-xy)^2} = y^2 + \dfrac{1}{y^2} \\ \\ h(y) = \dfrac{y^3}{3} - \dfrac{1}{y} + C \\ \\ \text{Substituting to }F(x,y),\text{we get} \\ \\ \dfrac{1}{y(1 - xy)} + \dfrac{y^3}{3} - \dfrac{1}{y} = C \\ \\ \quad \quad \text{or} \\ \\ \boxed{\dfrac{x}{1 - xy} + \dfrac{y^3}{3} = C} \Longleftarrow \textit{Answer} \end{array} [/tex]

[tex] \\ [/tex]

[tex] \Large \bold{SOLUTION\ 2:} [/tex]

[tex] \small \begin{array}{l} \text{Since we already know that the equation is exact,} \\ \text{we can then continue solving for the solution by} \\ \text{inspection method or by algebraic manipulation.} \\ \\ (1-xy)^{-2} dx + \big[y^2 + x^2 (1-xy)^{-2}\big]dy = 0 \\ \\ \dfrac{dx}{(1-xy)^2} + \left[y^2 + \dfrac{x^2}{(1-xy)^2}\right]dy = 0 \\ \\ \dfrac{dx}{(1-xy)^2} + y^2 dy + \dfrac{x^2}{(1-xy)^2} dy = 0 \\ \\ \dfrac{dx + x^2dy}{(1-xy)^2} + y^2 dy = 0 \\ \\ \text{Divide both numerator and denominator of the} \\ \text{fraction by }x^2. \end{array} [/tex]

[tex] \small \begin{array}{c} \dfrac{\dfrac{1}{x^2}dx + dy}{\dfrac{(1-xy)^2}{x^2}} + y^2 dy = 0 \\ \\ \dfrac{\dfrac{1}{x^2}dx + dy}{\left(\dfrac{1}{x}-y\right)^2} + y^2 dy = 0 \\ \\ -\dfrac{\left(-\dfrac{1}{x^2}dx - dy\right)}{\left(\dfrac{1}{x}-y\right)^2} + y^2 dy = 0 \\ \\ \displaystyle {\large{\int}} -\frac{d\left(\dfrac{1}{x}-y\right)}{\left(\dfrac{1}{x}-y\right)^2} + \int y^2 dy = \int 0 \\ \\ \implies \dfrac{1}{\dfrac{1}{x}-y} + \dfrac{y^3}{3} = C \\ \\ \text{or} \\ \\ \boxed{\dfrac{x}{1 - xy} + \dfrac{y^3}{3} = C} \Longleftarrow \textit{Answer} \end{array} [/tex]

[tex]\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{(2x^3-xy^2-2y+3)dx-(x^2y+2x)dy=0}[/tex]

The DE is in the form M(x, y)dx + N(x, y)dy = 0

[tex]\\[/tex]

Test for exactness:

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{{\partial}M}{{\partial}y}=-2xy-2}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{{\partial}N}{{\partial}x}=-2xy-2}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{{\partial}M}{{\partial}y}=\dfrac{{\partial}N}{{\partial}x}} \ \therefore \textsf{the equation is exact}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{M=\dfrac{{\partial}F}{{\partial}x}=2x^3-xy^2-2y+3} \longleftarrow \textsf{equation 1}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{N=\dfrac{{\partial}F}{{{\partial}}y}=-x^2y-2x} \longleftarrow \textsf{equation 2}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Integrate equation 2 with respect to y:

[tex]\mathsf{F=\dfrac{-x^2y^2}{2}-2xy+f(x)} \longleftarrow \textsf{equation 3}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Differentiate equation 3 with respect to x:

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{{\partial}F}{{\partial}x}=-xy^2-2y+f'(x)} \longleftarrow \textsf{equation 4}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Equate equation 1 and equation 4:

[tex]\mathsf{2x^3-xy^2-2y+3=-xy^2-2y+f'(x)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{f'(x)=2x^3+3}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Integrating f'(x) with respect to x

[tex]\mathsf{f(x)=\dfrac{2x^4}{4}+3x}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{f(x)=\dfrac{x^4}{2}+3x}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Substitute f(x) in equation 3

[tex]\mathsf{F=-\dfrac{x^2y^2}{2}-2xy+\dfrac{x^4}{2}+3x}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

General solution of exact DE: F(x, y) = c

[tex]\mathsf{-\dfrac{x^2y^2}{2}-2xy+\dfrac{x^4}{2}+3x=c}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{-x^2y^2-4xy+x^4+6x=2c}[/tex]

C = 2c

[tex]\mathsf{x^4-x^2y^2-4xy+6x=C}[/tex]
