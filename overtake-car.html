4. at 9 a.m., car A began it's trip from a point, traveling at 50km/h at 10 a.m. car B started traveling from the same point at 60km/h in the same direction as car A. After what time will car B overtake car A? ​


Answer:
3pm

Step by step explanation:

Since car A started one hour earlier than car B, the time traveled by car A can be expressed as:

t = (start time of car B) - (start time of car A) = 10 - 9 = 1 hour

Using the formula:

distance = rate x time

we can express the distance traveled by car A as:

d = 50 km/h x t = 50 km/h x 1 hour = 50 km

Now, we need to determine how long it takes for car B to cover the same distance "d". We can express the time traveled by car B as:

t = d / (60 km/h - 50 km/h) = d / 10 km/h

This formula takes into account the relative speed between car B and car A, which is 10 km/h (60 km/h - 50 km/h).

Substituting the value of "d" that we calculated earlier, we get:

t = 50 km / 10 km/h = 5 hours

Therefore, car B will overtake car A after 5 hours from the time it started its journey, which is:

10 a.m. + 5 hours = 3 p.m.
