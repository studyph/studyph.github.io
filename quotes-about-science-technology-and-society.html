1. Explain Tom Head’s quote, “We live in a society absolutely dependent on Science and Technology and yet have clearly arranged things so that almost no one understands Science and Technology. That’s a clear prescription of danger.”​


Answer:

Civilization is a thin and fragile layer.

If we care about the future of humanity (i.e. preventing a self-inflicted apocalypse), we have a personal moral obligation to be technologically and scientifically literate.

Modern societies are entirely dependent upon the uninterrupted functioning of extraordinarily complex systems.

Infrastructure, energy, food production, transportation, communications, manufacturing…all technologically endowed, are more efficient and powerful than ever before.

But it’s not the most resilient.

We’ve built civilization upon pillars of scientific and technological advances, but it’s become very easy to take them for granted- turning a blind eye as they weather and erode from lack of maintenance.

Most things seem to work just fine without requiring our daily attention and contribution.

The are so many humans it’s easy to start assuming that someone has things under control. It enables a kind of willful ignorance and social loafing of knowledge.

The effects of our actions are hard to conceptualize, and no snowflake feels responsible for the avalanche. Society is the most complex, interconnected, and interdependent it’s ever been.

10. Give a speech about the following quote:"Providing Learner's Resilience Through Showcasing Skills and Talents in Culture and Arts and Technology"​


Dear friends,

Today, I want to talk about the importance of resilience in learning and how showcasing skills and talents in culture and arts and technology can contribute to building this resilience.

As we all know, learning can be a challenging and sometimes even a frustrating process. We encounter difficulties and setbacks that can make us want to give up. However, resilience is the ability to bounce back from these setbacks, to persevere through challenges, and to continue moving forward towards our goals.

One way to develop resilience is through the showcasing of our skills and talents in culture, arts, and technology. When we have the opportunity to demonstrate our abilities in these areas, we are building our self-confidence and self-esteem. We are also able to receive feedback and recognition for our accomplishments, which can motivate us to continue pushing ourselves and developing our skills.

Culture and arts can provide a unique outlet for individuals to express themselves and to connect with others. By participating in cultural activities and creating art, we can develop a sense of belonging and community. These activities can also be a source of inspiration and comfort during difficult times, helping us to cope with stress and adversity.

Technology is another area where individuals can showcase their skills and talents. In today's digital age, technology is an essential part of our lives, and those who are proficient in this area have a distinct advantage. By learning to code, develop apps, or design websites, individuals can create innovative solutions to real-world problems, and make a positive impact on their communities and society.

In conclusion, showcasing skills and talents in culture and arts and technology can help individuals develop resilience in learning. By participating in these activities, we can build self-confidence, receive recognition for our accomplishments, develop a sense of community, and create innovative solutions to real-world problems. Let us embrace these opportunities and continue to grow and develop as individuals. Thank you.

The influence of mass media has an effect on many aspects of human life, which can include voting a certain way, individual views and beliefs, or skewing a person's knowledge of a specific topic due to being provided false information. Not all effects result in change; some media messages reinforce an existing belief.

The media can shape our attitudes about a multitude of things from what we buy, the people we admire (and those we don't), our perceptions of political issues like immigration and health care, to social issues focused on diversity facets such as race, gender, sexual orientation, and age.
