22. Make your own article about "shell fossils" ​


Answer:

What is a Fossilized Shell?

One of the most common samples of fossils include different types of fossilized shell, these are also called ammonites, which are fossils of coiled up shells. These kinds of seashell fossils are from animals that lived in the sea between 240 and 65 million years ago.

Seashell fossils are formed when a sea animal with a shell dies and their body and shell begin to decompose. Seashell fossils are more common than other fossils because the shell is hard and therefore more likely to be preserved, compared to organisms with only soft tissue. Animals without a shell or bones hardly ever become fossilized.

Fossils tend to turn up in specific areas where conditions allowed proper preservation to occur. You can even find a handy map tool to show you where the most fossils have been found, and if anyone has found fossils in your area (see Resources Section).

Answer:

Mussel shells as tiles is an innovative and sustainable investigatory project that proposes the use of mussel shells as an eco-friendly alternative to traditional tiles. The main objective of the project is to determine the feasibility of using mussel shells as a sustainable, inexpensive and durable substitute to conventional tiles. The project intends to explore the properties of mussel shells, such as their hardness, strength, porosity and texture, and to determine whether they can be used as a suitable material for floor tiling. After studying the properties of mussel shells, the project will also look into the process of creating mussel shell tiles and how it can be made cost-efficient. Furthermore, the project will also evaluate the potential environmental impact of using mussel shells as tiles compared to other conventional tiles. Ultimately, the project aims to create a viable alternative to traditional tiles that is eco-friendly, cost-effective and durable, while still providing the same aesthetic appeal.
