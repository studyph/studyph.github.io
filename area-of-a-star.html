9. In a circle with diameter of 20 CM. a regular five-pointed star touching its circumference is inscribedFind the area of the star.​


[tex]\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

Refer to the figure

[tex]\mathtt{2\theta=\dfrac{360^{\circ}}{n}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{2\theta=\dfrac{360^{\circ}}{5}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{2\theta=72^{\circ}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{\theta=36}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{\dfrac{\theta}{2}=18^{\circ}}[/tex]

Consider ΔABC:

[tex]\mathtt{\alpha=180^{\circ}-18^{\circ}-36^{\circ}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{\alpha=126^{\circ}}[/tex]

Using Law of Sines:

[tex]\mathtt{\dfrac{AB}{sin(\theta)}=\dfrac{r}{sin(\alpha)}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{\dfrac{AB}{sin(36^{\circ})}=\dfrac{10}{sin(126^{\circ})}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{AB=\dfrac{10sin(36^{\circ})}{sin(126^{\circ})}}[/tex]

AB = 7.265 cm

Area of the star is equal to 10 times the area of ΔABC

[tex]\mathtt{A=10\left[\dfrac{1}{2}(10)(7.265)sin(18^{\circ})\right]}[/tex]

A = 112.25

[tex]\mathbb{ANSWER:}[/tex]

The area of the star is:

[tex]\large{\boxed{\mathtt{A=112.25 \: \: cm^2}}}[/tex]
