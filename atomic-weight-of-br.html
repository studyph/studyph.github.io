1. Bromine is made up of two isotopes Br-79 ( 78.9183 amu) and Br-81 ( 80.9163 amu ) Given bromines atomic weight of 79.904 amu, what is the percentage abundance of each isotope?


The percentage abundance of each isotope is:

51% Br-7949% Br-81

[tex]\texttt{ }[/tex]

Further explanation

Given:

Mass of Br-79 = 78.9183

Mass of Br-81 = 80.9163

Atomic Weight of Br = 79.904

Unknown:

Percentage abundance of each isotope = ?

Solution:

Let:

Percentage abundance of Br-79 = x%

Percentage abundance of Br-81 = (100-x)%

[tex]\texttt{Atomic Weight of Br} = x\% (\texttt{Mass of Br-79}) + (100-x)\%\texttt{(Mass of Br-81)}[/tex]

[tex]79.904 = x\% \times 78.9183 + (100-x)\% \times 80.9163[/tex]

[tex]79.904 = x\% \times 78.9183 - x\% \times 80.9163 + 100\% \times 80.9163[/tex]

[tex]79.904 - 80.9163 = x\% ( 78.9183 - 80.9163 )[/tex]

[tex]x\% = -1.0123 \div -1.998[/tex]

[tex]x\% \approx 51 \%[/tex]

[tex]\texttt{ }[/tex]

Conclusion:

The percentage abundance of each isotope is:

51% Br-7949% Br-81

2. How many atoms of bromine (Br) are in 90g br?


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

How many atoms of bromine (Br) are in 90g Br?

Note:

The molar mass of Bromine atom is equal to 79.9 g/mol

This means that in every 1 mole of bromine, the mass is equal to 79.9 grams/

There are 6.022 × 10²³ # of  atoms in every 1 mole of Bromine.

This means that in every 79.9 grams of bromine there are 6.022 × 10²³ # of bromine atoms

Solution:

We can compute the problem using the conversion of

79.9 grams / 6.022 × 10²³ # of bromine atoms

       90g Br × 6.022 × 10²³ # of bromine atoms / 79.9 grams

→     541.98 × 10²³ # of bromine atoms · grams / 79.9 grams

→     6.78 × 10²³ # of bromine atoms

Answer:

There are 6.78 × 10²³ # of bromine atoms
