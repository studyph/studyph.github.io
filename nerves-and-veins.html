1.FLOWERS-Vascular plants have two distinct organ systems: a shoot system, and a root system. The shoot system consists stems, leaves, and the reproductive parts of the plant (flowers and fruits). The shoot system generally grows above ground, where it absorbs the light needed for photosynthesis.

7. 17. In which blood vessels does the blood transfer food substances and oxygen to cells andremove carbon dioxide and other wastes?A. in the venulesC. in the capillariesB. B. in the veinsD in the arteries18. Which part of nerve picked up the stimulus?A. sensory nerveC. spinal cordB. motor nerveD. association nerve​


Answer:

17. C. in the Capillaries

18. Sensory nerves

Explanation:

Capillaries surround body cells and tissues to deliver and absorb oxygen, nutrients, and other substances.

Capillaries absorb oxygen from inhaled air into the bloodstream and release carbon dioxide for exhalation. In other parts of the body,  oxygen and other nutrients diffuse from blood in the capillaries to the tissues they supply. The capillaries absorb carbon dioxide and other waste products from the tissues and then flow the deoxygenated blood into the veins.

Sensory neurons can be classified by the type of stimulus that generates a response in the receptor.

During vision, rod and cone photoreceptors respond to light intensity and color.

During hearing, mechanoreceptors in hair cells of the inner ear detect vibrations conducted from the eardrum.

During taste, sensory neurons in our taste buds detect chemical qualities of our foods including sweetness, bitterness, sourness, saltiness, and umami (savory taste).

During smell, olfactory receptors recognize molecular features of wafting odors. During touch, mechanoreceptors in the skin and other tissues respond to variations in pressure.

Answer:

B. Pulmonary veins

Explanation:

The pulmonary veins carry oxygenated blood from the lungs into the left atrium where it is returned to systemic circulation. The aorta is the largest artery in the body. It carries oxygenated blood from the left ventricle of the heart into systemic circulation.
