16. SCENARIO 1: A professor copies one article from a periodical for distribution to the class. FAIR USE? Yes. Distribution of multiple copies for classroom use is fair use. what is the responsible action?​


Answer:

The responsible action is for the professor to make sure that the article is copied and distributed only to the students in the professor's class, and to include a disclaimer stating that the copyrighted article is used within the parameters of Fair Use legislation. The professor should also ensure that the copyright information is visible, and check with the periodical or publication to make sure that their terms of use allow for classroom copies.

Answer:

While copying one article from a periodical for distribution to a class may be considered fair use, it is important for the professor to ensure that the use of the material meets all four factors of fair use as outlined in copyright law. These factors are:

The purpose and character of the use, including whether such use is of a commercial nature or is for nonprofit educational purposes;The nature of the copyrighted work;The amount and substantiality of the portion used in relation to the copyrighted work as a whole; andThe effect of the use upon the potential market for or value of the copyrighted work. If the use of the article meets these four factors, then it may be considered fair use. However, the responsible action would be for the professor to provide proper attribution for the source material, including the author, publication, and copyright owner, and to ensure that the distribution of the material is limited to the class for educational purposes only.
