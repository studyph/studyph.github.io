20. "Communication work for those who work at it" by john powell what is your 3 insights about the quotation?​


Three insights from the quotation "Communication works for those who work on it" by John Powell.

1. Communication is not just talking.

The first insight is that communication is not just an activity where two or more people talk to each other. More than that, communication is an effort made by people to convey or give information or messages to others. Therefore, there are aspects that we must pay attention to ensure that communication can run efficiently and effectively so that messages or information can be conveyed and received properly.

2. Focus is one of the most important things when communicating

The second insight is that we must focus when communicating. Both parties who communicate must pay attention to each other and their efforts so that communication can occur effectively and efficiently. If only one of the parties focuses on communicating, it is very likely that messages and information are not conveyed properly.

For example, when a mother tells her child to turn off the stove after fifteen minutes but the child is listening while playing games or on a cellphone, there is a possibility that the child will forget and not carry out his mother's message.

3. Communication must be two-way.

The third insight is that communication must be two-way. This means that when communicating, the people involved must give their efforts to achieve the goal of communication, not just one person. For example, when two friends are in conflict, they both have to make an effort to communicate well to resolve the conflict. Because if only one person tries, then the purpose of communication will not succeed.
