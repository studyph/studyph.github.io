4. 15. Which of the following is incorrect?A. Two equinoxes occur in one yearB. The winter solstice is the shortest day of the yearC. The summer solstice is the longest day of the year.D. During summer equinox, days are longer than nights.​


Answer:

A. Two equinoxes occur in one year

Explanation:

An equinox is the instant of time when the plane of Earth's equator passes through the geometric center of the Sun's disk. This occurs twice each year, around 20 March and 23 September. In other words, it is the moment at which the center of the visible Sun is directly above the equator.

16. why can't the constellation orion be seen in the summer night sky?​


Answer:

We see constellations at different times of the year - spring, summer, fall, & winter. This occurs because the Earth is orbiting the Sun. ... This is why you cannot see Orion or any one constellation all year long… Unless it is circumpolar.

Why dont we see the constellation Orion at night all throughout the year?

-They move in a circle around the North star, Polaris, in the course of a single night. ... Why don't we see the constellation Orion at night all throughout the year? As the Earth orbits the Sun, its nighttime side points to different constellations at different points along its orbit.

Can you see Orion in the summer?

-Orion is most visible in the evening sky from January to March, winter in the Northern Hemisphere, and summer in the Southern Hemisphere. In the tropics (less than about 8° from the equator), the constellation transits at the zenith.

19. Why are the days longer than the nights during summer?


Explanation:

As the Earth circles the Sun during the year, half of the Earth get more or less sunlight than the other half of the Earth. In the summer months, the northern half of the Earth, where we live, tilts towards the Sun. This means we get more sunlight, making the days longer.

27. the summer night sky 8 constellations what is the answer?​


There are many constellations visible in the summer night sky, but here are eight popular ones that can be seen in the Northern Hemisphere:

Ursa Major (Great Bear): This constellation contains the well-known Big Dipper, which is used to find the North Star.Cygnus (Swan): This constellation is also known as the Northern Cross and contains the bright star Deneb.Lyra (Lyre): This constellation contains the bright star Vega, which is the fifth-brightest star in the sky.Aquila (Eagle): This constellation contains the bright star Altair and is associated with the mythological bird of the same name.Delphinus (Dolphin): This small constellation is named after the dolphin in Greek mythology that rescued the poet Arion.Scorpius (Scorpion): This constellation is best seen in the southern part of the sky and contains the bright red star Antares.Sagittarius (Archer): This constellation is also best seen in the southern part of the sky and contains the bright center of our Milky Way galaxy.Hercules: This constellation is named after the mythical Greek hero and contains the bright star Vega.

Answer:

Summer constellations are the constellations that are best seen in the evening night sky from late June to late September in the northern hemisphere and from late December to late March in the southern hemisphere.
