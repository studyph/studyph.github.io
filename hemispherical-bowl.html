2. A bowl in the form of a hemisphere is 20 cm in diameter.How many cubic centimeters of soup can it contain?


Answer:

2094.40 cubic centimeters

Step-by-step explanation:

A hemisphere is essentially half of a sphere, so we can simply multiply the formula by half.

[tex]V_{hemisphere} = (\frac{1}{2})(\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3)\\V_{hemisphere} = \frac{2}{3} \pi r^3[/tex]

Take note that diameter is twice the radius, so the radius of the bowl is 10.

[tex]V = \frac{2}{3}\pi(10^3)\\\\V = \frac{2}{3}\pi(1000)\\\\V = \frac{2000}{3}\pi\\\\V \approx 2094.3951[/tex]

The value used for pi above is not the approximation but instead the value that is available in the calculator.

Therefore, the bowl if filled to the brim has approximately 2094.40 cubic centimeters of soup.

21. A hemispherical bowl of internal and external radii 3 cm and 5 cm is melted and formed into right circular cylinder of radius 14 cm .find the height of the cylinder


Answer:

h = 1/3 cm

Step-by-step explanation:

A hemispherical bowl of internal and external radii 3 cm and 5 cm is melted and formed into right circular cylinder of radius 14 cm .find the height of the cylinder

First, calculate the volume of the hemispherical bowl:

V = (2/3) × π × r³

using internal radii (r = 3 cm) : V = (2/3) × π × (3 cm)³  = 18π cm³

using external radii (r = 5 cm) : V = (2/3) × π × (5 cm)³  = (250/3)π cm³

Subtract the volume of external and internal to get the volume of the bowl:

Volume of the bowl = external volume - internal volume

Volume of the bowl = (250/3)π cm³ - 18π cm³ = (196/3)π cm³

Since it is melted and formed to right circular cylinder, then the volume must be constant:

Volume of right circular cylinder = (196/3)π cm³ = π × r² × h

(196/3)π cm³ = π × (14 cm)² × h

solve for h

h = 1/3 cm

23. Typhoon winds spiral in ____ direction. A.Clockwise in the northern hemisphere B.Clockwise in the easthern hemisphere C.Counterclockwise in the Northern Hemisphere D.Counterclockwise in the Southern hemisphere


The correct answer is C.

The wind spirals in a counter-clockwise direction going towards the center of the typhoon when it is in the Northern hemisphere.

However, the complete opposite is true for typhoons occurring in the Southern hemisphere because the direction would be clockwise towards the center of the typhoon.
