6. 4. The teacher wants to decorate her classroomwall by putting border along the top of it. The wallis a square wall with an area of 729 square feet.What is the length of the border needed by theteacher to the top of the classroom wall?​


Answer:

There is a wall which is shaped like a square.

She wants to put the border on the side of the square.

We then use his formula:

Area = side²

Substitute.

⇒ 729 = side²

⇒ side = √729

⇒ side = 27 feet.

So she needs 27 feet border at the top of her classroom.

15. How are the expense recognition and income recognition principles related to the matching principle?​


Answer:

Expense recognition, also known as the matching principle, occurs when a company incurs expenses and it recognizes the revenue associated with the expenses. A company shouldn't record expenses when they receive payment, but at the time they collect revenue.

28. Can too much recognition lead to constantly escalating forms of recognition or unfulfilled employee expectations?​


Giving too much recognition to employees can lead to the employees’ over confidence and unwanted bossiness in the workplace over the other employees who do not receive enough recognition.  Thus, giving too much of anything is always not a good idea.  Frequent recognitions like, appreciating their insights after benefiting the whole project, is definitely encouraging.  But we have to consider the possible effects of giving too much recognition.

One reason is, other employees may think that you are giving favoritism.  These employees may feel envious making them perform less since they could think that they are not valued enough.  Another is, the workplace will create a toxic competition among the employees.  If these incidences happen, the employees will develop unhealthy relationships with others.
