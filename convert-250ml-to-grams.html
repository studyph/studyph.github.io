6. how many grams of concentrated nitric acid solution should be used to prepare 250mL of 2.00 M HNO3? The concentrated acid is 70.0% HNO3


Hello!

How many grams of concentrated nitric acid solution should be used to prepare 250mL of 2.00 M HNO3 ? The concentrated acid is 70.0% HNO3

We have the following data:

m1 (solute mass) = ?

M (molarity) = 2.00 M (or 2 Mol/L)

V (volume) = 250 mL → 0.25 L

MM (Molar Mass of HNO3)

H = 1*(1u) = 1 u

N = 1*(14u) = 14 u

O = 3*(16u) = 48 u

------------------------------

MM (Molar Mass of HNO3) = 1 + 14 + 48 = 63 g/mol

We apply the data to the formula of Molarity, see:

[tex]M = \dfrac{m_1}{MM*V}[/tex]

[tex]2 = \dfrac{m_1}{63*0.25}[/tex]

[tex]2 = \dfrac{m_1}{15.75}[/tex]

[tex]m_1 = 2*15.75[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{m_1 = 31.5\:g}[/tex]

The concentrated acid is 70.0% HNO3, then, how many grams of concentrated nitric acid solution should be used ?

70 g (HNO3) ----------- 100 g (concentrated nitric acid solution)

31.5 g (HNO3) --------- y

[tex]\dfrac{70}{31.5} = \dfrac{100}{y}[/tex]

multiply the means by the extremes

[tex]70*y = 31.5*100[/tex]

[tex]70\:y = 3150[/tex]

[tex]y = \dfrac{315\diagup\!\!\!\!0}{7\diagup\!\!\!\!0}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{\boxed{y = 45}}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

Answer:

45 grams of concentrated nitric acid solution should be used.

18. Calculate the mass (in grams) of 250mL of ether 25 degrees celsius. The density of ether at 25 degrees celsius is 0.71g/mL


Given:

[tex]V = \text{250 mL}[/tex]

[tex]\rho = \text{0.71 g/mL}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]m[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\rho = \frac{m}{V}[/tex]

[tex]m = \rho V[/tex]

[tex]m = (\text{0.71 g/mL})(\text{250 mL})[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{m = \text{178 g}}[/tex]
