26. How many circles with a diameter of 4 inches can be cut from a cartolina if the cartolina has a length of 35 inches and width of 10 inches


Answer:

How many circles with a diameter of 4 inches can be cut from a cartolina if the cartolina has a length of 35 inches and width of 10 inches?

27 circles can be cut from the cartolina.

Step-by-step explanation:

What is asked?

How many circles can be but from the cartolina?

What are given?

diameter of a circle - 4 inches

length of the cartolina - 35 inches

width of the cartolina - 10 inches

What is the formula?

area of the cartolina ÷ area of the circle = N

(length × width) ÷ (πr²) = N

To know the number of circles that can be cut from the cartolina, we need to know the surface area of the cartolina, how big is it? And the area that one circle covered.

Solution:

Area of the Cartolina

Area = l × w

= 35 inches × 10 inches

= 350 inches²

Area of the Circle

Area = πr²

= 3.14(2 inches)²

= 3.14(4 inches²)

= 12.56 inches²

(π has a constant value of 3.14 and r is the radius or half of the diameter.)

Now that we already know the area of the cartolina and circle, we need to divide to see how many circles can be cut from the cartolina.

area of the cartolina ÷ area of the circle

= 350 inches² ÷ 12.56 inches²

= 27.87

For the final answer, just get the whole number, because the problem is asking for the total number of circles and it should be an exact count.

Final Answer:

27 circles can be cut from the cartolina.
