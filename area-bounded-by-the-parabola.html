What is the area of the region bounded by the parabola y = x² - x - 6 and the line y = -4.

Answer:

The area of the region is 9/2 or 4.5 square units

Step-by-step explanation:

We will calculate the area bounded by two functions using integration.

In order to find the integral that represents the area bounded by the functions, we need to first find the intersection of the functions. The intersection of the functions will indicate the bounds of the integral we are going to solve.

We can determine the intersection by either plotting the two functions then identify the intersection, or solve for it algebraically. The graph shown in the attached file shows that the intersections are at [tex]x=-1[/tex] and [tex]x=2.[/tex] To solve it algebraically, equate the two functions and solve for [tex]x.[/tex]

[tex]x^2-x-6=-4[/tex]

[tex]x^2-x-2=0[/tex]

[tex](x+1)(x-2)=0[/tex]

[tex]\therefore x=-1, 2[/tex]

Therefore, the lower and upper bounds are -1 and 2. The area of the region is now equal to:

[tex]\displaystyle A=\int \limits_{a}^{b} \textsf{upper f.}-\textsf{lower f.} \ dx[/tex]

[tex]\displaystyle A=\int \limits_{-1}^{2} (-4)-(x^2-x-6) \ dx[/tex]

[tex]\displaystyle A=\int \limits_{-1}^{2} -x^2+x+2 \ dx[/tex]

[tex]\displaystyle A= \bigg[-\frac{x^{2+1}}{2+1}+\frac{x^{1+1}}{1+1}+2x\bigg]_{-1}^{2}[/tex]

[tex]\displaystyle A=\bigg[-\frac{x^3}{3}+\frac{x^2}{2}+2x\bigg]_{-1}^{2}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{gathered} A=\bigg[-\frac{2^3}{3}+\frac{2^2}{2}+2(2)\bigg]-\bigg[-\frac{(-1)^3}{3}+\frac{(-1)^2}{2}+2(-1)\bigg] \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\displaystyle A=\frac{9}{2}=4.5}[/tex]

29. Find the area bounded by the parabola y^2=4x and the line y= 2x - 4?


Answer:

This problem has two solutions. It's either complex or real depends on what derivative of what axis you will choose. If you choose the 'y-world' you will have a solution which is a real number, but if you choose the 'x-world' (which is the most common), you will have a complex solution. In this situation I choose to answer just the solution in 'x-world' since it is the most common.

To find this Area we must find first the value of x then find the definite integral of two functions whose the value of range a and b is the value of the two root of x.

[tex]\int\limits^x_x {f(x)} \, dx - \int\limits^x_x {g(x)} \, dx[/tex]

Find the value of x by letting the two functions to be equal:

[tex]{2\sqrt{x}} = 2x - 4\\2\sqrt{x}} - 2x + 4 = 0\\\\sqrt{x} - x + 2 = 0\\x-x^2+4=0\\x^2-x-4=0\\(x-4)(x+1)=0\\x=4 ; x=-1[/tex]

Find the definite integral of the two functions whose range are the roots of x:

[tex]F(x) = \int\limits^4_1 2\sqrt{x} \, dx \\G(x) = \int\limits^4_1 {2x-4} \, dx[/tex]

Find F(x):

[tex]F(x) = \int\limits^4_1 2\sqrt{x} \, dx \\\ F(x) = \int\limits^4_1 {3{\sqrt{x^3} }} \, dx \\\\\ F(x) = {3{\sqrt{-1^3} } } \, - {3{\sqrt{-4^3} }} \, \\\\\ F(x) = {3i}- 3{\sqrt{2^2^3} }\\ F(x) = {3i} - 3(8)\\ F(x) = 3i-24}[/tex]

Find G(x):

[tex]G(x) = \int\limits^4_1 {2x-4} \, dx\\G(x) = \int\limits^4_1 {x^2} - 4x\, dx \\G(x) = (-1^2 - 4(-1) )-(4^2-4(4) \\G(x) = 5 -0 = 5[/tex]

Therefore:

[tex]Area = F(x) - G(x)\\Area = 3i+24 - 5\\Area = 3i + 19[/tex]

The Area is 3i + 19 sq units

(Note that Area should be on absolute value, so 3i - 24 will be 3i+24)

As I said this solution is  just for the 'x-world' but if you want to know the other solution, it is 28/3 sq units. You can easily calculate this solution if you choose to find y  rather than x.
