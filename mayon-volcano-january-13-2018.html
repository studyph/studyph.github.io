21. Which of the following would best classify the 2018 eruption of Mayon Volcano: Phreatic or hydrothermal, Phreatomagmatic, Strombolian, Vulcanian, or Plinian? Explain your answer.​


Answer:

The 2018 eruption of Mayon Volcano would best classify as phreatomagmatic.

Phreatomagmatic eruptions occur when magma comes into contact with water, either from groundwater, a lake, or the sea, causing a violent explosion. The Mayon Volcano eruption in 2018 produced ash plumes, lava flows, and pyroclastic density currents, which are all typical features of a phreatomagmatic eruption.

Moreover, the 2018 eruption of Mayon Volcano was characterized by the interaction of rising magma with the groundwater in the vicinity of the volcano. This resulted in steam-driven explosions and the production of ash plumes and volcanic debris.

Therefore, based on the characteristics of the eruption and the interaction between the magma and the groundwater, the 2018 eruption of Mayon Volcano would best classify as phreatomagmatic.
