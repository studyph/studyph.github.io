17. compare understanding by design and obe/obtl. what are their similarities? what are their differences? you may wish to use a table for the comparison.​


Answer:

Understanding by Design (UbD) and Outcome-Based Education (OBE) or Outcome-Based Teaching and Learning (OBTL) are two educational frameworks that aim to improve the quality of learning outcomes. While they share some similarities, they differ in their approach to curriculum design, assessment, and teaching methodologies.

Similarities:

Both frameworks focus on improving student learning outcomes.Both frameworks emphasize the importance of aligning curriculum design with the desired learning outcomes.Both frameworks prioritize assessment as a critical aspect of the teaching and learning process.Both frameworks promote the use of student-centered teaching methodologies.

Differences:

UbD focuses on curriculum design, while OBE/OBTL focuses on learning outcomes.UbD uses a backward design approach, while OBE/OBTL uses a forward design approach.UbD begins with the end in mind, while OBE/OBTL begins with learning outcomes.UbD emphasizes authentic assessment, while OBE/OBTL emphasizes direct and indirect assessment.UbD uses a variety of teaching methods, while OBE/OBTL emphasizes student-centered teaching methods such as problem-based learning.
