5. GIVE AN EXAMPLES - psychological freedom - moral freedom ​


Explanation:

Psychological freedom refers to the sense of personal agency and autonomy that an individual feels in their life. It means that a person is able to make choices and decisions for themselves without being excessively influenced by external factors or by their own psychological limitations. For example, a person who feels psychologically free might be able to resist the temptation to procrastinate, or might be able to overcome their fear of public speaking in order to give a presentation.

Moral freedom refers to the ability to act in accordance with one's own moral values and principles, rather than being constrained by external demands or expectations. For example, a person who feels morally free might be able to stand up for their beliefs even if it means going against the wishes of their family or community. Alternatively, a person who feels morally free might be able to make a difficult but ethical decision, even if it means facing consequences or backlash.

14. Examples of physical freedom


Physical freedom can be defined as the freedom that a person has in using his physic or his body and determining what is good for his physic or body without any interference or coercion from other people.

Examples of physical freedom are:

1. Freedom to move.

This is the most basic physical freedom. If a person is unable to move, for example, because they are handcuffed, bound, or restricted, then they are said to have no physical freedom. To have physical freedom, one must be free to move.

2. Freedom to use the physical in various activities.

This activity can be any activity, such as climbing, dancing, playing ball, and other activities. If a person cannot do physical activity according to what he wants, then he does not have physical freedom.

3. Freedom to determine what is best for his physique.

For example:

Doing a diet program.Go to the gym three times a week to exercise.Take vitamins and other decisions related to physical.

What needs to be remembered is that this physical freedom must be accompanied by the awareness that being free does not mean we can do everything, we still have to ensure that what we do to or with our physic is truly positive and does not destroy or harm ourselves.

17. example of freedom and explain​


Answer:

Article 10 protects your right to hold your own opinions and to express them freely without government interference.

This includes the right to express your views aloud (for example through public protest and demonstrations) or through:

published articles, books or leaflets

television or radio broadcasting

works of art

the internet and social media

The law also protects your freedom to receive information from other people by, for example, being part of an audience or reading a magazine.

Are there any restrictions to this right?

Although you have freedom of expression, you also have a duty to behave responsibly and to respect other people’s rights.

Public authorities may restrict this right if they can show that their action is lawful, necessary and proportionate in order to:

protect national security, territorial integrity (the borders of the state) or public safety

prevent disorder or crime

protect health or morals

protect the rights and reputations of other people

prevent the disclosure of information received in confidence

maintain the authority and impartiality of judges

An authority may be allowed to restrict your freedom of expression if, for example, you express views that encourage racial or religious hatred.

However, the relevant public authority must show that the restriction is ‘proportionate’, in other words that it is appropriate and no more than necessary to address the issue concerned.  


18. examples of economic freedom ​


Answer:

Here are some examples of economic freedom:

Free market: A free market allows businesses to operate with minimum interference from the government. It provides individuals with the freedom to choose what to buy and sell, and businesses with the freedom to set prices and wages.

Private property rights: Individuals and businesses have the right to own property and use it as they see fit. This includes the right to buy, sell, or transfer property without interference from the government.

Entrepreneurship: Economic freedom allows individuals to start and run their own businesses. They have the freedom to innovate, create, and pursue new opportunities without the fear of government intervention.

Trade: Economic freedom encourages free trade and open markets. It allows businesses and individuals to buy and sell goods and services across borders without restrictions.

Limited government intervention: Economic freedom promotes the idea of limited government intervention in the market. It allows the market to function without excessive regulation and encourages competition and innovation.

26. GIVE AN EXAMPLES - psychological freedom - moral freedom ​


Answer:

Psychological freedom refers to a person's ability to think, feel, and act independently, without being influenced or constrained by external factors such as social norms, expectations, or other people's opinions.

Here are a few examples of psychological freedom:

A person who is free to express their own thoughts and feelings, even if they differ from the dominant viewpoint, is experiencing psychological freedom.

A person who is able to make their own choices and decisions, without feeling pressure or coercion from others, is experiencing psychological freedom.

A person who is able to pursue their own interests and goals, without feeling limited or hindered by external factors, is experiencing psychological freedom.

Moral freedom refers to a person's ability to act in accordance with their own moral principles, without being constrained by external factors such as laws or social norms.

Here are a few examples of moral freedom:

A person who is free to act on their own conscience, even if it means disobeying laws or social norms, is experiencing moral freedom.

A person who is able to choose to do the right thing, even if it means facing consequences or backlash, is experiencing moral freedom.

A person who is able to live according to their own moral values, without feeling pressure to conform to the expectations of others, is experiencing moral freedom.
