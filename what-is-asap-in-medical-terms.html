8. What is like and Unlike terms? Pa help po ASAP


Answer:

In algebra, like terms are terms that have the same variables and powers. The coefficients do not need to match. Unlike terms are two or more terms that are not like terms, i.e. they do not have the same variables or powers. The order of the variables does not matter unless there is a power.

[tex]\huge\blue{\overline{\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad\qquad}}[/tex]

Like terms are terms that contain the same combination of variables with the same exponents. Only the numerical coefficient may be different.

Examples of like terms are:

[tex]3x², 6x², x² \: and -x².[/tex]

Unlike terms have different variables or different exponents on the same variable.

Examples of unlike terms are:

[tex]5x, 6x³, 2x², 3xy \: and \: y.[/tex]

15. what is DNR? A medical term but i don't know it's meaning


 do-not-resuscitate order, or DNR order, is a medical order written by a doctor. It instructs health care providers not to do cardiopulmonary resuscitation (CPR) if a patient's breathing stops or if the patient's heart stops beating.
---------------------------------------------------
DNR order is created, or set up, before an emergency occurs. A DNR order allows you to choose whether or not you want CPR in an emergency. It is specific about CPR. It does not have instructions for other treatments, such as pain medicine, other medicines, or nutrition.

The doctor writes the order only after talking about it with the patient (if possible), the proxy, or the patient's family.

24. What is the significance of technology based arts in terms of science/medical field?


Answer:

Technology-based arts can have significant implications for the science and medical fields in a number of ways. Here are a few examples:

1. Medical visualization and simulation: Technology-based arts can be used to create visualizations and simulations of medical procedures, anatomy, and other concepts in a way that is more engaging and interactive than traditional methods. This can be particularly useful for education and training purposes.

2. Data visualization: Technology-based arts can be used to create visual representations of complex data sets, such as medical records or research findings. This can help researchers and medical professionals better understand and analyze the data, and communicate their findings to others.

3. Virtual and augmented reality: Virtual and augmented reality technologies can be used in the medical field to provide immersive training experiences, or to help patients visualize and understand their conditions and treatment options.

4. Telemedicine: Technology-based arts can be used to facilitate telemedicine, which allows medical professionals to provide care remotely using video conferencing and other digital tools. This can improve access to care for patients in underserved areas or those who are unable to travel.

5. Medical art: Technology-based arts can also be used to create artistic representations of medical concepts and procedures, which can be used for educational or artistic purposes.
