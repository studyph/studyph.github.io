1. The body weight of 500 students in Grade 11 are approximately normally distributed with a mean of 72.9 kilograms and a standard deviation of 5 kilograms. If 100 students are randomly sampled, determine the mean, variance, and standard error of the sampling distribution of the sample mean. tysm sa magsasagot ng matino​


Given information:

Population size (N) = 500Population mean (μ) = 72.9 kgPopulation standard deviation (σ) = 5 kgSample size (n) = 100

The mean of the sampling distribution of the sample mean (also known as the expected value of the sample mean) is equal to the population mean:

μx̄ = μ = 72.9 kg

The variance of the sampling distribution of the sample mean is equal to the population variance divided by the sample size:

σ²x̄ = σ²/n = (5²)/100 = 0.25

The standard error of the sampling distribution of the sample mean is equal to the square root of the variance of the sampling distribution of the sample mean:

σx̄ = sqrt(σ²x̄) = sqrt(0.25) = 0.5

Therefore, the mean of the sampling distribution of the sample mean is 72.9 kg, the variance of the sampling distribution of the sample mean is 0.25, and the standard error of the sampling distribution of the sample mean is 0.5 kg.
