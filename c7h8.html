1. in a solution of naphthalene (C10H8) in toluene (C7H8), the mole fraction of naphthalene is0.2. What is the % by mass toluene and naphthalene in the solution?​


Answer:

When in doubt, make something up. By that I mean: if you think a problem would be easier to solve if you had certain pieces of data that are not included in the problem statement, it probably means that those pieces of data can have any value without changing the result. So make something up.

Making up: we have a total of 100 moles

A mole fraction of 0.2 for naphthalene means we have 20 moles of naphthalene.

Mass of naphthalene: 128.17 g/mol ×20 mol=2563.4 g

The rest is 80 moles of toluene.

Mass of toluene: 92.14 g/mol×80 mol=7371.2 g

Total mass: 2563.4+7371.2=9934.6 g

Mass % naphthalene = mass naphthalenetotal mass×100%=2563.4 g9934.6 g×100%=25.8%

By difference, the mass % toluene would be 74.2% .

Both of those results overstate the number of significant figures. The value 0.2 given in the problem limits the answers to one sig fig. Rounding the above answers to one sig fig is entirely unsatisfying.


2. Determine the volume % of toluene (C7H8) in a solution made by mixing 40 mL toluene with 75 mL of benzene (C6H6).


Percent by VolumeDiscussion:

Percent by volume is the ratio of the volume of a solute to the volume of the solution, multiplied by 100. The formulas used in solving percent by mass are as follows:

[tex]\boxed{\text{percent by volume} = \frac{\text{volume of solute}}{\text{volume of solution}} × 100}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{percent by volume} = \frac{\text{volume of solute}}{\text{volume of solute + volume of solvent}} × 100}[/tex]

The percent by volume is a unitless number because it is a ratio of two similar quantities.

[tex]\\[/tex]

Problem:

Determine the volume % of toluene (C₇H₈) in a solution made by mixing 40 mL toluene with 75 mL of benzene (C₆H₆).

Given:

volume of solute = 40 mL

volume of solvent = 75 mL

solute: toluene

solvent: benzene

Required:

volume percent

Solution:

[tex]\text{volume percent} = \frac{\text{volume of solute}}{\text{volume of solute + volume of solvent}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume percent} = \frac{\text{40 mL}}{\text{40 mL + 75 mL}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{volume percent} = 34.8\%}[/tex]

4. The density of toluene (C7H8) is 0.867 g/mL, and the density of thiophene (C4H4S) is 1.065 g/mL. A solution is made by dissolving 8.10 g of thiophene in 250.0 mL of toluene. (a) Calculate the mole fraction of thiophene in the solution. (b) Calculate the molality of thiophene in the solution. (c) Assuming that the volumes of toluene and thiophene are additive, what is the molarity of the solution?


(a) SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & mass_{\text{solute}} = \text{8.10 g} \\ & MM_{\text{solute}} = \text{84.136 g/mol} \\ & d_{\text{solvent}} = \text{0.867 g/mL} \\ & MM_{\text{solvent}} = \text{92.141 g/mol} \\ & V_{\text{solvent}} = \text{250.0 mL} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Determine the mass of solvent (C₇H₈).

[tex]\begin{aligned} mass_{\text{solvent}} & = d_{\text{solvent}}V_{\text{solvent}} \\ & = \text{(0.867 g/mL)(250.0 mL)} \\ & = \text{216.75 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of each component.

• For solute (C₄H₄S)

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{mass_{\text{solute}}}{MM_{\text{solute}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{8.10 g}}{\text{84.136 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.096273 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

• For solvent (C₇H₈)

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_{\text{solvent}} & = \frac{mass_{\text{solvent}}}{MM_{\text{solvent}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{216.75 g}}{\text{92.141 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{2.3524 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the mole fraction of solute.

[tex]\begin{aligned} X_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{n_{\text{solute}}}{n_{\text{solute}} + n_{\text{solvent}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.096273 mol}}{\text{0.096273 mol + 2.3524 mol}} \\ & = \boxed{0.0393} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the mole fraction of thiophene is 0.0393.

------------------------------------------------------------

(b) SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & n_{\text{solute}} = \text{0.096273 mol} \\ & mass_{\text{solvent}} = \text{216.75 g = 0.21675 kg} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the molality of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} m & = \frac{n_{\text{solute}}}{mass_{\text{solvent}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.096273 mol}}{\text{0.21675 kg}} \\ & = \text{0.444 mol/kg} \\ & = \boxed{0.444 \: m} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molality of the solution is 0.444 m.

------------------------------------------------------------

(c) SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & n_{\text{solute}} = \text{0.096273 mol} \\ & V_{\text{solvent}} = \text{250.0 mL} \\ & d_{\text{solute}} = \text{1.065 g/mL} \\ & mass_{\text{solute}} = \text{8.10 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the volume of solute.

[tex]\begin{aligned} V_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{mass_{\text{solute}}}{d_{\text{solute}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{8.10 g}}{\text{1.065 g/mL}} \\ & = \text{7.61 mL} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the volume of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} V_{\text{solution}} & = V_{\text{solute}} + V_{\text{solvent}} \\ & = \text{7.61 mL + 250.0 mL} \\ & = \text{257.61 mL} \\ & = \text{0.25761 L} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} M & = \frac{n_{\text{solute}}}{V_{\text{solution}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.096273 mol}}{\text{0.25761 L}} \\ & = \text{0.374 mol/L} \\ & = \boxed{0.374 \: M} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molarity of the solution is 0.374 M.
