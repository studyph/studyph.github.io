17. Morphology is one of the factors that affect the occurrence of the landslide, which of the following referred to the morphology of the soil​


Answer:

A LANDSIDE is defined as the movement of a mass of rock, debres., or earth down a slope. Landslides are a type of "mass wasting," which denotes any down-slope movement of soil and rock under the direct influence of gravity. The term "landslide" encompasses five modes of slope movement: falls, topples, slides, spreads, and flows. These are further subdivided by the type of geologic material (bedrock, debris, or earth). Debris flows (commonly referred to as mudflows or mudslides) and rock falls are examples of common landslide types.

23. Disadvantage of plant morphology


Answer:

None because this is a study, not a method.

Explanation:

Plant morphology is the study of the external structure of plants. Since this is a study and not a method, there are no disadvantages in plant morphology. Plant morphology is a specific and smaller branch under botany.

Of course, since this is a specific branch, there are some limitations to the scope of this study. For example, a detailed explanation or research about photosynthesis is not the work of plant morphologists since it describes how plants live and function.

To summarize, there are no disadvantages in the branch of plant morphology; however, combining its discoveries with those of other related branches under botany will help the reader see a bigger picture of how plants work.
