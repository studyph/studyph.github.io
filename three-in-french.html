15. Three hundred twenty people are traveling in an airplane. Sixty seven of them speak only English. Seventy three of them speak only French. Twenty one speak both French and English. What percentage of passengers speak neither French nor English?


Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the percentage of passengers who speak neither French nor English, we need to subtract the total number of passengers who speak English, French, or both from the total number of passengers on the plane.

Let's start by finding the total number of passengers who speak English or French:

English only = 67

French only = 73

Both English and French = 21

Total = 67 + 73 - 21 = 119

So, out of the 320 passengers on the plane, 119 speak English or French.

To find the number of passengers who speak neither language, we can subtract the number of passengers who speak English or French from the total number of passengers on the plane:

320 - 119 = 201

Therefore, 201 passengers speak neither French nor English.

To find the percentage of passengers who speak neither language, we can divide the number of passengers who speak neither language by the total number of passengers on the plane, and multiply by 100:

(201/320) x 100 = 62.81%

So, approximately 62.81% of the passengers speak neither French nor English.

28. How did philosophers and thinkers help in shaping the public opinion during the French revolution? Explain by giving any three examples.


Answer:

(i) The philosophers played an important role in the French Revolution. They inspired the common mass of France with their revolutionary ideas and prepared them to fight against injustices.

(ii) They did not believe in the doctrine of the divine and absolute right of the monarch. In his Two Treatises of Government John Locke refuted this doctrine strongly.

(iii) Rousseau carried the idea forward proposing a form of government based on a social contract between people and their representatives.

(iv) In The Spirit of the Laws, Montesquieu proposed a division of power within the government between the legislative, the executive and the judiciary.

(v) The ideas of these philosophers were discussed intensively in salons and coffee-houses and spread among people through books and newspapers. These were frequently read aloud in groups for the benefit of those who were illiterate. Thus, the philosophers contributed a lot in bringing of the French Revolution.
