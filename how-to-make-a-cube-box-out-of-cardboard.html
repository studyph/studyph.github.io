ANSWER:


OPTIMIZATION PROBLEM

Problem:

An open-typed rectangular box is to be made from a piece of cardboard 25 cm long and 10 cm wide by cutting out identical squares from the four corners and turning up the sides. What should be the dimensions of the box to get the maximum volume out of the box?

Solution:

If we were to illustrate this, the square which will be cut from the corners has unknown side lengths which we can represent with the variable "x".

Recall the volume of rectangular prism is:

V = LWH

where: V is the volume of the box, W is the width, L is the length and H is the height.

From the piece of cardboard, we let the length as L = 25 - 2x and width as W = 10 - 2x, while the height H = x.

By direct substitution:

V = LWH = (25 - 2x)(10 - 2x)(x)

Using the FOIL Method, we can simplify this:

V = (250 - 50x - 20x + 4x^2)(x) = (250 - 70x + 4x^2)(x) = 250x - 70x^2 + 4x^3

Then, let us arrange this in standard form:

[tex]V = 4x^3 - 70x^2 + 250x[/tex]

In order to get the maximum volume, we will apply differential calculus. We will differentiate the polynomial.

[tex]V = 4x^3 - 70x^2 + 250x\\\frac{dV}{dx} = 12x^2 - 140x + 250[/tex]

Then we let dV/dx be equal to zero:

[tex]0 = 12x^2 - 140x + 250[/tex]

And solve for the values of x:

Using mode 5 -3 of our scientific calculators, we get two values of x:

x = 9.47 and x = 2.20. Since we want to maximize our rectangular box, we will substitute each value to V = (25 - 2x)(10 - 2x)(x).

At x = 9.47:

V = [25 - 2(9.47)][10 - 2(9.47)](9.47) = -513.05

At x = 2.20:

V = [25 - 2(2.20)][10 - 2(2.20)](2.20) = 253.79 cubic centimeters

Since only one root has given a positive value of the volume, therefore, x = 2.20 will be taken as the value of x. But we are looking for the dimensions, so:

L = 25 - 2x = 25 - 2(2.20) = 20.60 cm

W = 10 - 2x = 10 - 2(2.20) = 5.60 cm

H = 2.20 cm

Therefore, the dimensions of the rectangular box should be 20.60 cm by 5.60 cm by 2.20 cm.
