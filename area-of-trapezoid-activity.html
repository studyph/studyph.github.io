11. the trapezoid bases are 7cm and 11cm it's height is 4cm what is the area of trapezoid​


AREA OF A TRAPEZOID

==================================

Problem

the trapezoid bases are 7cm and 11cm it's height is 4cm what is the area of trapezoid

Solution

The area of a trapezoid is found where 'a' and 'b' are the bases and 'h' is the height using the formula,

[tex]\bold{Formula\:||\:A = \frac{a+b}{2}h}[/tex][tex]\tt{A=\frac{7\:cm+11\:cm}{2}(4\:cm)}[/tex]

'Multiply the sum of the parallel sides by the perpendicular distance between the bases and then divide by 2.

[tex]\tt{A = \frac{a+b}{2}(h)}[/tex][tex]\tt{A = \frac{7\:cm+11\:cm}{2}(4\:cm)}[/tex][tex]\tt{A = (9\:cm) (4\:cm) }[/tex][tex]\tt\green{A = 36\:cm²}[/tex]

Final Answer

The area of trapezoid is 36 centimeter²

28. Find the AREA of the trapezoid.


Given:

Height = 9ft
Base(a) = 8ft
Base(b) = 12ft

Formula:

Note! The formula that I have used is not expressed as a fraction, like in the original form it was written... which is: Area of Trapezoid =  [tex]\frac{a+b}{2}H[/tex]

Area of Trapezoid = [(1/2)(a + b)] ⋅ (H)

Solution:

Area of Trapezoid = [(1/2)(a + b)] × (H)
                               = [(1/2)(8+12)] × (9)
                               = (1/2)(20) × (9)
                               = 10 × 9
                               = 90

Final Answer:
Therefore, the area of the given trapezoid is 90 square feet.
