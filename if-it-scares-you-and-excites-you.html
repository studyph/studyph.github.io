11. Direction: Reflect on how changes of matter can be related to life experiences.How do you perceive change, in general?Do you feel scared or excited when something you are used to do suddenly changed? Why?​


Answer:

Changes in matter can be related to life experiences in a variety of ways. For example, changes in states of matter such as the melting of ice or the boiling of water can be related to everyday experiences like cooking or weather patterns. Similarly, chemical reactions like rusting or fermentation can relate to experiences such as the decay of food or the oxidation of metals.

In general, I perceive change as a necessary and inevitable part of life. While change can be scary or uncomfortable at times, I try to approach it with an open mind and a willingness to adapt. I believe that change can offer opportunities for growth and learning, and that it can ultimately lead to positive outcomes.

That being said, I do sometimes feel scared or apprehensive when something I am used to suddenly changes. This is often because I feel a sense of loss or uncertainty about what the future holds. However, I try to remind myself that change can also bring new opportunities and experiences, and that it is important to embrace these changes and find ways to adapt and thrive in new situations.
