2. An interest calculated on the sum of principal plus accrued interest ​


An interest calculated on the sum of principal plus accrued interest is known as compound interest. Compound interest is interest that is calculated on the original principal of a loan or investment as well as any accumulated interest from previous periods. This means that the interest earned in one period is added to the principal, and the next interest calculation is based on the new, larger principal. The more frequently the interest is compounded, the more quickly the interest will grow, and the greater the amount of compound interest earned over time. This type of interest is different than simple interest which is calculated only on the principal amount.

#For example, let's say an individual deposits $1000 into a savings account with an annual interest rate of 2%. After the first year, the account would earn $20 in interest (2% of $1000).

In the second year, the interest would be calculated on the original principal ($1000) plus the interest earned in the first year ($20) for a total of $1020. The interest earned in the second year would be $20.40 (2% of $1020).

In the third year, the interest would be calculated on the original principal plus the interest earned in the first and second years, for a total of $1040.40. The interest earned in the third year would be $20.81 (2% of $1040.40).

This process continues, with the interest being calculated on the sum of the principal and all the accrued interest from previous periods, resulting in a higher amount of interest earned over time.

An interest calculated on the sum of principal plus accrued interest is called compound interest.
