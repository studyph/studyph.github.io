18. How to responds to the needs of the diverse learners?


(1) Use a variety of learning activities and materials in class. Some learners are visual learners, so make sure to use visual aids such as illustrations, graphs, and charts. However, do not rely on one kind of material alone. Use other activities such as class discussion, group work, laboratory experiments, and more. Make sure that everyone is engaged and participating.

(2) Encourage activities that would make them use their skills and strengths. For example, some students learn better when they discuss things among their peers, so debates and group presentations would be useful. Some do best when they use their psychomotor skills, so it would be beneficial to have plays, skits, or games.

(3) Evaluate learning through different means. Do not simply rely on written tests to evaluate their learning. Use different methods such as project or performance-based activities, use peer evaluation, and rubrics.

Students are diverse and their learning styles differ from one another, so try to make the lessons, activities, and evaluations as inclusive and diverse as possible.

Answer:

A sense of adaptability implies the openness and willingness to learn, acceptance of life's numerous challenges, and adjustment of expectations through the application of hard and soft skills.

As a learner studying Philippines' diverse history, appreciating the importance of history not only as a school subject but also as a means of discovering one's background is paramount. An improved future cannot happen without today's input.

Neither should the learner be satisfied with the past to the extent of complacency. The past cannot be fulfilling without the future. You must study literature and history and make efforts to document stories of life today. These form the history of tomorrow.
