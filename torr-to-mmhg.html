5. a sample of neon gas at 760 mmHg has a volume of 15 L and a temperature of 32 degree celsius.Find final volume of the gas after the temperature increase 73 degree celsius at 760 torr.


Answer:

The answer to this problem is 17.02 L.

Step-by-step explanation:

First, we can observe that the pressure did not change nor the moles and nature of the gas. Thus, the formula that is needed to solve this is called the Charles's Law. Charles's Law  is a derived formula from ideal gas law where it relates two situations which have a change in volume and temperature but not on the number of moles, pressure and the nature of the gas. The formula is shown below

[tex]\frac{V_1}{T_1} = \frac{V_2}{T_2}[/tex]

Using this formula, the solution goes like this

[tex]\frac{15L}{32+273.15K} = \frac{V_2}{73+273.15K}\\V_2  = \frac{15L(73+273.15K)}{32+273.15K}\\ V_2 = 17.02 L[/tex]

17. a 2.5 L of nitrogen gas exerts a pressure of 760 mmhg at 473k.what temperature is needed to reduce the volume to 1.75L at 1140 torr?


Hello!

A 2.5 L of nitrogen gas exerts a pressure of 760 mmhg at 473k.what temperature is needed to reduce the volume to 1.75L at 1140 torr ?

We have the following information:  

V1 (initial volume) = 2.5 L

V2 (final volume) = 1.75 L

T1 (initial temperature) = 473 K

T2 (final temperature) = ? (in Kelvin)

P1 (initial pressure) = 760 mmHg → P1 (initial pressure) = 1 atm

P2 (final pressure) = 1140 torr (in atm)  

1 atm --- 760 torr

x atm ---- 1140 torr

760x = 1140

x = 1140/760

x = 1.5 atm → P2 (final pressure) = 1.5 atm

***Note: In SI the pressure can be mmHg or atm

Now, we apply the data of the variables above to the General Equation of Gases, let's see:

[tex]\dfrac{P_1*V_1}{T_1} =\dfrac{P_2*V_2}{T_2}[/tex]

[tex]\dfrac{1*2.5}{473} =\dfrac{1.5*1.75}{T_2}[/tex]

[tex]\dfrac{2.5}{473} =\dfrac{2.625}{T_2}[/tex]

multiply the means by the extremes

[tex]2.5*T_2 = 473*2.625[/tex]

[tex]2.5\:T_2 = 1241.625[/tex]

[tex]T_2 = \dfrac{1241.625}{2.5}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{T_2 = 496.65\:K}}\Longleftarrow(final\:temperature)\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

Answer:

The temperature is 496.65 Kelvin

22. A sample of neon gas at 760 mmHg has a volume of 15.0 L and a temperature of 32°C. Find thefinal volume of the gas after the temperature is increased to 73°C at 760 torr.​


Since 760 mmHg is equal to 760 torr, the pressure is constant. Therefore, we will use Charles' Law to solve for the final volume.

Given:

[tex]V_{1} = \text{15.0 L}[/tex]

[tex]T_{1} = \text{32°C + 273 = 305 K}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \text{73°C + 273 = 346 K}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]V_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\frac{V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \frac{V_{2}}{T_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = V_{1} × \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{15.0 L} × \frac{\text{346 K}}{\text{305 K}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{V_{2} = \text{17.0 L}}[/tex]

25. A 2.5L of nitrogen gas exerts a pressure of 760 mmHg at 473 K. What temperature is needed to reduce the volume to 1.75 L at 1140 torr


Temperature to reduce volume to 1.75 L at 1140 torr = 496.65 K

Further explanation

There are several gas equations in various processes:

Boyle's Law

At constant temperature, the gas volume is inversely proportional to the pressure applied

[tex]\rm P_1.V_1=P_2.V_2[/tex]

Gay Lussac's Law

When the volume is not changed, the gas pressure is proportional to its absolute temperature

[tex]\rm \dfrac{P_1}{T_1}=\dfrac{P_2}{T_2}[/tex]

 Combined with Boyle's law and Gay Lussac's law

 [tex]\rm \dfrac{P_1.V_1}{T_1}=\dfrac{P_2.V_2}{T_2}[/tex]

P1 = initial gas pressure (N / m2 or Pa)

V1 = initial gas volume (m3)

P2 = final gas pressure

V2 = the final volume of gas

T1 = initial gas temperature (K)

T2 = final gas temperature

Initial conditions

2.5L N₂ gas: P 760 mmHg at 473 K.

Final conditions

volume of 1.75 L at 1140 torr, so that

V1 = 2.5 L

P1 = 760 mmHg = 1 atm

T1 = 473 K

V2 = 1.75

P2 = 1140 Torr (1 torr = 0.00131579 atm) = 1.5 atm

then T2:

[tex]\rm T_2=\dfrac{P_2\times V_2\times T_1}{P_1\times V_1}\\\\T_2=\dfrac{1.5\times 1.75\times 473}{1\times 2.5}\\\\T_2=496.65\:K[/tex]
