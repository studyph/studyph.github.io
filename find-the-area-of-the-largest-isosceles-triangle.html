2. find the area of the largest isosceles triangle that can be inscribed in a circle of radius 6 inches


There is a theorem that says that the largest possible area of an inscribed polygon is that of a regular polygon.

In this case, the largest possible isosceles triangle, is the equilateral triangle.

Now, since it's equilateral, each angle is 60 degrees. We don't know anything except the radius, so we draw the radii to each vertex.

Remember that the area of a triangle is 1/2 (Base) (height). For the height, we have the radius + something, let's say, x. So the height is r + x.

We find x by using the properties of a 30-60-90 triangle. Since in the smaller triangle, the hypotenuse is the radius, then x, which is opposite the 30 degree angle, is r/2.

This means that the whole height is 3/2 x.

Now for the base, we also use 30-60-90. Since it's opposite the 60 degree angle, we use r*sqrt(3)/2. And since there are two of them, we double that to get r*sqrt(3).

Plugging everything into 1/2(base)(height),

[tex]A = \frac{1}{2}(r\sqrt{3} )(\frac{3r}{2} ) \\

A = \frac{3\sqrt{3}r^2}{4}[/tex]

From there, we just substitute r = 6 inches to get: 27 sqrt(3) sq. inches.

5. an isosceles triangle has an area of 24 cm if the height is an isosceles triangle has an area of 24 cm if the height is 8 cm what is the base ​


Answer:

The base of the isosceles triangle is 6 cm.

Step-by-step explanation:

Given :

The area of a isosceles triangle is 24 cm². The height of the triangle is 8 cm.

To Find :

Base of the triangle.

Solution :

Here, we have : Area = 24 cm², Height = 8 cm. So, by using the formula of area of triangle, we will find the base of the triangle.

According to the question :

[tex] \sf \longrightarrow {Area^{triangle}\ =\ \dfrac{1}{2} \times Base \times Height} [/tex]

[tex] \sf \longrightarrow {24\ =\ \dfrac{1}{\cancel 2} \times Base \times \cancel{8}} [/tex]

[tex] \sf \longrightarrow {24\ =\ 4Base} [/tex]

[tex] \sf \longrightarrow {Base\ =\ \dfrac{\cancel{24}}{\cancel 4}} [/tex]

[tex] \sf \longrightarrow {\underline{Base\ =\ 6\ cm}} \bigstar [/tex]

∴ Hence, the base of isosceles triangle is 6 cm.
