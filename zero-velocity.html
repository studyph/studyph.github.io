Answer:

C

Explanation:

Since acceleration is the change in velocity over time, there has to be a change in velocity for something to accelerate. In other words, if something is accelerating, it has to have a variable velocity. If the velocity is constant however, the acceleration is zero (because the velocity isn't changing over time).

24. If an object has zero accerelation, does the mean it's velocity is zero? Explain.


Good Day

No, even if you have 100 m/s of velocity for as long as it is constant, acceleration is zero.

Acceleration is measure as the rate of change of velocity over time.

Acceleration is zero when your velocity is constant over a period of time.

If the change of velocity is zero over a period of time, acceleration is zero since velocity is constant.

Example:

A car is moving at a velocity of 100 m/s and still constant 100 m/s after 10 seconds. What will be its acceleration.

Given:

a (acceleration) = ? unknown

Vi (initial velocity) = 100 m/s

Vf (final velocity) = 100 m/s

t (time)   =  10 s

ΔV (rate of change in velocity) = Vf - Vi

                                                   = 100 m/s - 100 m/s

                                                   = 0 m/s

Solution:

the formula of acceleration is,   a = ΔV/t

acceleration = ΔV/t

                      = 0 m/s / 10 s

acceleration = 0 m/s²

In the given example above, velocity is not zero but rather 100 m/s but since its constant the acceleration is zero.

Only if the rate of change in velocity is zero the acceleration is zero.
