A pharmaceutical saga from Zaragoza

Botamen of the Ríos pharmacy.

Los Ríos was one of the main pharmaceutical sagas in Aragón during the 19th and 20th centuries. This paper describes one of the most representative pharmacies of end-of-century Zaragoza, which is currently preserved in the Pharmacy Service of the Hospital Real y Provincial Nuestra Señora de Gracia (Zaragoza). The authors describe the apothecary and recall the history of the most distinguished pharmaceutical members of the Ríos family.

Shortly after the Fiestas del Pilar of 1895, specifically on October 24, the Ríos brothers inaugurated their new pharmacy located in the Coso, at numbers 43-45, in the Plaza de la Constitución in Zaragoza (currently, Plaza de Spain), where the apothecary had moved from its former location in Coso number 33. The Ríos brothers' pharmacy was one of the 29 pharmacies that operated at the end of the 19th century in the Aragonese capital, for a population that had not yet reached 100,000 inhabitants.

Legacy

The entire Ríos pharmacy is furnished with exquisite carpentry work.

In 1985, the premises of the Farmacia Ríos were sold to the Savings Bank of Zaragoza, Aragón and Rioja. The date of the closing of the apothecary almost coincided with the ninetieth anniversary of its opening in Coso 43 (the roof was removed on September 16, 1985). The furniture and decorative elements, with the exception of the botamen, were donated by Constantino Ríos, on behalf of the family, initially to the Official College of Pharmacists of Zaragoza, after rejecting several purchase offers made to Constantino Ríos Ríos. Later, in 1990, an agreement was reached between the heirs of the Ríos family and the Zaragoza Provincial Council to place it in the Pharmacy Service of the Royal and Provincial Hospital of Our Lady of Grace in Zaragoza, where it is currently located. ,

Artistic style

As a summary, it can be said that the Ríos pharmacy shows an eclectic historicist style, typical of turn-of-the-century taste. Plant motifs linked to the world of pharmacy (opium, ivy) predominate in the decoration, covering the structural elements of the store's cabinets and shelves in an orderly manner. On the roof there are again allegorical allusions to the activity that took place in that place, such as the beaver, the musk deer, the autoclave or the medicinal plants. Likewise, the names of famous professors and scientists linked in one way or another to the professional career of the Ríos brothers appear.
