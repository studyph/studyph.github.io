8. In a 450 mL of water, 50 mL food coloring is added. What is the concentration of the solution? A. 5% B. 10% C. 15% D. 50%

volume of solute = 50 mL

volume of solvent = 450 mL

percent by volume

[tex]\text{percent by volume} = \dfrac{\text{volume of solute}}{\text{volume of solute + volume of solvent}} \times 100\%[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\text{percent by volume} = \dfrac{\text{50 mL}}{\text{50 mL + 450 mL}} \times 100\%[/tex]

Answer:

percent by volume = 10%

9. How many grams of Ca(NO3)2 is dissolved in 450 ml of water to a 2.25 molal solution?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & m = 2.25 \: m = \text{2.25 mol/kg} \\ & MM_{\text{solute}} = \text{164.086 g/mol} \\ & V_{\text{solvent}} = \text{450 mL} \\ & d_{\text{solvent}} = \text{1.00 g/mL} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Determine the mass of solvent.

[tex]\begin{aligned} mass_{\text{solvent}} & = d_{\text{solvent}}V_{\text{solvent}} \\ & = \text{(1.00 g/mL)(450 mL)} \\ & = \text{450 g} \\ & = \text{0.450 kg} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_{\text{solute}} & = (m)(mass_{\text{solvent}}) \\ & = \text{(2.25 mol/kg)(0.450 kg)} \\ & = \text{1.0125 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the mass of solute (Ca(NO₃)₂).

[tex]\begin{aligned} mass_{\text{solute}} & = (n_{\text{solute}})(MM_{\text{solute}}) \\ & = \text{(1.0125 mol)(164.086 g/mol)} \\ & = \boxed{\text{166 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, there are 166 g of Ca(NO₃)₂ dissolved in water.

12. The volume of air in the marshmallow's blaster is 450 mL. The pressure is 1.00 atm. If you decrease the volume to 60 mL, what will the new pressure be?​


Given:

[tex]P_{1} = \text{1.00 atm}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{450 mL}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{60 mL}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]P_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]P_{1}V_{1} = P_{2}V_{2}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = P_{1} × \frac{V_{1}}{V_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{1.00 atm} × \frac{\text{450 mL}}{\text{60 mL}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{P_{2} = \text{7.50 atm}}[/tex]

What is the molarity of a 450 ml solution containing 45.3 g nacl (58 g/mol)?

450ml × ( 1 mole NaOH / 45.3g = 9.93377 moles NaOH

The volume of the solution in liters will be

58g/mol × ( 1 g / 0.0087094358027487 mole )= 6659.444g

Therefore, the molarity of the solution will be

c=nV

c=9.9337 moles × 6659.444g = 66153.3850 M

Rounded to one sig fig, the answer will be

c= 66153.385 M
