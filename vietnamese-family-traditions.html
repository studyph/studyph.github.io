1. What are the Vietnamese traditional music?


Answer:

Traditional music in Vietnam can be divided into two genres: Imperial Court Music and Folk Music. Folk music has a richer history and is woven into the everyday lives of the Vietnamese. Some of the more popular forms are the Chèo, Quan Ho, Hát Chau Van, Ca Trù, Hò, and Hát Xam.

Answer:

Oral traditional , or oral lore, is a form of mortal communication wherein knowledge, art, ideas and artistic material is entered, saved, and transmitted orally from one generation to another. The transmission is through speech or song and may include reports, ditties, chants, prose or verses. In this way, it's possible for a society to transmit oral history, oral literature, oral law and other knowledge across generations without a jotting system, or in resemblant to a jotting system.

Oral tradition is information, recollections, and knowledge held in common by a group of people, over numerous generations; it isn't the same as evidence or oral history. In a general sense," oral tradition" refers to the recall and transmission of a specific, saved textual and artistic knowledge through oral utterance. As an academic discipline, it refers both to a set of objects of study and the system by which they're studied.
