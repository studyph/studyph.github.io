5. A bowling ball has a mass of 10 kg. If Andy applies a force of 35 N to the bowling ball, find the ball's acceleration.


Given:

mass (m) = 10 kg

force (F) = 35 N

N = kg-m/s²

Therefore,

force (F) = 35 N = 35 kg-m/s²

acceleration (a) = ?

Solution:

F = ma

a = F/m

a = (35 kg-m/s²)/10 kg

a = 3.5 m/s²

10. A bowling ball of 45.2kg, generates 319 kg.m/s units of momentum. What is the velocity of the bowling ball?


PROBLEM

A bowling ball of 45.2kg, generates 319 kg.m/s units of momentum. What is the velocity of the bowling ball?

ANSWER

Use the formula for finding the velocity of a bowling ball ( V = p/m) means, velocity is equal to momentum divided by mass.

[tex] \tt Velocity(V) = \frac{Momentum(p)}{Mass(m)} [/tex]

[tex] \tt V = \frac{319 \: kg .m/s}{45.2 \: kg} [/tex]

[tex] \tt V \approx7.05 \: m/s[/tex]

Therefore, the velocity of a bowling ball is approximately 7.05 m/s.

14. A 7.0 kg bowling ball is moving on a bowling lane with a speed of 5.0 m/s. what is the kinetic energy of the ball?​


KINETIC ENERGY

Problem:

» A 7.0 kg bowling ball is moving on a bowling lane with a speed of 5.0 m/s. What is the kinetic energy of the ball?

Given:m (mass) = 7.0 kgv (velocity) = 5.0 m/s

Solution:

- To find the kinetic energy (K.E), use formula: KE = ½ × m × v²

KE = ½ × m × v²KE = ½ × 7.0 × 5.0²KE = ½ × 7.0 × 25KE = 87.5 J

Answer: The kinetic energy of the ball is 87.5 J (joules)
