7. Activity 2: Distinguish if the following equations are Linear, Quadratic, or neither Shade the box of Linear Equation with YELLOW, Quadratic Equation with color BLUE and neither quadratic nor linear with RED.​


LINEAR EQUATION vs QUADRATIC EQUATIONStep-by-step explanation:A linear equation is an equation with the highest degree is 1. In other words, if you arrange the variables in descending order, from the highest up to the lowest degree, the variable should only have an exponent of 1. When you graph a linear equation, its graph will look like a ramp or a diagonal line. It should also have one kind of variable.A quadratic equation is an equation which highest degree is 2. This means that as you arrange the terms in descending order, depending on the exponents of the variables, the variable should have a degree of 2. If you try to graph it as a function, the graph is called a parabola. It shapes like letter U.
