3. What is doppler effect?


DOPPLER EFFECT

DEFINITION

The Doppler Effect is the apparent variation in wave frequency during the relative motion happening in the midst of wave source and its observer.

EXAMPLE

One very good example of this phenomenon, is when an ambulance accelerates towards you, the frequency of the sound waves emanating from the siren increases, leading to a higher pitch and then it lowers down or weakens again after the ambulance passes by.

9. how does a doppler effect occur in space?​


The Doppler effect is a change in the frequency of sound waves that occurs when the source of the sound waves is moving relative to a stationary listener. As the source of sound waves approaches a listener, the sound waves get closer together, increasing their frequency and the pitch of the sound.

Answer:

16. how is doppler effect related to the motion of heavenly bodies from the observer's view?


Doppler effect is an effect that is observed in sound and light waves as they move away or toward an observer. The effect of Doppler in light is the decrease or increase of their frequency. First, let us identify the order of visible light in increasing frequency, we have Red, orange, yellow, green, blue, indigo, and violet respectively.

The effect of the Doppler effect in light is when an observer move toward the source of the light, the frequency of the light increases while it moves away, the frequency of the light decreases.

Let us say for example If the source of light is a Blue light. When the observer will move towards the source of light shift would be indigo then violet. After the observer passed from the source of light, it will decrease in frequency and shift to green, yellow, orange and lastly red.

What is the application of the Doppler effect in the motion of heavenly bodies?

Doppler effect in astronomy is very useful information to the astronomers. the point of reference of the observers is the Earth. If the observed star light shift is red, it means that the star’s motion is away from the Earth. On the other hand, if the starlight shift is perceived to be blue, then the motion of the stars is towards the Earth.

17. what is the general concept of doppler effect?


Doppler effect is a change in frequency of a wave whenever there is a relative motion of the source and the observer..Frequency increases(Pitch becomes higher) when a source is approaching an observer. Frequency decreases(Pitch becomes lower) when a source has passed the observer...

Example scenario:..You are standing on the side walk when an ambulance passes by...As it approaches and nearing you, you noticed that the siren wails at a faster rate(increasing frequency)  and at a higher pitch(noisy). And when the ambulance is right in front of you and it then passes you, you noticed that the siren wails a little bit at a slower rate compared to as the ambulance is approaching,and the pitch becomes lower(somewhat like flat sound).....

The scenario above is the example of Doppler Effect...and the most significant application of this Effect is in Radar technology specifically on Speed Detection Radars for overspeeding vehicles..
