10. From the Comic Strips Lesson "Adventures in Electroworld" by E.B. Clarion (2011)Figure 3. The triboelectric series comic stripQ5. Does the charge of the material remain the same when paired up withother material?Q6. What rule could be applied in determining the charged of the materials?​


Answer:

Explanation:

In the triboelectric series comic strip shown in Figure 3 of the "Adventures in Electroworld" lesson, each material is shown as either gaining or losing electrons when it comes into contact with another material.

To answer your questions:

Q5. Does the charge of the material remain the same when paired up with other material?

No, the charge on the material can change when it is paired up with another material. When two materials come into contact, there can be a transfer of electrons between them, leading to a buildup of charge on one or both materials.

Q6. What rule could be applied in determining the charged of the materials?

The rule that could be applied in determining the charge of the materials is the triboelectric effect, which is the process by which materials become charged when they come into contact and are then separated. The materials are ranked in a triboelectric series based on their tendency to gain or lose electrons. When two materials are rubbed together, the material that is higher on the triboelectric series gains electrons and becomes negatively charged, while the material that is lower on the series loses electrons and becomes positively charged. This can be used to predict the charge on a material when it is paired up with another material.
