2. a connective tissue that provides cushion at the ends of bone​


In synovial joints, the ends of the bones are covered with cartilage (called articular cartilage) which cushions the joint and prevents friction and wear and tear between the bone ends. Cartilage is a soft, spongy connective tissue. The articular capsule prevents wear and tear on the bones.

21. 3. The body has no support and cannot move without the bones. This is because the skeleton serves as the of the body. A. protection B. framework C. cushion D. reflexes ​


Answer:

B. Framework

» The body has no support and cannot move without the bones. This is because the skeleton serves as the framework of the body. The skeletal system or the bones gives the body structural support, shape and framework. It holds the body as one and allows it to move.

Explanation about Skeletal System

Skull - consists of cranium and facial bones. Cranium is made of eight bones joint together to form a rigid case that protects the brain.

The face is made up of 14 facial bones. Eye socket - protects the eyeball, Mandible or jawbone - protects the teeth.

2 divisions of Skeleton

1. Axial skeleton - includes the skull, the vertebral column, the ribs and the sternum. It protects the delicate organ in the body.

2. Appendicular skeleton - includes the bones of the shoulder, arms, hands, hips, legs, and feet. It provides support and aids the body in movement.

Classification of Bones

1. Long bones - are found in the limbs like arms and legs.

2. Short bones - are found in the wrists, and ankles.

3. Flat bones - are found in the shoulder blades and the skull.

4. Irregular bones - are found in face and vertebrae.

Functions of Skeleton

1. It gives the body much needed support.

2. It protects the internal organs in the body.

3. It makes body movement possible through the muscles attached to them.

4. It stores important minerals like calcium.

5. It is where millions blood cells are produced.
