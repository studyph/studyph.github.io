1. A 180.0 mL volume of gas is measured at 87.0ºC. If the pressure remains unchanged, what is the volume of the gas at standard temperature? [show calculations]


Given:

[tex]V_{1} = \text{180.0 mL}[/tex]

[tex]T_{1} = \text{87.0°C + 273.15 = 360.15 K}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \text{0°C + 273.15 = 273.15 K}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]V_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\frac{V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \frac{V_{2}}{T_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = V_{1} × \frac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{180.0 mL} × \frac{\text{273.15 K}}{\text{360.15 K}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{V_{2} = \text{136.5 mL}}[/tex]

2. 1. A 3 L of helium gas is filled in a balloon at 12 atm pressure. If the volume of the gas increases to 6 L , then calculate the final pressure of the gas. 2. The temperature of a 4.00 L sample of gas Is changed from 10.0 °C to 20.0 °C. What will the volume of this gas be at the new temperature if the pressure is held constant? 3. A gas has a pressure of 699.0 mmHg at 40 ° C. What Is the temperature at standard pressure.


Answer:

Using Boyle's Law (P1V1 = P2V2), we can find the final pressure of the gas:

P1V1 = P2V2

(12 atm)(3 L) = P2(6 L)

P2 = 6 atm

Therefore, the final pressure of the gas is 6 atm.

Using Charles's Law (V1/T1 = V2/T2), we can find the final volume of the gas:

V1/T1 = V2/T2

(4.00 L)/(283.15 K) = V2/(293.15 K)

V2 = (4.00 L)(293.15 K)/(283.15 K)

V2 = 4.14 L

Therefore, the final volume of the gas at the new temperature is 4.14 L.

Using the Ideal Gas Law (PV = nRT), we can solve for the temperature at standard pressure:

PV = nRT

(699.0 mmHg)(1 L) = (1 mol)(0.08206 L atm/mol K) T

T = (699.0 mmHg)(1 L)/(1 mol)(0.08206 L atm/mol K)

T = 846.8 K

Therefore, the temperature at standard pressure is 846.8 K, which is equal to 573.7 °C.
