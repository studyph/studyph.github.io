10. A circular garden has a radius of 1.9 m. How many fencing materials are needed to enclose the garden? What are the given facts in the problem? What is asked in the problem? What operations are needed to solve the problem? How will you solve the problem? ​


full detailed answer

Given facts:

- The circular garden has a radius of 1.9 m.

What is asked in the problem:

- How many fencing materials are needed to enclose the garden?

Operations needed to solve the problem:

- We need to calculate the circumference of the circular garden, which can be done using the formula: C = 2πr, where C is the circumference, π (pi) is a constant value of approximately 3.14, and r is the radius.

- Once we have the circumference, we can divide it by the length of each fencing material to find out how many materials are needed.

Solution:

- Using the formula for the circumference of a circle, we get: C = 2πr = 2 x 3.14 x 1.9 = 11.94 meters (rounded to two decimal places).

- If each fencing material is, for example, 2 meters long, we can divide the circumference by 2 to get the number of materials needed: 11.94 ÷ 2 = 5.97. Since we can't have a fraction of a fencing material, we need to round up to the nearest whole number. Therefore, we need 6 fencing materials to enclose the garden.

Simple answer

Radius = 1.9 m, circumference = 2πr = 11.94 m, 6 fencing materials are needed to enclose the garden.
