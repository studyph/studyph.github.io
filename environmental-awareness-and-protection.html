3. critique of environmental awareness and protection?​


Environmental awareness and protection refer to the understanding and preservation of the natural world and its resources. While there has been a significant increase in environmental awareness and protection in recent years, there are still a number of issues that need to be addressed.

One issue is that many people are still not fully aware of the environmental problems facing our planet. Some may be aware of the general issues, such as climate change and pollution, but may not understand the full extent of the problems or the ways in which their own actions contribute to them. This lack of understanding can make it difficult for people to make informed decisions about how to protect the environment.

Another issue is that even when people are aware of environmental problems, they may not prioritize them in their daily lives. Many people may be more focused on their own immediate needs and may not see the value in making changes to protect the environment. This can make it difficult to motivate people to take action to protect the environment, even when they understand the importance of doing so.

There is also a lack of political will in some cases to address environmental issues. Some governments and corporations may prioritize economic growth and profits over environmental protection, leading to decisions that have negative environmental consequences. This can make it difficult to implement effective environmental protections, even when there is widespread support for them.

Despite these challenges, there are also many positive developments in environmental awareness and protection. There are now many organizations and individuals working to raise awareness about environmental issues and to advocate for protective measures. There are also a growing number of policies and regulations in place to protect the environment, and more companies are taking steps to reduce their environmental impact.

Overall, while there is still much work to be done to protect the environment, there has been progress in raising awareness and taking action to preserve the natural world. It is important to continue to educate ourselves and advocate for change to ensure that future generations can inherit a healthy planet.

16. An individual who promotes environmental awareness and advocates for the protection ofthe environment.a. Ecologistc. Environmentalistb. Environmental scientistd. Scientist​


Answer:

Environmentalism or environmental rights is a broad philosophy, ideology, and social movement regarding concerns for environmental protection and improvement of the health of the environment, particularly as the measure for this health seeks to incorporate the impact of changes to the environment on humans, animals, plants and non-living matter. While environmentalism focuses more on the environmental and nature-related aspects of green ideology and politics, ecology combines the ideology of social ecology and environmentalism.

Environmentalism advocates the preservation, restoration and improvement of the natural environment and critical earth system elements or processes such as the climate, and may be referred to as a movement to control pollution or protect plant and animal diversity.[1] For this reason, concepts such as a land ethic, environmental ethics, biodiversity, ecology, and the biophilia hypothesis figure predominantly.

At its crux, environmentalism is an attempt to balance relations between humans and the various natural systems on which they depend in such a way that all the components are accorded a proper degree of sustainability. The exact measures and outcomes of this balance is controversial and there are many different ways for environmental concerns to be expressed in practice. Environmentalism and environmental concerns are often represented by the colour green,[2] but this association has been appropriated by the marketing industries for the tactic known as greenwashing.

Environmentalism is opposed by anti-environmentalism, which says that the Earth is less fragile than some environmentalists maintain, and portrays environmentalism as overreacting to the human contribution to climate change or opposing human advancement.[3]
