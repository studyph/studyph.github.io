2. What is the importance of conversion of units in measurement?


The importance of conversion is a tool to change the value from one unit system to another.

WHAT IS UNIT CONVERSION AND HOW DOES IT WORKS

Unit conversion is a tool for finding the value from one unit system to others. Unit conversion is usually used to determine the magnitude of the value in a particular unit. For example, if you want to know how many meters the distance traveled by a vehicle whose speed is expressed in kilometers, you need to do the conversion. Unit conversion will not change the actual value.

Unit conversion is carried out on the same or different unit systems.

The unit conversion can be done in units of length units, weight units, units of time units, etc.

The following are examples for performing a conversion calculation in weight units:

1 kilogram = 1000 grams

If we want to convert 10 kg, then:

10kg x 1000gr/kg = 10,000 grams

1 kilogram = 2.2lb

If you want to convert 10 kg, then:

10 kg x 2.2 lb/kg = 22 lb

From these examples above, you have known how to use conversion.

4. Conversion of units is important because it shows the relationship between the two units without changing the value. Cite two real-life situations you can think of where there is a need to convert a unit of measure to another. Answer this in 3 - 5 sentences only. (7.5 pts)


Answer:

One real-life situation where there is a need to convert units of measure is when using a recipe to cook a meal. Recipes often specify the amounts of ingredients in units such as cups, tablespoons, or teaspoons, which may not be the units typically used by the cook. In order to accurately measure out the ingredients, the cook may need to convert the units in the recipe to the units they are familiar with, such as milliliters or grams.

Another real-life situation where unit conversion is necessary is when traveling to a country that uses a different system of measurement than the one you are accustomed to. For example, if you are used to using the imperial system of measurement and you travel to a country that uses the metric system, you will need to convert between units such as miles, pounds, and inches to kilometers, kilograms, and centimeters in order to understand the local road signs, prices, and other information. Without the ability to convert units, you may have difficulty navigating and understanding the local environment.

Answer:

MEASURING

The act of measuring is a comparison between standard units and an unknown size. Without mentioning the standard units to which the unknown is compared, the number by itself is meaningless.

Perhaps, you say, the unit is obvious. Everyone in some field of study uses SI, or uses USCS, and the units are obvious. So a human is 2 high, and a beetle is 10 long. How does this make sense?

The measurement only makes sense when the standard unit being compared is noted.

CALCULATING

As important as it is to note units when measuring, it is ten times as crucial to use units in calculating.

In any reasonably complex calculation, there will be a mix of units. When the calculation is finished and the units are equal in both sides of the equation, you have a good chance that the numbers are also correct.

Raul eats one apple each day, except for Sundays when he eats a pomegranate. There are a gross of apples in a case. How many cases of apples does he eat in a typical year?

We need to convert days to years, using the irregular eating through the week. And then we need to convert apples to cases.

Do we multiply or divide by the conversion factors? It is determined by the units. We choose which part of the conversion goes in the numerator and denominator so that it cancels the other units already in the equation.
