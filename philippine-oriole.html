FOOD WEB

Given:

The organisms in an ecosystem interact in many ways to survive. For example, a rosebush, aphids, beetles, spiders, and orioles all interact in a rosebush ecosystem. The diagram below shows how these organisms interact in a partial food web.

Answers with Explanation:

A. Identify the producer organism in this food web. Explain the reasoning for your answer.

Answer: The producer in the given food web is the rosebush. The rosebush is a plant, and plants produce their own food through photosynthesis. Unlike animals, plants are capable of producing their own food through the help of their specialized part called chloroplasts which contains a chemical substance called chlorophyll. And since they are tagged as producers, they serve as food for primary consumers.

B. Identify the primary consumer organism in this food web. Explain the reasoning for your answer.

Answer: The primary consumer in the given food web is the aphid because it feeds on the rosebush. It is the first and only animal that eats the rosebush based on the food web given, thus, it is considered as the primary consumer.

C. Describe what would most likely happen to each of the other organisms in the food web if the beetle population were suddenly destroyed. Explain the reasoning for your answer for each organism.​

Answer:  If the beetle population is destroyed, the spider will lose its food. The oriole, on the other hand, will have limited food also. If there's no food, the spider will starve and soon will die which will gradually wipe out their population in that certain ecosystem. What's worse is that the population of the aphids will drastically increase since there will no beetles that will control their population. This means that every organism in the food web has its own role and plays a vital responsibility that would control the population of the other.
