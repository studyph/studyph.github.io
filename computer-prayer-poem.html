It is meant to let women in general realize that having a baby might be the most challenging career to have yet it is also the most rewarding one. Pregnancy termination is legal in developed countries like USA, UK and Ireland. 18. what do you tink is the message of the poem of work and prayer?​


Answer:

The phrase pray and work (or "pray and labor", in Latin ora et labora) refers to the Catholic monastic practice of working and praying, generally associated with its use in the Rule of Saint Benedict.[1]

Relief with the motto (in gold) Ora et labora

Benedict viewed prayer and work as partners, and believed in combining contemplation with action.[2] The phrase expresses the need to balance prayer and work in monastic settings and has been used in many religious communities from the Middle Ages onwards.

Some orders (such as the Cistercians) applied the concept directly to farm work and became an element in the movement towards land reclamation from rot and agricultural development in Western Europe. Other orders such as the Humiliati applied the concept to the production of woolen cloth using wheels in the period prior to the industrial revolution.

30. To whom you think the poem is addressed to a prayer from the womb

The scream of an aborted fetus, or unborn child, is referred to as "A Prayer from the Womb." It is begging with its mother after being ripped from the secure environment of the womb while fully understanding that it has been expelled.

The author conveys the notion of how compassionate the earth is and how it experiences suffering in a manner comparable to people. The reader is supposed to be able to identify with the feelings of the country. The ideas this poem is trying to get across are still valid today.

The poem "Prayer Before Birth" is written in the form of a plea, a cry for protection from an unborn child who asks God to keep him safe from the cruel and unforgiving world he will soon join. The poem expresses the speaker's pessimism and depression, expressing the belief that the world would not change for the better but would instead carry on with its horrors.

The speaker, an unborn kid, expresses tremendous wisdom on humanity's propensity for self-destruction and violence while praying for future direction and protection from the tragedies of the present world. In the end, the speaker argues that if their petition is not granted, they would prefer not to be born at all.
