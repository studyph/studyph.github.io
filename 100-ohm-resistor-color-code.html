15. what is the conductance of 100-ohm resistor​


Answer:

1/100 mho

Step-by-step explanation:

The mho is the reciprocal (also known as inverse) of the ohm. To calculate the conductance of any circuit or component (including a single resistor), you just divide the resistance of the circuit or component (in ohms) into 1. Thus, a 100 resistor has 1/100 mho of conductance.

17. What is the total resistance of a circuit with three 100 resistors in parallel and two 200 resistors in series? Select one: a. 150 Ohms b. 100 Ohms c. 75 Ohms d. 50 Ohms​


b.100

To find the total resistance, we need to first calculate the equivalent resistance of the resistors in parallel and series.

Resistors in parallel:
1/Req = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + 1/R3
1/Req = 1/100 + 1/100 + 1/100
1/Req = 3/100
Req = 100/3

Resistors in series:
Rtotal = R1 + R2
Rtotal = 200 + 200
Rtotal = 400

Now we can find the total resistance:
Rtotal = Req + Rtotal
Rtotal = 100/3 + 400
Rtotal = 533.33 Ohms

Therefore, the total resistance of the circuit is approximately 533.33 Ohms.

The item in the color coding scheme that allows the color-coded value to deviate to a certain extent is the tolerance.

In resistors, most of the time there are four (4) lines which has its corresponding value.

The first 2 lines represent the first 2 digits of the value. The third color represents the multiplier. And the last color represents its tolerance.

For example: Red Red Red Gold

Where red represents 2 which means the first two colors represent 22.

The third line, being a multiplier, represents a multiple of 100.

Which means that the resistor above has a value of 2200 ohms.

Gold represents 5% tolerance.

This means that 2200 ohms can be between 2,090 up to 2,310.
