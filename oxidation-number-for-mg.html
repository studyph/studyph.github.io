13. 2.) Metals reacts with oxygen gas to form the metal oxide. For example, magnesium reacts as follows: 2 Mg (s) + O2 (g) ---> 2 MgO (s) (A) What is the mass (in grams) of magnesium oxide that can be prepared from 4.20 g of Mg and 1.40 g of O2? (B) Which is limiting reactant?


Solution (B):

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of each reactant.

For Mg

molar mass = 24.31 g/mol × 1

molar mass = 24.31 g/mol

For O₂

molar mass = 16.00 g/mol × 2

molar mass = 32.00 g/mol

Step 2: Determine the mole ratios needed.

Using Mg

mole ratio = 2 mol Mg : 2 mol MgO

Using O₂

mole ratio = 1 mol O₂ : 2 mol MgO

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of MgO produced by each reactant.

Using Mg

[tex]\text{moles of MgO = 4.20 g Mg} × \frac{\text{1 mol Mg}}{\text{24.31 g Mg}} × \frac{\text{2 mol MgO}}{\text{2 mol Mg}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of MgO = 0.1728 mol}[/tex]

Using O₂

[tex]\text{moles of MgO = 1.40 g O₂} × \frac{\text{1 mol O₂}}{\text{32.00 g O₂}} × \frac{\text{2 mol MgO}}{\text{1 mol O₂}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of MgO = 0.0875 mol}[/tex]

Step 4: Determine the limiting reactant.

Since O₂ produces less number of moles of MgO

[tex]\boxed{\text{O₂ is the limiting reactant.}}[/tex]

Solution (A):

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of MgO.

molar mass = (24.31 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 1)

molar mass = 40.31 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the mass of MgO produced.

Since O₂ is the limiting reactant

[tex]\text{mass of MgO = 0.0875 mol MgO} × \frac{\text{40.31 g MgO}}{\text{1 mol MgO}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of MgO = 3.527 g}}[/tex]

You're dealing with an ion, the phosphate anion to be precise, which means that the oxidation states of each atom must add up to give the overall charge of the ion.

So, start by writing down what you know. You know that

the ion has a (3-) overall charge

the ion is made up of one phosphous atom and four oxygen atoms

oxygen's oxidation state in most compounds is −2.
