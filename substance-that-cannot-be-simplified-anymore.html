1. Substance that cannot be simplified anymore


Elements

All matter is composed of one or more substances (elements and/or compounds). Compounds can be broken down into simpler substances while elements cannot be broken down chemically. We can use the periodic table to identify the known elements as metals, nonmetals, and semimetals or metalloids. A stair step line separates metals and nonmetals.  The majority of the elements on the left side of the table are metals. The nonmetals are confined to the right side of the table. Moreover, the elements along the stair step line are the semimetals. Semimetals have the appearance and some properties of a metal but behave like a nonmetal in certain instances.
