4. what you have learned about ethics and communication skill​


Answer:

Truthfulness & honesty is the most core principle of ethical communication. This means that speaking 99 percent of the truth in a matter - while leaving out one percent of the facts - is not ethical communication, as omitting any detail (intentionally) changes the way that a listener will perceive an event.


5. Is the contribution of the ethical considerations in effective communication?​


Answer:

Communicate fact-based messages truthfully and completely. Respect freedom of expression, diverse perspectives and dissenting opinions. Never offend or provoke others. Make the facts and resources on which the communication relies accessible to others.

Communication etiquette

According to Abdul Samad Arief, et al in the book Basics of Business Communication (2021), ethics is a principle to regulate behavior in society. While communication is a relationship between human interactions, in the form of sending and receiving messages.

It can be concluded that communication ethics is the principle that regulates the interaction relationship in the community in the form of sending and receiving messages.

Functions of Communicating Ethics

Quoted from the book Communication Ethics in Social Media: Filter Before Sharing (2021) by Rahmanita Ginting, et al, here are some functions of communication ethics:

Serves as a moral foundation between humans. For example with good language, polite, clear and concise.Simplify the process of delivering messages. If the process of delivering the message is good, the way it is conveyed will be good.As a human guide in communicating. By applying good communication ethics, humans will receive messages with a good response because ethics will produce polite delivery.

Effective and attractive way of communication etiquette

According to pintek.id there are 9 ways to convey messages properly, including:

Speak well. Deliver messages clearly, not too fast, and speak with bullet points.Be still, listen, and listen carefully. You are digesting the information conveyed by the other person. The response you make will be greatly influenced by how effectively you listen.Adjust to the context. Make sure when you are a beginner to learn to communicate, deliver the material according to the context of the discussion so that you are not confused with what is being presented.Body gestures. Gestures also include the language of how to convey messages, with body style also very effective in attracting the attention of the audience.Insert humor. To keep your presentation from looking so stilted that it's boring, insert a joke for ice-breaking.
