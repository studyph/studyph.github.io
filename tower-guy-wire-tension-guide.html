13. write the 3 guide on adjusting the tension​


How do you adjust the tension on a sewing machine?

- You should see a small screw on the flat/closed side of the bobbin case. Turning the screw a tiny bit counterclockwise will loosen the bobbin tension; turning it clockwise will tighten the tension.

How will you adjust tension on lower thread?

- To tighten your bobbin tension, turn the tiny screw on the bobbin case a smidgen clockwise. To loosen bobbin tension, turn the screw counterclockwise. A quarter turn or less is a good place to start.

How to adjust the tension on top thread?

- To increase your top tension if it's too loose, turn your knob so that the numbers are increasing. Try ½ to 1 number higher, then test the stitches on a piece of scrap fabric. Continue until it looks even on both sides and you can no longer see the bobbin thread on the right side of the fabric.

18. Topic: Law of Sines A communications tower is located at the top of a steep hill. The angle of inclination of the hill is 58 degrees. A guy wire is to be attached to the top of the tower and to the ground, 100 m downhill from the base of the tower, forming an angle of 12 degrees. Find the length of cable required for the guy wire.


Answer:

The length of the cable required for the guy wire is 154.938 meters.

Step-by-step explanation:

This problem can be solved using the Sine Law or Law of Sines.

This law says that "the ratio of the length of a side of a triangle to the sine of the angle opposite that side is the same for all sides and angles in that certain triangle".

The mathematical expression for the Sine Law is

[tex]\frac{a}{sin A} = \frac{b}{sin B} = \frac{c}{sin C}[/tex]

where

a, b, and c are the sides of the triangleA, B, and C are the interior angles of the triangle

An image is attached for the illustration of the problem.

Solution:Solve for ∠B on the smaller right triangle (it is also the ∠CBD of the oblique triangle above the right triangle)∠B = 180° - 90° - 58°∠B = ∠CBD = 32°

Solve for ∠ABC on the oblique triangle above the right triangle∠ABC = 180° - 32°∠ABC = 148°

Solve for ∠BAC on the oblique triangle above the right triangle∠BAC = 180° - 148° - 12°∠BAC = 20°

**Note that we used 180° in solving ∠B (∠CBD), ∠ABC, and ∠BAC because the sum of the interior angles of a triangle is 180°.

Solve for side b using sine law[tex]\frac{a}{sin A} = \frac{b}{sin B}[/tex][tex]\frac{100}{sin 20}[/tex] = [tex]\frac{b}{sin 148}[/tex]b = [tex]\frac{(sin 148) * (100)}{ sin 20}[/tex]b = 154.938 meters

Therefore, the length of the cable required for the guy wire is 154.938 meters.
