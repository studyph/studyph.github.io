1. The reason why the volume is constant in an isochoric process and no work is done is because the volume change is zero, in this case, the work done is zero.


Answer:

The work done by an ideal gas is defined as its pressure times its change in volume, or PΔV (or PdV). Because the volume change ΔV, is zero in an isochoric process, however, no work is done by the gas. Hence, the change in internal energy of the gas is simply equal to the amount of heat added.

7. An expandable container contains 4500 ml of Neon gas (Ne) at 75°C. At constant pressure, what will be the new volume of the gas inside the container if the temperature was changed to 150°C? Assume that no gas escapes the container during the heating process.​


Charles LawV1/T1 = V2/T2

where in, V1 = 1st given volume

T1 = 1st given temperature

V2 = 2nd given volume

T2 = 2nd given temperature

Given: V1 = 4.5L

T1 = 348.15K

T2 = 423.15K

Note: Dealing with Temperature in Gas Law must always be in Kelvin, so convertion is needed.

°C -----> K

T1 = 75°C + 273.15 = 348.15K

T2= 150°C + 273.15 = 423.15K

I also converted mL to L, so things will get easier,

mL -----> L

V1 = 4500mL × 1 L / 1000mL = 4.5L

Unknown: V2 = ?

Formula: V2 = V1T2 / T1

Note: To get the formula, use cross-multiplication and division to cancel out like terms in the original formula of Charles Law.

• original formula: V1/T1 = V2/T2

• cross-multiply: V1 × T2 = V2 × T1

• divide both sides with corresponding term and cancel out like terms: V1 × T2 / T1 = V2 × T1 / T1

• final formula: V2 = V1T2 / T1

Solution: V2 = V1T2 / T1

= (4.5L)(423.15K) / 348.15K

= 1904.175 L•K / 348.15K

= 5.46L or 5.5L

Final Answer: 5.5L

Note: The problem above did not ask that the volume must be in mL, however if your professor insist that the volume must be in mL just multiply the answer by 1000 to convert to L.

L -----> mL

5.5L × 1000mL / 1L = 5500mL

1 V2 = (0.50 + 0.75) mol * 8.31 J/(mol*K) * T / (1 atm) = 1.43T L

1 V2 = (0.50 + 0.75) mol * 8.31 J/(mol*K) * T / (1 atm) = 1.43T L2.V2 = (1.50 + 3.75) mol * 8.31 J/(mol*K) * T / (1 atm) = 9.45T L

Explanation:

1.

n1 = 0.50 moles

n2 = 0.75 moles

V1 = 5.0 L

Since the temperature and pressure are constant, we can write:

n1 + n2 = total number of moles

V1 = V2 (the volume is constant)

Therefore, we can solve for V2 as follows:

(n1 + n2)RT = V1RT

V2 = (n1 + n2)RT/P

Plugging in the given values, we get:

V2 = (0.50 + 0.75) mol * 8.31 J/(mol*K) * T / (1 atm) = 1.43T L

2.

n1 = 1.50 moles

n2 = 3.75 moles

V1 = 15.0 L

Using the same formula, we can solve for V2:

V2 = (n1 + n2)RT/P

Plugging in the given values, we get:

V2 = (1.50 + 3.75) mol * 8.31 J/(mol*K) * T / (1 atm) = 9.45T L

21. 2. A system containing ideal gas at a constant pressure of 1.42 x 105 Pa gains 2320 J of heat. During the process, the internal energy of the system increases by 2540 J. What is the change in volume of the gas?


Answer:

The change of volume of the gas is 0.001549 m³

Explanation:

Here, we are to solve for the change in volume of the gas using the concept of Ideal Gas Law.

In our case, the problem did not provide number of moles and temperature but internal energy which is equivalent to the product of the number of moles, ideal gas constant and temperature which is nRT. Here, the pressure is constant which means that it is the same even when the internal energy changes.

For the formula, we will be using:

PV = nRT

where

P     is the pressure, unit is in Pascal (Pa) or N/m²

V     is the volume, unit is in m³

n     is the number of moles

R     is the ideal gas constant, 8.3145 J/mol·K

T     is the temperature, unit is in Kelvin

For the given information

P = 1.42 x 10⁵ Pa = 1.42 x 10⁵ N/m²

nRT₁ = 2320 J = 2320 N·m

nRT₂ = 2540 J = 2540 N·m

ΔV = V₂ - V₁ = ?

Solving the problem

1. Let us solve first for V₁ using the ideal gas law formula.

PV₁ = nRT₁

(1.42 x 10⁵ N/m²) V₁ = 2320 N·m

Divide both sides of the equation by 1.42 x 10⁵ N/m²

[tex]V_{1}=\frac{2320N.m}{1.42x10^{5}N/m^2 }[/tex]

V₁ = 0.0163 m³

2. Now, let us solve for V₂ using the same procedure.

PV₂ = nRT₂

(1.42 x 10⁵ N/m²) V₂ = 2540 N·m

Divide both sides of the equation by 1.42 x 10⁵ N/m²

[tex]V_{2}=\frac{2540N.m}{1.42x10^{5}N/m^2 }[/tex]

V₂ = 0.0178 m³

3. Now, we are ready to solve for the change in volume of the gas. Let us use the formula:

ΔV = V₂ - V₁

Then substitute the given information, we have:

ΔV = 0.01788 m³  - 0.01633 m³

ΔV = 0.001549 m³

Therefore, the change of the volume of the gas is 0.001549 m³.
