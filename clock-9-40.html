21. The minute hand of a clock is 14 cm long. What is the area of the face of the clock which it traverses 40 minutes? add solution plz


Answer:

410.5 cm^2


Step-by-step explanation:

The hands (seconds, minutes, and hour) is revolving around the clock in 360 degrees. That is basically a full revolution. We all know that in every full revolution of the minute hand around the clock, it moves an hour or 60 minutes. Associating the degrees a full revolution has, and a full revolution of the minute hand, we can get how many DEGREES IN A MINUTE.


360 deg / 60 mins = 6°/min


The area that the problem asks for is a sector. Because it asks for the area that the minute hand of the clock covers after 40 minutes.  

AREA OF THE SECTOR is described by the formula


A = (1/2)(r)^2(Θ)


where r is the radius of the circle or the length of the minute hand; and


Θ is the degrees it covered in radians


We first find how many degrees does the minute hand covered after 40 minutes.


Θdeg = (40 mins)(6 °/min) = 240°

CONVERTING DEGREES TO RADIANS

Θrad = (240°)(π/180°) = 4 π/3 rad

Substituting the values, we have

A = (1/2)(r)^2(Θ)

A = (1/2)(14 cm)^2(4 π/3 rad)


A = 410.5 cm^2


Therefore, the area that the minute hand covered after 40 minutes is 410.5 cm^2
