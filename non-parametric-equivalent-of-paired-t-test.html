9. What is the difference between parametric statistics and non-parametric statistics?​


Answer:

Parametric tests are those that make assumptions about the parameters of the population distribution from which the sample is drawn. This is often the assumption that the population data are normally distributed. Non-parametric tests are “distribution-free” and, as such, can be used for non-Normal variables.

28. non parametric statistics involved ratio and internal data​


Answers:

Interval and ratio data are parametric, and are used with parametric tools in which distributions are predictable (and often Normal).

Unlike parametric tests that can work only with continuous data, nonparametric tests can be applied to other data types such as ordinal or nominal data.

In contrast, nonparametric statistics are typically used on data that nominal or ordinal. Nominal variables are variables for which the values have not quantitative value.

30. Activity 1: Statistic RecallDefinition or Description/Purpose or Function1.Data2.Frequency3.Percentage4.Mean5.Standard Deviation6.Table7.Figure8.Parametric Test9.Non-Parametric Test10.Correlation11.Regressionplssss...paki sagutan​


Data: Facts, figures, or other pieces of information that can be analyzed to reveal patterns or trends.

Frequency: The rate at which something occurs or is repeated over a particular period of time.

Percentage: A proportion or fraction of a whole expressed in hundredths.

Mean: The numerical average of a set of data calculated by dividing the sum of the values by the total number of values.

Standard Deviation: A measure of the amount of variation or dispersion of a set of data values from the mean.

Table: A structured arrangement of data in rows and columns for easy reference and analysis.

Figure: A graphical representation of data or information.

Parametric Test: A statistical test that makes assumptions about the distribution of the population being studied, such as a normal distribution.

Non-Parametric Test: A statistical test that does not make any assumptions about the distribution of the population being studied.

Correlation: A statistical measure that shows the strength and direction of a relationship between two variables.

Regression: A statistical technique used to investigate the relationship between a dependent variable and one or more independent variables.
