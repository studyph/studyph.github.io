10. What is more important in human freedom: INNER FREEDOM or OUTER FREEDOM? why??


Inner freedom is more important in human freedom because it is the freedom of the mind, emotions, and spirit. It's the freedom to make choices without the influence of external forces. This type of freedom gives us the power to choose our beliefs, values, and goals. Inner freedom allows us to take control of our lives and determine our own destiny. Outer freedom, or physical freedom, is also important but it is not as powerful as inner freedom because it is limited by external factors such as laws, customs, and cultures. Outer freedom gives us the right to move and act in physical space, but it does not guarantee the freedom of our minds, hearts, and spirits.

Answer:

Both inner freedom and outer freedom are important for human freedom. Inner freedom is the ability to think, feel and make choices independently, free from external pressures and constraints. Outer freedom is the ability to act on these choices and to be free from physical and legal restrictions. Both are necessary for a person to experience true freedom. Inner freedom is the foundation for outer freedom, as without inner freedom a person cannot make choices or be truly autonomous. Outer freedom is necessary to ensure that a person can act on their choices and to gain access to resources to do so. Together, inner and outer freedom are essential for a person to experience true freedom.

23. What is humanism philosophy?


Humanism is a philosophical stance that emphasizes the value and agency of human beings, individually and collectively. Generally, however, humanism refers to a perspective that affirms some notion of human freedom and progress.

Humanists believe that human experience and rational thinking provide the only source of both knowledge and a moral code to live by. They reject the idea of knowledge 'revealed' to human beings by gods, or in special books.
