Your choice will probably depend on the region you live in, because some of these services may or may not support your native language. Also, some of these services can offer significant discounts, which will most likely affect your choice as well.

Basically, all you need in order to master a new field of knowledge is your laptop with Internet access, and perhaps additional materials or software depending on the discipline you are studying. You can watch videos whenever and wherever it is convenient for you, and do the assignments as you find it suitable. Udemy, Coursera, and other similar services often host highly skilled professionals, so you can be sure you are paying for real knowledge. You get a lifetime access to all the materials, and even if you did not get some part of the material, you can always return to it later, rewatch the video, and address the community for  additional assistance.

19. Learning about singapure Country? Essay​


Singapore is well-known for its efficient and effective education system that produces high-performing students. The education system in Singapore is modeled after the British system, with English being the primary medium of instruction. The Ministry of Education (MOE) is responsible for overseeing the education system and ensuring that it remains relevant and effective in preparing students for the future.

One of the unique features of the Singaporean education system is the emphasis on bilingualism. All students are required to learn a second language, with Mandarin being the most commonly chosen language. This helps to prepare students for the global economy by equipping them with language and cultural skills that are in high demand.

Another key feature of the Singaporean education system is its focus on academic excellence. Students are challenged to perform at their best through a rigorous curriculum and high academic standards. Top-performing students are given opportunities to attend gifted programs, scholarships, and advanced placement courses.

However, the education system in Singapore is not without its challenges. The intense pressure to perform at a high level can lead to stress and burnout for students, and there is a growing concern about the lack of emphasis on creativity and innovation in the classroom.

In conclusion, the education system in Singapore has produced remarkable results and continues to be a model for other countries around the world. While there are some challenges that need to be addressed, the MOE is committed to ensuring that Singaporean students are well-equipped to succeed in the 21st century.

A Primer on Course Design

Using a Modular Approach to Course Design

Using a Modular Approach to Course Design

This section of “A Primer on Course Design” introduces a fundamental concept: modular course design or, put simply, building courses out of smaller, discrete units known as modules.

Modules Defined

modules_overview

A module can be defined as a unit, chapter, topic, or segment of instruction. It is a standard unit or instructional section of your course that is a “self-contained” chunk of instruction. A week is a common module length, but it can be shorter or longer depending upon content and your teaching style.

A module structure is especially important in online learning environments, as it provides an aid in the presentation and application of the online teaching and learning process. When students are aware of the structure of the course, they spend less time guessing about what is expected of them and more time focusing on the content and activities.

KEY: Essentially, the module structure, as portrayed in the folder structure in your Blackboard course site design and in your schedule, provides students with a “road map” and can help keep them on track.

The structure, including the elements and how they are sequenced, is one you define. The structure of each individual module may be unique, or it may be a consistent pattern that is repeated throughout the course, in which case, the structure of the first module you create can serve as a pattern or template for other modules in your course. Keep in mind a possible module for your own course as we consider what could be a part of a module.
