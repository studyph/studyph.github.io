10. Philippines facing unemployment​


Answer:

Unemployment rate is at 5.1%

Explanation:

The Philippines has an unemployment rate of 5.1% which means that those who are employed are at 94.9% based on recent statistics. The rate is relatively low compared to other countries. Other nations have as high as 50% rate (in war-torn Syria) while others go as low as .3% in Cambodia.

What needs to be looked at, however, is the underemployment rate. These are those who are working but are in jobs not in line with what they studied in college. This also means that they are working below what they are capable of. The underemployment rate currently stands at 13.5% which is comparatively high.

There is, in contrast, bad underemployment and good underemployment. Bad underemployment happens when the person is underemployed because he or she has no choice, whether because there is no open job positions or he was recently retrenched from a previous post. Good underemployment, on the other hand, is when an engineer for example, decides to become a flower shop owner because that is his passion and it was out of his own free will.

As for unemployment, this can be solved by preparing potential workers for what is available in the market. There are many job openings but there are only a few who are qualified to take on the job. The job cannot adjust, it should be the applicant. If skills are lacking then skill-building must be done and this is something that the government can work on.

It is a special kind of unemployment. It is also known as search unemployment. It refers to the time gap that occurs when a person shifts from one job to the next.

A person who leaves a company or an employed position isn’t immediately employed. It takes some time for them to find another job that suits their interests and set of skills. It might take a week, a month or a year.

During this time, people are capable of working and find jobs but do not accept them because they are willing to work for something better.

23. summarize the causes and the effect of the following contemporary issues. inflation, unemployment, migration​


Answer:

Inflation, unemployment and migration are three of the most pressing contemporary issues that affect economies worldwide.

Inflation refers to a general increase in prices of goods and services over time.

Unemployment is a situation where people who are willing and able to work are unable to find jobs.

Migration refers to the movement of people from one place to another.

Inflation can be caused by a variety of factors such as an increase in demand for goods and services, an increase in production costs, or an increase in the money supply.

Inflation can lead to a decrease in purchasing power and can make it difficult for people to afford basic necessities such as food and housing.

Unemployment can be caused by factors such as a lack of demand for labor, changes in technology, or changes in government policies. High unemployment rates can lead to a decrease in consumer spending and can negatively impact economic growth.

Migration can be caused by factors such as economic opportunities, political instability, or environmental factors. Migration can have both positive and negative effects on economies. For example, migration can lead to an increase in labor supply which can help boost economic growth.
