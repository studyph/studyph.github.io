1. what is the moon Leda?(explanation ASAP)​


Answer:

The moon Leda is a natural satellite of Jupiter. It is a small, irregularly shaped object that was discovered in 1974 by a team of astronomers from the Palomar Observatory in California. Leda is the smallest of Jupiter's four Galilean moons, and is the outermost of the four. It is believed to be composed of a mixture of ice and rock, and is thought to have formed from the same material that formed the other Galilean moons. Leda is also the least dense of the four moons, with a density of only 0.85 grams per cubic centimeter. Its surface is heavily cratered, and its rotation is retrograde, meaning it orbits Jupiter in the opposite direction of the other Galilean moons. Leda is an important object in the study of the formation and evolution of the Jovian system.
