Answer:

3.21 m

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

Length of ladder (hypotenuse): 5 meters

Angle of ladder foot with the ground (θ): 50°

Distance between ladder foot and wall base (adjacent):  x

Use trigonometric ratio to find the distance along the ground:

Cos θ = adjacent/hypotenuse

Cos 50° = x/5 meters

(transpose the component)

(in this case, cross multiply Cos50° to 5 m and do the same with x.)

(it will then give you this)

(cosine will be multiplied by the length.)

x = Cos 50° (5 meters)

x = 0.6427 (5 meters)

x ≈ 3.21 m

x ≈ 3.21 meters

The base of the ladder is 3.21 meters far from the wall.

8. How far (in meters) must a 5 N force pull a 50 g toy car if 30 J of work is done? ​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The work done by an object exerting a force F at a distance d is given by

[tex]\boxed{W = Fd}[/tex]

where:

W = work

F = force

d = distance

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for d

W = Fd

[tex]d = \dfrac{W}{F}[/tex]

[tex]d = \dfrac{\text{30 J}}{\text{5 N}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{d = \text{6 m}}[/tex]

Therefore, the distance is 6 m.
