You are to make a scale drawing of a rectangular garden with a dimensions of 48 m by 54 m and a choice of scale: 1 in = 2 m,1 in = 3 m, and 1 in = 4 m. Which makes the simplest calculation? Justify your answer.

The simplest calculation would be to use the scale of 1 inch = 2 meters, as this will require fewer calculations than the other two scales. For example, if we choose a scale of 1 inch = 3 meters, then each side of the garden (48m and 54m) would have to be divided by 3 in order to calculate how many inches it should measure on the drawing. This requires more calculations than using a scale of 1 inch = 2 meters which only requires dividing each side by 2.

The simplest calculation would be to choose a scale of 1 inch = 2 meters.

Justification:

When choosing a scale for a scale drawing, it's important to consider two factors: simplicity and accuracy.

Simplicity: A simpler scale makes the calculation easier and more convenient. In this case, a scale of 1 inch = 2 meters is simpler compared to 1 inch = 3 meters or 1 inch = 4 meters. This is because 1 inch is a smaller unit compared to 2 meters, making it easier to visualize and draw on paper.

Accuracy: While a larger scale like 1 inch = 3 meters or 1 inch = 4 meters may result in a larger drawing, it may not be as accurate in representing the actual dimensions of the rectangular garden. A scale of 1 inch = 2 meters provides a good balance between simplicity and accuracy, allowing for a clear and reasonably accurate representation of the garden's dimensions.

Therefore, choosing a scale of 1 inch = 2 meters would result in the simplest calculation for creating a scale drawing of the rectangular garden with dimensions of 48 m by 54 m.
