Step-by-step explanation:

What are Complementary Angles?

Complementary angles are pair angles with a sum of 90 degrees. When two angles equal to 90°, they "complement" each other. Complement comes from the Latin completum, meaning "completed" because the right angle is viewed being a perfect angle.

Equation

m∠A + m∠B = 90°

What are Supplementary Angles?

Supplementary angles are two angles whose measures add up to 180 degrees. When the two angles add to 180°, we say they "supplement" each other.  Supplement comes from Latin supplere, meaning to "supply" what is needed.

Equation

m∠A + m∠B = 180°

Answer:

1. 120°

2. 75.50°

Step-by-step explanation:

1. supplementary angles always add up to 180°, so to get the supplement angle, subtract the given angle to 180.

Let x be the missing supplement.

60° + x = 180°

x = 180° - 60°

x = 120°

2. while supplementary angles add up to 180°, complementary angles always add up to 90°. so to get the complementary angle, subtract the given angle to 90.

Let x be the missing complement.

14.50° + x = 90°

x = 90° - 14.50°

x = 75.50°

The supplement of an angle is 4 times greater than its complement, find the measure of angle x, its supplement and complement

Let,

x = the angle

y = the supplement of x

z = the complement of x

Properties:

Supplementary Angles,

x + y = 180

y = 180 - x

Complementary Angles,

x + z = 90

z = 90 - x

Conditions:

The supplement of an angle is 4 times greater than its complement,

y = 4z

So,

180 - x = 4 (90 -x)

now solve for angle x,

180 - x = 4 (90 -x)

180 - x = 360 - 4x

4x - x = 360 - 180

3x = 180

x = 180/3

x = 60°

solve for Supplement y,

y = 180 - x

y = 180 - 60

y = 120°

solve for Complement z,

z = 90 - x

z = 90 - 60

z = 30°

Therefore the unknown angle x is 60° and its supplement is 120° and its complement is 30°
