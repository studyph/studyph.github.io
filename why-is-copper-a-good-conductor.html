Answer:

Washing machine: A washing machine is not typically considered a good conductor of electricity as it is primarily made of plastic and rubber, which are insulators. However, some parts of the washing machine, such as the motor, wiring, and electrical components, are made of metals such as copper and aluminum, which are good conductors of electricity. These metal components allow for the flow of electricity throughout the machine, enabling it to function properly. The machine may also contain heating elements made of metals such as copper or stainless steel, which are good conductors of heat, allowing for efficient heating of the water used in the machine.

Rice cooker: A rice cooker typically consists of a metal pot, a heating element, and a thermostat. The metal pot is usually made of materials such as stainless steel or aluminum, which are good conductors of heat. The heating element, which is typically made of metal, such as copper, provides the heat necessary for cooking the rice. The thermostat regulates the temperature of the heating element to ensure that the rice is cooked properly. The metal components in a rice cooker allow for efficient transfer of heat, making it a good conductor of heat.

Copper wire: Copper wire is a good conductor of both heat and electricity due to its high electrical conductivity and thermal conductivity. Copper has a high number of free electrons, which can easily move through the metal lattice and transfer both thermal and electrical energy. Additionally, copper has a relatively low resistance to electrical current, which means that it can efficiently transfer electricity with minimal energy loss.

Extension wire: Like copper wire, an extension wire is typically made of materials such as copper or aluminum, which are good conductors of both heat and electricity. The metal components of the wire allow for efficient transfer of both thermal and electrical energy, making it a good conductor of heat and electricity. However, it is important to note that the length and thickness of the wire can affect its conductivity, with longer and thinner wires having higher resistance and lower conductivity.

25. why is copper a good conductor for electric current​


Copper has the best electrical conductivity of any metal, except silver.

A good electrical conductivity is the same as a small electrical resistance. An electric current will flow through all metals, however they still have some resistance, meaning the current needs to be pushed (by a battery) in order to keep flowing. The bigger the resistance, the harder we have to push (and the smaller the current is). Current flows easily through copper thanks to its small electrical resistance, without much loss of energy. This is why copper wires are used in mains cables in houses and underground (although overhead cables tend be aluminium because it is less dense).
