If you are trying to describe the answer, the answer is SHUTTLECOCK or BIRDIE.

A shuttlecock is light in weight. Around 4.75 to 5.50 g (0.168 to 0.194 oz.). Its shape was designed to be aerodynamically stable. Its name originates in 1570's England, when badminton first became popular. The "shuttle" part or the rounded bottom made of cork that is covered with thin leather, derived its name from its back and forth motion like the shuttle of a loom used in weaving. The feathery part of the shuttlecock derived its name from the resemblance of the feathers to those on a chicken.
