15. If you want to burn 1200 grams of carbon, how many grams of oxygen would you need?


First of all, we need to know how to write and interpret chemical reactions. In writing chemical reactions, we need to consider both the reactants and the product. The reactant and the product is written at the left and right  side of the arrow, respectively. The reactants are the elements or compounds that will be needed in order for the reaction to proceed, or in simpler words the starting materials. On the other hand, the product is the substance which is formed by the chemical reaction that took place.

In this problem, the reactants is considered to be the carbon and oxygen. The carbon will be in its elemental form while the oxygen will be in its gaseous form. The reaction that will occur is shown below:

[tex]C_{(s)}[/tex]   +    [tex]O_{2 (g)}[/tex] →   [tex]CO_{2 (g)}[/tex]

This type of reaction is a combination reaction however this specific type of reaction can be classified as combustion. It can be further classified into complete or incomplete. Complete combustion occurs for combustion reactions when 100% of the product formed is carbon dioxide ([tex]CO_{2}[/tex] ) while incomplete combustion occurs when some of the reactants form carbon monoxide (CO). For other types of reactions, please refer to the given link: https://brainly.ph/question/547427

Before proceeding with the solution, the reaction should be balanced. Balancing chemical reactions is significant since we should know the amount of reactants that we will utilize to produce the desired amount of product; however, it is not as easy in reality since there are different factors to be considered. To check if the reaction is balanced, the number of atoms of each element should be equal in the reactants and product side. In case that it is not equal, then try different coefficients for each reactant or product to make the reaction balanced. To practice balancing of chemical reactions, please refer to https://brainly.ph/question/1468231 and https://brainly.ph/question/8677.

Once the reaction is balanced, then the reaction interpretation shown in Chang, R. & Goldsby, K.A.(2013) could be used.

[tex]C_{(s)}[/tex]   +   [tex]O_{2(g)}[/tex]   →   [tex]CO_{2(g)}[/tex]One molecule   +   One molecule   →   One moleculeOne mole   +    One mole   →   One moleStoichiometric ratio of  [tex]O_{2}[/tex] to C is 1 is to 1 based on the coefficient of the the respective reactants.

Since the stoichiometric ratio is already known then the mass of oxygen can now be calculated.

Step 1: Write the given

1200 grams of carbon

Step 2: Convert the mass of carbon to moles of carbon

In converting mass to moles, the mass shall be multiplied to the reciprocal of molar mass of the element or compound. The molar mass of carbon is 12.01 g/mol.

[tex]moles of carbon=1200 grams*\frac{moles}{12.01 g}[/tex][tex]moles of carbon=99.92 moles[/tex]

Step 3: Multiply the moles of carbon to the stoichiometric ratio to get the moles of [tex]O_{2}[/tex].

[tex]moles of O_{2} = moles of carbon*\frac{moles O_{2} }{moles carbon}[/tex][tex]moles of O_{2}=99.92 moles carbon*\frac{1 mole O_{2} }{1 mole C}[/tex][tex]moles of O_{2} =99.92 moles of O_{2}[/tex]

Step 4: Find the mass of [tex]O_{2}[/tex] in grams

The molar mass of [tex]O_{2}[/tex] is [tex]32 \frac{g}{mol}[/tex] (since it is a diatomic oxygen, it will have 2 times of the molar mass of oxygen)[tex]mass of O_{2}= moles O_{2}*molar mass O_{2}[/tex][tex]mass of O_{2}= 99.92 moles O_{2} * 32 \frac{g O_{2}}{mole O_{2}}[/tex][tex]mass of O_{2}= 3197.34 g of O_{2}[/tex]

Therefore, [tex]3197.34 g of O_{2}[/tex] is necessary to burn 1200 grams of C (carbon).

22. what is the kinetic energy of a 1200 kg car with a speed of 60.0 m/s​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The kinetic energy of a moving object is given by

[tex]\boxed{KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^{2}}[/tex]

where:

KE = kinetic energy

m = mass

v = speed

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the kinetic energy

[tex]KE = \dfrac{1}{2}mv^{2}[/tex]

[tex]KE = \dfrac{1}{2}(\text{1,200 kg})(\text{60.0 m/s})^{2}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{KE = \text{2,160,000 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the kinetic energy of the car is 2,160,000 J.
