6. What is purposive communication?​


Explanation about purposive communication

Purposive communication is a form of communication by speaking directly, writing, or explaining something to a certain audience in a way that has been arranged in such a way as to achieve a goal.

Different from the communication that we do every day, purposive communication is done with careful preparation to achieve the goals of communication.

Purposive communication is mostly done in professional environments such as official organizations, companies, or agencies.

An example of purposive communication is talking to a client or presenting in front of investors. When we talk with clients or make presentations in front of investors, we do purposive communication by preparing the material and what we want to convey carefully.

An example of purposive communication by writing is writing an email for a client. In contrast to writing a regular letter, we have to pay more attention to the structure and content of the email we write to clients. The purposive form or format of oral and written communication may be different, but in essence, purposive communication is communication to achieve what we want in a professional manner.

Although it is different from everyday communication, the skills needed to carry out purposive communication are the same as everyday communication.

1. Good listening skills

To achieve the purposive goal of communication, we must listen carefully to the words of the opponent in order to prepare the best possible response.

2. Good speaking skill

To do purposive communication, of course, we must have good speaking skills

3. Reading

To carry out purposive communication we have to read a lot to get data for communication material

4. Writing

The ability to write is very necessary in purposive communication through writing, for example, e-mails, letters, official notices, and others.
