1. 1. the figure is composed of two identical squares and four identical triangles,find the perimeter of the shaded region and its area. 2.find the area of the region below.


CALCULATING AREA AND PERIMETER

Perimeter refers to the total distance around an enclosed figure. In the given item, it seems that this is a complicated one but using the basic knowledge learned from geometry and algebra, we can calculate for this problem with the use of analysis.

To solve for this, the first step is to analyze the shapes included in the figure. As what we can observe, there are four identical triangles and two identical squares. The perimeter of the figure is covered by the hypotenuse of three triangles plus one side of the square. Technically, the hypotenuse is the longest side of the triangle.

The next step is to find other relevant details. So as we go over the figure, we can see that the base of the triangles is equal to the side of the square. The side of the square  measures 5 units while the leg of the triangle measures 12 units.

Since we are looking for the length of the hypotenuse of the triangles, we recall the Pythagorean theorem which states that the square of the hypotenuse is equal to the sun of the squares of two other sides of a right triangle. A right triangle is a kind of triangle whose one of its angles measures 90 degrees. Mathematically, this relationship can be expressed as an equation:

[tex]c^{2} = a^{2}+ b^{2}[/tex]

where a and b are either of the two sides of right triangle, and c is the hypotenuse.

Since, the base of the triangle is equal to the side of square, then the base of triangle is equal to 5 units. The other side of triangle measures 12 units. By direct substitution:

[tex]c^{2} = 5^{2} + 12^{2}\\c^{2} = 25 + 144\\c^2 = 169[/tex]

Let us then get the square root of both sides to get the value of c. Since the square root of 169 is 13, then the measure of the hypotenuse is 13 units.

Then, we can formulate an equation to solve for the perimeter of the figure. The formula will be the sum of thrice the length of hypotenuse and the length of the side of the square:

P = 3H + S

where:

P refers to the perimeter of the figure;H refers to the hypotenuse of the right triangle;S refers to the side of the square.

By direct substitution:

P = 3(13) + 5

P = 39 + 5

P = 44 units

Therefore, the perimeter of the given figure is 44 units.

Solving for the area, the area refers to the total surface covered by the figure. The formula for the area of the square is just simply getting the square of one of its side while the formula for getting the area of the triangle is getting the half of the product of its base and height. We can put this into an equation:

[tex]A_T = A_{square} + A_{triangle} = s^2 + 3(\frac{1}{2} bh)[/tex]

where:

[tex]A_T[/tex] refers to the total area of shaded figuress refers to the side of the squareb refers tp the base of the triangleh refers to the height of the triangle

[tex]A_T = 5^2 + 3(\frac{1}{2} (5)(12))\\A_T = 25 + 3 (\frac{1}{2} (60))\\A_T = 25 + 3(30)\\A_T = 25 + 90\\A_T = 115[/tex]

Therefore, the area of the shaded figure is 115 square units.

Two factors to consider:

The local material should be indigenous, which means it should be original, native, and can not be seen or is rare in other regions. This gives the region a unique identity. For example, limestone is rarely used in Asia because of the rainy weather. It is very weak and easily corrodes to low carbonic acid found in weather rain. For this reason, limestones are more suited in regions with fewer rains like savannahs and deserts. It should represent the daily lives and culture of the local region.
