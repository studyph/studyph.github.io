2. What if literature does not exist


Answer:

The way you phrase your question sounds like you are asking about a real future possibility. Unless we suffer a complete global breakdown of society, we will always continue to have literature. But I think maybe you wanted to ask hypothetically: What if there were no literature? What would happen? I think the answer to that is that we would not be human. To my understanding, every known human culture, even (maybe especially) those without a written language, have storytellers and an oral literature of some sort. I remember reading about a primitive tribe of hunters, and after they returned from the hunt, they would spend the evening re-telling the story of the hunt, acting it out, rehearsing it and bragging and laughing. I think we can assume that shortly after humans became human and developed language, this is what they were doing—and this is the origins of literature. And all known human societies has myths or stories about creation, demons, spirits, etc. That’s literature.
