4. what is the molecular weight of an unknown gas if 200 ml of this gas diffuses to an apparatus in 180 seconds while 250 ml of NO2 under the same condition diffuses through the same apparatus in 170 seconds.


Finding for the molecular rate of a gas can be determine through understanding of Graham’s Law of Effusion. In which it describe the relationship between the rate of effusion and the mass of the gas. In finding the molecular weight of an unknown gas if 200 ml of this gas diffuses to an apparatus in 180 seconds while 250 ml of NO2 under the same condition diffuses through the same apparatus in 170 seconds we are going to use the Graham’s Law:

(rate of effusion A)/(rate of effusion B)=√(mass of B)/(mass of A)

We are going to use this equation in finding the molecular weight of the unknown gas.

But before that. Let us find first the mass of NO2.

N=1 x 14g/mol =14 g/mol

O= 2 x 16 g/mol = 32 g /mol

NO2 = 14 g/mol + 32 g/mol

=46 g/mol

Therefore the molecular weight of NO2 is 46 g/mol

Now, Let us find the rate of effusion of A and B.

Let A be the unknown gas and B the NO2

Rate of effusion of A = 200 ml / 180 sec = 1.11

Rate of effusion of B = 250 ml /170 sec = 1.47

Now let us solve for the mass of the unknown gas using the Graham’s Law.

1.11/1.47 = √ 46 g/mol / mass of A

0.755=√ 46 g/mol / mass of A

Square both side

0.570 = 46 g/mol / mass of A

Cross multiply

46 g/mol = (0.570) mass of A

Divide both sides with 0.570

46 g/mol /0.570 = mass of A

80.7 g /mol = mass of A

Therefore, the mass of the unknown gas is 80.7 g /mol

6. 4. How many times faster will NH3 diffuse compared to NO2 gas?(Atomic mass: N= 14, H = 1,0 = 16)​


Answer:

NH₃ diffuses 1.64 times faster than NO₂

Explanation:

Quantities:

MM NH₃ → molar mass of NH₃

MM NO₂ → molar mass of NO₂

r NH₃ → rate of diffusion of NH₃

r NO₂ → rate of diffusion of NO₂

Calculating the molar mass of NH₃ and NO₂

MM NH₃ = (14 g × 1) + (1 g × 3) = 17 g

MM NO₂ = (14 g × 1) + (16 g × 2) = 46 g

Calculating the relative rate of diffusion

r NH₃ / r NO₂ = √(MM NO₂ / MM NH₃)

r NH₃ / r NO₂ = √(46 g / 17 g)

r NH₃ / r NO₂ = 1.64

∴ NH₃ diffuses 1.64 times faster than NO₂

10. Are there the same number of atoms in a mole of nitrogen gas (N2) and mole of nitrogen dioxide (NO2)? Explain your answer.


Answer:

No, there are not the same number of atoms in a mole of nitrogen gas (N2) and a mole of nitrogen dioxide (NO2).

A mole of any substance contains Avogadro's number of particles, which is approximately 6.02 x 10^23 particles. In the case of N2, one mole of N2 contains 2 nitrogen atoms, because N2 is a diatomic molecule where two nitrogen atoms are bonded together. Therefore, one mole of N2 contains 2 x Avogadro's number of atoms, or approximately 1.204 x 10^24 atoms.

On the other hand, in NO2, there is one nitrogen atom and two oxygen atoms in each molecule. Therefore, one mole of NO2 contains 1 nitrogen atom and 2 x Avogadro's number of oxygen atoms, or approximately 1.204 x 10^24 atoms.

So, the number of atoms in a mole of N2 and a mole of NO2 are not the same.

16. A gas sample is composed of 234 mol NO2 and 312 mol Cl2. Determine the partial pressure of each gas if the sample's total pressure is 30 atmosphere.


Answer:

PNO2 = 13 atm

PCl2 = 17 atm

Explanation:

Given: nNO2 = 234 mol

nCl2 = 312 mol

PT = 30 atm

Required: PNO2 and PCl2

Equation: PNO2 = XNO2 • PT

PCl2 = XCl2 • PT

XNO2 = nNO2 / (nNO2 + nCl2)

XCl2 = nCl2 / (nNO2 + nCl2)

Solution:

Step 1: Compute the mole fractions of NO2 and Cl2.

For XNO2

XNO2 = 234 mol / (234 mol + 312 mol)

XNO2 = 0.429

For XCl2

XCl2 = 312 mol / (234 mol + 312 mol)

XCl2 = 0.571

Step 2: Solve for the partial pressures.

For PNO2

PNO2 = (0.429)(30 atm)

PNO2 = 13 atm

For PCl2

PCl2 = (0.571)(30 atm)

PCl2 = 17 atm

Answer: PNO2 = 13 atm

PCl2 = 17 atm

21. If 50.o mL of no2 gas is completely converted to n2o4 gas, under the same conditions, what volume will rhe n2o4 occupy?


Answer:

N₂O₄ will occupy a volume of 100 mL.

Explanation:

First we have to know the relation of NO₂ gas with N₂O₄ gas. To do that we must write the balanced equilibrium equation of this reaction.

The problem said that the NO₂ is converted to N₂O₄. The reaction equation is as follows

NO₂ ⇔ N₂O₄

Notice that the elements are not balanced that is why we have to balance it first. There are 2 nitrogen molecules in the right side of the reaction while there is only 1 molecule of nitrogen on the left side. Same case with the oxygen molecules, there are only 2  molecules on the left while there are 4 molecules on the right. We need to multiply the NO₂ with 2. The balanced equation is shown below.

2 NO₂ ⇔ N₂O₄

We can now see that the molecules of nitrogen and oxygen on both sides of the reaction are now same or balanced.

In order to have 1 mole of N₂O₄ we need 2 moles of NO₂. Hence their ratio is 1:2. If NO₂ occupies 50.0 mL then the volume of N₂O₄ is twice of 50.0 mL which is 100.0 mL.

O: 2 ×  16[tex]\frac{g}{mol}[/tex]= 32 [tex]\frac{g}{mol}[/tex]
