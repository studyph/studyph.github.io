18. If the mass of Steve is 50 kg, what is his weight?50 N50 n500 kg500 NWhat is the value of "g" or acceleration due to gravity on Earth?10 meters per second squared10 mass per second squared10 meters per second10 meters squared per secondIf the weight of a table is 150 N, what is the table's mass?150 kg150 N15 kg15 N


Answer:

If the mass of Steve is 50 kg, his weight would be 500 N, assuming that he is on or near the surface of the Earth.

The value of "g" or acceleration due to gravity on Earth is approximately 9.81 meters per second squared.

If the weight of a table is 150 N, its mass would be approximately 15.3 kg, calculated as follows:

Weight = Mass x Acceleration due to gravity (W = m x g)

150 N = m x 9.81 m/s^2

m = 150 N / 9.81 m/s^2

m ≈ 15.3 kg
