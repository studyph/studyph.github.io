11. explain the Tonnes per centimeter immersion​


Answer:

The Tonnes Per Centimetre Immersion(TPC) for any draft is the mass which must be loaded or discharged to change a ship's mean draft in salt water by one centimetre. The TPC varies with the draught and with the water density.

29. A vessel of 6,000 tonnes displacement has a KG of 6.7 meters, KM 7.3 meters. Find GM.


Answer:

The distance between G and M is known as the metacentric height (GM). A stable vessel when upright is said to have a positive metacentric height (GM), i.e. when the metacentre (M) is found to be above the centre of gravity (G). This is usually referred to as having a positive GM or a positive initial stability.

answer:

metacentric height

Step-by-step explanation:

A cubic foot of sea water weighs 64 pounds, or one-thirty fifth of an English long ton of 2,240 pounds avoirdupois. Thus the contents in cubic feet of that part of the vessel's hull that is below the water line divided by 35 equal the vessel's displacement tonnage.
