[tex] \blue {\overline{ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: }} [/tex]

PROBLEM:

Eunice bought 220 cups of dessert. 50 cups of which are salad. What percent of the cups of dessert are cups of salad?

[tex] \blue {\overline{ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: }} [/tex]

SOLUTION:

• The given problem can be solved by applying what you've learned from lesson on base, rate and percentage.

• The given quantities are 220 cups of dessert, in which 50 of them are salad. Thus, let 220 cups be our base and 50 as our percentage.

• Since base and percentage are known, you're tasked to find the rate. We'll use the formula: [tex]\rm Rate=Percentage÷Base.[/tex]

[tex] \blue {\overline{ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: }} [/tex]

[tex] \large\rm Rate=Percentage÷Base[/tex]

[tex] \large\rm R = 50 \div 220[/tex]

[tex] \large\rm R = 0.227[/tex]

• Thus, the rate is 0.23, when converted to percent, we'll have[tex] \rm 0.227 \times 100 = 22.7\%.[/tex]

[tex] \blue {\overline{ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: }} [/tex]

ANSWER:

Approximately, twenty-three percent of the cups of dessert are cups of salad.

23. A cylinder with a movable piston contains 220 mL of air at 293 K. If the pressure is kept constant, at what temperature would you expect the volume to be 150 mL?​


Answer:

Use Charles' Law:

(Pressure = constant)

[tex] \frac{ V_{1} }{ T_{1}} = \frac{ V_{2} }{ T_{2} } [/tex]

where:

V1 = 220 mL

T1 = 293 K

V2 = 150mL

T2 = ?

Solution:

[tex] \frac{220}{293} = \frac{150}{ T_{2} } [/tex]

[tex] T_{2} = 199.77 \: K[/tex]
