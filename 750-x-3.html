26. Calculate the wavelength of violet light with a frequency of 750 x 10^12Hz


The equation for wavelength can be derived from the equation of the speed of a wave, given by

[tex]v = \lambda f[/tex]

where:

[tex]v[/tex] is the speed

[tex]f[/tex] is the frequency

[tex]\lambda[/tex] is the wavelength

divide both sides by frequency, [tex]f[/tex], to get a working equation for the wavelength, and you get:

[tex]\lambda = \frac{v}{f}[/tex]

Since we're talking of a violet light, we can use the speed of light, [tex]c[/tex].

[tex]\lambda = \frac{c}{f}[/tex]

[tex]\lambda = \frac{3.0 \times 10^8 m/s}{750 \times 10^{12} Hz}[/tex]

[tex]\lambda = 4.00 \times 10^{-7} m = 40 \mu m[/tex]
