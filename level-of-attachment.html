11. A 15-kg block rests on a level, frictionless surface and is attached by a light string to a 10-kg hanging mass where the string passes over a massless frictionless pulley. if g = 9.8 m/s2, what is the tension in the connecting string?


This can be answered with an aid of a force diagram (see attached).

Before going over the problem, assumptions have to be made that are aligned with the Laws of Motion.

Since the block is on a frictionless surface and is attached to a hanging block, the 15-kg block must be accelerating to the right because there is no opposing force that cancels out the Ft force acting on the 15-kg block. Since the string is assumed to have no mass, and that the pulley is frictionless and massless, the tension pulling the 15-kg block to the right (see diagram) is equal to the tension pulling the 10-kg mass upward.The 15-kg block is accelerating to the right while the 10-kg block is accelerating downards. They have the same numerical value of the acceleration.

Doing the force diagram and looking at the entire system as a single body (meaning the two blocks, the string and the pulley are treated as one body), the resulting equation from Newton's 2nd Law is:

[tex]\Sigma F = m_{T}a \\F_T - F_T + W_2 = m_{T}a\\W_2 =m_{T}a\\a = \frac{W_2}{m_T} =  \frac{m_{2}g}{m_T} \\\\a = \frac{m_{2}g}{m_1 + m_2}[/tex]

It is not necessary to solve for the acceleration since what is looked for is tension on the string F_T. To solve this, we apply Newton's 2nd law on any of the two bodies (since they have the same value of tension). For simplification, the 15-kg mass will be chosen.

Again, look at the diagram as a guide.

[tex]\Sigma F = m_1 a\\F_T = m_1 (\frac{m_{2}g}{m_1 + m_2} )\\\\F_T = \frac{m_{1}m_{2}}{m_1+m_2} g\\\\F_T = \frac{15(10)}{15+10} (9.8)\\\\F_T = 58.8  N[/tex]

Thus, the tension on the string is 58.8 N

Two blocks of masses 20 kg and 8.0 kg are connected together by a light string and rest on a frictionless, level surface. attached to the 8.0-kg mass is a second light string, which a person uses to pull both blocks horizontally. if the two-block system accelerates at 1.0 m/s2, what is the tension in the second string attached to the 8.0-kg mass


Answer:

Both blocks each have the acceleration

a = 1 m/s2.

So the net horizontal force on the 20kg block is

Ma = (20)(1) = 20 N

But there is only one horizontal force on the 20 kg block; the string tension "T" so

T = 20 N.

OR

The net horizontal force on the two block system

is (M+m)a = (28)(1) = 28 N

But there is only one "external" horizontal force on the two block system; pulling force "P" so

P = 28 N

The net horizontal force on the 5 kg block is

ma=(8)(1) = 8 N

But there are two forces on the 5 kg block;

pulling force "P" and oppositely directed string tension "T" so,

P - T = 8

T = P - 8

= 28 - 8

= 20 N

21. 33. A 15-kg block rests on a level frictionless surface and is attached by a light string to a 5.0-kg hanging mass where the string passes over a massless frictionless pulley. If g = 9.8 m/s2, what is the tension in the connecting string?


Answer:mass (m₁) = 15 kgmass (m₂) = 5 kgacceleration due gravity = 9.8 m/s²

See the attachment!

Firstly we need to calculate the Acceleration of the both blocks :

• The only one Force is acting on the whole system is 'mg'.

[tex]\implies \sf Acceleration = \dfrac{Force_{(net)}}{Mass_{(net)}} \\ [/tex]

[tex]\implies \sf Acceleration = \dfrac{mg}{ m_{1} + m_{2}} \\ [/tex]

[tex]\implies \sf Acceleration = \dfrac{5 \times 9.8}{15 + 5} \\ [/tex]

[tex]\implies \sf Acceleration = \dfrac{49}{20} \\ [/tex]

[tex]\implies \underline{ \boxed{ \red{ \bold{ Acceleration = 2.45 \: {ms}^{ - 2}}}}} \\ [/tex]

Now, let's calculate the tension 'T' in the string:

[tex]\longrightarrow \: \sf mg - T = ma \\ [/tex]

[tex]\longrightarrow \: \sf 5 \times 9.8- T = 5 \times 2.45\\ [/tex]

[tex]\longrightarrow \: \sf 49- T = 12.25\\ [/tex]

[tex]\longrightarrow \: \sf T = 49 - 12.25\\ [/tex]

[tex]\longrightarrow \: \underline{ \boxed{ \orange{\bold{ T = 36.75 \: N}}}}\\ [/tex]
