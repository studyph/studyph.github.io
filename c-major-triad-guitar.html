8. What is Major Triad and Minor Trias?​


Major Triad

It is built with a sequence of half steps or semitones: O-4-7.0 represents the starting point or root. To get the third from the root, we count four half steps or semitones to get to the third interval. It makes the quality o interval a major third. Then, we count seven half steps or semitones from the root again, this time to get the fifth interval, It now makes the quality of the interval a perfect fifth. The quality of the intervals for the major triad starting trom the root is a major third and a pertect fifth.

Minor Triad

The difference between the major triad and the minor triad lies on the third interval. It is built with a sequence of half steps: 0-3-7. Its sequence has one less half step from the root to the third interval It makes the quality of the interval a minor third. Therefore, the quality of the intervals for the minor triad starting trom the root is a minor third and a perfect fitth.

For us to easily remember it, the interval ot the major triad from root to the third is a major third; meanwhile the interval ot the minor triad trom root to the third is a minor third.

21. why is it important to recognize a major triad from a minor triad?​


Answer:

it's important because Triads are classified according to intervals formed above the root. If the factors of the triad are a major third and a perfect fifth above the root, the triad is a major triad; if a minor third and a perfect fifth, it is a minor triad.
