21. Q36 what would be an observable change in sound when the frequency changes?


Answer:

ncrease in frequency of the sound is manifested as change in pitch

Explanation:

When the frequency of the sound wave increases, the pitch of the sound we heard also increases. On the other hand, low frequency sound produces a sound in low pitch. The mathematical relationship between frequency and sound pitch is directly proportional, high frequency sound produces high pitch while low frequency sound produces low pitch.

Sound wave

Is an example of longitudinal wave and it travels in a medium. A medium can be gas (air) , solid, liquid. Sound wave travels faster in solid and travel slower in gas medium. Unlike electromagnetic wave (sunlight, radiowave, microwave etc.), sound wave cannot travel in a vacuum like the outer space which means, there will be no sound produced in the outer space.

Pitch

Is the highness and lowness of sound. You can hear the sound of motorcycle increases in pitch when you increase or pull the accelerator, and decreases in pitch as you released the accelerator.

Frequency

Is the number of sound waves released by vibration passes a certain point per second. So we can say that frequency is the number of cycles of sound wave per second. The S.I. unit of frequency is Hertz. Frequency can be affected by the length of its wavelength. Long wavelength produces low frequency while short wavelength produces high frequency when both have the same wave speed.

Amplitude

Is the basis of the loudness of the sound. The higher the amplitude, the louder the sound produced. It is the distance from the equilibrium to the wave crest or wave trough.

Wavelength

Is the distance from one point of a wave to the same point of another wave. It could be the distance from crest to crest or the distance from trough to trough.
