3. what is the ionic strength of 0.10M AlCl3?​


Solution:

The formula used for the ionic strength is

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{I = \frac{1}{2} \sum\limits_{i = 1}^{n} c_{i}z_{i}^{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

where:

I = ionic strength

c = ion concentration

z = ion charges

[tex]\\[/tex]

Writing the ionic equation of AlCl₃

AlCl₃ → Al³⁺ + 3Cl⁻

[tex]\\[/tex]

Obtaining the concentrations and charges of Al³⁺ and Cl⁻

c₁ = [Al³⁺] = 0.10 M

c₂ = [Cl⁻] = 3(0.10 M) = 0.30 M

z₁ = +3

z₂ = -1

[tex]\\[/tex]

Solving for the ionic strength

[tex]I = \frac{1}{2} \sum\limits_{i = 1}^{n} c_{i}z_{i}^{2}[/tex]

[tex]I = \frac{1}{2}(c_{1}z_{1}^{2} + c_{2}z_{2}^{2})[/tex]

[tex]I = \frac{1}{2}[(0.10 \: M)(+3)^{2} + (0.30 \: M)(-1)^{2}][/tex]

[tex]\boxed{I = 0.60 \: M}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Therefore, the ionic strength of 0.10 M AlCl₃ is 0.60 M.

4. what is the molarity of a solution made from 325.4g of AlCl3 with enough water to make 500.0 mL?​


Molarity (M)Discussion:

Molarity is one of the commonly used units in chemistry. Molarity is defined as the number of moles of solute per liter of solution --- that is,

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}}[/tex]

For example, a 1.46 [tex]M[/tex] glucose solution contains 1.46 mol of the solute (glucose) in 1 L of the solution. Of course, we do not always work with solution volumes of 1 L. Thus, a 500-mL solution containing 0.730 mol of glucose also has a concentration of 1.46 [tex]M.[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Problem:

What is the molarity of a solution made from 325.4 g of AlCl₃ with enough water to make 500.0 mL?

Given:

mass of solute = 325.4 g

volume of solution = 500.0 mL = 0.5000 L

solute: AlCl₃

Required:

molarity

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of solute.

molar mass of solute = (26.98 g/mol × 1) + (35.45 g/mol × 3)

molar mass of solute = 133.33 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{325.4 g}}{\text{133.33 g/mol}}[/tex]

moles of solute = 2.440561 mol

Step 3: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{2.440561 mol}}{\text{0.5000 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity} = 4.881 \: M}[/tex]

15. The mass percent of alcl3 in water is 15%. the density of the solution is 1.17g/ml. what is the molarity of the solution


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \%(m/m) = 15\% \\ & MM_{\text{solute}} = \text{133.33 g/mol} \\ & d_{\text{solution}} = \text{1.17 g/mL} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the mass of solute (AlCl₃).

Assume that the mass of solution is 100 g.

[tex]\begin{aligned} mass_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{\%(m/m)}{100\%} \times mass_{\text{solution}} \\ & = \frac{15\%}{100\%} \times \text{100 g} \\ & = \text{15 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{mass_{\text{solute}}}{MM_{\text{solute}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{15 g}}{\text{133.33 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.1125 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the volume of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} V_{\text{solution}} & = \frac{mass_{\text{solution}}}{d_{\text{solution}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{100 g}}{\text{1.17 g/mL}} \\ & = \text{85.47 mL} \\ & = \text{0.08547 L} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 5: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} M & = \frac{n_{\text{solute}}}{V_{\text{solution}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.1125 mol}}{\text{0.08547 L}} \\ & = \text{1.3163 mol/L} \\ & \approx \boxed{1.32 \: M} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molarity of the solution is 1.32 M.

17. AlCl3 + H2O arrow Al(OH)3 + HCl


Explanation:

The chemical equation you provided represents a reaction between aluminum chloride (AlCl3) and water (H2O) to form aluminum hydroxide (Al(OH)3) and hydrochloric acid (HCl).

This is a double displacement reaction where the aluminum chloride and water molecules exchange their ions to form new compounds. The aluminum chloride dissociates in water to form Al3+ ions and Cl- ions, while water dissociates into H+ ions and OH- ions.

When the Al3+ ions react with the OH- ions, they form aluminum hydroxide, which is a white precipitate. At the same time, the H+ ions react with the Cl- ions to form hydrochloric acid, which is a strong acid.

Overall, this reaction represents a neutralization reaction where an acid (HCl) and a base (Al(OH)3) react to form a salt (AlCl3) and water (H2O).

23. make a mole conversion of this equation"2 Al + 3CI2 ---> AlCl3"​


Answer:

To convert the equation "2 Al + 3 CI2 -> AlCl3" into molar amounts, you would need to have information about the number of moles of each substance present in the equation.

The coefficients in the equation (2, 3, and 1) indicate the relative numbers of moles of each substance involved in the reaction.

For example, the coefficient 2 in front of Al indicates that 2 moles of aluminum (Al) are used in the reaction. Similarly, 3 moles of chlorine gas (Cl2) are used, and 1 mole of aluminum chloride (AlCl3) is produced.

So the balanced equation in terms of moles will be "2 moles Al + 3 moles Cl2 -> 1 mole AlCl3"
