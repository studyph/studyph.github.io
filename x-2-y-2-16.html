19. 4. What is the y-intercept of the function y = (x+2)(x-2)(x-4) ?A. -16 B. 8 C. 16 D. 32​


Answer:

C. 16

Step-by-step explanation:

Expand the equation

y = (x+2)(x-2)(x-4)

y = (x+2)(x-2)*(x-4) by associative property

y = (x2-4)*(x-4) by sum and difference of squares

y = x3 - 4x2 - 4x +16 by FOIL Method

set x = 0 to get y-intercept

y = 0^3 - 4*0^2 - 4*0 +16

y = 16

[tex]\sf x - y = - 2[/tex]

[tex]\sf x + \cancel{y} = 16 \\ \sf x \: \cancel{- y} = - 2[/tex]

[tex]\sf 2x = 14 \\[/tex]

Third, solve this equation by dividing both sides of the equation by 2.

[tex]\sf \frac{ \cancel{2}x}{ \cancel{2}} = \frac{14}{2} [/tex]

[tex]\sf \cancel{7 - 7}+ y = 16 - 7[/tex]

[tex]\sf y = 9[/tex]

Finally, let's check if the values of x and y are correct.

[tex]\sf (7) + (9) \stackrel ? = 16[/tex]
