A rhythmic phrase is a musical term that refers to a unit of rhythm or a series of notes or beats that are played or sung together in a particular pattern or sequence. It can be thought of as a musical sentence, where the rhythmic patterns create a sense of direction and momentum in the music. In other words, a rhythmic phrase is a group of notes or beats that work together to create a musical idea or theme within a larger piece of music. It is an essential component of many music genres, including classical, jazz, rock, and pop music.

7. Ano ang rhythmic phrase​


Answer:

In music theory, a phrase (Greek: φράση) is a unit of musical meter that has a complete musical sense of its own, built from figures, motifs, and cells, and combining to form melodies, periods and larger sections. A phrase is a substantial musical thought, which ends with a musical punctuation called a cadence.
