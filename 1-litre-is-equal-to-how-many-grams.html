11. 1 litre is equal to​


Answer:

The litre (British and Commonwealth spelling) or liter (American spelling) (SI symbols L and l,[1] other symbol used: ℓ) is a metric unit of volume. It is equal to 1 cubic decimetre (dm3), 1000 cubic centimetres (cm3) or 0.001 cubic metre (m3). A cubic decimetre (or litre) occupies a volume of 10 cm × 10 cm × 10 cm (see figure) and is thus equal to one-thousandth of a cubic metre.

22. Nathalie has 6 litres of cold water equally poured into glasses of 3/4 Liter,how many glasses of water will there be?​


There will be 8 glasses of water.

Step-by-step explanation:

Natalie has 6 L of cold water equally poured into glasses of 3/4 L, how many glasses of water will there be?

[tex]\begin{gathered}6 \div \frac{3}{4} \\ 6 \times \frac{4}{3} = \frac{24}{3} = 8\end{gathered} [/tex]

Therefore, there will be 8 glasses of water.
