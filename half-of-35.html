9. It takes 35 days for a 512 gram of a radioactive sample to decay to a final amount of 4g.What is the half- life of the element?​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & m_{0} = \text{512 g} \\ & m = \text{4 g} \\ & t = \text{35 days} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the rate constant.

[tex]\begin{aligned} t & = -\frac{\text{ln} \: \left(\dfrac{m}{m_{0}}\right)}{k} \\ & = -\frac{\text{ln} \: \left(\dfrac{\text{4 g}}{\text{512 g}}\right)}{\text{35 days}} \\ & = \text{0.1386294 /day} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the half-life.

[tex]\begin{aligned} t_{\frac{1}{2}} & = \frac{\text{ln 2}}{k} \\ & = \frac{\text{ln 2}}{\text{0.1386294 /day}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{5 days}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the half-life of the element is 5 days.

14. Jack is 35 years younger than Karen. frank is half of Jack's age. Jennifer is 17 years older than Frank. Jennifer is 35 years old, how old is Karen?​


Answer:

Let's use algebra to solve this problem.

Let's start by assigning variables to the ages of Karen and Jack. We'll let K be Karen's age, and since Jack is 35 years younger than Karen, we can say that J = K - 35.

Next, we're told that Frank is half of Jack's age, so F = 0.5J.

Finally, we're told that Jennifer is 17 years older than Frank and that Jennifer is 35 years old, so J = F + 17 and J = 35.

We can use the equation J = F + 17 to substitute for J in the equation J = K - 35:

35 = K - 35 + 17

Simplifying this equation, we get:

K = 53

Therefore, Karen is 53 years old
