16. A force F1 of magnitude 6.00 units acts on an object at the origin in a direction theata=30 degrees above the positive x-axis. A second force F2 of magnitude 5.00 units acts on the object in the direction of the positive y-axis. Find graphically the magnitude and direction of the resultant force F1+F2


The magnitude and direction of the resultant force : 9.54 and 56.98°

Further explanation

In the coordinates of the two-dimensional plane (xy) there is a position vector.

Two dimensions because the coordinates are only x and y, three dimensions if the coordinates are x, y, and z.

If there is a point P then

You can write P as:

P = xi + yj

where i and j denotes the unit vector in the x and y directions

While the size of the vector:

[tex]\rm \displaystyle P=\sqrt {x ^ 2 + y ^ 2}[/tex]

and its direction

[tex]\rm \displaystyle \theta = arc \: tan \: \dfrac {y} {x}[/tex]

Each vector can be described in two perpendicular vectors

The first vector is a vector component on the x-axis (Fx) and the second vector is a vector component on the y-axis (Fy)

Using the principle rules of cosines and sines in triangles then:

Fx = F cos θ

Fy = F sin θ

The magnitude of a vector is always positive, while the angle is formed (θ) can be both positive and negative depending on the quadrant (direction) of the vector in the Cartesian coordinate system

A force F₁ of magnitude 6.00 units acts at the origin in a direction 30.0 above the positive x axis.

Then the component of F₁

F₁ = 6 cos(30)i + 6 sin (30)j

F₁= 5.2 i + 3 j

A second force F₂ of magnitude 5.00 units acts at the origin in the direction of the positive y axis

F₂ = 5 cos 90 i + 5 sin 90 j

F₂ = 0 i + 5 j

The Resultant of vectors is the sum of F₁ and F₂ vector units (components x and y)

R = F₁ + F₂

R = (5.2 + 0) i + (3 + 5) j

R = 5.2 i + 8 j

The magnitude of resultant force:

[tex]\rm R=\sqrt{R_x^2+R_y^2}\\\\R=\sqrt{5.2^2+8^2}\\\\R=\sqrt{91.04}=9.54[/tex]

The direction of the resultant force

tan Θ = (Ry / Rx)

tan Θ = (8 / 5.2) = 1.5385

Θ = 56.98⁰

23. how to solve for static equilibrium with 3 forces (F1, F2, F3) in grams coverting into Newton?​


To solve for static equilibrium with three forces (F1, F2, F3) in grams converting into Newton, use the following equation:

F1 + F2 + F3 = 0

Each force can be converted to Newton by dividing the force in grams by 9.8, which is the gravitational acceleration of Earth. Thus, the equation can be written as:

F1/9.8 + F2/9.8 + F3/9.8 = 0

Solving this equation will give you the value of each force in Newton.
