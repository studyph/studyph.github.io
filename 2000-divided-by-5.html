28. During the “Brigada Eskwela” 2000 parents joined the cleanliness campaign. Teacher Sonia thought of dividing them into 8 members each. Was she able to divide them with every student as a member of a group? Why?


DIVISIBILITY RULE OF 8

Answer: Yes. Teacher Sonia was able to divide 2000 parents equally into groups of 8 members during the Brigada Eskwela's cleanliness campaign because 2000 is divisible by 8.

Step-by-step explanation:

We need to know how to determine if the number is divisible by 8. A number is divisible by 8 when the last 3 digits are divisible by 8 or it ends in 3 zeroes. Divisibility by 8 means that a certain number can be divided by 8 without leaving any remainder. Since 2000 ends in 3 zeros, we can say that it can be divided by 8 equally without leaving members outside of the group of 8 members. The greatest remainder that a certain divisor can have is just one less than that divisor. So 8 can have a remainder as much as 7.

29. If 2000 is divided into three parts proportional to 1:4:15, then the largest part is? A. 1,000 B. 1, 200 C. 1, 500 D. 1, 800​


Answer:

C. 1,500

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x = the proportional part of 2000.

Proportions: x:4x:15x

The sum of the three proportions must be equal to 2000.

x + 4x + 15x = 2000

20x = 2000

x = 100

Largest Part: 15x = 15(100) = 1,500
