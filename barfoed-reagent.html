17. They are the reactants that are not used up when the reaction is finished? A. Excess Reagents B. Solute C. Limiting Reagents D. Solution


Answer:

A. Excess Reagents

Explanation:

The reactants that are not used up when the reaction is finished are called excess reactants or excess reagents. Excess reactants are the reactants that are present in a quantity greater than what is needed to completely react with the limiting reactant, and therefore remain unreacted when the reaction is completed.

The limiting reactant, on the other hand, is the reactant that is completely consumed in the reaction, limiting the amount of product that can be formed. Once the limiting reactant is consumed, any excess reactants that are present will remain unreacted and will not contribute to the formation of additional product.

It is important to identify the limiting reactant in a chemical reaction to accurately predict the amount of product that can be formed, and to minimize the waste of excess reactants.
