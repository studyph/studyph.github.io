24. Which equation has a graph that is a parabola with a vertex at (5, 3)? y = (x – 5)2 + 3 y = (x + 5)2 + 3 y = (x – 3)2 + 5 y = (x + 3)2 + 5


Answer:

 [tex]y=(x-5)^{2} +3[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

A parabola is an equation in a form of [tex]y=a^{2} +bx+c[/tex], where [tex]a\neq 0[/tex].The vertex of a parabola is represented as (h,k) in either formula below.

        [tex]y=a(x-h)^{2} +k[/tex]   (parabola opens vertically)

        [tex]x=a(y-k)^{2} +h[/tex]   (parabola opens horizontally)

Referring to the formula above,

        the vertex of the equation [tex]y=(x-5)^{2} +3[/tex] is at [tex](5,3)[/tex]

        the vertex of the equation [tex]y=(x+5)^{2} +3[/tex] is at [tex](-5,3)[/tex]

        the vertex of the equation [tex]y=(x-3)^{2} +5[/tex] is at [tex](3,5)[/tex]

        the vertex of the equation [tex]y=(x+3)^{2} +5[/tex] is at [tex](-3,5)[/tex]

Hence, the answer is [tex]y=(x-5)^{2} +3[/tex]
