29. facts that you learned in 21st century literature​


The 21st century was marked by the very rapid development of information technology and the development of automation where many routine and repetitive jobs began to be replaced by machines, both production machines and computer machines.

Entering the 21st century, these technological advances have entered various aspects of life, including the field of education. Educators and students are required to have teaching and learning skills in the 21st century.

In general, 21st century competence is called 4C. 21st century competence is a collection of skills needed by the times. The 21st century competencies in question include creative thinking skills, critical thinking and problem solving, communication, and collaboration. The 4C competencies are starting to be well instilled in the learning process.

Literacy skills focus on how you can distinguish facts, determine sources of information, be able to ward off false information (hoaxes), and know the technology behind it. This skill is very necessary in the midst of a rapidly developing information age. There is a lot of information flooding the internet, therefore skills are needed to sort and check whether the information is correct or not.

The three 21st century literacy skills (literacy skills) are:

Information literacy: understanding facts, figures, statistics, and dataMedia literacy: understanding the methods and channels through which information is publishedTechnological literacy: understanding the machines that create information
