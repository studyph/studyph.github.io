3. A triangle has sides of lengths 14 cm and 50 cm. If the triangle is a right triangle, which of the following could be the length of the third side? Explain further please.... Below are the possibilities: If 50cm is a hypotenuse, (50)^2 = (14)^2 + (x)^2 x = 48cm If the unknown is the hypotenuse, (x)^2 = (50)^2 + (14)^2 x = 51.92cm


The length of the third side : 48 cm or 51.92 cm

Further explanation

Triangles are flat fields bounded by 3 intersecting sides and 3 angles

This side can be the same length or different.

If there is an angle of 90 degrees, this triangle is said to be a right angle

For the right triangle, we can use the Pythagorean theorem :

the sum of the squares of the length of the two sides equals the square of the length of the hypotenuse

Can be formulated :

[tex]\rm c^2=a^2+b^2[/tex]

c = the hypotenuse

A triangle has sides of lengths 14 cm and 50 cm

Both sides are known to be the hypotenuse or both sides are forming sides of the right angle

so the possibilities above are true

the first possibility, 50cm is the hypotenuse

c = 50

a = 14

then

[tex]\rm b=\sqrt{c^2-a^2}\\\\b=\sqrt{50^2-14^2}\\\\b=48~cm[/tex]

the second possibility, the length of the third side is the hypotenuse

a = 50

b = 14

[tex]\rm c=\sqrt{50^2+14^2}\\\\c=51.92~cm[/tex]
