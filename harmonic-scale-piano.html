17. scale degrees and intervals harmonic and melodic


Answer:

Harmonic and melodic interval

Explanation:

Harmonic interval happens when the two notes are sounded simultaneously.

Melodic interval happens when the two notes are sounded one at a time.

On the staff, when you see two notes that are aligned vertically, it is a harmonic interval. On the other hand, when two notes are written horizontally, it is a melodic interval.

On the keyboard, when you play two notes together at one strike, the two pitches sound at the same time, the notes are played at harmonic interval.

On the other hand, when you play one key for 1 count and play another key   for two counts, the sounds are heard one after the other. It is a melodic interval.
