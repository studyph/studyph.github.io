Answer:

When the forces acting on an object are unbalanced, they do not cancel out one another. An unbalanced force acting on an object results in the object's motion changing. The object may change its speed (speed up or slow down), or it may change its direction.

Explanation:

Example: When we push a concrete wall with our hands the wall doesn't move. This is because the force by us is balanced by the other forces acting on the wall. If the resultant of all the forces acting on a body is not zero, the forces are called unbalanced forces.
