4. How many grams of K2SO4 are contained In 50.0 mL of 0.200 N solution? How many millimoles of K2SO4 are present? What Is the molarity of this solution? ​


mass = 0.87 gr

millimoles = 5

molarity of solution = 0.1 M

Further explanation

Molarity is a way to express the concentration of the solution  

Molarity shows the number of moles of solute in every 1 liter of solute or mmol in each ml of solution  

 [tex]\rm \boxed{\bold{M=\dfrac{n}{V}}}[/tex]

Where  

M = Molarity  

n = Number of moles of solute  

V = Volume of solution  

Normality = the number of moles equivalent of solute in 1 liter of solution

[tex]\rm \boxed{\bold{ N=\dfrac{eq}{L}}}[/tex]

[tex]\rm eq=\dfrac{mass}{equivalent~weight}[/tex]

Relationship between normality and molarity

N = M x n ⇒ n = valence

1. mass

50.0 mL of 0.200 N solution

K₂SO₄ , there are two potassium ions per sulfate ion, so :

[tex]\rm BE~K_2SO_4=\dfrac{molar~mass}{2}=\dfrac{174}{2}=87[/tex]

mass of K₂SO₄ :

[tex]\rm N=\dfrac{mass/equivalent~weight}{L}\\\\0.2=\dfrac{mass/87}{0.05~L}\\\\mass=\boxed{\bold{0.87~gr}}[/tex]

2. milimoles

equivalent = mol x valence (valence = 2 for K₂SO₄)

[tex]\rm mol=\dfrac{equivalent}{2}=\dfrac{mass/EW}{2}=\dfrac{0.87/87}{2}=\boxed{\bold{0.005~mol=5~mlmol}}[/tex]

3. Molarity

[tex]\rm M=\dfrac{n}{V}=\dfrac{0.005}{0.05}=\boxed{\bold{0.1~M}}[/tex]

or :

[tex]\rm M=\dfrac{Normality}{2}=\dfrac{0.2}{2}=0.1~M[/tex]

10. How many millimoles of solute are contained in 250.0 mL of 0.0423 M KSCN?


Millimoles of solute (KSCN) = 10.575

Further explanation

Stochiometry in Chemistry studies about chemical reactions mainly emphasizes quantitative, such as the calculation of volume, mass, which is related to the number of molecules, elements, etc.

Mole itself is the number of particles contained in a substance

1 mole = 6.02.10²³ particles

Mole can also be found from the amount of substance mass and its molar mass

[tex]\large{\boxed{\boxed{\bold{mole=\frac{mass}{molar\:mass}}}}[/tex]

Molarity is a way to express the concentration of the solution

Molarity shows the number of moles of solute in every 1 liter of solute or mmol in each ml of solution

[tex]\large{\boxed {\bold {M ~ = ~ \frac {n} {V}}}[/tex]

Where

M = Molarity

n = Number of moles of solute

V = Volume of solution

So to find the number of moles can be expressed as

[tex]\boxed{\bold{n=V\times M}}[/tex]

250.0 mL of 0.0423 M KSCN, then millimoles of solute (KSCN)

[tex]\rm mmol=250~ml\times 0.0423~M\\\\mmol=\boxed{\bold{10.575}}[/tex]

23. if 8 moles of potassium superoxide reacts with 5 moles of water how many moles will remain underacted? A. 1 mole KO2 B. 3.0 moles KO2 C. 1.0 mole H2O D. 3.0 moles H2O


Number of unreacted moles : B. 3.0 moles KO₂

Further explanation

A reaction coefficient is a number in the chemical formula of a substance involved in the reaction equation. The reaction coefficient is useful for equalizing reagents and products.

Limiting reagents are reactants that limit the outcome of the reaction. Where if two substances are reacted there is a possibility that both substances are used up or one is used up and the other is left.

The method that can be used to find limiting reagents is to divide the number of moles of known substances by their respective coefficients, and the small quotient will be used up or as a limiting reagent

Reaction

2 KO₂ + 2 H₂O ⇒ 2 KOH + H₂O₂ + O₂

8 moles of potassium superoxide reacts with 5 moles of water, then :

Comparison of both moles:

KO₂ : H₂O = 8 : 5

Comparison of both moles with coefficients :

[tex]\rm KO_2=\dfrac{8}{2}=4\\\\H_2O=\dfrac{5}{2}=2.5[/tex]

So H₂O : limiting reactants

mol KO₂  that reacts :

[tex]\rm mol~KO_2 = \dfrac{coefficient~KO_2}{coefficient~H_2O}\times mol~H_2O\\\\mol~KO_2=\dfrac{2}{2}\times 5\\\\mol~KO_2~=5[/tex]

                2 KO₂ + 2 H₂O ⇒ 2 KOH + H₂O₂ + O₂

initial            8             5

reaction       5             5           5           2.5      2.5

remain         3             0           5           2.5      2.5
