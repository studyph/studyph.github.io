2. b) If the perimeter of the figure is 198 cm, what is the area of the rectangle?​


In the given problem, apply your knowledge in algebraic expression in finding perimeter, area and its dimensions.

Formula for perimeter of a rectangle is 2L + 2W.

Formula for area of a rectangle is LW.

Given:

Perimeter of the figure is 198 cm

Problem:

What is the area of the rectangle?

Solution:

Use the two formula to derive the equation for its dimension.

P = 2l + 2w

198 = 2l + 2w

99 = l + w

l = 99 - w

Then solve for area, if l = 99 - w.

A = LW

A = (99-w)w

A = 99w - w²

Answer:

Area is equal to (99w - w²) cm².

6. the length of a rectangle is 7 cm longer than its width its area is 198 cm² find its dimension​


Answer:

18cm x 11cm

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x be the width

x + 7 be the length

A = lw = 198

(x + 7) x = 198

x² + 7x = 198

x² + 7x - 198 = 0

(x - 11)(x + 18) = 0

x - 11 = 0 x + 18 = 0

x = 11 x = - 18

The positive value of x should be used since there is no negative measurement.

Thus, width is 11 cm and length is (11 + 7) = 18 cm.

22. Gold-198 has a half-life of approximately 3 days. If a 100 g sample of gold-198 decays for 9 days, approximately how much gold-198 remains in the sample?


The remaining gold-198 in the sample is 12.5 grams

Further explanation

The atomic nucleus can experience decay into 2 particles or more due to the instability of its atomic nucleus.

Usually radioactive elements have an unstable atomic nucleus.

The main particles are emitted by radioactive elements ,so that they generally decay, are alpha (α), beta (β) and gamma (γ) particles

General formulas used in decay:

[tex]\large {\boxed {\bold {N_t = N_0 (\dfrac {1} {2}) ^ {T/t\frac {1} {2}}}}[/tex]

T = duration of decay

t 1/2 = half-life

N₀ = the number of initial radioactive atoms

Nt = the number of radioactive atoms left after decaying during T time

The half-life can be expressed in a decay constant (λ)

[tex]\displaystyle t_ {1/2} = {\dfrac {\ln (2)} {\lambda}}[/tex]

The half-life of Gold-198 is 3 days

Initial sample amount: 100 g

duration of decay = 9 days

Then the remaining gold-198 in the sample is

[tex]\rm \large {\boxed {\bold {N_t = 100 (\dfrac {1} {2}) ^ {\frac{9}{3} }}}}\\\\N_t=\dfrac{100}{2^3}\\\\N_t=\dfrac{100}{8}\\\\N_t=\boxed{\bold{12.5~grams}}[/tex]
