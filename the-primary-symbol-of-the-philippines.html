  • 10. what is the meaning behind the flag of the philippines? The Philippine national flag has a rectangular design that consists of a white equilateral triangle which symbolizing liberty, equality and fraternity. A horizontal blue stripe symbolize peace, truth, and justice and a horizontal red stripe for patriotism and valor. In the center of the triangle is a golden-yellow sun with eight primary rays, each rays represents a Philippine province. At each vertex of the triangle is a five-pointed anf golden-yellow star, each of it represents one of the country's three main island groups which is; Luzon, Visayas (though originally referring to Panay) and Mindanao. A unique feature of this flag is its usage to indicate a state of war if it is displayed with the red side on top, which is effectively achieved by flipping the flag upside-down.

1. what is the primary symbol of the philippines?


The primary symbol of the Philippines is the Philippine flag. The flag is a rectangular piece of cloth with three horizontal bands of equal width. It is a rectangular flag with two horizontal bands of blue and red, with a white triangle at the hoist. The triangle is inscribed with a golden sun with eight rays, and in each corner of the triangle is a five-pointed star. The flag has a ratio of two to three, meaning that it is twice as long as it is wide.

The blue color of the flag symbolizes justice and equality, while the red color represents courage and bravery. The white triangle stands for purity and innocence, while the sun represents unity and freedom. The stars represent the different regions of the country. The Philippine flag was first used in 1898 during the country's revolt against Spanish colonial rule. It was designed by General Emilio Aguinaldo, who would later become the country's first president.

The Philippine flag is a very important symbol for the country and its people. It represents who they are and where they come from. The flag is flown with pride and honor by the people of the Philippines.

10. what is the meaning behind the flag of the philippines? The Philippine national flag has a rectangular design that consists of a white equilateral triangle which symbolizing liberty, equality and fraternity. A horizontal blue stripe symbolize peace, truth, and justice and a horizontal red stripe for patriotism and valor. In the center of the triangle is a golden-yellow sun with eight primary rays, each rays represents a Philippine province. At each vertex of the triangle is a five-pointed anf golden-yellow star, each of it represents one of the country's three main island groups which is; Luzon, Visayas (though originally referring to Panay) and Mindanao. A unique feature of this flag is its usage to indicate a state of war if it is displayed with the red side on top, which is effectively achieved by flipping the flag upside-down.
