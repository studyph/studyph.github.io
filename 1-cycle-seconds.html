1. What is the first stage of life cycle of a complete metamorphosis? Describe it.

Answer: The first stage is egg, a female insect lays egg.

2. What is the second stage, third and the final stage in the life cycle of the butterfly, mosquito and fly? Describe each stage.

Answer: The second stage is larvae, hatch from eggs but do not look adult insects. And the third stage is pupa, larvae make cocoon around themselves, and become busy changing into their adult forms.

3. When do we say that a certain life cycle is a complete metamorphosis?

Answer: The life cycle of an animal such as insects is considered a complete metamorphosis.

1. The complete metamorphosis starts with the laying of eggs by the female insect. The larva, which is the second stage of the complete metamorphosis, are hatched from the eggs. The larval stage can completely differ from the adult stage in morphology, behavior, and/or habitat. The larval body is soft and worm-like.

2. second stage is larva, third stage is pupa, and fin stage is adult.

3. Complete metamorphosis refers to change in anatomical and physiological form through a series of life stages. This occurs in the animal world, more specifically the insect world. This set of four stages – egg, larva, pupa, and adult – makes up the process of complete metamorphosis.

15. TRUE OR FALSE 1.) 1 hertz means 10 vibration cycle per second. 2.) Visible ray is compared to nuclei of an atom.


Answer:

1. True

One hertz simply means "one cycle per second" (typically that which is being counted is a complete cycle); 100 Hz means "one hundred cycles per second", and so on. The unit may be applied to any periodic event—for example, a clock might be said to tick at 1 Hz, or a human heart might be said to beat at 1.2 Hz.

2.False

The number of protons, neutrons, and electrons in an atom can be determined from a set of simple rules.
