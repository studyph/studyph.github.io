Sperm Cell + Egg cell   =      Zygote        →     Embryo    → Fetus

Explanation:

Sexual reproduction is a kind of reproduction wherein it requires reproductive cells, namely the sperm cell and egg cell. The union of the sperm cell and egg cell is known as fertilization. The fertilization results in a diploid zygote. This zygote will develop and grow into an embryo, then a fetus, then eventually an organism that is genetically unique from parent organisms. This is opposite of the asexual reproduction where the organism reproduces without reproductive cells or gametes. Also, the offspring produced by asexual reproduction is the exact clone of the parents.

"What's the difference between an omelet and scrambled eggs?"

is a question that every home cook who likes eggs has asked at some point.

That's probably why you're here as well. It all boils down to how the eggs are prepared and which of these two methods you — and the people for whom you cook — prefer.

While both omelets and scrambled eggs are made with whisked eggs, the cooking methods are different. Scrambled eggs are cooked slowly over medium-low heat and purposefully mangled, whereas an omelet is cooked flat over medium-high heat and properly folded.

You're basically using the same tools (a frying pan and a spatula) and the same three components (whisked eggs, cooking oil, and butter), but you're employing a different technique to achieve distinct results:

There are, of course, exceptions to the rule. Many people, for example, add cubed ham, shredded cheese, and, in some cases, sautéed mushrooms in a strip down the center of the omelet before folding it in half. This adds to the decadence of the omelet.

Whether you're making an omelet or scrambled eggs, there are a few things to keep in mind if you want the best results.

Omelette vs. scrambled eggs

Scrambled eggs are egg curds, whereas an omelet is a pile of cooked whisked eggs. Scrambled eggs are frequently the result of a failed attempt to flip an omelet or a timing error when attempting to produce a French omelet.

Scrambled eggs are still cooked eggs, but they tend to be drier than omelets since most people try to cook the eggs fully, overcooking them in the process.

Scrambled eggs can be made in a variety of ways, including creamy, little curds, huge curds, or just slicing an almost-done omelet into fragments. It's easier to create omelets if you don't try to flip them too soon or don't turn them at all.

Both of these dishes necessitate the use of fat, such as butter or cooking oil.

Continuous stirring is required while making scrambled eggs.

Making scrambled eggs results in the formation of egg curds. Those runny whisked eggs are surrounded by little particles of egg that have already started to cook. To prepare scrambled eggs, whisk, fold, and swirl every few seconds to allow the curds to form but not to the point where they solidify into an omelet.
