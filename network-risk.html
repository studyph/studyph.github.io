24. 1. What are the key considerations in maintaining computer systems and networks? Discuss the role of system updates, security protocols, and user training in ensuring effective maintenance. 2. What are the main security risks and vulnerabilities facing computer systems and networks? Discuss strategies for minimizing these risks, including user training, network segmentation, and security software. 3. Describe the steps involved in designing and implementing a backup and recovery plan for computer systems and networks. Analyze the factors that can impact the effectiveness of backup and recovery, including data storage requirements and recovery time objectives. 4. Discuss the importance of network performance optimization in maintaining effective computer systems and networks. Describe common performance issues and propose strategies for optimizing network performance, including hardware upgrades and software optimization. 5. Analyze the challenges of maintaining legacy computer systems and networks. Discuss the factors that organizations should consider when deciding whether to replace legacy systems or upgrade them.


Answer:

1. Key considerations in maintaining computer systems and networks include regular system updates, adherence to security protocols, and user training. System updates provide bug fixes, security patches, and performance enhancements. Security protocols, such as firewalls, antivirus software, and access controls, are crucial in preventing unauthorized access, data breaches, and other cyber threats. User training is also essential in ensuring that users are aware of the risks and threats facing the system, and they are following best practices to maintain system security and integrity.

2. The main security risks and vulnerabilities facing computer systems and networks include malware, phishing attacks, social engineering, and insider threats. Strategies for minimizing these risks include user training to raise awareness of these risks, network segmentation to limit access to critical systems and data, and security software such as antivirus, firewalls, and intrusion detection systems.

3. The steps involved in designing and implementing a backup and recovery plan for computer systems and networks include identifying critical data and systems, selecting appropriate backup methods and storage media, scheduling backups, testing backups, and establishing recovery procedures. Factors that can impact the effectiveness of backup and recovery include data storage requirements, recovery time objectives, and the complexity of the system.

4. Network performance optimization is crucial in maintaining effective computer systems and networks. Common performance issues include slow network speeds, network congestion, and bandwidth limitations. Strategies for optimizing network performance include upgrading hardware components such as routers, switches, and network cards, implementing software optimizations such as compression and caching, and monitoring network traffic and usage to identify and address performance bottlenecks.

5. Legacy computer systems and networks can pose challenges in terms of maintenance, security, and performance. Factors that organizations should consider when deciding whether to replace legacy systems or upgrade them include the age and condition of the systems, the availability of support and maintenance, the cost of upgrading or replacing, and the impact on business operations. It is essential to weigh the benefits and drawbacks of each option carefully and make informed decisions to ensure the system's continued effectiveness and reliability.
