2. The bond formed by Ca2+ and O2+​


Because the bond forms between a metal (Ca) and a non-metal (O), the bond will most likely be ionic. If a bond forms between two non-metals, then the bond is most likely covalent.

Ionic Compound:

Ionic Compound:The compound that is formed from two or more elements is dictated by their type of chemical bond. Two main chemical bonds, which focus on the behavior of valence electrons, are the ionic and covalent bond. Particularly, the former is defined as a transfer of valence electrons between two charged atoms, called ions. The compound formed from this bond, referred as an ionic compound, is named or written depending on the charges of the composing ions.

In the ionic bond between calcium and oxygen, the calcium atom donates two of its valence electrons to the oxygen atom to form a calcium cation and oxygen anion. Since an oxygen atom has 2 valence electrons, it can accept both valence electrons from the calcium atom leading to a net charge of 0 for the ionic compound. A 2-to-2 correspondence of valence electrons suggest that the formula of the ionic compound formed between the two ions is CaOCaO.

8. Non-polar covalent bonding involves the equal sharing of electrons between atoms to attain stability. Which of the following compounds has a non-polar covalent bond? A. HCl B. CH4 C. O2 D. NaC


Methane(CH4) makes a non-polar covalent bond. This is because the electronegativity of carbon and hydrogen is almost the same. So, they will share their electrons in a fairly even way.

Further explanation:

So, first of all, methane (CH4) isn't polar because the difference in electronegativity (EN) between its C—H bonds isn't big enough for the bond to be called polar. EN is the ability of an element to pull electrons toward it in a bond. A polar bond needs a large EN difference so that the electrons are strongly pulled toward one atom and away from the other. The partial charges of the bond are caused by the unequal distribution of the electrons, which makes the bond polar.

Carbon has an EN of 2.55 on the Pauling EN Scale, while Hydrogen has an EN of 2.20. So, the difference in ENs is only 0.35. Most people agree that for a bond to be considered polar, the difference in ENs must be at least 0.4. Since methane's bonds aren't polar, it can't be polar.

So, answer B is the right one.

People say that HCl is a polar covalent compound.People say that the bond in an O2 molecule is not polar. The oxygen molecule, O2, is held together by two covalent bonds between the atoms. So, O2 is a molecule with a covalent bond that is not polar.Since NaCl is made up of ions, it is polar.

But even if the bonds were polar, methane would still not be polar because of the way it is made up.

Here is what methane looks like in 3D: (You can see the image below)

Since all the outer atoms are the same and have the same dipoles that point in the same direction (towards the carbon atom), the molecule as a whole is no longer polar.

So, the bonds in methane are not polar, and the molecule itself is not polar. Remember that molecules can have polar bonds and still be non-polar as a whole.

16. 1.how does the electronegativity of bonding atoms determine the kind of bond formed between them? 2.why does NO require more energy to change into gas state than O2?


Answer:

1.Electronegativity is a measure of the tendency of an atom to attract electrons (or electron density) towards itself. It determines how the shared electrons are distributed between the two atoms in a bond. The more strongly an atom attracts the electrons in its bonds, the larger its electronegativity.

2.In the phase change from liquid to gas, the bonds between atoms are completely broken. The phase change from liquid to gas requires more energy because the bonds must be completely broken for it to take place, rather than just loosened as in the phase change of solid to liquid.

26. The electrolysis of water forms H2 and O2. 2H2O → 2H2 + O2 What is the % yield of O2 if 12.3 g of O2 is produced from the decomposition 14 g of H2O? Actual yield is 12.3 g of O2


Answer:

The percent yield : 99.034%

Further explanation

Percent yield is the comparison of the amount of product obtained from a reaction with the amount you calculated

General formula:

An actual yield is the amount of product actually produced by the reaction. A theoretical yield is the amount of product that you calculate from the reaction equation according to the product and reactant coefficients or the maximum amount of product that can be produced from the reactant.

Given

12.3 g O₂ (actual)

14 g H₂O

Required

The percent yield

Solution

The reaction of the decomposition of H₂O :

2H₂O⇒2H₂ + O₂

mol H₂O(MW=18 g/mol) :

= 14 g : 18 g/mol

= 0.777

From the equation, mol ratio of H₂O : O₂ = 2 : 1, so mol O₂ :

= 1/2 x mol H₂O

= 1/2 x 0.777

= 0.388

Mass O₂(MW=32 g/mol) ⇒theoretical :

= 0.388 x 32 g/mol

= 12.42 g

29. how many moles of oxygen (O2) are needed to react with ammonia in order to produce 20 grams of water? NH3 + O2 ---> N2 + H20​


Answer:

0.55 mol

Explanation:

Step 1: Balance the chemical equation.

4NH3 + 3O2 → 2N2 + 6H2O

Step 2: Perform stoichiometric calculation.

n O2 = 20 g H2O × (1 mol H2O / 18.02 g H2O) × (3 mol O2 / 6 mol H2O)

n O2 = 0.55 mol
