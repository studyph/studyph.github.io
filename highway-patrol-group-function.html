16. A group of 60 students were asked whether they watch TV Patrol or 24 Oras or both programs (depending on the news). Thirty- eight of these students watch TV Patrol and 32 watch 24 Oras . a.How many watch TV Patrol only? b.How many watch 24 Oras only? c.How many watch both news programs?​


Answers:

28 students watch ONLY TV Patrol

22 students watch ONLY 24 Oras

10 students watch Both

Step-by-step explanation:

Use a 2 circle Venn diagram like the photo attached in this answer

For the part where Elements in A only is written, we will write the number of students that ONLY watch TV patrol.

To solve: let x be the students that only watch TV Patrol

Since 32 students watch 24 Oras, we will subtract 60 by 32

  x =  60 - 32

   x = 28 Students

For the part where Elements in B only is written, we will write the number of students that ONLY watch 24 Oras.

To solve: let y be the students that only watch 24 Oras.

Since 38 students watch TV Patrol, we will subtract 60 by 38

  y =  60 - 38

  y = 22 Students

For the part where Elements in A and B only is written, we will write the number of students that watch both programs.

To solve: let z be the students that watch both programs.

   z =  60 - 28 - 22

 z = 10 Students

a) On the part where Elements in A only is written, write 28

b) On the part where Elements in B only is written, write 22

c) On the part where Elements in A and B only is written, write 10
