6. ASAP. Saturated liquid water undergoes an isothermal process from 95°F to 62 psi. Find the heat added to the steam in kJ/kg​


Answer:

To find the heat added to saturated liquid water during an isothermal process, we can use the following formula:

Q = m * h

where Q is the heat added, m is the mass of the substance, and h is the specific enthalpy of the substance. For saturated liquid water, the specific enthalpy can be found using a steam table.

At a temperature of 95°F (35°C), the saturation pressure of water is approximately 0.2396 psi. At a pressure of 62 psi, the saturation temperature of water is approximately 294.6°F (146.9°C). Therefore, the water undergoes an isothermal process at a temperature of 294.6°F.

Using a steam table, we can find the specific enthalpy of saturated liquid water at a pressure of 62 psi to be approximately 316.9 kJ/kg.

Assuming a mass of 1 kg for the water, the heat added is:

Q = m * h

= 1 kg * 316.9 kJ/kg

= 316.9 kJ/kg

Therefore, the heat added to the saturated liquid water during the isothermal process from 95°F to 62 psi is approximately 316.9 kJ/kg.

12. During an isothermal compression of an ideal gas, 410 J of heat must be removed from the gas to maintain a constant temperature. How much work is done by the gas during the process?


Answer:

Work done = -410J

Explanation:

For an isothermal process, the temperature is constant and thus the heat transfer into and out of the system happens slowly so that the system is in thermal equilibrium.

Also, from the first law of thermodynamics, the change in internal energy (ΔU) of a closed system is equal to the sum of the heat transferred (Q) and the work done (W) by the system. i.e

ΔU = W + Q         —————(i)

The internal energy is dependent on the temperature of the system and since this temperature is constant the change in internal energy is zero (0).

i.e

ΔU = 0

Substitute this value into equation (i) as follows;

⇒ 0 = W + Q

⇒ W = -Q              ————–(ii)

Therefore, for such process, the work done is the negative of the heat transferred (heat removed in this case).

Given

Q = 410J

Substitute into equation (ii)

W = -410J

Therefore, the work done is -410 J

The value is negative to show that the process is a compression meaning that work is done by the surrounding on the gas.

14. a 65.0 l gas kept in a movable piston has an initial pressure of 4.5 atm at 27°c. if the piston is allowed to expand isothermally to 95.0 l ,what will be the resulting pressure of gas?​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_1 & = \text{4.5 atm} \\ V_1 & = \text{65.0 L} \\ V_2 & = \text{95.0 L} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final pressure by using Boyle's law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_2 & = \frac{P_1V_1}{V_2} \\ & = \frac{(\text{4.5 atm})(\text{65.0 L})}{\text{95.0 L}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{3.1 atm}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the resulting pressure of the gas is 3.1 atm.
