28. a train accelerates from 10 km/h to 40km/h to 60km/h in 30 seconds. Find its acceleration​


Converting the velocity from km/h to m/s.

v in m/s = (v in km/h / 3.6)

v in m/s = (10 / 3.6)

= 2.78 m/s

(40 / 3.6)

= 11.11 m/s

(60 / 3.6)

= 16.67 m/s

Calculating the acceleration.

Given

Changes in velocity (Δv) = 2.78 m/s → 11.11 m/s → 16.67 m/sTime (t) = 30 sAcceleration (a) = ?

a = Δv / t

a = 16.67 m/s - 11.11 m/s - 2.78 m/s / 30 s

a = 2.78 m/s / 30 s

a = 0.09 m/s²

30. average speedA man drives at 40km/h for 3hours and at 50km/h for 2hours.What is the average speed for the journey?​


Solution:

The formula used for the average speed is

average speed = total distance / total time

Solving for the average speed

average speed = total distance / total time

average speed = [(40 km/h)(3 h) + (50 km/h)(2 h)] / (3 h + 2 h)

average speed = (120 km + 100 km) / (5 h)

average speed = 220 km / 5 h

average speed = 44 km/h

Therefore, the average speed for the journey is 44 km/h.
