2. A seven tons are lifted 12 ft. Find the energy required in Btu to lift the 7 tons. a. 210 Btu b. 217 Btu C. 216 Btu d. 200 Btu ​


The amount of energy required in BTU to lift 7 tons at a distance of 12 feet is 216 BTU.

What we are going to do here is to convert the work done in Joules into BTU (British Thermal Unit). For us to get Joules, we have to convert the units of the given. Since N x m = J, we have to get the values in meters and Newton.

Converting 12 ft to meters:

Note: In 1 foot there are 0.3048 meters.

12 ft x [tex]\frac{0.3048 m}{1 ft}[/tex] = 3.6576 meters

Converting tons to Newton:

First, let us convert tons to kilograms.

Note: There are 907.185 kilograms in one ton.

7 tons x [tex]\frac{1000 kg}{1 ton}[/tex] = 6350.3 kg

Then, we have to convert kilograms into Newton. The simplest way to do this is to multiply it by the constant value of acceleration due to gravity: 9.81 m/[tex]s^{2}[/tex] because 1kg= 9.81 N

6350.3 kg x 9.81 m/[tex]s^{2}[/tex] = 62,296.39 kg-m/[tex]s^{2}[/tex]  = 62,296.39 N

Now, let us apply the work formula: W= Fd

W= 62,296.39 N (3.6576 meters)= 227,855.29 J

Finally, let's convert Joules into BTU.

1 J=0.000948 BTU

227,855.29 J x [tex]\frac{0.000948 BTU}{1J}[/tex] = 216 BTU

5. 28. A 2kg object is moving at 6m/s. what is the kinetic energy of the object?A. 12 JoulesB. 36 JoulesC. 18 JoulesD. 6 Joules​


PROBLEM:

A 2kg object is moving at 6m/s. What is the kinetic energy of the object?

A. 12 Joules

B. 36 Joules

C. 18 Joules

D. 6 Joules

FORMULA:

[tex]KE = \frac{1}{2} mv^{2} [/tex]

where:

KE = kinetic energy

m = mass

v = velocity

SOLUTION:

Substitute the given velocity and mass, simplify the following.

[tex]KE = \frac{1}{2} mv^{2} \\ \\ KE = \frac{1}{2} (2kg)(6m/s {)}^{2} \\ \\ KE = \frac{1}{2} (2kg)(36m/s {)} \\ \\ KE = \frac{2}{2} kg(36m/s) \\ \\ KE = 1kg(36m/s) \\ \\ KE = 36 \: J[/tex]

ANSWER:

The kinetic energy is 36 Joules.

Letter B is the answer.

28. what is the potential of the 30kg object at the height of 30 meters? a. 900 joules b. 450 joules c. 3550 joules d. 2970 joules​


Solution:

The potential energy of an object is

PE = mgh

where:

PE = potential energy

m = mass

g = acceleration due to gravity

g = 9.81 m/s² ≈ 10 m/s²

h = height

Listing the values

m = 30 kg

h = 3 m

Solving for PE

PE = mgh

PE = (30 kg)(10 m/s²)(3 m)

PE = 900 J

Answer:

A. 900 joules

Given:

v = 10 m/s

Step 2: Identify what is being asked.

Required:

Kinetic Energy

Solution:

The formula for Kinetic Energy is given by:

m - mass
