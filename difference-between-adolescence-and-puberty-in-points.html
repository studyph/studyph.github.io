Answer:

What is the difference between puberty and adolescence stage?

Puberty is the time in which a child's sexual and physical characteristics mature. It occurs due to hormone changes. Adolescence is the period between puberty and adulthood.

What are the characteristics of puberty

But these are average ages when puberty changes may happen:

Penis enlargement. ...

Pubic hair appears. ...

Adolescence stage?

Adolescence is the period of transition between childhood and adulthood. Children who are entering adolescence are going through many changes (physical, intellectual, personality and social developmental). Adolescence begins at puberty, which now occurs earlier, on average, than in the past.

Go through less conflict with parents.

Show more independence from parents.

Have a deeper capacity for caring and sharing and for developing more intimate relationships.

Spend less time with parents and more time with friends.

Adolescent development

The development of children ages 12 through 18 years old should include expected physical and mental milestones.

Information

During adolescence, children develop the ability to:

Understand abstract ideas. These include grasping higher math concepts, and developing moral philosophies, including rights and privileges.

Establish and maintain satisfying relationships. Adolescents will learn to share intimacy without feeling worried or inhibited.
