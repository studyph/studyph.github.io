3. Celsius and Fahrenheit temperatures are related by F=9/5C + 32. What is the Celsius temperature when the Fahrenheit temperature is -40°?​


Answer:

-40°C

Step-by-step explanation:

Celsius = (Fahrenheit - 32) x 5/9

= (-40 - 32) x 5/9

= -72 x 5/9

= -8 x 5

= -40°C

27. among the given temperatures, where will sound travel the slowest?10 degree Celsius20 degree Celsius30 degree Celsius40 degree Celsius​


Answer:

10 degrees Celcius

Explanation:

The speed of sound decreases as the temperature decreases.

"In fact sound travels faster when the air is hotter." (Strong, 2019)

Answer:

10 degree Celcius

The higher the temperature, the more the gas particles wiggle and bounce. It takes less time for particles that are already moving quickly to push against the particles around them than it takes particles that are moving slowly. Sound, therefore, travels faster in high temperature than in low temperature.
