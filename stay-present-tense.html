12. B. LET'S DO MORE Directions: Rewrite the sentences from present progressive tense to past progressive tense.1. I am answering the test.2. We are staying with my brother. 3. They are joining the contest. 4. The president is speaking in front. 5. The teachers are reviewing the modules.​


Answer:

1. I am answering the test.

Answer: I WAS answering the test.

2. We are staying with my brother.

Answer: We WERE staying with my brother.

3. They are joining the contest.

Answer: They WERE joining the contest.

4. The president is speaking in front.

Answer: The president WAS speaking in front.

5. The teachers are reviewing the modules.​

Answer: The teachers WERE reviewing the modules.​

Explanation:

The PAST PROGRESSIVE TENSE indicates continuing action, something that was happening, going on, at some point in the past. This tense is formed with the helping "to be" verb, in the past tense, plus the present participle of the verb (with an -ing ending): I was riding my bike all day yesterday.

The present progressive tense (sometimes called the present continuous) is a tense which describes an action which began in the past and is still going on now. ... Any sentence that uses 'is' or 'are' and then a verb with the '–ing' suffix is present progressive tense.
