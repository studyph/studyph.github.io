10. What are your practices which breaks your accountability as a student? ​


Answer:

As a student, I recognize that accountability is crucial to maintaining academic integrity and ethical behavior. Some practices that could break my accountability as a student include:

Plagiarism: Using someone else's work without giving proper credit or citation.

Cheating: Copying answers or using unauthorized materials during an exam.

Falsification: Fabricating data or results to support a research project.

Misrepresentation: Misrepresenting oneself, such as claiming to have completed an assignment or attended a class when one has not.

Inappropriate collaboration: Working with others on an individual assignment that is supposed to be done alone.

Failure to meet deadlines: Not submitting work on time, or without sufficient effort and preparation.

Not respecting academic standards: Ignoring ethical and academic standards in the pursuit of success or recognition.

30. demonstrate accountabilities and responsibilities in the practice of child's rights​


Answer:

Accountabilities and responsibilities in the practice of child's rights involve several key stakeholders:

Government: responsible for creating laws, policies and programs that promote and protect child's rights, and ensuring that they are adequately implemented and monitored.

Parents/Caregivers: responsible for providing a safe and supportive environment, meeting the basic needs of children, and allowing them to exercise their rights.

Schools/Educational Institutions: responsible for providing a safe and inclusive learning environment, promoting child's rights and well-being, and educating children about their rights.

Community: responsible for creating a supportive and inclusive community that recognizes and respects child's rights and provides opportunities for children to participate and express themselves.

Media: responsible for providing accurate, non-violent and non-discriminatory information to children, and promoting positive images of children and their rights.

International Organizations: responsible for promoting and protecting child's rights globally, and providing support to countries in their efforts to fulfill their obligations.

It's important for all these stakeholders to work together to ensure that children's rights are respected, protected, and fulfilled.

Accountabilities and responsibilities in the practice of children's rights are shared among various individuals and organizations, including:

Governments: Governments have a primary responsibility to respect, protect, and fulfill children's rights. They must ensure that national laws and policies reflect the principles of the Convention on the Rights of the Child (CRC) and other international human rights instruments.

Parents/Caregivers: Parents and caregivers have a responsibility to provide children with a safe and nurturing environment, and to respect their rights, including their right to express their views and participate in decisions that affect their lives.

Educators: Teachers and other education professionals have a responsibility to promote children's rights, and to create safe and inclusive learning environments where children can grow, learn, and develop to their full potential.

Service providers: Service providers, including health care providers, social workers, and child protection agencies, have a responsibility to respect and protect children's rights, and to provide services that meet their needs and support their well-being.

Civil society organizations: Civil society organizations, including NGOs and community-based organizations, play an important role in advocating for children's rights, raising awareness about children's issues, and supporting children and families in need.

All individuals and organizations involved in the practice of children's rights have a responsibility to be accountable for their actions, and to ensure that the rights of children are respected and protected at all times
