1. Explain the differences in boiling point in terms of intermolecular forces (IMF). 1. HCI (-85 °C) and HBr (-66 °C) 2. Cl2 (-34 °C) and Br2 (59 °C)​


Answer:

Both of these molecules experience dipole-dipole forces, but since the bromine atom (Br) is much bigger than the chlorine atom (Cl), HBr is, therefore, the larger, more polarizable molecule compared to HCl. This makes the IMFs in HBr stronger than that those in HCl, making HBr have a higher boiling point because stronger dipole-dipole forces = higher boiling point.The same can be said here. Br2 molecule is larger and is thus more polarizable than the Cl2 one. This makes the former have stronger dispersion forces (take note that these molecules are nonpolar due to two atoms of the same identity bonded to one another which then means that they experience dispersion forces instead of dipole-dipole). Stronger dispersion forces = higher boiling point.

16. 1.25 mole bromine gas, Br2 occupies a volume of 20.6L at 35.0°C. Determine the pressure of Br2 at these conditions


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & V = \text{20.6 L} \\ & n = \text{1.25 mol} \\ & T = 35.0^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{308.15 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Solve for the pressure of the gas by using ideal gas equation.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P & = \frac{nRT}{V} \\ & = \frac{(\text{1.25 mol})\left(0.082057 \: \dfrac{\text{L}\cdot\text{atm}}{\text{mol}\cdot\text{K}}\right)(\text{308.15 K})}{\text{20.6 L}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{1.53 atm}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the pressure of bromine gas is 1.53 atm.

21. in the rate law k[NO]2[Br2], the reaction is first order for NO and second order for Br2. True False


Answer:

False

Explanation:

The rate law k[NO]2[Br2] indicates that the reaction is second order with respect to NO and first order with respect to Br2, not the other way around.

This means that if the concentration of NO is doubled (while keeping the concentration of Br2 constant), the rate of the reaction will increase by a factor of 2 squared (i.e., 4). If the concentration of Br2 is doubled (while keeping the concentration of NO constant), the rate of the reaction will increase by a factor of 2 (since it is a first-order reaction with respect to Br2).
