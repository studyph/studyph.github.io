11. A spring (k = 200 N/m) is suspended with its upper endsupported from a ceiling. With the spring hanging in itsequilibrium configuration. an object (mass = 2.0 kg) is attachedto the lower end and released from rest. What is the speed of theobject after it has fallen 4.0 cm?​


Answer:

The speed of the object is 0.57 m/s.

Explanation:

Formula to be use

1. Hooke's law formula for spring

F = kx                    equation 1  

where

F     is the force, unit is in Newtons (N)

k     is the force or spring constant, unit is in Newton per meter (N/m)

x     is the distance of elongation or compression of the spring

2. 2nd Newton's law of motion

   F = ma                 equation 2

where

F     is the force

m    is the mass, unit is in kg

a     is the acceleration, unit is in m/[tex]s^2[/tex]

3. Constant acceleration formula

   [tex]v_{2} ^2=v_{1} ^2+2ax[/tex]       equation 3

where

[tex]v_{2}[/tex]     is the final velocity, unit is in m/s

[tex]v_{1}[/tex]     is the initial velocity, unit is in m/s

[tex]a[/tex]      is the acceleration

[tex]x[/tex]      is the distance traveled when an object was attached at

       other end of the spring or the elongation of the spring        

For the given information

k = 200 N/m

m = 2.0 kg

x = 4.0 cm

Solving the problem

1. Convert 4 cm ⇒ meter

x = 4.0 cm x [tex]\frac{1m}{100cm}[/tex] = 0.04 m

2. Use Hooke's law formula to solve for force

F = kx

F = (200 N/m)(0.04 m)

F = 8 N

3. Use the formula for 2nd Newton's law of motion and constant acceleration equation

a. Using equation 2, solve for the acceleration

   F = ma

Substitute the given information

  8 N = (2kg) a

   a = [tex]\frac{8N}{2kg}[/tex]

   a = 4 m/[tex]s^2[/tex]

b. Using equation 3, solve for the speed of the object

   [tex]v_{2} ^2=v_{1} ^2+2ax[/tex]

Substitute the given information

[tex]v_{2} ^2=0+2(4 m/s^2)(0.04m)[/tex]      [tex]v_{1}[/tex] (initial speed) is zero because the

                                             object was released from rest,

                                             meaning zero (0) to a

                                             its final velocity, [tex]v_{2}[/tex] (which we are

                                             looking for)                                          

[tex]v_{2} ^2=0.32[/tex]

[tex]v_{2} =\sqrt{0.32} =0.57 m/s[/tex]

Therefore, the speed of the 2 kg object hanged from one end of a spring is 0.57 m/s.
