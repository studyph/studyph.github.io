1. Calcium hydride reacts with water to form calcium hy-droxide and hydrogen gas. (a) Write a balanced chemical equation for the reaction. (b) How many grams of calcium hydride are needed to form 4.500 g of hydrogen?​


(a) ANSWER:

CaH₂ + 2 H₂O → Ca(OH)₂ + 2 H₂

(b) SOLUTION:

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 1 mole of CaH₂ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 2 moles of H₂.

The molar masses of CaH₂ and H₂ are 42.094 g and 2.016 g, respectively.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{CaH}_2 & = \text{4.500 g} \: \text{H}_2 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{H}_2}{\text{2.016 g} \: \text{H}_2} \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{CaH}_2}{\text{2 mol} \: \text{H}_2} \times \frac{\text{42.094 g} \: \text{CaH}_2}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{CaH}_2} \\ & = \boxed{\text{46.98 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, 46.98 g of CaH₂ are needed to from 4.500 g of H₂.

The naming of chemical compounds is giving a special name to a compound that aims to facilitate the classification of compounds and facilitate the identification of a compound.

This nomenclature includes the names of ion compounds, covalent compounds, and polyatomics

For ionic compounds consisting of cations (ions +) and anions (ions -)

The naming is preceded by the cation then the anion with the -ide suffix added

Generally for this ion compound consists of metal elements as cations and non-metals as anions

1. For metal and non-metal compounds

Metal: cation, positively charged.

Nonmetal: negative anion.

The anion cation's charge is crossed

Example:

Fe₂S₃

Fe charge = +3

S charge = -2

The name: metal + non-metal + -ide

Example: BaCl₂ -> Barium chloride

For transition metals, which have several oxidation charges / numbers, write the oxidation number in roman numbers

Example: SnO: lead (II) oxide

SnO₂: lead (IV) oxide

2. For non-metal and non-metal compounds

The naming: non-metal + non-metal + suffix -ide

In the elements given the prefix mono, di, tri, tetra, penta if more than one element is formed

Example:

SO₂: Sulfur dioxide

SO₃: Sulfur trioxide

Ca²⁺ + Cl⁻ ⇒ CaCl₂

23. Calcium Hydride reacts with water to yield H2(g) and Ca(OH)2(s) . How many grams of of CaH2(s) is required to prepare 3.00 L of H2(g) at STP?​


[tex]CaH_{2 \ (g)} + 2H_2O_{(l)} \rightarrow 2H_{2 \ (g)}+Ca(OH)_{2 \ (g)}[/tex]

First you have to find the number of moles to use the balanced equation. Get the number of moles of 3 L of hydrogen gas at STP.

[tex]PV=nRT \\ @STP: P=1 \ atm \ ; T=273K \\

(1 \ atm)(3 \ L)=n\cdot \left(0.0821 \frac{L-atm}{mol-K}\right) (273 \ K) \\

n=0.1338 \ mol \ H_2[/tex]

Use the balanced equation to calculate for the moles of the reactant and use the molar mass to calculate for the mass.

[tex]0.1338 \ mol \ H_2 \times \frac{1 \ mol \ CaH_2}{2 \ mol \ H_2}\times \frac{42.094 \ g \ CaH_2}{1 \ mol \ CaH_2} = 2.8171 \ g \ CaH2[/tex]

27. Calcium hydride reacts with water to form hydrogen gas: How many grams of are needed to generate 48.0 L of gas at a pressure of 0.888 atm and a temperature of 32 °C?


Answer:

mass CaH₂ = 35.8 g

Explanation:

Step 1: Writing the balanced chemical equation

CaH₂ + 2H₂O → Ca(OH)₂ + 2H₂

Step 2: Converting °C to K

T = 32°C + 273.15 = 305.15 K

Step 3: Calculating the number of moles of H₂

n H₂ = PV / RT

n H₂ = (0.888 atm)(48.0 L) / (0.082057 L•atm/mol•K)(305.15 K)

n H₂ = 1.7023 mol

Step 4: Calculating the mass of CaH₂ needed

mass CaH₂ = 1.7023 mol H₂ × (1 mol CaH₂ / 2 mol H₂) × (42.094 g CaH₂ / 1 mol CaH₂)

mass CaH₂ = 35.8 g
