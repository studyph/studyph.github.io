12. helium gas has a volume of 250 ml at 0°c at 1.0 atm


#BrainlyFast

Complete statement of the problem:

Helium gas has a volume of 250 mL at 0°C at 1.0 atm. What will be

the final pressure if the volume is reduced to 100 mL at 45°C?

Given data:

V₁ (initial volume) = 250 ml

T₁ (initial temperature) = 0 + 273 = 273 K

P₁ (Initial pressure) = 1 atm

V₂ (final volume) = 250 ml

T₂ (Final temperature) = 45 + 273 = 318 K

P₂ (Final pressure) = ? unknown

Solution:

We will use the formula of combined gas law to solve the problem:

P₂ = V₁P₁T₂ / V₂T₁

   = (250mL) (1.0atm.)(318 K ) / (100. mL)  (273 K)

   =  79,500 atm / 27,300

   = 2.9 atm.

Answer:

To make a 250.0 g solution that is 5.0% water, you need to determine how much of the solution is water. You can do this by multiplying the mass of the solution by the concentration of the water in the solution:

water mass = 250.0 g * 5.0% = 12.5 g

Now that you know the mass of the water, you can subtract it from the total mass of the solution to find the mass of the other component, in this case, salt:

salt mass = 250.0 g - 12.5 g = 237.5 g

So, to make a 250.0 g solution that is 5.0% water, you need 12.5 g of water and 237.5 g of salt.
