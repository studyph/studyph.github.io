14. BROKEN FAMILY EXPLAIN?​


Answer:

Broken family actually means the family tears apart. Technically, I agree.

We call it broken family when one parent is not present usually due to family issues, divorce, or desertion: children from broken homes.

Be careful though. "Broken Family" is not an accepted term anymore these days.

There are two specific terms to address it:

1. Family Estrangement

2. Dysfunctional Family

Family estrangement (or, simply, estrangement) is loss of previous relationship between family members. This happened via physical or emotional distancing which is often to the extent that there's no communication at all between the the members of family in a prolonged period.

This estrangement may result from direct interactions between those affected such as traumatic experiences, family violence, abuse, neglect, parental misbehavior that includes repetitive explosive outbursts, intense marital conflict, attachment disorders, differing values and beliefs, disappointment, major life events or change, or poor communication.

The estrangement is often unwanted, or considered unsatisfactory, by at least one party involved. Then again, yes, I agree that it creates tension and tears family apart.

However, a dysfunctional family is in conflict, misbehavior of member, and there are child neglect/abuses.  Children nowadays sometimes grow up in dysfunctional families with understanding that such arrangement is normal and typical.
