3. The characteristic of the main character in the novel of Les Miserables


Jean Valjean

Cosette’s adopted father. Valjean is an ex-convict who leaves behind a life of hatred and deceit and makes his fortune with his innovative industrial techniques. He finds fulfillment in loving his adopted daughter and helping people who are in difficult situations, even when it means risking his own life and welfare. Valjean adopts pseudonyms to evade the police and combines a convict’s street smarts with his newfound idealism and compassion. His whole life is a quest for redemption, and he ultimately finds bliss on his deathbed.

Cosette

Fantine’s daughter, who lives as Valjean’s adopted daughter after her mother dies. Cosette spends her childhood as a servant for the Thénardiers in Montfermeil, but even this awful experience does not make her hardened or cynical. Under the care of Valjean and the nuns of Petit-Picpus, Cosette ultimately blossoms into a beautiful, educated young woman. She finds fulfillment in her love for Marius. Cosette is innocent and docile, but her participation in Valjean’s many escapes from the law show that she also possesses intelligence and bravery.

Javert

A police inspector who strictly believes in law and order and will stop at nothing to enforce France’s harsh penal codes. Javert is incapable of compassion or pity, and performs his work with such passion that he takes on a nearly animal quality when he is on the chase. He nurses an especially strong desire to recapture Valjean, whose escapes and prosperity he sees as an affront to justice. Ultimately, Javert is unable to say with certainty that Valjean deserves to be punished. This ambiguity undermines the system of belief on which Javert bases his life and forces him to choose between hypocrisy and honor.

12. Who are the dynamic and static characters in the story Les miserables


DYNAMIC VS STATIC

Dynamic characters undergo changes throughout the story, while static characters do not change regardless of what happened to them.

Dynamic Characters in Les Miserables:

1. Jean Valjean- He is the main protagonist of the book. When he had a chance to talk to a bishop, he realized that he should not act as if he were still a criminal. From being a theif, then a criminal, he became a wealthy man who used his riches to help other people.

2. Inspector Javert- He was a true man of the law because he was very stict with it. He was saved by Jean Valjean so he contemplated his life. He had a change of heart about it before he died.

3. Marius- Marius was a well-off and dependent boy, but when he left his grandfather, he became an independent man.

4. Cosette- Cosette had no self-esteem. She was poor and the Thenardiers made sure that she was miserable. But when Jean Valjean took her in as his own, she became a better version of herself.  

5. Eponine Thenardier- Eponine is the daughter of the people who abused Cosette. She was rude and a spoiled brat too. She was selfish and even tried to end the relationship of Cosette and Marius. And because falling in love with Marius influenced most of her decisions, she redeemed herself and became selfless when she took the bullet which was originally for Marius

Static Characters in Les Miserables:

1. Fantine- Fantine was the most miserable character in the story. She was introduced in the book as the poor woman. She got sick and she died remaining poor and miserable.  

2.Monsieur and Madame Thenardier- The parents of Eponine and Azelma, they were those who abused Cosette in the past. They used to own an inn. There they would cheat on their customers. Even after they got bankrupt, they would still steal from others. They were abusive and brutal 'til the end.
