13. Elvin bowled 7 games last weekend. His scores are: 155, 165, 138, 172, 127, 193, 142. What is the range of Ervin's scores? a. 193 b. 127 c.60 d. 66​


The range of Ervin's scores is 66.

Explanation:

The range is the largest data value and the smallest data value. The range is used to calculate the highest value and the smallest value in the data set. Therefore, the formula to find the range is:

[tex]R = X_{max} - X_{min}[/tex]

where R is the range, [tex]X_{max}[/tex] is the largest data value, and [tex]X_{min}[/tex] is the smallest data value.

The range is closely related to the spread of data. The main function of using a range is to find a range in data. In addition, with the calculation of the range, we can measure data.

The range has several data to be measured, either single data or group data. Measuring the range of single data is easier than measuring group data.

The range can be further divided into two types, namely:

1. Inter-quartile range (H)

It is the difference between the upper and lower quartile, or the inter-quartile range called the overlay denoted by (H).

2. Semi-inter-quartile range (quartile deviation)

Is half of the stretch or commonly called the quartile deviation. The semi-inter-quartile range is denoted by Qd.

Ways to find a range include:

Arrange a set of numbers from smallest to largest.Identify the smallest and largest numbers in the data set.Subtract the smallest number from the largest.Write down the range results.

Given:

Ervin bowled 7 games last weekend.

His scores are: 155, 165, 138, 172, 127, 193, 142.

To Find:

What is the range of Ervin's scores?

Solution:

We can solve the question as follows:

It is given that we have to find the range of Ervin's scores from his scores:

Ervin’s score: 127, 138, 142, 155, 165, 172, 193

The range of a given set of observations is equal to the difference between the largest data and smallest data.

[tex]R = X_{max} - X_{min}[/tex]

In the given data,

[tex]X_{max}[/tex] = 193

[tex]X_{min}[/tex] = 127

Therefore,

[tex]R = X_{max} - X_{min}[/tex]

= 193 - 127

= 66

Hence, the range of Ervin's scores is 66.
