15. A rectangular lot was measured using a 50 m steel tape which was found to a 0.025 m too short. if the recorded length and width of the lot are 180.455 m and 127.062m, respectively, determine the actual dimensions of the lot.


To determine the actual dimensions considering that the steel tape was too short, we only need to determine how many times the 50m steel tape was used at its full length to measure the lot.

Length

The length is 180.455m. This means that the 50m steel tape was used at full length 3 times to measure up to 150m. The remaining measurement after 150m is 30.455m and is not affected by the shortness of the steel tape.

Therefore, the length was affected by the short steel tape 3 times or by 3 x 0.025m = 0.075 m. The correct length is 180.455 m - 0.075m = 180.38 m.

Width

The width is 127.062m. This means that the 50m steel tape was used at full length 2 times to measure up to 100m. The remaining measurement after 100m is 27.062m and is not affected by the shortness of the steel tape.

Therefore, the width was affected by the short steel tape 2 times or by 2 x 0.025m = 0.05 m. The correct width is 127.062 m - 0.05m = 127.012 m.
