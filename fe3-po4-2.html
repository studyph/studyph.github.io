2. If 23 grams of iron(II) chloride reacts with 41 grams of sodium phosphate, what is the limiting reactant? How much sodium chloride can be formed?Balanced Eqn: 3FeCl2 + 2 Na3PO4= Fe3(PO4) +6NaCl​


Given:

mass of FeCl₂ = 23 g

mass of Na₃PO₄ = 41 g

balanced chemical equation: 3FeCl₂ + 2Na₃PO₄ → Fe₃(PO₄)₂ + 6NaCl

Required:

limiting reactant

mass of NaCl

Solution: (limiting reactant)

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of each reactant.

For FeCl₂

molar mass of FeCl₂ = (55.85 g/mol × 1) + (35.45 g/mol × 2)

molar mass of FeCl₂ = 126.75 g/mol

For Na₃PO₄

molar mass of Na₃PO₄ = (22.99 g/mol × 3) + (30.97 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 4)

molar mass of Na₃PO₄ = 163.94 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of NaCl produced by each reactant.

Using FeCl₂

Since [tex]\text{3 mol FeCl}_{2} \bumpeq \text{6 mol NaCl}[/tex], the number of moles of NaCl formed is

[tex]\text{moles of NaCl} = \text{23 g FeCl}_{2} \times \frac{\text{1 mol FeCl}_{2}}{\text{126.75 g FeCl}_{2}} \times \frac{\text{6 mol NaCl}}{\text{3 mol FeCl}_{2}}[/tex]

moles of NaCl = 0.3629191 mol

Using Na₃PO₄

Since [tex]\text{2 mol Na}_{3}\text{PO}_{4} \bumpeq \text{6 mol NaCl}[/tex], the number of moles of NaCl formed is

[tex]\text{moles of NaCl} = \text{41 g Na}_{3}\text{PO}_{4} \times \frac{\text{1 mol Na}_{3}\text{PO}_{4}}{\text{163.94 g Na}_{3}\text{PO}_{4}} \times \frac{\text{6 mol NaCl}}{\text{2 mol Na}_{3}\text{PO}_{4}}[/tex]

moles of NaCl = 0.750 mol

Step 3: Determine the limiting reactant.

Since FeCl₂ produces less number of moles of NaCl

[tex]\boxed{\text{FeCl}_{2} \: \text{is the limiting reactant.}}[/tex]

Solution: (mass of NaCl)

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of NaCl.

molar mass of NaCl = (22.99 g/mol × 1) + (35.45 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of NaCl = 58.44 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the mass of NaCl formed.

Since FeCl₂ is the limiting reactant, the mass of NaCl formed is

[tex]\text{mass of NaCl} = \text{0.3629191 mol NaCl} \times \frac{\text{58.44 g NaCl}}{\text{1 mol NaCl}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of NaCl = 21 g}}[/tex]

11. What percent composition of phosphorus is in Zn3(PO4)2?​


The percent composition of phosphorus in Zn₃(PO₄)₂ : 16.04%

Further explanation  

Proust states the Comparative Law that compounds are formed from elements with the same Mass Comparison so that compounds have a constant composition of elements  

The empirical formula is the mole ratio of compounds forming elements  

In the same compound, although from different sources and formed by different processes, it will still have the same composition/comparison  

[tex]\tt \%~A~in~A_xB_y=\dfrac{x.Ar~A}{MW~A_xB_y}\times 100\%[/tex]

Given

Compound Zn₃(PO₄)₂

Required

The percent composition of phosphorus

Solution

Molar mass Zn₃(PO₄)₂ = 386.1 g/mol

Molar mass P = 30.97 g/mol

There are 2 P atoms in the Zn₃(PO₄)₂ compound, so :

%P in Zn₃(PO₄)₂ :

= (2 x 30,97)/ 386.1 g/mol x 100%

=  16.04%

16. Molar mass of Mg33(PO4) 2​


Answer:

To calculate the molar mass of Mg33(PO4)2, we need to add the atomic masses of each element present in the compound, multiplied by their respective subscripts.

Mg33(PO4)2 can be broken down into:

33 magnesium (Mg) atoms

2 phosphate (PO4) groups

To find the atomic masses of the elements, we can refer to the periodic table.

The atomic mass of Mg is 24.31 g/mol.

The atomic mass of P is 30.97 g/mol, and there are 2 P atoms in each phosphate group.

The atomic mass of O is 15.99 g/mol, and there are 8 O atoms in each phosphate group.

Now we can calculate the molar mass:

Mg33(PO4)2 = (33 x 24.31 g/mol) + (2 x (2 x 30.97 g/mol + 8 x 15.99 g/mol))

= 801.56 g/mol

Therefore, the molar mass of Mg33(PO4)2 is approximately 801.56 g/mol.
