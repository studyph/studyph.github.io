Answer:

Quarry Tile

Explanation:

Quarry tile is a hard, impervious paving tile made from the ground minerals formed and fired in much the same way as is used to make brick. They are typically harder than ordinary clay bricks, due to the minerals used and the high firing temperatures thus it doesn't have a surface glaze layer. Red most closely approximates the color of the natural clays which comprise many quarry tiles.

17. 1) ln rabbits black coat (B) is dominant over brown (b)and straight hair (H) is dominant to curly (h). Cross 2hybrid rabbits and give the phenotypic ratio for thefirst possible generation of offepring.​


Answer:

9:3:3:1 for black coat straight hair : black coat curly hair : brown coat straight hair : brown coat curly hair.

Explanation:

A hybrid rabbit would have a genotype of BbHh. Crossing 2 hybrids together would mean solving for the the first generation offspring of BbHh x BbHh. To illustrate this we need to make a 4x4 Punnett Square in which all possible gene combinations for each parent is listed against a row or column. To list these down, remember FOIL, wherein for 2 pairs of alleles (Bb x Hh), we simply combine the Firsts (BH), the Outers (Bh), the Inners (bH), and the Lasts (bh). Do this for both parents and list them down, each parent on a row or column. Then, for each of the 16 cells, we fill out the result of crossing the corresponding row with the column. We thus have a Punnett Square that looks like this:

           BH       Bh        bH         bh

BH    | BBHH  BBHh   BbHH   BbHh

Bh     | BBHh   BBhh   BbHh    Bbhh

bH   |  BbHH   BbHh  bbHH   bbHh

bh     |  BbHh   Bbhh   bbHh    bbhh

We can now count the number of rabbits for each phenotype.

Black coat straight hair (BBHH, BbHH, BBHh, BbHh) = 9

Black coat curly hair (BBhh, Bbhh) = 3

Brown coat straight hair (bbHH, bbHh) = 3

Brown coat curly hair (bbhh) = 1

Our phenotypic ratio therefore is 9:3:3:1 for black coat straight hair: black coat curly hair: brown coat straight hair: brown coat curly hair.

19. Wolves are sometimes observed to have black coats and blue eyes. Assume further that normal coat color (N) is dominant to black (n) and brown eyes (B) are dominant to blue (b). Suppose the alpha male and alpha female of a pack (these are the dominant individuals who do most of the breeding) are black with blue eyes and normal colored with brown eyes, respectively. The female is also heterozygous for both traits. What are the possible genotypes of the offspring? Identify the parental genotype: Parent 1: _____________ Parent 2: _____________ Genotypic ratio: __________ Phenotypic ratio: _________


Parent 1: nnbb

Parent 2: NnBb

Genotypic ratio: 1:1:1:1

Phenotypic ratio: 1:1:1

To begin, we can assign the following genotypes to the parental wolves:

Parent 1 (alpha male): nnbb (black coat and blue eyes)

Parent 2 (alpha female): NnBb (normal coat and brown eyes, heterozygous for both traits)

To determine the possible genotypes of their offspring, we can use a Punnett square:

| n n | b B

--|------|-----

N | Nn | NB

N | Nn | NB

n | nn | nb

n | nn | nb

From this Punnett square, we can see that there are four possible genotype combinations for the offspring: Nnbb, NnBb, nnbb, and nnBb.

The genotypic ratio of the offspring can be determined by counting the number of squares that correspond to each genotype:

Nnbb: 2 squares

NnBb: 2 squares

nnbb: 2 squares

nnBb: 2 squares

Thus, the genotypic ratio is 1:1:1:1.

The phenotypic ratio can be determined by considering the expression of the dominant and recessive traits. A wolf with a normal coat (N) will always have brown eyes (B), so the only way for an offspring to have blue eyes is if they inherit the recessive b allele from both parents. Similarly, an offspring with a black coat (n) can only occur if they inherit the recessive n allele from the black-coated parent. Therefore, the phenotypic ratio can be determined as follows:

Normal coat and brown eyes: 2 squares (corresponding to NnBb)

Normal coat and blue eyes: 2 squares (corresponding to Nnbb)

Black coat and brown eyes: 0 squares

Black coat and blue eyes: 2 squares (corresponding to nnbb and nnBb)

Thus, the phenotypic ratio is 1:1:0:1, or simply 1:1:1.
