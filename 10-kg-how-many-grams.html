21. If there are 10 kg of Carbon Dioxide molecules inside a container, how many grams of Oxygen atoms do you think are there?​


Answer:

mass O = 7.3 × 10³ g

Explanation:

Calculating the molar mass of CO2

molar mass = (12.01 g × 1) + (16.00 g × 2)

molar mass = 44.01 g

Calculating the mass of O

mass O = 10000 g CO2 × (1 mol CO2 / 44.01 g CO2) × (2 mol O / 1 mol CO2) × (16.00 g O / 1 mol O)

mass O = 7.3 × 10³ g
