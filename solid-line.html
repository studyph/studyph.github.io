Answer:

The boundary of an inequality is a broken line when the given inequality sign is > or <. On the other hand, it is a solid line when the given inequality sign is ≥ or ≤.

20. give two equations with broken line and solid line ​


Answer:

One example of an equation with a broken line and a solid line is the equation of a vertical asymptote of a rational function, such as:

x = 2

The vertical line x = 2 is shown as a broken line on a graph because it represents a value that the function cannot take on.

Another example of an equation with a broken line and a solid line is the equation of a linear inequality, such as:

y < 2x + 1

The graph of this inequality is a shaded region below the line y = 2x + 1, with the line itself shown as a solid line. The boundary line represents the points where the inequality is true, while the shaded region represents the points where the inequality is false.
