13. From your own point of view, give your own meaning of circle and the what you call your "center and circumference"​.


Answer:

A circle is a two-dimensional shape that is defined by a set of points that are all the same distance from a fixed point, called the center. The fixed point is the "center" of the circle, and the set of points that make up the circle are called the "circumference." In other words, the center of a circle is the point around which the shape is defined, and the circumference is the outer edge of the circle.
