28. example of Intervening words


Intervening words or phrases come between a subject and a verb. The intervening words do not affect the subject and verb agreement. These words can be ignored in locating the subject or the verb.  To be sure that your subject agrees with the verb, read the sentence without the intervening words.   The intervening words in the following sentences are italicized.

Examples:

1. My friends, together with their parents, are going to the zoo.

• Here, the intervening words are “together with their parents”.  Without the intervening words, the sentence would read “My friends are going to the zoo tomorrow.” The plural subject friends take a plural verb are.

2. The flowers in that garden are beautiful.

• In this case, “in that garden” are the intervening words.  Without the intervening words, the sentence would read “The flowers are beautiful.” The plural subject flowers have a plural verb are.

Banana cue, along with orange juice, makes a delicious afternoon snack.

• Here, without the intervening words, the sentence would read “Banana cue makes a delicious afternoon snack.”  The singular subject banana cue agrees with the singular verb makes.

Plural nouns have plural verbs. Singular nouns have singular verbs. Intervening words do not affect this relationship even if they are present in a sentence.
