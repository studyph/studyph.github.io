7. What is the kinetic energy of a 50 kg object moving at 20 m/s?A. 1000 kg m²/s²B. 10000 kg m²/s2C. 10000 kg m²/s²D. 10000 kg m²/s²​


KINETIC ENERGYWhat is the kinetic energy of a 50 kg object moving at 20 m/s?

Answer:

10,000 kg m²/s²

Step-by-step explanation:

Use the K.E. = ½ mv² formula to solve the given problem.

Given:

K.E. = ?m = 20 m/sv = 50 kg

Solution:

[tex]K.E. = \frac{1}{2}m {v}^{2} \\ K.E. = \frac{1}{2} (20 \: m/s) {(50 \: kg)}^{2} \\ K.E. = 4 {(50)}^{2} \\ K.E. = 4(2 \: 500) \\ K.E. = 10 \: 000 \: kg \: {m}^{2}/ {s}^{2} [/tex]

Thus, 10,000 kg m²/s² is the Kinetic Energy.
