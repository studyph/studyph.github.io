8. a solution containing 20.0g sodium sulfite reacts with 7.00mL


Answer:

The balanced equation for this reaction is:

Na2SO3 + 2HCl → 2NaCl + SO2 + H2O

Moles of sodium sulfite:

20.0 g Na2SO3 × (1 mol Na2SO3/126.05 g Na2SO3) = 0.158 mol Na2SO3

Moles of hydrochloric acid:

7.00 mL HCl × (0.200 mol HCl/1000 mL) = 0.014 mol HCl

Since the moles of hydrochloric acid is less than the moles of sodium sulfite, the limiting reagent is hydrochloric acid.

Moles of sodium chloride produced:

0.014 mol HCl × (2 mol NaCl/2 mol HCl) = 0.014 mol NaCl

Mass of sodium chloride produced:

0.014 mol NaCl × (58.44 g NaCl/1 mol NaCl) = 0.822 g NaCl

22. If 5.0 grams of aluminum sulfite, Al(SO) a react completely with 5.0 grams of sodium hydroxide, NaH, how many grams of Al(OH) can be formed? what is the limiting and excess reagent


Answer:

2.6054 g

Explanation:

[tex]\text{Write the Balanced Equation: }Al_2(SO_3)_3 + 6NaOH \rightarrow 2Al(OH)_3 + 3Na_2SO_3 \\

5 \ g \ Al_2(SO_3)_3 \times \frac{1 \ mol}{294.143 \ g} \times \frac{2 \ mol \ Al(OH)_3}{1 \ mol \ Al_2(SO_3)_3} = 0.0334 \ mol \ Al(OH)_3 \\

5 \ g \ NaOH \times \frac{1 \ mol \ NaOH}{40 \ g \ NaOH} \times \frac{2 \ mol \ Al(OH)_3}{6 \ mol \ NaOH}= 0.0417 \ mol \ Al(OH)_3 [/tex]

Therefore the limiting reagent is aluminum sulfite and the excess reagent is sodium hydroxide. Aluminum sulfite will deplete first (5 g).
