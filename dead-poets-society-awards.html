Criteria

In order to become a national living treasure, the candidate must have the following qualifications:

1. is an inhabitant of an indigenous/traditional cultural culture in any region of the Philippines that has retained indigenous practices, beliefs, rituals and values and/or has syncretized any foreign factors that have influenced it.  

2. must include a tradition of folk art that has been in existence and registered for at least 50 years.  

3. Works of exceptional and distinctive nature must have been continuously executed or produced over a sustained period of time.

1. A traditional artist who holds all the attributes of a Manlilikha ng Bayan candidate, but who, due to age or infirmity, has left him incapable of further teaching his craft, can still be recognised if:

 

2. has been instrumental in the reinvigoration of the musical legacy of their culture.

3. has passed on to other members of the group the expertise of folk art that the community has historically known for.

4. The society regarded them as masters and teachers of their art.

26. how awards of gamaba help filipino society?


Answer:

In April 1992, the Gawad sa Manlilikha ng Bayan or the National Living Treasures Award was institutionalized through Republic Act No. 7355. Tasked with the administration and implementation of the Award is the National Commission for Culture and the Arts, the highest policy-making and coordinating body for culture and the arts of the State. The NCCA, through the Gawad sa Manlilikha ng Bayan Executive Council, conducts the search for the finest traditional artists of the land, adopts a program that will ensure the transfer of their skills to others, and undertakes measures to promote a genuine appreciation of and instill pride among our people about the genius of the Manlilikha ng Bayan.

The Gawad sa Manlilikha ng Bayan logo is a stylized representation of the human form used in traditional cloth. Below the motif is ‘Manlilikha ng Bayan’ written in ancient Filipino script extensively used throughout the Philippines at the time of Western contact in the sixteenth century.
