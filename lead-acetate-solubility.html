9. A mixture of 0.20M acetic acid and 0.30M sodium acetate is given. Calculate the pH of the medium if the pKa of the acetic acid is 4.76.


Answer:

The mixture of acetic acid and sodium acetate is a buffer solution that resists changes to pH. To calculate the pH of the buffer solution, we can use the Henderson-Hasselbalch equation:

pH = pKa + log([A^-]/[HA])

where pKa is the dissociation constant of acetic acid, [A^-] is the concentration of acetate ion, and [HA] is the concentration of acetic acid.

In this case, the pKa of acetic acid is known to be 4.76, the concentration of acetic acid is 0.20 M and the concentration of sodium acetate is 0.30 M.

Acetic acid dissociates according to the following reaction:

CH3COOH + H2O ⇌ CH3COO^- + H3O^+

The equilibrium constant for this reaction is given by:

Ka = [CH3COO^-][H3O^+]/[CH3COOH]

Taking the negative logarithms of both sides, we can obtain:

-pKa = log(Ka) = log([CH3COO^-][H3O^+]/[CH3COOH])

Rearranging and simplifying the equation, we get:

pH = pKa + log([CH3COO^-]/[CH3COOH])

Substituting the given concentrations of acetic acid and sodium acetate, we have:

[CH3COOH] = 0.20 M

[CH3COO^-] = 0.30 M

So,

pH = 4.76 + log(0.30/0.20)

pH = 4.76 + log(1.5)

pH = 4.76 + 0.176

pH = 4.94

Therefore, the pH of the buffer solution is 4.94.


10. The solubility of lead(ii) chloride is 0.45 g/100 ml of solution. what is the ksp of pbcl2


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Summarize the concentrations using the ICE table.

Let s be the molar solubility of PbCl₂.

[tex]\begin{array}{lccccc} & \text{PbCl}_2(s) & \rightleftharpoons & \text{Pb}^{2+}(aq) & + & 2\text{Cl}^-(aq) \\ \text{Initial} \: (M): & & & 0 & & 0 \\ \text{Change} \: (M): & -s & & +s & & +2s \\ \hline \text{Equilibrium} \: (M): & & & s & & 2s \\ \end{array}[/tex]

Step 2: Write the Ksp expression.

[tex]\begin{aligned} K_{\text{sp}} = [\text{Pb}^{2+}][\text{Cl}^-]^2 = (s)(2s)^2 = 4s^3 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molar solubility.

The molar mass of PbCl₂ is 278.1 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} s & = \frac{\text{0.45 g} \: \text{PbCl}_2}{\text{100 mL soln}} \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{PbCl}_2}{\text{278.1 g} \: \text{PbCl}_2} \times \frac{\text{1000 mL soln}}{\text{1 L soln}} \\ & = \text{0.01618 mol/L} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the Ksp.

[tex]\begin{aligned} K_{\text{sp}} & = 4s^3 \\ & = 4(0.01618)^3 \\ & = \boxed{1.7 \times 10^{-5}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the Ksp of PbCl₂ is 1.7 × 10⁻⁵.

16. Calculate the pH of a mixture of 1M acetic acid and 0.5M sodium acetate.​


Answer:

pH = 4.44

Explanation:

Use the principle of common ion effect.

NaC2H3O2 → Na^(+) + C2H3O2^(-)

[i]: 0.5 M 0.5 M 0.5 M

Use ICE method.

HC2H3O2 = H^(+) + C2H3O2^(-)

[i]: 1 0 0.5

[c]: - x + x + x

[e]: 1 - x x 0.5 + x

Solve for x.

Ka = [H^(+)][C2H3O2^(-)] / [HC2H3O2]

1.8 × 10^(-5) = (x)(0.5 + x) / (1 - x)

1.8 × 10^(-5) - [1.8 × 10^(-5)]x = 0.5x + x²

x² + 0.500018x - 1.8 × 10^(-5) = 0

Using quadratic formula

a = 1, b = 0.500018, c = -1.8 × 10^(-5)

x = [-b ± √(b² - 4ac)] / (2a)

x = [-0.500018 ± √(0.500018² - 4(1)(-1.8 × 10^(-5)))] / [2(1)]

x(+) = 3.6 × 10^(-5) M (accept)

x(-) = -0.50 M (reject)

Determine the hydrogen ion concentration.

[H^(+)] = x = 3.6 × 10^(-5) M

Solve for the pH.

pH = -log [H^(+)]

pH = -log (3.6 × 10^(-5))

pH = 4.44

23. write the ksp expression for the sparingly soluble compound lead phosphate


Solubility Product ConstantDiscussion:

The solubility product constant of a compound is the product of the molar concentrations of the constituent ions, each raised to the power of its stoichiometric coefficient in the equilibrium equation.

Consider a saturated solution of [tex]\text{A}_{x}\text{B}_{y}[/tex] that is in contact with a solid [tex]\text{A}_{x}\text{B}_{y}[/tex]. The solubility equilibrium can be represented as

[tex]\text{A}_{x}\text{B}_{y}(s) \rightleftharpoons x\text{A}^{y+}(aq) + y\text{B}^{x-}(aq)[/tex]

The solubility product constant [tex]K_{\text{sp}}[/tex] for this reaction is

[tex]\boxed{K_{\text{sp}} = [\text{A}^{y+}]^{x}[\text{B}^{x-}]^{y}}[/tex]

There are two ways to express a substance's solubility: molar solubility, which is the number of moles of solute in 1 L of a saturated solution (mol/L), and solubility, which is the number of grams of solute in 1 L of a saturated solution (g/L).

[tex]\\[/tex]

Problem:

Write the ksp expression for the sparingly soluble compound lead phosphate

Given:

[tex]\text{compound} = \text{Pb}_{3}(\text{PO}_{4})_{2}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]K_{\text{sp}} \: \text{expression}[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Write the balanced chemical equation for the dissociation of Pb₃(PO₄)₂.

[tex]\text{Pb}_3(\text{PO}_4)_2(s) \rightleftharpoons 3\text{Pb}^{2+}(aq) + 2\text{PO}_4^{3-}(aq)[/tex]

Step 2: Write the Ksp expression for Pb₃(PO₄)₂.

[tex]\boxed{K_{\text{sp}} = [\text{Pb}^{2+}]^{3}[\text{PO}_{4}^{3-}]^{2}}[/tex]
