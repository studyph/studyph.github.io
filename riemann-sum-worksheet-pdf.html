12. the Riemann Hypothesis?​


Answer:

The Riemann Hypothesis is a conjecture in mathematics that deals with the distribution of prime numbers. It was proposed by mathematician Bernhard Riemann in the 19th century.

The conjecture states that the nontrivial zeros (zeros that are not equal to 1) of the Riemann zeta function, which is a function that encodes the distribution of prime numbers, all lie on the critical line of 1/2. In other words, if z is a zero of the Riemann zeta function, then the real part of z is equal to 1/2.

The Riemann Hypothesis is considered to be one of the most important unsolved problems in mathematics and has attracted the attention of many mathematicians over the years. If the conjecture is true, it would have significant implications for the distribution of prime numbers and the distribution of their multiples. However, despite much effort, a proof or disproof of the conjecture has yet to be found.

[tex]\begin{gathered} \begin{cases} \bold{1)} \: contextualization \\ { \bold{2)} } \: partition \: width \\ \bold{3)} \: rectangle \: height\\ \bold{4)} \: summation \end{cases}\end{gathered}[/tex]

The first thing we have to do is draw the function we want to approximate, and then make the partitions according to what is sought or requested.

The sum we must do, according to the statement, is through the right Riemann sum, that is, make our rectangles touch the curve with their upper right corners. Depending on the curve, we will generate areas with underestimations or overestimations.

From the statement, we know that there are 6 partitions, that is, 6 rectangles within f(x) in the given interval.

Now let's actually go to the Riemann sum method, which is a tool that tries to approximate areas that are difficult to calculate. This being done through geometric and smaller partitions of this area, in order to find an approximation for it. The relationship used for this calculation is:

[tex]\begin{gathered} \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \boxed{ \bold{ A \approx \sum_{i = 1}^{n} f( x_i) \: . \: \Delta x}}\\ \end{gathered}[/tex]

As we are not going to use the term "n" which represents the number of partitions, tending to infinity, the expression boils down to the one above.

These terms within the summation represent the area of each rectangle, where f(x) represents the height and ∆x the base. Each of these has a particular relationship:

[tex]\begin{gathered} \: \: \: \: x_{i} = a + i \cdot \Delta x \\ \Delta x = \frac{b - a}{n} \: \to \: a \ leqslant x \leqslant b \\ \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{gathered} \Delta x = \frac{b - a}{n} \to \: 0 \leqslant x \leqslant 3 \: e \: \: n = 6 \\ \\ \: \Delta x = \frac{3 -0 }{6} \to \: \Delta x = \frac{3}{6} \: \to \: \boxed{ \Delta x = \frac{1}{2} }\end {gathered}[/tex]
