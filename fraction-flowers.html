17. a heterozygous axial flowered plant is crossed with a terminal flowered plant, what is the probability (fraction) of producing offsprings of the same phenotype as the first parent?​


Answer:

The phenotypic ratio of the above cross will be 3:1, that is 3 axial flowers and 1 terminal flower.

The genotypic ratio of the above cross will be 1:2:1, that is 1 homologous plant with axial flowers (AA), 2 heterozygous plants with axial flowers (Aa) and 1 homologous plant with terminal flower (aa).

Answer:

½

3 dozens are just 12 multiplied by 3, which is 36.

12 plus 6 is 18.

18 is one-half of 36.

Terminal flowered plant: aa

Offsprings when crossed: Aa, Aa, aa, aa = 2/4 = 1/2 = 50%
