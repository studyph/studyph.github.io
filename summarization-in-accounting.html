8. Explain recording, classifying and summarizing in relation to the communicating component of accounting.​


Answer:

Accounting starts with

-identifying a transaction and then recording in Journal

-then classifying in the ledger

-then summarising in final accounts

-then interpretation through Ratio analysis

-then finally communicating the information to the users of the financial statement.

Thus, the first step is identifying and the last step is communicating the information.

19. The phase of accounting which business transaction is recorded in the book of original entry.a. Recording c. Summarizingb. Classifying d. Interpreting ​


Answer:

A. Recording

Step-by-step explanation:

Recording is a basic phase of accounting that is also known as bookkeeping. In this phase, all financial transactions are recorded in a systematical and chronological manner in the appropriate books or databases. Accounting recorders are the documents and books involved in preparing financial statements. Accounting recorders include records of assets, liabilities, ledgers, journals and other supporting documents such as invoices and checks.
