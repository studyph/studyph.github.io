1. describe the i-it relationship compared to i-you cite example​


An explanation of i-it relationship and i-you relationship.

Just in case you never heard of i-it relationship and i-you relationship, then I will explain them to you in this thread. In English, if we want to express or point an inanimate object or animals or plants, we use it to refer to them, regardless of the genders. Surely you must have noticed about the use of she and he to refer to male or female human beings, and here, it is just the third variation.

Meanwhile, the i-you relationship is between a subject of I with you as other subject of the same position. Usually, the relationship of i-you is used in direct speech since it also indicates that the subject I is talking directly with the other subject or, in this case, the "you".

A simple example would be: I reach down to my feet to take my pencil on the floor. It fell five minutes earlier. As you can see, it is used to replace the pencil, so in this case the i-it relationship is pretty clear. As for the example of i-you: Haven't I told you about the football match last week?

In the I-Thou relationship, what is key is how I am with you in my own heart and mind. The I-It encounter is the opposite in that we relate to another as object, completely outside of ourselves
