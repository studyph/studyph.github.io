2. The drinking water needs of an office are by large water bottles. One end of a 0.25-in-diameter plastic hose is inserted into the bottle placed on a high stand, while the other end with an on/off valve is maintained 2 ft below the bottom of the bottle. If the water level in the bottle is 1.5 ft when it is full, determine how long it will take at the minimum to fill an 8-oz glass (= 0.00835 ft3) (a) when the bottle is first opened and (b) when the bottle is almost empty. Neglect frictional losses.​


[tex]\mathbb{FORMULAS:}[/tex]

Bernoulli's energy equation:

[tex]\mathtt{\dfrac{P_{1}}{{\rho}g}+\dfrac{v_{1}^2}{2g}+z_{1}=\dfrac{P_{2}}{{\rho}g}+\dfrac{v_{2}^2}{2g}+z_{2}}[/tex]

Flow rate:

[tex]\mathtt{Q=Av=\dfrac{V}{t}}[/tex]

where:

P = pressure

ρ = density

g = gravitational acceleration

v = velocity

z = elevation

Q = flow rate

V = volume

t = time

[tex]\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

Refer to figure

We take point 1 to be at the surface of water in the bottle and point 2 at the exit of the tube.

Since the bottle and the exit of the tube is open to atmosphere, P₁ = P₂ = 0

Since the bottle is large compare to the diameter of the tube, v₁ = 0

We take point 2 as the reference for our elevations, z₂ = 0

The Bernoulli's equation simplifies to:

[tex]\mathtt{z_{1}=\dfrac{v_{2}^{2}}{2g}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathtt{v_{2}=\sqrt{2gz_{1}}}[/tex]

Equating Q = Q, we get:

[tex]\mathtt{Av=\dfrac{V}{t}}[/tex]

Since we want to solve for t, isolate t

[tex]\mathtt{t=\dfrac{V}{Av}}[/tex]

a.) When the bottle is first opened (the bottle is full, z₁ = 2 + 1.5 = 3.5 ft)

[tex]\mathtt{v_{2}=\sqrt{2(32.2)(3.5)}}[/tex]

v₂ = 15.013 ft/s

[tex]\mathtt{t=\dfrac{0.00835}{(\dfrac{\pi}{4})(\dfrac{0.25}{12})^2(15.013)}}[/tex]

t = 1.631 s

b.) When the bottle is almost empty (z₁ = 2 ft)

[tex]\mathtt{v_{2}=\sqrt{2(32.2)(2)}}[/tex]

v₂ = 11.349 ft/s

[tex]\mathtt{t=\dfrac{0.00835}{(\dfrac{\pi}{4})(\dfrac{0.25}{12})^2(11.349)}}[/tex]

t = 2.158 s

[tex]\mathbb{ANSWER:}[/tex]

a. [tex]\boxed{\texttt{1.631 s}}[/tex]

b. [tex]\boxed{\texttt{2.158 s}}[/tex]
