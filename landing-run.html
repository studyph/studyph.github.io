18. Contamination of lands caused by run off from agricultural fieldexaplain ang give examples​


Answer:

Runoff from agricultural fields can contaminate land with a variety of pollutants, including fertilizers, pesticides, and animal waste. These pollutants can enter nearby water sources, such as rivers and streams, and cause algal blooms that deplete oxygen levels in the water. They can also leach into groundwater and contaminate drinking water supplies. Examples of this type of contamination include the Gulf of Mexico dead zone caused by runoff from the Mississippi River basin, and nitrate contamination in groundwater caused by runoff from agricultural fields in California's Central Valley.
