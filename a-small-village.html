13. similarities and differences between big city and small villageplss help po need ko na ​


Question:

similarities and differences between big city and small village?

Answer:

There are usually big differences in commercial opportunities, employment types, levels, varieties. In a big city, there are non-agricultural production-oriented opportunities, like factories, big box stores, stores for market sectors, whereas in a small town, the majority of the economy is strictly service and small supply, like home/farm hardware, whereas in the big city there are stores and services to support businesses. In a small town, there might be one church, one car dealership, and in a big city, many churches, and dealerships. There might be an electrical supply, a plumber, a butcher (maybe), a barbershop, a salon, a gas station, and depending on the town size, maybe 1 cafe and 1 diner. In a city, there are many food service outlets, restaurants, shops fr clothing, toys, auto parts, multiple service stations, computers, flowers, and plants. I grew up in a village in the New England part of the USA that had 1400 residents, where you had to make a long-distance call to reach anyone in the local government. Nearby was a town where I went to high school and it served 7 towns, thus the students and their families came from small towns like mine. My graduating class for the seven small towns had a total of 75 students in it. The nearest cities were 35+ miles away, and these were certainly not big cities. Going to the big city was going to Manchester, Boston or New York.

Explanation:

Public services are dramatically different between small towns and big cities. None of the small towns I ever knew of had a clinic or a hospital, but every once in a while there was a dentist. Sanitation in small towns is usually done privately. Many homes have their own wells. A lot of small towns have a place where people can bring garbage and recycling weekly. Some areas with more administration, perhaps, have a small garbage pickup service. Likewise, in the small town, you knew the guy who came to read your home’s water or electric meter, but in the big city, it’s done automatically via the internet. To this day, my small town hasn’t a taxi nor a bus service. If you want to go to Boston, you need to drive 30 miles to the nearest bus stop. There are two policemen, there used to be only one. There was only one gas station for a long time, but now there are two. If it snows, the town plows its streets, but one has to have their own arrangements for private drives and walkways. The town has one shed for its town trucks, graders, sand, and salt. There is one post office, with daily delivery.

Ignorance flourishes in some small towns, where people don’t get out much and base their judgments about others on what they perceive from magazines, TV, radio, and perhaps talk shows. I don’t know think it has changed much since I was a kid, but there were no non-white people in my high school. Very few people spoke Spanish in the entire area. So in a small town, there are not likely to be groupings of ethnicities living in one part or another, whereas in a big city, there are many different types of ethnic groups, and commonly they live in their own neighborhoods with restaurants that ply their favorite food types and shops (e.g. China town, Latino barrios).
