11. Explain on how you will solve limits of transcendental functions using table of values and graph.​


Well you could make a chart of the values of x and the values of f(x) to see the trend toward the limit.

So, let’s say f(x) = (x^2 - 9)/(x - 3) and we want to know the limit as x approaches 3. Well, if you just find f(3) you will get 0/0 which does us no good. So what we do is set up the chart

x …………..…….. f(x)

2.9 ………….………. 5.9

2.95 ……………….. 5.95

2.99 ……………….. 5.99

3.01 ………….………6.01

It is pretty clear from the chart that as x approaches 3 of f(x) = (x^2 - 9)/(x - 3) that f(x) is approaching 6. If you graph f(x) = (x^2 - 9)/(x - 3) on a graphing calculator, you will see the same phenomenon. The graph will be approaching 6 as you let x get closer and closer to 3 from either side.

Now, this is not a proof. I insist that my algebra students prove the assertion algebraically.

So we have lim as x—-> 3 of f(x) = (x^2 - 9)/(x - 3) is

lim x—->3 of (x-3)(x+3) / (x-3) and since we are not letting x ever become 3 (just very close to it), we can “cancel” out the (x - 3)‘s and get

lim as x—-> 3 of (x + 3) which is clearly 3 + 3 or 6
