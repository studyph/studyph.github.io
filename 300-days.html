23. find means distance of a planet from the sun if it's period of revolution is 300 days?​


Answer:

The mean distance is 44.81 AU

Explanation:

Keppler's 3rd Law formula

[tex]T^{2} = r^{3}[/tex]

where

T     is the orbital period, unit is in Earthly years

r      is the mean distance of a planet from the Sun, unit is in AU

Astronomical units (AU) is a unit for length which is the distance of the Earth to the Sun.

Given information

T = 300 revolutions

Take note:  that 1 revolution around the sun is equivalent to one (1 ) year, , therefore 300 revolutions = 300 years

Solving the problem

Using Keppler's 3rd Law formula

[tex]T^{2} = r^{3}[/tex]

Substitute the given information

(300)2 = r3

90,00 = r3

r =

r = 44.814 AU

Therefore, the mean distance of the planet from the sun is 44.814 AU.

My day usually starts very early, with me getting up before my loved one to prepare their breakfast and medications. This includes making sure that their clothes are clean and ready to wear, their bed is made, and their room is tidy.
