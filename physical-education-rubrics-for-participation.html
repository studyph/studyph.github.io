8. Age and gender are the two most consistent demographic and biological determinants of physical activity participation. Physical activity participation is consistently higher in men, and consistently higher in younger compared to older adults. Socio-economic status and education have also consistently been related to physical activity, with adults of higher social economic status or higher education being more likely to participate in physical activity. Being of nonwhite race or ethnicity is consistently associated with lower levels of physical activity. There is emerging evidence (Trost et al., 2002) that overweight and obesity influences physical activity behavior with those reporting high levels of physical activity being less likely to be classified as obese. explain​


Answer:

The statement highlights that age and gender are two consistent demographic factors that influence physical activity participation. Men are more likely to engage in physical activity than women, and younger adults are more likely to participate in physical activity than older adults. This may be due to physiological factors, such as differences in muscle mass, hormones, and metabolism, as well as social and cultural norms that encourage men and younger individuals to be more active.

Additionally, socio-economic status and education are related to physical activity levels. Individuals of higher social economic status or higher education are more likely to participate in physical activity, possibly because they have greater access to resources such as gym memberships, personal trainers, and recreational facilities.

Race and ethnicity are also related to physical activity levels, with non-white individuals being less likely to engage in physical activity. This may be due to systemic barriers such as discrimination, lack of access to safe and affordable recreational spaces, and cultural norms that do not prioritize physical activity.

Finally, there is emerging evidence that overweight and obesity influence physical activity behavior. Studies have found that individuals who engage in high levels of physical activity are less likely to be classified as obese. This may be due to the fact that physical activity can help regulate body weight by burning calories and increasing muscle mass, which in turn increases metabolism. Additionally, individuals who engage in physical activity may be more likely to have a healthier diet, which can also help prevent obesity.

21. What are some ways you could avoid injuries while participating in physical education?​


Answer:

Take time off. Plan to have at least 1 day off per week and at least one month off per year from training for a particular sport to allow the body to recover.

Wear the right gear. Players should wear appropriate and properly fit protective equipment such as pads (neck, shoulder, elbow, chest, knee, shin), helmets, mouthpieces, face guards, protective cups, and eyewear. Young athletes should not assume that protective gear will prevent all injuries while performing more dangerous or risky activities.

Strengthen muscles. Conditioning exercises during practice strengthens muscles used in play.

Increase flexibility. Stretching exercises after games or practice can increase flexibility. Stretching should also be incorporated into a daily fitness plan.

Use the proper technique. This should be reinforced during the playing season.

Take breaks. Rest periods during practice and games can reduce injuries and prevent heat illness.

Play safe. Strict rules against headfirst sliding (baseball and softball), spearing (football), and checking (in hockey) should be enforced.

Do not play through pain.

Avoid heat illness by drinking plenty of fluids before, during and after exercise or play; decrease or stop practices or competitions during high heat/humidity periods; wear light clothing.

If children are jumping on a trampoline, they should be supervised by a responsible adult, and only one child should be on the trampoline at a time; 75% of trampoline injuries occur when more than one person is jumping at a time.
