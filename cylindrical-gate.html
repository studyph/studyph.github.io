17. A cylindrical sardine can has a radius of 3 cm and a height of 7 cm. What is the volume of the cylindrical can?


✏️CYLINDER===============================

Problem: A cylindrical sardine can has a radius of 3 cm and a height of 7 cm. What is the volume of the cylindrical can?

Solution: Using the Formula to find the volume of Cylindrical Can.

[tex] \begin{aligned} & \bold{ \color{lightblue}Formula: } \\ & \boxed{Volume = \pi {r}^{2}h } \end{aligned}[/tex]

[tex]Volume = (\pi)( {3cm})^{2} (7cm)[/tex]

[tex]Volume = (\pi)(9cm^{2} ) (7cm)[/tex]

[tex]Volume = (\pi)63cm^{3}[/tex]

- Let 3.14 be the approximate value of pi.

[tex]Volume = (3.14)63cm^{3}[/tex]

[tex]Volume = 197.82cm^{3}[/tex]

- Therefore, the volume of the cylindrical can is:

[tex] \large \boxed{ \rm \green{ \: 197.82 {cm}^{3} \: }}[/tex]
