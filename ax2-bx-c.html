3. ax2 + bx+c=0->ax2 + bx = -c Why?​


Answer:

[tex]ax^2 + bx + c = 0[/tex] is equal to [tex]ax^2 + bx = -c[/tex]

Why?

They are both similar or the same since if we want to transpose or change the position of the c to the other side, we change the sign of it to its inverse sign or opposite sign. On the equation above, if we transpose c to the other side based on the law of transposition it will change to the sign of its opposite. On what we observe, c is a positive integer before transposing. So if we change the position to the right side or the opposite side, we will change the sign to its opposite. Thus,

[tex]ax^2 + bx + c = 0[/tex] is equal to [tex]ax^2 + bx = - c[/tex]

Although two binomial can be multiplied using the distributive property, a shorter method is used more frequently.

Solution.

     Multiply the First terms

            (a)(a) = a²

     Multiply the Outer terms

            (a)(-b) = -ab

     Multiply the Inner terms

            (b)(a) = ab

     Multiply the Last terms

            (b)(b) = b²
