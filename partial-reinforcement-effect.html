What is the minimum effective depth for a beam with no shear reinforcement if its width is limited to 450 mm? b. Determine the width if the minimum amount of web reinforcement is needed and the effective depth is limited to 670 mm. c. Determine the location from the support of the joints where minimum amount of reinforcement is required if it has a span of 8.85 m.


Answer:

Explanation:

Given:

[tex]V_u=250 \ \text{kN}[/tex]

[tex]f_c'=27.6 \ \text{MPa}[/tex]

[tex]f_y=420 \ \text{MPa}[/tex]

[tex]\lambda=0.75[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

a. minimum effective depth, d if no shear reinforcement ([tex]V_s=0[/tex]) and b = 450 mm:

[tex]V_u=\phi(V_c+V_s)[/tex]

For shear, [tex]\phi=0.75[/tex]

[tex]250=0.75(V_c+0)[/tex]

[tex]V_c=333.33 \ \text{kN}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]V_c=0.17\lambda\sqrt{f_c'}\:b\:d[/tex]

[tex]333.33(10)^3=0.17(0.75)\sqrt{27.6}(450)d[/tex]

[tex]d=1105.85 \ \text{mm}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

b. minimum width, b if minimum amount of shear reinforcement is needed and effective depth, d = 670 mm:

from NSCP 2015 416.4.6.1, Avmin is the greater of (a) and (b)

(a)

[tex]A_{v,min}=0.062\sqrt{f_c'}\dfrac{bs}{f_y}[/tex]

(b)

[tex]A_{v,min}=0.35\dfrac{bs}{f_y}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Solving for Avmin/s in terms of b

(a)

[tex]\dfrac{A_{v,min}}{s}=0.062\sqrt{27.6}\dfrac{b}{420}[/tex]

[tex]\dfrac{A_{v,min}}{s}=0.00076b[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

(b)

[tex]\dfrac{A_{v,min}}{s}=0.35\dfrac{b}{420}[/tex]

[tex]\dfrac{A_{v,min}}{s}=0.00083b[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

since case (a) < case (b), case (b) governs

[tex]\\[/tex]

from

[tex]V_u=\phi(V_c+V_s)[/tex]

[tex]V_c=0.17\lambda\sqrt{f_c'}bd[/tex]

[tex]V_s=\dfrac{A_vf_{yt}d}{s}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Assuming fyt = fy = 420 and substituting Av/s = Avmin/s = 0.00083b

[tex]250(10)^3=0.75\left[0.17(0.75)\sqrt{27.6}b(670)+0.00083b(420)(670)\right][/tex]

[tex]b=488.51 \ \text{mm}[/tex]
