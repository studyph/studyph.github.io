18. how to apply distance and midpoint formula?​


The distance and midpoint formulas are used to find the distance between two points and the midpoint of those two points, respectively. To use these formulas, you will need the coordinates of the two points.

The distance formula is given by the following equation:

Distance = √((x2 - x1)^2 + (y2 - y1)^2)

To use this formula, simply substitute the x- and y-coordinates of the two points into the equation and simplify. For example, if you have the two points (3, 4) and (6, 8), the distance between them would be calculated as follows:

Distance = √((6 - 3)^2 + (8 - 4)^2)

= √(3^2 + 4^2)

= √(9 + 16)

= √25

= 5

The midpoint formula is given by the following equation:

Midpoint = ((x1 + x2)/2, (y1 + y2)/2)

To use this formula, simply add the x-coordinates of the two points and divide by 2 to find the x-coordinate of the midpoint, and then add the y-coordinates of the two points and divide by 2 to find the y-coordinate of the midpoint. For example, if you have the two points (3, 4) and (6, 8), the midpoint would be calculated as follows:

Midpoint = ((3 + 6)/2, (4 + 8)/2)

= (9/2, 12/2)

= (4.5, 6)

Therefore, the midpoint of the two points (3, 4) and (6, 8) is (4.5, 6).
