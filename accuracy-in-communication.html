27. how do you evaluate the accuracy reliability timeliness consistency relevant and uniqueness of information in our everyday communication​


These are ways to evaluate the accuracy, reliability, timeliness, consistency, relevance, and uniqueness of information in our daily communications​:

1. Accuracy.

In order to measure whether the information we get is accurate, we have to find out if the information is the same as what actually happened and describe the details. We can check it by trying to find valid data for example from the official media or from other people and compare it with the information we already have.

2. Reliability.

To check the reliability of the information we get, we can compare it with other information that we find. If the information we find from each source is the same, then the information has reliability.

3. Timeliness.

To measure the timeliness of the information we get is to check if the information is new information that is the most up-to-date and can help us in any case, for example making decisions.

4. Relevance.

To measure the relevance of the information we get, we can check whether the information we get from daily communication is information that is useful and we need. If it is not, the information is irrelevant.

5. Unique.

To find out whether the information we get is unique information, we can compare it with other information we get. If the information is not mainstream and contains new info and ideas that have not been often discussed, then the information is unique.

To evaluate the things mentioned above, we must understand the concept and meaning of each aspect that we want to evaluate.
