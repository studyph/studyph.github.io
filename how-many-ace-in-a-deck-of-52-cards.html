If a card is drawn from a standard deck of 52 cards and it is a heart, then there are 13 possible cards that could have been drawn (since there are 13 hearts in a deck of cards).

Out of these 13 cards, there is only one ace of hearts. Therefore, the probability that the card is an ace given that it is a heart is:

P(ace|heart) = number of favorable outcomes / total number of possible outcomes

P(ace|heart) = 1/13

11. 6) A card is pulled in a deck of 52 cards and noted. The card is then replaced, the deck is shuffled, and a second card is removed and noted. What is the probability that both cards are Aces? ​


Answer:

Since there are 4 Aces in a deck of 52 cards, the probability of drawing an Ace on the first draw is 4/52.

Since the card is replaced, the probability of drawing an Ace on the second draw is also 4/52.

The probability of both events happening together (drawing two Aces in a row) is the product of their probabilities:

P(both cards are Aces) = P(first card is an Ace) × P(second card is an Ace)

P(both cards are Aces) = (4/52) × (4/52)

P(both cards are Aces) = 0.0059 or about 0.59%

Therefore, the probability that both cards drawn are Aces is approximately 0.59%.
