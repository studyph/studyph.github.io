THE BRITISH FUNCTIONALIST SCHOOL

        A culture is viewed as an interconnected whole, rather than a collection of individual qualities, according to functionalism. Society is a system of interrelated pieces that make the entire function efficiently, much as a human individual has many organs that are interconnected and necessary for the body to function appropriately.

FUNCTIONALIST

       Functionalists see society as a system in which all of the components work together to form the total. Culture is necessary for societies to exist in this way. Cultural values assist people in making decisions, whereas cultural norms promote the fluid operation of society.

CONFLICT THEORY

       Conflict theorists believe that social structures are intrinsically uneven because of power disparities based on factors such as class, gender, ethnicity, and age. Culture, according to a conflict theorist, reinforces issues of privilege for particular groups depending on race, sex, class, and other factors.
