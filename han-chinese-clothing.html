5. what are the difference of japanese,korean and chinese traditional clothes?​


Answer:

CHINESE:

Chinese call the overall Han fashion as Hanfu 漢服. The last cross-collared dress popularized by Chinese was aoqun 襖裙 during mid Ming period. It was characterized by its tightened collars, loose sleeves with sewn pockets, and a folded skirt. The shirt only reached the waist.

KOREA:

Korean fashion was influenced by Chinese Ming period, so naturally their Hanbok 韓服 would resemble Ming. During early Joseon period, it looked no different from Ming, but it would evolve to be its distinct form by late Joseon.

JAPANESE:

Japan does have another style called hakama that closely resembled the other two countries). The most recognized kimono around the word is furisode 振袖. Japanese fashion was called Wafuku 和服. Their most popular form was kimono 着物, also called gofuku 吳服 before the Taisho. There are a ton.

Answer:

25. which if the following clothes was known as Han fu since it was created during Han dynasty?A. ChangpaoB. CheongsamC. Mao suitD. Shenyi​


Answer:

Shenyi

Explanation:

Hanfu in the Zhou Dynasty (1045 – 221 BC)

In the Western Zhou era (1045 – 771 BC), Kings of the Western Zhou Dynasty set up a strict hierarchical system linked by blood lineage and ethical norms based on families.

Clothes were used as a status symbol to accentuate their privileges, which had a substantial impact on clothes and ornaments, owing to great differences between the nobility and the common people.

Shenyi (one-piece clothing) and Mianfu (a religious court dress of ancient emperors and officials) emerged in the Eastern Zhou period (770 – 221 BC).

Music of China refers to the music of the Chinese people, which may be the music of the Han Chinese as well as other ethnic minorities within mainland China.
