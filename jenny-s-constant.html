15. JENNY ON EARTH, HAS A WEIGHT OF 700N. WHAT IS JENNYS MASS?


#BrainlyFast

Problem:

JENNY ON EARTH, HAS A WEIGHT OF 700N. WHAT IS JENNYS MASS?

Note:

The gravitational acceleration on Earth is 9.8 m/s². The formula for Force is  ( F = m × a ), and to solve for mass we can have the formula ( m = F / a )

Given Data:

a ( acceleration ) = 9.8 m/s²

F ( Force ) = 700 N or 700 kg·m/s²

m ( mass ) = ? unknown

Solution:

We can use the formula of Force ( F = m × a ), and to solve for mass we can have the formula ( m = F / a )

m = F / a

 = 700 kg·m/s² / 9.8 m/s²

 = 71.43 kg

Answer:

Jenny's mass on Earth will be equal to  71.43 kg

21. Diane, Lerma and Jenny went grocery shopping. Diane spent three times asmuch as Lerma and half as much as Jenny. If they spent a total of PhP5,000on groceries, how much did Jenny spend?​


Answer:

Diane spent 1500 pesos

Lerma spent 500 pesos

Jenny spent 3000 pesos

Step-by-step explanation:

Step 1: Assign variables on each member's expenses.

  x = Diane's expenses

  y = Lerma's expenses

  z = Jenny's expenses

Step 2: Convert each phrases into algebraic expression

  Diane(x) spent three times as much as Lerma(y) and as half as Jenny(z)

    [tex]x=3y=\frac{1}{2} z[/tex]         equation 1

  They spent a total of Php5,000

    [tex]x+y+z=5000[/tex]         equation 2

Step 3: Create another equation using substitution method. Solve for y from equation 2.

   [tex]x+y+z=5000[/tex]

   [tex]y=5000-x-z[/tex]           equation 3

Step 4: Express equation 1 in terms of y then substitute to equation 3.

  [tex]y=5000-x-z[/tex]

  [tex]y=5000-3y-6y[/tex]

Step 5: Solve for the value of y.

  [tex]y=5000-3y-6y[/tex]

  [tex]10y=5000[/tex]

  [tex]y=500[/tex]     Lerma spent 500 pesos

Step 6: Substitute the value of y to equation 1 to solve for the value of x.

  [tex]x=3y[/tex]

  [tex]x=3(500)[/tex]

  [tex]x=1500[/tex]    Diane spent 1500 pesos

Step 7: Substitute the value of y to equation 1 to solve for the value of z.

  [tex]3y=\frac{1}{2} z[/tex]

  [tex]3(500)=\frac{1}{2} z[/tex]

  [tex]1500=\frac{1}{2} z[/tex]

  [tex]z=3000[/tex]    Jenny spent 3000 pesos
