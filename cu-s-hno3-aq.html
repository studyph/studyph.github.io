Answer:

The balanced chemical equation for the reaction is:

2 Bi₂S₃(s) + 9 HNO₃(aq) → 2 Bi(NO₃)₃(aq) + 9 NO(g) + 6 S(s) + 6 H₂O(l)

Explanation:

The equation is balanced because there are the same number of atoms of each element on both sides of the equation.

On the reactant side, there are 2 Bi atoms, 6 S atoms, 18 H atoms, 9 N atoms, and 27 O atoms.

On the product side, there are 2 Bi atoms, 9 N atoms, 27 O atoms, 9 NO molecules, 6 S atoms, and 6 H₂O molecules.

To balance the equation, we need to adjust the coefficients in front of each molecule or compound. We start by balancing the elements that appear in only one molecule or compound on each side of the equation. In this case, we start with Bi and S on the reactant side, and NO on the product side. We add coefficients until the number of atoms of each element is the same on both sides.

2 Bi₂S₃(s) + 9 HNO₃(aq) → 2 Bi(NO₃)₃(aq) + 9 NO(g) + 6 S(s) + 6 H₂O(l)

This equation is now balanced because there are 2 Bi atoms, 6 S atoms, 18 H atoms, 9 N atoms, and 27 O atoms on both sides.

11. chemical reaction for  Cu (s) + 4 HNO₃ (aq) --> Cu ( NO₃ ) ₂ (aq) + 2 NO₂ (q) + 2 H₂O (1)​


Answer:

Copper is a reddish-brown metal, widely used in plumbing and electrical wiring; it is perhaps most familiar to people in the United States in the form of the penny.  (Although since 1983, pennies are actually made of zinc surrounded by a paper-thin copper foil to give them the traditional appearance of pennies.)  Copper is oxidized by concentrated nitric acid, HNO3, to produce Cu2+ ions; the nitric acid is reduced to nitrogen dioxide, a poisonous brown gas with an irritating odor:

Cu(s)  +  4HNO3(aq)  ——>  Cu(NO3)2(aq)  +  2NO2(g)  +  2H2O(l)

When the copper is first oxidized, the solution is very concentrated, and the Cu2+ product is initially coordinated to nitrate ions from the nitric acid, giving the solution first a green, and then a greenish-brownish color.  When the solution is diluted with water, water molecules displace the nitrate ions in the coordinate sites around the copper ions, causing the solution to change to a blue color.

In dilute nitric acid, the reaction produces nitric oxide, NO, instead:

3Cu(s)  +  8HNO3(aq)  ——>  3Cu(NO3)2(aq)  +  2NO(g)  +  4H2O(l)

 

In the following demonstration, a balled-up piece of thin copper wire is added to about 100 mL of concentrated nitric acid; once the copper is added the evolution of nitrogen dioxide occurs quickly.  Once all of the copper has reacted, the solution is diluted with distilled water, changing the solution from a dark brown to a pale blue color.

12. Sn2+(aq) + Cu2+(aq) ⟶ Sn4+(aq) + Cu+(aq)​


Answer:

Sn2+(aq) + 2Cu2+(aq) ⟶ Sn4+(aq) + 2Cu+(aq)

Explanation:

Balancing half-reactions

Sn^(2+) → Sn^(4+) + 2e

Cu^(2+) + e → Cu^(+)

Multiplying the second half-reaction by 2

Sn^(2+) → Sn^(4+) + 2e

2Cu^(2+) + 2e → 2Cu^(+)

Putting the coefficients to the original redox equation

Sn2+(aq) + 2Cu2+(aq) ⟶ Sn4+(aq) + 2Cu+(aq)

26. Exactly 18 grams of CuS is treated with excess dilute HNO3.3CuS + 6HNO3→ 3Cu(NO3)2 + 2NO + 3Sa. How many grams of Cu(NO3)2, is produced?b. How many grams of S is produced?c. What is the minimum number of grams of HNO3, required?d. How many liters of NO will be produced at STP?​


Solution (a)

Step 1: Calculate the molar masses of CuS and Cu(NO₃)₂.

For CuS

molar mass = (63.55 g/mol × 1) + (32.07 g/mol × 1)

molar mass = 95.62 g/mol

For Cu(NO₃)₂

molar mass = (63.55 g/mol × 1) + (14.01 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 6)

molar mass = 187.57 g/mol

Step 2: Determine the mole ratio needed.

mole ratio = 3 mol CuS : 3 mol Cu(NO₃)₂

Step 3: Calculate the mass of Cu(NO₃)₂ produced using the mole ratio.

[tex]\text{mass of Cu(NO₃)₂ = 18 g CuS} × \frac{\text{1 mol CuS}}{\text{95.62 g CuS}} × \frac{\text{3 mol Cu(NO₃)₂}}{\text{3 mol CuS}} × \frac{\text{187.57 g Cu(NO₃)₂}}{\text{1 mol Cu(NO₃)₂}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of Cu(NO₃)₂ = 35 g}}[/tex]

Solution (b)

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of S.

molar mass = 32.07 g/mol × 1

molar mass = 32.07 g/mol

Step 2: Determine the mole ratio needed.

mole ratio = 3 mol CuS : 3 mol S

Step 3: Calculate the mass of S produced by using the mole ratio.

[tex]\text{mass of S = 18 g CuS} × \frac{\text{1 mol CuS}}{\text{95.62 g CuS}} × \frac{\text{3 mol S}}{\text{3 mol CuS}} × \frac{\text{32.07 g S}}{\text{1 mol S}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of S = 6.0 g}}[/tex]

Solution (c)

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of HNO₃.

molar mass = (1.008 g/mol × 1) + (14.01 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 3)

molar mass = 63.018 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the mole ratio needed.

mole ratio = 3 mol CuS : 6 mol HNO₃

Step 3: Calculate the mass of HNO₃ required using the mole ratio.

[tex]\text{mass of HNO₃ = 18 g CuS} × \frac{\text{1 mol CuS}}{\text{95.62 g CuS}} × \frac{\text{6 mol HNO₃}}{\text{3 mol CuS}} × \frac{\text{63.018 g HNO₃}}{\text{1 mol HNO₃}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of HNO₃ = 24 g}}[/tex]

Solution (d)

Note: At STP, there are 22.414 L in a mole.

Step 1: Determine the mole ratio needed.

mole ratio = 3 mol CuS : 2 mol NO

Step 2: Calculate the volume of NO produced at STP.

[tex]\text{volume of NO = 18 g CuS} × \frac{\text{1 mol CuS}}{\text{95.62 g CuS}} × \frac{\text{2 mol NO}}{\text{3 mol CuS}} × \frac{\text{22.414 L NO}}{\text{1 mol NO}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{volume of NO = 2.8 L}}[/tex]
