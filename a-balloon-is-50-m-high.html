8. A ball is dropped from a balloon at a height of 195 m. if the balloon is rising 29.3 m/s. Find the highest point reached by the balloon.​


Explanation:

The highest point reached by the balloon can be found using the equation h = h0 + v0t - (1/2)gt^2, where h is the height of the balloon at time t, h0 is the initial height, v0 is the initial velocity, and g is the acceleration due to gravity.

Plugging in the given values, we have:

h = 195 + 29.3t - (1/2) x 9.81t^2

Solving for t, we get:

t = (29.3 + (sqrt[(29.3)^2 - 4(1/2)(-9.81)(195)]) )/(2(-9.81))

t = 4.43 seconds

Substituting this value in the equation, we get:

h = 195 + 29.3(4.43) - (1/2) x 9.81(4.43^2)

h = 271.25 m

Therefore, the highest point reached by the balloon is 271.25 m.

Answer:

To solve this problem, we need to use the kinematic equation:

h = vt - (1/2)gt^2

where h is the height, v is the initial velocity, t is the time, and g is the acceleration due to gravity.

At the highest point, the velocity of the balloon is zero, so we can find the time it takes to reach the highest point by setting v = 0:

0 = 29.3 - gt

t = 29.3/g

Now we can use this time to find the height reached by the balloon:

h = vt - (1/2)gt^2

h = 29.3(29.3/g) - (1/2)g(29.3/g)^2

h = 29.3^2/(2g)

Using the value of acceleration due to gravity g = 9.8 m/s^2, we get:

h = 29.3^2/(2*9.8) = 442.77 meters

Therefore, the highest point reached by the balloon is 442.77 meters.

30. 1. How much energy is present in a book sitting on top of a table if it has potential energy of 300 J? 2. A load on a desk, 1.0m high, was pushed with a speed of 5m/s from rest. How much energy is there in there in the load if it weighs 3.0 kg? 3. A balloon, after reaching the maximum height of 20 km, deflates and falls down the earth at a speed of 15 m/s. If the balloon weighs 50 mg, what is the amount of energy acting on the balloon?​


Answer:

1. The potential energy of an object is a measure of the energy it possesses due to its position or configuration. If a book sitting on top of a table has potential energy of 300 J, then that is the amount of energy it possesses. The book's kinetic energy (energy of motion) is 0 J because it is stationary.

2. The kinetic energy of an object is a measure of the energy it possesses due to its motion. To determine the kinetic energy of a load on a desk that was pushed with a speed of 5m/s from rest, we can use the formula:

KE = (1/2) * m * v^2

KE = (1/2) * 3.0 kg * (5m/s)^2

KE = (1/2) * 3.0 kg * 25 m^2/s^2

KE = 37.5 J

Therefore, the load has a kinetic energy of 37.5 J.

3. The energy acting on a falling object is equal to its gravitational potential energy. To determine the amount of energy acting on a balloon that weighs 50 mg and falls from a maximum height of 20 km at a speed of 15 m/s, we can use the formula:

GPE = m * g * h

GPE = (50 mg) * (9.81 m/s^2) * (20,000 m)

GPE = 98,100 J

Therefore, the amount of energy acting on the balloon is 98,100 J.
