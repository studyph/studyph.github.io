9. Pasagot po please How long will it take to “fully charge” the capacitor when switch is On? Show your solution.


CAPACITOR

After one time period, a capacitor charges to 63 percent of the source voltage that is charging it. A capacitor charges up to nearly 99 percent of its source voltage after 5 time periods. As a result, it's acceptable to state that a capacitor needs 5 time constants to charge up to the supply voltage.

The formula for calculating a capacitor's time constant is =RC. This figure represents the time it takes a capacitor to charge to 63 percent of the voltage that is charging it up (in seconds). The capacitor will be charged to over 99 percent of the voltage supplied after 5 time constants.

As a result, the formula for calculating how long a capacitor takes to charge is:

Time for a Capacitor to Charge= 5RC

For all intents and purposes, the capacitor will be charged up to very close to the source voltage after 5 time constants. A capacitor never fully charges to its source voltage's maximum voltage, but it comes near. The capacitor charges with infinite current in 0 time if the voltage source and capacitor are both "ideal" (zero internal resistance). Interesting. Furthermore, half of the energy absorbed from the source is stored in the capacitor, while the other half is radiated away.

When a charged ideal capacitor is subjected to an ideal short, it discharges to zero volts in zero time, and the energy is radiated away once more.
