4. ABCD is a parallelogram. If AB = 2x + a, BC = a, CD = x + 2a + 3 and DA = 6a - 2x, find its perimeter.


[tex] \large \mathcal{ANSWER:} [/tex]

[tex] \quad \large \boxed{28\textsf{ units}} [/tex]

[tex] \large \mathcal{SOLUTION:} [/tex]

[tex] \begin{array}{l} {\begin{array}{l} \textsf{Opposite sides of} \\ \textsf{the parallelogram} \\ \textsf{are congruent.}\end{array}} \begin{cases} AB = CD \\ \\ BC = DA \end{cases}\\ \\ \textsf{Solving for }x\textsf{ and } a, \\ \\ \begin{aligned} AB &= CD \\ 2x + a &= x + 2a + 3 \end{aligned} \\ (1) \quad x - a = 3 \\ \\ \begin{aligned} \quad BC &= DA \\ a &=6a - 2x \end{aligned} \\ (2)\quad 2x - 5a = 0 \\ \\ \textsf{By elimination,} \\\begin{aligned} 2x - 2a &= 6 \quad (1)\\ - \quad 2x - 5a &=0\quad (2)\\ \hline 3a &=6 \\ \implies a&=2 \\ \implies x &=a + 3 = 5 \end{aligned} \\ \\ \implies AB = CD = 2x + a = 2(5) + 2 = 12 \\ \implies BC = DA = a = 2 \\ \\ \therefore P = 2(12) + 2(2) = \boxed{28\textsf{ units}} \end{array} [/tex]

One of the properties of a parallelogram is that it has congruent opposite sides. In parallelogram ABCD, AB≅CD and BC≅AD.

We are given the sides of the parallelogram. We can substitute the values and equate the opposite sides.

[tex]AB = CD\\\\3x-5 = x+7\\\\3x-x= 7+5\\\\2x = 12\\\\x = 6[/tex]

We have x = 6.

[tex]AB = 3x - 5\\\\AB = 3(6)-5\\\\AB = 18-5\\\\AB = 13[/tex]

AB is 13 cm. Since AB = CD; CD is also 13 cm.

Let us do the other pair of opposite sides, BC and AD. They are also equal to each other.

[tex]BC = AD\\\\2y-7 = y+3\\\\2y-y = 7+3\\\\y = 10[/tex]

We have y = 10.

[tex]AD = y+3\\\\AD = 10 +3\\\\AD = 13[/tex]

AD is 13 cm. Since AD = BC; BC is also 13 cm.

The perimeter of a polygon is the sum of the measures of all its sides. To get the perimeter of parallelogram ABCD, we add the measures of all its sides.

[tex]Perimeter (ABCD) = AB+BC+CD+CD\\\\Perimeter (ABCD) = 13+13+13+13\\\\Perimeter (ABCD) = 52[/tex]

The perimeter of Parallelogram ABCD is 52 cm.

18. If AB - 5X + 1 cm, what value of X will make ABCD aparallelogram. Find the dimensions of the parallelogramand it's perimeter.​


To make ABCD a parallelogram, we need to have opposite sides that are parallel. This means AB = CD. Therefore:

AB - 5X + 1 = CD = AB

Solving for X:

-5X + 1 = 0

X = 0.2 cm

So, if X is 0.2 cm, then AB - 5X + 1 = AB - 5(0.2) + 1 = AB - 0.9 cm. Since AB = CD, then CD = AB - 0.9 cm.

The dimensions of the parallelogram are AB = CD = AB - 0.9 cm, and BC = AD = 1 cm.

The perimeter of the parallelogram is 2 times the sum of the lengths of AB and BC:

Perimeter = 2(AB + BC) = 2(AB + 1) = 2(AB - 0.9 + 1) = 2AB + 0.2 cm

Substituting AB = CD = AB - 0.9 cm:

Perimeter = 2(CD - 0.7) + 0.2 = 2CD - 1.2 cm

Therefore, if X is 0.2 cm, the dimensions of the parallelogram are AB = CD = AB - 0.9 cm, and BC = AD = 1 cm, and the perimeter of the parallelogram is 2CD - 1.2 cm.

27. In a parallelogram ABCD, AB = 3x - 3, BC = 6x and AD = 4x + 10. Find: 24. value of x = 25. perimeter of parallelogram ABCD​


In a parallelogram, opposite sides are equal in length. Therefore, we have:

AB = CD = 3x - 3

BC = AD = 6x

And, AD = 4x + 10

To find the value of x, we can equate AD to BC, and solve for x:

4x + 10 = 6x

Subtracting 4x from both sides, we get:

10 = 2x

Dividing both sides by 2, we get:

x = 5

Therefore, the value of x is 5.

To find the perimeter of the parallelogram, we add the lengths of all four sides:

Perimeter = AB + BC + CD + AD

Perimeter = (3x - 3) + 6x + (3x - 3) + (4x + 10)

Perimeter = 16x + 4

Substituting x = 5, we get:

Perimeter = 16(5) + 4

Perimeter = 84

Therefore, the perimeter of the parallelogram ABCD is 84 units.
