26. family emergency Preparedness in action plan. role of father, mother and sister and brother​


Answer:

In a family emergency preparedness action plan, each family member has a specific role to play in ensuring the safety and well-being of the entire household.

The father can take on the role of being the family's emergency coordinator, responsible for leading the family's response during an emergency situation. This may include organizing emergency supplies, creating evacuation plans, and communicating with local authorities.

The mother can play the role of caregiver, making sure that all family members are safe and have their basic needs met during the emergency. This may include managing first-aid supplies, ensuring that everyone has access to food and water, and comforting and supporting family members who may be feeling overwhelmed.

The sister and brother can play the role of support staff, helping the father and mother with various tasks as needed during an emergency. This may include assisting with evacuation, helping to gather emergency supplies, or providing support to other family members.

It's important for the entire family to work together during an emergency and to have clear communication and understanding of each person's role. By having a plan in place and working as a team, families can increase their chances of staying safe and minimizing the impact of an emergency situation.

WHAT TO DO BEFORE, DURING, AND AFTER AN EARTHQUAKE?

In the case of an earthquake, there are several things you may do to assist yourself. An earthquake may be broken down into three stages: before, during, and after. Understand what to do at each level.

BEFORE

Create an earthquake strategy for your household. Complete the steps on this checklist to prepare yourself and your house.

If your family is separated, decide how and where you will rejoin.

Choose a friend or relative who lives outside the region that separated family members may contact after the earthquake to report their location and condition.

Know where you can hide in each room: behind heavy tables, desks, or inside walls.

Keeping a list of emergency phone numbers handy is a good idea.

Heavy furniture, hanging plants, large paintings or mirrors should all be secured.

Liquids that are flammable or toxic should be kept in cabinets or on lower shelves.

Maintain an emergency kit.

DURING

If you're inside, stay still and seek shelter beneath a table, desk, or other strong piece of furniture.

Keep your back to windows and glass doors.

It's allowed to stand in a doorway that doesn't have a door.

Lie, kneel, or sit against a sound interior wall or corner away from windows, brick fires, glass walls, and other potentially dangerous elements.

Falling or flying things should be avoided at all costs.

Continue to stand where you are until the shaking stops. First, plan your course of action, and then take action.

If you're in a business facility, be aware of the evacuation routes. Take cover and don't move until the tremors have subsided.

If you're outside, find a place that's open and free of trees, buildings, walls, and electricity lines.

To stay balanced, lie down or hunch low.

If there isn't an open area available, go to the best available shelter.

If you're driving, come to a safe stop as quickly as possible. Stay in your car until the shaking subsides.

AFTER

Make sure there are no injuries. Assist with first aid. If the victims are gravely injured, do not transfer them unless they are in imminent danger. Unless there is a significant accident, fire, or other emergency, do not use the phone right away. Look for dangers.

Make sure you have enough food and drink.

Listen for emergency broadcasts/announcements, news updates, and directions over the radio.

For a faster recovery, collaborate with your neighbors. Maintain your composure and provide a helping hand to others.

Be on the lookout for the earthquake aftershocks.

Prepare for evacuation in the event that it becomes required due to unforeseen circumstances. Leave written remarks for other members of your family or for searchers.
