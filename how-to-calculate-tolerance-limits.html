3. Difference between Law of Tolerance and Limiting factor? ​


Answer:

Explanation:

The LAW OF TOLERANCE states that the ability of an organism to survive in a particular habitat or to have the size of a population in a community is determined by its ability to tolerate a range of physical and chemical factors present in the area. The LAW OF LIMITING FACTOR states that a single factor that is either too much or too little determines the growth rate of a species or the presence or absence of its population in a particular place even if all other required factors are in optimum levels.

DEFINITION:

Plants and animals that can tolerate a wide range of salinities are called euryhaline. These are the plants and animals most often found in the brackish waters of estuaries. There are far fewer euryhaline than stenohaline organisms because it requires a lot of energy to adapt to constantly changing salinities.
