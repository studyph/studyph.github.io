PRINTED SOURCE:

Book (One Author):
Author's Last Name, First Name. Title of Book. Place of Publication: Publisher, Date of Publication.
Example:
Smith, John. The Power of Positive Thinking. New York: Simon & Schuster, 1952.

Book (Two Authors):
Author 1's Last Name, First Name Middle Initial., and Author 2's First Name Middle Initial. Last Name. Title of Book. Place of Publication: Publisher, Date of Publication.
Example:
Johnson, Mary K., and David M. Smith. The History of Modern Art. New York: Pearson, 2011.

Book (Three or More Authors):
Author 1's Last Name, First Name Middle Initial., Author 2's First Name Middle Initial. Last Name, Author 3's First Name Middle Initial. Last Name, et al. Title of Book. Place of Publication: Publisher, Date of Publication.
Example:
Brown, Susan T., David J. Johnson, Sarah L. Lee, et al. The Psychology of Human Memory. London: Psychology Press, 2016.

INTERNET SOURCE:
4. Scholarly Journal Article:
Author's Last Name, First Name Middle Initial. Initial. (Date of Publication). Title of Article. Title of Journal, volume number(issue number), page numbers. URL. (Date of Article Access).

Example:
Garcia, Maria C. J. (2019). The Effects of Music Therapy on Depression and Anxiety in Cancer Patients. Journal of Music Therapy, 56(1), 23-38. https://doi.org/10.1093/jmt/thy012. (Accessed on June 25, 2021).

Encyclopedia Article:
Author's Last Name, First Name Middle Initial. Initial. (Date of Publication). Title of Article. Title of Encyclopedia. URL. (Date of Access).
Example:
Brown, Michael L. (2018). African American History. Encyclopedia Britannica. https://www.britannica.com/topic/African-American-history. (Accessed on June 25, 2021).
