28. Two angles are complementary and one of them is four times as big as the other. What is the size of the larger of the two angles? * a. 67 ½ degrees b. 72 degrees c. 80 degrees d. 144 degrees


Answer:

b. 72 degrees

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x and y be the two angles.

Two angles are complementary: x + y = 90

One of them is four times as big as the other: Let y be four times bigger than x (y = 4x)

So, since y = 4x, you can substitute that to the previous equation (x + y = 90) and this will give you the equation x + 4x = 90 --> 5x = 90 (combined like terms). Divide both sides by 5 and you will get 18 as the value of x.

Now, substitute 18 to y (the bigger angle): y = 4x = 4(18) = 72
