8. Using the Health Promotion Model , assess yourself of health promotion behaviors that you ought to do but do not, indicate the perceived benefits of the behavior, perceived barriers, interpersonal /situational influences and your plan of action towards the commitment 


Answer:

According to the Health Promotion Model, health promotion behaviors are influenced by a person's perceived benefits and barriers to the behavior, as well as interpersonal and situational influences. To assess myself in terms of health promotion behaviors that I ought to do but do not, I would first need to identify a specific behavior that falls into this category. For the purposes of this example, let's say that I have identified the behavior of exercising regularly as something that I ought to do but currently do not.

Perceived benefits: Some of the perceived benefits of exercising regularly for me might include improved physical health and fitness, reduced risk of chronic diseases, increased energy and stamina, and improved mental health and wellbeing.

Perceived barriers: Some of the perceived barriers that prevent me from exercising regularly might include a lack of time, lack of access to appropriate facilities or equipment, lack of motivation or support, and competing priorities or demands.

Interpersonal / situational influences: Some of the interpersonal and situational influences that might affect my commitment to exercising regularly might include the attitudes and behaviors of my friends, family, and peers, as well as the physical and social environment in which I live and work.

23. is Observing animal behavior as indicator of environmental condition biotechnology why or why not?


Answer:

A bioindicator is any species (an indicator species) or group of species whose function, population, or status can reveal the qualitative status of the environment. For example, copepods and other small water crustaceans that are present in many water bodies can be monitored for changes (biochemical, physiological, or behavioural) that may indicate a problem within their ecosystem. Bioindicators can tell us about the cumulative effects of different pollutants in the ecosystem and about how long a problem may have been present, which physical and chemical testing cannot.

Biological indicators can be described as the introduction of a highly resistant microorganisms to a given environment before sterilization, tests are conducted to measure the effectiveness of the sterilization processes. As biological indicators use highly resistant microorganisms, any sterilization process that renders them inactive will have also killed off more common, weaker pathogens.
