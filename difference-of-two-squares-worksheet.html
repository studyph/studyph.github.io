2. Search for an example of a worksheet. Explain the different columns included in the worksheet.


Accounting Worksheet Columns Included:

-Unadjusted Trial Balance

-Adjusting Entries

-Adjusted Trial Balance

-Income Statement

-Balance Sheet

Explanations:

Unadjusted Trial Balance

A column contains all the company’s assets, liability, expenses, and revenue accounts, which are used in a concerned year.

Adjusting Entries

All the accounts of the company, which requires the passing of the adjustment entry will get listed in the adjustment column.

Adjusted Trial Balance

The adjusted trial balance will be prepared by combining the entries of the previous two columns, Unadjusted trial balance, and adjustments.

Income Statement

A column contains the values concerning the expenses and revenue accounts only. In this case, if the value of total revenue exceeds the expense column, then the difference will be the net income of the year of the company as it is earning more revenues in the year than what it is spending on its expenses.

Balance Sheet

A column contains the values with respect to the assets, liabilities, and owner’s capital only.

Find:   [tex]c=?[/tex]

Formula:   [tex]c^2=a^2+b^2[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]c^2=a^2+b^2\\c^2=(10)^2+(10)^2\\c^2=100+100\\c^2=200\\\sqrt{c^2}=\sqrt{200}\\c=\sqrt{(100)(2)}\\c=\sqrt{(10)^2(2)}\\\boxed{\boldsymbol{c=10\sqrt{2}}}[/tex]

18. Give 5 differences of workbook and worksheet​


Answer:

1. The workbook is the entire book, while the worksheet is a single page within that book.

2. A workbook can contain, at minimum, one worksheet, and up to a very large number of worksheets that is only limited by the available memory on your computer.

3. Worksheets can be added, deleted or renamed within the workbook.

4. You can also rename a workbook without affecting the name of any of the worksheets.

5. You can toggle between worksheets within your workbook by clicking the sheet tabs at the bottom of the window.
