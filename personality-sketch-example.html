24. Disscuss and illustrate the two types of sketch namely: A. Rough Sketch B. Final Sketch​


Answer:

A. Rough Sketch- is the first full visual prototype. They're produced quickly and without much concern for composition or even aesthetics. It's far easier in this early stage to move stuff around and switch things out, than it is later on down the line when composition and details have begun to tighten out.

B. Final Sketch- Finished product of the rough sketch. • Prepared for courtroom presentation and. often will not show all measurements and distances originally recorded on the rough sketch.

GRETA THUNBERG

Greta Thunberg is a Swedish environmental activist. Thunberg rose to prominence after protesting outside Sweden's parliament in 2018, at the age of 15. She carried a placard that read "School Strike for Climate" in order to put pressure on the government to fulfill carbon emission targets. Her tiny movement had a worldwide impact, motivating hundreds of young people to organize their own strikes.

By December 2018, more than 20,000 pupils from the United Kingdom to Japan had joined her in protesting by skipping school.

The teenage activist is known to walk the talk. She persuaded her parents to adopt a lifestyle that would reduce their carbon footprints such as using boats or ships to travel instead of using planes.

She is a brave girl who scolded leaders and kept calling them out for their inaction and their gravitation towards billionaires whose lifestyles and companies contribute to the environmental problems.

Thunberg traveled across the Atlantic on a boat in 2019 to attend a United Nations climate meeting in New York.

She passionately scolded world leaders that they were not doing enough in what is likely her most memorable address.

"You all come to us young people seeking optimism; how can you? Your meaningless words have stolen my hopes and my youth," she added.

Thunberg has Asperger syndrome, a developmental disability that he describes as a blessing. She stated that being unique may be a "superpower."

Sure, here are the character sketches of a person who is spiritual but not religious, and a person who is religious but not spiritual:

Spiritual but not Religious:

This person is someone who believes in a higher power or energy that exists beyond what we can see and touch. They may practice meditation, yoga, or other forms of mindfulness to connect with this energy and cultivate a sense of inner peace and harmony. They are not tied to any specific religion or belief system, and instead, they draw from various spiritual practices and philosophies to create their own unique spiritual path. They may value things like compassion, empathy, and personal growth, and seek to live their life in a way that aligns with these values. They may be seen as open-minded and accepting of others' beliefs and perspectives.

Religious but not Spiritual:

This person is someone who follows a specific religion and its teachings but may not have a strong connection to a higher power or spiritual energy. They may attend religious services regularly and participate in religious rituals and practices, but these activities may not necessarily bring them a sense of inner peace or personal growth. They may focus more on following religious rules and traditions, rather than seeking to understand the deeper spiritual meanings behind them. They may be seen as conservative or traditional in their beliefs and may be less open to alternative perspectives or beliefs outside of their own religion.
