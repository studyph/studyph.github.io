10. a rope is divided in to a ratio of 1:3:5. if the length of the rope is 180cm what is the length of the shortest one


✒️RATIO

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM}:} [/tex]

A rope is divided in to a ratio of 1:3:5. if the length of the rope is 180cm what is the length of the shortest one?

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \Large \:\: \rm{20 \: centimeters} [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

» Let x be multiplied to each part of the ratio having a total of 180. Find x.

[tex] 1x + 3x + 5x = 180 [/tex]

[tex] 9x = 180 [/tex]

[tex] \frac{9x}{9} = \frac{180}{9} \\ [/tex]

[tex] x = 20 [/tex]

» The value of x is 20. Multiply it to the smallest part of the ratio to find the length of the shortest piece.

[tex] \rm Shortest = \mathcal{1x } [/tex]

[tex] \rm Shortest = 1(20) [/tex]

[tex] \rm Shortest = 20 [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The length of the shortest piece of rope is 20 cm.
