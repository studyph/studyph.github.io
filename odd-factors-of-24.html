29. The sum of all odd integers between, 6 and 24 is ?


Answer:

The sum of all odd integers between 6 and 24 is 135.

Step-by-step explanation:

A Sequence is an ordered collection of objects. When we say "ordered" this means that it follows a ranking pattern, having the first term, second term, third term, and so on.

A series is the sum of the given sequence. Simply add all the terms in a sequence, the result is the series.

Types of Sequence

1. Arithmetic Sequence

2. Geometric Sequence

Types of Series

1. Arithmetic Series

2. Geometric Series

We can list all the odd numbers between 6 and 24.

     [tex]7[/tex], [tex]9[/tex], [tex]11[/tex], [tex]13[/tex], [tex]15[/tex], [tex]17[/tex], [tex]19[/tex], [tex]21[/tex], [tex]23[/tex]

By looking at the sequence, we have an arithmetic sequence. This is because we have a common difference. The common difference is the difference between each number in the sequence. In this case, we have a common difference of 2.

To get the sum, we can simply add all those numbers or we can use the formula for the arithmetic series given by [tex]S_n=\frac{n(a_1+a_n)}{2}[/tex] where [tex]n[/tex] is the number of terms, [tex]a_1[/tex] is the first term, and [tex]a_n[/tex] is the last term. Use the values of [tex]n=9[/tex], [tex]a_1=7[/tex], and [tex]a_n=23[/tex] into the formula, then simplify.

                       

                       [tex]7+9+11+13+15+17+19+21+23&=135[/tex]

                                                          or

                                       [tex]\begin{aligned}S_9&=\frac{9(7+23)}{2}\\S_9&=\frac{9(30)}{2}\\S_9&=\frac{270}{2}\\S_9&=135\end{aligned}[/tex]

Thus, the sum of all odd integers between 6 and 24 is 135.
