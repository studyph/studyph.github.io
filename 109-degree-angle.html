3. A plane is 109 mi north and 162 mi east of an airport. Find x, the angle the pilot should turn in order to fly directly to the airport. Round your answer to the nearest tenth of a degree.​


TRIGONOMETRY

===========================================

[tex]\large\underline{\sf{Problem:}}[/tex]

A plane is 109 mi north and 162 mi east of an airport. Find x, the angle the pilot should turn in order to fly directly to the airport. Round your answer to the nearest tenth of a degree.

===========================================

[tex]\large\underline{\sf{Answer:}}[/tex]

[tex] \qquad\qquad \large \boxed{ \bold{ \: x = 33.9^{\circ} \: (nearest \: tenth) \: }} \\ [/tex]

===========================================

[tex]\large\underline{\sf{Solution:}}[/tex]

Trigonometric ratios

[tex]\boxed{ \bold{ \: \sin(\theta)=\dfrac{O}{H}\quad\cos(\theta)=\dfrac{A}{H}\quad\tan(\theta)=\dfrac{O}{A} \: }} \: \: \bigstar[/tex]

where :

θ is the angle.O is the side opposite the angle.A is the side adjacent the angle.H is the hypotenuse (the side opposite the right angle).

To find the value of x in the given diagram use the tan ratio.

From inspection of the given diagram :

θ = xO = 109A = 162

Substitute the given values into the tan ratio and solve for x :

[tex] \qquad \sf\implies \tan(x)=\dfrac{\mathfrak{109}}{\mathfrak{162}} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \sf\implies x=\tan^{\mathfrak{-1}}\left(\dfrac{\mathfrak{109}}{\mathfrak{162}}\right) \\ [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \sf\implies x=\mathfrak{33.934224...} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \sf\implies \underline{\boxed{\bold{\pmb{x=\mathfrak{33.9}^{\circ}\; (nearest\;tenth)}}}} \:\: \green{✓} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \therefore[/tex] The value of x to the nearest tenth is 33.9°.
