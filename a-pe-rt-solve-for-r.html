Answer:

The formula for compound interest is A = P(1 + r/100)^t, where A is the final amount, P is the principal, r is the interest rate, and t is the time in years. In this case, we are given that the interest rate r is 8 3/4%, which we can write as 8.75% to make it easier to use in the formula. We are also given that the time t is 16 months, which we can convert to years by dividing by 12, so t = 16/12 = 1.3333 years. Plugging these values into the formula, we get:

A = 672,000 * (1 + 0.0875/100)^1.3333

To compute this, we first calculate (1 + 0.0875/100) = 1.008750, then raise it to the power of 1.3333 to get 1.023422. Finally, we multiply this by the principal to get:

A = 672,000 * 1.023422 = 685,598.4

Therefore, the value of the missing quantity A in the given formula is approximately 685,598.4.

What Is Gravitational Potential Energy

Potential Energy is the stored energy of an object given its position relative to a body. Gravitational potential energy is one type of potential energy and is equal to the product of the object's mass (m), the acceleration caused by gravity (g), and the object's height (h) as distance from the surface of the ground (the body).

In this example, a 3 kilogram mass, at a height of 5 meters, while acted on by Earth's gravity would have 147.15 Joules of potential energy, PE = 3kg * 9.81 m/s2 * 5m = 147.15 J.

9.81 meters per second squared (or more accurately 9.80665 m/s2) is widely accepted among scientists as a working average value for Earth's gravitational pull. This figure is based on a measure of gravity at sea level at a latitude of 45°.

At higher altitudes, gravity decreases slightly.

The effect of latitude on gravitational force is relevant as gravity increases with increasing distance from the Equator. At the Equator, the Earth's gravity is 9.780 m/s2 and at the poles it is 9.832 m/s2 (source: CRC Handbook of Chemistry and Physics).
