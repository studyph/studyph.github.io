2. 5 problems of direct variation and 5 problem of inverse variation. show your solution​


Step-by-step explanation:

For problems of Direct Variation:

1. If a car travels 50 miles per hour, how long will it take to travel 200 miles?

Solution: Let t be the time it takes to travel 200 miles. Then,

50/1 = 200/t

50t = 200

t = 4

Therefore, it will take 4 hours to travel 200 miles.

2. If a machine can produce 20 units in 2 hours, how many units can it produce in 5 hours?

Solution: Let u be the number of units the machine can produce in 5 hours. Then,

20/2 = u/5

2u = 100

u = 50

Therefore, the machine can produce 50 units in 5 hours.

3. The cost of 5 pounds of apples is $7.50. What is the cost of 10 pounds of apples?

Solution: Let c be the cost of 10 pounds of apples. Then,

7.5/5 = c/10

1.5 = c/10

c = 15

Therefore, the cost of 10 pounds of apples is $15.

4. If 3 workers can finish a job in 8 days, how many workers are needed to finish the same job in 4 days?

Solution: Let n be the number of workers needed to finish the job in 4 days. Then,

3/8 = n/4

n = 6

Therefore, 6 workers are needed to finish the job in 4 days.

5. If a recipe calls for 2 cups of flour to make 24 cookies, how many cups of flour are needed to make 48 cookies?

Solution: Let c be the number of cups of flour needed to make 48 cookies. Then,

2/24 = c/48

c = 4

Therefore, 4 cups of flour are needed to make 48 cookies.

For problems of Inverse Variation:

1. If 4 workers can build a house in 120 days, how long will it take 8 workers to build the same house?

Solution: Let t be the time it takes 8 workers to build the house. Then,

4 x 120 = 8t

480 = 8t

t = 60

Therefore, it will take 60 days for 8 workers to build the house.

2. If 6 pipes can fill a tank in 4 hours, how many hours will it take 4 pipes to fill the same tank?

Solution: Let t be the time it takes 4 pipes to fill the tank. Then,

6 x 4 = 4t

24 = 4t

t = 6

Therefore, it will take 6 hours for 4 pipes to fill the tank.

3. If the distance between two cities is 120 miles and a car travels at an average speed of 60 miles per hour, how many hours will it take to travel between the cities?

Solution: Let t be the time it takes to travel between the cities. Then,

t = 120/60

t = 2

Therefore, it will take 2 hours to travel between the cities.

4. If it takes 4 hours to paint a house with 2 painters, how long will it take 1 painter to paint the same house?

Solution: Let t be the time it takes 1 painter to paint the house. Then,

2 x 4 = 1t

8 = t

Therefore, it will take 8 hours for 1 painter to paint the house.

5. If the price of 6 cans of soda is $4.50, what is the price of 10 cans of soda?

Solution: Let p be the price of 10 cans of soda. Then,

6p = 4.5

p = 0.75

Therefore, the price of 10 cans of soda is $0.75.


3. I need, DIRECT VARIATION, INVERSE VARIATION, JOINT VARIATION,and COMBINED VARIATION...THE FORMULAS PROBLEM and SOLUTIONS ​


Answer:

What are the 4 types of variation?

Examples of types of variation include direct, inverse, joint, and combined variation.

Step-by-step explanation:

When you start studying algebra, you will also study how two (or more) variables can relate to each other specifically. The cases you’ll study are:

Direct Variation, where one variable is a constant multiple of another. For example, the number of dollars I make varies directly (or varies proportionally) to the number of hours I work. Or, the perimeter of a square varies directly with the length of a side of the square.

Inverse or Indirect Variation, where when one of the variables increases, the other one decreases (their product is constant). For example, the temperature in my house varies indirectly (same or inversely) with the amount of time the air conditioning is running. Or, the number of people I invite to my bowling party varies inversely with the number of games they might get to play (or you can say is proportional to the inverse of).

Joint Variation, where at least two variables are related directly. For example, the area of a triangle is jointly related to both its height and base.

Combined Variation, which involves a combination of direct or joint variation, and indirect variation. For example, the average number of phone calls per day between two cities has found to be jointly proportional to the populations of the cities, and inversely proportional to the square of the distance between the two cities.

Partial (Direct) Variation, where two variables are related by a formula, such as the formula for a straight line (with a non-zero y-intercept). For example, the total cost of my phone bill consists of a fixed cost per month, and also a charge per minute.

25. how do you know if a situation or problem is direct or inverse variation?​


Answer:

Direct & Inverse Variation

Direct variation describes a simple relationship between two variables . We say y varies directly with x (or as x , in some textbooks) if:

y=kx

for some constant k .

This means that as x increases, y increases and as x decreases, y decreases—and that the ratio between them always stays the same.

The graph of the direct variation equation is a straight line through the origin.

for 3 different values of k

Inverse variation describes another kind of relationship. We say y varies inversely with x (or as x , in some textbooks) if :

xy=k ,

or, equivalently,

y=kx

for some constant k .

This means that as x increases, y decreases and as x decreases, y increases.

The graph of the inverse variation equation is a hyperbola .
