1. Seismograph

2. Seismograms contain information that can be used to determine how strong an earthquake was, how long it lasted, and how far away it was. Modern seismometers record ground motions using electronic motion detectors. The data are then kept digitally on a computer. These seismograms show the arrival of P-waves and S-waves.

3. Seismic waves travel through different materials inside the Earth at different speeds. They also get bent when they pass from one kind of material to another. By observing the path and speed of seismic wave, scientists can find out the types and arrangement of the materials through which it passed.
