A balloon contains 4L of air at a pressure of 1.5 atm. What pressure in torr must be applied to compress the air to a volume of 1L with no temperature change?

Solution :-Boyle's Law

[tex] \qquad \quad \sf \implies \boxed{\bold{ \: \: P_1 \cdot V_1=P_2 \cdot V_2 \: \: }}\: \: \bigstar \\ [/tex]

where:

[tex] \qquad \sf \to{V_1 = Initial \: Volume}[/tex]

[tex] \qquad \sf \to\sf V_2 = Final \: Volume[/tex]

[tex] \qquad \sf \to\sf P_1 = Initial \: Pressure \\ [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \sf \to\sf P_2 = Final \: Pressure \\ [/tex]

Given :

[tex] \qquad \qquad\sf \mapsto V_1 = 4 L[/tex]

[tex] \qquad \qquad \mapsto\sf V_2 = 1 L[/tex]

[tex] \qquad \qquad \mapsto\sf P_1= 1.5 \: atm \\ [/tex]

Substituting the given values into the formula :

[tex]\quad\implies \sf 1.5 \cdot 4=P_2 \cdot 1 \\ [/tex]

[tex]\quad\implies \sf P_2=6\:atm \\ [/tex]

To convert atm to Torr, multiply atm by 760:

[tex]\qquad\quad\Rrightarrow\sf P_2=6\cdot 760= \bold{ \red{4560\:Torr}}[/tex]

[tex] \\ [/tex]

[tex] \therefore[/tex] The pressure in torr that must be applied to compress the air to a volume of 1L with no temperature change is 4560 Torr.

13. There are supplied 400 kj per cycle to an ideal diesel engine operating on 500 g air, suction pressure, and temperature of 100 kpa and 50c. at the end of compression, the pressure rises to 4000 kpa. determine the compression ratio


Answer:

To determine the compression ratio, we need to find the ratio of the volume of the cylinder during the compression stroke to the volume of the cylinder during the intake stroke.

The volume of the cylinder during the intake stroke can be calculated using the ideal gas law:

PV = nRT

Where:

P = pressure (100 kPa)

V = volume of the cylinder during the intake stroke

n = number of moles of air (0.005 moles, since the mass of air is 500 g and the molar mass of air is 28.97 g/mol)

R = ideal gas constant (8.31 J/mol*K)

T = temperature (50 C, or 323 K)

Solving for V, we get:

V = (nRT)/P = (0.0058.31323)/100 = 0.0136 m^3

The volume of the cylinder during the compression stroke can be calculated in a similar manner, using the pressure at the end of the compression stroke (4000 kPa) instead of the suction pressure:

V = (nRT)/P = (0.0058.31323)/4000 = 0.00034 m^3

The compression ratio is then:

V_compression/V_intake = (0.00034 m^3)/(0.0136 m^3) = 2.5

So the compression ratio is 2.5.

16. One liter of an air sample at 250 degrees Celsius and 1 atm is compressed to 3 mL at a pressure of 1000 atm. Determine the temperature of the air sample in kelvins.​


Given:

[tex]P_{1} = \text{1 atm}[/tex]

[tex]T_{1} = \text{250°C + 273 = 523 K}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{1 L = 1000 mL}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{1000 atm}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{3 mL}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]T_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\frac{P_{1}V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \frac{P_{2}V_{2}}{T_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = T_{1} × \frac{P_{2}}{P_{1}} × \frac{V_{2}}{V_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \text{523 K} × \frac{\text{1000 atm}}{\text{1 atm}} × \frac{\text{3 mL}}{\text{1000 mL}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{T_{2} = \text{1569 K}}[/tex]

26. what is the use of a compressed air​


Answer:

Compressed Air - Compressed air is air kept under a pressure that is greater than atmospheric pressure. Compressed air is an important medium for transfer of energy in industrial processes, and is used for power tools such as air hammers, drills, wrenches and others, as well as to atomize paint, to operate air cylinders for automation, and can also be used to propel vehicles. Brakes applied by compressed air made large railway trains safer and more efficient to operate. Compressed air brakes are also found on large highway vehicles.

Compressed air is used as a breathing gas by underwater divers. It may be carried by the diver in a high pressure diving cylinder, or supplied from the surface at lower pressure through an air line or diver's umbilical. Similar arrangements are used in breathing apparatus used by firefighters, mine rescue workers and industrial workers in hazardous atmospheres.

In Europe, 10 percent of all industrial electricity consumption is to produce compressed air—amounting to 80 terawatt hours consumption per year.

An early major application of compressed air was in the drilling of the Mont Cenis Tunnel in Italy and France in 1861, where a 600 kPa (87 psi) compressed air plant provided power to pneumatic drills, increasing productivity greatly over previous manual drilling methods. Compressed air drills were applied at mines in the United States in the 1870s. George Westinghouse invented air brakes for trains starting in 1869; these brakes considerably improved the safety of rail operations.
