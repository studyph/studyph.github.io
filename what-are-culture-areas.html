8. The Chavin influence Andean cultures in what areas? List 2


Answer:

The Chavín culture is an extinct, pre-Columbian civilization, developed in the northern Andean highlands of Peru around 1000 BCE. It extended its influence to other civilizations along the Peruvian coast. The Chavín people (whose name for themselves is unknown) were located in the Mosna Valley where the Mosna and Huachecsa rivers merge. This area is 3,150 metres (10,330 ft) above sea level and encompasses the quechua, suni, and puna life zones In the periodization of pre-Columbian Peru, the Chavín is the main culture of the Early Horizon period in highland Peru, characterized by the intensification of the religious cult, the appearance of ceramics closely related to the ceremonial centers, the improvement of agricultural techniques and the development of metallurgy and textiles.

The best-known archaeological site for the Chavín culture is Chavín de Huántar, located in the Andean highlands of the present-day Ancash Region. Although Chavín de Huántar may or may not have been the center or birthplace of the Chavín culture, it was of great importance.

20. The Chavin influence Andean cultures in what areas? List 2


Answer:

Here are two areas in which the Chavin influenced Andean cultures:

Art and architecture: The Chavin are known for their distinctive artistic style, which is characterized by complex geometric patterns and depiction of animal and supernatural motifs. This style was influential in the development of the art and architecture of subsequent Andean cultures, such as the Moche, Nazca, and Inca.Religion: The Chavin had a complex religious system that centered around a powerful deity known as the "Staff God." This deity was depicted in Chavin art as a human figure with the head of a jaguar, and was associated with natural phenomena such as lightning and thunder. The Chavin religion influenced the development of later Andean religions, including the Inca religion.

It is worth noting that the Chavin influence on Andean cultures extended beyond these two areas, and the Chavin civilization had a significant impact on the development of Andean culture as a whole.

Explanation:

The Chavin culture was a pre-Columbian civilization that flourished in the Andes region of South America from around 1000 BCE to 200 BCE. The Chavin are known for their sophisticated art, architecture, and religion, which had a lasting influence on the development of Andean cultures.
