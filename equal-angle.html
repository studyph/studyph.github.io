We may construct three linked statements from an "if p, then q" if-then statement.

17. In △ABC, angle A is equal to 70° and angle B is equal to 60° and angle C is equal to 8x + 2. What is the value of x? *


Answer:

6°

Explanation:

△ABC is a triangle so the sum of the measure of its interior angles is 180°.

So, ∠A + ∠B +∠C = 180°

Wherein ∠A = 70°; ∠B = 60°; ∠C = 8x + 2

Solution:

70 + 60 + 8x + 2 = 180°

Combining like terms, we will have:

132 + 8x  = 180°

Transpose 132 to the other side.

8x = 180 - 132

8x = 48

Divide 8 on both sides.

x = 6°

Therefore, the value of x is 6°.

22. In △ABC, angle A is equal to 68° and angle B is equal to 23°. What is the measurement of angle C? *


✏️TRIANGLE

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM:}}[/tex]

In ΔABC, angle A is equal to 68° and angle B is equal to 23°. What is the measurement of angle C?

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER:}}[/tex]

[tex]\qquad\LARGE\rm» \:\: \green{m\angle{C}=89\degree}[/tex]

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION:}}[/tex]

- Since ΔABC is the triangle, then the sum of the measures is 180 degrees.

[tex]m\angle{A} + m\angle{B} + m\angle{C} =180 \degree[/tex]

- Substitute the values to find the measure of angle C.

[tex]68 \degree + 23 \degree + m\angle{C} =180 \degree[/tex]

[tex]91\degree + m\angle{C} =180 \degree[/tex]

[tex]m\angle{C} =180 \degree - 91 \degree[/tex]

[tex]m\angle{C} = 89\degree[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The measure of angle C is 89 degrees.
