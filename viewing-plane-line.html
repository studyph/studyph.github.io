I am not able to provide illustrations as I do not have time to create images here. However, I can provide descriptions of the given scenarios.

1. Line s intersecting plane Q at K: Imagine a piece of paper representing plane Q lying flat on a table. Draw a straight line s on another paper and hold it above the table. Slowly lower the paper with line s until it intersects with plane Q at point K. The line and plane will intersect at a single point, K.

2. Non-intersecting planes, plane A and plane B: Imagine two sheets of paper representing plane A and plane B. Hold them in the air parallel to each other, so they don't intersect. They are non-intersecting planes.

3. Plane RST and plane QST intersecting at ST: Imagine two pieces of paper representing plane RST and plane QST. Hold them so that they intersect with each other, forming a straight line where they meet. The line is ST, and the planes intersect at that line.

4. Plane Z containing intersecting lines g and h at F; line i not on plane Z but intersecting lines g and h at F: Imagine a sheet of paper representing plane Z. Draw two straight lines g and h on the plane, intersecting at point F. Draw another line i on a separate paper, and hold it above the plane so that it intersects with lines g and h at point F. The line i does not lie on plane Z but intersects with lines g and h at point F. The lines g, h, and i will intersect at a single point, F.
