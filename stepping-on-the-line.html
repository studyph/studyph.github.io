19. bisecting a line segment with a ruler (only 2 steps NO COMPASS in the steps)​


Answer:To bisect a line segment with a ruler in two steps:

Step 1: Draw a straight line segment AB using your ruler.Step 2: Place the ruler so that one end is at point A and the other end is at point B. Draw a line that passes through the midpoint of AB. This line will bisect the line segment AB into two equal parts.

20. How to answer Forms of Equation of a Line? Step by step.


Answer:

The equation, y = mx + b, is in slope-intercept form for the equation of a line. When an equation is in this form, the slope of the line is given by m and the y-intercept is located at b. For example, a line with the equation y = 2x + 4 has a slope of 2 and a y-intercept of 4.

30. 1. What are the steps in constructing a line segment?Step 1:Step 2Step 32. What are the steps in constructing perpendicular bisector?Step 1Step 2Step 3Step 43. Construct a line through a given point A that is parallel to a given line B​


Answer:

1.)

Step 1 -Place the compass at one end of line segment.

Step 2 -Adjust the compass to slightly longer than half the line segment length.

Step 3 -Keeping the same compass width, draw arcs from other end of line.

Step 4 -Place ruler where the arcs cross, and draw the line segment.

Step 5-Draw arcs above and below the line.

2.)

Step 1 - Open the compass more than half of the distance between A and B, and scribe arcs of the same radius centered at A and B

Step 2 - Call the two points where these two arcs meet C and D. Draw the line between C and D.

Step 3 -CD is the perpendicular bisector of the line segment AB.

Step 4 - Proof.
