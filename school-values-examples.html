20. Give one example of how each agency has socialized you in terms of values, norms, status and roles? choices: 1.family 2.friend 3.mass media 4.religion 5.school​


1. Family - Families are the primary agents of socialization. They are responsible for teaching children values, norms, and appropriate behavior. For example, families may socialize children to have a strong work ethic, to respect authority figures, and to prioritize family values over individual desires. Families also impart status and roles within the family structure, such as the roles of parent and child or the status of being the oldest or youngest child.

2. Friends - Friends can also be a source of socialization, especially during adolescence when peer relationships become increasingly important. Friends may influence an individual's values, norms, and behavior, such as encouraging risk-taking behaviors or supporting academic success. Friends may also establish status and roles within the social group, such as the role of leader or the status of being popular.

3. Mass media - Mass media, including television, movies, and social media, can also shape an individual's values, norms, and behavior. For example, mass media can normalize certain behaviors or attitudes, such as the acceptance of violence or the importance of material possessions. Mass media can also create status and roles within the media industry, such as the role of celebrity or the status of being a successful media mogul.

4. Religion - Religious institutions can socialize individuals by imparting values, norms, and behavior based on religious beliefs. For example, religions may promote kindness, compassion, and humility as important values, and may encourage adherents to engage in certain behaviors, such as regular prayer or community service. Religions may also establish status and roles within the religious hierarchy, such as the role of priest or the status of being a devout follower.

5. School - Schools are responsible for socializing children and adolescents to be responsible citizens and productive members of society. Schools may teach values such as honesty, respect, and hard work, and may encourage certain behaviors such as academic achievement and civic engagement. Schools also establish status and roles within the academic community, such as the role of teacher or the status of being a successful student.

Answer:

Sure, I can provide an example for each agency:

1. Family: My family has socialized me to value honesty, hard work, and respect for elders. They have also taught me the norms of our family culture, such as celebrating certain holidays and traditions, and have assigned me certain roles within the family, such as being the eldest child and taking on responsibilities to help care for my younger siblings.

2. Friend: My friends have socialized me to value loyalty, trust, and communication. They have taught me the norms of our friend group, such as how we communicate and interact with each other, and have assigned me certain roles within the group, such as being the mediator in disagreements.

3. Mass media: Mass media has socialized me to value consumerism, individualism, and entertainment. It has taught me the norms of popular culture, such as what is considered attractive, fashionable, or trendy, and has assigned me roles as a consumer of products and media.

It has taught me the norms of my religious community, such as participating in certain rituals and practices, and has assigned me roles within the community, such as volunteering or serving as a leader in the congregation.

5. School: School has socialized me to value achievement, discipline, and conformity. It has taught me the norms of academic culture, such as the importance of grades, attendance, and following rules, and has assigned me roles as a student, classmate, and member of the larger school community.
