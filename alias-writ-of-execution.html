Habeas Corpus

[Latin, You have the body.] A writ (court order) that commands an individual or a government official who has restrained another to produce the prisoner at a designated time and place so that the court can determine the legality of custody and decide whether to order the prisoner's release.

19. The alias that you use when you sign up in a website is called a _____


Username

A username is the alias you use when you join up for a website. Although authentication does not imply authorisation, a user's account enables them to log in to a system and maybe get authorization to access resources offered by or related to that system. For the reasons of accounting, security, logging, and resource management, a user is often needed to authenticate themselves with a password or other credentials in order to log into an account.

Further explanation

Through a procedure called as identity correlation, the operating system will frequently refer to the user after they have logged on using an identifier, such as an integer, rather than their username. The username and a user identification, often known as a user ID, are associated in Unix systems.

On a multi-user system, each user account typically has a home directory where they can store files specific to their activities that are guarded against access by other users. A public user profile, which includes the account owner's basic information, is a common feature of user accounts.

A user's username serves as a unique identifier for them on a computer network. Usernames can be chosen by the user or assigned to them by the network administrator. They are also known as account names, login IDs, or user IDs. In any scenario, it is prohibited to use the same username for more than one person, as doing so would negate the username's primary function of separating users. Email serves as the classic illustration of a system or program that cannot be shared, hence a user's email address is frequently used as a default or required username.

27. what is the similarity of academic writting and literary writting ​


Answer:

Creative writing and academic writing are two different types of writing that though have some similarities.

What these two types of writing have got in common is probably has to do with the general writing guidelines, that concern the basic writing principals such as deviding your piece of writing into parts (the introduction, the main body, the conclusion), the usage of linking words that will differ of course in creative and academic writing types. The linking words help structure the piece of writing. The means of structuring the academic and creative writing will be different though.

What is more, it is important to put the ideas in a special appropriate way that is similar in both of the types, i.e. writing the topic sentence and then support it with the supportive sentences to express the main idea.

And of course, both the academic and creative writing should be proper from the linguistic point of view. What I mean by this is the correct spelling and punctuation.

On the other hand, the creative and academic writing will have completely different ‘language filling’, i.e. vocabulary usage and grammar patterns.
