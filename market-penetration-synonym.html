5. III. WRITE A REFLECTION PAPER indentifying your :A. Potential MarketB. Target MarketC. Available MarketD. Penetrated Market​


Answer:

A. Potential Market

The potential market refers to the total number of individuals or entities that could potentially be interested in a particular product or service. In my business, the potential market includes all individuals who have a need for our products, irrespective of their geographical location, level of education, age or gender.

B. Target Market

Target market is the group of individuals or entities that we are actually targeting with our marketing efforts. Our target market has been identified as individuals aged 18-35 years old, who are tech-savvy and have an appreciation for high-quality AI-powered products. We believe that this group will be most interested in our products, and it is therefore where we focus most of our marketing resources.

C. Available Market

The available market is the portion of the target market that we can realistically reach with our marketing efforts. This includes those individuals who are aware of our products and services and have access to them. For us, the available market refers to individuals in the Philippines who have access to the internet and our digital platform.

D. Penetrated Market

Penetrated market refers to those customers who have already purchased our products or services. To determine our penetrated market, we track the number of individuals who have signed up and completed a purchase through our app, including those who continue to use our services regularly. This allows us to understand how successful we have been in capturing a segment of our target market, and what strategies we may need to develop to attract new customers.

17. ano ang market penetration at magbigay ng example​


Answer:

Market penetration refers to the successful selling of a product or service in a specific market. It is measured by the amount of sales volume of an existing good or service compared to the total target market for that product or service.[1] Market penetration is the key for a business growth strategy stemming from the Ansoff Matrix (Richardson, M., & Evans, C. (2007). H. Igor Ansoff first devised and published the Ansoff Matrix in the Harvard Business Review in 1957, within an article titled "Strategies for Diversification". The grid/matrix is utilized across businesses to help evaluate and determine the next stages the company must take in order to grow, and the risks associated with the chosen strategy. With numerous options available, this matrix helps narrow down the best fit for an organization.

Example:

1. Penetration pricing

2. Product launches

3. Define new target segments

4. Expand into different territories

5. Start a chain or franchise

6. Develop strategic alliances
