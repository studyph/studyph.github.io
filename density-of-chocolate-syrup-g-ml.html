1. A cough syrup contains 5.0% ethyl alcohol, C2H5OH, by mass. If the density of the solution is 0.9928 g/mL, determine the molarity of the alcohol in the cough syrup.​


Given:

concentration = 5.0% by mass

density of solution = 0.9928 g/mL

solute: ethyl alcohol (C₂H₅OH)

Required:

molarity

Solution:

Step 1: Determine the mass of solute and mass of solution.

5.0% by mass of solution means that there are 5.0 g of solute in 100.0 g of solution.

mass of solute = 5.0 g

mass of solution = 100.0 g

Step 2: Calculate the molar mass of solute.

molar mass of solute = (12.01 g/mol × 2) + (1.008 g/mol × 6) + (16.00 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of solute = 46.068 g/mol

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{5.0 g}}{\text{46.068 g/mol}}[/tex]

moles of solute = 0.108535 mol

Step 4: Calculate the volume of solution.

[tex]\text{volume of solution} = \frac{\text{mass of solution}}{\text{density of solution}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solution} = \frac{\text{100.0 g}}{\text{0.9928 g/mL}}[/tex]

volume of solution = 100.725 mL = 0.100725 L

Step 5: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{0.108535 mol}}{\text{0.100725 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity = 1.08 M}}[/tex]

Ratio

A ratio shows the relative sizes of two or more values. It indicates how many times one number contains another.

Different Ways to Express Ratio

Word Form (to)

Fraction Form (/)

Colon Form (:)

Examples:

"30 mL of chocolate syrup for every 2 ounces of milk"

Word Form = 30 to 2

Fraction Form = ‌30⁄2‌

Colon Form = 30:2

Fraction

Fraction represents a part of a whole.

Parts of Fraction

Numerator

- the top number

- tells the number of parts taken from the whole

- the shaded parts

Denominator

- the bottom number

- tells the total number of equal parts

Fraction bar (/)

- separates the numerator and the denominator

Types of Fraction

Proper Fractions - The numerator is less than the denominator.

Examples: 1/3, 3/4, 2/7

Improper Fractions - The numerator is greater than or equal to the denominator.

Examples: 4/3, 11/4, 7/7

Mixed Fractions - A whole number and proper fraction together.

Examples: 1 1/3, 2 1/4, 16 2/5

9. What is the molarity of a solution when 30 g of NaOH is mixed with 200 g of water? Density of NaOH = 2.13 g/mL; density of water = 1 g/mL.​


Molarity (M)Discussion:

Molarity is one of the commonly used units in chemistry. Molarity is defined as the number of moles of solute per liter of solution --- that is,

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}}[/tex]

For example, a 1.46 [tex]M[/tex] glucose solution contains 1.46 mol of the solute (glucose) in 1 L of the solution. Of course, we do not always work with solution volumes of 1 L. Thus, a 500-mL solution containing 0.730 mol of glucose also has a concentration of 1.46 [tex]M.[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Problem:

What is the molarity of a solution when 30 g of NaOH is mixed with 200 g of water? Density of NaOH = 2.13 g/mL; density of water = 1 g/mL.

Given:

[tex]\text{mass of solute = 30 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass of solvent = 200 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{density of solute = 2.13 g/mL}[/tex]

[tex]\text{density of solvent = 1 g/mL}[/tex]

[tex]\text{solute = NaOH}[/tex]

[tex]\text{solvent = water}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]\text{molarity}[/tex]

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the volume of solute.

[tex]\text{density of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{volume of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{density of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solute} = \frac{\text{30 g}}{\text{2.13 g/mL}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solute} = \text{14.0845 mL}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the volume of solvent.

[tex]\text{density of solvent} = \frac{\text{mass of solvent}}{\text{volume of solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solvent} = \frac{\text{mass of solvent}}{\text{density of solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solvent} = \frac{\text{200 g}}{\text{1 g/mL}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solvent} = \text{200 mL}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the volume of solution.

volume of solution = volume of solute + volume of solvent

volume of solution = 14.0845 mL + 200 mL

volume of solution = 214.0845 mL = 0.2140845 L

Step 4: Calculate the molar mass of solute

molar mass of solute = (22.99 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 1) + (1.008 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of solute = 39.998 g/mol

Step 5: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{30 g}}{\text{39.998 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 0.7500375 mol}[/tex]

Step 6: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{0.7500375 mol}}{\text{0.2140845 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity} = 3.5 \: M}[/tex]

Percent volume (% v/v) : total volume (ml) of solute in 100 ml of solution

24. How many liters of 3.5 % KOH by mass with density 1.02 g/mL can be obtained by diluting 0.250 L of 30.0% KOH by mass (density = 1.288 g/mL)?


Given:

volume of solution before dilution = 0.250 L = 250 mL

percent by mass before dilution = 30.0%

density of solution before dilution = 1.288 g/mL

percent by mass after dilution = 3.5%

density of solution after dilution = 1.02 g/mL

solute: KOH

Required:

volume of solution after dilution

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the mass of solution before dilution.

mass of solution before dilution = density of solution before dilution × volume of solution before dilution

mass of solution before dilution = 1.288 g/mL × 250 mL

mass of solution before dilution = 322 g

Step 2: Calculate the mass of solute before dilution.

[tex]\text{mass of solute before dilution} = \frac{\text{percent by mass before dilution}}{100} × \text{mass of solution before dilution}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass of solute before dilution} = \frac{30}{100} × \text{322 g}[/tex]

mass of solute before dilution = 96.6 g

Step 3: Calculate the molar mass of solute.

molar mass of solute = (39.10 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 1) + (1.008 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of solute = 56.108 g/mol

Step 4: Calculate the number of moles of solute before dilution.

[tex]\text{moles of solute before dilution} = \frac{\text{mass of solute before dilution}}{\text{molar mass of solute before dilution}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute before dilution} = \frac{\text{96.6 g}}{\text{56.108 g/mol}}[/tex]

moles of solute before dilution = 1.72168 mol

Step 5: Calculate the mass of solute after dilution (assumed).

Assuming that the mass of solution is 100 g, the mass of solute is

[tex]\text{mass of solute after dilution} = \frac{\text{percent by mass after dilution}}{100} × \text{mass of solution after dilution}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass of solute after dilution} = \frac{3.5}{100} × \text{100 g}[/tex]

mass of solute after dilution = 3.5 g

Step 6: Calculate the number of moles of solute after dilution (assumed).

[tex]\text{moles of solute after dilution} = \frac{\text{mass of solute after dilution}}{\text{molar mass of solute after dilution}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute after dilution} = \frac{\text{3.5 g}}{\text{56.108 g/mol}}[/tex]

moles of solute after dilution = 0.0623797 mol

Step 7: Calculate the volume of solution after dilution (assumed).

[tex]\text{volume of solution after dilution} = \frac{\text{mass of solution after dilution}}{\text{density of solution after dilution}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solution after dilution} = \frac{\text{100 g}}{\text{1.02 g/mL}}[/tex]

volume of solution after dilution = 98.0392 mL = 0.0980392 L

Step 8: Calculate the molarity of solution after dilution.

[tex]\text{molarity after dilution} = \frac{\text{moles of solute after dilution}}{\text{volume of solution after dilution (L)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molarity after dilution} = \frac{\text{0.0623797 mol}}{\text{0.0980392 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molarity after dilution} = 0.636273 \: M[/tex]

Step 9: Calculate the volume of solution after dilution.

moles of solute before dilution = moles of solute after dilution

moles of solute before dilution = molarity after dilution × volume of solution after dilution

[tex]\text{volume of solution after dilution} = \frac{\text{moles of solute before dilution}}{\text{molarity after dilution}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{volume of solution after dilution} = \frac{\text{1.72168 mol }}{0.636273 \: M}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{volume of solution after dilution = 2.71 L}}[/tex]
