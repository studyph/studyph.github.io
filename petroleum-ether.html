8. Calculate the mass (in grams) of 250mL of ether 25 degrees celsius. The density of ether at 25 degrees celsius is 0.71g/mL


Given:

[tex]V = \text{250 mL}[/tex]

[tex]\rho = \text{0.71 g/mL}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]m[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\rho = \frac{m}{V}[/tex]

[tex]m = \rho V[/tex]

[tex]m = (\text{0.71 g/mL})(\text{250 mL})[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{m = \text{178 g}}[/tex]

16. Which compound is an ether


Answer:

Alcohols

Explanation:

Ether, any of a class of organic compounds characterized by an oxygen atom bonded to two alkyl or aryl groups. Ethers are similar in structure to alcohols, and both ethers and alcohols are similar in structure to water. In an alcohol one hydrogen atom of a water molecule is replaced by an alkyl group, whereas in an ether both hydrogen atoms are replaced by alkyl or aryl groups.

30. The density of ether is 0.714 g/mL. What is the mass of 34.0 milliliters of ether?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The mass of an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{m = dV}[/tex]

where:

d = density

m = mass

V = volume

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the mass

m = dV

m = (0.714 g/mL)(34.0 mL)

[tex]\boxed{m = \text{24.3 g}}[/tex]

Therefore, the mass of 34.0 mL of ether is 24.3 g.
