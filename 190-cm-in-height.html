Answer:

1. С

2. A

3. D

4. B

5. A

Step-by-step explanation:

1. The surface area of a cube is made up of the areas of its six faces. The surface area can be found by using formula: [tex]6*s^2[/tex], where "s" represents a side of the cube.  

Find the surface area of the cube. [tex]SA=6*6^2=216\;sq.in[/tex]

2. The surface area of a prism is made up of the areas of its six faces.

Find the area of the bottom face. Area (bottom edge) = length times width = lw=20 in*16 in=320 sq.in.

Find the area of the top face. The top and bottom faces are the same size.

Find the area of the front and back faces. The area of the front face = width times height = wh = 16 in*35*in=560 sq.in. The area of the back is also wh.

Find the area of the left and right faces. The area of the left face is lh and the area of the right face is also lh. lh=20 in* 35in=700 sq.in.

Add the six areas together. SA=320+320+560+560+700+700=3160 sq.in

3. The formula is SA=π*r*s+π*r², where SA equals the surface area of the cone, r equals the length of the radius of the cone’s base, and s equals the slant height of the cone.

Plug the value of the radius into the formula. SA=π*5*s+π*5²

Plug the value of the slant height into the formula. SA=π*5*8+π*5²

Multiply the radius, slant height, and π. SA=3.14*5*8+3.14*5²=125.6+78.5=204.1 sq.in.

4. The formula is SA = 4πr².

Plug the value of the radius into the formula. SA=4*3.14*5²=314 sq.in

5.  The formula is SA =2πr²+2πrh.

A circle's radius is always half the length of its diameter.

Find the radius. r=d:2=5:2=2.5 sq.cm.

Plug the values of the radius and height into the formula. SA =2πr²+2πrh=2π*2.5²+2π*2.5*9=12.5π+45π=57.5π=57.5*3.14=180.55 sq.cm
