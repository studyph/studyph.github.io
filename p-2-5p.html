Factoring Difference of Squares

Explanation and Solution:

Recall that the difference of squares has a factored form of the product of the conjugates of the square root of each terms

If you have the expression, [tex]a^2 -b^2[/tex], it can be rewritten as [tex](a -b)(a+b)[/tex], as they are both equivalent.

Here, you can make [tex]a^2[/tex] as [tex]9r^2[/tex] which if you take the square root of them, you will have [tex]a = 3r[/tex]. Same goes for [tex]b[/tex]:

[tex]b^2 = 25p^4 \\ \sqrt{b^2} = \sqrt{25p^4} \\ b = 5p^2[/tex]

Now we have [tex]a = 3r[/tex] and [tex]b = 5p^2[/tex], We can rewrite your difference of squares expression in the form of [tex](a -b)(a+b)[/tex]

[tex](a-b)(a+b)\\((3r)-(5p^2))((3r)+(5p^2))\\(3r-5p^2)(3r+5p^2)[/tex]

Answer:
