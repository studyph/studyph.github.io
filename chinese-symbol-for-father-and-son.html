4. Give the nine chinese new year's artistic symbols and traditions​


Answer:

The Color Red: in Chinese culture the color red is closely associated with good fortune and happiness.

The Color Yellow: the color yellow was traditionally the symbol of the Chinese emperor, and even in modern society can be seen as denoting a higher status.

Fú Characters: a very common decoration during the Chinese Spring Festival is the “Fú (福)” character, which means good fortune in Chinese.

Couplets: couplets, or “duì lián (对联)” in Chinese, consist of two vertical strips usually comprising 7-11 characters, and sometimes a third horizontal strip comprising 4 characters.

Red Envelopes: during the Chinese Spring Festival it is a common practice for elders and married couples to gift small amounts of money to children and young adults.

Fish: fish are a very common theme in Chinese Spring Festival decorations, mainly due to a common blessing during the New Year, “nián nián yǒu yú (年年有鱼).

Paper Cutting: paper cutting is another traditional Chinese craft commonly seen during the Spring Festival.

Lanterns: while there are many types of Chinese lanterns, the ones prevalent during the Chinese Spring Festival are always red in color, and are almost always round or oval in shape.

Fireworks: in China, fireworks and firecrackers were first made out of bamboo stems filled with gunpowder, and used to drive away evil spirits.

The Kitchen God: Zào Jūn (灶君), or the kitchen god, is a traditionally deity widely revered in ancient China.

The God of Gates: the tradition of gate gods dates back to the Tang dynasty when the emperor fell sick after having a bad dream in which ghost were searching for him in the palace.

The God of Money: “Cái Shén (财神),” the Chinese god of money/wealth, is a deity believed to be able to bring wealth to those that revere him.
