7. A square piece of cardboard is to be used to form a box without a top by cutting off squares, 3cm on a side from each corner and then folding up the sides. The volume of the box must be 300cm3.​


Answer:

Let x be the length of a side of the cardboard. When a 3cm square is cut off from each corner and then folded up, a box with square base x – 6 cm on each side, and a height of 3cm will be formed. Therefore, its volume is 3(x – 6)². Since the volume must be 300cm³, we have:

Step-by-step explanation:

x – 6cm – length of the box

x – 6cm – width of the box

3cm – height of the box

300cm³ – volume of the box

V = lwh

300 = (x – 6)(x – 6)(3)

3(x – 6)² = 300

(x – 6)² = 100

x – 6 = ±10

We reject the value of –10 since the dimensions of the box must be positive Thus we get

x – 6 = 10

x – 6 + 6 = 10 + 6

x = 16

Therefore the square cardboard must be 16cm on each side.

5cm on each side, and then folding up the sides. If the volume of the box is 320cm³, what is the length of a side of the cardboard?

Given:

The cardboard is a square piece

Volume = 320cm³

The corners were cut by 5cm

height = 5cm (because it was folded)

Solution:

Since the cardboard is a square,

let x be the unknown length of the cardboard

W = x + 5 + 5

L = x + 5 + 5

Volume = LWH

320 = (x + 5 + 5)( x + 5 + 5)(5)

320/5 = (x + 10)(x + 10)

(x + 10)² = 64

x + 10 = √64

x = √64 - 10

x = - 2

L = x + 5 + 5

L = -2 + 10

L = 8cm

Checking:

Volume = LWH

320 = 8(8)(5)

320 = 320
