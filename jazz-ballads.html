1. differentiate the blues ballads and jazz ballads​


Answer:

Blue Ballads is used to refer to a specific form of popular music which fused Anglo-American and Afro-American styles from the late 19th century onwards. Early versions combined elements of the European influenced "native American ballad " with the forms of African American music. A jazz ballad, on the other hand, is a love song that is slow, sentimental, and intimate. Often in 4/4 and with a 32-bar form. Examples of classic jazz ballads include “It Might As Well Be Spring,” “My Funny Valentine,” “Some Other Spring,” “The Man I Love,” “In a Sentimental Mood,” “Misty,” and “’Round Midnight.” In popular music, the term ballad has been frequently used to refer to Beatles' songs such as “Yesterday” and “Norwegian Wood.”

2. differentiate blues ballads and jazz ballads​


differentiate blues ballads and jazz ballads​

The main focus of jazz music is an ensemble's dynamics and improvisations, but blues music is usually focused on a solo guitarist/vocalist and the song's personal lyrical content. The majority of jazz songs are instrumental, whereas blues songs always have lyrics.

Blues music predates jazz and can be considered a subgenre of the latter. Jazz, on the other hand, would not be labeled blues music in and of itself.

What's the difference between jazz and blues?

Guitar, bass, piano, harmonica, percussion, saxophone, vocals, trumpet, trombone, and fiddle are all used in blues. Guitar, piano, bass, saxophone, trumpet, clarinet, drums, and tuba are all used in jazz. Jazz is almost always instrumental, whereas Blues is almost always lyrical.

A. Folk music

Explanation:

Folk music is the music that is sung by the people, not music recorded in a studio or performed on a stage.

People learn these songs by singing them with others.

The songs are passed down from generation to generation, and are often changed as they are passed down (such changing is known as the "folk process").

20. Your own definition of Latin-American music, Ballad Music, Jazz Music, Hip-hop and Rock and Roll​


Answer:

LATIN AMERICAN MUSIC- The music of Latin America refers to music originating from Latin America, namely the Romance-speaking countries and territories of the Americas and the Caribbean south of the United States.

BALLAD MUSIC- ballad is a form of verse, often a narrative set to music. Ballads derive from the medieval French chanson balladée or ballade, which were originally "dance songs"

JAZZ MUSIC- the development of the jazz genre was an offshoot of the music of african slaves who were brought to america. As an outlet for their deepest feeling, the africans used music to recall their nostalgic past and their home country as well as to voice out their sentiments on their desperate condition as slaves in america.

HIP-HOP- A style of popular music of US black and Hispanic origin, featuring rap with an electronic backing.

ROCK AND ROLL- it was greatly popular song from united states during the late 1940s to the 1960s that combine the afro-american from such as the blues, jump, blues,jazz, and gospel music with western swing and country music.
