12. what genri is it?ADVENTURE


Answer:

Adventure Genre Definition – What’s the best definition for the adventure fiction genre? The adventure genre consists of books where the protagonist goes on an epic journey, either personally or geographically. Often the protagonist has a mission and faces many obstacles in his way. A good example would be The Lord of the Rings series. The adventure genre often pairs up with the action genre, since long epic journeys have a lot of action in them. The adventure genre can be combined with almost any other genre; it’s versatile like romance and drama in that it can be meshed with almost any other genre and still work.

21. What was the good fortune found by cenon on his adventure? English ​


Answer:

In the short story "The Adventure of Cenon," written by Francisco Arcellana, the protagonist Cenon embarks on a journey to find his fortune. Through his travels, Cenon experiences various challenges, but eventually, he discovers a newfound sense of good fortune.

At the beginning of the story, Cenon was an impoverished young man who lived in a small, rural village. He was dissatisfied with his life and longed for something more. When he heard tales of the riches and opportunities that awaited him in the city, he decided to leave his village and embark on an adventure to find his fortune.

Cenon's journey was not an easy one, and he faced many obstacles along the way. He had to navigate unfamiliar places and people and was often taken advantage of because of his naivete. Despite these difficulties, Cenon persisted, driven by his desire for a better life.

Finally, after a long and exhausting journey, Cenon arrived in the city. He found work as a messenger and began to earn a modest living. Through his job, he met new people and gained new experiences, which helped him to grow and mature as a person. He also met a woman named Juliana, who he fell in love with, and they began a relationship.

Although Cenon's newfound good fortune was not necessarily the wealth he had been seeking, he had found something more valuable: happiness and fulfillment. He realized that his journey had led him to a place where he was content and satisfied with his life.

In conclusion, the good fortune that Cenon found on his adventure was not material wealth, but rather a sense of happiness and fulfillment. Through his journey, he grew as a person and gained new experiences that helped him find his place in the world. The story of Cenon's adventure teaches us that sometimes, the most valuable things in life are not material possessions, but rather the intangible experiences and relationships that bring us joy and fulfillment.
