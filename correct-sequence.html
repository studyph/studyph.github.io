9. what is the correct desription of an arithmetic sequence​


Answer:

It is a sequence where the next term after the first is obtained by adding a constant called common difference.

Example:

7, 14, 21, 28

Common difference is 7

Answer:

An arithmetic sequence is a pattern of numbers that have common difference between them.

Each numberin the sequence is called a term. The common difference, d, between them can be positive or negative.

Any term of the sequence can be found explicitly with the following formula:

An = A1 + d(n-1)

Where a_(n) is the nth term, A1 is the first term of the sequence, d is the common difference, and n is the term number.
