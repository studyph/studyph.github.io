Rainfall and snowmelt are the primary sources of water for rivers and streams in the Salish Sea, but many other factors can also impact stream flows, including:

Dams and other hydrological modifications.

Loss and change of vegetative cover.

Wells that tap ground water.

11. Strong and fast-moving stream of water or other liquid.


A strong and fast-moving stream of water or other liquid is often referred to as a "powerful current" or a "rapid." When a stream of water or other liquid moves quickly, it can have a lot of force behind it, making it capable of moving large objects or even eroding away at land and other structures. This is why it is important to be cautious around fast-moving bodies of water, and to avoid entering them if possible.

What creates a drought?

When there is less rainfall than usual for several weeks, months, or years, the flow of streams and rivers decreases, water levels in lakes and reservoirs fall, and the depth to water in wells increases. If the dry weather continues and water supply issues arise, the dry spell may turn into a drought.
