Trees, and all photosynthesizing plants, use the energy of the sun to split atmospheric carbon dioxide into its constituents: oxygen and carbon. And carbon is the primary building block of the tree — and all of life on earth, too

2)A tree growing in the open has 360 degrees of sunlight from top to bottom. That increases the productive leaf surface. That increased volume of productive leaf surface allows the tree to use much more reproductive energy. The tree no longer has to spend so much energy reaching up and can focus on fruiting.

3)Most like a well-drained soil. Fruit trees should be selected according to fruit type, variety, pollination, harvest date and root stock. Select fruit-tree varieties that will grow well in your area. Some will require more than one variety to ensure proper pollination and fruit set.

30. What images or ideas come to mind when you think of mental illness? Where do these ideas come from—movies, television, personal experience? What personal characteristics do you have that indicate good mental health?


Answer:

-When I think of mental illness, I often think of people struggling with depression, anxiety, or other mental health issues. Images of people struggling to manage their symptoms come to mind, as well as the frustration and stress of trying to cope and function in everyday life. These ideas often come from movies, television, and personal experience, as well as from stories I hear from friends and family.

-Personal characteristics that indicate good mental health include a strong sense of self-worth, the ability to cope with stress and difficult emotions, the ability to recognize and manage one’s own emotions, and the ability to maintain healthy relationships with friends and family. Additionally, having a sense of purpose and meaning in life, engaging in activities that are enjoyable and fulfilling, and having a positive outlook on life can also contribute to good mental health.
