4. Activity 1 Find the circumference of the following circle. Show your solutions. 1. 9m2. 15cm3. 10in4. 16cm5. 7m​


Answer:

The formula for the circumference of a circle is:

C = 2πr

where C is the circumference and r is the radius of the circle.

For a circle with a radius of 9m:

C = 2π(9m) = 18π m ≈ 56.55 m

Therefore, the circumference of the circle is approximately 56.55 meters.

For a circle with a radius of 15cm:

C = 2π(15cm) = 30π cm ≈ 94.25 cm

Therefore, the circumference of the circle is approximately 94.25 centimeters.

For a circle with a radius of 10in:

C = 2π(10in) = 20π in ≈ 62.83 in

Therefore, the circumference of the circle is approximately 62.83 inches.

For a circle with a radius of 16cm:

C = 2π(16cm) = 32π cm ≈ 100.53 cm

Therefore, the circumference of the circle is approximately 100.53 centimeters.

For a circle with a radius of 7m:

C = 2π(7m) = 14π m ≈ 43.98 m

Therefore, the circumference of the circle is approximately 43.98 meters.

Solve the following problems.

1. What is the radius if the center of the circle is (-7. 1) and tangent to origin?

Solution:

(x₁, y₁), (x₂, y₂)

(-7, 1), (0, 0)

Using distance formula to get the radius

r² = (x₂ - x₁) + (y₂ - y₁)

r² = (0 - (-7))² + (0 - 1)²

r² = 7² + (-1)²

r² = 49 + 1

r² = 50

r = √50

r = 7.07

Answer:

radius = √50 or 7.07

2. If the diameter has endpoints at (-9, -2) and (-3, 4), what must be the area of the circle?

Solution:

(x₁, y₁), (x₂, y₂)

(-9, -2), (-3, 4)

Using distance formula,

since the two given endpoints are the diameter, then

d² = (x₂ - x₁) + (y₂ - y₁)

d² = (-3 - (-9))² + (4 - ( -2))²

d² = 6² + 6²

d² = 36 + 36

d² = 72

diameter = √72

diameter = 2r

√72 = 2r

√72/2 = r

r = 4.24

Area = πr²

Area = π(4.24)²

Area = 18π

Area = 56.52 square units

Answer:

Area = 56.52 square units

3. If the diameter has endpoints at (-6, -1) and (-8,5), what must be the equation of the circle?

Solution:

(x₁, y₁), (x₂, y₂)

(-6, -1), (-8, 5)

Using distance formula,

since the two given endpoints are the diameter, then

d² = (x₂ - x₁) + (y₂ - y₁)

d² = (-8 - (-6))² + (5 - ( -1))²

d² = -2² + 6²

d² = 4 + 36

d² = 40

diameter = √40

diameter = 2r

√40 = 2r

√40/2 = r

r = 3.16

Diameter = 2 x Radius

Diameter = 2 x 43cm

Diameter = 86cm

Therefore, the diameter of the circle is 86cm.

The radius of a circle is half the length of its diameter. Therefore, to find the radius of a circular park with a 250 meter diameter, we divide 250 by 2.

Radius = Diameter ÷ 2

Radius = 250m ÷ 2

Radius = 125m

Therefore, the radius of the circular park is 125 meters.

The diameter of a circle is twice the length of its radius. Therefore, to find the radius of a circle with a diameter of 8cm, we divide 8 by 2.

Radius = Diameter ÷ 2

Radius = 8cm ÷ 2

Radius = 4cm

Therefore, the radius of the circle is 4cm.
