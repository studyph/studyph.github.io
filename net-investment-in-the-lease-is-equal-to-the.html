30. 1. Company X has investments of Php3,000,000 and net income after taxes of Php1,250,000 a year. How many years will it take for the company to recover the investment?2. A company's total investment is Php 1,500,000. If Return on Investments is 35%, how much is the net profit after taxes?3. company's net profit after taxes is Php 94,000. If Return on Assets is 40%, what are the total assets?pls pa answer pooo ty​


Answer:

1. To determine the number of years it will take for Company X to recover the investment, we can use the formula:

Years to recover investment = Total investment / Net income

Plugging in the given values, we get:

Years to recover investment = 3,000,000 / 1,250,000

Years to recover investment = 2.4 years

Therefore, it will take Company X 2.4 years to recover their investment.

2. To determine the net profit after taxes for the company with a total investment of Php 1,500,000 and a Return on Investments of 35%, we can use the formula:

Net profit after taxes = Total investment x Return on Investments x (1 - Tax rate)

Assuming a tax rate of 30%, we can plug in the given values to get:

Net profit after taxes = 1,500,000 x 0.35 x (1 - 0.30)

Net profit after taxes = Php 192,500

Therefore, the net profit after taxes for the company is Php 192,500.

3. To determine the total assets of the company with a net profit after taxes of Php 94,000 and a Return on Assets of 40%, we can use the formula:

Total assets = Net profit after taxes / Return on Assets

Plugging in the given values, we get:

Total assets = 94,000 / 0.40

Total assets = Php 235,000

Therefore, the total assets of the company are Php 235,000.
