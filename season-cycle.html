6. - EXERCISES Make a plan for your garden using one (1) year crop rotation .Set the cycle and the type of crops you want to grow. Show this table. Season 2 Season 1 name of specific crop ? Season 3​


ANSWER

Sure, here's an example plan for a garden using a one-year crop rotation cycle:

Season 1 (Spring/Early Summer):

Plant cool-weather crops such as lettuce, spinach, kale, peas, and radishes.

Season 2 (Mid-Late Summer):

Plant warm-weather crops such as tomatoes, peppers, cucumbers, squash, and beans.

Season 3 (Fall):

Plant fall crops such as broccoli, cauliflower, cabbage, carrots, and beets.

By rotating crops each season, you can help prevent soil-borne diseases, reduce pest infestations, and improve soil fertility. This can result in healthier plants, higher yields, and a more sustainable garden. Additionally, rotating crops can help you plan your garden better and ensure that you have a variety of fresh produce throughout the year.

Note: This is just an example plan, and the specific crops you choose to plant will depend on your climate, soil type, and personal preferences. It's important to do research and select crops that are well-suited to your local growing conditions.
