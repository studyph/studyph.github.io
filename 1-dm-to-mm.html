Diameter Height Volume1. 14 mm 12 mm _____2. 20 cm 10cm _____3. 18 mm 24 mm _____find the volume of each sphereRadius Volume1. 7dm ______2. 10cm ______3. 1/2 dm _____find The volume of each square pyramidSide of the base Height Volume1. 14dm 20 dm _____2. 12 dm 15 dm ______3. 8 cm 5 cm ______​


Volume

Volume of a cone

[tex] \boxed{ \sf{V = \frac{ \pi r^2h}{3}}}[/tex] or [tex] \boxed{ \sf{V = \frac{bh}{3}}} [/tex]

Where volume is equal to [tex]\sf\frac{1}{3}[/tex] the base area (B) times the height (h)

Find the volume of each cone.

Diameter Height Volume

1. 14 mm 12 mm _____

[tex]\sf\underline{Solution:}[/tex]

[tex] \sf{r=\frac{d}{2}}[/tex][tex] \sf{r=\frac{14mm}{2}}[/tex][tex] \sf{r=7mm}[/tex]

[tex] \sf{V = \frac{\pi r^2h }{3}} [/tex][tex] \sf{V = \frac{(3.14)(7mm)^2(12mm)}{3}} [/tex][tex] \sf{V = \frac{(3.14)(49mm)(12mm)}{3}} [/tex][tex] \sf{V = \frac{(3.14)(588mm)}{3}} [/tex][tex] \sf{V = \frac{1846.32mm}{3}}[/tex][tex] \sf{V = \green{615.44mm^3}}[/tex]

Therefore, the volume of a cone is 615.44mm³

2. 20 cm 10cm _____

[tex]\sf\underline{Solution:}[/tex]

[tex] \sf{r=\frac{d}{2}}[/tex][tex] \sf{r=\frac{20cm}{2}}[/tex][tex] \sf{r = 10cm}[/tex]

[tex] \sf{V = \frac{\pi r^2h }{3}} [/tex][tex] \sf{V = \frac{(3.14)(10cm)^2(10cm)}{3}} [/tex][tex] \sf{V = \frac{(3.14)(100m)(10cm)}{3}} [/tex][tex] \sf{V = \frac{(3.14)(1000cm)}{3}} [/tex][tex] \sf{V = \frac{3140cm}{3}}[/tex][tex] \sf{V = \green{1046.67cm^3}}[/tex]

Therefore, the volume of a cone is 1046.67cm³

3. 18 mm 24 mm _____

[tex]\sf\underline{Solution:}[/tex]

[tex] \sf{r=\frac{d}{2}}[/tex][tex] \sf{r=\frac{18mm}{2}}[/tex][tex] \sf{r=9mm}[/tex]

[tex] \sf{V = \frac{\pi r^2h }{3}} [/tex][tex] \sf{V = \frac{(3.14)(9mm)^2(24mm)}{3}} [/tex][tex] \sf{V = \frac{(3.14)(81mm)(24mm)}{3}} [/tex][tex] \sf{V = \frac{(3.14)(1944mm)}{3}} [/tex][tex] \sf{V = \frac{6104.16mm}{3}}[/tex][tex] \sf{V = \green{2034.72mm^3}}[/tex]

Therefore, the volume of a cone is 2034.72mm³

Volume of a sphere

[tex] \boxed{ \sf{V = \frac{4}{3} \pi r^2}}[/tex]

Where the volume of the sphere is [tex]\sf\frac{4}{3} \pi [/tex] multiplied by the cube of the radius.

find the volume of each sphere

Radius Volume

1. 7dm ______

[tex]\sf\underline{Solution:}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{V = \frac{4}{3} \pi r^2}[/tex][tex]\sf{V = \frac{4}{3} \times 3.14 \times 7dm^2}[/tex][tex]\sf{V = \frac{4}{3} \times 3.14 \times 49dm}[/tex][tex] \sf{V = \frac{609.56dm}{3}}[/tex][tex] \sf{V = \green{203.19dm^3}}[/tex]

Therefore, the volume of a sphere is 203.19dm³

2. 10cm ______

[tex]\sf\underline{Solution:}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{V = \frac{4}{3} \pi r^2}[/tex][tex]\sf{V = \frac{4}{3} \times 3.14 \times10cm^2}[/tex][tex]\sf{V = \frac{4}{3} \times 3.14 \times 100cm}[/tex][tex] \sf{V = \frac{1256cm}{3}}[/tex][tex] \sf{V = \green{418.67cm^3}}[/tex]

Therefore, the volume of a sphere is 418.67cm³

3. 1/2 dm _____

[tex]\sf\underline{Solution:}[/tex]

[tex]\sf{V = \frac{4}{3} \pi r^2}[/tex][tex]\sf{V = \frac{4}{3} \times 3.14 \times0.5dm^2}[/tex][tex]\sf{V = \frac{4}{3} \times 3.14 \times0.25dm}[/tex][tex] \sf{V = \frac{3.14dm}{3}}[/tex][tex] \sf{V = \green{1.05dm^3}}[/tex]

Therefore, the volume of a sphere is 1.05dm³

Volume of a pyramid

[tex] \boxed{ \sf{V = \frac{1}{3}bh}} [/tex] or [tex] \boxed{ \sf{V = \frac{1}{3} lwh}}[/tex]

Where the product of the length, width, and height is multiplied by [tex]\sf\frac{1}{3}[/tex] or divided by 3.

find The volume of each square pyramid

Side of the base Height Volume

1. 14dm 20 dm _____

[tex]\sf\underline{Solution:}[/tex]
