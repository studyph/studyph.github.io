17. How much is the voltage drop across a light bulb with a resistance of 50 ohm which allows a current of 15 A to flow


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The voltage across an element in a circuit is given by

[tex]\boxed{V = IR}[/tex]

where:

I = current

V = voltage

R = resistance

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the voltage

V = IR

V = (15 A)(50 ohm)

[tex]\boxed{V = \text{750 V}}[/tex]

Therefore, the voltage drop across the light bulb is 750 V.

Answer:

1.)Actually in principle they could.

What solar cells do not have is Carnot losses, because the light energy is converted directly into electrical energy avoiding a step involving heat. That is in contrast to conventional coal burning, nuclear plants or solar thermal plants. They all must dump most of their heat as waste in a river or the atmosphere. Therefore they all have a serious thermodynamic limit to their efficiency due to the Second Law that solar cells do not have. Not that anyone wants to close them down for that reason.
