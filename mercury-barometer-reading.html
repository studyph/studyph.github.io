13. The mole fraction of nitrogen gas (Ny) in air is 0.78. This means that 78% of the surrounding air is nitrogen gas. What is the partial pressure exerted by nitrogen gas if the reading from a nearby mercury barometer is 745 mm of Hg?


Answer:

—The mole fraction of nitrogen gas (N2) in air is 0.78. To determine the partial pressure of nitrogen gas, we can use the formula:

Partial Pressure = Mole Fraction x Total Pressure

Given that the reading from a nearby mercury barometer is 745 mm of Hg, we can substitute this value for the total pressure:

Partial Pressure = 0.78 x 745 mm Hg

To calculate the partial pressure of Nitrogen gas, we can multiply 0.78 with 745mm Hg.

Partial Pressure = 581.1 mm Hg

So, the partial pressure exerted by nitrogen gas is 581.1 mm Hg.

A gas occupies 250 ml when the barometer reads 720 mm. How many milliliters will it occupy when the barometer reads 740 mm?

V₁ (initial volume) =  250 ml

V₂ (final volume) = unknown

P₁V₁ = P₂V₂

V₂ = P₁V₁ / P₂

26. A gas sample occupies a volume of 250 mL when the barometer reads 700 mmHg and the thermometer reads 23 C. Find its volume at 800 mmHg and 53 C.​


Given:

[tex]P_{1} = \text{700 mmHg}[/tex]

[tex]T_{1} = 23^{\circ}\text{C + 273 = 296 K}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{250 mL}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{800 mmHg}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = 53^{\circ}\text{C + 273 = 326 K}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]V_{2}[/tex]

Strategy:

This is a gas law problem. What gas law should we use?

Since the given quantities are pressure, temperature, and volume, we will use combined gas law. According to this gas law, the pressure of a gas is inversely proportional to its volume and directly proportional to its absolute temperature keeping the amount of gas constant.

Caution: The temperature must be converted to kelvin. If the given temperature is in degree Celsius, add 273 or 273.15 to convert it to kelvin.

The formula used for combined gas law is

[tex]\boxed{\frac{P_{1}V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \frac{P_{2}V_{2}}{T_{2}}}[/tex]

where

[tex]P_{1} = \text{initial pressure}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{initial volume}[/tex]

[tex]T_{1} = \text{initial temperature}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{final pressure}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{final volume}[/tex]

[tex]T_{2} = \text{final temperature}[/tex]

Solution:

Starting with the formula of combined gas law

[tex]\dfrac{P_{1}V_{1}}{T_{1}} = \dfrac{P_{2}V_{2}}{T_{2}}[/tex]

Multiplying both sides of the equation by T₂/P₂ to solve for V₂

[tex]V_{2} = V_{1} \times \dfrac{P_{1}}{P_{2}} \times \dfrac{T_{2}}{T_{1}}[/tex]

Substituting the given values and solving for V₂

[tex]V_{2} = \text{250 mL} \times \dfrac{\text{700 mmHg}}{\text{800 mmHg}} \times \dfrac{\text{326 K}}{\text{296 K}}[/tex]

Therefore, the final volume is

[tex]\boxed{V_{2} = \text{241 mL}}[/tex]

Answer:

[tex]V_{2} = \text{241 mL}[/tex]

30. what are two reason why mercury rather than water is used in barometers?​


Answer:

Mercury is commonly used in barometers because its high density means the height of the column can be a reasonable size to measure atmospheric pressure. A barometer using water, for instance, would need to be 13.6 times taller than a mercury barometer to obtain the same pressure difference.

What will happen if water is used in place of mercury in a barometer tube?

That is, if water is used in barometer tube instead of mercury, the length of the tube must be greater than 10.326 cm. A greater height will be difficult to hold in a vertical position and needs a high tower to install the barometer. So, we cannot replace mercury by water in the barometer.
