6. is Observing animal behavior as indicator of environmental condition biotechnology why or why not?


Answer:

A bioindicator is any species (an indicator species) or group of species whose function, population, or status can reveal the qualitative status of the environment. For example, copepods and other small water crustaceans that are present in many water bodies can be monitored for changes (biochemical, physiological, or behavioural) that may indicate a problem within their ecosystem. Bioindicators can tell us about the cumulative effects of different pollutants in the ecosystem and about how long a problem may have been present, which physical and chemical testing cannot.

Biological indicators can be described as the introduction of a highly resistant microorganisms to a given environment before sterilization, tests are conducted to measure the effectiveness of the sterilization processes. As biological indicators use highly resistant microorganisms, any sterilization process that renders them inactive will have also killed off more common, weaker pathogens.

13. what is the role of biotechnology in plant and animal reproduction​


Answer:

Biotechnology, in general, is the implementation of science and engineering concepts to create or alter objects, improve plants or animals, or grow microorganisms for particular purposes that are useful to society, using living things or compounds from these organisms. It includes various types of biotechnology, also known as traditional biotechnology, which is the use of biotechnology with a simplified task such as baking, using yeast, to complex biotechnology, such as recombinant DNA technology or the classical, that includes biofertilizers, biological nitrogen fixation, and fermentation, to modern biotechnology, which involves plant tissue culture, cell culture, genetic engineering, and recombinant DNA technology. Biotechnology is now mainly associated with medical applications and agriculture that are focused on understanding the genetic code of life. The ability to provide improved livestock productivity is one of the advantages of biotechnology in animal husbandry.
