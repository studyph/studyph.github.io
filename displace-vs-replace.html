17. Notes on reactants in:1. synthesis2. Decomposition3. Single Replacement4. Double Displacement5. NeutralizationPaki sagot po please ​


ANSWER

A synthesis reaction occurs when two or more reactants combine to form a single product. This type of reaction is represented by the general equation: A + B → AB.

A decomposition reaction occurs when one reactant breaks down into two or more products. This can be represented by the general equation: AB → A + B.

A single replacement reaction occurs when one element replaces another in a single compound. This type of reaction has the general equation: A + BC → B + AC.

A double displacement reaction is a type of chemical reaction in which the reactant ions exchange places to form new products. Usually, a double displacement reaction results in precipitate formation. The chemical bonds between the reactants may be either covalent or ionic.

A neutralization reaction is when an acid and a base react to form water and a salt and involves the combination of H+ ions and OH- ions to generate water. When a solution is neutralized, it means that salts are formed from equal weights of acid and base.

29. Distance vs. Displacement?​


Answer:

The complete length of the path between two points is called distance. Meanwhile, displacement is the direct and shortest possible length between the two points (usually solved by Pythagorean Theorem).

For example, say you walked 3km south and 4km west afterwards.

The distance would be: 3+4=7km

The displacement would be: \sqrt{3^{2}+4^{2} } =5
