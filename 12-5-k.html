28. ACTIVITY 5: Applications of Factor and Remainder Theorems A. The binomial (x + 4) is a factor of the following polynomials. Find the value of k. 1. 2x3 -3x2 – kx + 60 k = ____ 2. 7x2 + kx – 12 k = _____ 3. kx3 – 10x2 – 137x + 60 k = ______ 4. 4x2 + kx – 4 k = ______ 5. 8x3 + kx2 – 53x + 12 k = ______


Answer:

1. k = 29

Step-by-step explanation:

the binomial (x+4) is a factor of the following polynomials, find the value of k.\r\n

2x^3 - 3x^2 - kx + 60\r\n"

Since (x+4) is the root of the given polynomial, it means, due to the Remainder Theorem, \r\n

that (-4) is the root of the polynomial.\r\n

So, we substitute x= -4 into the polynomial and equate this expression to zero (since -4 is the root (!) )\r\n

We get then\r\n"

2*(-4)^3 - 3*(-4)^2 - k*(-4) + 60 = 0.\r\n

It is our BASIC EQUATION to find the unknown value of \"k\" :\r\n

2*(-64) - 3*(16) - k*(-4) + 60 = 0\r\n

-128 - 48 + 4k + 60 = 0\r\n

-116 + 4k = 0\r\n

4k = 116.\r\n

k = 116/4 = 29. \r\n

ANSWER. k = 29.\r\n
