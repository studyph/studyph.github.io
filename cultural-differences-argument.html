PARTS OF THE ARGUMENTATIVE ESSAY: Introductory ParagraphBody/Supporting Paragraphs Conclusion Transitional PhrasesESSAY TITLE:

Every Little Girl Wants to Be a Princess, Right?

FIRST PART:

The Introductory Paragraph

Beauty pageants have become a staple in American culture. Winners of pageants such as Miss America are icons, representations of the ideal woman, and positive role models for young girls. So, society says. More recently, however, a new type of pageant has increased in popularity to the point of being considered a national phenomenon. These pageants are just as glitzy, and the competition is just as fierce; the only difference is that these contestants are the miniature model.

Every last statement in the introductory argument is the thesis statement , arguably the most important component of the intro. It should explain what the essay would be arguing about.

SECOND PART:

The Body/Supporting Paragraphs

They are child beauty queens. The world of child beauty pageants has become a source of fascination, as well as contention, in our society. Networks such as TLC and WE TV have produced hit reality shows featuring the munchkin-sized divas because it sells, but one cannot help but wonder, how can parading children about on stage like show ponies be a positive thing? The truth is it is not. Beauty pageants are not a healthy activity for children because they force young girls to act like little adults, exhibit age-inappropriate sexuality, and have negative body image and mental health problems later in their lives.

The Body and supporting paragraphs includes the evidence and support the claims that arguments about the thesis statement.

LAST PART:

The Conclusion and Transitional Phrases

Children are the future of society, and even those who do not have children should be concerned about the direction of the culture they have to live in. Just because child beauty pageants are socially acceptable does not mean they should be. Our culture needs to eliminate child beauty pageants, at least in their current form. ​

The whole last paragraph will be the conclusion, and the last statement in that paragraph will serve as the transitional phrase. The transitional phrase would unify all the ideas from the top paragraph, to the body, and the conclusion.
