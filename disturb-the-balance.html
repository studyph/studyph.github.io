23. distinguish the disturbances in an ecosystema.when an ecosystem is in a state of balanceb.when an ecosystem change into a new ecosystemc.any change can that causes disruption in an ecosystem d.when an ecosystem is no longer in a state of balance and cannot longer​


Answer:

a. When an ecosystem is in a state of balance, there are minimal disturbances, and the ecosystem functions smoothly with a delicate balance between its living and non-living components.

b. When an ecosystem transforms into a new ecosystem, it is referred to as ecological succession, which is a gradual process of change and development in the species composition of an ecosystem.

c. Any change that causes disruption in an ecosystem is referred to as a disturbance. This can be caused by natural events such as fires, hurricanes, or earthquakes, or human activities such as deforestation, pollution, or introduction of non-native species.

d. When an ecosystem is no longer in a state of balance and cannot be restored to its original state, it is referred to as ecological collapse or ecosystem collapse. This can occur due to overuse or degradation of resources, the loss of key species, or the introduction of invasive species that disrupt the balance of the ecosystem.

29. Ecological balance is the state of dynamic equilibrium of a community of organisms. The organisms' relationship with each other and the environment remain relatively stable. Disturbing this balance will generate harmful effects on the ecosystem. Since all life forms are connected, what can we, humans, do to maintain the balance in the environment?​


Answered by wHanari:

• Contamination from sewage, and pollution from manufacturing and agricultural runoff threatens the balance of marine ecosystems. Sewer and agricultural runoff can cause a cascade of damaging effects on the ecosystem. Taking steps to reduce or eliminate pollution from nonpoint sources such as streets and farms will help to maintain the ecological balance. Sewage and run-off of agricultural fertilizer can cause the rapid growth of algae in lakes and streams. The growth of algae blocks sunlight and depletes the oxygen in the water. This causes a reduction in the amount of natural plant life in the marine ecosystem. The animals that feed on the plants die, which leads to the death of animals that prey on them. The decaying algae promote the growth of anaerobic organisms, which release compounds into the water that are toxic to marine animals.
