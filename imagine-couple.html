2. imagine that you are a medical technologist in a hospital you attended a mother who happens to be with a blood type A.she is wondering why her newborn child nis blood type O while her husband is a blood type B.is the couple has the possibility of having a childwith blood type O


Blood type is controlled by multiple alleles. A blood type A individual like the mother in the problem could either have AA and AO as her genotype. A blood type B individual like the father above could have either BB or BO genotypes. If we try to cross the genotypes of mother and father, respectively;

AO x BO 

then after using a Punett Square, do we expect that the parents will have a child having OO as genotype?

Yes! The genotypic ratio will be 1/4AB: 1/4AO: 1/4BO: 1/4 OO. 

Which genotype will give blood O? OO it is.  Therefore the answer to your question is YES.
