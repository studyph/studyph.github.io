30. 18. Consider a a bicycle with a mass of 20 kg traveling at a velocity of 4 m/s. What is its kinetic energyA. 80 joules B. 100 Joules C. 140 Joules D. 160 Joules​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The kinetic energy of a moving object is given by

[tex]\boxed{KE = \frac{1}{2}mv^{2}}[/tex]

where:

KE = kinetic energy

m = mass

v = speed

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the kinetic energy

[tex]KE = \dfrac{1}{2}mv^{2}[/tex]

[tex]KE = \dfrac{1}{2}(\text{20 kg})(\text{4 m/s})^{2}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{KE = \text{160 J}}[/tex]

Therefore, the answer is D. 160 Joules
