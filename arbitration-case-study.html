8. What are the advantages of implementing arbitration process in thebarangay?​.


There are several advantages to implementing an arbitration process in the barangay, or community, level:

Cost-effective: Arbitration is typically less expensive than going to court, as it involves fewer legal procedures and does not require hiring an attorney.

Time-efficient: Arbitration proceedings are generally quicker than going to court, which can take years to resolve a case.

Confidential: Arbitration proceedings are usually private and confidential, which can be important for parties who want to avoid publicity or who have sensitive issues to discuss.

Customizable: Parties in arbitration can choose their own arbitrator and can tailor the arbitration process to meet their specific needs.

Expertise: An arbitrator may have specialized knowledge or expertise in a particular area, which can be helpful in resolving complex disputes.

Informal: The arbitration process is generally less formal than going to court, which can make it easier for parties to communicate and reach a resolution.

Enforceable: Arbitration awards are generally enforceable in the same way as court judgments.

Overall, arbitration can be a useful alternative to going to court for resolving disputes, especially for parties who want a quick, cost-effective, and confidential resolution to their conflict.

18. paghambingin Ang conciliation at complusary arbitration​


Answer:

Conciliation is an alternative out-of-court dispute resolution instrument. Like mediation, conciliation is a voluntary, flexible, confidential, and interest based process. The parties seek to reach an amicable dispute settlement with the assistance of the conciliator, who acts as a neutral third party.

Compulsory arbitration is arbitration of labor disputes which laws of some communities force the two sides, labor and management, to undergo. These laws mostly apply when the possibility of a strike seriously affects the public interest.
