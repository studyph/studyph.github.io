5. An aquarium is 72 cm long,35cm wide,and 60 cm high.how many liters of waters does it contain if its is 3/4 full?


Answer:

there are 113.4 Liters that the aquarium can contain if it is 3/4 full

Step-by-step explanation:

In the given problem, we can notice that it asks for volume of the water in "liters".

We all know that "CENTIMETER" IS A UNIT OF LENGTH. Thus, converting it directly to the liters is not possible. However, we are looking of the "volume". So basically, if we find the volume by using the FORMULA IN FINDING FOR VOLUME described by the equation below, we can have a unit of volume in cubic centimeters (cm^3).

V = L x W x H

Where L is length

W is width; and

H is height

Take note that the units used in the dimensions given is in centimeter (cm).

So getting the volume, we have

V = L x W x H

V = (72cm)(35cm)(60cm)

V= 151, 200 cm^3

Notice that the volume is not in liters, so we CONVERT CUBIC CENTIMETERS TO LITERS.

Recall that in 1 Liter, there are 1000 cubic centimeters of any liquid.

Converting the volume from cubic centimeters into liters, we have

V = (151,200 cm^3)(1 L / 1000cm^3)

V =  151.2 Liters

But recall that the problem only asks for "how many liters of water does it contain if it is 3/4 full". So we multiply the volume (full volume) we got by 3/4 in order to get the answer.

V(3/4) = (151.2L)(3/4)

V (3/4) = 113.4 L

Therefore, there are 113.4 Liters that the aquarium can contain if it is 3/4 full.
