23. how many moles of CO2 will be produced from 5.0 moles of C4H10?​


Solution:

Step 1: Write and balance the chemical equation.

2C₄H₁₀ + 13O₂ → 8CO₂ + 10H₂O

Step 2: Determine the mole ratio needed.

mole ratio = 2 mol C₄H₁₀ : 8 mol CO₂

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of CO₂ produced using the mole ratio.

[tex]\text{moles of CO₂ = 5.0 mol C₄H₁₀} × \frac{\text{8 mol CO₂}}{\text{2 mol C₄H₁₀}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{moles of CO₂ = 20. mol}}[/tex]
