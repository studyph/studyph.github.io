About Industrial Organization

Industrial Organization is a field of economics that studies the market as an institution, the conditions of business competition and strategic interactions between companies, industrial policies, and business decisions made by companies within a market framework.

This training looks at the market from three different perspectives, namely economic theory; applied business perspective; and institutional and legal perspectives. The focus of the training is divided equally between economic theory and business perspectives, but there is a significant legal component incorporated into the various topics. This training includes economic modeling, game theory, and several case studies.

Organizing is a step towards implementing a plan that has been prepared previously. Basically, the organization is just a tool to achieve the goals that have been planned.

The management and organizational process will include a series of activities starting with orientation towards the goals that are planned to be achieved and ending with an organizational framework that is equipped with the procedures, work methods, authority, personnel, and facilities needed.

Humans are the most important resource in the organization, which can determine the effectiveness of the established strategy. Organizational design is a process that seeks to match the key elements of an organization's structure, people, tasks, cultural decision-making systems, and so on, with the strategy decided in order to produce high performance. Basically, organization is a subject that is always changing, either slowly or rapidly, so the design must be adjusted.

24. List all the factors to consider when choosing a staffing firm and state why it is considered.​


Answer:

1. The Type of Talent You Need

First and foremost, when choosing a staffing agency, you need to ensure that the firm has the type of talent that you need. You certainly don’t want to partner with a firm that can’t deliver, no matter how specialized the positions are or how unique your industry is. Your agency must be able to provide the talent you seek.

2. Recruitment Techniques

The way a staffing firm recruits can be very telling of its potential success as your partner. Why would you need a staffing firm recruiting for you if it uses the same recruiting channels you use and finds the same mediocre candidates that you can find in house?

3. Additional Services

Do you want your staffing partner to only take care of recruiting and hiring? Or are you looking for support when it comes to skills testing, onboarding and candidate training, and other functions? Not all staffing firms offer the same additional services, so choose wisely. You need the staffing provider’s options to align with all of your needs and expectations.

4. Transparency                                                                        

Staffing, human resources, payroll, compliance—this world is already difficult enough to navigate. You don’t need an agency mystifying it any further. Rather, your staffing provider should help clarify and explain. A transparent staffing agency won’t hide soft costs, won’t be vague when answering your questions about its commitments to you or its guarantees.

5. Market Knowledge

You should be able to trust your staffing partner as a strategic advisor to your company. Staffing firms do more than just send warm bodies to your company. They should become trusted assets that you can count on for accurate, enlightening, and useful industry and market information.
