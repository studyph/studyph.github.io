2. discuss the implications of trade theories on the present economic scenario in the international trade​


Answer:

Trade theories have several implications on the present economic scenario in international trade.

The theory of comparative advantage, proposed by economist David Ricardo in the early 19th century, argues that countries should specialize in producing goods and services in which they have a lower opportunity cost, and trade with other countries for goods and services in which they have a higher opportunity cost. This theory has implications for how countries allocate resources and can lead to increased efficiency and economic growth.

The Heckscher-Ohlin theory, which builds on the idea of comparative advantage, suggests that a country's comparative advantage in production is determined by its endowments of factors of production, such as labor and capital. This theory has implications for how trade can affect the distribution of income within a country and can inform trade policies aimed at protecting certain industries or worker groups.

The New Trade Theory, which emerged in the late 20th century, suggests that trade can be driven by increasing returns to scale and the ability to access new markets and technologies. This theory has implications for the types of industries that are likely to experience the most growth in a globalized economy and for the types of policies that can promote innovation and productivity.

In the present economic scenario, these trade theories have an impact on the trade policies adopted by countries, for example, trade agreements, tariffs, and subsidies, and how these policies influence the allocation of resources, productivity, and competitiveness of different sectors in the economy. Additionally, these theories also have an impact on the distribution of benefits and costs of trade among different groups in society.

12. difference between International trade and International marketing?​


Answer:

While both international trade and marketing have to do with the international market, there are more differences between them than similarities. International trade refers to commerce between countries and involves the sale and purchase of products and services. International marketing refers to the promotion of products and services on the international market in order to reach and attract consumers from other countries. As an example, international trade covers all the sales that a company, such as Coca Cola, makes outside of the United States. Coca-Cola's international marketing, however, covers the different labels and flavors that the company sells in different regions of the world so as to intentionally capture market share based on consumer preference.

2. What is the importance of international trade on the economy?​

International trade plays a crucial role in the economy of countries around the world. Some of the importance of international trade on the economy are:

- It promotes economic growth: International trade allows countries to expand their markets beyond their borders, leading to an increase in economic growth and job opportunities.

- It increases competitiveness: International trade promotes competition, which encourages businesses to become more efficient and innovative, leading to lower costs and higher quality products.

- It allows countries to specialize: International trade allows countries to specialize in the production of goods and services that they have a comparative advantage in, leading to increased efficiency and productivity.

- It provides access to new markets: International trade provides access to new markets, which can help businesses increase their sales and profits.

- It promotes international relations: International trade promotes cooperation and understanding between countries, which can lead to improved political relationships and greater stability in the world.
