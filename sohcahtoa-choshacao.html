TRIGONOMETRY==============================

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{Direction:}} [/tex] Solve the following problems using SOH-CAH-TOA. Round your answer to the nearest unit. Use separate sheet for your answers.

[tex] \: [/tex]

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{Problem \ 1:}} [/tex] An airplane is observed to be approaching the air port. It is at a distance of 12 km from the point of observation and makes an angle of elevation of 50° What is the height above the ground?

Think:

» The referred angle is 50° that is in the airport, the adjacent side of it will be the ground 12 km away from the bottom altitude of the plane, and the altitude of the plane is the opposite. We need to find the height of the plane above ground, so we are going to use the TOA ratio or the tangent ratio.

Solve:[tex] \sf tan \ \theta = \frac{opposite}{adjacent} [/tex]

[tex] \sf tan \ 50° = \frac{x}{12km} [/tex]

[tex] \sf x = 12km(tan \ 50°) [/tex]

[tex] \sf x = 14km [/tex]

[tex] \boxed{\sf Final \ Answer: \large\purple{14 \ kilometers}} [/tex]

[tex] \: [/tex]

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{Problem \ 2:}} [/tex] A tree 100 ft tall casts a shadow 120 ft long. Find the angle of elevation of the sun.

Think:

» The referred angle is missing and the only given are the height of the tree that is opposite measures 100 ft and the distance of the casted shadow that measures 120 ft adjacent to the referred angle. To find the referred angle, we also gonna use the TOA ratio or the tangent ratio.

Solve:[tex] \sf tan \ \theta = \frac{opposite}{adjacent} [/tex]

[tex] \sf tan \ \theta = \frac{100ft}{120ft} [/tex]

[tex] \sf \theta = {tan}^{-1} (\frac{100}{120}) [/tex]

[tex] \sf \theta = 40° [/tex]

[tex] \boxed{\sf Final \ Answer: \large\purple{40 \ degrees}} [/tex]
