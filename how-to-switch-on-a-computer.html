2. How to compute the fuse and switch with their formula?


The formulas for computing the fuse and switch are as follows:

Fuse:

I = P/V, where I is the current in amperes, P is the power in watts, and V is the voltage in volts.

The rating of the fuse should be greater than or equal to the computed value of I.

Switch:

P = IV, where P is the power in watts, I is the current in amperes, and V is the voltage in volts.

The rating of the switch should be greater than or equal to the computed value of P.

Note that these formulas are based on the principle of electrical power, which states that power is equal to the product of current and voltage.

13. steps in switching on of the computer peripheral devices​


Answer:

STEP 1: Unpack the monitor and the computer make sure that they are clean and no packing materials that got stuck in the monitor or in the computer

STEP 2: Locate the monitor cable. They're are 2 types of monitor cables. The VGA or the DVI cable.

STEP 3: Connect one end of the cable to the monitor port on the back of the computer case, and the other end to the monitor. Hand-tighten the plastic-covered screws on the monitor cable to secure it.

STEP 4: Unpack the keyboard and determine whether it uses a USB(rectangular) connector or a PS/2 (round) connector. If it uses a USB connector, plug it into any of the USB ports on the back of the computer. If it uses a PS/2 connector, plug it into the purple keyboard port on the back of the computer.

STEP 5: Unpack the mouse and determine whether it uses a USB(rectangular) connector or a PS/2 (round) connector. If it uses a USB connector, plug it into any of the USB ports on the back of the computer. If it uses a PS/2 connector, plug it into the green mouse port on the back of the computer.

STEP 6: If you have external speakers or headphones, you can connect them to your computer's audio port (either on the front or the back of the computer case). Many computers have color-coded ports. Speakers or headphones connect to the greenport, and a microphone can connect to the pink port. The blueport is the line in, which can be used with other types of devices.

STEP 7: Locate the two power supply cables that came with your computer. Plug the first power supply cable into the back of thecomputer case, and then into a surge protector. Then, using the other cable, connect the monitor to the surge protector.

30. How important it is to follow the step-by-step procedures in starting up and switching off a computer​


Answer:

Following the step-by-step procedures in starting up and switching off a computer is very important for several reasons:

Avoiding hardware damage: Turning off the computer improperly or starting it up incorrectly can cause hardware damage, especially to the hard drive. This can result in data loss and costly repairs.

Saving energy: Turning off the computer properly helps to conserve energy and reduce electricity bills.

Maintaining system stability: Properly shutting down the computer helps to maintain system stability and prevent errors or crashes.

Protecting data: If you don't shut down the computer properly, you risk losing unsaved data and damaging important system files.

Extending the life of the computer: Following proper startup and shutdown procedures can help to extend the life of your computer by preventing unnecessary wear and tear on hardware components.

Overall, it's important to follow the step-by-step procedures in starting up and shutting down your computer to avoid hardware damage, save energy, maintain system stability, protect data, and extend the life of your computer.
