See the image for the graph.

Find the x-coordinate of the center, h, using the midpoint formula.

[tex]\sf h=\dfrac{-5+9}{2}=\dfrac{4}{2}\\\\h=2[/tex]

So, the center point is (2,−3).

Let the distance d₁ is from the vertex point (x,y) to focus (−5,−3), and the distance d₂ is from the focus (9,−3) to vertex point (x,y). Using the distance formula along the horizontal axis,

[tex]\sf d_1=x-(5)=x+5\\d_2=9-x_1[/tex]

The absolute difference of d₁ and d₂ is 10.

[tex]\sf |d_1-d_2|=10[/tex]

[tex]\sf d_1-d_2=10\\x+5-(9-x)=10\\x+5-9+x=10\\2x-4=10\\2x=10+4\\2x=14\\x=7[/tex]

Solve for the other vertex point.

[tex]\sf d_1-d_2=-10\\x+5-(9-x)=-10\\x+5-9+x=-10\\2x-4=-10\\2x=-10+4\\2x=-6\\x=-3[/tex]

Thus, the vertex points are (−3,−3) and (7,−3).

The transverse axis is 10. So,

[tex]\sf 2a=10\\a=5[/tex]

[tex]\sf c=9-2\\c=7[/tex]

[tex]\sf b^{2}=c^2-a^2 \\b^2=7^2-5^2=49-25\\b^2=24[/tex]

8. What are the foci of the hyperbola x2/144 - y2/25=1?


HYPERBOLA

[tex]\normalsize{\blue{\overline{\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad}}}[/tex]

What are the foci of the hyperbola:

[tex]\boxed{\sf{\dfrac{x^{2} }{144} - \dfrac{y^{2} }{25} = 1}}[/tex]

[tex]\large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:}[/tex]

The equation of a hyperbola in standard form is:

[tex]\sf{\dfrac{(x-h)^{2} }{a^{2} } - \dfrac{(y-k)^{2} }{b^{2} } =1 }[/tex]

The center is (h,k), the vertices are (h±a,k), and the co-vertices are (h,k ± b). Note that the vertices are on the hyperbola, but the co-vertices are not.

The transverse axis is the line segment with endpoints at the vertices and has length 2a. The conjugate axis is the line segment with endpoints at the co-vertices and has length 2b.

The distance from the center to each focus is c, where [tex]\sf{c^2=a^2+b^2}[/tex]. The foci lie on the same line as the transverse axis. If the hyperbola has a horizontal orientation, the coordinates of the foci are (h ± c,k).

To find the foci, first find the center (h,k). Then, calculate the distance from the center to the foci, which is c.

Find the center using the equation in standard form:

[tex]\begin{gathered} \begin{array}{ccc} \sf{\: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \dfrac{x^{2} }{144} - \dfrac{y^{2} }{25} = 1}\\\\ \sf{\dfrac{(x-0)^{2} }{144 } - \dfrac{(y-0)^{2} }{25} =1 }\\ \end{array} \end{gathered}[/tex]

The center is (0,0), which is the origin.

Calculate c.

[tex]\sf{\dfrac{x^{2} }{144} - \dfrac{y^{2} }{25} = 1}[/tex]

Where a² is 144 and b² is 25. Calculate c², then take the square root.

[tex]\boxed{\begin{array}{ccc} \sf{c^{2} =a^{2} +b^{2} \: \: \: } \\\\ \sf{c^{2} =144+25 } \\\\ \sf{c^{2} =169 \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: } \end{array}}[/tex]

Since c is a distance, it is positive. So c = [tex]\sf{\sqrt{169} =13}[/tex]

Since the hyperbola has the form [tex]\sf{\dfrac{(x-h)^{2} }{a^{2} } - \dfrac{(y-k)^{2} }{b^{2} } =1 }[/tex], it has a horizontal orientation. This means you should add and subtract c from the x-coordinate of the center to find the foci. So, the foci are (0+13, 0)=(-13, 0) and (0-13, 0) = (13, 0).

Hence, the foci is (-13, 0) and (13, 0)

11. What happens to the shape of an ellipse as the distance between the two foci increases?​


Answer:

The larger the distance between the foci, the larger the eccentricity of the ellipse. In the limiting case where the foci are on top of each other (an eccentricity of 0), the figure is actually a circle.

24. standard form of hyperbola where foci at (+-5,0) and vertices at (+-4,0).find the Asymptotes


foci (±5, 0), vertices (±4, 0)

Since the foci and vertices have the same y-coordinate, this is a horizontal hyperbola.

The foci are at c distance from the center, which means that the center is at the origin (0, 0) and c = 5. The vertices are at a distance from the center, which means a = 4.

Next, we need to find the value of b. We can use a² + b² = c² to do so.

a² + b² = c²

b² = c² - a²

b² = 25 - 16

b² = 9, b = 3

The equation of the hyperbola is [tex] \frac{x^{2}}{16} - \frac{y^{2}}{9} = 1 [/tex]

The asymptotes of a horizontal hyperbola are y = ±(b/a)x.

y = ± (3/4)x

y = ±3x/4, 4y = ±3x

The asymptotes are 3x ± 4y = 0.
