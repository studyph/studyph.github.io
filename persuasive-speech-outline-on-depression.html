Answer:

I. Introduction

A. Definition of depression

B. Prevalence of depression

II. Causes of depression

A. Biological factors

1. Genetics

2. Hormonal imbalances

B. Psychological factors

1. Negative thinking patterns

2. Trauma or abuse

C. Environmental factors

1. Stressful life events

2. Lack of social support

III. Symptoms of depression

A. Changes in mood

1. Persistent feelings of sadness or hopelessness

2. Loss of interest in activities that were once enjoyable

B. Changes in physical health

1. Changes in appetite and weight

2. Sleep disturbances

3. Fatigue

C. Changes in behavior

1. Withdrawal from social activities

2. Decreased productivity or difficulty completing tasks

IV. Treatment of depression

A. Therapy

1. Cognitive-behavioral therapy

2. Interpersonal therapy

B. Medication

1. Selective serotonin reuptake inhibitors (SSRIs)

2. Tricyclic antidepressants

C. Lifestyle changes

1. Exercise and physical activity

2. Adequate sleep

3. Healthy diet

V. Conclusion

A. Depression is a common and treatable mental health condition

B. A combination of therapy, medication, and lifestyle changes can be effective in managing depression
