7. Heterozygous tall (Tt) and dwarf(tt) pea plant are crossed. The ratio in the offspring shall be A. All Tall B. All dwarf C. 3 Tall and 1 Dwarf D. 1 Tall and 1 Dwarf


Answer:

D. 1 tall and 1 dwarf

Explanation:

In a cross between heterozygous tall plants (Tt) and a homozygous dwarf plant (tt), the ratio in the offspring shall be 1 tall : 1 dwarf. This is because the heterozygous plant will make two types of gametes, one type having a T allele and the other having a t allele. Hence, the correct answer is ‘1 tall : 1 dwarf’

13. Examples of Dwarf Planets​


EXAMPLES OF DWARF PLANETS

Like any other planets in the solar system and other interstellar bodies, a dwarf planet is an interstellar body and is somewhat round because of its own gravity. However, these planets does not have the mass and capability to clear out other smaller celestial bodies along its orbit around the Sun.

THE FIVE DWARF PLANETS

1.) Ceres - Named after the Roman goddess of Agriculture or Demeter in Greek Mythology, it is located between the orbit of Mars and Jupiter.

2.) Haumea - Located beyond the orbit of Neptune, or at the Kuiper Belt.

3.) Makemake - Located at the Kuiper Belt.

4.) Pluto - Named after the Roman god of the underworld or Hades in Greek Mythology. Located next to Neptune.

5.) Eris - Named after the Greek goddess of strife and discord, located beyond Pluto.

15. What type of star is the sun?A. Red giantB. White dwarfC. Yellow dwarfD. Blue giant​


What type of star is the sun?

✅ The type of star that the sun is a Yellow Dwarf star. A yellow dwarf star is a type of main-sequence star that is similar to our sun. It is called a yellow dwarf because it has a yellowish color due to its surface temperature of about 5,500 degrees Celsius (9,932 degrees Fahrenheit). The sun is classified as a G-type main-sequence star, which is a specific type of yellow dwarf star.

Yellow dwarf stars are relatively small and stable stars that are powered by nuclear fusion reactions in their cores. They are the most common type of star in the universe and are estimated to make up about 90% of all stars in the Milky Way galaxy.

The sun is an average-sized yellow dwarf star with a mass of about 1.989 × 10³⁰ kilograms (333,000 times the mass of Earth) and a radius of about 695,700 kilometers (109 times the radius of Earth). It is about 4.6 billion years old and is expected to remain in its current state for about another 5 billion years before it evolves into a red giant star.

16. In the second experiment of Mendel's work, you cross a dwarf garden pea with another dwarf pea. What will the first generation of peas look like? Choices: 25% dwarf 75% dwarf 50% dwarf all dwarf


In the second experiment of Mendel's work, where a dwarf garden pea is crossed with another dwarf pea, all the first-generation peas will be dwarf. This is because the dwarf trait is a homozygous recessive trait and both parent plants are homozygous for the dwarf allele. Therefore, all the offspring will inherit two copies of the dwarf allele, resulting in a dwarf phenotype. This is known as a monohybrid cross where only one trait is being studied.

22. Cicle and label dwarf stars,red giants,blue giants and main sequence ​


Answer:

Dwarf stars, red giants, blue giants, and main sequence stars are classifications of stars based on their properties and characteristics, particularly their size, temperature, and age. Here is a brief explanation and labeling of each:

Dwarf stars: These are relatively small stars, typically less than half the size of the sun, with low luminosity and cooler surface temperatures. They are further classified into:

a) Red dwarfs: The smallest and most common type of dwarf star, with a reddish color due to their relatively low surface temperature.

b) White dwarfs: These are the remnants of stars that have exhausted their nuclear fuel and collapsed to a very small size, typically the size of Earth.

Red giants: These are large, relatively cool stars that have exhausted the hydrogen fuel in their cores and have started to burn helium. They are characterized by their reddish color, high luminosity, and large size.

Blue giants: These are massive, hot stars that burn hydrogen at a very high rate, resulting in a bluish color. They are relatively rare and short-lived, typically lasting only a few million years.

Main sequence stars: These are stars that are still in the process of burning hydrogen in their cores and are in a stable state of balance between gravity and radiation pressure. They are characterized by their size, temperature, and luminosity, and are further classified based on their spectral type, from the hottest O-type stars to the coolest M-type stars.

27. technical definition of dwarf​

An unusually short, small person is sometimes called a dwarf. There are many different genetic medical conditions that can cause a person to be classified as a little person or a dwarf.

Some people are offended by dwarf, preferring to be called "little people," "LP," or "people of short stature." The general condition that leads to delayed growth is called "dwarfism."
