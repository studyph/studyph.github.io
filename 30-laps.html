4. A rectangular lap pool measures 50 feet long by 30 feet wide. If it needs to be filles to four feet deep and each cubic foot holds 5 gallons, how many gallons will it take to fill the lap pool?


Step-by-step explanation:

Firstly, we need to establish the formula for calculating the volume of a rectangular pool. The formula is:

Volume = Length x Width x Depth

In this case, the length of the pool is 50 feet, the width is 30 feet, and the depth required is four feet. Substituting these values in the formula, we can calculate the volume of the pool as follows:

Volume = 50 x 30 x 4

Volume = 6,000 cubic feet

Next, we need to convert cubic feet to gallons. It is given that each cubic foot holds five gallons. Therefore, we can multiply the volume in cubic feet by five to get the total number of gallons needed to fill the pool. The calculation is as follows:

Total gallons = Volume x Conversion factor

Total gallons = 6,000 x 5

Total gallons = 30,000 gallons

Therefore, it would take 30,000 gallons to fill the rectangular lap pool that measures 50 feet long by 30 feet wide and four feet deep.

11. A runner covered a 400-m track,divide into 4 laps of 10m each. She completed the first lap in 15s, the second lap in 10s,the third lap in 13s, the fourth lap in 12s. What her average Speed?​


Answer:

The total distance covered by the runner is 400m.

The total time taken to cover the distance is:

15s + 10s + 13s + 12s = 50s

Therefore, the average speed of the runner is:

Average speed = Total distance / Total time

Average speed = 400m / 50s

Average speed = 8 m/s

Therefore, the average speed of the runner is 8 m/s.
