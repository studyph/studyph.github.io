1. What compounds other than naphthol can be used for molisch's test?


Molisch's test is a test for the presence or determination of carbohydrates in a solution by the use of Molisch reagent or alpha-napthol and CONCENTRATED SULFURIC ACID (H2SO4, used preferably and often). When the test solution inside a test tube is added with the napthol, it is inclined before adding the concentrated sulfuric acid. The reaction will yield a purple ring complex at the junction of the two fluids. If sulfuric acid is not available, concentrated hydrochloric acid (HCl) can be used. The result is still the same.
