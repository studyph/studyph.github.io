Answer:

It is not accurate to say that monkeys have been cloned, paving the way for human cloning. While there have been several instances of primates, including monkeys, being cloned using the same technique that was used to clone the first mammal (Dolly the sheep), these instances have been limited and have not necessarily paved the way for human cloning.

Human cloning, which involves creating a genetically identical copy of a human being, remains a highly controversial and complex issue. While some scientists have expressed interest in exploring the potential of human cloning for medical purposes, such as growing organs for transplantation, the technology, and ethics involved in human cloning are still being debated.

In general, the successful cloning of monkeys does not necessarily mean that human cloning is imminent or even possible. The cloning of primates, including monkeys, remains a difficult and controversial area of scientific research, and there are many ethical and technical hurdles to overcome before human cloning could become a reality.
