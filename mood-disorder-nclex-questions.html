23. What is substance induced mood disorders?


Substance induced mood disorders

Substance induced mood disorder is a chronic disorder that requires regular treatment. It is a situation where a person uses illegal drugs or substances, prescribed drugs or drugs that can be purchased at pharmacies in inappropriate doses. In addition, people with substance induced mood disorder also consume excessive doses of alcohol. Patients can stop or change their habits. This is different from addiction. Addiction is a disease that makes it difficult for a person to escape dependence. Substances that are often abused include: cigarettes and other tobacco products, marijuana, cocaine, and alcohol.

A person can be called abusing prescription or over-the-counter drugs if they take drugs prescribed for someone else, take more than the recommended dose of drugs, and take drugs for non-medical reasons. Some of the symptoms experienced are loss of interest in things one used to enjoy, spending more time alone, disrupted sleep cycles, problems with social interactions, rapid emotional changes, and often making excuses or lying to use drugs.
