what is aromatic oil?

ANSWER

Aromatic oils are concentrated plant-based substances that are used for their pleasant scents. Aromatic Oil

Aromatic oils are concentrated plant extracts that are used for their pleasant scents. They are typically made by distilling plant materials, such as flowers, leaves, bark, or roots, in a carrier oil. Aromatic oils are used in perfumes, candles, soaps, and other products for their pleasant scents. They can also be used in aromatherapy, which is a form of alternative medicine that uses essential oils to improve physical and emotional well-being. Aromatic oils are typically highly concentrated and should be used with caution to avoid skin irritation.

8. aldehydes. description and uses​


Description of Aldehydes

-Aldehyde, any of a class of organic compounds in which a carbon atom shares a double bond with an oxygen atom, a single bond with a hydrogen atom, and a single bond with another atom or group of atoms (designated R in general chemical formulas and structure diagrams).

25. - Aldehydes and Ketones?​


Answer:

Aldehydes derive their name from the dehydration of alcohols. Aldehydes contain the carbonyl group bonded to at least one hydrogen atom. Ketones contain the carbonyl group bonded to two carbon atoms. Aldehydes and ketones are organic compounds which incorporate a carbonyl functional group, C=O.
