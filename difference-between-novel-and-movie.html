16. what is the difference between novel and drama and their similarities?​


Answer:

Similarities

The primary similarities between a novel and a drama are that they both have plots, complete with rising action, climax, falling action, and resolution; they both have conflicts, characters, and themes. ... It does not typically have a narrator, someone who can interpret characters' actions or thoughts, as some novels do.

Difference

The main difference between a novel and a drama is derived from the formatting of each piece. A novel is generally written in basic prose while a drama is almost exclusively written in dialogue. Both types of work have similar story elements such as characters, plot, settings, etc.
