3. Three point charges are placed at the following points on the x-axis: +2 μC at x = 0, - 3 μC at x = 40 cm, - 5μC at x = 120 cm. Find the force on the - 3 μC charge Find the force on the - 5 μC charge


Answer:

a. The force on the - 3 μC charge is 0.3375 N

b. The force on the - 5 μC charge is 0.0625 N

Explanation:

In our case, we are to find the force of attraction between charges of different polarities. Here, we are to use Coulomb's Law.

For the formula, we will use:

[tex]F= 9x10^{9} \frac{N.m^2}{C^2} (\frac{qq'}{r^2} )[/tex]

where

F       is the force of attraction between charges, unit is in Newtons (N)

q, q'  are the charges, units is in coulombs (C)

r        is the distance between charges, unit is in meters (m)

For the given information

Let us assign for the variables:

q1 = +2 μC

q2 = - 3 μC

q3 = - 5 μC

r12 = 40 cm = 0.40 m    is the distance between q1 and q2

r13 = 120 cm = 1.2 m      is the distance between q1 and q3

Solving the problem

1. First is to find the force between q1 and q2. Use the formula for force, we have:

Take note that we take the absolute values of the product between charges.

[tex]F_{12} = 9x10^{9} \frac{N.m^2}{C^2} (\frac{ qq'}{r^2} )[/tex]

[tex]F_{12}= 9x10^{9} \frac{N.m^2}{C^2} (\frac{(+2x10^{-6}C)(-3x10^{-6}C)}{(0.40m)^2} )[/tex]

[tex]F_{12} = 0.3375N[/tex]

2. Next is to find the force of attraction between q1 and q3. Use again the formula for force, we have:

[tex]F_{13} = 9x10^{9} \frac{N.m^2}{C^2} (\frac{qq'}{r^2} )[/tex]

[tex]F_{13} = 9x10^{9} \frac{N.m^2}{C^2} (\frac{(+2x10^{-6)}C) (-5x10^{-6} C)}{(1.2m)^2} )[/tex]

[tex]F_{13} =0.0625N[/tex]

Therefore, the force between +2 μC and - 3 μC is 0.3375 N and the force between +2 μC and - 5 μC  is 0.0625 N.

This means that the farther the distance between charges, the lesser the force of attraction.

6. Particles of charge +75,+48,and -85 μC are placed in a line. The center one is 0.35 from each of the others. Calculate the net force on the +75 μC charge due to the other two.?


Answer:

The net force is - 147.39 N.

Explanation:

Here, we are to solve for the net force on the first charge due to the remaining two charges. We are going to use the concept of electric force and will consider the polarities of the charges whether they repel or attract. Electric force is a vector and we consider directions to solve for the net force.

For the formula, we will use:

F = k [tex]\frac{q.q'}{r^2}[/tex]

where

F               is the electric force, unit is in Newtons, N

k               is the permittivity constant, 9 x 10⁹ N·m²/C²

q and q'   are the charges, units are in Coulombs, C

r               is the separation between charges, unit is in meter, m

Given information

q1 = +75 μC

q2 = +48 μC

q3 = -85 μC

r12 = 0.35 m     (let us assume that the given unit in this problem is in meter)

r13 = 0.7 m

∑Fx = ?

Solving the problem

1. First, let us solve for the electric force between charges  +75μC and +48μC and their separation is 0.35 m, so we have:

F12 = k [tex]\frac{q.q'}{r^2}[/tex]

F12 = 9 x 10⁹ N·m²/C² [tex]\frac{(75x10^{-6}C)(48x10^{-6}C)}{(0.35m)^2}[/tex]

F12 = 264.49 N

Take note that the charges repels with each other because both of them are positive, therefore +75μC goes to the left or the electric force is said to be negative.

2. Next, let us solve for the electric force between +75 μC and -85 μC and their separation 0.7 m, so we have:

F13 = k [tex]\frac{q.q'}{r^2}[/tex]

F13 = 9 x 10⁹ N·m²/C² [tex]\frac{(75x10^{-6}C)(85x10^{-6}C)}{(0.7m)^2}[/tex]

F13 = 117.09 N

Take note that these charges attracts one another because they have different poles. So +75 μC goes to the right in order to be attracted to -85 μC. This time the electric force is positive.

3. Now, we are ready to solve for the net force. Let us use summation of forces along the horizontal to solve for it, we have:

∑Fx = F12 + F13

∑Fx = - 264.49 N  + 117.09 N

∑Fx = - 147.39 N
