Explanation:

The cell wall is the extracellular matrix that envelops each cell as a protective and forming cell. The cell wall is composed of cellulose when the cells are young and lignin accumulates when the cells start to age. Cell walls are only found in plants. The cell membrane is a semipermeable membrane in a cell that surrounds and encloses the contents of the cytoplasm and nucleoplasm. The cell membrane separates the cell from the surrounding interstitial fluid. The formation of cell membranes is carried out with the basic ingredients in the form of lipoproteins which are formed by fats and proteins. Leucoplast serves as a storage place for starch (amyloplast), lipid (elaioplas), and protein (proteinoplas) in plants. Leucoplasts are also used by plants to help the process of making amino acids and fatty acids.

Vacuoles are the largest organelles enclosed by a cell membrane. When observed using a light microscope, the vacuole has a shape like an empty and transparent space. Nucleus or cell nucleus is a cell organelle that functions to regulate all cell activities. The nucleus is the largest cell that contains genetic information in the form of DNA and is round to oval in shape, depending on the type of cell. The cytoplasm is the part of the cell that is enclosed by the plasma membrane. The cytoplasm consists of water, proteins, carbohydrates, fats, minerals and vitamins. The cytoplasm serves as a storage area for cell chemicals that are important for cell metabolism, such as enzymes, ions, sugars, fats and proteins.
