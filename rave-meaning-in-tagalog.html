15. 2. Two planes make a 600 mile flight, one flying 50mph faster than theother. Of the slower plane takes 2 hours longer, what is the rave of eachplane?With Solution​


Answer:

100 MPH AND 150 MPH

Step-by-step explanation:

Since S = D/T

Thus

The time it takes the faster plane to make the flight is [tex]\frac{600}{s+50}[/tex]

The time it takes the slower plane to make the flight is [tex]\frac{600}{s}[/tex]

The difference in these two times is 2 hours, so

[tex]\frac{600}{s}[/tex] - [tex]\frac{600}{s+50}[/tex] = 2

([tex]\frac{s+50}{s+50}[/tex])([tex]\frac{600}{s}[/tex]) - [tex](\frac{s}{s})[/tex][tex](\frac{600}{s+50} )[/tex] = 2

[tex]\frac{600(s)+30,000-600(s)}{s^2+50(s)}[/tex] = 2

[tex]\frac{30,000}{s^2+50(s)}[/tex] = 2

[tex](s^2+50(s))(\frac{30,000}{s^2+50(s)}) = (s^2 +50(s))2[/tex]

30,000 = 2[tex]s^2[/tex] + 100s

15,000 = [tex]s^2[/tex] + 50s

[tex]s^2[/tex] + 50s - 15,000 = 0

We can solve for s using quadratic equation [tex]s = \frac{-50±\sqrt{50^2-4(1)(-15000)} }{2(1)}[/tex]

[tex]s = \frac{-50±\sqrt{62500} }{2}[/tex]

[tex]s = \frac{-50±250 }{2}[/tex]

So s = -150 or s = 100.

We'll ignore the negative answer because the planes are not flying backward.

So the slower plane is flying at 100 mph and completes the trip in 6 hours

Thus, the faster plane is flying at 100 + 50 = 150 mph and completes the trip in 4 hours.
