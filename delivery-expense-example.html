Speeches are a great way to get your message across. They can be used in a variety of situations, from the boardroom to the classroom.

Here are some tips for delivering a speech:

-Make sure you're comfortable with what you're going to say. If you're not, practice it until you feel confident.

-Keep your audience in mind at all times. Don't talk about yourself or tell stories that only apply to you--focus on what everyone else needs to hear first!

-Be prepared for questions from your audience. Answer them as honestly as possible, but also make sure that they don't stray off topic!

Here are some examples of different types of speeches:

-An informative speech: An informative speech is like an encyclopedia of all things related to the topic at hand. It should be very detailed and thorough, but it should also be organized in such a way that it's easy to follow along with the speaker's ideas.

-A persuasive speech: Persuasive speeches are meant to persuade the audience that their opinion is correct. They should have clear evidence or logic behind them, as well as some sort of emotional appeal at the end so that people will be more likely to agree with them after listening (and maybe even change their minds!).

-A comedic speech: Comedy speeches usually involve humor or sarcasm from start to finish--they're meant to entertain as well as inform!
