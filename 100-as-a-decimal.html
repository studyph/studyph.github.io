15. B. How to rename fractions whose denominators are 10 and 100 into a decimal number? In renaming fractions whose denominators are 10 and 100 into a decimal numbe the (5) will be the number after the decimal point. If the denominator is 10 he number will have (6) decimal place. If 100, the number will have (7) decimal places.​


The number before the decimal point is called the whole part or integral part, whereas the number after the decimal point is called the fractional part.

Take the given fraction's numerator. Then mark the decimal point after one place or two places places from right towards left if the given fraction's denominator is 10 or 100 respectively.

Note that: insert zeroes at the left of the numerator if the numerator has fewer digits.

Answer:

In renaming fractions whose denominators are 10 and 100 into a decimal number the fractional part will be the number after the decimal point. If the denominator is 10 he number will have one decimal place. If 100, the number will have two decimal places.
