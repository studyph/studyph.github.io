8. why is barium have a larger atomic radius than magnesium


Why is barium have a larger atomic radius than magnesium?

Barium is in period 6 i.e. ,it has 6 electron shells. magnesium is in period 3 i.e. ,it has only 3 electrons shells. As such, barium, which has a greater umber of electron shells has a larger atomic radius.Magnesium's atom is smaller than barium's atom and barium has more shielding than magnesium. This increased shielding counteracts barium's greater number of protons in the nucleus. The increased shielding and increase in atomic radius gives barium a smaller effective nuclear charge when compared to magnesium.

10. What is the mass of 4.02 mol Barium nitrite Ba(NO2)2?​


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of Ba(NO₂)₂.

molar mass = (137.3 g/mol × 1) + (14.01 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 4)

molar mass = 229.32 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the mass of Ba(NO₂)₂.

[tex]\text{mass of Ba(NO₂)₂ = 4.02 mol Ba(NO₂)₂} × \frac{\text{229.32 g Ba(NO₂)₂}}{\text{1 mol Ba(NO₂)₂}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of Ba(NO₂)₂ = 922 g}}[/tex]

19. What are the mass of 4.02 mol Barium nitrite Ba(NO2)2


Answer:

921.9468 grams

••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••

In this case, being the atomic mass:

Ba: 137.34 g/moleN: 14 g/moleO: 16 g/mole

The molecular mass of Ba(NO₂)₂ can be calculated as:

Ba(NO₂)₂= 137.34 g/mole× 2× (14 g/mole +2×16 g/mole)

Ba(NO₂)₂= 229.34 g/mole

Then, the mass of 4.02 mol Ba(NO₂)₂ can be calculated as:

mass= 4.02 moles× 229.34 g/mole

mass= 921.9468 grams

So, the mass of 4.02 mol Ba(NO₂)₂ is 921.9468 grams.
