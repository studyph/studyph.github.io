4. Explain Social and culturalInnovation please pa answer po ​


Answer:

Cultural Innovation and Social innovations are new social practices that aim to meet social needs in a better way than the existing solutions, resulting from - for example - working conditions, education, community development or health. These ideas are created with the goal of extending and strengthening civil society.

Social innovation is the process of developing and deploying effective solutions to challenging and often systemic social and environmental issues in support of social progress. Social innovation is not the prerogative or privilege of any organizational form or legal structure.

A culture of innovation is an environment that supports creative thinking and advances efforts to extract economic and social value from knowledge, and, in doing so, generates new or improved products, services or processes

27. what is social and cultural innovation?give real life example.​


Examples of social and cultural innovations:

1. Distance Learning

2. Fair Trade System

3. Microfinancing

4.Open Source

5. Human Rights Organization (HAM)

EXPLANATION:

Innovation is not only about business, but also about having a positive social and cultural impact. The key lies in the values that benefit society, not just individuals. Five social innovations that have changed the world:

1. Distance Learning

Currently, various universities have distance learning or e-learning programs that allow us to learn from all corners of the world.

2. Fair Trade System

The Fair Trade movement has succeeded in promoting equality in commodity trade from developing to developed countries, so that workers in developing countries receive a fairer outcome.

3. Microfinancing

The concept, which was first initiated by Grameen Bank, provides credit to the common people without collateral, so that the small people have incentives to increase their income.

4.Open Source

The open source method that gives every one universal access to contribute has transformed many fields.

5. Human Rights Organization

Once upon a time, there was no such thing as Human Rights. Over time, people have become more aware that there is a need for the concept of Human Rights. Thanks to the development of human rights defender organizations, such as Amnesty International, human rights have become a concept that is commonly understood by today's society.

28. liberating education through innovation


"Liberating education through innovation" means taking education into the next levels by applying to the education system various existing and future forms of innovation such as technology. The concept of innovation has indeed the power to transform and liberate something from its old and outdated form into an up-to-date and more relatable form.

The phrase "liberating education through innovation" also means that old systems in education can already be replaced by new systems. An example for this is the idea of exploring the usage of online tools in promoting education. This includes the utilization of online classes (instead of the traditional classes within the four walls of the classroom), online books (instead of the traditional text books), online trainings (instead of the traditional one-day or multi-day trainings), and other various forms of online materials. By doing this maximization of online materials, education can be made more accessible to anyone, wherever and whenever.
