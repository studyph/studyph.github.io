8. Written work #2.1 Answer the following question in a yellow paper. Minimum of 150 words for each questions. • Why is planning important in the success of an organization? • How is controlling related to planning? • Enumerate the different steps in planning and discuss each briefly.​


Answer:

Planning is an essential component of organizational success for several reasons. Firstly, it provides direction and focus for an organization by outlining specific goals and objectives that need to be achieved. This helps to ensure that resources are used effectively and efficiently to meet those goals. Without planning, an organization may lack a clear sense of direction and may be unable to make informed decisions about how to allocate resources.

Secondly, planning helps to anticipate and mitigate potential problems. By identifying potential risks and developing strategies to address them, an organization can proactively prepare for potential challenges and minimize their impact. Additionally, by regularly reviewing and updating plans, an organization can stay responsive to changes in its internal and external environment.

Thirdly, planning enables an organization to make the best use of its resources. By identifying objectives, the required resources, the available resources and the gaps between the two, the management can focus on how to best use the resources available to achieve the objectives.

Controlling is related to planning in that it is used to ensure that the organization stays on track to achieve its goals and objectives. This includes monitoring progress towards goals, analyzing variances, and taking corrective action when necessary. By regularly measuring and evaluating performance against established goals and plans, an organization can identify and address issues that may be preventing it from achieving its objectives.

The different steps in planning are:

Setting Objectives: The first step in planning is to establish clear and measurable goals for the organization.

Situation Analysis: This step involves analyzing the internal and external factors that may affect the organization and its ability to achieve its objectives.

Strategy Formulation: Based on the situation analysis, the management formulate the strategy for achieving the objectives.

Implementation: After strategy has been formulated, the next step is to put the plans into action. This step involves developing detailed plans and allocating resources.

Monitoring and Control: This step involves monitoring the progress of plans and taking corrective action when necessary.

Review and Evaluation: After the completion of the plan, the management must evaluate the plan to see if the objectives have been achieved, and identify any areas that need improvement.

In conclusion, Planning is an essential component of organizational success as it provides direction, focus, help organizations anticipate and mitigate potential problems and make the best use of the resources. Controlling is related to planning as it ensures that the organization stays on track to achieve its goals and objectives.
