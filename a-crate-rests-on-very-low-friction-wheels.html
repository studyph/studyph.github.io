[tex]____________________________________[/tex]

A crate is at rest and force of 500 N is applied to it. If the frictional force

is 300 N, determine the net force.

Given:

Frictional Force : 300 N

Applied Force : 500 N

Solution:

Since the two forces are in opposite direction, both forces must be subtracted.

[tex]\sf F_{net} = F-fk[/tex]

Instead of

[tex]\sf F_{net}=f_{1} +f_{2}[/tex]

Solve

[tex]\sf F_{net} = F-fk[/tex]

[tex]\sf F_{net} = 500 \: N-300 \: N[/tex]

[tex]\sf F_{net} =\boxed{\red{\sf200 \: N}}[/tex]

Therefore, the net force of the crate is 200 Newtons.

27. A 25.0 kg cart is being pulled horizontally by a force of 200 N against a frictional force. The crate accelerates from rest to speed of 10.0 m/sÂ² in 2.0 seconds. Find the acceleration of the crate and the frictional force.


The acceleration of the crate and the frictional force : 5 m/s² and 75 N

Further explanation  

Newton's 2nd law explains that the acceleration produced by the resultant force on an object is proportional and in line with the resultant force and inversely proportional to the mass of the object  

∑F = m. a  

F = force, N  

m = mass = kg  

a = acceleration due to gravity, m / s²

The crate accelerates from rest to speed of 10.0 m/s in 2.0 seconds.

The acceleration :

[tex]\tt vf=vi+at\\\\10=0+a.2\Rightarrow vi=0(from~rest)\\\\10=a.2\\\\a=5~m/s^2[/tex]

The frictional force

[tex]\tt \sum F=m.a\\\\F-F_r=m.a\Rightarrow F_r=friction~force\\\\200-F_r=25\times 5\\\\200-F_r=125\\\\F_r=\boxed{\bold{75~N}}[/tex]

[tex]F_s=[/tex] μmg

[tex]F_k=[/tex] μmg

μ = [tex]\frac{F_k}{mg}[/tex]
