30. Which of the following is an example of psychological barrier?A. ability to read mindsB. dissatisfaction to customer serviceC. inattentiveness to detailD. poor listening skills​


One psychological barrier is poor listening skill. Communication difficulties are referred to as psychological or emotional barriers when they are caused by a person's psychological condition. Opinions, attitudes, status consciousness, emotions, lack of attention, reliance on the written word, and poor memory are a few examples. Personal discomfort brought on by illness, poor vision, or hearing issues might lead to physiological hurdles.

Typical types of psychological obstacles include:

1. Erroneous assumptions

Because the sender presumes that the recipient has grasped the message, communication breakdown may happen if the sender fails to convey instructions clearly. However, the recipient may have only partially or not at all understood what was communicated.

2. Anger

A person's emotional condition might be a harmful communication barrier. The recipient will read the message differently if the sender is upset when the communication is sent.

3. Attitudes and Values

An attitude is a pre-learned disposition that is intimately related to a person's core values and worldview.

4. Poor listening

Major communication issues might result from poor listening skills. If people would pay close enough attention when listening to the message, misunderstandings and disputes may be reduced. Due to a variety of distractions, emotions, enthusiasm, indifference, aggression, and wandering attention, the majority of individuals do not listen very well.
