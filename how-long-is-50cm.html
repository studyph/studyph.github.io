28. A simple pendulum is 50cm long and a frequency of 3Hz. Find its wavelength


SOLUTION:

A simple pendulum oscillates back and forth with a frequency given by:

[tex] f = \frac{1}{(2\pi)} \times \sqrt{(\frac{g}{L})} [/tex]

where L is the length of the pendulum and g is the acceleration due to gravity.

In this case, we are given that the pendulum has a length of [tex]L = 50[/tex] cm and a frequency of [tex]f = 3[/tex] Hz. We can solve for the acceleration due to gravity:

[tex] g = \frac{(4\pi ^2 L)}{T^2} [/tex]

where [tex] T [/tex] is the period of the pendulum, which is the inverse of the frequency:

[tex] T = \frac{1}{f} [/tex]

Substituting the values given, we get:

[tex] T = \frac{1}{3} = 0.3333 \: \mathrm{s} [/tex]

[tex] g = \frac{(4\pi ^2 (50))}{(0.3333)^2} \approx 981 \: \mathrm{cm/s^2} [/tex]

Now, the wavelength of the pendulum can be found using the formula:

[tex] \lambda = \frac{v}{f} [/tex]

where [tex] v [/tex] is the velocity of the wave. For a pendulum, the velocity is given by:

[tex] v = \sqrt{(gL)} [/tex]

so we have:

[tex] \lambda = \sqrt{\frac{(gL)}{f}} = \sqrt{\frac{981 \times 50}{3}} \approx 31.8 \: \mathrm{cm} [/tex]

Hence, the wavelength of the pendulum is approximately 31.8 cm
