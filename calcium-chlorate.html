12. lithium chlorate is decomposed by heat to form lithium chloride and ixygen gas, convert it to balanced chemical equation


In order to determine the balanced equation for the reaction, we first need to define the chemical formula of the reactants and products involved.

Lithium chlorate is a solid compound composed of the ions lithium (Li^+) and chlorate (ClO3^-), thus, will have a chemical formula of LiClO3.
Lithium chloride is a salt composed of lithium ion (Li^+) and chlorine ion (Cl^-), thus, will have a chemical formula of LiCl.
Oxygen gas exists as diatomic molecule which has the chemical formula of O2.

We can then show the reaction through a chemical equation. That is,
                  Δ
LiClO3(s) ------> LiCl(s) + O2(g)

*The triangle on top of the arrow indicates that for the reaction to proceed, heat is required.

Balancing a chemical equation requires that the left and rights side of the equation should have equal number of atoms of the elements involved in the reaction. We then have the final balanced equation of,
                 Δ
2LiClO3(s) ------> 2LiCl(s) + 3O2(g)

15. How many grams of potassium chlorate is required to yield 3 L of oxygen at STP?


Hello!

How many grams of potassium chlorate is required to yield 3 L of oxygen at STP ?

We have the following data:

Molar mass of KClO3 (potassium chlorate)

K = 1*(39 u) = 39 u

Cl = 1*(35.5 u) = 35.5 u

O = 3*(16 u) = 48 u

------------------------------------

Molar mass of KClO3 = 39 + 35.5 + 48 = 122.5 g/mol

In STP (Standard Temperature and Pressure), one mole of gas occupies 22.4 liters, so we have the following relation, let us see:

122.5 g of HClO3 ------------- 22.4 L (in STP)

y g of HClO3 ------------------- 3 L (in STP)

[tex]\dfrac{122.5}{y} = \dfrac{22.4}{3}[/tex]

multiply the means by the extremes

[tex]22.4*y = 122.5*3[/tex]

[tex]22.4\:y = 367.5[/tex]

[tex]y = \dfrac{367.5}{22.4}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{y \approx 16.4\:grams\:of\:HClO_3}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

Answer:

16.4 grams of potassium chlorate

24. Difference between a calcium atom and a calcium ion


The difference between a calcium atom and a calcium ion is the number of electrons

Electron atom Ca = 20 while calcium ion (Ca²⁺)= 18

Further explanation

The Atomic Number (Z) indicates the number of protons in an atom of an element. If the atom is neutral then the number of protons will be equal to the number of electrons. So the atomic number can also indicate the number of electrons.

 So atomic number = number of protons = number of electrons

Mass Number (A) is the sum of protons and neutrons

Mass Number (A) = Number of protons + Number of Neutrons

So that the relationship between atomic numbers and mass numbers can be formulated as follows:

Atomic Number (Z) = Mass Number (A) - Number of Neutrons

In the following element notation,

[tex]\large {{{A} \atop {Z}} \right X}[/tex]

X = symbol of elemental atom

A = mass number

   = number of protons + number of neutrons

Z = atomic number

   = number of protons = number of electrons, on neutral elements

The Ca element is in groups 2A and period 4 in the periodic system of elements with atomic number 20 and mass number 40

So the composition:

₂₀⁴⁰Ca,

proton = no.atom = 20

electron = proton = 20

neutron = no. mass - proton = 40 - 20 = 20

This cation is obtained by removing 2 electrons, with the aim of getting stability like the configuration of the noble gas electron ₁₈Ar

So the composition:

26. Calculate the mass of sodium chlorate that must decompose to form 6.5 g of oxygen 2naclo3 2nacl s 3o2 solution


SOLUTION:

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 2 moles of NaClO₃ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 3 moles of O₂.

The molar masses of NaClO₃ and O₂ are 106.44 g and 32.00 g, respectively.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{NaClO}_3 & = \text{6.5 g} \: \text{O}_2 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{O}_2}{\text{32.00 g} \: \text{O}_2} \times \frac{\text{2 mol} \: \text{NaClO}_3}{\text{3 mol} \: \text{O}_2} \times \frac{\text{106.44 g} \: \text{NaClO}_3}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{NaClO}_3} \\ & = \boxed{\text{14.4 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, 14.4 g of NaClO₃ must be decomposed to form 6.5 g of O₂.
