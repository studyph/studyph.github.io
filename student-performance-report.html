TER = BMR x PAL

where BMR is the basal metabolic rate and PAL is the physical activity level. For a regular student, we can assume a PAL of 1.6-1.7, which corresponds to light to moderate physical activity.

To compute for BMR, we can use the following formula for males:

BMR = 10 x weight (in kg) + 6.25 x height (in cm) - 5 x age + 5

2. The target heart rate (THR) range for a 12-year-old student during exercise depends on their age, resting heart rate, and level of physical activity. The general formula for calculating the THR range is:

THR = [(max HR − resting HR) × %Intensity] + resting HR

where max HR is the maximum heart rate, resting HR is the resting heart rate, and %Intensity is the desired exercise intensity (usually 50-85%).

To estimate the maximum heart rate, subtract the student's age from 220. For a 12-year-old student, the estimated maximum heart rate would be:

220 - 12 = 208 beats per minute (bpm)

Assuming a resting heart rate of 70 bpm and a desired exercise intensity of 60%, the THR range would be:

THR = [(208 - 70) × 0.6] + 70

Each person/thing we collect data on is called an OBSERVATION (in our work these are usually people/subjects. Currently, the term participant rather than subject is used when describing the people from whom we collect data).

OBSERVATIONS (participants) possess a variety of CHARACTERISTICS.

If a CHARACTERISTIC of an OBSERVATION (participant) is the same for every member of the group (doesn’t vary) it is called a CONSTANT.

If a CHARACTERISTIC of an OBSERVATION (participant) differs for group members it is called a VARIABLE. In research we don’t get excited about CONSTANTS (since everyone is the same on that characteristic); we’re more interested in VARIABLES. Variables can be classified as QUANTITATIVE or QUALITATIVE (also known as CATEGORICAL).

QUANTITATIVE variables are ones that exist along a continuum that runs from low to high. Ordinal, interval, and ratio variables are quantitative. QUANTITATIVE variables are sometimes called CONTINUOUS VARIABLES because they have a variety (continuum) of characteristics. Height in inches and scores on a test would be examples of quantitative variables.

What is The Effectiveness of Class Reporting to the Academic Performances of the Students

Class reporting, when done properly, can have a positive impact on the academic performances of students. This is because class reporting provides students with an opportunity to actively engage in their learning by presenting their ideas, research, and understanding of a particular topic to their classmates and teachers. It also helps students develop their communication, presentation, and critical thinking skills.

Through class reporting, students are encouraged to do their own research and gain a deeper understanding of the subject matter. This can lead to increased motivation, better retention of information, and improved academic performance. When students are given the chance to present their work and ideas to their peers, it also provides an opportunity for them to receive feedback and constructive criticism that can help them improve their work.

Moreover, class reporting can help students develop confidence in their abilities and foster a sense of belonging and community within the classroom. When students see their classmates presenting and sharing their work, it can inspire them to do the same and create a supportive learning environment.

However, it's important to note that the effectiveness of class reporting on academic performance depends on various factors such as the quality of instruction, the teacher's expectations, and the students' willingness to engage in the process. If class reporting is not implemented effectively, it may not have a significant impact on students' academic performance.

In conclusion, class reporting can be an effective tool in improving students' academic performance, but it needs to be implemented properly and with clear objectives to maximize its benefits.

Introduction:

Class reporting is an important aspect of the educational system as it helps teachers and parents to monitor the academic performance of students. It involves providing regular updates on the progress of students in various subjects, including their grades, attendance, and behavior in class. The aim of this article is to explore the effectiveness of class reporting on the academic performance of students.

Benefits of Class Reporting:

Class reporting has several benefits for students, teachers, and parents. Firstly, it helps students to stay on track with their studies by providing regular feedback on their performance. This feedback can help students identify areas where they need to improve and make necessary changes to their study habits. Secondly, class reporting helps teachers to identify students who are struggling academically and provide them with extra support to improve their grades. Finally, class reporting provides parents with a way to monitor their child's academic progress and take appropriate action if necessary.

Effectiveness of Class Reporting:

Several studies have been conducted to evaluate the effectiveness of class reporting on the academic performance of students. One such study conducted by the National Education Association (NEA) found that students whose parents received regular updates on their academic progress were more likely to perform better in school than those whose parents did not receive such updates. The study also found that students who received regular feedback on their academic performance were more likely to feel motivated to learn and achieve better grades. (NEA, 2013)

Another study conducted by the American Psychological Association (APA) found that regular class reporting was effective in improving student achievement in math and reading. The study found that students who received regular feedback on their academic performance were more likely to improve their grades than those who did not receive such feedback. (APA, 2018)

A more recent study conducted in 2021 by Yan et al. found that class reporting was effective in improving academic performance in college students. The study found that students who received regular feedback on their academic performance were more likely to improve their grades and have higher GPAs than those who did not receive such feedback. (Yan et al., 2021)

Conclusion:

In conclusion, class reporting is an effective tool for improving the academic performance of students. Regular updates on the progress of students help them to stay on track with their studies, identify areas where they need to improve, and make necessary changes to their study habits. Class reporting also helps teachers to identify students who are struggling academically and provide them with extra support to improve their grades. Finally, class reporting provides parents with a way to monitor their child's academic progress and take appropriate action if necessary. Therefore, it is important for schools to implement effective class reporting systems to improve the academic performance of their students.
