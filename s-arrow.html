17. The arrow on a crossbow undergoes uniform acceleration over a distance of 38.1 cm. If the acceleration takes place over 8.93 × 10^-3 s and the arrow is initially at rest, what is the arrow’s final speed?


Solution:

Use the equation for displacement with uniform acceleration.

[tex]\Delta x = \dfrac{1}{2}(v_{i}+v_{f})\Delta t[/tex]

The arrow starts at rest, so the initial velocity is zero.

Solving for the final velocity, we get

[tex]v_{f}=\dfrac{2\Delta x}{\Delta t}-v_{i} \\\\v_{f}=\dfrac{(2)(38.1\text{ cm})\left(\dfrac{1\text{ m}}{100\text{ cm}} \right)}{8.93\times10^{-3}\text{ m/s}} - 0\text{ m/s}\\\\\boxed{v_{f}=85.3\text{ m/s}}[/tex]

26. An arrow is shot at 30.00 angle with the horizontal. It has a velocity of 49 m/s. How high will the arrow go? What horizontal distance will it travel?


If an archer were to fire an arrow and you want to know how high it gets and how far it gets then this is how you can find out.

First, identify your given. You have an angle of 30.00° and the angle is your theta so θ = 30.00°. You also have the velocity or INITIAL VELOCITY of 49m/s (Vi = 49m/s). It looks like you only have 2 given but you actually have 3, gravity is your third and we all know that the gravitational constant is 9.8m/s² but it will become negative because the arrow will be going against gravity so it's actually -9.8m/s².

Now you are trying to figure out how high it is, which is the dmax or distance max, and how far it goes horizontally, which is the Range.  

To solve for this, you will be needing FIVE FORMULAS.

FIRST formula you will be using is Vx = Vi cosine (Theta). Substitute the values you already have and the solution will become Vx = 49m/s cosine 30.00 degrees, by doing that, you should get 42.435... but let's just make it 42.44 for now.

Formula:

Vx = vicosθ

Solution:

Vx = 49 cos30.00

Vx = 42.435

Vx = 42.44 m/s

SECOND formula you will be using is Vy = Vi sine (Theta) and then substitute again. Vy = 49m/s sine 30.00 degrees and you should get Vy = 24.5m/s. Now those are going to be our basis for the next three formulas.

Formula:

Vy = visinθ

Solution:

Vy =  49 sin30.00

Vy = 24.5

THIRD formula to use is Tt or Total Time = -2Vy/g, substitute the values and your solution will become -2(24.5m/s) divided by -9.8m/s^2. If you input it correctly, your total time or Tt = 5 seconds. You may be wondering why you need to find the time, the FIFTH formula is the reason why you need to find the total time or Tt.

Formula:

[tex]Tt=\frac{-2Vy}{g}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]Tt = \frac{-2(24.5)}{-9.8}[/tex]

[tex]Tt = 5[/tex]

[tex]Tt=5s[/tex]

FOURTH formula to use, dmax = -Vy^2/2g and by substituting, the solution is dmax = -24.5m/s squared or to the power of 2 making it -24.5^2 divided by 2( -9.8 ). If inputed correctly, you will get dmax = 30.625m and that answers your first question.

Formula:

[tex]dmax=\frac{-Vy^{2} }{2g}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]dmax = \frac{24.5^{2} }{2(-9.8)}[/tex]

[tex]dmax = 30.625[/tex]

[tex]dmax = 30.625m[/tex]

FIFTH and final formula is R = VxTt and this is the reason you need to solve for Total Time as it is required. Substitute and your solution will become R = 42.44m/s (5 seconds) and after multiplying, the answer is R = 212.2m.

Formula:

[tex]R = VxTt[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]R = 42.44(5)[/tex]

[tex]R = 212.2[/tex]

[tex]R = 212.2m[/tex]

So the answer to how high it is then it went as high as 30.625m and the answer to how far it went horizontally is 212.2m BUT YOU MUST APPLY SIGNIFICANT RULES. Looking at your given, (30.00 degrees has 4 SF, 49m/s has 2 SF, -9.8m/s^2 has 2 SF) you have 2 SF because that's the least SF used.

So the FINAL ANSWER would be:

dmax or distance max or how high it went is 31m .

R or Range or how far it went horizontally is 210m.
