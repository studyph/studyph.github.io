11. 500 ml alcohol and 1 L ( 1000 ml) watergiven,solute, equation​


The solution has a mass of 33.33% concentration.Given:

500 ml of alcohol and 1 L of water.

To find:

We need to figure out how much of each solution is in each one.

Solution:

The thing that dissolves in a solution is called the solute, and the thing that dissolves it is called the solvent.

The ratio of the mass of the solute to the mass of the solution multiplied by 100 is the % concentration. I

1 L of water is the same as 1000 ml of water.

So, the solution has a mass of 500 + 1000 = 1500 L.

% concentration:

500/1500 x 100 = 33.33%

When mixing rubbing alcohol and water, the molecules of the latter form hydrogen bonds with the water molecules. The alcohol mixes with the water to make a solution that is the same all the way through. You can no longer tell the difference between the alcohol and the water.

(8 baso sumusukat hanggang 1.89 liters.)

Gayundin, Ilang tasa ang 2 litro ng tubig?

2 litro = 8.45 tasa.

Gayundin, Sapat ba ang 1 litro ng tubig sa isang araw? Karaniwang inirerekumenda ng mga eksperto sa kalusugan walong 8-onsa na baso, na katumbas ng halos 2 litro, o kalahating galon sa isang araw. Ito ang tinatawag na 8 × 8 na panuntunan at napakadaling tandaan. Gayunpaman, ang ilang mga eksperto ay naniniwala na kailangan mong humigop ng tubig palagi sa buong araw, kahit na hindi ka nauuhaw.

Pangalawa, Ilan sa 8 onsa na baso ang nasa isang litro?

Ang isang 8 onsa na baso ay katumbas ng sa ilalim lamang ng 1/4 ng isang litro, na nangangahulugang na higit sa apat na 8 onsa na baso ng tubig ay katumbas ng 1 litro.

OK lang bang uminom ng 1.5 Litro ng tubig sa isang araw? Kailangan ng mga matatanda uminom kahit 1.5 litro ng tubig sa isang araw.

Sumasang-ayon ang mga eksperto na sa paligid 1.5-2 litro ng likido a araw ay sapat na para sa karamihan ng mga tao (na may higit na kailangan sa mas mainit na panahon, kung ikaw ay nag-eehersisyo ng marami, o gumagawa ng pisikal na hinihingi na trabaho).


28. 5. A mountain climber inhales 580 ml of air at temperature of -10°C. Whatvolume (in L) will the air occupy in the mountain climber's lungs if his bodytenperature is 37°C assuming that the preasure remains the same? (Note: 1L-1000 ml) patulong po to with solution po​


Charles LawV1/T1 = V2/T2

where in, V1 = first volume

T1 = first temperature

V2 = second volume

T2 = second temperature

Given: V1 = 0.58L

T1 = 263.15K

T2 = 310.15K

Note: Since the problem asked for volume in liters, we must apply conversion.

mL → L

580mL × 1 L / 1000mL = 0.58L

Note: Dealing with Temperature from each of the Gas Law must always be in Kelvin.

°C → K

-10°C + 273.15K = 263.15K

37°C + 273.15K = 310.15K

Unknown: V2 = ?

Formula: V2 = V1T2 / T1

Note: To get the formula, apply cross-multiplication, division and cancellation of like terms using the original formula of Charles Law.

V1/T1 = V2/T2 ← cross-multiply

V1T2 = V2T1 ← divide both sides by T1

V1T2 / T1 = V2T1 / T1 ← cancel like terms

V1T2 / T1 = V2 ← final formula for V2

Solution: V2 = V1T2 / T1

= (0.58L)(310.15K) / 263.15K

= 179.887L•K / 263.15K

= 0.68L

Note: "K" is cancelled out, so the unit for volume will remain which is "L"

Reminder: Cancellation of units is applicable when the other same unit is on the denominator.

Final Answer: V2 = 0.68L


29. Determine the molarity of a solution that contained 800 mL of volume and, after receiving 1000 mL of solvent, had its molarity decreased to 10 mol/L. a) 13.5 mol/L b) 16.5 mol/L c) 22.5 mol/L d) 12.5 mol/L​


Hello!

Let's go by part, the volume of the final solution ( Vf ) is :

initial volume + final volume = 800 mL + 1000 mL = 1800 mL, ( that is, it has 800 mL and another 1000 mL was added

Initial volume (Vi) = 800 mL

Initial molarity ( Mi ) = is what we will have to find

Final molarity ( Mf ) which is the concentration that was decreased after adding more solvent to the solution = 10 mol/L

We then use the dilution calculation:

Mi = Vi = Mf × Vf

Mi = 800 = 10 × 1800

Mi = 800 = 18000

Mi = 18000 / 800

Mi= 22.5 mol/L

The correct answer is c) 22.5 mol/L
