Now ask yourself, is this the place without the centuries and years, this the place where each child expects to grow up when they open their innocent eyes and see the world for the first time? Are you able to look in the eyes of those children without the feeling of shame for this reality full of evil, which they have to face?

Isn’t Earth the only place all of us were born in? Are the borders that someone has drawn on the map powerful enough to build in our souls the borders of prejudice, alienating us from each other? Yes, they are! As long as we let them be. As long as we teach our children to let them be powerful enough.

When the world and this life are nearly destroyed by the evil and hate we spread, we would hardly care about the power we have towards others. We would hardly care about the God others are praying for, as long as those prayers can give us a chance to survive. So why won’t we stop caring about it right now, at this very moment? Why won’t we start making this world a better place by creating peace and compassion? And finally, why won’t we earn the right to look at our children’s eyes with the feeling of pride for the world we have built for them?  

Think about these questions and, no doubt, you will find the answers. Because the answers to those questions are buried deep inside each one of us. And we will discover those answers as soon as we dispel the dust of evil and hate from our hearts...

He once said, "When we have inner peace, we can be at peace with those around us. When our community is in a state of peace, it can share that peace with neighboring communities."

This quote speaks to the power of inner peace and its ability to positively impact not only our individual lives, but also the lives of those around us and even entire communities. When we are at peace within ourselves, we are able to interact with others in a more positive and compassionate way. This, in turn, can create a ripple effect of peace throughout our community, and beyond.

It is important to note that inner peace is not something that can be achieved overnight or by simply wishing for it. It takes consistent effort and commitment to cultivate inner peace, whether it be through meditation, mindfulness, or other spiritual practices.

At the same time, it's also important to remember that inner peace is not something that can be achieved in isolation. It is closely tied to the well-being of our community and the world around us. That's why it's important to work towards creating a more peaceful society in which everyone can thrive.

In conclusion, the quote of The Dalai Lama reminds us that inner peace is not only important for our individual well-being, but also for the well-being of our community and world. So let us strive to cultivate inner peace within ourselves and work towards creating a more peaceful society for all.

Thank you.
