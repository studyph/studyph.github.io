What is moral is worth legalizing: if something is good then it should be also legal. E.g., we might say that love and marriage between two consenting adults is moral, so it should be legal for adults to choose whom they marry. Or we might say that certain psychedelic substances help people to process trauma, which is moral, so we ought to make these substances legal so that people can heal from their trauma.

Morality. The law is a system of rules that a state enforces to regulate behavior through penalties. Legal principles are based on the rights of the citizens and the state expressed in the rules. Morality is a body of principles that attempt to define what is good and bad conduct.

A moral argument is an argument in which the conclusion is a moral statement. A moral statement is a statement asserting that an action is right or wrong (moral or immoral) or that a person or motive is good or bad. In a moral argument, we cannot establish the conclusion without a moral premise.

Thus something can be ethical, but immoral; society imposing the death penalty on someone is often viewed as immoral by some individuals. Likewise, something can be unethical, but moral, such as shoplifting in order to feed a hungry child in immediate need.
