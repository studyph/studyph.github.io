29. D. Solve the following. (With solution po sana) 1. The height of a triangle varies directly with its area and inversely with its base. The height of a triangle is 5 cm when its area and base are 10 cm² and 4 cm, respectively. A. Find an equation for the height of the triangle. B. Determine the base of a triangle whose area is 75 cm² and height is 10 cm. 2. The number of men needed to cement a barangay road varies directly with the length of the road at a constant width and inversely with the number of days required to do the job. If a 30 m road can be cemented by 10 men in 12 days, how wide is the road? How many days will it take 50 men to cement a road that is 1 km long? 3. Suppose that a is directly proportional to b and inversely proportional to c. If a = 4/3 when b = 10 and c = 12, find "a" when b = 9 and c = 16.​


Answer:

1.

A. Let H be the height of the triangle, A be the area of the triangle, and B be the base of the triangle. We are told that H varies directly with A and inversely with B, so we can set up the following proportion:

H / A = k

H / B = k

Where k is a proportionality constant. We are also told that when A = 10 cm² and B = 4 cm, H = 5 cm. Substituting these values into the first proportion gives us:

5 cm / 10 cm² = k

Solving for k, we find that k = 0.5. Substituting this value into the second proportion gives us:

H / B = 0.5

This equation represents the relationship between the height of a triangle, its area, and its base.

B. To find the base of a triangle whose area is 75 cm² and height is 10 cm, we can substitute these values into the equation from part A:

H / B = 0.5

10 cm / B = 0.5

B = 20 cm

Therefore, the base of the triangle is 20 cm.

2.

Let M be the number of men needed, L be the length of the road, W be the width of the road, and D be the number of days required. We are told that M varies directly with L and inversely with D, so we can set up the following proportion:

M / L = k

M / D = k

Where k is a proportionality constant. We are also told that when L = 30 m, M = 10 men, and D = 12 days, the road is cemented. Substituting these values into the first proportion gives us:

10 men / 30 m = k

Solving for k, we find that k = 1/3. Substituting this value into the second proportion gives us:

M / D = 1/3

We can then solve for W by substituting these values into the equation for the area of a rectangle (A = L * W):

A = L * W

A = 30 m * W

A = (10 men / 1/3) * W

A = 30 men * W

A = 10 men * 12 days * W

A = 120 men-days * W

W = A / (120 men-days)

Substituting the given value of A (10 men * 12 days = 120 men-days) gives us:

W = 120 men-days / 120 men-days

W = 1

Therefore, the width of the road is 1.

To find the number of days it will take 50 men to cement a road that is 1 km long, we can substitute these values into the equation from part A:

M / D = 1/3

50 men / D = 1/3

D = 150 days

Therefore, it will take 150 days for 50 men to cement a 1 km long road.
