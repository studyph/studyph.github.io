1. 3. The sides of a triangle are consecutive integers. If the perimeter of the triangle i198cm, find the length of each side.a. 65cm, 66cm, 67cmc. 67cm, 68cm, 69cmb. 66cm, 67cm, 68cmd. 68cm, 69cm, 70cmcertainratio off​


Answer:

a.  65cm, 66cm, 67cm

Step-by-step explanation:

Consecutive integers for 3 sides of a tringle:

(x),  (x+1),    (x+2)Perimeter = 198 cm

Find the length of each side:

Perimeter = (Side 1) + (Side 2) + (Side 3)

198 = (x) + (x+1) +(x+2)

198 = 3x + 3

3x = 198 - 3

3x/3 = 195/3

x = 65  (shortest side)

Side 1: x = 65 cm

Side 2:  (x+1) = 65+1 = 66 cm

Side 3:  (x+2) = 65+2 = 67 cm

The length of sides are 65 cm, 66 cm, and 67 cm.

Step-by-step explanation:

Heron's formula:

[tex] \sqrt{s(s - a)(s - b)(s - c)} [/tex]

Where s is half of perimeter

a,b,c are the three given measures

When to use?

- Given 3 sides of triangle (a,b,c) to find the area.

s=(42+51+67)/2=80

[tex] \sqrt{80(80 - 42)(80 - 51)(80 - 67)} \\ = \sqrt{80 \times 38 \times 29 \times 13} \\ = 4 \sqrt{71630} [/tex]
