1. describe the dance rumba​


Rumba, also spelled rhumba, ballroom dance of Afro-Cuban folk-dance origin that became internationally popular in the early 20th century. Best known for the dancers' subtle side to side hip movements with the torso erect, the rumba is danced with a basic pattern of two quick side steps and a slow forward step.

3. 5. How is the Rumba dance performed?​


The basic rhythm in rumba is slow-quick-quick, with the first step taking 2 beats and the last two steps each taking one beat. It is done as a box step, just like waltz! However, that's where the similarity ends. Since rumba is a latin style, the hips are active and always moving in “cuban motion”.

13. Social Dances are classified into Latin-American Dance and Modern Standard Dance, which of the following is an example of Modern Standard Dance?a. Chac-chac-chab. Sambac. Rumbad. Foxtrot​


Answers:

A. cha-cha-cha

Explanation:

The cha-cha requires small steps and lots of hip motion (Cuban motion), as it is danced in 4/4 time. The fourth beat is split into two, giving it the characteristic rhythm of 2, 3, 4 and 1. Therefore, five steps are danced to four beats. You may have heard it counted like, "One, two, cha-cha-cha."
