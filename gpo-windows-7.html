16. We have learned that Group Policy Object (GPO) has some benefits and limitations briefly discuss some of them.​


Answer:

Types of GPOs

There are three types of GPOs: local, non-local and starter.

- Local Group Policy Objects. A local Group Policy Objectrefers to the collection of group policy settings that only apply to the local computer and to the users who log on to that computer. Local GPOs are used when policy settings need to apply to a single Windows computer or user. Local GPOs exist by default on all Windows computers.

- Non-local Group Policy Objects. A non-local group policy objectis used when policy settings have to apply to one or more Windows computers or users. Non-local GPOs apply to Windows computers or users once they’re linked to Active Directory objects, such as sites, domains or organizational units.

- Starter Group Policy Objects. Introduced in Windows Server 2008, starter GPOs are templates for Group Policy settings. These objects enable an administrator to create and have a pre-configured group of settings that represent a baseline for any future policy to be created.

Explain the importance of GPO.​

A Group Policy object (GPO) is a collection of Group Policy settings that govern the appearance and behavior of a system for a certain group of users. A user configuration and a computer configuration are the two sections, or nodes, of every GPO.

It essentially gives administrators a consolidated location to manage and modify operating systems, programs, and user preferences. When implemented effectively, Group Policies may help you strengthen the security of your users' PCs and guard against both insider and external threats.

Better password policy enforcement — To keep a company's network safe, Group Policy Object's specify password length, reuse limits, and other criteria for passwords. Group Policy Object's let enterprises to ensure that key company files are kept on a centralized and monitored storage system by configuring folder redirection.
