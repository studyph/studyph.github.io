6. which lines are higher in number, undeflected lines or lines deflected at certain angel​


Answer:

Undeflected lines are higher in number than lines deflected at a certain angle in the context of light passing through a diffraction grating.

Explanation:

A diffraction grating is an optical component with a series of closely spaced parallel slits or grooves that are used to separate light into its individual wavelengths. When light passes through the diffraction grating, it diffracts and forms a pattern of bright spots and dark areas on a screen behind the grating.

The number of bright spots or lines in the pattern is determined by the spacing of the slits or grooves in the grating and the wavelength of the light. The greater the number of slits or grooves in the grating, the greater the number of bright spots or lines in the pattern.

The lines that are deflected at a certain angle correspond to the bright spots or lines in the pattern that are formed by the constructive interference of light waves that travel different distances through the slits or grooves in the grating. The number of these lines depends on the spacing of the slits or grooves and the wavelength of the light.

The undeflected lines correspond to the dark areas in the pattern where there is destructive interference of the light waves. These lines are formed when the light waves from different slits or grooves in the grating cancel each other out.

Since the dark areas in the pattern are larger than the bright spots, there are more undeflected lines than deflected lines in the diffraction grating pattern.
