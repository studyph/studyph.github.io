10. calculate the boiling and freezing point of a solution prepared by dissolving 68g of glucose, C6H12O6, in 800g of water​


Solution (Boiling Point)

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of solute (glucose).

molar mass = (12.01 g/mol × 6) + (1.008 g/mol × 12) + (16.00 g/mol × 6)

molar mass = 180.156 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \text{68 g glucose} × \frac{\text{1 mol glucose}}{\text{180.156 g glucose}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \text{0.377451 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molality of the solution.

[tex]m = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{mass of solvent (kg)}}[/tex]

[tex]m = \frac{\text{0.377451 mol}}{\text{0.800 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]m = 0.471814 \: m[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the boiling point elevation.

[tex]∆T_{b} = mk_{b}[/tex]

[tex]∆T_{b} = (0.471814 \: m)(\text{0.52 °C/}m)[/tex]

[tex]∆T_{b} = \text{0.245°C}[/tex]

Step 5: Calculate the boiling point of the solution.

[tex]T_{b} = T°_{b} + ∆T_{b}[/tex]

[tex]T_{b} = \text{100°C + 0.245°C}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{T_{b} = \text{100.245°C}}[/tex]

Solution (Freezing Point)

Step 1: Calculate the freezing point depression.

[tex]∆T_{f} = mk_{f}[/tex]

[tex]∆T_{f} = (0.471814 \: m)(\text{1.86 °C/}m)[/tex]

[tex]∆T_{f} = \text{0.88°C}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the freezing point of the solution.

[tex]T_{f} = T°_{f} - ∆T_{f}[/tex]

[tex]T_{f} = \text{0°C} - \text{0.88°C}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{T_{f} = -\text{0.88°C}}[/tex]

14. An 800g solution of Kool-Aid contains 780g of water. what is the masspercent of solute in this solution?


The mass percent of solute in the given problem is 2.5%.

A solute is any substance (example: sugar) dissolved in another substance called solvent (example: water). The combination of the solute and solvent is called a solution.

Here are the steps to calculate the mass percent of solute:

Determine the mass of the solute. Divide the mass of the solute by the mass of the solution. Multiply the resulting quotient by 100 to change it to percent.

Using the given problem, here is the solution:

Mass of solute = 800g of solution - 780g water of water = 20g of solute Mass of solute / mass of solution = 20g of solute / 800g of solution = 0.025 Quotient x 100 = 0.025 x 100 = 2.5%

15. How many grams of sodium chloride (NaCl) is needed to prepare a 800g of 18% NaCl solution in water?​


Answer:

To prepare an 18% NaCl solution, we need 18 grams of NaCl per 100 grams of solution. Therefore, for an 800-gram solution, we need:

18/100 x 800 = 144 grams of NaCl.

So, we need 144 grams of NaCl to prepare an 800-gram solution of 18% NaCl in water.

27. calculate the boiling and freezing point of a solution prepared by dissolving 68g of glucose, C6H12O6, iin 800g of water​


The boiling and freezing point of a solution :

Tb solution = 100.245 °C

Tf solution  = - 0.876 °C

Further explanation  

Solution properties are the properties of a solution that don't depend on the type of solute but only on the concentration of the solute.  

The term is used in the Solution properties  

1. molal  

that is, the number of moles of solute in 1 kg of solvent  

[tex]\tt m=\dfrac{mol~solute}{mass~1~kg~solvent}[/tex]

 

2. Freezing point  

Solutions from volatile substances have a higher boiling point and lower freezing points than the solvent  

[tex]\tt \Delta Tf=Kf\times m[/tex]

ΔTf = Tf solvent - Tf solution

Kf = The molal freezing point depression constant

m = molal solution  

3. Boiling points  

Solutions from non-volatile substances have a higher boiling point and lower freezing points than the solvent  

ΔTb = Tb solution - Tb solvent  

ΔTb = boiling point elevation  

ΔTb = Kb. m  

Kb = molal boiling point increase  

m = molal solution  

Given

68 g of glucose, C6H12O6⇒solute

800 g of water​ = 0.8 kg⇒solvent

Required

the boiling and freezing point

Solution

Boiling point

The boiling point constant for water(Kb) : 0.52 °C / m

100°C (boiling point of water)

mol glucose :

= mass : molar mass

= 68 g : 180,156 g/mol

= 0.377

molal :

= 0.377 mol : 0.8 kg

= 0.471 m

ΔTb = Tb solution - Tb solvent  = Kb.m

Tb solution - 100 = 0.52 x 0.471

Tb solution = 100.245 °C

Freezing point

Kf water = 1.86 °C/m

Tf solvent = 0 °C (water)

ΔTf = Tf solvent - Tf solution  = Kf . m

0 - Tf solution  = 1.86 x 0.0471

Tf solution  = - 0.876 °C
