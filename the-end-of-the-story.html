18. It is the end of the story. Itoccurs after the climax of the story


Answer:

Denouement is the point in a story in which the conflict is resolved. Borrowed from French, the word derives from Latin and literally means “untie the knot,” which refers to the narrative entanglements the author has woven through the first four stages of plot development. The denouement always occurs after the climax, in the final part of a story’s narrative arc.
