4. A hockey puck on a frozen pond is given an initial speed of 20.0 m/s. If the puck always ramains on the ice and slides 115 before coming to rest, determine the coefficient of kinetic friction between the puck and ice. ​


Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

We can use the equation of motion for an object with uniform acceleration to solve this problem. The equation is:

d = (v_i^2 - v_f^2) / (2a)

where d is the distance traveled, v_i is the initial velocity, v_f is the final velocity (which is zero in this case, since the puck comes to rest), and a is the acceleration.

We are given v_i = 20.0 m/s and d = 115 m. We need to find the coefficient of kinetic friction, which is related to the acceleration by:

a = -μ_k * g

where μ_k is the coefficient of kinetic friction and g is the acceleration due to gravity (9.81 m/s^2).

Substituting these values into the equation of motion, we get:

115 m = (20.0 m/s)^2 / (2 * (-μ_k * 9.81 m/s^2))

Simplifying and solving for μ_k, we get:

μ_k = (20.0 m/s)^2 / (2 * 9.81 m/s^2 * 115 m)

= 0.139

Therefore, the coefficient of kinetic friction between the puck and ice is 0.139.

18. A 0.160-kg hockey puck is moving on an icy, frictionless, horizontal surface. at the puck is moving to the right at 3.00 m/s. (a) calculate the velocity of the puck (magnitude and direction) after a force of 25.0 n directed to the right has been applied for 0.050 s.


Given:

m = 0.160kg

v1 = 3.00m/s

F = 25.0N

t = 0.050s

v2=?

In Kinematics, we have the formula v2 = v1 + at. We can get the acceleration by following Newton's second law of motion:

F = ma

a = F/m

Substituting that in the Kinematics equation:

v2 = v1 + (F/m)(t)

= 3.00m/s + {(25.0N/0.160kg)](0.050s)

v2 = 10.8m/s to the right

Take note that we used the positive value of the acceleration because the initial velocity of the puck and the applied force are headed to the same direction, that is both of them are directed to the right.
