13. Is Machiavelli's idea of ruling with force and not of the love applicable in the philippines? ​


Answer:

Is Machiavelli's idea of "ruling with force and not of love" applicable in the Philippines? I believe it is! But let me just remind you that having said that it is applicable is not a way of saying "it should be applied". We wear raincoats when it rains, not when the sun is up. Hence, the idea of ruling with force can only be applied when the situation calls for it, and not when the head of the state feels to.

The Philippines as a democratic country values peoples freedom and opinions.  Hence, the idea of ruling with force, (especially if the ruler has placed all the powers of the government to himself, and his way of ruling by force has compromised freedom and has violated people's rights) may surely be met with resistance from the people itself.

I personally believe that ruling by force is only applicable as a weapon of last resort and it has to be done according to the rules of law. As a leader, it is preferable to be both feared and loved, but if you can't have both, and the situation demands a strong leadership, then to be feared is more reasonable than to be loved.

15. talambuhay ni niccolo machiavelli


Talambuhay ni Niccolo Machiavelli

Isinilang sa Florence, Italy noong  May 3, 1469, ang pangatlong anak at unang anak ng abugado na si Bernardo di Niccolò Machiavelli at ang kanyang asawang si Bartolomea  Stefano Nelli.Isang diplomang Italyano, politiko, isoryador, pilosopo, kalaro, makata ng panahon ng Renaissance.Madalas siyang tinawag na Ama ng moderong pilosopiyang pampulitika.Pinakasalan si Marietta Corsini noong 1502 , nagkaroon ng 4 na anak na lalaki at 2 anak na babae.Naniniwala sa kaisipan na "The end justifies the means."Naging sekretarya sa Florence Council of TenYumao noong Mayo 22,1527Nakilala sa Machiavellianism tumutukoy sa isang katangian ng pagkatao na nakikita ang isang tao nakatuon sa kanilang sariling mga interes na kanilang gagayahin, linlangin, at pagsamantalahan ang iba upang makamit ang kanilang mga layunin.

Mga Akda

The Prince Tungkol sa isang pinuno na gumamit ng katusuhan, kalupitan at panlilinlang upang makamit ang kapangyarihan.

  2.  The Livy

Isang detalyadong talakayan ng mga panloob na operasyon ng sinaunang Roma.Malamang na si Machiavelli, kapag tinutukoy niya ang "marawal na kalagayan" ng bawat uri, ay mayroong sinaunang pilosopo na Greek Plato's Republic. Ang pagtatasa ni Plato ay nagtatampok sa pagkabulok ng monarkiya sa paniniil, ng aristokrasya sa oligarkiya, at ng demokrasya sa anarkiya.  Tinatalakay ni Machiavelli ang mga pakinabang ng tribune.Pinapanatili ni Machiavelli na ang salungatan sa pagitan ng aristokratikong Senado ang karaniwang mga tao sa sinaunang Roma ang mainspring ng kalayaan sa panahon ng Roman Republic.Ipinapakita ng Machiavelli ang kanyang mga analytic na kapangyarihan sa pagsasaalang ng tanong, pagdaragdag ng mga kalamangan at kahinaan para sa bawat posisyon.

27. how did machiavelli expand the field of political science​


Answer:

Niccolò Machiavelli

First published Tue Sep 13, 2005; substantive revision Tue May 28, 2019

Why Machiavelli? That question might naturally and legitimately occur to anyone encountering an entry about him in an encyclopedia of philosophy. Certainly, Machiavelli contributed to a large number of important discourses in Western thought—political theory most notably, but also history and historiography, Italian literature, the principles of warfare, and diplomacy. But Machiavelli never seems to have considered himself a philosopher—indeed, he often overtly rejected philosophical inquiry as beside the point—nor do his credentials suggest that he fits comfortably into standard models of academic philosophy. His writings are maddeningly and notoriously unsystematic, inconsistent and sometimes self-contradictory. He tends to appeal to experience and example in the place of rigorous logical analysis. Yet there are good reasons to include Machiavelli among the greatest of political philosophers, some of which are internal to his writings. In spite of the temptation to emphasize his political pragmatism, a lively scholarly debate rages about the presence of a coherent and original philosophy, addressed to topics of concern to philosophers, at the core of his thought (Benner 2009; Zuckert 2017, 2018; Baluch 2018).

Moreover, succeeding thinkers who more obviously qualify as philosophers of the first rank did (and still do) feel compelled to engage with his ideas, either to dispute them or to incorporate his insights into their own teachings. Even if Machiavelli grazed at the fringes of philosophy, the impact of his extensive musings has been widespread and lasting. The terms “Machiavellian” or “Machiavellism” find regular purchase among philosophers concerned with a range of ethical, political, and psychological phenomena, regardless of whether or not Machiavelli himself invented “Machiavellism” or was in fact a “Machiavellian” in the sense commonly ascribed to him. Machiavelli's critique of utopian philosophical schemes (such as those of Plato) challenges an entire tradition of political philosophy in a manner that that commands attention and demands consideration and response. Finally, a new generation of so-called “neo-Roman” political theorists (such as Philip Pettit [1997], Quentin Skinner [1998] and Maurizio Viroli [1999 [2002]]) finds inspiration in Machiavelli’s version of republicanism. Thus, Machiavelli deserves a place at the table in any comprehensive survey of political philosophy.
