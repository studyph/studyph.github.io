3. The customer pays for the goods and takes these homes with him/her. A. Cash-send or Cash deliver sale b. Charge-send or charge-deliver c. Cash-take-sale d. Charge-take-sale


Customers pays for goods and take the goods home is called cash send or cash delivery sale (A).

• Cash send or cash delivery sale (COD)

cash send or cash delivery sale is a method of payment where the buyer immediately makes payment when the goods have been received. Delivery times for COD are often faster than for normal invoicing. This is advantageous since a middleman requires payment at delivery from the customer. Customers can gather the funds to make a complete payment with COD shipment.

• Charge send or charge deliver

Delivery fees are the costs incurred by the seller of goods or services in connection with preparation and delivery of goods or services to a place specified by the buyer, including but not limited to transportation, shipping, postage, handling, crating, and packing.

• Cash take sale

Cash sales are transactions in which the buyer's payment obligation is immediately satisfied. Bills, coins, checks, credit cards, and money orders are all regarded as acceptable forms of payment for cash sales. The seller won't have to give the buyer credit if the transaction is done in cash.

• Charge take sale

A sales charge is an additional cost incurred by the investor to reimburse the broker or salesperson for facilitating the transaction. The sales fee for mutual funds is sometimes referred to as a "load," and it may be assessed beforehand, at the time of sale, or under other circumstances.
