1. How can one apply NPK fertilizer in soil before planting maize


Answer:

Apply the right fertilizer at planting to ensure the maize has nutrients after germinating. Put the fertilizer in the planting holes and cover with a little soil to avoid contact with the seeds - this may lead to scorching of the seeds • Place one seed per hole and cover the hole with soil.

Fertilizers may be distinct from liming materials or other non-nutrient soil amendments. Many sources of fertilizer exist, both natural and industrially produced.

21. NPK are called marco nutrients why they are called marco nutrients?​


Macro-nutrients are those nutrients that are required in large quantities for the growth and development of plants. Since they are required in large quantities, they are known as macro-nutrient.


22. B. Give your answer in one complete sentence, write your idea about thefollowing:1. NPK2. Fertilizer3. Commercial Fertilizer4. Compost​


Answer:

NPK- stands for "nitrogen, phosphorus, and potassium," the three nutrients that compose complete fertilizers.

FERTILIZER-A fertilizer or fertiliser is any material of natural or synthetic origin that is applied to soil or to plant tissues to supply one or more plant nutrients essential to the growth of plants.

COMMERCIAL FERTILIZER- or other substance containing one or more essential available plant nutrients that is distributed for its plant nutrient content and is designed for use and has value in promoting plant growth.

COMPOST-composts do not usually contain enough of the primary nutrients to qualify as a fertilizer or to satisfy the needs of most plants. But compost does contribute to plant nutrition, not primarily through the nutrients it contains but through those it makes available to plants.
