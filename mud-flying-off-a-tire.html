11. A pilot takes off from city A to fly city B 1400 miles away. After flying 360 miles, he diacovers that he has been traveling 7°50' off course. At this poibt, how far from city B is he?


360 miles would be the hypotenuse of our right triangle. 7°50' would be our angle A. Since we are looking for the base of this triangle. We will be using the cosine function.

[tex]cos= \frac{adjacent}{hypotenuse} [/tex]
First, we need to derive the formula for the adjacent or the base of the triangle since that is the unknown. The formula would be:
[tex]adjacent= hypotenuse(cosA)[/tex]
Substituting the given,
[tex]adjacent= 360 miles(cos7degrees50minutes) = 356.64 miles[/tex]

Subtracting the answer from the needed 1,400 miles we get 1,043.36 miles.

30. Why doesn't the moon fly off into space?​


Answer:

The reason is that the Moon is never still. It constantly moves around us. Without the force of gravity from the Earth, it would just float away into space. This mix of velocity and distance from the Earth allows the Moon to always be in balance between fall and escape.
