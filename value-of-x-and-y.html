24. The value of y varies directly with x. When y = 32, x = 4. What is the value of y when x is 10?


✏️DIRECT VARIATION===============================

[tex]\large\bold{\red{PROBLEM:}}[/tex] The value of y varies directly with x. When y = 32, x = 4. What is the value of y when x is 10?

[tex]\large\bold{\red{SOLUTION:}}[/tex] Use the simple formula. Then plug in our initial statement values of y = 32 when x = 4.

[tex] \begin{gathered} \begin{aligned} & \bold{ \color{lightblue}Formula:} \\ & \boxed{ \sf y = kx} \end{aligned} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex] \sf32 = 4k[/tex]

[tex] \begin{gathered} \sf \frac{32}{4} = \frac{4k}{4} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{gathered} \sf \frac{32}{4} = \frac{ \cancel4k}{ \cancel4} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex] \sf k = 8[/tex]

- We have a constant which is 8, then solve the second part of the variation equation. Substitute the constant (k) and the x value to the original formula to find the y.

[tex] \sf y = 8x[/tex]

[tex] \sf y = 8(10)[/tex]

[tex] \sf y = 80[/tex]

- Therefore, the value of y when x is 10 is:

[tex] \large \boxed{ \sf{ \green{ \: \: 80 \: \: }}}[/tex]===============================
