28. A trapezoid has bases b1=9 inches and b2= 3inches and height h = 4inches.


AREA OF TRAPEZOID

[tex]\normalsize{\blue{\overline{\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad}}}[/tex]

A trapezoid has bases b₁=9 inches and b₂= 3 inches and height h = 4 inches.

[tex]\large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:}[/tex]

The area of a trapezoid can be found using the formula

[tex]\boxed{A = \dfrac{1}{2} (b_1 + b_2) h}[/tex]

Where:

[tex]{b_1 \rm{(The \: 1st \: base \: of \: the \: trapezoid/ The \; wider\: base) = 9 \: inches}}[/tex][tex]{b_2 \rm{(The \: 2nd \: base \: of \: the \: trapezoid/ The \; smaller\: base) = 3\: inches}}[/tex][tex]{h \rm{(The \: height\: of \: the \: trapezoid) = 4 \: inches}}[/tex]

Substitute the given values into the formula, then solve for area:

[tex]A = \dfrac{1}{2} (b_1 + b_2) h[/tex]

[tex]A = \dfrac{1}{2} (9 + 3) (4)[/tex]

[tex]A = \dfrac{1}{2} (12) (4)[/tex]

[tex]A = 6 (4)[/tex]

[tex]A = \green{24}[/tex]

Hence, the area of the trapezoid is 24 inches.
