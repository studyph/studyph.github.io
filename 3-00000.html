Equivalent Ratios

The relationship between quantities or items is known as a ratio. It can be expressed in three ways: word form (y to z), fraction form (y/z) and the most used way is the colon form (y:z). Some ratios are equal and these ratios are known as equivalent ratios. To tell if the given ratios are equivalent, the product of the means should be equal to the product of the extremes. Remember that the means are the inner terms and the extremes are the outer terms. The symbol = is used to tell that the ratios are equal and the symbol ≠ is used if the ratios are not equal.

Let us now identify if the given ratios are equal (=) or not (≠).

1) 64:12 = 80:15

means: 12 × 80 = 960

extremes: 64 × 15 = 960

2) ‌3⁄7‌ = ‌24⁄56

means: 7 × 24 = 168

extremes: 3 × 56 = 168

3) ‌6⁄7‌ ≠ ‌75⁄100

means: 7 × 75 = 525

extremes: 6 × 100 = 600

4) 15:12 = 35:28

means: 12 × 35 = 420

extremes: 15 × 28 = 420

5) 14:40 ≠ 9:24

means: 40 × 9 = 360

extremes: 14 × 24 = 336

6) 8:12 = 10:15

means: 12 × 10 = 120

extremes: 8 × 15 = 120

7) 16⁄32 ≠ ‌2⁄6

means: 32 × 2 = 64

extremes: 16 × 6 = 96

8) 10:13 ≠ 20:23

means: 13 × 20 = 260

extremes: 10 × 23 = 230

9) 15:6 = 20:8

means: 6 × 20 = 120

extremes: 15 × 8 = 120

10) 60⁄100 = ‌9⁄15

means: 100 × 9 = 900

extremes: 60 × 15 = 900
