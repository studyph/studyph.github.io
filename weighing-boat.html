1. A boat floating in fresh water displaces water weighing 35.6 kn.


a. weight of salt water displaced = 35.6 kN

b. the difference between the volume of fresh water and the volume of salt water displaced : 0.33 m³

Further explanation  

Archimedes states that  

The buoyant force acting on an object which is partially or completely immersed into a fluid is equal to the weight of the fluid displaced by the object  

Buoyancy force can be formulated  

Fb = ρf. Vf. g  

Fb = the buoyant force (N)

ρf = fluid density, kg / m³

Vf = volume of objects immersed in fluid (m³)

If the object is completely immersed, Vf = the volume of the object, Vo  

(a) What is the weight of the water this boat displaces when floating in salt water of density  : 1.10×10³kg/m³

The law of floatation :

Buoyant force = weight  

Fb=w

so weight of boat = weight of fresh water displaced ==weight of salt water displaced = 35.6 kN

(b) What is the difference between the volume of fresh water displaced and the volume of salt water displaced?

the volume of fresh water displaced :

[tex]\tt \rho_f.V_f.g=35.6.10^3~N\\\\10^3~kg/m^3.V_f.9.8~m/s^2=35.6.10^3~N\\\\V_f=3.63~m^3[/tex]

the volume of salt water displaced

[tex]\tt \rho_s.V_s.g=35.6.10^3\\\\1.1\times 10^3kg/m^3.V_s.9.8=35.6.10^3\\\\V_s=3.30~m^3[/tex]

the difference

[tex]\tt =V_f-V_s\\\\=3.63-3.30\\\\=0.33~m^3[/tex]


2. With one-third of its volume submerged, a 4,000-kilogram boat floats. If two more people, each of whom weighs 690 newtons, get into the boat, what additional volume of water is displaced?


Answer:

The weightiness of the added water displaced is equivalent to the joined weight of the two extra people who come to be into the boat:

m water g                   = 2 x 690 N

                                  = 1,380 N

The mass of the water displace is then

m water g                   = 1,380 N

                                  = 1,380 N / 9.8 m/s^2

                                  = 141 kg

Compute the calculation for density for the volume of water displace and practice this outcome for the mass of the water displace to get the answer:

p water                      = mass of water / volume of water

volume of water        = mass of water / p water

                                 = 141 kg / 1000 kg /m^3 eliminate kilogram

                                 = 0.14 m^3 the additional volume of water that is displaced
