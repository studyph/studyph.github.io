19. What is the 6th term of the geometric sequence 2, -4.8.-16, A. 32 B. -64 C.-128 D.-256


✏️GEOMETRIC SEQUENCE===============================

[tex]\large\bold{\red{QUESTION:}}[/tex]What is the 6th term of the geometric sequence 2, -4.8.-16,

[tex]\large\bold{\red{ANSWER:}} \: \large\underline{\sf{\green{\:\: B. \: \text-64\: \:}}} [/tex]

- Determine the common ratio.

[tex] \boxed{ \begin{gathered} \sf r = \frac{a_n}{a_{n - 1}} \end{gathered}}[/tex]

[tex] \sf r = \text - 16 \div 8 = \text - 2[/tex]

[tex] \sf r = 8 \div \text - 4 = \text - 2[/tex]

[tex] \sf r = \text - 4 \div 2= \text - 2[/tex]

- Now, we have a common ratio which is -2, then solve the other terms using the formula.

[tex] \boxed{ \sf a_n = a_1(r)^{n - 1} }[/tex]

[tex] \sf a_6 =2( \text - 2)^{6 - 1}[/tex]

[tex] \sf a_6 =2( \text - 2)^{5}[/tex]

[tex] \sf a_6 =2( \text - 32)[/tex]

[tex] \sf a_6 = \text -64[/tex]

- Therefore, the 6th term of the geometric sequence is -64

27. A certain ball bounces back at one-half of the height it fell from. if the ball is dropped from a height of 64 inches, how high is the ball at its 6th bounce?


Answer:

≈ 23.40676025 inches or 23 In

Explanation:

To find the bounce height, we must multiply the original height by 0.85. To find the height of the 2nd bounce, we multiply the height of the first bounce by 0.85 and to find the rest we just copy this method.

Solution:

Original height: 64 inches

64 × 0.85 = 54.4 (1st bounce height )

Find 2nd bounce height:

54.4 × 0.85 = 44.24 (2nd bounce height)

Find 3rd bounce height :

44.24 × 0.85 = 38.114 (3rd bounce height)

Find 4th bounce height:

38.114 × 0.85 = 32.3969 (4th bounce height)

Find 5th bounce height:

32.3969 × 0.85 = 27.537365 (5th bounce height)

Find 6th bounce height :

27.537365 × 0.85 = 23.40676025 (6th bounce height)
