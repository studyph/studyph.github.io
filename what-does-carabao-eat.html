Carabaos (Spanish: Carabao; Tagalog: Kalabaw; Cebuano: Kabaw) are a domestic swamp-type water buffalo (Bubalus bubalis) native to the Philippines. They were imported to Guam in the 17th century from the Spanish Philippines. Carabaos have a draught animal's low, wide, and hefty build. They are light grey to slate grey in color. Sickle-shaped horns or horns that curl backward toward the neck. Chevrons are a frequent pattern. Albinoids account up approximately 3% of the buffalo population. Carabaos weigh 420–500 kg (930–1,100 lb) in mature males and 400–425 kg (882–937 lb) in mature females. Males' withers range from 127 to 137 cm (50 to 54 in), while females' withers range from 124 to 129 cm (50 to 54 in) (49 to 51 in). Carabao is a very helpful animal.

28. a carabao is tied to a peg using an 8.5 meterrope how large is the area within which the carabao room and eat grass​


Answer:

226.865 m²

Step-by-step explanation:

Since it is in peg, the carabao can move in circular direction around the peg, just like compass.

The area of the circle is needed for it.

A = πr²

= (3.14)(8.5)²

= (3.14)(72.25)

= 226.865 m²

The area which carabao can room and eat grass is 226.865 m².
