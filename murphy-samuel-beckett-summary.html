3. what is the summary of comments on moro massacre by Samuel clemens


The Moro massacre by Samuel Langhorne Clemens happened near the crater of Bud Dajo, an extinct volcano in the southern Philippines. It is a story known to many Muslims but is new to people from the Northern Islands. It started with their objection against the “cedula” or community tax certificate. For the Muslim people, to pay for such a fraudulent piece of paper meant being subservient to a foreign power.

The more or less six hundred Moros who resided by the crater were lowland residents who moved up there to get away from the Americans. They were tired of their former Sultans and Datus that seemed at the time to be siding with the Christians from the two Northern Islands and the Americans. After some time, the population increased to somewhere along a thousand people, and they were able to build a village.

Unknown to the villagers, the Americans led by General Leonard Wood were hiking towards them armed with more advanced weapons. Then it all happened, both men and women fought against the invaders, and in the end, more than six hundred men women and children died.

It is worth noting that Samuel Clemens, who is more popularly known as “Mark Twain” was an imperialist earlier in his life, he supported the takeover of Hawaiian Islands. But there is something about the conquest of the Philippines that changed him. He became a critic of the American atrocities that went on in this country, and that at least is something we should be thankful for when remembering those died in 1906.

who is the rabbi of samuel?

Answer:

Samuel of Nehardea or Samuel bar Abba, often simply called Samuel and occasionally Mar Samuel, was a Jewish Amora of the first generation; son of Abba bar Abba and head of the Yeshiva at Nehardea, Babylonia. He was a teacher of halakha, judge, physician, and astronomer. He was born about 165 CE at Nehardea, and died there in 254 CE. In the Talmud, Samuel is frequently associated with Abba Arikha with whom he debated on many issues.

30. sino si gobernador heneral Frank Murphy?​


Answer:

William Francis Murphy (April 13, 1890 – July 19, 1949) was an American politician, lawyer and jurist from Michigan. He was a Democrat who was named to the Supreme Court of the United States in 1940 after a political career that included serving as United States Attorney General, 35th governor of Michigan, and the mayor of Detroit. He also served as the last governor-general of the Philippines and the first high commissioner of the Philippines.
