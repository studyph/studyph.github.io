14. why is it important to learn the physical activity barriers?


Many technological advances and conveniences have made our lives easier and less active, many personal variables, including physiological, behavioral, and psychological factors, may affect our plans to become more physically active. Understanding common barriers to physical activity and creating strategies to overcome them may help make physical activity part of daily life. People experience a variety of personal and environmental barriers to engaging in regular physical activity.

With technological advances and conveniences, people’s lives have in many ways become increasingly easier, as well as less active. In addition, people have many personal reasons or explanations for being inactive. The most common reasons adults don't adopt more physically active lifestyles are cited as:

insufficient time to exercise inconvenience of exercise lack of self-motivation non-enjoyment of exercise boredom with exercise lack of confidence in their ability to be physically active (low self-efficacy) fear of being injured or having been injured recently lack of self-management skills, such as the ability to set personal goals, monitor progress, or reward progress toward such goals lack of encouragement, support, or companionship from family and friends non-availability of parks, sidewalks, bicycle trails, or safe and pleasant walking paths close to home or the workplace

24. What are the barriers to our collective learning in the age of information.


Answer:

The Age of Information (which can also be called Digital Age, Computer Age, or New Age), brought tremendous changes in the way we access information. In this age, information can now be easily produced and accessed by a great number of people. However, along with these changes, we encounter various barriers to our collective learning such as:

Proliferation of fake news - since information can now be produced in real time, some use this to spread fake news. Unfortunately, not everyone has the skill and discipline to verify real from fake news. And this can result to unnecessary fear or anxiety for the receivers of information.Information Overload - not only can we easily access information, but we can also have access to many information which can result to information overload. Our brain can only process information, depending on one's readiness and capacity. Information overload can result to lack or loss of engagement as well as productivity.
