2. What are the differences between the two types of cell?

Prokaryotic cell -

Characteristics / Description

Membrane bound cell organelles such as Mitochondria, Golgi apparatus, Chloroplasts are absent. Prokaryotic cells include the four structures already described, but not much else, although bacteria do have cell walls. Many of them also have a cell capsule; the primary function of these is protection. Some prokaryotes also have whiplike structures on their surface called flagella. As you can guess from their appearance, these are used mainly for locomotion.

Term that describe organisms with this type of cell

Organisms that have prokaryotic cells are unicellular. They are called “prokaryotes.”

Examples

Examples of prokaryotes are bacteria, archaea, and cyanobacteria (blue-green algae)

Eukaryotic cell -

Characteristics / Description

Eukaryotic cells are larger than prokaryotic cells and have a “true” nucleus, membrane-bound organelles, and rod-shaped chromosomes. Eukaryotic cells, in contrast, are rich in organelles, which are membrane-bound entities that serve the cell in particular ways.

Term that describe organisms with this type of cell

Organisms with eukaryotic cells are called eukaryotes, and include fungi, animals, protists, and plants.

Examples

Organisms such as animals, plants, fungi, and protists are examples of eukaryotes because their cells are organized into compartmentalized structures called organelles, such as the nucleus

3. Give the main parts of the cell and their functions

the plasma membrane, the cytoplasm and the nucleus are the basic parts of cells

what are their functions?

Plasma Membrane or the cell membrane this structure forms the outer lining of the cell and is responsible for both the cell's physical integrity and for allowing certain substances to pass in and out while denying others passage. It actually consists of a double plasma membrane.

The cytoplasm

This forms the interior substance of cells and consists of a watery matrix that supports other interior cell contents, like a scaffolding. The liquid, non-organelle portion is called the cytosol, and most chemical reactions in the cell occur here with he help of proteins called enzymes.

The nucleus

controls and regulates the activities of the cell and carries the genes, structures that contain the hereditary information.
