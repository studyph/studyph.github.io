1. what are formula weights and molecular weights?


Answer:

Formula Mass Versus Molecular Mass

D. ... The formula mass (formula weight) of a molecule is the sum of the atomic weights of the atoms in its empirical formula. The molecular mass (molecular weight) of a molecule is its average mass as calculated by adding together the atomic weights of the atoms in the molecular formula.

4. What is the theoretical yield of chromium that can be produced by the reaction of 40.0 g of Cr2O3 with 8.00 g of aluminum according to the chemical equation below? 2Al + Cr2O3 LaTeX: \longrightarrow⟶ Al2O3 + 2Cr


Answer:

First, find the atomic weights from the periodic table.

Cr = 51.9961

Al = 26.981539

O = 15.9994

Then calculate the molecular weight of Cr2O3 = 151.9904

Find the limiting reagent by converting grams of each reactant to moles.

8.00 g Al / 26.981539 = 0.2965 mol Al

40.0 g Cr2O3 / 151.9904 = 0.2632 mol Cr2O3

The limiting reagent is Al, because twice as much Al is needed as Cr2O3.

From the balanced chemical equation, the moles of aluminum used equals the moles of chromium produced.

Now calculate the grams of chromium produced.

0.2965 mol Cr * (51.9961) = 15.42 g Cr

The answer is (B) 15.4 g

Edit: The answer below is incorrect because aluminum is the limiting reagent; that is, the aluminum is all used up before all the Cr2O3 can be used. Thus, you cannot calculate the Cr produced by assuming all the Cr in Cr2O3 is converted to Cr.

12. A compound contains 86.88% of carbon and 13.12% of hydrogen, and the molecular weight is 345. What will be the empirical and molecular formula of the compound?​


SOLUTION: (Empirical Formula)

Step 1: Assume that the mass of a compound is 100 g.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \text{mass C} = \text{86.88 g} \\ & \text{mass H} = \text{13.12 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of each element.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & n \: \text{C} = \text{86.88 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{12.01 g}} = \text{7.234 mol} \\ & n \: \text{H} = \text{13.12 g} \times \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{1.008 g}} = \text{13.02 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Represent an empirical formula.

[tex]\text{empirical formula} = \text{C}_x\text{H}_y[/tex]

Step 4: Divide the number of moles of each element by the least number of moles to get the subscripts of each element.

Since the smallest number of moles is 7.234 mol, we divide each number of moles by 7.234 mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & x = \frac{\text{7.234 mol}}{\text{7.234 mol}} = 1 \\ & y = \frac{\text{13.02 mol}}{\text{7.234 mo}} = 1.8 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 5: Multiply the subscripts obtained by a certain number to become whole number.

Since the decimal part of 1.8 is 0.8, we need to multiply all the subscripts by 5. Therefore,

[tex]\begin{aligned} & x = 1 \times 5 = 5 \\ & y = 1.8 \times 5 = 9 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 6: Write the empirical formula.

[tex]\boxed{\text{empirical formula} = \text{C}_5\text{H}_9}[/tex]

Hence, the empirical formula is C₅H₉.

------------------------------------------------------------

SOLUTION: (Molecular Formula)

Step 1: Represent a molecular formula.

[tex]\text{molecular formula} = (\text{C}_5\text{H}_9)_n[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the empirical weight.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{empirical weight} & = \text{5(12.01 g/mol) + 9(1.008 g/mol)} \\ & = \text{69.12 g/mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Divide the molecular weight by the empirical weight to find the value of n.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n & = \frac{\text{molecular weight}}{\text{empirical weight}} \\ & = \frac{\text{345 g/mol}}{\text{69.12 g/mol}} \\ & = 5 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Multiply the subscripts by the value of n to obtain the molecular formula.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{molecular formula} & = (\text{C}_5\text{H}_9)_5 \\ & = \boxed{\text{C}_{25}\text{H}_{45}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molecular formula of the compound is C₂₅H₄₅.

The atomic weight of carbon is 12.0107 grams per mole, the atomic weight of oxygen is 15.999 grams per mole, and the atomic weight of nitrogen is 14.00674 grams per mole. Using these values, you can calculate the molecular weight of urea to be approximately 60.06 grams per mole. Here's the calculation:

12.0107 + (2 * 14.00674) + (2 * 15.999) = 60.06 grams/mole
