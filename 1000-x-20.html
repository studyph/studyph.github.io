4. Two consecutive angles of a parallelogram have measures (x+20)0 and (2x-20)0. What is the measure of the larger angle? A.1000 B. 800 C. 600 D. 200


QUESTION:

Two consecutive angles of a parallelogram have measures (x+20)° and (2x-20)°. What is the measure of the larger angle?

SOLUTION:

Property of a parallelogram: Two consecutive angles are supplementary.

Using this property and the given measures of the two angles, we can have the equation:

[tex] {\boxed{\tt {(x + 20)° + (2x - 20)° = 180°}}}[/tex]

Now, let's use this equation to find the value of x

[tex]\large\tt (x + 20)° + (2x - 20)° = 180° \\ \\ \large\tt x + 20+ 2x - 20= 180 \\ \\ \large\tt 3x = 180 \\ \\ \large \tt \frac{3x}{3} = \frac{180}{3} \\ \\ \large \tt x = 60[/tex]

Then, substitute the value of x to the measures of the two angles and compare which is larger.

[tex] \tt{ \footnotesize{first \: angle : }} \\ \\ \large \tt (x + 20)° \\ \\ \large \tt = (60 + 20)° \\ \\ \large = { \tt{80°}} \\ \\ \\ \tt{ \footnotesize{second \: angle : }} \\ \\ \large \tt (2x - 20)° \\ \\ \large \tt = (2(60) - 20)° \\ \\ \large \tt = 120 - 20 \\ \\ \large = { \boxed{ \tt{100°}}}[/tex]

FINAL ANSWER: [tex]\huge\bold\red{\boxed{\tt{A.100°}}}[/tex]

y = 120x + 1000 (equation 1)

y - 100x = 850 (equation 2)

To eliminate y, we can subtract equation 2 from equation 1:

y - y - 100x = 120x - 100x + 1000 - 850

Simplifying:

20x = 150

Dividing both sides by 20:

x = 7.5

Now we can substitute x = 7.5 into either equation to find y. Using equation 1:

y = 120(7.5) + 1000 = 1900

Therefore, the solution to the system of equations is x = 7.5 and y = 1900.
