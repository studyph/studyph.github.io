Adults were also much faster at figuring out how their actions would affect themselves and other people. "Adults, on the other hand, ask: 'What would I do, given how I would feel and given how the people around me would feel as a result of my actions?'" Developing sensitivity Children start taking into account other people's feelings around the age of five. But the ability develops well beyond this age, the new research suggests. Answer:

The correct answer is b. between the pre-central and post-central gyrus.

Explanation:

The central sulcus is a large, deep groove in the cerebral cortex that separates the frontal lobe from the parietal lobe. It is located between the pre-central gyrus (part of the motor cortex) and the post-central gyrus (part of the sensory cortex). The central sulcus is an important landmark in the brain and helps to distinguish different areas of the cerebral cortex based on their function. It is not located between the right and left hemispheres, the frontal lobe and the occipital lobe, or the lateral sulcus and the precentral gyrus.
