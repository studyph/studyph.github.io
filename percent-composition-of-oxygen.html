Problem (1)Solution:

Step 1: Assume that the mass of a compound is 100 g.

[tex]\text{mass H = 11.19 g}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass O = 88.81 g}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of each element.

[tex]n \: \text{H = 11.19 g} × \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{1.008 g}} = \text{11.10 mol}[/tex]

[tex]n \: \text{O = 88.81 g} × \frac{\text{1 mol}}{\text{16.00 g}} = \text{5.551 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Represent an empirical formula.

[tex]\text{empirical formula} = \text{H}_{x}\text{O}_{y}[/tex]

Step 4: Divide the number of moles of each element by the least number of moles.

[tex]x = \frac{\text{11.10 mol}}{\text{5.551 mol}} = 2[/tex]

[tex]y = \frac{\text{5.551 mol}}{\text{5.551 mol}} = 1[/tex]

Step 5: Write the empirical formula.

[tex]\boxed{\text{empirical formula} = \text{H}_{2}\text{O}}[/tex]

Step 3: Represent an empirical formula.

[tex]\text{empirical formula} = \text{Mg}_{x}\text{C}_{y}\text{O}_{z}[/tex]
