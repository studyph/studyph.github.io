2. PROBABILITY (Grade 10) A card is drawn at random from a deck of 52 playing cards. Find the probability that the card drawn is: a.) an ace or a king b.) a king or a diamond


Answer:

A. An ace or a king= 15.38% or 2/13 or 2:13

B. A king or a diamond= 30.77% or 4/13 or 4:13

Step-by-step explanation:

Formula:

[tex]\frac{number of outcomes favorable to the event}{total number of possible outcomes}[/tex]

A. An ace or a king

There are 4 aces and 4 kings on a deck of 52 playing cards.

Aces and Kings= 8

Total number of playing cards= 52

[tex]\frac{8}{52}[/tex]= 15.38% or 2/13 or 2:13

B. A king or a diamond

There are 4 kings and 13 diamonds, but since there is 1 king on a diamond we will just add the 3 kings and 13 diamonds.

Kings and Diamonds= 16

Total number of playing cards= 52

[tex]\frac{16}{52}[/tex]= 30.77% or 4/13 or 4:13

PROBABILITIES

Two packs of 52-playing cards are shuffled and placed on a jar. If a card is randomly selected, what is the probability of selecting:-A diamond number card-A number card and a red diamond card

There are 2 packs of cards, so there would be 104 cards in total.

For the diamond number card

P(diamond card) = 26/104

P(number card) = 72/104

P(diamond number card) = P(diamond card) • P(number card)

P(diamond number card) = 26/104 • 72/104

P(diamond number card) = 9/52 or 17.31%

For the number card and a red diamond card

P(number card) = 72/104

P(red card) = 52/104

P(diamond card) = 26/104

P(number card and a red diamond card) = P(number card) • P(red card) • P(diamond card)

P(number card and a red diamond card) = 72/104 • 52/104 • 26/104

P(number card and a red diamond card) = 9/104 or 8.65%
