Finding a Part of a Fraction

Finding a part of a fraction is finding a part of a part. To know the part of a fraction is we need to multiply the fractions. The word "of" is being replaced by a multiplication sign (×).

Let is analyze the given problem.

What is asked?

The fraction of the tank of gas Jenny uses.

What are the given?

amount of gas in the tank - ⅞

amount of the gas in the tank Jenny uses - ⅔

What is the equation?

⅔ of ⅞ = ⅔ × ⅞ = N

Remember that the word "of" is being replaced by a multiplication sign (×).

What is the solution?

⅔ × ⅞ = ‌14⁄24‌ or 7⁄12‌

What is the final answer?

Jenny uses the 7⁄12‌ of the tank of gas.

Steps in Multiplying Fractions

There are three steps to multiply fractions.

Multiply the numerators.

Multiply the denominators.

Simplify, if needed.

Example:

⅔ × ⅞ = N

Step #1 : 2 × 7 = 14 (numerator)

Step #2 : 3 × 8 = 24 (denominator)

Step #3 : ‌14⁄24‌ or 7⁄12‌
