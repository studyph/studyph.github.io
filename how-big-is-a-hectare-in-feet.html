[tex]\large\bold\blue{\tt{given:}}[/tex]

0.573 hectares of land planted with sugarcane

[tex]\large\bold\blue{\tt{unknown:}}[/tex]

hectares of land not planted

[tex]\large\bold\blue{\tt{solution:}}[/tex]

First add the hectares of land that was planted,

[tex]0.993 \\ + 0.573 \\ = 1.566 \: hectares[/tex]

[tex]1.75 \\ - 1.566 \\ = 0.184hectares[/tex]

Therefore, [tex]\bold\red{0.184\:hectares}[/tex] of land was not planted.

9. Mr Ramirez had 3 pieces of land totaling 2 hectares . He gave each of his 3 sons their share of the land. To the first son he gave 2/3 of a hectare,to the second ,he gave 3/4 of a hectare . How big was the share of the third son?


Answer:

⅞

Step-by-step explanation:

Imagine 3 squares arranged side-by-side. Obviously considering that 1 square = 1 hectare wouldn't make the first santence of the problem true. Therefore we need to add half of a square for it to be true. This tells us that 1½ (or 3/2) of 3 pieces of land is to 1 hectare.

First son

⅔ of a hectare ⇒ (⅔)(³/₂) = 1 piece of the land

Second son

¾ of a hectare ⇒ (¾)(³/₂) = ⁹/₈ piece of the land

We know that Mr. Ramirez gave 3 pieces of land to his sons. Then the sum of their shares should equal to 3.

Let x = third son

1 + ⁹/₈ + x = 3

¹⁷/₈ + x = 3

x = 3 - ¹⁷/₈ = ⅞

17. Mr. Perez had 2 hectares of land. He gave each of three sons the share of the land. To the first son,he gave 2/3 of a hectare; to the second ke gave 3/4 of a hectare. How big was the share of the third son?​

[tex]\tt{} \dfrac{7}{12} \: hectare[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

[tex]\\ \tt{} \frac{2}{3} + \frac{3}{4} + x = 2[/tex]

Simplify the right side of the equation. LCD is 12. Make the denominators the same as the LCD.

[tex]\\ \tt{} \frac{2 \times 4}{3 \times 4} + \frac{3 \times 3}{4 \times 3} + x = 2[/tex]

[tex]\\ \tt{} \frac{8}{12} + \frac{9}{12} + x = 2[/tex]

[tex]\\ \tt{} \frac{8 + 9}{12} + x = 2[/tex]

[tex]\\ \tt{} \frac{17}{12} + x = 2[/tex]

Subtract [tex] \tt\frac{17}{12}[/tex] to both sides.

[tex]\\ \tt{} \frac{17}{12} - \frac{17}{12} + x = 2 - \frac{17}{12} [/tex]

[tex]\\ \tt{}x = 2 \times \frac{12}{12} - \frac{17}{12} [/tex]

[tex]\\ \tt{}x = \frac{2 \times 12}{12} - \frac{17}{12} [/tex]

[tex]\\ \tt{}x = \frac{24}{12} - \frac{17}{12} [/tex]

[tex]\\ \tt{}x = \frac{24 - 17}{12} [/tex]

[tex]\\ \tt{}x = \frac{7}{12} [/tex]
