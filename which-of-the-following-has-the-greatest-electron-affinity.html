1. Which of the following has the greatest electron affinity?a. Fluorineb. Berylliumc. Carbond. Nitrogen​


Answer:

A.Flourine

Explanation:

As fluorine sits atop chlorine in the periodic table, most people expect it to have the highest electron affinity, but this is not the case. Fluorine is a small atom with a small amount of space available in its 2p orbital. Because of this, any new electron trying to attach to fluorine experiences lower electron affinity from the electrons already living in the element's 2p orbital. Since chlorine's outermost orbital is a 3p orbital, there is more space, and the electrons in this orbital are inclined to share this space with an extra electron. Therefore, chlorine has a higher electron affinity than fluorine, and this orbital structure causes it to have the highest electron affinity of all of the elements.

15. Which of the following elements has the lowest electron affinity?a. Ironb. Manganesec. Rutheniumd. Technetium​


Answer:

A. Iron

Explanation:

Iron has the lowest electron affinity because it already has a half-filled d-orbital in its outermost shell, which makes it relatively stable. Adding an electron to this already stable configuration would require a significant amount of energy, which is why iron has a low electron affinity. Additionally, the smaller size of the iron atom makes it difficult for an additional electron to be added due to the increased repulsion between the negatively charged electron and the already existing electrons.
