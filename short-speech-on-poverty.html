How Poverty is Measured?

For measuring poverty United nations have devised two measures of poverty – Absolute & relative poverty. Absolute poverty is used to measure poverty in developing countries like India. Relative poverty is used to measure poverty in developed countries like the USA. In absolute poverty, a line based on the minimum level of income has been created & is called a poverty line. If per day income of a family is below this level, then it is poor or below the poverty line. If per day income of a family is above this level, then it is non-poor or above the poverty line. In India, the new poverty line is Rs 32 in rural areas and Rs 47 in urban areas.

Causes of Poverty

According to the Noble prize winner South African leader, Nelson Mandela – “Poverty is not natural, it is manmade”. The above statement is true as the causes of poverty are generally man-made. There are various causes of poverty but the most important is population. Rising population is putting the burden on the resources & budget of countries. Governments are finding difficult to provide food, shelter & employment to the rising population.

The other causes are- lack of education, war, natural disaster, lack of employment, lack of infrastructure, political instability, etc. For instance- lack of employment opportunities makes a person jobless & he is not able to earn enough to fulfill the basic necessities of his family & becomes poor. Lack of education compels a person for less paying jobs & it makes him poorer. Lack of infrastructure means there are no industries, banks, etc. in a country resulting in lack of employment opportunities. Natural disasters like flood, earthquake also contribute to poverty.

In some countries, especially African countries like Somalia, a long period of civil war has made poverty widespread. This is because all the resources & money is being spent in war instead of public welfare. Countries like India, Pakistan, Bangladesh, etc. are prone to natural disasters like cyclone, etc. These disasters occur every year causing poverty to rise.

Ill Effects of Poverty

Poverty affects the life of a poor family. A poor person is not able to take proper food & nutrition &his capacity to work reduces. Reduced capacity to work further reduces his income, making him poorer. Children from poor family never get proper schooling & proper nutrition. They have to work to support their family & this destroys their childhood. Some of them may also involve in crimes like theft, murder, robbery, etc. A poor person remains uneducated & is forced to live under unhygienic conditions in slums. There are no proper sanitation & drinking water facility in slums & he falls ill often & his health deteriorates. A poor person generally dies an early death. So, all social evils are related to poverty.

Government Schemes to Remove Poverty

The government of India also took several measures to eradicate poverty from India. Some of them are – creating employment opportunities, controlling population, etc. In India, about 60% of the population is still dependent on agriculture for its livelihood. Government has taken certain measures to promote agriculture in India. The government constructed certain dams & canals in our country to provide easy availability of water for irrigation. Government has also taken steps for the cheap availability of seeds & farming equipment to promote agriculture. Government is also promoting farming of cash crops like cotton, instead of food crops. In cities, the government is promoting industrialization to create more jobs. Government has also opened ‘Ration shops’. Other measures include providing free & compulsory education for children up to 14 years of age, scholarship to deserving students from a poor background, providing subsidized houses to poor people, etc.

Poverty is a social evil, we can also contribute to control it. For example- we can simply donate old clothes to poor people, we can also sponsor the education of a poor child or we can utilize our free time by teaching poor students. Ladies and gentlemen, today I would like to talk to you about poverty and its impact on society.

Poverty is a significant issue that affects millions of people around the world. It is defined as the state of being extremely poor, characterized by a lack of basic necessities such as food, shelter, and clothing.

Poverty not only affects individuals but also entire communities and societies. It leads to poor health, lack of education, and limited opportunities. Children growing up in poverty are more likely to drop out of school and less likely to succeed in life. Adults living in poverty are more likely to suffer from chronic health conditions and have limited access to healthcare.

Poverty also contributes to crime, violence, and social unrest. People living in poverty are more likely to turn to criminal activity as a means of survival. This not only harms the individuals involved but also the communities they live in.

However, poverty is not an unsolvable problem. There are several ways to address and reduce poverty, such as through economic growth, job creation, and social welfare programs. Investing in education and job training can also provide individuals with the skills and resources they need to break the cycle of poverty.

But it's not just the government's responsibility, we all have a role to play in addressing poverty. We can support organizations and charities that provide assistance to those in need, we can volunteer our time and resources, and we can advocate for policies that address poverty.

In conclusion, poverty is a significant issue that affects millions of people around the world, and it is important that we all work together to address it. We can do this by supporting those in need, investing in education and job training, and advocating for policies that address poverty. Thank you.

24. 300 word speech about "poverty is not due to overpopulation but because of inequality and corruption"​


Answer:

Ladies and gentlemen,

It is a common misconception that poverty is solely caused by overpopulation. However, the reality is far from it. Poverty is primarily a result of inequality and corruption, and not due to the number of people living in a certain area.

Inequality is a fundamental issue that results in a small group of people having the majority of resources and wealth, while the vast majority of people have little to no access to basic necessities like food, clean water, and healthcare. This inequality is perpetuated by systemic issues such as unequal distribution of resources, biased economic policies, and inadequate social safety nets. The wealthy have access to quality education and job opportunities while the poor are denied access to these essential tools. As a result, the cycle of poverty continues.

Similarly, corruption also plays a significant role in poverty. When people in power use their position to gain wealth and power for themselves, they are actively taking resources away from those in need. Corruption also leads to a lack of transparency and accountability, which means that resources meant to help the poor are often misused or stolen, leaving those in need without any aid.

Moreover, it is crucial to recognize that overpopulation is not the main cause of poverty. While population growth can lead to increased strain on resources, it is not the root cause of poverty. Countries with high population densities such as Japan, Singapore, and Hong Kong have managed to develop successful economies and provide their citizens with a high standard of living.

In conclusion, poverty is a complex issue that cannot be solely attributed to overpopulation. It is caused by inequality and corruption, which perpetuate a vicious cycle of poverty. To eradicate poverty, we need to address these systemic issues and promote equitable distribution of resources and opportunities. We must also fight corruption and ensure that resources meant for the poor are not misused or stolen. It is time for us to come together and work towards a future where poverty is no longer a reality. Thank you.
