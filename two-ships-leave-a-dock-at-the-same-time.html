2. two ships leave the dock at the same time. one sails northeast at rate of 8.5 miles an hour, the other sails north at the rate of 10 miles an hour. how far apart are they at the end of 2 hours.


Answer:

The two ships are 3 miles far apart at the end of 2 hours.

Step-by-step explanation:

Word Problem

Let us analyze the given problem above.

The problem above is asking about how far apart the two ships are at the end of two hours.

Given:

rate of the ship sails northeast - 8.5 mi/hr

rate of the ship sails north - 10 mi/hr

Equation:

There are two steps to know how far apart the two ships are:

1. Find the distance traveled by each ship.

8.5 mi/hr × 2 hrs = ?

10 mi/hr × 2 hrs = ?

2. Find the difference of the distances.

(10 mi/hr × 2 hrs) - (8.5 mi/hr × 2 hrs) = ?

Solution:

Step #1

8.5 mi/hr × 2 hrs = 17 miles

10 mi/hr × 2 hrs = 20 miles

Step #2

20 miles - 17 miles = 3 miles

Final Answer:

They are 3 miles far apart.

| Ship's Path

The distance between the lighthouse and the ship forms a right triangle with the ship's path and the line connecting the lighthouse to the ship. From this triangle, we can use the Pythagorean theorem to find the distance between the ship and the lighthouse:

distance^2 = (10)^2 + (8)^2

distance = sqrt(164) or about 12.80 miles

Now that we know the distance, we can figure out how long it will take the ship to travel this distance at a speed of 12 miles per hour:

time = distance / speed

time = 12.8 / 12

time = 1.07 or about 1 hour and 4 minutes

Therefore, the ship will be 8 miles from the lighthouse about 1 hour and 4 minutes after it departs from the dock at noon.

14. Two ships leave the same port. ship a travels at 30km/hr. ship b travels at 37km/hr. the angle between their path is 62∘. how far are the two cruises apart three hours later?


Application of Trigonometry: The Law of Cosines

[tex]\qquad \qquad \qquad \boxed{ \begin{minipage}{8cm} \qquad \qquad \qquad The Law of Cosines \\ \\ If \textit{A, B,} and \textit{C} are the measures of the angles of a triangle and \textit{a, b,} and \textit{c} are the lengths of the sides opposite these angles, then, \\ \\ \qquad \qquad $ \begin{aligned} \qquad c^2 & = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab\cosC \\ a^2 &= b^2 + c^2 - 2bc\cosA \\ b^2 &= a^2 + c^2 - 2ac\cosC \end{aligned}$ \end{minipage} }[/tex]

Question

Two ships leave the same port. Ship A travels at 30 km/hr. Ship B travels at 37 km/hr. The angle between their path is 62°. How far are the two cruises apart three hours later?

Answer

The two ships, A and B, would be 105 km apart from one another after three hours.

In this problem, we can apply one of the Law of Cosines, [tex]\bold{c^2 = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab \cos(C)}[/tex]

But before that, let us get the distances of each ship after three hours.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \rm Ship \ A & = \rm 30 \frac{km}{hr} \times 3 hr & = \bold{90 \ km} \\ \rm Ship \ B & = \rm 37 \frac{km}{hr} \times 3 hr & = \bold{111 \ km} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Now, we can solve for the distance of the two ships after three hours.

[tex]\begin{aligned} c^2 & = a^2 + b^2 - 2ab\cosC \\ \\ c^2 & = 90^2 + 111^2 - \left[2(90)(111)\cos(62^{\circ})\right] \\ \\ c^2 & = 8 100 + 12 321 - 9 380 \\ \\ c^2 & = 11 041 \\ \\ c & = \sqrt {11 041} \\ \\ \bold{c & = 105} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Solutions:

fish - fist

Change the word fist to fest by changing the vowel i to e.

fist - fest

Change the word fest to feet by changing the consonant s to vowel e.

fest - feet

Change the word feet to feel by changing the consonant t to l.

feet - feel

Change the word feel to reel by changing the consonant f to r.

feel - reel

25. Two ships leave the same port at the same time. One ship sails on a course of 110° at 32 mi/h. The other sails on a course of 230° at 40 mi/h. Find the distance between them after 2 hours. Express your answer to the nearest mile.


Answer:

The distance between the two ships after 2 hours is 125 miles.

Step-by-step explanation:

In Unit Circle, the location of the angle in the Cartesian Plane is measured counterclockwise from the x-axis (the initial side) about the origin (the standard position) to the terminal side.

The reference angle is the acute angle from the terminal side of the given angle to the x-axis.

In the given problem, we have to solve for the following:

Distance traveled by each ship going to different directionThe reference angleDistance between the ships (displacement) after two hours using Law of Cosines

Given:

Ship a on a course of 110°, Northwest

Speed = 32 mi/hourTime traveled: 2 hours

Ship b on a course of 230°, Southwest

Speed = 40 mi/hrTime traveled: 2 hours

Distance c between a and b: Unknown

Solve for the distance traveled by each ship after 2 hours:

Ship a:

Distance = Speed ×  Time

Distance = (32 mi/hr) × (2 hr)

Distance = 64 miles, Northwest

a = 64

Ship b:

Distance = Speed ×  Time

Distance = (40 mi/hr) × (2 hr)

Distance = 80 miles, Southwest

b = 80

Find the reference angle:

Ship a:

θ = 180 - 110

θ = 70°

Ship b:

θ = 230 - 180

θ = 50°

Add the angles to solve for C, the angle formed between the locations of the ships after two hours:

C = 70° + 50°

C = 120°

The Law of Cosines where a, b, and c are the sides or lengths:

a² = b² + c² - 2bc (Cos A)b² = a² + c² - 2ac (Cos B)c² = a² + b² - 2ab (Cos C)

Solve for the c, the distance between a and b where:

a = 64b = 80C = 120°

Find c:

c² = a² + b² - 2ab (Cos C)

c² = (64)² + (80)² - 2(64)(80)(Cos 120°)

c² = 4,096 + 6,400 - (10,240)(-0.5)

c² = 10,496 + 5,120

c² = 15,616

√c² = √(15,616)

c ≈ 124.96

c ≈ 125 miles (to the nearest mile)

The distance between the two ships after 2 hours is 125 miles.
