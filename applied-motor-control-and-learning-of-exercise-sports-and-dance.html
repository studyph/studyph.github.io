12. what is motor control?​


Answer:

Motor control is the regulation of movement in organisms that possess a nervous system. Motor control includes reflexes as well as directed movement. To control movement, the nervous system must integrate multimodal sensory information and elicit the necessary signals to recruit muscles to carry out a goal.

15. what is the underlining diffrrence between motor control and motor learning?​


ANSWER:

Difference: Motor learning helps us understand how we learn skills so that the skill becomes automatic. Motor control is essential for every movement from poorly skilled to well skilled. Motor learning is responsible for the shift from poorly skilled to highly skilled movements.
