2. How much charge can a 4µF capacitor store on its plates when it is connected to a 12V battery?


Answer:

The stored electric charge in a capacitor, Q (in coulombs, abbreviated C) is equal to the product of the capacitance C (in Farads, abbreviated F) of the capacitor, and the voltage V (in volts, abbreviated V) across its terminals. That is, Q = C٠V.

For example, if C = 33μF and V = 20V, then Q = (33٠10^-6)٠(20) = 660μC.

If you take the same capacitor and increase the voltage from 20V to, say, 50V, then Q = (33٠10^-6)٠(50) = 1650μC.

Answer:

The energy stored in a battery can be calculated using the formula: Energy = 1/2 * C * V^2, where C is the charge stored in the battery and V is the voltage of the battery.

Given, C = 30C and V = 12V.

Substituting these values into the formula, we get:

Energy = 1/2 * 30 * 12^2 = 1/2 * 30 * 144 = 8640 J.

So, the energy stored in the 12V battery is 8640 J.

19. how are the bulbs, wires and batteries connected​


Answer:

The bulbs, wires, and batteries are connected in a circuit. The batteries provide electrical energy, which flows through the wires to the bulbs, causing them to light up. The circuit can be either a series circuit or a parallel circuit. In a series circuit, the bulbs and batteries are connected end-to-end in a single loop, so the current flows through each bulb in turn. In a parallel circuit, the bulbs and batteries are connected in separate branches, so the current flows through each bulb independently of the others. The wires are used to connect the bulbs and batteries together, completing the circuit.

Answer:

The bulbs, wires, and batteries can be connected in different ways depending on the type of circuit being created. Here are three common ways these components can be connected:

Series Circuit: In a series circuit, the bulbs, wires, and batteries are connected in a single path. The positive terminal of one battery is connected to the negative terminal of the other battery. The first bulb is then connected to the positive terminal of the first battery, and the second bulb is connected to the negative terminal of the second battery. The wires connect the bulbs to the batteries, creating a complete circuit.

Parallel Circuit: In a parallel circuit, the bulbs, wires, and batteries are connected in multiple paths. The positive terminal of one battery is connected to the positive terminal of the other battery, and the negative terminal of one battery is connected to the negative terminal of the other battery. Each bulb is then connected to the positive terminal of one battery and the negative terminal of the other battery. The wires connect the bulbs to the batteries, creating multiple complete circuits.

Combination Circuit: A combination circuit is a mix of series and parallel circuits. In this type of circuit, some bulbs may be connected in series while others are connected in parallel. The batteries and wires are connected in the same way as in a series or parallel circuit.

In all of these circuits, the batteries provide the electrical energy, the wires conduct the electricity, and the bulbs convert the electrical energy into light. The way the bulbs, wires, and batteries are connected determines the properties of the circuit, such as the current, voltage, and resistance.
