Before we simplify the fractions, how can we simplify them?

Simplifying fractions steps:

List out the factors.Find the greatest common factors or so called GCF.Divide top (numerator) and bottom (denominator) by the GCF.

Example:

[tex] \frac{32}{36} [/tex]

Factors of 32: 1, 2, 4, 8, 16, 32

Factors of 36: 1, 2, 4, 6, 9, 18, 36

The GCF is 4.

[tex] \frac{32}{36} = \frac{32 \: \div \: \red{4}}{36 \: \div \red{4}} = \green{ \frac{8}{9}} [/tex]

The simplify form of [tex] \frac{32}{36} [/tex] is [tex] \frac{8}{9} [/tex]

==========================================

29. Calculate the mole fraction of each component of a solution containing 65 g of ethanol (CHO)in 350 g of water.​


Mol fraction of ethanol : 0.068

Mol fraction of water : 0.932

Further explanation

The mole fraction shows the mole ratio of a substance to the mole of solution /mixture  

[tex]\tt x_a=\dfrac{n_a}{n_a+n_b}[/tex]  

xa + xb = 1

Given

65 g ethanol

350 g water

Required

The mole fraction

Solution

mol ethanol( C₂H₅OH), MW=46,07 g/mol :

= mass : MW

= 65 g : 46.07 g/mol

= 1.41

mol water (H₂O), MW=18 g/mol :

= 350 g : 18 g/mol

= 19.44

mol total :

= 1.41 + 19.44

= 20.85

mol fraction of ethanol :

= 1.41 : 20.85

= 0.068

mol fraction of water :

= 19.44 : 20.85

= 0.932
