Steps to Take to Insulate Yourself from Misinformation

The most important step to take is to interrupt your own reflex of sharing posts that outrage you or resonate with your beliefs. Scammers capitalize on that instinct by tailoring their stories to fit the beliefs of their targets so they will slip under their defenses.

Never share a post on social media without fact checking

This is especially true if it comes from a source you trust. If you want to spread truth, you need to assume that other people aren't perfect and may make mistakes.

Look for the sources

Mainstream news is only as good as the sources they rely on. Anonymous sources, or stories coming from only a single source are more suspect than stories with multiple sources of confirmation. This doesn't mean that they are false; many important leaks and stories have come from single anonymous sources. But don't assume they are true, especially if they are sensational, until there is confirmation.

Consider the story's agenda

Stories are colored by the agendas of their writers.

News organizations typically operate for money, and part of their brand (and thus their profits) lies in their accuracy. However, many news groups tailor their content to particular segments of customers, and thus are more likely to cover stories with a particular perspective. This can skew their accuracy, turning it into affinity with the preconceptions of their target audience.

Political organizations exist to get their candidates elected and agenda passed, and thus any stories from a campaign or candidate are more suspect because they will always be intended to persuade. This does not make them automatically false, but watch for emotional appeals, arguments that disaster is just around the corner unless you act, or other measures designed to make you fearful or anxious.

Don't discount stories that play to emotions, but view them with caution

Injustices and corruption are things that make any ethical person upset or angry. A story that promotes emotion is not automatically false just because it provokes strong feelings.

Unfortunately, scammers and propagandists know the power of emotion and use that tool in their work. Therefore, fact check before you share.

Background:Social Science

The field of research devoted to the study of communities and the relationships among persons within those societies is known as social science. The phrase was previously applied to sociology, the first "science of society," which was founded in the nineteenth century. It now incorporates a wide range of academic disciplines, including anthropology, archaeology, economics, human geography, linguistics, management science, political science, psychology, and history, in addition to sociology.

What Are Social Sciences and How Do They Work?

The social sciences are a collection of academic disciplines that study society. This field of study looks at how people connect with one another, behave, grow as a culture, and impact the world.

Important Notes:The social sciences are a collection of academic disciplines that study how people interact in society.Anthropology, psychology, political science, and economics are examples of social science degrees.Working as an economist, psychologist, or social worker, or in law, government, non-profits, or academia are all common jobs in the social sciences.Social work and economics are two of the most rapidly developing industries in the United States.We gain a better knowledge of how to establish more inclusive and effective institutions as a result of social science research.

Field of Social Sciences

The social sciences include:

Anthropology.

Economics.

Political science.

Sociology.

Social psychology.

Choose one to interpret...Let's saySocial Psychology

The study of how the presence and actions of others impact individual or group behavior is known as social psychology.

The main question that social psychologists consider is: How and why are environmental elements like social contact influencing people's perceptions and actions?

How do people's thinking and decisions change as a result of social interactions? Is human conduct a good predictor of one's personality?Is social behavior goal-oriented?What effect does social perception have on behavior?How might potentially harmful social attitudes like prejudice emerge?

Since the turn of the twentieth century, social psychology has been a formal discipline. In 1898, Indiana University psychology researcher Norman Triplett conducted a study of "social facilitation" to explain why bicycle racers seemed to outperform their solo performances while competing against others.

26. explanation po dito tungkol sa REFLECTING SOCIAL INEQUALITIES IN OUR SOCIETY SA ANO PO ITO HMM SA POSTER MAKING PO ​


Social inequality refers to relational processes in society that have the effect of limiting or harming a group's social status, social class, and social circle.

Areas of social inequality include access to voting rights, freedom of speech and assembly, the extent of property rights and access to education, health care, quality housing, traveling, transportation, vacationing and other social goods and services.

Apart from that it can also be seen in the quality of family and neighbourhood life, occupation, job satisfaction, and access to credit.

Three classroom climates in courses focusing on inequality are identified, those of resistance, paralysis, and rage. In resistant classes, students deny the importance of class, gender, race, and other lines of stratification or fail to see their structural sources. In paralyzed classes, students are so overwhelmed by the pervasiveness of inequality that they become debilitated and depressed; social structures are reified, giving them a false aura of inevitability. In enraged classes, the existence of stratification sparks so much anger that students lash out in an unfocused manner that is often blind to the complexities of stratified societies. In this article, I offer suggestions for responding to each of these three classroom climates.
