1. Compelling plot: "The Star-Child" has a captivating plot that keeps the reader engaged till the end. It tells the story of a boy who is born with a star on his forehead, but his beauty and arrogance make him treat others poorly, leading to his downfall.

2. Vivid descriptions: Wilde's use of descriptive language helps readers visualize the story's setting and characters. The vivid descriptions make the story more immersive and engaging.

3. Moral lesson: "The Star-Child" teaches a valuable lesson about the consequences of treating others poorly. The story encourages readers to be kind, compassionate, and considerate to others, regardless of their status or appearance.

4. Symbolism: The star on the child's forehead represents beauty and uniqueness, but it also serves as a reminder of the boy's arrogance and pride. The use of symbolism adds depth to the story and makes it more thought-provoking.

5. Timeless themes: The themes explored in "The Star-Child," such as beauty, arrogance, and redemption, are still relevant today. The story's enduring themes make it a classic that continues to resonate with readers of all ages.

On a balanced lever, weight varies inversely as the distance from thefulcrum to the weight. The diagram shows a balanced lever. How muchdoes the child weigh?

SOLUTION⤵️

equation of the inverse variation:

w=k/d

substituting the values given to get the constant:

w=k/d60=k/460x4=k258=k

Plugging the value of k we will get:

w=258/3.2 w=80.625
