1. The two types of bright nebulae are emission nebulae and _______________ nebulae.


Bright nebulae are dense clouds of gas and dust within the interstellar medium from which stars have been or are being formed from. The two types of bright nebulae are emission nebulae and reflection nebulae. Emission nebulae are typically red and reflection nebulae blue. The colors are manifestation of their compositions. Emission nebulae are composed of hydrogen, glowing due to the intense radiation of hot stars inside it. Reflection nebulae, on the other hand, do not emit light on its own but instead just reflects the light of nearby stars.ANSWER: Reflection

9. what is nebula? and how this nebula happen?


NEBULA

Nebula usually refers to a cluster of stars that are very far away and that look like a luminous cloud at the night sky.

Composition

It is a huge cloud of gas and dust. Like stars nebulae also composed of mostly helium and hydrogen and contains a little amount of other heavier elements.

Origin

A nebula can be compressed under duress by a strong external forces, such as supernova, or the explosion of a neighbor star. This causes the nebula to bond and contract, then eventually cools afterward. Gravity often causes parts of the nebula to break apart and collapse.

30. what is the planetary nebula


A planetary nebula, is a type of emission nebula consisting of an expanding, glowing shell of ionized gas ejected from red giant stars late in their lives. The term "planetary nebula" is a misnomer because they are unrelated to planets.

Formation

A planetary nebula is created when a star blows off its outer layers after it has run out of fuel to burn. These outer layers of gas expand into space, forming a nebula which is often the shape of a ring or bubble.

Size

1 light-year

Forms and structure. Compared with diffuse nebulae (see H II region), planetary nebulae are small objects, having a radius typically of 1 light-year and containing a mass of gas of about 0.3 solar mass.

Temperature

The temperature of the gas in the nebula is about 10,000 degrees Celsius, and the central stars of planetary nebulae are among the hottest stars in the Universe, with temperature in the range of 25,000 to over 200,000 degrees Celsius.

Purpose

Planetary nebulae are important objects in astronomy because they play a crucial role in the chemical evolution of the galaxy, returning material to the interstellar medium which has been enriched in heavy elements and other products of nucleosynthesis (such as carbon, nitrogen, oxygen and calcium).
