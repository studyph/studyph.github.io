30. Based on the Philippine Professional Standards for School Heads what are the main functions of a school Head


Answer:

This principle revolves around the idea that school heads are responsible for developing people (such as teachers and the community) and improving school practices by addressing the professional and personal needs of teachers and enhancing the school's learning environment.

Answer:

A school head, also known as a principal or headteacher, is responsible for the overall operation and management of a school. Some of the main functions of a school head may include:

Setting and enforcing school policies: The head is responsible for establishing and enforcing policies related to academic standards, behavior, and other areas that affect the school community.

Leading and managing staff: The head is responsible for hiring, evaluating, and developing staff members, as well as managing their performance and professional development.

Managing the budget: The head is responsible for managing the school's budget, including developing and implementing financial plans, monitoring expenses, and making recommendations for budget changes.

Building relationships with the community: The head is responsible for building and maintaining positive relationships with parents, students, and members of the community, as well as collaborating with other schools and organizations.

Providing academic leadership: The head is responsible for setting academic goals and standards for the school, monitoring student progress, and implementing strategies to improve student achievement.

Ensuring the safety and well-being of students: The head is responsible for ensuring the safety and well-being of all students, including implementing safety protocols and responding to emergencies.

Advocating for the school: The head is responsible for advocating for the school and its students, including representing the school in the community and working with policymakers to address issues affecting the school.
