  • 27. What is It An analogy is a literary device that compares the relationship between things that have differences and similarities. It is a way to clarify an idea by comparing it to something familiar. It is like simile and metaphor We use analogies all the time, informally. In written, or in conversation when you compare one situation to another, you are using analogy. Example of analogy cats: clutter: wolves: pack You can read this as: Cats is to clutter as wolves is to pack, In this example, clutter refers to a group of cats. Similarly, pack is the specific term for a group of wolves. Both pairs of words in the analogy illustrate the same relationship. The relationship between the words in the first pair is compared to the relationship of words in the second pair. This is what we call an analogy. 4. infographics, graphic texts, poster,comics and pictures are example of _____type of a multimodal text.a. Analogb. Digitalc. Lived. paper based​


Multimodal Text

Answer:

D. Paper Based

Explanation:

infographics, graphic texts, posters, comics, and pictures are examples of paper-based multimodal text. They use paper to print ads, educational materials, messages, contents, or even persuasive messages to gain the attention of the public.

What is Multimodal Text?

There are five modes of communication and they are visual, linguistic, spatial, aural, and gestural.

Visual

It refers to what we see every day such as signing, advertisements in print, etc.

Aural

It refers to the audio mode such as music, sound effects, npises, songs, tone, etc.

Gestural

The interpretation is interpreted or understood.

Linguistic

Written or spoken words such as word of use, professionalism, coherence of ideas, organization of thoughts, etc.

Spatial

The arrangement of things or elements in an environment or a given space. It involves a proper organization of items.

27. What is It An analogy is a literary device that compares the relationship between things that have differences and similarities. It is a way to clarify an idea by comparing it to something familiar. It is like simile and metaphor We use analogies all the time, informally. In written, or in conversation when you compare one situation to another, you are using analogy. Example of analogy cats: clutter: wolves: pack You can read this as: Cats is to clutter as wolves is to pack, In this example, clutter refers to a group of cats. Similarly, pack is the specific term for a group of wolves. Both pairs of words in the analogy illustrate the same relationship. The relationship between the words in the first pair is compared to the relationship of words in the second pair. This is what we call an analogy.
