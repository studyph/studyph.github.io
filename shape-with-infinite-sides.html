Another square is drawn in such a way that its vertices would lie also at the midpoints of the sides of the second square. This process is continued infinitely. Find the sum of the areas of the infinite square.


The areas of the square form a geometric sequence where a1=256, a2=64,... and the common ratio will be [tex] \frac{1}{4} [/tex].

[tex]Sum= \frac{a_{1} }{1-r} [/tex]
[tex]Sum= \frac{256}{1- \frac{1}{4} } [/tex]
[tex]Sum= \frac{256}{ \frac{3}{4} } [/tex]
[tex]Sum= \frac{1024}{3} [/tex]
