7. what is the volume of a 2.20 grams carbon dioxide at 27 degrees celsius and 1.2 atm?​


Answer:

Volume = 1.027 L

Explanation:

PV = nRT

Rearranging, V = (nRT)/P

n = 2.20g CO2 (mol CO2/44g CO2) = 0.05mol CO2

T = 27C+ 273.15 = 300.15 K

P = 1.2 atm

R = 0.08206 (L atm/mol K)

Substituting into the equation,

V = [ (0.05mol CO2)(0.08206 L atm/mol K)(300.15 K) ] / 1.2 atm

V = 1.027 L

21. a gas measures 10 ml at 27 degrees celsius and 760 mmhg what will be the volume of the gas at 15 degrees celsius and 750 mm Hg?​


Answer:

10mL

Explanation:

When working with gas laws, the temperature is always in Kelvins. To get Kelvins from a Celsius temperature, add 273.15 to the Celsius temperature.

Given:

P1 = 760 mmHg

V1 = 10mL

T1 = 27°C + 273.15 = 300 K

P2 = 750 mmHg

T2 = 15°C + 273.15 = 288 K

Missing:

V2 = ?

Solution:

Rearrange the equation to isolate V2. Substitute the known values into the equation and solve.

P1V1/T1 = P2V2/T2

V2 = P1V1T2/T1P2

= 760mmHg × 10mL × 288 K/300 K × 750mmHg

V2 = 2,188,800 mL / 225,000

V2 = 9.7 or 10mL

24. A 9.0L volume of gas is heated from 27 degree Celsius to 12 degree Celsius at constant pressure.whatis the final volume?​


Answer:

To determine the final volume of the gas, you can use the ideal gas law, which states that the volume of a gas is directly proportional to its temperature at constant pressure. This means that if the temperature of the gas decreases, its volume will also decrease.

To calculate the final volume, you can use the following formula:

V2 = V1 * (T2/T1)

Where:

V1 is the initial volume of the gas

T1 is the initial temperature of the gas

V2 is the final volume of the gas

T2 is the final temperature of the gas

Plugging in the values from the problem, you get:

V2 = 9.0L * (12C/27C)

V2 = 4.0L

So, the final volume of the gas is 4.0L.
