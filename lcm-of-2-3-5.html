1. LCM - 70

14 and 35 is (2 × 7) = 21 × 71 and (5 × 7) = 51 × 71 respectively. LCM of 14 and 35 can be obtained by multiplying prime factors raised to their respective highest power, i.e. 21 × 51 × 71 = 70.

Hence, the LCM of 14 and 35 by prime factorization is 70.

GCF - 7

14 = 2 x 7

35 = 5 x 7

2. LCM - 8

2 and 8 is (2) = 21 and (2 × 2 × 2) = 23 respectively. LCM of 2 and 8 can be obtained by multiplying prime factors raised to their respective highest power, i.e. 23 = 8.

Hence, the LCM of 2 and 8 by prime factorization is 8.

GCF - 2

8 = 4 x 2

2 = 2 x 1

3. LCM - 65

5 and 13 is (5) = 51 and (13) = 131 respectively. LCM of 5 and 13 can be obtained by multiplying prime factors raised to their respective highest power, i.e. 51 × 131 = 65.

Hence, the LCM of 5 and 13 by prime factorization is 65.

GCF - 1

5 = 5 x 1

13 = 1 x 13

4. LCM - 45

9 and 15 is (3 × 3) = 32 and (3 × 5) = 31 × 51 respectively. LCM of 9 and 15 can be obtained by multiplying prime factors raised to their respective highest power, i.e. 32 × 51 = 45.

Hence, the LCM of 9 and 15 by prime factorization is 45.

GCF - 3

9 = 3 x 3

15 = 3 x 5

5. LCM - 12

4, 6, and 12 is (2 × 2) = 22, (2 × 3) = 21 × 31, and (2 × 2 × 3) = 22 × 31 respectively. LCM of 4, 6, and 12 can be obtained by multiplying prime factors raised to their respective highest power, i.e. 22 × 31 = 12.

Hence, the LCM of 4, 6, and 12 by prime factorization is 12.
