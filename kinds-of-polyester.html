23. Which Losses Electrons When Rubbing Hair and Polyester?​


Answer:

When you rub a piece of hair against a piece of polyester, the friction created between the two materials causes the transfer of electrons between them. The electrons from the hair are transferred to the polyester, leaving the hair with a positive charge and the polyester with a negative charge.

So, in this case, the hair loses electrons and becomes positively charged, while the polyester gains electrons and becomes negatively charged.
