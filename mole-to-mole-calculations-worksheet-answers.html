Stoichiometry is the study of the relative quantities of reactants and products in chemical reactions and how to calculate those quantities.

Key Terms

stoichiometry: The field of chemistry that is concerned with the relative quantities of reactants and products in chemical reactions and how to calculate those quantities.

Chemical equations are symbolic representations of chemical reactions. The reacting materials (reactants) are given on the left, and the products are displayed on the right, usually separated by an arrow showing the direction of the reaction. The numerical coefficients next to each chemical entity denote the proportion of that chemical entity before and after the reaction. The law of conservation of mass dictates that the quantity of each element must remain unchanged in a chemical reaction. Therefore, in a balanced equation each side of the chemical equation must have the same quantity of each element.

11. When 21.44 moles of Si reacts with 17.62 moles of N2, how many moles of Si3N4 are formed? This will require two separate calculations.


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Step 1: Write the balanced chemical equation.

3Si + 2N₂ → Si₃N₄

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of Si₃N₄ formed by each reactant.

• Using Si

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 3 moles of Si is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mole of Si₃N₄.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of} \: \text{Si}_3\text{N}_4 & = 21.44 \: \cancel{\text{mol Si}} \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Si}_3\text{N}_4}{3 \: \cancel{\text{mol Si}}} \\ & = \text{7.147 mol} \: \text{Si}_3\text{N}_4 \end{aligned}[/tex]

• Using N₂

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 2 moles of N₂ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mole of Si₃N₄.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of} \: \text{Si}_3\text{N}_4 & = 17.62 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{N}_2} \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Si}_3\text{N}_4}{2 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{N}_2}} \\ & = \text{8.810 mol} \: \text{Si}_3\text{N}_4 \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Determine the limiting reagent.

Since Si produced less amount of Si₃N₄ than N₂, Si is the limiting reagent.

Final Step: Determine the number of moles of Si₃N₂ formed

Note that the (maximum) number of moles of a product is dictated by the limiting reagent. Since Si is the limiting reagent, the number of moles of Si₃N₄ that can be formed is

[tex]\boxed{\text{moles of} \: \text{Si}_3\text{N}_4 = \text{7.147 mol}}[/tex]

2. To calculate the number of moles of Fe2O3 in a 5.00 g sample, you will need to use the formula: moles = mass / molar mass. The molar mass of Fe2O3 is 159.69 g/mol. Therefore, the number of moles of Fe2O3 in a 5.00 g sample is:

moles = 5.00 g / 159.69 g/mol

moles = 0.0313 moles

moles = 0.472 g / 42.99 g/mol

moles = 0.0109 moles

24. Calculate the mole fraction of NaCl and H2O, if 0.010 moles of NaCl is dissolved in 5.56 moles of pure water


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

• For NaCl

[tex]\begin{aligned} X_{\text{NaCl}} & = \frac{n_{\text{NaCl}}}{n_{\text{NaCl}} + n_{\text{H}_2\text{O}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.010 mol}}{\text{0.010 mol + 5.56 mol}} \\ & = \boxed{0.0018} \end{aligned}[/tex]

• For H₂O

[tex]\begin{aligned} X_{\text{H}_2\text{O}} & = \frac{n_{\text{H}_2\text{O}}}{n_{\text{NaCl}} + n_{\text{H}_2\text{O}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{5.56 mol}}{\text{0.010 mol + 5.56 mol}} \\ & = \boxed{0.998} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the mole fractions of NaCl and H₂O are 0.0018 and 0.998, respectively.
