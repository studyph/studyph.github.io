There is a relationship between the strength of an acid (or base) and the strength of its conjugate base (or conjugate acid): ⚛ The stronger the acid, the weaker its conjugate base. ⚛ The weaker the acid, the stronger its conjugate base. ⚛ The stronger the base, the weaker its conjugate acid.

8. Calculate the ph of a 0.42 m nh4cl solution. hint: kb of the conjugate base of nh4cl, nh3, is 5.6 ×10-10


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Summarize the concentrations using the ICE table.

[tex]\begin{array}{lccccc} & \text{NH}_4^+(aq) & \rightleftharpoons & \text{NH}_3(aq) & + & \text{H}^+(aq) \\ \text{Initial} \: (M): & 0.42 & & 0 & & 0 \\ \text{Change} \: (M): & -x & & +x & & +x \\ \hline \text{Equilibrium} \: (M): & 0.42-x & & x & & x \\ \end{array}[/tex]

Step 2: Write the Ka expression.

[tex]K_{\text{a}} = \dfrac{[\text{NH}_3][\text{H}^+]}{[\text{NH}_4^+]}[/tex]

Step 3: Solve for the hydrogen ion concentration.

[tex]\begin{aligned} 5.6 \times 10^{-10} & = \frac{(x)(x)}{0.42 - x} \\ 5.6 \times 10^{-10} & = \frac{x^2}{0.42 - x} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Because Ka is too small and the initial concentration of the acid is large, we can apply the approximation 0.42 - x ≈ 0.42

[tex]\begin{aligned} 5.6 \times 10^{-10} & = \frac{x^2}{0.42} \\ x^2 & = (5.6 \times 10^{-10})(0.42) \\ x^2 & = 2.352 \times 10^{-10} \\ x & = \sqrt{2.352 \times 10^{-10}} \\ x & = 1.534 \times 10^{-5} \: M\end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the pH of the solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{pH} & = -\text{log} \: [\text{H}^+] \\ & = -\text{log} \: (1.534 \times 10^{-5}) \\ & = \boxed{4.81} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the pH of a 0.42-M NH₄Cl solution is 4.81.
