12. Moto of being stressed in school


The motto of being stressed in school should be to "manage stress, not to let it manage you." Stress is a normal part of the school experience, but it can also be overwhelming and disruptive to academic and personal success. By learning how to manage stress, students can focus on their studies and enjoy their school experience.

Managing stress starts with recognizing the symptoms of stress and taking steps to reduce them. This may involve taking breaks, practicing relaxation techniques, getting enough sleep, eating well, and exercising regularly. It may also involve seeking support from friends, family, or professionals.

It's important to remember that stress is a natural response to challenging situations, and it can be a motivator for growth and success. Rather than letting stress control your thoughts or actions, use it as a tool to stay focused, motivated, and productive. By managing stress effectively, you can achieve your academic and personal goals while maintaining good physical and mental health.
