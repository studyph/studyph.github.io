4. WHAT IS THE MOLE FRACTION OF HCl in 20% aqueous HCl solution?​


Solution:

Step 1: Assuming that the mass of an aqueous HCl solution is 100 g, the mass of HCl and H₂O are

[tex]mass_{\text{HCl}} = 20 \: g[/tex]

[tex]mass_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = 100 \: g - 20 \: g = 80 \: g[/tex]

Step 2: Calculating the number of moles of the masses above

[tex]n_{\text{HCl}} = 20 \: g \times \frac{1 \: mol}{36.46 \: g} = 0.55 \: mol[/tex]

[tex]n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = 80 \: g \times \frac{1 \: mol}{18.02 \: g} = 4.44 \: mol[/tex]

Step 3: Calculating the total number of moles

[tex]n_{T} = n_{\text{HCl}} + n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{T} = 0.55 \: mol + 4.44 \: mol[/tex]

[tex]n_{T} = 4.99 \: mol[/tex]

Step 4: Calculating the mole fraction of HCl

[tex]X_{\text{HCl}} = \frac{n_{\text{HCl}}}{n_{T}}[/tex]

[tex]X_{\text{HCl}} = \frac{0.55 \: mol}{4.99 \: mol}[/tex]

[tex]\blue{X_{\text{HCl}} = 0.11}[/tex]

8. 3. An aqueous solution of hydrochloric acid contains 36% HCl by mass. (a)Calculate the mole fraction of HCl in the solution. (b) Calculate themolality of HCl in the solution​


Given:

concentration = 36% by mass of aqueous HCl solution

solute: HCl

solvent: water

Required:

mole fraction

molality

Solution: (a)

Step 1: Determine the mass of the components present.

Since the given is in terms of percent by mass, we assume that the mass of the solution is 100 g. Therefore, 36% by mass aqueous HCl solution contains 36 g of HCl (solute) in 64 g of water (solvent).

mass of solute = 36 g

mass of solvent = 64 g

Step 2: Calculate the molar mass of each component.

For solute

molar mass of solute = (1.008 g/mol × 1) + (35.45 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of solute = 36.458 g/mol

For solvent

molar mass of solvent = (1.008 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of solvent = 18.016 g/mol

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of each component.

For solute

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{36 g}}{\text{36.458 g/mol}}[/tex]

moles of solute = 0.9874376 mol

For solvent

[tex]\text{moles of solvent} = \frac{\text{mass of solvent}}{\text{molar mass of solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solvent} = \frac{\text{64 g}}{\text{18.016 g/mol}}[/tex]

moles of solvent = 3.5523979 mol

Step 4: Calculate the mole fraction of solute.

[tex]\text{mole fraction} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{moles of solute + moles of solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mole fraction} = \frac{\text{0.9874376 mol}}{\text{0.9874376 mol + 3.5523979 mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mole fraction} = 0.22}[/tex]

Solution: (b)

Step 1: Calculate the molality of solution.

Since the moles of solute is already obtained in Step 3 of Solution (a), which is 0.9874376 mol, and the mass of solvent is already given, which is 64 g or 0.064 kg, the molality of solution is

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{mass of solvent (kg)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{0.9874376 mol}}{\text{0.064 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molality} = 15 \: m}[/tex]

10. Initially, HCl has a volume of 50 mL and a concentration of 5 molar. What will be the final concentration of the solution if the solution is diluted to 175 mL ?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & M_{\text{i}} = 5 \: M \\ & V_{\text{i}} = \text{50 mL} \\ & V_{\text{f}} = \text{175 mL} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final concentration of the solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} M_{\text{f}} & = \frac{M_{\text{i}}V_{\text{i}}}{V_{\text{f}}} \\ & = \frac{(5 \: M)(\text{50 mL})}{\text{175 mL}} \\ & = \boxed{1.43 \: M} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the final concentration of the solution will be 1.43 M.

19. Calculate the molar concentration of a dilute HCl solution if 50.00 mL aliquot yielded 0.5902 g of AgCl.​


Answer:

0.08 M

Explanation:

AgNO3 + HCl ⇌ AgCl + HNO3

Given:

HCl = 50 mL

AgCl = 0.5902 g

Solution:

We are looking for the molar concentration (mol AgNo3 / L HCl) and the volume of the HCl is given, we just need to solve for the mol of AgNO3 using the AgCl that was yield.

Conversion of volume from mL to L:

HCl = 50 mL = 50/1000 = 0.05L

Solving for mol of AgNO3 from g of AgCl:

(By looking at the chemical reaction we can see that 1 mol AgCl yields 1 mol AgNO3)

mol of AgNO3 = (g of AgCl)* (1/Molar Mass of AgCl)*(mol AgNO3 / mol AgCl)

mol of AgNO3= 0.5920g * (1 mol AgCl / 143.32 g AgCl) * (1mol AgNO3 / 1 mol AgCl)

mol of AgNO3 = 0.004 mol

Solving for Molar concentration:

Molar concentration = mol AgNo3 / L HCl

                                  = 0.004 mol/ 0.05L

                                 = 0.08 M

C2 = (6.0 M x 0.25 L) / 0.45 L

C2 = 3.33 M

Therefore, the concentration of the diluted solution is 3.33 M.
