2. Public Administration vs Private Administration


Public Administration versus Private Administration

The difference between public administration and private administration is that while public administration is a political activity that is run by the government with a service motive, private administration is a business activity that is run by a group of individuals not related to government in any way whatsoever with the sole motive of profit.

Main differences between Public Administration and Private Administration

Public administration is set up by the government. While Private administration is not set up by the government instead it is set up by a group of private individuals.

Public administration follows a bureaucratic approach while private administration follows an egalitarian approach.

Public administration follows a pluralistic approach of decision making while private administration follows a monopolistic approach to decision making.

Public administration has a service and welfare motive. Private administration is profit-oriented i.e the sole motive here is profit maximization.

Public administration is accountable to the general public while private administration is accountable to its employees.

Public Administration earns revenue through taxes, fines, fees, etc. while private administration earns revenue through profit.

Administrators working in a public administration are paid salaries while administrators working in a private administration earn their remuneration through profits.

Public administration is political activity while private administration is a business activity.

14. what is the main purpose of a skills vs knowledge in education essay?​


Answer:

Skills vs Knowledge in Education

Education is one of the most important aspects of human development, providing individuals with the tools they need to succeed in life. Traditionally, education has focused on the acquisition of knowledge and information, but in recent years there has been a shift towards developing skills that are essential in the modern world. While both knowledge and skills are crucial components of education, their purposes are distinct and serve different needs.

Knowledge refers to the information that an individual has acquired through learning, observation, and experience. In education, knowledge is often imparted through textbooks, lectures, and other forms of instruction. The main purpose of knowledge in education is to provide individuals with a broad understanding of the world around them, including its history, culture, and scientific principles. Knowledge also allows individuals to develop critical thinking and problem-solving skills, which are essential for success in many professions.

Skills, on the other hand, refer to the practical abilities that an individual has developed through practice and application. These can include communication, teamwork, creativity, adaptability, and leadership. In education, the main purpose of skills is to prepare individuals for the challenges of the modern world, including the demands of the workforce. By developing these skills, individuals are better equipped to navigate the complexities of the modern workplace, including rapid technological change, global competition, and shifting economic conditions.

One of the main benefits of developing skills is that they are transferable across a range of different contexts. For example, the ability to communicate effectively is a skill that is valuable in both personal and professional settings, and can be applied in a variety of different industries and roles. By contrast, knowledge is often context-specific and may be less relevant outside of a particular domain.

While knowledge and skills are both important in education, there has been a growing recognition of the value of skills in recent years. This is due in part to the changing nature of work, which increasingly requires individuals to be adaptable and versatile in their abilities. In response to these trends, many educational institutions have shifted their focus towards developing skills that are essential in the modern workplace.

In conclusion, while both knowledge and skills are essential components of education, their purposes are distinct. The main purpose of knowledge in education is to provide individuals with a broad understanding of the world around them, while the main purpose of skills is to prepare individuals for the challenges of the modern world, including the demands of the workforce. By developing both knowledge and skills, individuals can acquire the tools they need to succeed in life, both personally and professionally.

The enhanced community quarantine in Luzon is a series of stay-at-home orders and cordon sanitaire measures implemented by the Inter-Agency Task Force for the Management of Emerging Infectious Diseases (IATF-EID) on the island of Luzon and its associated islands. It is part of the COVID-19 community quarantines in the Philippines, a larger scale of COVID-19 containment measures with varying degrees of strictness. The "enhanced community quarantine" (ECQ) is the strictest of these measures and is effectively a total lockdown.

There were three instances of the ECQ being implemented in Luzon. The first was implemented between March 17 and May 31, 2020. This was announced on March 16, two days after the government of the Philippines under Rodrigo Duterte placed Metro Manila under a "community quarantine" on March 14. It was implemented throughout Luzon from March 17 until April 30, and remained in areas with a high risk of infection until May 31. New degrees such as "modified enhanced community quarantine" (MECQ) and "general community quarantine" (GCQ) were introduced by the IATF-EID during the month of May as easing restrictions commenced, until all restrictions under ECQ and MECQ were downgraded to GCQ and "modified general community quarantine" (MGCQ) on June 1.

After two months of a relaxed GCQ status, a second MECQ was reimplemented in Metro Manila and its immediate surrounding provinces on August 4 and was lifted on August 18. Finally, on January 24, 2021, a third ECQ was restored in Tabuk while an MECQ was raised in four more municipalities in Kalinga. It was lifted on February 15. Due to a recent spike in COVID cases, especially in the Greater Manila Area, another ECQ was reimplemented for the Holy Week starting on March 29 until April 4, which has since been extended until April 11, 2021.

The ECQ affected around 57 million people in Luzon during its peak. It also resulted in the mobilization of the national and local governments, with the Bayanihan to Heal as One Act passed to combat the epidemic. The effectiveness of the ECQ implementation was noted by a study made by the University of the Philippines, although there were also several documented cases of violations of ECQ regulations. Authorities then pushed for stricter enforcement, which in turn raised concerns of human rights violations.
