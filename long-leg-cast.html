Answer:

Casts and splints support and protect injured bones and soft tissue. When you break a bone, your doctor will put the pieces back together in the right position. Casts and splints hold the bones in place while they heal. They also reduce pain, swelling, and muscle spasm.If the broken bone is still in position, you'll usually just need a plaster cast. 7. If an 8-meter tree cast as 12-meter long shadow, Then how long does a 6-meter tall tree cast?


Answer:

Assuming the ratio of the height of the tree to the length of its shadow remains the same, we can set up a proportion:

8 meters (height of the first tree) is to 12 meters (length of its shadow) as 6 meters (height of the second tree) is to x (length of its shadow).

Mathematically, this can be represented as:

8/12 = 6/x

To solve for x, we can cross-multiply:

8x = 72

x = 9

Therefore, a 6-meter tall tree would cast a 9-meter long shadow under the same conditions.
