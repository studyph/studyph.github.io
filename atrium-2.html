2. 1. ventricle 2. atrium​


Answer:

1. A ventricle is one of two large chambers toward the bottom of the heart that collect and expel blood received from an atrium towards the peripheral beds within the body and lungs. The atrium (an adjacent/upper heart chamber that is smaller than a ventricle) primes the pump.

2. The atrium (Latin ātrium, “entry hall”) is the upper chamber through which blood enters the ventricles of the heart. There are two atria in the human heart – the left atrium receives blood from the pulmonary (lung) circulation, and the right atrium receives blood from the venae cavae (venous circulation).

PARTS OF THE HEART

• Ventricle

A ventricle is the hollow parts of human's heart or each of the two section of cavities of the heart that pump blood throughout the body.

• Left Atrium

Left atrium is known as the part of the heart that receives blood coming from the lung circulation within the respiratory system.

• Right Atrium

The right atrium in contrast, is the upper chamber of the heart that receives deoxygenated blood from the vena cava and pumps it into the right ventricle, that also sends the blood out to the lungs so that it become oxygenated.

• Left Ventricle

The left ventricle is known as the thickest of all the heart's chambers and that enables or responsible for the pumping of oxygenated blood to tissues all throughout the body.

• Right Ventricle

The right ventricle on the other hand, is only responsible for pumping blood throughout the lungs within our body.

• Valve

The main function of the valves in our heart, is that it prevent the backward flow of blood.
