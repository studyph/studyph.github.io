12. if 620J of energy are absorbed by a resistor in 10 minutes, what is the power to the resistor​


Answer:

1.0333 Joules/sec

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

Energy = 620 J

Time = 10 mins

The formula is given as: E = PT; where

E = energy in Joule

P = power in Joule/sec

T = time in seconds

First, we have to convert 10 minutes into seconds.

There are 60 seconds in 1 minute, so to convert this, we have to multiple 60 seconds/1 minute to 10 minutes. See the equation below:

[tex]10 minutes(\frac{60 seconds }{1 minute}) = 600 seconds[/tex]

Now that we have the time in seconds, we can now use the equation given earlier.

E = P x T

620 = 600P

Now, we divide both sides by 600 to isolate P

[tex]\frac{620}{600} = \frac{600P}{600}[/tex]

We can now cancel 600 since 600 divided by 600 equals to 1.

[tex]P = \frac{620}{600}[/tex]

In simpler terms, the power would be:

P = 1.0333 Joules/sec

Therefore, the power is equal to 1.0333 Joules/sec.
