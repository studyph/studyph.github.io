1. short story about Cyber bullying


In this day and age, more and more people are turning to technology for comfort and support. With this comes the need to be aware of the dangers of cyberbullying.

Cyberbullying is defined as an intentional act of online aggression. This can include sending hateful messages, spreading rumors, or sharing sensitive information about a person. It can occur in an instant through a wide variety of platforms, whether it be social media, forums, or even instant messaging.

Cyberbullying is a form of harassment, and it can make its victims feel isolated and scared, making them reluctant to even seek help or talk to anyone. The effects of cyberbullying can be devastating, leading to depression, anxiety, and even suicide in some cases.

This story takes place in a quiet, small town. Nicole, a 17 year old high school student, had been a victim of cyberbullying for quite some time. She never saw it coming.

One day, a malicious rumor was spread about her, saying that she had an affair with her teacher. Everyone in her school read the post and Nicole soon became the talk of the town. Everyone was throwing hateful comments and insults at her and Nicole quickly began to feel overwhelmed and scared.

She didn't know who had posted the malicious rumor and was left feeling helpless and alone. She didn't want to talk to anyone about it, she just wanted to hide in her room and never come out.

However, one person eventually came to her rescue. Her best friend, Matthew, showed up at her doorstep with a bouquet of flowers and a casserole. He had heard about the cyberbulling, and had spent all day cooking her food and picking her flowers to remind her of the kind, compassionate people in the world.

Seeing his genuine display of love and support broke Nicole out of her despair and she finally opened up to him about her experience. With his help, she was able to confront the bullies, put an end to their taunts, and seek professional help.

This story reminds us that even in moments of darkness, hope and compassion can be found in the most unexpected places. Cyberbulling is an alarming issue that can have devastating consequences, but with the support of friends and family, there is always a light at the end of the tunnel.

B. Write a short non-fiction story with its complete elements about a school scenario a group of boys bullies a helpless girl.

Non-fiction Story: "Standing Up to Bullies"

Sarah was a shy and introverted girl who had just started at a new school. She didn't know anyone and was struggling to make friends. But things got worse when a group of boys started bullying her. They would call her names, push her in the hallways, and make fun of her in front of others.

At first, Sarah tried to ignore them and hoped they would eventually leave her alone. But the bullying continued, and it was taking a toll on her self-esteem and happiness. She felt alone and helpless, with no one to turn to.

One day, Sarah decided enough was enough. She couldn't let the bullies win. She took a deep breath and, with a newfound courage, confronted the boys. She told them she wouldn't stand for their bullying anymore and demanded they stop.

The boys were taken aback by Sarah's sudden assertiveness. They had never expected her to stand up to them. But instead of backing down, they began to laugh and taunt her even more.

Sarah felt her heart sink. Had she made a mistake? But then something incredible happened. A group of other students stepped forward and stood with her. They told the bullies they wouldn't tolerate their behavior and that Sarah was their friend.

The bullies looked shocked and intimidated by the sudden show of support. They mumbled some apologies and walked away, defeated.

Sarah felt a surge of gratitude and relief. She had finally found the courage to stand up to the bullies, and it had paid off. She had made new friends and had shown the bullies that their behavior wouldn't be tolerated.

From that day on, Sarah continued to stand up for herself and others who were being bullied. She became a beacon of strength and courage for her school, and her story inspired others to speak up and take action against bullying. In the end, Sarah's bravery had transformed her school into a kinder and more inclusive community.

26. suggest short film script about bullying ​


Answer:

Title: Alone

Opening shot of a school playground. The camera focuses on a group of kids playing basketball. One of the kids, JASON (12), is watching from a distance. He looks lonely and sad. Suddenly, a group of boys approach him.

BULLY 1: Hey, what are you doing here? You're not cool enough to play with us.

JASON: (nervously) I wasn't trying to play. I was just watching.

BULLY 2: (pushing Jason) Well, you shouldn't be here. You're a loser.

The bullies continue to taunt and push Jason until he runs away. The camera follows him as he runs to the bathroom and locks himself in a stall. He's crying.

Flashback to Jason at home. He's sitting on his bed, looking at a picture of himself and his old best friend. He remembers the good times they had together.

Back to the present. Jason is still crying in the bathroom stall. Suddenly, he hears a knock on the door. It's a boy named TYLER (12).

TYLER: Hey, are you okay in there?

JASON: (hesitantly) Y-yeah, I'm fine.

TYLER: (suspicious) Are you sure? You don't sound fine.

JASON: (sighs) No, I'm not okay. Those guys were bullying me.

TYLER: (sympathetically) I know how you feel. They used to bully me too.

Tyler talks to Jason and tells him about how he stood up to the bullies and made new friends. He convinces Jason to come out of the stall and face the bullies.

The camera cuts to Jason and Tyler walking out of the bathroom. The bullies are waiting for them.

BULLY 1: (sarcastically) Oh, look who's back. The crybaby and his new friend.

TYLER: (firmly) Leave him alone. He hasn't done anything to you.

The bullies try to start a fight, but Tyler stands up to them and scares them away. Jason is amazed at Tyler's bravery.

JASON: (grateful) Thank you for standing up for me.

TYLER: (smiling) No problem. We're friends now, right?

Jason smiles and nods. The camera follows Jason as he walks away, feeling happy and confident for the first time in a long time.

Closing shot of the school playground. The camera pans to the basketball court. Tyler and Jason are playing basketball together, and the other kids are cheering them on.
