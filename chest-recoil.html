A gun’s recoil, or kickback, is the backward movement a shooter feels when the bullet is discharged. When a gun exerts a force on a bullet as it’s launching it forward, the law of physics says the bullet will exert an equal force in the opposite direction of the gun.

24. A 5.5kg rifle fires an 11g bullet with a velocity of 1000m/s. Determine (a) the velocity of recoil of the rifle(b) the velocity of recoil on the assume mass of the shooter is 120kg.​


Answer:

0.09 m/s.

Explanation:

Initial momentum:

P_i=mv=(0.011)(1000) kgm/s

Initial momentum:

P_f=(M+m^' )V.

From the conservation of momentum:

(M+m^' )V=mv

The recoil velocity is

V=m/((M+m^' ) ) v=(0.011)(1000)/(120+5.5)=0.09 m/s.
