Primary Function of Farm Irrigation System

The primary function of a farm irrigation system is to provide crops with water at the appropriate time and in the appropriate quantity. Diverting water from the source is one of the specific functions. Transporting water to specific fields on the farm.

Farm Irrigation System

Farm irrigation systems are three types of methods for applying water to crops: surface irrigation, sprinkler irrigation, and micro-irrigation. It is difficult to decide whether to install an irrigation system or convert it to a more efficient irrigation system. From the standpoint of water conservation, the choice is straightforward, with water savings increasing as surface irrigation systems are converted to sprinkler systems and sprinkler systems are converted to micro-irrigation systems. The success of an irrigation system, on the other hand, is highly dependent on site and situational factors, as well as the level of management, employed. Before switching to alternative irrigation systems, existing irrigation systems should be thoroughly evaluated.

Categories of Farm Irrigation System:Flood irrigation system suppliers have equipment to distribute water for flood irrigation in furrow irrigation and graded border irrigation.Sprinkler irrigation can irrigate fields of virtually any size, slope, or shape. The various ways sprinklers are used in farm irrigation systems to distribute water over a field are listed below.Drip irrigation employs the use of small diameter poly tubing with emitters to apply water to a small area directly to a crop's root zone. To water a specific tree or plant, the emitters can be manually installed into the tubing. Emitter tubing, which has drip emitters installed at the factory on a specific spacing to reduce installation costs, is also used. Drip systems can be installed above ground or buried to protect the tubing.Micro irrigation is the use of small, low-volume sprinklers known as micro-sprinklers on farm irrigation systems to irrigate orchards and vineyards. The micro-sprinkler is typically installed at the base of a tree to water only that tree. The micro-sprinkler is sometimes installed between two trees. Water can be delivered to the micro-sprinklers via above-ground polyethylene or poly pipe, or via buried PVC pipe.

30. What are the precautionary measures in draining and irrigating the farm?


Answer:

IRRIGATION SAFETY

This story is available at our safety resource center, SafeAtWorkCA.com, and will be removed from this location by December 31.

Irrigation systems move and deliver water that is vital for the survival of agricultural crops. Moving equipment, electricity, water pressure, pumping systems, and field conditions related to irrigation and agriculture require workers to follow safety protocols.

To be safe, read the operator’s manual and receive training for each irrigation system and specific equipment components that you use and maintain. Follow all of the manufacturer’s suggestions for inspection, maintenance, and repair schedules and procedures. Some general safety guidelines include:

Reading and understand the Danger, Warning, and Caution signs on all equipment parts.

Wearing personal protective equipment such as sturdy boots, coveralls, gloves, and a hardhat. Add hearing and eye protection if needed.
