3. Differentiate technical writing from non technical writing and give examples


Technical Writing :

Technical writing is a piece of writing which focuses on factual and straight forward content and technical papers are published to inform and instruct and educate the user about some specific topic. There exists specific readers who prefers technical papers. It gives readers information about some technical topics or it gives directions on how to do something.

For example writing any articles on GeeksforGeeks related to computer science field comes under technical writing.

General Writing :

General writing refers to any piece of writing which focuses in general subjects of writing and general papers are published for amusement of the reader. But sometimes many general topics also gives life lessons, moral, inspiration etc. There is not such specific set of audience or readers like technical writing. It is written on subjective tone and purely personal style.

For example writing any prose or story which will be published in a local magazine comes under general writing

7. Compare technical report write with other forms of writing?​


Answer:

1. Audience: Technical reports are usually written for a specific audience, such as engineers, scientists, or supervisors. Other forms of writing can be written for a broader audience or to a specific individual.

2. Purpose: The purpose of a technical report is to inform, analyze, and make recommendations based on data or research. Other forms of writing may aim to persuade, entertain, inform, or educate.

3. Structure: Technical reports generally follow a specific structure that includes an introduction, methodology, results, analysis, and conclusion. Other forms of writing may have a different structure or none at all.

4. Style: Technical reports use a more formal and objective tone compared to other forms of writing that may be more conversational or personal.

5. Language: Technical reports often use technical terms and jargon specific to the field or industry being discussed, whereas other forms of writing may use simpler language geared towards a broader audience.

Overall, technical reports are a specialized form of writing that requires a deep understanding of the subject matter and a focus on providing accurate and detailed information.

11. examples of technical writing


Technical writing is a form of written communication where the writer intends to convey information in a professional manner. This is commonly used when writing resumes, job application letters, user guides and manuals, business proposals, employee or student handbooks, etc. Its purpose is to provide clear instructions for the reader to follow or to walk the reader through complex information.
A teacher, for example, becomes a technical writer when creating instructional materials and/or lesson plans. He/She enumerates the steps on how to facilitate a certain activity in the module or just simplifies a complex idea to make it more understandable for the students by presenting them in bullets, graphs, etc.
