1. 136. Physical exercises designed to developed strength and coordination.​


Answer:

First, let's determine what we mean by strength. For preschool and kindergarten children, we are not talking about strength/conditioning programs (such as those in high schools) that focus on developing targeted muscle groups. Rather, young children need overall strength so that they can participate in a wide variety of activities, derive pleasure from those activities, gain confidence in their abilities to "do things" and, in fact, have the strength to "do things" - particularly new things.

Coordination is another ability that begins developing "on its own" as infants explore their bodies and their world. Eye-hand coordination begins to develop as babies first start to put fingers together, reach for toes, and reach for objects. Overall coordination grows as babies learn to roll over, sit, crawl, stand, and walk. So by coordination, we mean a, series of movements organized and timed to occur in a particular way to bring about a particular result.

When we start thinking about and planning for strength and coordination in young children, we have to realize that like all developmental issues, there are going to be individual differences, and, in general, development is going to happen at its own rate. You cannot "make" development happen; you can only support it by creating the right environment for each child as he reaches a particular point on the developmental continuum.

6. A particular cell phone tower is designed to service a 12-mile radius. The tower is located at (-3,5) on a coordinate plane whose units represent miles. What is the general equation of the outer boundary of the region serviced by the tower


x²+6x+y²-10y-110=0

Step-by-step explanation:

This is the equation of a circle. Center is the tower, located at (-3, 5)

Standard form of the equation (plug in the values):

(x-h)² + (y-k)² = r²

(x+3)² + (y-5)² = 144

The general form of the equation of a circle is given by: x²+y²+2hx+2ky+c=0 (don't be confused by c; this just represents a constant number)

So your answer should look something like this: x²+y²+x+y+number=0

Now, translate into general equation by expanding:

(x+3)(x+3) + (y-5)(y-5) = 144

x²+6x+9+y²-10y+25 = 144    

(now transpose 144 to equate everything to 0, and then combine like terms)

x²+6x+9+y²-10y+25-144 = 0  

x²+6x+y²-10y-110=0

How to Use a Coordinate Plane:

Understanding a concept like the coordinate plane often means putting the abstract terminology and descriptions into a real-world setting. Mathematics describes the real world, but often it isn’t clear how the concepts translate to real life. Coordinate planes range from being abstract representations of other variables to spatial coordinates that are easy to find real-world examples of. To use a coordinate plane in real life, simply choose what type of system you’re going to use and define the directions they go in. However, you need to consider a few more complicated ideas to get the most out of it.

Production management, frequently known as operations management, is the planning and control of industrial processes to ensure that they run as smoothly as possible. Production management techniques are used in both the service and distribution industries. A production manager is in charge of overseeing a theatrical production's overall fund and schedule. This usually entails coming up with new ways to cut expenses, hiring and scheduling crew personnel, and resolving any mission that threaten to hold down the production's development toward opening night.

Functionality, branding, packaging, service, quality, appearance, and warranty conditions are all factors to consider while developing a product. Consider the major features, benefits, and customer needs and wants when developing your item.PRICE- The way you set prices for your products or services is referred to as the price factor. Sales, public relations, direct marketing, advertising, sponsorship, and social media are all part of it.PLACE- The place element describes how you deliver your product or service to clients at the appropriate time, in the right place, and in the right amount. It covers things like disposition routes (such as a storefront, online, or through a distributor), location, logistics, service standards, and market coverage.PEOPLE- Customers, yourself, and your employees are all part of the people factor. If you want to expand your firm, you must consider both your employees and your customers. It entails determining what your consumers want and needs, defining goals, and tracking your customer service levels in order to attract and retain loyal customers.

Explanation:

Henri Fayol was one of the first theorists to define functions of management in his 1916 book “Administration Industrielle et Generale”. Henri Fayol identified 5 functions of management, which he labelled: planning, organizing, commanding, coordinating and controlling. Henri Fayol theorized that these functions were universal, and that every manager performed these functions in their daily work.

The 5 functions put forward by Henri Fayol may therefore not completely represent the total complexity faced by managers, and the normative approach may be too rigid to illustrate which functions managers need to perform in modern contemporary companies and organizations.
