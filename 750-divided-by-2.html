19. An amount of P 12 750 will be divided to Sarah, James and Carla in the ratio of 3:5:7 respectively. How much will each receive?​


[tex]\sf{\underline{{Problem:}}}[/tex]

An amount of P12,750 will be divided to Sarah, James and Carla in the ratio of 3:5:7 respectively. How much will each receive?

[tex]\sf{\underline{{Answer:}}}[/tex]

[tex] \rm{Sarah= 3}[/tex][tex] \rm{James = 5}[/tex][tex] \rm{Carla = 7}[/tex][tex] \rm{3 + 5 + 7 = 15}[/tex]

[tex] \rm{12,750 ÷ 15 = 850}[/tex]

[tex] \rm{850 × 3 = 2,550}[/tex][tex] \rm{850 × 5 = 4,250}[/tex][tex] \rm{850 × 7 = 5,950}[/tex]

Hence, Sarah will receive Php 2,550.00 ,James will receive Php 4,250.00 and Carla will receive Php 5,950.00 from the amount Php 12,750.

[tex] \text{A = 2,550 + 4,250 + 5,950}[/tex][tex] \text{A = 6,800 + 5,950 }[/tex][tex] \text\green{A = 12,750 }[/tex]

Problem: An amount of P 12,750 will be divided to Sarah, James and Carla in the ratio of 3:5:7 respectively. How much will each receive?

Solution: Let x be the number multiplied to each part of the ratio and equate of an amount of P 12,750.

[tex]3x + 5x + 7x = 12750[/tex]

[tex]15x = 12750[/tex]

[tex] \begin{gathered} \frac{15x}{15} = \frac{12750}{15} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{gathered} \frac{ \cancel{15}x}{ \cancel{15}} = \frac{12750}{15} \end{gathered}[/tex]

[tex]x = 850[/tex]

- Substitute the value of x to each part of the ratio.

[tex]3(850) : 5(850) : 7(850)[/tex]

[tex]2550 : 4250 : 5950[/tex]

- Therefore, the amount of P 12,750 was divided into 3 person. Sarah has an amount of P 2,550, James has an amount of P 4,250 and Carla has an amount of P 5,950.
