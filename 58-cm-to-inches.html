Given Statement:

A diameter of a circle measure 58 cm.

Problem:

What is the length of its radius?

Diameter is the is the length of the line segment passes through the center of the circle from two edge of the circle.

Radius is half of the diameter.

radius (r) = 1/2diameter (d)

d = 58 cm

r = 1/2(58 cm)

r = 58/2 cm

r = 29 cm

[tex]\sf Perimeter = 2(Length + Width)[/tex]

Given:

[tex]\sf Perimeter=58[/tex][tex]\sf Width = 8[/tex]

Substituting the value in the formula,

[tex]\sf 58 = 2(Length + 8)[/tex]

[tex]\implies \sf \frac{58}{2}= Length + 8[/tex]

[tex]\implies \sf 29 = Length + 8[/tex]

[tex]\implies \sf 29 - 8 = Length[/tex]

[tex]\implies \boxed{\boxed{\sf 21 = Length}}[/tex]
