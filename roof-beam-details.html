3. Bob is building a wooden cabin. The cabin is 303030 meters wide. He obtained a bunch of 171717 meters long wooden beams for the roof of the cabin. Naturally, he wants to place the roof beams in such an angle that each pair of opposite beams would meet exactly in the middle. What is the angle of elevation, in degrees, of the roof beams? Round your final answer to the nearest tenth. ^\circ ∘ degrees


Answer:

28.1°

Step-by-step explanation:

The roof will be in the shape of an isosceles triangle with a base length of 30 m and two sides that are 17 m. The two 17 m beams will have the same angle of elevation since they have to might in the middle.

So to find the angle of elevation, we can split the roof in half vertically to create a right triangle. The base will now be 15 m, and the hypotenuse will be 17. Now we can use a trigonometry function to solve for the angle. We know the hypotenuse and the side adjacent to the angle, so we can use cosine.

[tex] \cosθ = \frac{adjacent}{hypotenuse} \\ \\ \cosθ = \frac{15}{17} \\ \\ \cos^{ - 1} ( \frac{15}{17} ) = θ \: \\ \\ θ = 28.1°[/tex]

30. A rafter is any parallel beams that support a roof. if the rafter is 4 meters long and makes an angle of 35° with horizontal, what is the height of the rafter's peak? ​


Problem:

A rafter is any parallel beams that support a roof. if the rafter is 4 meters long and makes an angle of 35° with horizontal, what is the height of the rafter's peak?

Formula:

Use the formula of trigonometric ratios to find their the height of the problem.

sin θ = opposite side/hypotenuse

Solution:

One trigonometric ratios form the SOH may be used in order to come up with height of the rafter's peak. Let x be the height of the rafter's peak.

Solve the mathematical equation.

sin θ = opposite side/hypotenusesin 35° = x/4 mx = (sin 35°)(4 m) x = (0.573576)(4 m) x = 2.29431 mx = 2.3 m

Answer:

∴ The height of the rafter's peak is 2.3 meters.
