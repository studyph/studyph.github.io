3. How can you say when a person is physically fit?


A Physically Fit Person

A physically fit person is one that has:

1) aerobic (cardiovascular) endurance, which is the ability of your heart and lungs to supply oxygen during prolonged physical activity

2) muscular endurance and strength, which is the ability to perform activity without fatigue and with the force required;  

3) a healthy body composition, which is determined by the relative amounts of body fat, muscle, bone mass, and femur mass.

A fit individual can execute daily duties with agility, power, and coordination, as well as have the stamina to enjoy leisure time and respond to unexpected events.

What are the health benefits of exercise and being physically fit?

Reduced risk of heart disease,Heart attacks, and cancer;  Better diabetes control;Enhanced bone health  Exercise is beneficial to persons who are depressed or trying to quit smokingThe elderly develop strength and are less likely to fall.

Fitness can mean a variety of things to different people.

The main point is that starting any type of regular exercise is beneficial to one's health. They will look and feel healthier if they exercise more.
