14. Titulong iginawad sa unang pangulo ng Hilagang Korea na si Kim II-sung.


Si Kim Il-sung (Abril 15, 1912 – Hulyo 8, 1994) ay isang Hilagang Koreanong komunistang manghihimagsik at politiko na nagtatag ng Hilagang Korea at namahala nito simula sa pagkatatag ng bansa noong 1948 hanggang sa kanyang pagkamatay noong 1994, kung saan siya'y sinundan ng kanyang anak na si Kim Jong-il. Siya ang naging unang kataas-taasang pinuno ng bansa. Hinawakan niya ang mga posisyong Tagapangulo (1949-1966) at Pangkalahatang Kalihim (1966-1994) ng Partido ng mga Manggagawa ng Korea, Kataas-taasang Kumander (1950-1991) ng Hukbong Bayan ng Korea, Tagapangulo (1972-1993) ng Komisyon ng Tanggulang Pambansa, at Premiyer (1948-1972) at Pangulo (1972-1994) ng bansa. Nanungkulan siya sa Hilagang Korea sa loob ng higit 45 na taon, kung saan nabuhay siya ng mas matagal sa kanyang mga kaalyado na sina Iosif Stalin ng apat na dekada at Mao Zedong ng halos dalawang dekada, at nanatili sa kapangyarihan sa panahon ng panunungkulan ng anim na pangulo ng Timog Korea at sampung pangulo ng Estados Unidos, samakatuwid ginagawa siyang ikatlong pinakamatagal na pinunong naglingkod sa estado (na hindi makahan) sa ika-20 dantaon. Inilahad niya ang pilosopiyang Juche, ang pampamahalaang ideolohiya ng Hilagang Korea na nagbibigay ng pagpapahalaga sa Koreanong pagkamakabansa, pag-asa sa sarili, at sosyalismo.

16. A critique of titanic


Titanic is a classic love story in the form of a cinematic masterpiece. Released in 1997, the film was incredibly popular and spawned a new wave of romantic films. However, while Titanic is entertaining, dramatic, and visually impressive, it is also plagued with inaccuracies, melodrama, and a problematic power dynamic.

The story of Jack and Rose is a powerful narrative, and the acting performances from Leonardo DiCaprio and Kate Winslet make the characters' love believable. Unfortunately, the script is full of inaccuracies, from the way the passengers are portrayed to the actual Titanic’s sinking. Many of the conversations and behaviors between characters are also highly unrealistic and melodramatic.

In addition to factual inaccuracies, the power dynamic between Jack and Rose is cause for critique. While their romance is endearing, Rose is elevated to a position of higher status throughout the film by virtue of her class and gender. This reinforces a problematic notion of patriarchy, where Rose’s financial security depends on her romantic relationship with Jack, and her "promise" to never let go suggests her subordination to him.

Overall, though Titanic is an entertaining and visually impressive film, its inaccuracies and implications of sexism require close critique. While its love story may be an inspiration to many, its various flaws are heavily detracting.

Mini Case Study Mr. Kim, a supermarket manager, asks Sam to submit a report, which includes a background analysis of some data and recommendations based on the interpretation of that data. Mr. Kim asks Sam to get the report together within two weeks. Off Sam goes. He delegates the research, the analysis, and report writing to one of his subordinates, Sung, who completes the work in ten days and gives the report to Sam. Sam takes Sung's work, reads it, is satisfied with the content, retypes the page to show his name, not Sung's, and then submits it to Mr. Kim as if it were his work. He accepts praise and dit to Sung, who in fact, did the Text Box research and wrote the repor Guide Questions: 1. What is your reaction to Sam's act? 2. What do you think will be the reactions of Sung if he discovered it? How about Mr. Kim's reactions? 3. How can you apply virtue ethics in this case?


Answer:

1.objectively speaking, Sam's act of taking credit for Sung's work is unethical and dishonest.

2. If Sung discovers that Sam took credit for his work, he may feel disrespected, unappreciated, and betrayed. He may also lose trust in Sam and their working relationship may be affected. On the other hand, if Mr. Kim finds out, he may lose trust in Sam's abilities to manage and lead his team. He may also question Sam's integrity and honesty.

3. Virtue ethics emphasizes the importance of character and moral virtues in making ethical decisions. In this case, Sam's act of taking credit for Sung's work goes against virtues such as honesty, integrity, and fairness. Applying virtue ethics, Sam should have acknowledged Sung's contribution and given him credit for his work. He should have acted with honesty and fairness towards Sung and Mr. Kim.
