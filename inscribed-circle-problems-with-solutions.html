3. Theorem on Inscribed angles 5 problems with solutions​


Answer:

Theorem on Inscribed Angles: If two chords of a circle intersect at a point within the circle, then the measure of each angle formed by the chords is equal to half the measure of the intercepted arc.

27. Theorem on Inscribed angles 5 problems with solutions ​


1. If two chords AB and CD intersect within a circle at point E, then the measure of the inscribed angle AEB is half the measure of the central angle AEC.

Solution: Let m∠AEC = 2x. Then, m∠AEB = x, because the measure of an inscribed angle is half the measure of the central angle that intercepts it.

2. If an inscribed angle intercepts an arc of a circle, then the measure of the inscribed angle is half the measure of the arc that it intercepts.

Solution: Let m∠AEB = x and m∠ABE = y. Then, m∠AEB + m∠ABE = m∠AEB + m∠EAB = 180°. By the Inscribed Angle Theorem, m∠AEB = m∠EAB = x = y, so m∠AEB = m∠ABE. Thus, the measure of the inscribed angle is half the measure of the arc it intercepts.

3. If an inscribed angle and its intercepted arc are congruent, then the inscribed angle is a right angle.

Solution: If m∠AEB = m∠ABE, then m∠AEB + m∠ABE = 2m∠AEB = 180°, so m∠AEB = 90°. Therefore, the inscribed angle is a right angle.

4. If an inscribed angle of a circle is a right angle, then the intercepted arc is a semicircle.

Solution: If m∠AEB = 90°, then m∠AEB + m∠ABE = 180°. By the Inscribed Angle Theorem, m∠AEB = m∠EAB = 90°, so m∠EAB + m∠ABE = 180°. Therefore, the intercepted arc is a semicircle.

5. If two chords AB and CD of a circle intersect at a point within the circle, then the measure of the intercepted arc AB is equal to the measure of the intercepted arc CD.

Solution: Let m∠AEB = x and m∠CED = y. By the Inscribed Angle Theorem, m∠AEB = m∠EAB = x and m∠CED = m∠EDC = y. Therefore, m∠EAB + m∠EDC = x + y = 180°. Since the measure of an intercepted arc is equal to the sum of the measures of the intercepted arcs of the opposite angles, m∠ABE = m∠CDE, so the measure of the intercepted arc AB is equal to the measure of the intercepted arc CD.

Since we already computed the value of the total area of the triangle and lengths of the sides are given we then can find the value of the radius of the circle. Derive a formula from this information: r = (2A) / (a+b+c) = (2*27.72) / (8+8+8) = 2.31cm. We can then determine the circle's circumference: (2)(3.14)(2.31) = 14.51cm. 30. a square is inscribed in a circle with circumference of 25m. Find the length of the diagonal of the square. With solution po sana thanks.​


Step-by-step explanation:

If a square is inscribed in a circle, then the diagonal of the square is equal to the diameter of the circle. Also, the circumference of a circle can be expressed as:

C = πd

where C is the circumference and d is the diameter.

In this problem, we are given that the circumference of the circle is 25 m. So, we can write:

25 m = πd

Solving for d, we get:

d = 25 m / π

d ≈ 7.96 m

Therefore, the length of the diagonal of the square, which is equal to the diameter of the circle, is approximately 7.96 meters.
