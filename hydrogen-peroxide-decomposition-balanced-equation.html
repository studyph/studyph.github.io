10. A solution is prepared by dissolving 90 ml of hydrogen peroxide in enough water to make 3000 ml of solution. Identify the concentration of the hydrogen peroxide solution. Given: Required Equation: Solution: Answer:


Answer:

Given:

A solution is prepared by dissolving 90 mL of hydrogen peroxide in enough water to make 3000 mL of solution.

To find:

Identify the concentration of the hydrogen peroxide solution.

Solution:

We use the formula, Volume percent = Volume of solute / Volume of solution × 100 %

From given, we have the parameters,  

The volume of solute is 90 mL

The volume of solution is 3000 mL

Substitute the values in the given formula,

Volume percent = Volume of solute / Volume of solution × 100 %

= 90 mL / 3000mL × 100 %

∴ The volume percent = 3 %

Solution:

We can obtain the mass of hydrogen peroxide by just simply multiplying the percentage to the total mass of the solution. So:

mass of H2O2 = 100 x 3% = 100 x 0.03 = 3 grams

Therefore, the mass of hydrogen peroxide in the solution is 3 grams.
