7. The volume of a gas is 300 mL at 740 torr and 25°C. If the pressure remains constant and the temperature is raised to 100°C, the new volume will be ______ a. 75.0 mL b. 240 mL c. 376 mL d. 1.20 L


ANSWER: C. 376 mL

We'll use the Combined Gas Law, which is:
[tex] \frac{pressure1(volume1)}{temperature1} = \frac{pressure2(volume2)}{temperature2} [/tex]
We also need to convert the terms to the standard units:

300 mL = 0.3L
740 torr ÷ 760 = 0.97 atm
25C + 273 = 298K
100C + 273 = 373K

[tex] \frac{0.97 (0.3)}{298} = \frac{0.97(x)}{373} \\ \frac{0.97(0.3)(373)}{298(0.97)} = 0.376[/tex]
The answer is 0.376 L or 376 mL.

We have the following information:  

V1 (initial volume) = 300 mL → 0.3 L

V2 (final volume) = ?

T1 (initial temperature) = 25ºC (in Kelvin)  

TK = TC + 273.15 → TK = 25 + 273.15 → TK = 298.15

T1 (initial temperature) = 298.15 K

T2 (final temperature) = 100º C (in Kelvin)  

TK = TC + 273.15 → TK = 100 + 273.15 → TK = 373.15

T2 (final temperature) = 373.15 K

*** We have an isobaric transformation, that is, when a certain mass under pressure maintains its constant pressure, on the other hand, as we increase the temperature, the volume increases and if we lower the temperature, the volume decreases.

P1 = P2 (pressure remains constant)

We apply the data to the formula of isobaric transformation (Gay-Lussac), let us see:

[tex]\dfrac{V_1}{T_1} =\dfrac{V_2}{T_2}[/tex]

[tex]\dfrac{0.3}{298.15} =\dfrac{V_2}{373.15}[/tex]

multiply the means by the extremes

[tex]298.15*V_2 = 0.3*373.15[/tex]

[tex]298.15\:V_2 = 111.945[/tex]

[tex]V_2 = \dfrac{111.945}{298.15}[/tex]

[tex]V_2 = 0.37546...[/tex]

[tex]V_2 \approx 0.376\:L\to\:\boxed{\boxed{V_2 \approx 376\:mL}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]
