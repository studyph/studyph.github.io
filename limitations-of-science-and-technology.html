Answer:

1.) In Science, limits and continuity are important because they allow us to make predictions about physical phenomena by understanding how variables behave as they approach certain values. For example, the concept of limits is used in the study of rates of change, which is crucial in physics and chemistry.

2.) In Technology, limits and continuity are important because they enable us to design and optimize systems with complex behaviors. Understanding the limits of a system helps us identify potential problems and develop solutions, while continuity helps ensure that the system behaves smoothly and predictably.

3.) In Engineering, limits and continuity are important because they form the foundation of mathematical modeling, which is essential in designing and optimizing systems. Engineers use limits and continuity to analyze the behavior of complex systems and make predictions about their performance.

4.) In Mathematics, limits and continuity are important because they are fundamental concepts that underlie much of calculus and analysis.
