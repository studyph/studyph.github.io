22. A bicycle tire has a radius of 12 cm. What is the circumference of the tire? ​


Answer:

[tex]24\pi \text{ cm}[/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

Recall that the formula for the circumference C of a circle with radius r is given by

[tex]C=2\pi r[/tex].

Now, given that r=12cm, then the circumference is equal to [tex]C=2\pi r=2\pi (12)=24\pi \text{ cm}[/tex].

Therefore, the circumference of the circle with radius 12 cm is equal to  [tex]24\pi[/tex] cm.

Circumference of a circle

28. a 25 kg boy is riding a 12-kg bicycle moving at 4.5 m/s. what is the total momentum of the boy and the bicycle together? asked: given: solution: final answer: ???


Answer:

The total momentum of the boy and the bicycle together is 166.5 kg·m/s.

Explanation:

Momentum is referred to as the "mass in motion". It is defined to be the quantity of motion that an object contains. Momentum is directly proportional to the velocity of the object and mass of that certain object.

The mathematical expression of momentum is:

p = mv

where

p sands for momentum,m stands for mass, and v stands for velocity

From the problem, we have 2 objects, the 25 kg boy and the 12 kg bicycle. Of course the boy will be riding the bicycle for it to move. It is also asked in the problem the total momentum of the boy and the bicycle. That is why from here, we know that we need to add the mass of the boy and bicycle for us to obtain only one value for the m.

Asked:Momentum (p)Given:m₁ (mass of the boy) = 25 kgm₂ (mass of the bicycle) = 12 kgv = 4.5 m/sSolution:p = mvm = m₁ + m₂m = 25 kg + 12 kgm = 37 kgp = (37 kg) * (4.5 m/s)p = 166.5 kg·m/s

Final Answer:

The total momentum of the boy and the bicycle together is 166.5 kg·m/s.
