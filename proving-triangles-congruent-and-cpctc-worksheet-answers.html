What is CPCTC?

The geometric theorem known as CPCTC, or Corresponding Parts of Congruent Triangles are Congruent, states that if two or more triangles are congruent, then their corresponding angles and sides must also be congruent. According to the CPCTC theorem, if two or more triangles are similar in shape, then their corresponding angles and sides are similarly similar in shape.

In a triangle, there are three sides as well as three angles. For the two triangles to be congruent, they must be the same size and shape. It should be possible to superimpose the two triangles under examination. All three sides and angles of a triangle must coincide for two triangles to be congruent.

Two triangles' equivalent portions are those of the triangles that are situated in a similar relative position to one another. They are in the same place or space if the three sides of one triangle match those of another. When two triangles' three angles are in the same region as one another, the two triangles are said to have corresponding angles.
