18. if you push a 20-kilo gram mass with a force of 40 N, What will be The Object's Acceleration A. 40 m/s²B. 20 m/s²C. 10 m/s²D. 2 m/s²​


Answer:

I think it is D.2m/s^2....

Answer:

The answer is D, which is

[tex]2 \frac{m}{{s}^{2} } [/tex]

Explanation:

Recall that the formula for force is F = ma, where F is the force exerted, m is the mass of the object, and a is the acceleration of the object.

We can get the formula for the acceleration by deriving the formula, which gives us equation: a = F / m

Using the formula, we get

[tex]a = \frac{40}{20} = 2 \frac{m}{ {s}^{2} } [/tex]

Note: In your solution, it is better to include the units. In my case I was not able to include the units of the force and the units of the mass since the formula feature does not allow me to insert capital letters.
