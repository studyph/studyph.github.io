3. A rectangle has a perimeter of 60m. What could be the maximum area of the rectangle?​


• QUESTION : "A rectangle has a perimeter of 60m.

What could be the maximum area of the rectangle?"

• STEP-BY-STEP EXPLANATION/S :

- "To begin, we know that the perimeter P=2L+2W, where L is the Length and W is the Width.

Then, P=2L+2W. Since P=60, we have 60=2L+2W.

We know that Area A=LW. Solve 60=2L+2W for L to get L=(60-2W)/2=30-W.

Substituting back into our Area equation, we have A=(30-W)(W)=30W-W2.

Taking the derivative of each side, we get dA=30-2W. The Area function is a parabola that opens down.

To find the place at which we have maximum Area, we want to find the maximum of the parabola which will occur when the derivative is 0 (The tangent line at the maximum point has slope 0). So, we will set 30-2W=0 to find the critical points.

Solving for W, we get W=15. So,  the Width is 15, when the Area is maximized.

We can easily find L using 60=2L+2W. Plugging in W=15 we get 60=2L+2(15), which simplifies to L=15.

Thus, the Area A=15*15=225."

4. determine the dimensions of a rectangle whose perimeter is 300 m with a maximum area..(sys)​


Answer:

length = 75

width = 75

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x and y be the length and width of the rectangle respectively.

Given that the perimeter is 300 m, so

[tex]2(x+y) = 300[/tex]

Dividing both sides by 2 yields

[tex]\begin{aligned}x + y = 150\\x = 150-y\end{aligned}[/tex]

The area of the rectangle is

[tex]A = xy\\A = (150-y)y\\A = 150y-y^2[/tex]

Since the area is maximum, the derivative of the area of the rectangle is equal to zero.

[tex]\frac{\partial}{\partial y}(150y-y^2) = 0[/tex]

This can be written as:

[tex]\frac{\partial}{\partial y}(150y) - \frac{\partial}{\partial y}(y^2) = 0[/tex]

Take the constant out

[tex]\begin{aligned}(150)(\frac{\partial}{\partial y}y) - \frac{\partial}{\partial y}(y^2) = 0\\150 - \frac{\partial}{\partial y}(y^2) = 0\\150 - 2y^{2-1} = 0\\150 - 2y = 0\\150 = 2y\\\boxed{y = 75} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the dimensions of the rectangle is:

length = 150 - 75 = 75

width = 75

Note: The rectangle turns out to be a square, and by definition, a square is a special type of rectangle.

21. Find the dimensions of a rectangle with perimeter 1000 meters so that the area of the rectangle is a maximum.


Step-by-step explanation:

Example

Find the dimensions of a rectangle with perimeter 1000 metres so that the area of the rectangle is a maximum.

Solution

Let the length of the rectangle be x m, the width be y m, and the area be A m2.

Rectangle with sides x and y.

The perimeter of the rectangle is 1000 metres. So

1000=2x+2y,

and hence

y=500−x.

The area is given by A=xy. Thus

A(x)=x(500−x)=500x−x2.(1)

Because x and y are lengths, we must have 0≤x≤500.

The problem now reduces to finding the value of x in [0,500] for which A is a maximum. Since A is differentiable, the maximum must occur at an endpoint or a stationary point.

From (1), we have

dAdx=500−2x.

Setting dAdx=0 gives x=250.

Hence, the possible values for A to be a maximum are x=0, x=250 and x=500. Since A(0)=A(500)=0, the maximum value of A occurs when x=250.

The rectangle is a square with side lengths 250 metres. The maximum area is 62 500 square metres.

Notes.

dAdx>0, for 0≤x<250, and dAdx<0, for 250<x≤500. Hence, there is a local maximum at x=250.

d2Adx2=−2<0. This is a second way to see that x=250 is a local maximum.

The graph of A(x)=500x−x2 is a parabola with a negative coefficient of x2 and a turning point at x=250. This is a third way of establishing the local maximum.

It is worth looking at the graph of A(x) against x.

30. A rectangle has a perimeter of 40m. What could be the maximum area of the rectangle ?​


Problem:A rectangle has a perimeter of 40m. What could be the maximum area of the rectangle ?​Solution:

P = 40m

P = 2(L + W)

40 = 2(L + W)

20 = L + W

L = 20 - W

Area = LW

A = (20 - W)(W)

A = 20W - W^2

dA/dW = 20 - 2W = 0

20 = 2W

W = 10

if W = 10

L = 20 - W

L = 20 - 10

L = 10

Area = LW

Area = 10(10)

Max Area = 100 sqm

Notice, since the problem was asking for MAXIMUM area, then it turns out it is a square. We know that a square is a parallelogram with all sides that are equal - equal in length and equal in angle (right angle). This case is called as specialized rectangle.

Answer:The maximum area = 100 sqm
