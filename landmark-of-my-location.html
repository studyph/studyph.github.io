Landmark

1. Statue of Liberty

The Statue of Liberty, also known as the Statue of Liberty, is a monumental neoclassical statue on Liberty Island in New York Harbor, New York City.

The Statue of Liberty is a symbol of victory for the United States. Can be erected on October 28, 1886. This Statue of Liberty has the meaning that the American people have democracy and freedom.

2. Eiffel Tower

The Eiffel Tower is an iron tower built on the Champ de Mars on the banks of the River Seine, Paris. The tower has become a global icon of France and one of the most famous structures in the world.

The Eiffel Tower was built in conjunction with the World's Fair and also the celebration of the French revolution. This beautiful tower was inaugurated on March 31, 1889.

3. Big Ben

Big Ben is the name given to the large bell on the clock tower at the Palace of Westminster, City of London, England.

In 2012, this clock tower got its official name, namely Elizabeth Tower. This clock tower is a symbol of the British Empire. Construction of Big Ben was completed in 1858 and he celebrated his 160th birthday on May 31, 2019.

4. St. Peter's Basilica

St. Peter's is one of the most sacred Catholic shrines in the world, located in Vatican City west of the Tiber River and near Janiculum Hill and Hadrian's Mausoleum.

St. Peter's Basilica Located on Vatican Hill in the UNESCO World Heritage site of Vatican City, St. Peter's Basilica dominates Rome's skyline and attracts millions of visitors from all over the world.

5. Stonehenge

Stonehenge is a prehistoric monument in Wiltshire, England, 3 km west of Amesbury.

Stonehenge consists of standing stones, each about 4m high, seven 2.1m wide, and weighing about 25 tons that form a circular position resembling a ring. According to archaeological data, Stonehenge was built from 3000 BC to 2000 BC.

6. The Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal is a magnificent building with a predominantly white color that symbolizes holiness. It was erected in the Uttar Paradesh region, on the banks of the Yamuna river in Agra, India.

As one of the cultural heritages of Indian society, the Taj Mahal has always had a place in the hearts of the people. This is evidenced by the effort to protect the Taj Mahal from the dangers of war against British colonial rule in the colonial period.

7. Buckingham Palace

Buckingham Palace is the official residence of the British royal family. Buckingham Palace is located in the city of London, England.

Buckingham Palace is one of the most popular buildings and is visited by no less than 50,000 tourists per year to attend dinners, lunches, receptions and official royal family parties.

8. Pompeii

Pompeii is the name of an ancient city relic of the Roman Empire located in Campania, Italy, 23 kilometers southeast of Naples.

The city of Pompeii was destroyed by the massive eruption of Mount Vesuvius in 79 AD. Volcanic ash from Mount Vesuvius covered the City of Pompeii with everything in it to a depth of several feet, causing the city to disappear for 1600 years.

9. San Juanico Bridge

The San Juanico Bridge is an arch truss-shaped bridge in the Philippines spanning from Samar to Leyte crossing the San Juanico Strait. It is part of the Pan-Philippine Highway. It is the longest bridge in the Philippines spanning a body of water with a length of 2.16 kilometers (1.34 miles).

The San Juanico Bridge It is considered one of the most beautiful designed bridges in the Philippines. The bridge is supported by 43 spans rising 41 meters above the sea. The bridge has a large arch below which allows boats to enter.

10. Borobudur Temple

Borobudur Temple is a temple located in Borobudur Village, Borobudur District, Magelang Regency, Central Java Province.

Borobudur Temple is known to be magnificent, it is estimated that its construction requires two million pieces of stone. The shape of the temple building is in the form of nine terraces and there is a main stupa at the top. There are six terraces with a square plan, and three terraces with a circular plan.
