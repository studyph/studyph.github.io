1. An army recruit is on a training exercise and instructed to walk due west for 8 km, then in a north-easterly direction for 4 mi, and finally due north for 15,840 ft. How far will he be from where he started?​


Answer:

He will be 10 km far away from where he started.

Explanation:

In this problem, we will use the concept of resultant displacement but we will be getting the magnitude only.

Formula to use

R = [tex]\sqrt{(\Sigma x)^2+(\Sigma y)^2}[/tex]

where

R     is the resultant distance

∑x   is the sum of all horizontal distances

∑y   is the sum of all vertical distances

Given information

d₁ = 8 km (west)

d₂ = 4 mi (northeast)

d₃ = 15,840 ft (north)

R = ?

Solving the problem

First, let us assume that all the distances are in kilometers (km). Therefore, let us convert the remaining two distances to a kilometer.

d₂ = 4 mi = 6.44 km

d₃ = 15,840 ft = 4.83 km

Now, let us solve the sum of all horizontal distances

∑x = - 8 + 6.44 cos 45°

∑x = - 8 + 4.55

∑x = - 3.45 km

Here, we have:

north-easterly has an angle in between north and east direction which is 45 degrees. west as negative because it goes in the left direction

Now, let us solve the sum of all vertical distances.

∑y = 6.44 sin 45° + 4.83 km

∑y = 4.55 km + 4.83 km

∑y = 9.38 km

Finally, we are ready to solve the resultant distance. Let us use the given formula

R = [tex]\sqrt{(\Sigma x)^2+(\Sigma y)^2}[/tex]

Then, substitute the given data

R = [tex]\sqrt{(-3.45km)^2+(9.38km)^2}[/tex]

R = 9.99 km ≅ 10 km

Therefore, the distance traveled by the army recruit from where he started is almost 10 km.

The given problem is "A rectangular garden is 30 ft by 40 ft long. A covered walk with a uniform width that surrounds it has an area of 296 sq. ft. Find the width of the covered walk".

Step-by-step Solution and Discussion

How to find the area of a rectangle? Area of a rectangle is the space occupied by the shape express in square units. Finding area is the of the product of length and width (A = lw).

Step 1: Identify all the given in the problem

rectangular garden is 30 ft by 40 ft

an area of 296 sq. ft, covered walk

Step 2: Identify what is being ask

Find the width of the covered walk.

Step 3: Make a mathematical equation

let x for the width of the covered walk

A = 296 ft² → area of covered walk

→ 296 = [4(x)² + 2(l-2x)(x) + 2(w-2x)(x)]

Step 4: Solve for x

296 = [4(x)² + 2(l-2x)(x) + 2(w-2x)(x)]

when l = 40 and w = 30

296 = 4(x²) + 2(40-2x)(x) + 2(30-2x)(x)

296 = 4x² + 80x - 4x² + 60x - 4x²

296 = 80x - 4x² + 60x

4x² - 140x + 296 = 0

4x² - 140x + 296 = 0 solve for x using Quadratic Equation ½[-b±√(b²-4ac)], the the value of x is 2.26 ft.

Step 5: Checking

When x = 2.26 ft

Area of covered walk = 296

296 = [4(2.26)² + 2(40-2(2.26)(2.26) + 2(30-2(2.26)(2.26)]

296 = [20.4304 + 160.3696 + 115.1696]

296 = 295.9696

296 = 296 ft² ✓

8. light is hung 15 ft above a straight horizontal path. If a man 72 inches tall is walking away from the light at a rate of 5 ft/sec, how fast is his shadow lengthening?


The answer is 25/3 ft/sec or 8.33 ft/sec.

To compute:

Draw the figure with light 15 ft. above the horizontal, man who is "y" meters away from the vertical axis of light, tip of the shadow "x" meters away from the vertical axis of light, 5ft/sec from the light to the man.

dx/dt = the rate at which the tip of the shadow is moving.
dy/dt  5 ft/sec
Similar triangles, we will get
(15/x) = (6/x-y)
x = (5/3)y

Therefore, dx/dt = (dx/dy)*(dy/dt)= (5/3)(5) = 25/3 ft/sec or 8.33 ft/sec.

16. 6. An airline passenger walking between two airline terminals and decided to get a moving sidewalk that is 200 ft long. If the passenger walks at the rate of 3 ft/s and the moving sidewalk moves at a rate of 7 ft/s, how long, in seconds, will it take the passenger to walk from one end of the moving sidewalk to the other?​


Answer:

The passenger's speed relative to the moving sidewalk is the sum of their individual speeds, so:

speed = passenger's walking speed + moving sidewalk speed

speed = 3 ft/s + 7 ft/s

speed = 10 ft/s

To find the time it takes for the passenger to walk the length of the moving sidewalk, we can use the formula:

time = distance / speed

In this case, the distance is 200 ft, so:

time = 200 ft / 10 ft/s

time = 20 seconds

Therefore, it will take the passenger 20 seconds to walk from one end of the moving sidewalk to the other.

To find the combined area of the garden and the walk, we need to find the dimensions of the largest rectangle that contains them.

To get this, we need to add the width of the walk to the dimensions of the garden.
The new dimensions would be
35 + 2(x+2) ft    by    25 + 2(x+2) ft

Note that we need to add twice the width of the walk since it is around the garden. (top and bottom, left and right)
