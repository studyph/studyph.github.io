1. Why did Mary I Executed Lady Jane Grey?​


Answer:

pabrainties ako ha

Explanation:

Mary I executed Lady Jane Grey because she saw her as a threat to her claim to the throne. Lady Jane Grey was placed on the throne by Protestant nobles after the death of King Edward VI, but Mary I, a Catholic, had a stronger claim to the throne as the daughter of King Henry VIII. Mary I saw Lady Jane Grey's claim as illegitimate and believed that her execution would strengthen her own position as the rightful queen. Additionally, Lady Jane Grey's father had participated in a rebellion against Mary I, further fueling her desire to eliminate any potential threats to her power.
