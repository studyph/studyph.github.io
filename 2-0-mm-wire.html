6. what is the resistance of a 30 m silver wire with 2 mm diameter?​


Answer:

Ok, is this your homework? Just 2 very simple formulae, which you should know, then plugging in some numbers & calculating the result.

So, bear with me…

Resistance = rho x l/a …(p)

Where l is length in meters, a is area in square meters & rho is resistivity in ohm meters.

Resistivity is a bulk property of the material, and for silver, at 20 degrees C, is given in tables as 1.59 exp -8 [1.59 times 10 to the minus 8th power]

So, for formula (p), we know rho, as above; length is 2m, R is 0.5 ohm, and we want to find area, from which we can calculate diameter.

Rearranging (p), we get a = (rho x l)/R

So a = (1.59 exp -8 x 2)/0.5

Multiplying top & bottom of the fraction by 2 gives

a = 6.36 exp -8

So the cross sectional Area of the wire is 6.36 exp -8 square meters.

Since the question asks for a diameter, we assume the wire is circular in section. You should know that the are of a circle is given by Pi x radius squared…

A = Pi x r^2 (q)

Rearranging (q) to find radius gives

r = square root of (A/Pi)

So the radius of the wire is SQRT (6.36 exp -8/Pi) which is SQRT of approx 2.0445 exp -8

This calculates to approx 1.42283 exp -4

The diameter is of course twice the radius, so approx 2.84566 exp -4

Let's say about 2.85 exp -4 meters

This is 0.285 millimeters

So rather more than human hair thickness. It is roughly equivalent to 29 AWG wire.

Got it?

14. The electrical resistance of a wire varies directly as its length and inversely as the square of its diameter. If a wire 30 meters long and 0.75 mm in diameter has a resistance of 25 ohms, find the length of a wire of the same material whose resistance and diameter are 30 ohms and 1.25 mm, respectively.


Answer:

The length of the wire is 100 meters.

Step-by-step explanation:

The working equation or the formula that we are going to use to solve this problem is stated in the first sentence of the problem.

Let Ω be the electrical resistanceLet L be the length of the wirelet d be the diameter of the wireFormula:

The phrase "varies directly" means that the variation between two variables is constant. In mathematical expression, it is y = kx (y and x are the variables and k is the constant)

On the other hand, the phrase "varies inversely" means that when one variable increases then the other variable decreases by the same factor. In mathematical expression, it is y = [tex]\frac{k}{x}[/tex] (y and x are the variables and k is the constant or the factor)

So if the electrical resistance of the wire varies directly with the length and varies inversely with the square of its diameter, the equation will be:

Ω = [tex]\frac{(k)*(L)}{d^2}[/tex]   →  working equation / formula

Given:L₁ = 30 md₁ = 0.75 mmΩ₁ = 25 ohmsΩ₂ = 30 ohmsd₂ = 1.25 mm

Solution:

Solve for k first using the initial dimensions and resistance given.

Ω = [tex]\frac{(k)*(L)}{d^2}[/tex]25 = [tex]\frac{(k)*(30 )}{(0.75)^2}[/tex]k = [(25)*(0.75)²] ÷ 30k = 0.46875

Solve for new length of wire using the computed k above.

Ω = [tex]\frac{(k)*(L)}{d^2}[/tex]30 = [tex]\frac{(0.46875)*(L)}{(1.25)^2}[/tex]L = [(30)*(1.25)²] ÷ 0.46875L = 100

Therefore, the length of the wire is 100 m.

19. The resistance of a wire varies inversely as its cross-sectional area. Which has a higher resistance: thick wire or thin wire? The resistance of a 2.5 mm radius wire is 5.62 ohms. What is the resistance of a wire of the same material whose radius is 4.25 mm?


[tex]R = \frac{k}{A} [/tex]      , where R=resistance , A=area, k=constant of variation

A thick wire would have less resistance than a thin wire.

Manipulating the equation will give us [tex]k=RA[/tex]

Since [tex]A=\pi r^2[/tex]

[tex]k=R\pi r^2 [/tex]


Given: R₁ = 5.62 Ω    r₁=2.5mm
           R₂ = ?           r₂=4.25mm


[tex]R_1\pi (r_1)^2=R_2\pi (r_2)^2[/tex]

[tex]R_2 =\frac{R_1\pi (r_1)^2}{\pi (r_2)^2}[/tex]

[tex]R_2 =\frac{R_1(r_1)^2}{(r_2)^2}[/tex]

[tex]R_2 =\frac{(5.62)(2.5)^2}{(4.25)^2}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{R_2=1.94ohms}[/tex]
