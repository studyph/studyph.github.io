Science (from Latin scientia, meaning "knowledge") is a systematic enterprise that builds and organizes knowledge in the form of testable explanations and predictions about the universe.

What is important is to cultivate the scientific methodology: how we know what we know and how to improve upon it.

If School Science is force-fed to kids as volumes of “unalterable facts” for memorization, or the teachers are not engaging in order to fuel the natural curiosity of their pupils to seek Science outside of the school books and the mundane assignments, it will be harder to inspire the precious 5–10% of the people that could actually engage Science in their professional careers, or the 1% that could actually seriously help with the next “breakthrough”.

All kids need to be exposed to scientific thinking. You cannot expect that the “right” minds will be self-interested and pursue it by themselves, or that the parents that are “proud of their little genius” and push to that direction are correct or even do it in a proper way.
