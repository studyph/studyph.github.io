7. What are the advantages and disadvantages of extemporaneous speech


The advantages of extemporaneous speech
- any good, well-versed speaker will find that this type of speech will come naturally
- the flexibility of this speech will allow for a joke, anecdote, short story, or even audience interactions
- a speaker who is familiar with the topic will be able to speak with passion, appear and sound lively, and will be able to demonstrate his expertise

The disadvantages of extemporaneous speech
- it is not for everybody, anyone who has a fear of public speaking might not do well
- if not planned properly, it can end up being too long or too short
- a person with no extensive vocabulary might end up at a loss for words and terms

9. what are the different advantages and disadvantages of types of speech delivery?​


Answer:

Advantages of Types of Speech Delivery:

1. Memorized Delivery: This type of delivery allows the speaker to deliver a speech without having to rely on notes or prompts. It also allows the speaker to focus on their delivery and body language, as they are not distracted by having to read from a script.

2. Impromptu Delivery: This type of delivery allows the speaker to be spontaneous and creative in their speech, as they do not have to rely on notes or prompts. It also allows the speaker to think quickly and respond to questions or comments from the audience in real time.

3. Manuscript Delivery: This type of delivery allows the speaker to deliver a speech that is well-rehearsed and polished, as they have had time to practice and perfect their words before delivering them. It also allows for greater control over the content of the speech, as it can be edited before being presented.

Disadvantages of Types of Speech Delivery:

1. Memorized Delivery: This type of delivery can be difficult for some speakers, as it requires memorization and practice in order for it to be effective. It can also lead to monotony if not delivered with enthusiasm or emotion.

2. Impromptu Delivery: This type of delivery can be difficult for some speakers, as it requires quick thinking and improvisation in order for it to be effective. It can also lead to mistakes if not done properly, which could detract from the overall message being conveyed by the speaker.

3. Manuscript Delivery: This type of delivery can be difficult for some speakers, as it requires a great deal of preparation and practice in order for it to be effective. It can also lead to a lack of spontaneity if not delivered with enthusiasm or emotion
