Answer:

Edmund's first meeting with the Queen of Narnia in C.S. Lewis's book "The Lion, the Witch, and the Wardrobe" is different from Lucy's meeting with Mr. Tumnus in several ways. In Edmund's meeting, he encounters the Queen alone, while Lucy meets with Mr. Tumnus in the forest. Additionally, while Mr. Tumnus is friendly and kind to Lucy, the Queen is initially distant and cold towards Edmund.

The Queen who is actually the White Witch, lures Edmund with promises of power and control, which is quite different than Mr. Tumnus, who cannot resist sharing his world with Lucy. Moreover, while Lucy's meeting with Mr. Tumnus was very pleasant and informative, Edmund's meeting with the Queen foreshadows his later deception, as she wishes to use Edmund for her own purposes. Therefore, the two encounters represent a marked contrast in terms of their significance and outcomes.

13. Queen Victoria died after only ten years of being a queen.


The statement is wrong or false. Queen Victoria (Alexandrina Victoria) was Queen of the United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland from June 20, 1837 until her death on January 22, 1901. She was crowned queen after his uncle, William IV, died at the age of 71. Victoria was only 18 at that time. She had reigned for 63 years and seven months, which is longer than any of her predecessors. Her reign was known as the Victorian Era. It was a period of industrial cultural, political, and military change within the United Kingdom.

The British Empire celebrated Queen Victoria’s Golden Jubilee in 1887. It marked the fiftieth anniversary of her accession on June 20 with a banquet to which 50 kings and princes were invited. On September 23, 1896, she has surpassed her grandfather George III as the longest-reigning monarch in British History. The queen requested that any special celebrations be delayed until 1897, to coincide with her Diamond Jubilee.
