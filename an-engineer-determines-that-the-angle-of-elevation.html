21. An airplane takes off 200 meters in front of a 70-meter building. Is a 18.3° angle of elevation already enough for the plane to take off and not crash into the building? Assume that the airplane flies in a straight line and the angle of elevation remains constant until the airplane flies over the building. a. No, a higher angle of elevation is needed. b. Yes, a 18.3° angle of elevation is the minimimum the plane requires. c. No, a lower angle of elevation is needed. d. Yes, a 18.3° angle of elevation is more than enough.


b. Yes, a 18.3° angle of elevation is the minimimum the plane requires.

To see why, we can use trigonometry. Let x be the horizontal distance the plane travels before it clears the building. Then we have:

tan(18.3°) = 70 / (200 + x)

Solving for x, we get:

x = (70 / tan(18.3°)) - 200

Plugging in the given angle of elevation, we get:

x ≈ 179 meters

So as long as the plane travels at least 179 meters horizontally before it clears the building, it will not crash. Since the plane takes off 200 meters in front of the building, a 18.3° angle of elevation is sufficient.
