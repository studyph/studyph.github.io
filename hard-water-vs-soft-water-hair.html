Hard copy refers to the digital document file which is printed on paper or other material like transparency. In hard copy the output is printed on the paper and somethings it is referred as permanent copy. We can touch the hard copy. We can say it is a physical copy.

9. hard work vs smart work essay English ​


In the modern world, ?hard work? And ?smart work? It is two terms commonly used to describe the approach to work. While both are considered valuable, they have clear differences and benefits. In this essay, we will explore the meaning of hard work and smart work, their differences, and the benefits of each.   Hard work refers to putting in a lot of effort and time to achieve a goal. It is often associated with perseverance and dedication, and it is widely believed that success comes through hard work. Those who subscribe to this philosophy believe that the only way to succeed is to work tirelessly and overcome any obstacles that come your way. This approach often involves working long hours, sacrificing personal time, and focusing solely on the task at hand.   On the other hand, smart work refers to an approach that aims to work efficiently and effectively to achieve the desired result. Instead of putting in long hours, intelligent employees prioritize their tasks and focus on the most important ones first. This approach often involves strategic planning, using resources effectively, and working with others to achieve the desired outcome. Smart employees often prioritize their work based on urgency and importance, and  use their time and  resources wisely to get the job done.   The main difference between hard work and smart work is the focus on effort versus efficiency. Hard workers focus on trying as hard as possible, while smart workers focus on working efficiently to get the best results. Hard work often means working more than necessary, while smart work means working smarter, not harder.   Both approaches have their advantages. Hard work can lead to a sense of accomplishment and personal satisfaction, and  can help people develop important skills like perseverance and determination. On the other hand, working smart  can help people achieve their goals faster and more  efficiently, which can help them  achieve  better work-life balance and maintain better mental health.   In summary, the choice between working hard  and working smart  ultimately comes down to individual goals and preferences. Those who prefer to work tirelessly and overcome obstacles may prefer hard work, while those who value efficiency and work-life balance may prefer working smart. Ultimately, both approaches can lead to success, and it is up to you to decide which one works best for you.

13. how does an effect of the manner of blowing (blowing soft vs blowing hard) differ? ​


Answer:

Observing the differences between bubbles

If you use a straw to blow air into a glass of water, you can make bubbles. The bubbles tend to be small, and they disappear quickly. Blowing bubbles in juice or a soft drink gets the same result – lots of little bubbles that don’t last long.

What happens if you blow through a straw into a cup of soapy water? The bubbles last much longer. Often, there are so many bubbles that they overflow the cup.

Another way to blow bubbles is with a bubble wand. These bubbles have a different shape to bubbles blown into liquids. Have you noticed that this type of bubble, whether big or small, is always round? Even huge bubbles formed by enormous bubble wands ‘wiggle’ until they form a sphere – unless they pop first.

Water’s strange property – surface tension

Imagine a glass of water is sitting on your desk. Unless someone bumps it, the water looks perfectly still. But if you could see the individual water molecules you would see something very different. Water molecules are in constant motion. The molecules touch each other, but they easily roll and slide past one another. The molecules are not rigidly stuck together as in a solid, but there is a very weak force that holds individual liquid molecules together.

It is difficult to determine which of the two industries - water distribution utilities or sugar producers - is "higher" in terms of business, as both industries play important roles in the economy and have their own unique challenges and opportunities.

Water distribution utilities are responsible for providing clean and safe drinking water to communities and businesses. This is a critical service that is necessary for human survival, and therefore, water utilities are often regulated and held to high standards by government agencies. Water utilities may face challenges such as aging infrastructure, changing regulations, and the impacts of climate change.

Sugar producers, on the other hand, are responsible for producing sugar and other sweeteners that are used in a wide variety of food and beverage products. The sugar industry can be highly competitive, with producers facing challenges such as fluctuations in demand, changing consumer preferences, and fluctuations in the cost of raw materials.
