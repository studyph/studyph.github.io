6. A 25-gram metal ball is heated 200 °C with 2330 Joules of energy. What is the specific heat of the metal?​


Answer:

The specific heat of the metal is 0.466

Step-by-step explanation:

Calorimetry is the measurement and calculation of the amounts of heat exchanged by a body or a system.

The equation that allows calculating heat exchanges is:

Q = c * m * ΔT

where Q is the heat exchanged by a body of mass m, made up of a specific heat substance c and where ΔT is the temperature variation.

In this case:

Q= 2330 J

c= ?

m= 25 g

ΔT= 200 °C

Replacing:

2330 J= c*25 g* 200 °C

Solving:

c=0.466

The specific heat of the metal is 0.466

17. A 27 g of metal is heated from 25°C to 50°C. How much heat in Joules was added to the metal?​


Answer:

To determine the heat added to the metal, we need to use the specific heat capacity of the metal.

Q = mcΔT

where Q is the heat added, m is the mass of the metal, c is its specific heat capacity, and ΔT is the change in temperature.

Let's assume that the metal is copper, which has a specific heat capacity of 0.385 J/g·°C.

Given:

m = 27 g

ΔT = 50°C - 25°C = 25°C

c = 0.385 J/g·°C

Substituting the values into the equation, we have:

Q = (27 g) x (0.385 J/g·°C) x (25°C)

Q = 260.2 J

Therefore, 260.2 Joules of heat were added to the 27 g of metal.
