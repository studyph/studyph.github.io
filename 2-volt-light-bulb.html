2. 1. Suppose we screw the 3-volt light bulb into a light bulb holder and connect the battery to thelight bulb holder with only one wire, will the bulb illuminate? Why?​


ILLUMINATING A LIGHT BULB

Suppose we screw the 3-volt light bulb into a light bulb holder and connect the battery to the light bulb holder with only one wire, will the bulb illuminate? Why?

ANSWER

No, the bulb won't illuminate.

The reason behind is that you've connected only one wire to the bulb holder. In this case, you've made an open circuit, wherein, the flow of electricity/charged particles (known as electric current) had been broken, blocked or disconnected since the path of the current goes only from the positive terminal of the battery to a light bulb.

In order to illuminate the bulb, you need a closed circuit, wherein, you have to connect one more wire that goes from a different terminal screw of the light bulb holder to the negative terminal of the battery.

NOTE: A simple electrical circuit works when the current flows from source to load, then load to source.

9. How many kw hours are consumed by 25-60 watt light bulbs burning 5 hours in a 120 volts circuit?


Answer:

To calculate the number of kilowatt-hours consumed by 25-60 watt light bulbs burning for 5 hours in a 120 volts circuit, you can use the following formula:

Kilowatt-hours = (watts * hours) / 1000

In this case, the watts can vary between 25 and 60, so you will need to calculate the kilowatt-hours consumed for both values and then add them together to get the total.

For 25 watts:

Kilowatt-hours = (25 * 5) / 1000

Kilowatt-hours = 0.125

For 60 watts:

Kilowatt-hours = (60 * 5) / 1000

Kilowatt-hours = 0.3

Therefore, the total number of kilowatt-hours consumed by the light bulbs is 0.125 + 0.3 = 0.425. This means that the light bulbs consumed 0.425 kilowatt-hours over the course of 5 hours.

Answer: 0.425 kWh.

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the number of kilowatt-hours (kWh) consumed by 25-60 watt light bulbs burning for 5 hours in a 120 volt circuit, you can use the formula:

kWh = (watts * hours) / 1000

For the 25 watt light bulb:

kWh = (25 watts * 5 hours) / 1000 = 0.125 kWh

For the 60 watt light bulb:

kWh = (60 watts * 5 hours) / 1000 = 0.3 kWh

So, in total, the 25-60 watt light bulbs will consume 0.125 + 0.3 = 0.425 kWh.
