4. what is the mass percent of 30g.of fructose dissolved in 400g. of water​


Answer:

mass percent = 6.98%

Explanation:

Solve for the mass of the solution.

mass solution = mass solute + mass solvent

mass solution = 30 g + 400 g

mass solution = 430 g

Solve for the mass percent.

mass percent = (mass solute / mass solution) × 100

mass percent = (30 g / 430 g) × 100

mass percent = 6.98%

5. what is the molality when 20.0g of sodium hydroxide is dissolved in 400g of water​


Answer:

To determine the molality of a solution, we need to use the following formula:

Molality (m) = moles of solute / mass of solvent in kg

First, we need to find the moles of sodium hydroxide (NaOH) using its molar mass:

NaOH molar mass = 23 + 16 + 1 = 40 g/mol

moles of NaOH = mass / molar mass

moles of NaOH = 20.0 g / 40 g/mol

moles of NaOH = 0.5 mol

Next, we need to convert the mass of the solvent (water) from grams to kilograms:

mass of water = 400 g

mass of water = 0.4 kg

Finally, we can use the formula to calculate the molality:

molality = moles of solute / mass of solvent in kg

molality = 0.5 mol / 0.4 kg

molality = 1.25 mol/kg

Therefore, the molality of the solution is 1.25 mol/kg.

30. what is the density of a cork block with a mass of 400g and a volume of 10 cm3?


Density is 40 g/cm³

Explanation:

Step 1: Enumerate the given values.

Given:

m = 400 g

v = 10 cm³

Step 2: Identify what is being asked.

Required:

Density

Step 3: Solve for Density

Solution:

The formula for Density:

ρ = m/v

where: ρ - density

m - mass

v - volume

Substituting the value of m and v in the formula,

ρ = (400 g)/(10 cm³)

ρ = 40 g/cm³
