1. equation d = kf/g2 ? (Where k is the constant of variation)​


Answer:

The equation is d = kf/g^2, where d is the distance between the object and the lens, f is the focal length, g is the distance between the lens and the image, and k is a constant of variation.

Step-by-step explanation:

The equation d = kf/g^2 is known as the inverse square law. It states that the intensity of a physical phenomenon, such as light or sound, decreases in proportion to the square of the distance from the source. The constant of variation, k, is a value that represents the proportionality between the intensity of the phenomenon and the distance from the source. In this equation, d represents the distance from the source, f represents the intensity of the phenomenon at a certain distance, and g is the acceleration due to gravity. It is commonly used in physics and engineering to describe the behavior of light, sound, and other forms of energy.

20. calculate the molality of solution 13.5 g KF dissloved in 250 g of water​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & mass_{\text{solute}} = \text{13.5 g} \\ & MM_{\text{solute}} = \text{58.096 g/mol} \\ & mass_{\text{solvent}} = \text{250 g = 0.250 kg} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{mass_{\text{solute}}}{MM_{\text{solute}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{13.5 g}}{\text{58.096 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.23237 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molality of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} m & = \frac{n_{\text{solute}}}{mass_{\text{solvent}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.23237 mol}}{\text{0.250 kg}} \\ & = \text{0.929 mol/kg} \\ & = \boxed{0.929 \: m} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molality of the solution is 0.929 m.

30. A solution that contains 4.32 g of naphthalene, C10H8, in 150. g of ethylene dibromide freezes at 7.13oC. The normal freezing point of ethylene dibromide is 9.79oC. What is the freezing point constant, Kf for ethylene dibromide?​


Answer:

kf = 11.8 °C/molal

Explanation:

Calculating for freezing point depression

∆Tf = FP solvent - FP solution

∆Tf = 9.79°C - 7.13°C

∆Tf = 2.66°C

Calculating the number of moles of C10H8

n = 4.32 g × (1 mol / 128.2 g) = 0.0337 mol

Calculating the molality of the solution

molality = n solute / kg solvent

molality = 0.0337 mol / 0.150 kg

molality = 0.2247 molal

Calculating the freezing point depression constant of ethylene dibromide

kf = ∆Tf / molality

kf = 2.66°C / 0.2247 molal

kf = 11.8 °C/molal
