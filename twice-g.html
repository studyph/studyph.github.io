Answer:

Since G is equal to 10 less than twice H, we get:

G = 2H - 10

Since the total number of rooms at G and H is 425, we get:

G + H = 425

H = 425 - G

Substituting H = 425-G into G = 2H - 10, we get:

G = 2(425-G) - 10

G = 850 - 2G - 10

3G = 840

7. Compute for the value of the acceleration due to gravity g of an object at an altitude equal to twice the radius of the earth ?(radius of earth= 6,4*10exponent power 5.


Answer:

To compute for the value of the acceleration due to gravity (g) at an altitude equal to twice the radius of the Earth, we need to use the formula for gravitational acceleration:

g = G * M / r^2

where:

g is the acceleration due to gravity

G is the gravitational constant, equal to 6.67 x 10^-11 N*m^2/kg^2

M is the mass of the Earth, equal to 5.97 x 10^24 kg

r is the distance from the center of the Earth to the object

In this case, the distance from the center of the Earth to the object is twice the radius of the Earth, which is 2 * 6.4 x 10^5 m = 1.28 x 10^6 m.

Plugging these values into the formula, we get:

g = (6.67 x 10^-11 N*m^2/kg^2) * (5.97 x 10^24 kg) / (1.28 x 10^6 m)^2

Simplifying this equation gives us:

g = 9.81 m/s^2

So the value of the acceleration due to gravity at an altitude equal to twice the radius of the Earth is approximately 9.81 m/s^2.

Hotel G = 2x - 10

Hotel G = 2(145) - 10
