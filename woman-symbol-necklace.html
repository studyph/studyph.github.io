6. A necklace was sold for Php3150.00 with a 40% discount.what was the original Price of the necklace?​


ANSWER


Php5250.00

SOLUTION

To determine the original price of the necklace, you can use the following formula:

Original price = selling price / (1 - discount%)

Plugging in the given values, you get:

Original price = 3150 / (1 - 40%) = 3150 / (1 - 0.4) = 3150 / 0.6 = 5250

Therefore, the original price of the necklace was Php5250.00.

12. point view ,plot,setting,character,theme and symbol of the Necklace story​


Answer:

Maupassant's story focuses on a young girl, Mathilde, who is dissatisfied with her lack of money and fine things. She and her husband, Monsieur Loisel, are invited to a fancy party, and she borrows a necklace from her rich friend, Madame Forestier, for the occasion. However, during the evening Mathilde loses the necklace, and she and her husband are unable to find it. They use all of their savings and borrow thousands of francs to buy a replacement necklace. When Mathilde runs into her friend after not seeing her for ten years, she tells Madame Forestier the whole story, only to find out that Madame Forestier's original necklace was an imitation and only worth 500 francs at most.

The Points of View

Before we can explore the POV of ''The Necklace,'' let's look at the different options. There are four different points of view in literature. One is first person. In this POV you're seeing the story as if you were inside the brain of a particular character. You can tell it's first person because of the use of ''I'' and ''we.''

There is also a second person POV, which uses ''you.'' This POV reads as though the narrator were speaking directly to the reader. It is the least common point of view found in literature.

Finally, there is third person POV, which uses ''she'' or ''he'' instead of ''I.'' In third person there may be no named narrator at all. Within third person there are two perspectives. The first is third person limited. In third person limited, you only see the thoughts of one or two characters. It's limited to those characters, but it is told as if an outside narrator were observing their thoughts and actions.

In third person omniscient the narrator sees all. In this POV you can see the thoughts and actions of many characters, and the perspective is not focused on one or two in particular.

The Story Itself

Maupassant's short story is written from a third person limited POV. Specifically, it is limited to Mathilde and Monsieur Loisel. We never hear the thoughts of any other characters, and we only see the actions of other characters as they affect Mathilde and her husband. It is not first person because ''I'' is never used. Instead, third person terms such as ''she'' and ''he'' are used. Take the following sentence for example:

''She suffered endlessly, feeling she was entitled to all the delicacies and luxuries of life.''

19. Lift 3 Symbolisms from the story of "The necklace".explain​


Answer:

"The Necklace" is a short story by French writer Guy de Maupassant. It tells the story of Madame Loisel, a woman who desires to be part of the wealthy upper class society and borrows a diamond necklace to wear to a ball, only to lose it and spend the rest of her life trying to replace it. Here are three symbols from the story:

The Necklace: The necklace symbolizes the materialism and vanity that Madame Loisel desires. It represents the upper class society and the desire to fit in and be accepted by it. The necklace also represents the illusion of wealth and happiness that Madame Loisel thinks it will bring her. However, in reality, it leads to her downfall and lifelong suffering.

The Mirror: The mirror is a symbol of Madame Loisel's vanity and self-absorption. She spends hours looking at herself in the mirror, dreaming of a better life and imagining herself as part of the upper class. The mirror also reflects the reality of Madame Loisel's situation, showing her the truth about her life and the consequences of her actions.

The Dress: The dress that Madame Loisel wears to the ball symbolizes her temporary transformation into the upper class society. It represents the illusion of social mobility and the idea that anyone can achieve success and happiness through hard work and determination. However, the dress is also a reminder of Madame Loisel's poverty and her inability to truly belong to the upper class. It also serves as a contrast to the reality of Madame Loisel's life, which is filled with hardship and struggle.

21. Edna already owns 4 necklaces, and additional necklaces are priced at 2 for a dollar. Is the total number of necklaces Edna owns proportional to the amount of money Edna spends on additional necklaces?


Answer:

Yes.

Explanation:

Two terms are proportional if one term increases with the other, or as one term increases, the other decreases.

Using this basis, we know that the number of necklaces is proportional to the amount of money spent.

As the amount increases by a dollar, the necklaces increase by 2.

We can also determine if this is proportional if we can come up with a linear equation that relates the two terms.

Let x = necklaces; let y = amount spent for additional necklaces.

Using what I previously said in the third paragraph, y = x/2. But, we need to add 4 to this since there are 4 necklaces already to start with. Thus, y = x/2 + 4.

To summarize, yes; the number of necklaces is proportional to the amount spent on additional necklaces.
