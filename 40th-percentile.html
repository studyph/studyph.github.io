9. A total of 8000 people visited a shopping mall over 12 hours. Estimate the third quartile(when 75% of the visitors had arrived). Estimate the 40th percentile(when 40% of the visitors had arrived)


Answer:

3rd Quartile: 6000 people had arrived at 9 hours

40th Percentile: 3200 people had arrived at 4 hours and 48 minutes

Step-by-step explanation:

This problem can be solved using the base, percentage, and rate. We need to find the total number of people who had already arrived and what hour is it for the given percentile.

Note that the formula for questions like these is P=BR, where P is percentage, B is base, and R is rate.

First Question: 3rd Quartile

The 3rd Quartile can also be written as the 75th Percentile. Thus, we have a rate of 75%. Since we already know the bases which is 8000 people and 12 hours, we just have to find the percentages.

P1a = 8000 people x 0.75 = 6000 people

P1b = 12 hours x 0.75 = 9 hours

Second Question: 40th Percentile

The only difference of this question and the previous one is the rate. Here, that quantity is 40%. Again using the given bases, we can multiply to solve for the percentages.

P2a = 8000 people x 0.4 = 3200 people

P2b = 12 hours x 0.4 = 4.8 hours or 4:48
