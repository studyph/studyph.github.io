25. compare and contrast the clastic vs non-clastic sedimentary rocks​


Answer:

Clastic sedimentary rocks are made of bits and pieces that eroded out of other rocks. Non-clastic sedimentary rocks are evaporites (like salt and gypsum) or precipitates like calcium carbonate (some types of limestone, and travertine). The tricky thing is that sedimentary rocks are not necessarily 100% of one type or the other. It’s true that some clastic rocks are cohesive because they underwent diagenesis (extreme pressure forced the crystals together), but many clastic rocks are cemented together because minerals precipitated out of water that flowed between the grains.


26. it is true that clastic sedimentary rocks are classified according to the size of the particles.​


Answer:

Clastic sediments or sedimentary rocks are classified based on grain size, clast and cementing material (matrix) composition, and texture. ... Grain size varies from clay in shales and claystones; through silt in siltstones; sand in sandstones; and gravel, cobble, to boulder sized fragments in conglomerates and breccias.

27. what make up clastic or fragmental sedimentary ​


Fragmental (or clastic) sediments are made up of particles of weathered rock, including rock fragments, residual mineral grains (especially quartz, which is both hard and chemically inert) and clay minerals. Examples include: conglomerate, sandstone, mudstone & shale.
