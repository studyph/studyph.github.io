3. why is abraham important in the salvation bible


Answer:

Abraham is considered an important figure in the salvation history of the Bible because of his role in God's plan for redemption. According to the Bible, God chose Abraham to be the father of a great nation, and through him, God made a covenant promising to bless his descendants and provide them with a land of their own. Abraham's faithfulness and obedience to God, as demonstrated by his willingness to sacrifice his son Isaac, serve as a model of faith for believers. In the New Testament, Abraham is also cited as an example of justification by faith, which is the belief that salvation comes through faith in Jesus Christ rather than through obedience to the law. Therefore, Abraham's story serves as an important foundation for the Christian faith and understanding of salvation.

17. describe Abraham from the bible and his traits


Abraham is pictured with various characteristic: a righteous man, with wholehearted commitment to God; a man of peace (in settling a boundary dispute with his nephew Lot), compassionate (he argues and bargains with God to spare the people of Sodom and Gomorrah), and hospitable (he welcomes three visiting angels); a quick-acting warrior (he rescues Lot and his family from a raiding party); and an unscrupulous liar to save his own skin (he passes off Sarah as his sister and lets her be picked by the Egyptian pharaoh for his harem). He appears as both a man of great spiritual depth and strength and a person with common human weaknesses and needs.
