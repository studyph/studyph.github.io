15. 3.What is the speed of sound in dry air at O OC?A. 31 m/sB. 331 m/sC. 3000 m/sD. 300000 m/s​


B. 331 m/s

Speed of sound

The distance a sound wave travels through an elastic medium in one unit of time is measured as its speed of sound. The speed of sound in air at 20 °C (68 °F) is approximately 343 m/s (1,125 ft/s; 1,235 km/h; 767 mph; 667 kn), or 2.91 s for a kilometer and 4.69 s for a mile. Temperature and the medium a sound wave travels through both have a significant impact. The speed of sound in air is approximately 331 m/s at 0 °C (32 °F).

Further explanation

In an ideal gas, the only factors affecting sound speed are temperature and composition. In typical air, the speed deviates just a little from optimal behavior by having a modest dependence on frequency and pressure. Speed of sound, as used in everyday speech, relates to the velocity of sound waves in air. However, the speed of sound varies depending on the medium. Typically, sound moves most slowly through gases, then more quickly through liquids, and then most quickly through solids. For instance, whereas sound moves at a speed of 343 m/s in the air, it moves at 1,481 m/s through water and 5,120 m/s through iron. The maximum speed at which sound can move under normal circumstances is 12,000 m/s in the very rigid substance diamond. This is 35 times the speed of sound in air.

Compression waves, which also exist in gases and liquids, and shear waves, a separate kind of sound wave that exclusively happens in solids, make up sound waves in solids. Seismology shows that shear waves in solids often move at a faster rate than compression waves. The compressibility, shear modulus, and density of the medium affect the speed of compression waves in solids. Only the shear modulus and density of the solid material affect the shear wave speed.

In fluid dynamics, the relative measurement of the speed of an item traveling through a fluid media (gas or liquid) is the speed of sound in that medium. The Mach number of an item is its velocity relative to the speed of sound in a given medium. Supersonic motion refers to movement of objects travelling faster than the speed of sound (Mach 1).
