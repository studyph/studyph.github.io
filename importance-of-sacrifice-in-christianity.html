2. What is our Christian view of sacrifice?


The Christian View of Sacrifice

The Christian view of sacrifice is

centered on spiritual and personal sacrifice. In the Old Testament, the people of God made sacrifices by killing or offering animals at the temple or altar. However, Jesus made the "ultimate" and "perfect sacrifice" which put an end to this tradition of shedding actual blood as an offering. This sacrifice is perpetuated through the celebration of the Eucharist where the priest transforms the bread and wine into body and blood, spiritually.

Further Explanation on The Christian View of Sacrifice

As mentioned above, the Christian view of sacrifice is also focused on personal or self-sacrifice where an individual offers up himself for others or the Lord.

Here are a few more examples of spiritual and personal/self-sacrifices observed by Christians:

Communion (during Eucharistic celebration) BaptismRepentance or confessionAlmsgiving or tithingHelping others or doing acts of mercyOffering up to God one's circumstances, plans, emotions, possessions, time, and moreOffering prayers of thanksgiving to Jesus

9. What is the reason why the early Christians were accused of practicing incest? a. Christians volunteer in order to share the love to the needy. b. Christians are willing to sacrifice their lives as offering to a feast. c. Christians refer each other as “brothers and sisters” sharing a “love feast.” d. Christians share their things and money in common.


The reason why early Christians were accused of practicing incest is option (c): Christians referred to each other as "brothers and sisters" and shared a "love feast."

In the early days of Christianity, believers often referred to one another as "brothers and sisters" because they saw themselves as part of a new spiritual family united by their faith in Jesus Christ. They also practiced a communal lifestyle where they shared their possessions and resources with one another.

In addition to this communal lifestyle, early Christians also held a "love feast" or agape feast, where they shared a meal together and commemorated the Last Supper of Jesus. This practice of sharing a meal with fellow believers was often misunderstood by outsiders, who accused Christians of engaging in immoral and incestuous behavior.

However, the accusations of incest were based on a misunderstanding of the term "brother" and "sister" as used in the early Christian community. The terms were used to denote spiritual kinship rather than blood relations, and there is no evidence to suggest that early Christians engaged in any form of sexual immorality or incestuous behavior.
