3. illustrate and explain the hydrologic cycle. describe the role of soil in the hydrologic cycle .​


Answer:

The hydrologic cycle is the process by which water is moved from the Earth's surface to the atmosphere and back down to the surface, where it is recycled. Water is evaporated from oceans, lakes, and rivers in the form of water vapor, then rises into the atmosphere. Up in the atmosphere, the water vapor condenses, forming clouds. These clouds are then pushed by wind and gravity to a location where they cool and precipitation falls back to the surface, in the form of rain, snow, hail, or sleet. The water then flows over the land, collecting in streams, rivers, and lakes and eventually makes it back to the ocean.

Soil plays an important role in the hydrologic cycle, acting as a reservoir for water and helping to regulate the flow of water into and out of the atmosphere. The soil retains water and slowly releases it, allowing it to be absorbed by plants and other organisms. The soil also absorbs any precipitation that falls, before it can run off the surface and become runoff. This runoff is an important part of the hydrologic cycle, as it carries water from land to the oceans, where it can be evaporated and start the cycle again.

19. Expound the Hydrologic Cycle.​


Answer:

The natural water recycling system on Earth is known as the hydrological cycle, sometimes known as the "water cycle." Water evaporates from the sea, lakes, and other bodies of water due to sun radiation. The mechanism of transpiration allows water to evaporate from plant leaves as well. As the steam rises in the atmosphere, it cools, condenses, and falls as precipitation on the land and sea. Precipitation falls as surface water on the earth's surface, forming streams of water that eventually become lakes and rivers. Aquifers are formed when rainwater penetrates the ground and flows downward via the perforations. With these mechanisms, you can transition between liquid, vapor, and ice phases. Finally, some surface and subsurface water flows into the sea. Water is changed into all three stages during this journey: gas, liquid, and solid. 23. give the meaning of hydrology​


Answer:

hydrology

Definition of hydrology: a science dealing with the properties, distribution, and circulation of water on and below the earth's surface and in the atmosphere

Give the meaning of hydrology​

Hydrology is the scientific study of the allocation, transportation, and management of water on Earth and other planets, including the water cycle, water resources, and the long-term sustainability of natural watersheds. A hydrologist is a person who works in the field of hydrology. Hydrologists are earth and environmental scientists who study civil and environmental engineering, as well as physical geography. They gather and evaluate data using various analytical methods and scientific procedures to aid in the resolution of water-related issues such as environmental preservation, natural catastrophes, and water management.

Surface water hydrology, groundwater hydrology (hydrogeology), and marine hydrology are the three types of hydrology. Hydrometeorology, surface hydrology, hydrogeology, drainage-basin management, and water quality are all domains of hydrology in which water plays a fundamental role.
