2. what is classification and description of accordion​


Explanation:

Accordions (from 19th-century German Akkordeon, from Akkord—"musical chord, concord of sounds") are a family of box-shaped musical instruments of the bellows-driven free-reed aerophone type, colloquially referred to as a squeezebox. A person who plays the accordion is called an accordionist.

16. 9. The main keyboard instruments of the baroque period were the organ and the a. accordion b.clavichord c. harpsichord d. piano​


Answer:

C. harpsichord

Explanation:

The harpsichord is a keyboard instrument in which the strings are plucked, rather than hit with a hammer (which is the mechanism for the piano, a more recent development). The distinctive sound of the harpsichord creates an almost immediately association with the baroque era.
