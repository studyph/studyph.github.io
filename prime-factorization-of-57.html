[tex]\huge\sf\green{Explanation:}[/tex]

A composite number is a positive integer that can be formed by multiplying two smaller positive integers. Equivalently, it is a positive integer that has at least one divisor other than 1 and itself.

Therefore, the number 1 - 4 is a composite number because they have more than 2 factors.

A prime number is a natural number greater than 1 that is not a product of two smaller natural numbers. A prime number can only be divided (without a remainder) by itself and 1.

Since 11 has exactly two factors, it is a prime number.
