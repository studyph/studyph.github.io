4. simple aspect simple present simple past simple future​


Answer:

Simple past -It is used principally to describe events in the past, although it also has some other uses.

Simple present -it is used to show repetition, habit or generalization and can be used to talk about scheduled actions in the near future and, in some cases, actions happening now.

Simple future -it is used to predict a future event. It refers to a time later than now, and expresses facts or certainty.

Simple present sentence :

•Cats hate water.

• I run every weekend.

• It usually rains every day here.

• We walk slowly.

Simple past sentence:

• I went to school yesterday.

• I watch TV last week.

• The rain stop an hour ago.

• He bought a new shoes last

month.

Simple future sentence:

• We will probably win.

• He is going to sleep.

• You are going to cry.

• We are going to eat

Explanation:

to form the simple past you may add d or -ed to regular verbs. to irregular verbs you may have to change their spelling or better yet memorize their past and participle forms.
