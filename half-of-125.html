11. A rock formed with 1,000 atoms of a radioactive parent element, but only contains 125 radioactive parent atoms today. if the half-life is 1,000,000 years, how old is the rock?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & N_{0} = \text{1,000 atoms} \\ & N = \text{125 atoms} \\ & t_{\frac{1}{2}} = \text{1,000,000 years} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the rate constant.

[tex]\begin{aligned} k & = \frac{\text{ln 2}}{t_{\frac{1}{2}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{ln 2}}{\text{1,000,000 years}} \\ & = 6.9314718 \times 10^{-7} \: \text{/year} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the age of the rock.

[tex]\begin{aligned} t & = -\frac{\text{ln} \: \left(\dfrac{N}{N_{0}}\right)}{k} \\ & = -\frac{\text{ln} \: \left(\dfrac{\text{125 atoms}}{\text{1,000 atoms}}\right)}{6.9314718 \times 10^{-7} \: \text{/year}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{3,000,000 years}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the rock is 3,000,000 years old.
