2. 1. Non-linear text is visuals by which a reader may not need to go through in a sequential way to get the meaning of the text.TRUE OR FALSE​


NON-LINEAR TEXTS

The statement is TRUE, non-linear texts refer to visual texts that do not require to be read in chronological order - from beginning to end to understand the meaning of a text. However, non-linear texts follow skimming to obtain certain information. For instance, infographic, charts, telephone directories, knowledge maps, encyclopedias, etc.

The major purpose of non-linear texts is to convey context and character background, as well as to highlight important theme conflicts by skipping over unnecessary details.

Hyper-linking allows for multiple paths of interaction in many digital texts, making it non-linear texts. These allow a reader to find the information they need when they need it, and non-linear texts also allow a writer to give the reader the opportunity to make these alternatives.
