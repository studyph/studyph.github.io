6. Distinguish between a holistic and analytic rubric​


Answer: Analytic and holistic rubrics are two types of rubrics used to assess student work. Analytic rubrics identify and assess individual components of a finished product, while holistic rubrics assess student work as a whole. Analytic rubrics are more detailed and specific, breaking down the characteristics of the work being assessed into separate components. Each component is evaluated separately, and the scores are combined to give an overall score for the assessed work. On the other hand, holistic rubrics are less detailed and assess the work as a whole. They provide a general description of the characteristics that define each level of performance, and the levels can be labeled with numbers, letters, or words.

Analytic rubrics are useful when a teacher wants to grade a student's work based on specific criteria and provide detailed feedback on each component of the work. They are also useful when the teacher wants to assess the student's progress in a particular area. Holistic rubrics are helpful when a teacher wants to grade a student's work based on general progress and performance and does not need the specificity that would come with an analytic rubric. Holistic rubrics are typically scored on a scale of 1-4 or 1-6 and are helpful in evaluating a student's general performance and grasp of the subject matter being taught.

Neither rubric is better than the other, and both have a place in authentic assessment, depending on the teacher's goals and the students being taught. Analytic rubrics are more detailed and specific, making them helpful in grading complex assignments or assessing particular skills. Holistic rubrics need to be more precise and assess the work as a whole, making them helpful in grading simpler terms or assessing general progress and performance. Whether to use an analytic or a holistic rubric depends on the teacher's goals and the students being taught.

16. differentiate the holistic rubric and analytic rubric​


Holistic rubric is a single criterion rubric (one-dimensional) used to assess participants' overall achievement on an activity or item based on predefined achievement levels. On the other hand, analytic rubric is a two-dimensional rubric with levels of achievement as columns and assessment criteria as rows.
