9. some examples indigenous beliefs ​


Answer:

Some features that characterize indigenous religions include the following: geographic location, the use of ritual and artifacts, community participation, a fluid structure, and belief in a supreme God or other divinities (gods).

Indigenous religions rarely have written sacred texts. Rather, their beliefs focus on dances, costumes, masks, ritual traditions, and sacred artifacts (material objects). These practices are part of a people's cultural identity and help them forge a sense of connection with their world.

30. Read articles on indigenous science on the internet. Examine what is common among these indigenous science beliefs and practices...


Answer:

Indigenous science is a diverse set of beliefs and practices that are unique to the different cultures and communities of Indigenous peoples around the world. Despite their diversity, there are some commonalities that can be observed across different indigenous science traditions.

One common theme is the interconnectedness of all things. Indigenous peoples view the natural world as a complex web of relationships between humans, animals, plants, and the environment. This holistic approach emphasizes the importance of maintaining balance and harmony between different elements of the ecosystem.

Another common belief is the importance of oral traditions and the transmission of knowledge from one generation to the next. Indigenous peoples have developed sophisticated systems for passing down knowledge, including storytelling, song, dance, and ritual practices.

Indigenous science also places a strong emphasis on observation and experimentation. Indigenous scientists often have a deep understanding of the natural world, based on centuries of careful observation and experimentation. This knowledge is often passed down through apprenticeships and other forms of informal learning.

Finally, indigenous science often includes spiritual and ceremonial practices. These practices are deeply rooted in the culture and worldview of Indigenous peoples and are often used to connect with the natural world and with ancestors.

In summary, common beliefs and practices found in indigenous science include interconnectedness, oral tradition, observation and experimentation, and spiritual and ceremonial practices. However, it is important to note that these beliefs and practices vary widely among different indigenous cultures and communities, and should not be seen as a monolithic or homogenous set of ideas.
