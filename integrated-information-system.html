5. The brain is one of the largest and most complex organs in the human body. Which of the following DOES NOT tell the function of the brain? *A. A highly developed organ in the central nervous system.B. It serves as the communication lines in the nervous systemC. It receives the nerve impulses and processes sensory information and sends the information to the spinal cord.D. It serves as the central control, integrates and processes the incoming​


C. It receives the nerve impulses and processes sensory information and sends the information to the spinal cord.

The brain is a vital and complex organ that is protected by the skull and lining of the brain (meninges). This organ consists of a number of tissues and billions of supporting nerve cells and is connected to the spinal cord. Together with the spinal cord and nerves, the brain is the command center and human nervous system.

As part of the nervous system, the brain has many important functions. This organ controls everything that happens in the body, such as thoughts, memories, speech, feelings, vision, hearing, arm and leg movements, and the functions of other organs in the body, including heart rate and breathing.

The brain is divided into three main parts, namely the cerebrum, cerebellum, and brainstem. These parts of the brain work together to run the body's systems. However, all three have specific functions that are different for each.

The brain is formed and develops since humans are still in the womb until adulthood. This brain development starts from the back of the brain and continues to the front or the frontal lobe.

During the developmental period in the womb, 250,000 neurons (nerve cells) are added every minute. Each neuron will connect with other neurons to form a nervous system with the help of fibers called dendrites and axons.
