Volcanoes are of three types: active, dormant, and extinct. Mount Vesuvius, Mauna Loa, and Mount Unzen are examples of volcanoes. CANYONS A canyon is a deep narrow valley. They are usually formed from the action of rivers. They have very steep sides and are usually made of sandstones or granite. The term “canyon” is used in the United States. In Europe, however, the word “gorge” is more commonly used to describe canyons.
