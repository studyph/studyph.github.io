1. During World War II, which battle denied the Japanese the opportunity to attack Australia? Battle of the Coral Sea Battle of Midway Battle of Guadalcanal Battle of Iwo Jima


The Battle of Coral Sea which took place in May 1942 is the main reason for the failed invasion. This had also been the very first air-sea combat in history. This skirmish happened because the Japanese wanted to gain control over the Western Pacific. Also known as “The Battle that Saved Australia”, this clash at Coral Sea went on for four days until the American and Australian forces strategically put an end to it.

American soldiers greatly relied geographically on Australia as a means to get to Japan. This has been the main reason why Japan wanted to advance in this area.  However, the damage done to the Japanese carrier ship Shokaku fighter jets had significantly decreased Japanese forces in another battle which occurred the following month - The Battle of Midway.

5. sanaysay battle of mactan an battle mactan covid​


Answer:

The Battle of Mactan (Cebuano: Gubat sa Mactan; Filipino: Labanan sa Mactan) was a fierce clash fought in the Philippines on 27 April 1521. The warriors of Lapulapu, one of the Datus of Mactan, overpowered and defeated a Spanish force fighting for Rajah Humabon of Cebu under the command of Portuguese explorer Ferdinand Magellan, who was killed in battle. The outcome of the battle resulted in the departure of the Spanish crew from the archipelago, and delayed the Spanish colonization of the Philippines by 44 years until the conquest by Miguel López de Legazpi in 1564–1565.

30. economic function of;HOYA OBSCURA-JAD E VINE-CEBU CINNAMON-SIBUYAN NEPHENTES-HEART-SHAPED NEPHENTES-PELTATE NEPHENTES-​


1. Hoya Obscura is from the Hoya genus. It’s one of the fastest-growing plants in the Philippines and has some gorgeous blooms that will grab your attention instantly. Compared to other Hoyas, the Hoya Obscura is small or medium-sized, has fast growth, and has stiff stalks that do not twine with each other.

2.Strongylodon macrobotrys, commonly known as jade vine, emerald vine or turquoise jade vine, is a species of leguminous perennial liana endemic to the tropical forests of the Philippines, with stems that can reach up to 18 m in length. Its local name is tayabak

3. Cinnamomum cebuense, the Cebu cinnamon or, locally, kaningag, is a species of cinnamon endemic to Cebu Island, Philippines. It was first discovered in Cantipla, Cebu in mid-1980s and described by Kostermans in 1986.

4. Nepenthes sibuyanensis is a tropical pitcher plant endemic to Sibuyan Island in the Philippines, after which it is named.

5. Nepenthes truncata is characterised by its heart-shaped (truncate) leaves and very large pitchers, which can reach up to 40 cm in height. Nepenthes robcantleyi was once considered a dark, highland form of this species.

6. Nepenthes peltata is a tropical pitcher plant known only from the upper slopes of Mount Hamiguitan on the island of Mindanao in the Philippines. It is characterised by a peltate tendril attachment and conspicuous indumentum.
