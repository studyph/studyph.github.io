23. a condition in which society provides little moral guidance to individuals


The answer is Anomie. Anomie is a condition in which society provides little moral guidance to individuals. Anomie is also spelled as "anomy". It is a condition wherein there is an issue or conflict involving the belief systems, social relationship, social standards, values, and ideals in a society or an individual. The presence of anomie is considered as a condition of dysfunctionality, disconnection, and instability.

Definition of Anomie Anomie is a condition wherein an individual or society experiences dysfunctionality, disconnection, and instability because of conflicts in belief systems, social relationship, social standards, values, and ideals. Moreover, anomie is a condition in which society provides little moral guidance to individuals. The term anomie was popularized by a French sociologist named Emile Durkheim. Signs that a Society is in Anomie

According to the aforementioned sociologist named Emile Durkheim, a social system is experiencing an anomie if it encounters the following:

Common values and meanings are not recognized and accepted in a society. No new values and meanings are being formed in a society.
