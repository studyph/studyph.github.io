12. What does 20/300 vision indicate, how is that corrected by LASIK surgery

LASIK surgery will have of a great benefit to the person having a 20/300 VA (Visual Acuity). These procedure uses a cool non thermal beam of light that will reshape the surface of the eye (cornea). For those who has nearsightedness, the laser will remove microscopic bits of tissues to flatten the surface of eye, for the farsightedness, the laser will steepen the surface of the eye, and for the astigmatism, the laser will smooth out any irregularities in the cornea

15. A person runs a distance of 300 m in 20 s, what is his speed? *


Answer:

Measuring Time and Speed

How long would it take you to walk 15 kilometers? How long would it take to travel 15 kilometers in a car?

You can travel the same distance in a shorter length of time in a car because a car can move at a higher speed. Speed is how fast and object's position changes over time or how fast of an object is. "Fastest" in a race means running with the greater speed.

Time can be measured in a variety of ways, usually with a watch or a clock. Time can be measured in seconds (s), minutes (min), hours (h), days, weeks, etc. An hour, for example, can be converted to minutes by multiplying the hour by 60; a minute can be converted into seconds by multiplying it also 60. Below are examples of common unit conversions of time.

1 hour = 60 minutes

1 minute = 60 seconds

1 day = 24 hours

1 week = 7 days

1 year = 12 months

Speed is measured using units of distance per unit of time, such as meters per second (m/s), kilometers per hour (km/h), or miles per hour (mph).

How is speed calculated? You can find the speed of an object if you know the distance traveled by the object and how long it took to travel that distance.

You divide distance by time to find speed. For example, a runner runs 100 meters in 20 seconds; the speed is 100 m divided by 20 s.

Calculating Speed

Data:

100m = distance

20 s = time

speed = distance / time

= 100 m / 20 s

= 5 m/s

Since speed, distance, and time are all related, we can calculate distances and times by the simple rearrangement of the formulas.

distance = speed x time

d = (s) x (t)

time = distance / speed

t = d/s

"15 m/s" is the answer of your Question

Solution

Given: 300 m = distance

20 s = time

Unknown: speed = ?

Formula: s = d/t

= 300 m / 20 s

= 15 m/s
