1. William is buying tickets for a school play. Adult tickets cost $2 more than student tickets. He buys 9 student tickets and 5 adult tickets and spends a total of $66. How much is an adult ticket?


[tex]\huge\color{iron}\bold{Answer:}[/tex]

[tex]________________________________________[/tex]

William is buying tickets for a school play. Adult tickets cost 2 dollar more than student tickets. He buys 9 student tickets and 5 adult tickets and spends a total of 66 dollar. How much is an adult ticket?

Adult ticket costs 6 dollars.

[tex]------------------[/tex]

[tex]\huge\color{iron}\bold{Solution:}[/tex]

[tex]5 \times x + 9 \times (x - 2) = 66[/tex]

[tex] = 5x + 9(x - 2) = 66[/tex]

[tex] = 5x + 9x - 18 = 66[/tex]

[tex] = 14x - 18 = 66[/tex]

[tex]14x = 66 + 18[/tex]

[tex]14x = 84[/tex]

[tex]x = \frac{84}{14} [/tex]

[tex]x = 6[/tex]

So 6 dollars is the costs of an adult ticket.

6. an adult ticket to a concert cost $35 and a concession ticket cost $18. Mr B. paid $598 in all for 20 such tickets did he buy? How many concession did he buy?


Answer:

Mr. B bought 6 concession tickets.He also bought 14 adult tickets.

Step-by-step explanation:

Adult Ticket: x

Concession Ticket: y

Number of tickets:  20

Equation (1):

x + y = 20

Concession tickets in terms of x:

y = 20-x

Cost of tickets:

Adult ticket: 35x

Concession ticket: 18(20-x)

Total cost of tickets: 598

Equation (2):

35x + 18(20-x) = 598

Solve for adult tickets, x:

35x + 18(20-x) = 598

35x + 360 - 18x = 598

35x - 18x = 598 - 360

17x = 238

17x/17 = 238/17

x = 14 ⇒  adult tickets

Find the number of concession tickets, y:

y = 20-x

y = 20-14

y = 6 ⇒  number of concession tickets

Check:

Total number of tickets:

14 adult tickets + 6 concession tickets = 20 tickets

10 = 20  (True)

Total Cost of tickets:

35(14) + 18(6) = 598

490 + 108 = 598

598 = 598  (True)

30. Wendy had 35 tickets for games at a carnival she used 1/5 of the tickets to play the ball toss game she then used 1/2 the remaining tickets to play the ring toss game in which she won 5 more tickets how many tickets did Wendy have after playing theses games


Answer:

After playing the games, Wendy had 19 tickets.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let us analyze the given problem above.

What is asked?

The total number of tickets that Wendy had after playing the games.

What are the given?

number of tickets at first - 35

tickets used to play the ball toss game - ⅕ of 35

tickets to play the ring toss game - ½ of the remaining tickets

number of tickets she won - 5

We have three steps to find the total number of tickets that Wendy had after playing the games.

Find the ⅕ of 35.Find the ½ of the remaining tickets.Find the total number of tickets in the end.

Solution:

1. Find the ⅕ of 35.

To find the ⅕ of 35, we need to replace the word "of" with a multiplication sign. Remember that "of" means you need to multiply.

⅕ of 35

= ⅕ × 35

= ⅕ × 35⁄1‌

= ‌35⁄5‌

= 7

Wendy used 7 tickets in ball toss game.

2. Find the ½ of the remaining tickets.

We already know that she used 7 tickets already. So we need to subtract 7 from 35, to know how many tickets remained. Then, get the half of the number.

35 - 7 = 28

½ of 28

= ½ × 28

= ½ × ‌28⁄1‌

= ‌28⁄2‌

= 14

She used 14 tickets in the ring toss game.

Therefore, there are still 14 remaining tickets.

3. Find the total number of tickets in the end.

We need to add the remaining tickets and the number of tickets she won in the ring toss game.

14 + 5 = 19

Final Answer:

After playing the games, Wendy had 19 tickets.
