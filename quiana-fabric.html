9. Luisa is buying a fabric. She bought ¼ meter of red fabric and 5/8 meter of blue fabric. How many meters of fabric did she buy?


Answer:

Red = 1/4

Blue = 5/8

[tex] \frac{1}{4} + \frac{5}{8} [/tex]

[tex] \frac{1 \times 2}{4 \times 2} + \frac{5}{8} [/tex]

[tex] \frac{2}{8} + \frac{5}{8} [/tex]

[tex] \frac{7}{8} [/tex]

#AnswerForTrees

#BrainlyBookSmart

Answer:

Therefore, Luisa bought [tex]\frac{7}{8}[/tex] meters of fabric.

Step-by-step explanation:

To know how many meters of fabric she bought, we will add how many meters of red fabric and blue fabric she bought.

[tex]\frac{1}{4}+ \frac{5}{8}[/tex]

We can't add fractions with different denominators so the next thing to do is to make them similar fractions. To do that, we will get the LCD of the two denominators (4 and 8). Let's use the listing method.

4: 4, 8, 12, 16, 20...

8: 8, 16, 24, 32, 40...

Our LCD is 8. To get the new numerator, divide the LCD by the old denominator then multiply by the old numerator.

[tex]\frac{1}{4} =\frac{2}{8}[/tex]

[tex]\frac{5}{8}= \frac{5}{8}[/tex]

Now, it's time to add.

[tex]\frac{2}{8}+ \frac{5}{8}[/tex]

[tex]\frac{7}{8}[/tex]

The answer is already simplified. Therefore, Luisa bought [tex]\frac{7}{8}[/tex] meters of fabric.
