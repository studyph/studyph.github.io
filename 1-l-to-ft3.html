13. if the specific weight of liquid is 52lb/ft3 what is it density?​


Answer:

the density of a liquid with a specific weight of 52 lb/ft^3 is approximately 1.62 slugs/ft^3.

Step-by-step explanation:

The specific weight of a liquid is defined as the weight per unit volume, and the density of a liquid is defined as its mass per unit volume.

Since the weight of an object is equal to its mass multiplied by the acceleration due to gravity, we can convert the specific weight of a liquid to its density by multiplying it by the acceleration due to gravity. The acceleration due to gravity is approximately 9.8 m/s^2, so we can use the following formula to convert the specific weight to density:

density = specific weight / (acceleration due to gravity)

For a liquid with a specific weight of 52 lb/ft^3, the density can be calculated as follows:

density = 52 lb/ft^3 / (32.2 ft/s^2) = 1.62 slugs/ft^3

So the density of a liquid with a specific weight of 52 lb/ft^3 is approximately 1.62 slugs/ft^3.

25. Trex is a manufactured substitute for wood compounded from post-consumer plastic and wood. It is frequently used in outdoor decks. Its density is reported as 60.0 lb/ft3. What is the density of Trex in kg/L?


Density

Density is a quantity derived from mass and volume. Density is the ratio of mass per unit volume. Using the conversion per unit then 60.0 lb/ft³ = 0.9611 kg/L

Further explanation

The unit of density can be expressed in g/cm³ or kg/m³  

Density formula:  

[tex]\tt \rho=\dfrac{m}{V}[/tex]

ρ = density  

m = mass  

V = volume  

Given

density : 60.0 lb/ft³

Required

the density of Trex in kg/L

Solution

We determine the conversion of each unit

mass : lb to kg = 0.453592volume : ft3 to L = 28,3168

so for 60.0 lb/ft³ it becomes:

[tex]\tt 60\dfrac{lb}{ft^3}\times \dfrac{1~ft^3}{28.3168~L}\times \dfrac{0.453592~kg}{1~lb}=\boxed{\bold{0.9611~kg/L}}[/tex]
