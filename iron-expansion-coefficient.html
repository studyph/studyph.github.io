Answer:

The statement: The linear expansivity of a solid is 1.0×10−5K−1 means that, A unit length of the solid will expand in length by a fraction 1.0×10−5 of the original per kelvin rise in temperature. (Note: 1.0×10−5=1100,000, a fraction).

What is the formula for the coefficient of linear expansion?

The coefficient of Linear Expansion is the rate of change of unit length per unit degree change in temperature. The coefficient of linear expansion can be mathematically written as α L = dL d T

How do you calculate new length in linear Expansivity?

Linear expansivity is the fractional increase in length of a specimen of a solid, per unit rise in temperature. If a specimen increases in length from l1 to l2 when its temperature is raised θ°, then the expansivity (α) is given by: l2 = l1(1 + αθ).

What is a linear expansion in physics?

Linear expansion means a change in one dimension (length) as opposed to a change in volume (volumetric expansion). To a first approximation, the change in length measurements of an object due to thermal expansion is related to temperature change by a coefficient of linear thermal expansion (CLTE).

What is the symbol for the coefficient of linear expansion?

symbol α

On Practical Precision, the coefficient of thermal expansion is represented in formulas by the symbol α. In-text, the abbreviation CTE may be used.

30. Determine the binomial for expansion with the given situation below. The literal coefficient of the 5th term is xy^4. The numerical coefficient of the 6th term in the expansion is 243. The numerical coefficient of the 2nd term in the expansion is 3840.


Answer:

(4x + 3y)⁵

Step-by-step explanation:

Characteristics of an expanded binomial The first and last terms are both ±αⁿxⁿ and ±βⁿyⁿ respectivelyThe second and second to the last terms are ±nαⁿ⁻¹xⁿ⁻¹βy and ±nαxβⁿ⁻¹yⁿ⁻¹

(α and β are the coefficients)

[tex] \:[/tex]

literal coefficient of the 5th term is xy^4

With the characteristics of a binomial raised to n, the binomial must have a degree greater than 4

⇒ (αx±βy)ⁿ where n > 4

[tex] \:[/tex]

In the second bullet above, notice that the second to the last term, it only has exponent in βy, the same as in xy⁴: only y has an exponent

[tex] \:[/tex]

Therefore, ±nαxβⁿ⁻¹yⁿ⁻¹ = ±xy⁴

[tex] \:[/tex]

Since we do not see any coefficient in y⁴, then it has 1 as its coefficient *

⇒ ±nαx1ⁿ⁻¹yⁿ⁻¹ = ±xy⁴

⇒ 1ⁿ⁻¹yⁿ⁻¹ = y⁴ (since n > 4, 1ⁿ⁻¹ is always 1)

⇒ yⁿ⁻¹ = y⁴ ( clearly n = 5)

[tex] \:[/tex]

We already have n = 5, therefore the binomial must be in this form, (±αx±βy)⁵

[tex] \:[/tex]

numerical coefficient of the 6th term in the expansion is 243

Take note: 243 is in the sixth term

[tex] \:[/tex]

(±αx±βy)⁵ = ±α⁵x⁵ ±5α⁴x⁴βy ±10α³x³β²y² ±10α²x²β³y³ ±5αxβ⁴y⁴ ±β⁵y⁵

[tex] \:[/tex]

The last term of (±αx±βy)⁵ is ±β⁵y⁵ which is also the sixth term

[tex] \:[/tex]

Then, ±β⁵ = 243

⇒ ⁵√(±β⁵) = ⁵√(243)

⇒ β = 3

Now, we have (±αx+3y)⁵

[tex] \:[/tex]

numerical coefficient of the 2nd term in the expansion is 3840

The second term in (±αx+3y)⁵ is ±15α⁴x⁴y

⇒ ±15α⁴ = 3840

⇒ α⁴ = 3840÷15 = 256

⇒ ⁴√(α⁴) = ⁴√(256)

⇒ α = 4

With α = 4 and β = 3, the binomial is (4x + 3y)⁵

[tex] \:[/tex]

(* The reason why 1 is not written as coefficient is that it can be distracting to see that there are so many 1s written)
