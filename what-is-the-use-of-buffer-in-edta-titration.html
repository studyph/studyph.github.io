2. calculate the CaCO3 content of a chalk sample weighing 0.2545 g, consuming 16.67 ml of 0.1150M Disodium EDTA in titration​


Answer:

The reaction equation of Disodium EDTA and Calcium Carbonate is:

CaCO3 + Na2EDTA → Ca(EDTA)2- + 2Na+ + CO2

The stoichiometry of the reaction is 1:1. It means that 1 mole of Disodium EDTA react with 1 mole of CaCO3.

Given data:

Weight of chalk sample = 0.2545 g

Volume of Disodium EDTA = 16.67 ml

Molarity of Disodium EDTA = 0.1150 M

To calculate the CaCO3 content, we need to find the number of moles of Disodium EDTA used in the reaction. We can calculate the number of moles by using the formula:

moles of Disodium EDTA = Molarity × Volume (in liters)

Volume in liters = 16.67 ml = 16.67/1000 = 0.01667 L

moles of Disodium EDTA = 0.1150 × 0.01667 = 0.00191755 moles

As the stoichiometry of the reaction is 1:1, the number of moles of CaCO3 consumed in the reaction is also 0.00191755 moles.

The molar mass of CaCO3 = 40.08 (for Ca) + 12.01 (for C) + 3 × 16 (for 3O) = 100.09 g/mol

The mass of CaCO3 in the sample = Number of moles of CaCO3 × molar mass of CaCO3 = 0.00191755 × 100.09 = 0.192171 g

The percentage of CaCO3 in the sample = (Mass of CaCO3 / Weight of sample) × 100%

= (0.192171 / 0.2545) × 100%

= 75.52%

Therefore, the CaCO3 content in the chalk sample is 75.52%.

15. what are the titration process ​


Answer:

The titration process are

1. Preparation: The solution of unknown concentration and the titrant are prepared and measured.

2. Initial measurement: The initial measurement of the solution of unknown concentration is taken. This is usually done using a burette, a piece of laboratory equipment that allows for precise measurement of the volume of the titrant.

3. Addition of titrant: The titrant is slowly added to the solution of unknown concentration, typically drop by drop, until a reaction between the two solutions occurs.

4. Indicator: A chemical indicator, such as litmus paper or a pH meter, is used to determine when the reaction between the two solutions is complete.

5. Stopping point: The reaction between the two solutions is considered complete when the indicator changes color or when a certain reading is obtained on the pH meter.

6. Calculation: The volume of titrant added to the solution of unknown concentration is used to calculate the concentration of the solution. This calculation is typically based on the balanced chemical equation for the reaction between the two solutions.

17. what is the difference between basic buffers and acidic buffers​


A basic buffer solution---(more precisely, pH buffer or hydrogen ion buffer) is an aqueous solution consisting of a mixture of a weak acid and its conjugate base, or vice versa. Its pH changes very little when a small amount of strong acid or base is added to it.

An acidic buffer solution--- is simply one which has a pH less than 7. Acidic buffer solutions are commonly made from a weak acid and one of its salts - often a sodium salt. A common example would be a mixture of ethanoic acid and sodium ethanoate in solution

20. what is the meaning of titration?​

Answer:

Titration, process of chemical analysis in which the quantity of some constituent of a sample is determined by adding to the measured sample an exactly known quantity of another substance with which the desired constituent reacts in a definite, known proportion. The process is usually carried out by gradually adding a standard solution (i.e., a solution of known concentration) of titrating reagent, or titrant, from a burette, essentially a long, graduated measuring tube with a stopcock and a delivery tube at its lower end. The addition is stopped when the equivalence point is reached.
