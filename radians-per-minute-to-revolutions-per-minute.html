1. A wheel with diameter 35 cm is rotating at 20 revolutions per minute. what is the speed of the rotating wheel in radians per second. This is the angular velocity of the wheel.


Since we are just being asked about giving the angular velocity (ω) of the rotating wheel in rad/s, we do not need to take into account the diameter of the wheel.

We only do that when the linear velocity is being asked. v=rω

Anyway.

So we are given with [tex]20 \frac{rev}{min} [/tex] and we want to make the unit as [tex] \frac{rad}{s} [/tex].

In order to do that, we just have to remember that 1 revolution=2π rad

Doing the necessary conversions:
[tex]20 \frac{rev}{min} * \frac{1 min}{60 s}* \frac{2 \pi rad}{1 rev} [/tex]

By doing the arithmetic you will be able to get the value of the angular velocity in rad/s.

10. If a tire rotates at 400 revolutions per minute when the car is traveling 72km/h, what is the circumference of the tire?


Answer:

Ans: C = 3m

Step-by-step explanation:

Formula: distance = Circumference x revolution

Convert

[tex]400 \frac{rev}{min} ( \frac{60min}{1hr} ) = 24000 \frac{rev}{hr} [/tex]

equate 24,000 rev/hr to the speed.

[tex]24000 \frac{rev}{hr} = 72 \frac{km}{hr} \\ 24000rev = 72km[/tex]

Substitute to the formula

d = C x rev

72km = C(24000)

72km(1000m/km) = C(24000)

72000m/24000 = C

C = 3m

25. Microwave ovens rotate at a rate of about 6 rev/min. What is this in revolutions per second? What is the angular velocity in radians per second?


Answer:

The microwave ovens rotate at a rate = 6 rev/min.

1 min = 60 seconds.

Therefore, the rotation of microwave ovens in revolutions per second is

[tex] = \frac{6rev}{60 \: s} = 0.1 \: rev )s.[/tex]

We know that,

[tex]1 \: revolution = 2\pi \:rad[/tex]

Thus, the angular velocity of microwave ovens,

[tex] = 0.1 \times 3.14 \: rad)s = 0.628 \: rad)s.[/tex]
