21. compare & contrast: sumerian astronomy and Egyptian astronomy​


Answer:

- Sumerian and Egyptian civilizations developed complex astronomical systems that were crucial to their societies.

- However, there are significant differences in the way they approached astronomy.

- Sumerians were more focused on predicting celestial events, such as eclipses and planetary movements, while Egyptians used their astronomical knowledge to develop a calendar system that could predict the flooding of the Nile River.

- The Sumerians also believed that the planets had divine powers and were associated with different gods, while Egyptians saw their gods as being connected to specific constellations.

- Despite these differences, both civilizations made significant contributions to astronomy and helped lay the foundation for modern astronomy today.

22. Search about the each HISTORY of 5 Branches of Physical Science: 1. ASTRONOMY 2. METEOROLOGY 3. CHEMISTRY 4. PHYSICS 5. GEOLOGY​


1. ASTRONOMY is the branch of physical science that studies celestial objects such as stars, planets, and galaxies. Its history can be traced back to ancient civilizations such as the Babylonians and Egyptians who observed and recorded the movements of celestial bodies.

2. METEOROLOGY is the branch of physical science that deals with the study of the Earth's atmosphere and its phenomena such as weather and climate. Its history dates back to ancient times when people observed and recorded weather patterns.

3. CHEMISTRY is the branch of physical science that deals with the composition, structure, properties, and reactions of matter. Its history can be traced back to ancient civilizations such as the Greeks and Egyptians who studied the properties of materials.

4. PHYSICS is the branch of physical science that deals with the study of matter, energy, and their interactions. Its history can be traced back to ancient Greece where philosophers such as Aristotle and Archimedes studied motion and mechanics.

5. GEOLOGY is the branch of physical science that deals with the study of the Earth's structure, composition, and processes. Its history can be traced back to ancient civilizations such as the Chinese and Greeks who studied rocks and minerals.

Answer:

While stars are the main long-lasting sources of visible light in space, the light that they emit can sometimes reflect off other objects. The most noticeable example is the Moon, which reflects the light from the Sun. The colors and spectra of such objects are the same as those of the primary source of light, for instance the Moon has a color similar to that of the Sun and the same spectrum. The Moon does not generate its own light, and the amount of the surface we see depends on the angle from which we are observing it. When the Moon is between the Earth and the Sun, we cannot see the Moon at all.

The light from other planets in the solar system is also reflected light from the Sun. All planets reflect the light from their star, which is the only way that we can see them with our eyes. However, most planets are so far away that the light they reflect is overwhelmed by the direct light shining from their star. Maybe one day an astronomer will be able to figure out another way to see these planets using visible light.

There is a class of nebulae in the galaxy that only reflects light, and these are naturally named reflection nebulae. In these cases light is reflected off small dust particles like small mirrors, changing its direction and allowing us to see the clouds containing the dust. As with the case of the planets and their host star, the reflected light appears to be much fainter than the light from the original source.
