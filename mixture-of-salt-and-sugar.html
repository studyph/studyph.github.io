16. SALT AND SUGAR WHAT MIXTURE IS THAT​


Answer:

Mixtures are made up of two or more substances or components, these mixtures possess variable composition. They can be classified as:

1. Homogeous Mixtures:These Mixtures have same composition                throughout, but they still possess variable composition,i.e., they do not have a clear ratio between their volumes or mole fraction they possess, (For example, the sugar solution made by a person in London will not have exact mole fraction and neither have same amount of sugar dissolved in same amount of water as and when compared with sugar solution made by a person in New Zealand, in contrast molar mass of Hydrogen will remain 1.0080 amu no matter from where you take it.) They also have an indistinguishable line of separation between dissolved constituents.

2.Heterogeneous Mixtures:These mixture do not have same composition throughout, (For example, mixture of sand and water can be clearly indicated as after the mud settles the sand is at the bottom of the beaker and water at the top), also they have variable composition. The line of separation between two constituents is seen clearly.

Hence if you apply this concept to your question, this clearly states that the mixture of salt and sugar is Heterogeneous Mixture as Sugar and salt can be clearly identified and the salt and sugar are not having same composition throughout the mixture.
