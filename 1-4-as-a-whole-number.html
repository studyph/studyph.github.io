28. The product of 2 whole numbers is 36, and their ratio is 1:4. Which of these is the smaller number?


The smaller number of two numbers which ratio is 1 : 4 and the product of their numbers is 36 is 3.

The definition of ratio

Ratio is a formula for comparing units. They can be used as equivalent ratios to help  scale the numbers. For example, the amount of ingredients for baking a cake. Mathematically, this can be used to solve the problem of direct ratios where the units increase or decrease by the same ratio.

Based on the definition, we can solve the problem:

First, we assume the first number with variable [tex]x[/tex] and second number with variable [tex]y[/tex]write the sentence "The product of 2 whole numbers is 36" into a mathematical statement: [tex]xy=36[/tex]The ratio between first number and second number is 1 : 4Next we check the possibility of the multiplication of two numbers with the ratio 1 ; 4(Multiply the ratio with 1) =

first number [tex](x)[/tex] = 1 × 1 = 1

second number [tex](y)[/tex]= 4 × 1 = 4

Then we multiply the first number with second number [tex]xy[/tex] = 1 × 4 = 4

this possibility is wrong because it is known that the multiplication result is 36.

2. (Multiply the ratio with 2) =

first number [tex](x)[/tex] = 1 × 2 = 2

second number [tex](y)[/tex] = 4 × 2 = 8

Then we multiply the first number with second number [tex]xy[/tex] = 2 × 8 = 16

this possibility is wrong because it is known that the multiplication result is 36.

3. (Multiply the ratio with 3) =

first number [tex](x)[/tex] = 1 × 3 = 3

second number [tex](y)[/tex] = 4 × 3 = 12

Then we multiply the first number with second number [tex]xy[/tex]  = 3 × 12 = 36

this possibility is right because it is known that the multiplication result is 36.

Based on checking on existing possibilities, we can conclude that [tex]x = 3[/tex] and [tex]y=12[/tex].

Then the smaller number is 3.
