Answer:

To solve this problem, we need to consider two cases: selecting exactly 2 black rubber shoes and selecting exactly 3 black rubber shoes.

Case 1: Selecting exactly 2 black rubber shoes

To select 2 black rubber shoes out of 3, we can use combinations:

C(3,2) = 3

Next, we need to select 3 rubber shoes from the remaining 7 rubber shoes (5 red and 2 blue). We can use combinations again:

C(7,3) = 35

Therefore, the total number of ways to make a selection with exactly 2 black rubber shoes is:

3 x 35 = 105

Case 2: Selecting exactly 3 black rubber shoes

To select 3 black rubber shoes out of 3, we only have one option.

Next, we need to select 2 rubber shoes from the remaining 7 rubber shoes (5 red and 2 blue). We can use combinations:

C(7,2) = 21

Therefore, the total number of ways to make a selection with exactly 3 black rubber shoes is:

1 x 21 = 21

Finally, we add the results from the two cases to get the total number of ways to make a selection with at least 2 black rubber shoes:

105 + 21 = 126

Therefore, there are 126 ways for Jacob to make his selection of 5 rubber shoes from his collection if his selection must contain at least 2 black rubber shoes.
