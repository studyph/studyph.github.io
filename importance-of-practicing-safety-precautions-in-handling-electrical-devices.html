Answer:

The reason why there is a need to practice safety in handling electrical devices: is that we need people to recognize the importance of educating one another about the safety procedures and ground rules that should be followed when dealing with electricity. Electricity can be very risky to work with, and a slight error or irresponsibility can cost you your life. These types of incidents can occur in various instances. Through direct contact with electrical energy, electricity can travel through the body can interfere with regular electrical signals between the brain and our muscles. This can lead to instances where the heart may stop beating regularly, breathing may stop and other bodily function may shut down.  

Electrical injuries are classified into these categories. Any electrical circuit has the potential to cause damage.

The following are the most common forms of electrical current injuries:

electrocution, electric shock, burns, and falls
