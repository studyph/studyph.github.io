9. body at rest:law of inertia::pushing a chair


#BrainlyFast

Law of Inertia

Law of Inertia states that:

An object at rest will remain at rest unless acted upon by a net force. Net force is an unbalanced force which causes the object at rest to move. An object in motion will remain in motion unless acted upon by a net force. Net force or unbalanced force causes the object to stop moving.

Inertia is directly proportional to the mass of the object which resist change in motion. When the mass is doubled, the Inertia will also be doubled and you need to have greater force to move the object at rest or to stop the object in motion.

Pushing a chair is an example of "The law of Acceleration"

Net force acted on the chair as you pushed it making it move in a certain direction. Force can make the object at rest to move and can make the moving object to stop. Force is directly proportional to acceleration and mass.

12. Suppose you are pushing a chair with a 500 newton force along a 7-meter aisle. How much work is done in pushing the chair from one end of the aisleto the other?


Answer:

The work done in pushing the cart is 3500 J

Explanation:

In this problem, we are to solve the work done in pushing the cart. To do this, we need to get the product of the pushing force to the distance it has traveled.

Formula to use

W = F·d

where

W     is the work, the unit is in Newton-meter (N·m) or Joules (J)

F      is the force, the unit is in Newton (N)

d      is the distance, the unit is in meter (m)

Given information

F = 500 N

d = 7 m

W = ?

Solving the problem

Let us solve the work using the given formula

W = F·d

Then, substitute the given data

W = (500N)(7m)

W = 3500 N·m or

W = 3500 J

Therefore, the work done in pushing the cart is 3500 J.

Suppose you are pushing a chair with a 500 Newton force along a 700 meter aisle. How much work is done in pushing the chair from one end of the aisle to the other.

Given:

Force - 500 NDistance - 700 m

Answer & Solution:

[tex]\large\rm W=fd[/tex]

[tex]\large\rm W=(500 \: N)(700 \: m)[/tex]

[tex]\large\rm W=350,000 \: N.m[/tex]
