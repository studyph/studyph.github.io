5. Find the volume of the prism. A hexagonal prism with height of 25 millimeters and base area labeled “B equals 197 square millimeters”.


The formula for the volume of a prism is V = Bh, where B is the area of the base and h is the height.

In this problem, the base is a hexagon with an area of 197 square millimeters. To find the height, we need more information about the shape of the prism.

Assuming that the hexagonal prism is regular (meaning all six sides of the base are equal), we can use the formula for the area of a regular hexagon:

A = (3√3 / 2) s²

where s is the length of one side of the hexagon.

If the base area is 197 square millimeters, we can set this formula equal to 197 and solve for s:

197 = (3√3 / 2) s²

s² = 197 / (3√3 / 2)

s² = 131.3333

s ≈ 8.111

Now we can use the height given in the problem, h = 25 millimeters, along with the area of the base, B = 197 square millimeters, to calculate the volume of the prism:

V = Bh

V = (197)(25)

V = 4925 cubic millimeters

Therefore, the volume of the hexagonal prism is 4925 cubic millimeters.

3D Shapes

Prisms

A prism is a polyhedron for which the top and bottom faces (known as the bases) are congruent polygons, and all other faces (known as the lateral faces) are rectangles. (Technically, when the sides are rectangles, the shape is known as a right prism, indicating that the lateral faces meet the sides of the base at right angles. In this lesson, when we use the term prism, we mean a right prism. But there are other types of prisms, too.)

A prism is described by the shape of its base. For instance, a rectangular prism has bases that are rectangles, and a pentagonal prism has bases that are pentagons.
