b. The number of ways to select 5 white balls from 7 white balls is 7C5 (7 combinations of 5) = 21.

c. The number of ways to select 5 black balls from 4 black balls is 4C5 (4 combinations of 5) = 1.

d. The number of ways to select 3 blue, 1 white and 1 black is 3C3 (3 combinations of 3) x 7C1 (7 combinations of 1) x 4C1 (4 combinations of 1) = 84.

e. The number of ways to select 3 black and 2 any color is 4C3 (4 combinations of 3) x 9C2 (9 combinations of 2) + 7C2 (7 combinations of 2) = 120.

We need to choose 5 blue balls out of 9. This can be done using the combination formula, which is:

nCr = n! / (r! * (n-r)!)

*where n is the total number of items, r is the number of items to be chosen, and ! represents the factorial operation. Using this formula, we get:

9C5 = 9! / (5! * (9-5)!) = 9! / (5! * 4!)= (98765!) / (543214321) = 126

Therefore, there are 126 ways to select 5 balls such that they are all blue.

Number of ways to choose 1 black ball out of 4: 4C1 = 4
