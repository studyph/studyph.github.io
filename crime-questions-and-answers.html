23. INTRODUCTION TO CRIMINOLOGYQUESTION:Explain why sociological factor has something to do with crime commission.​


Answer:

Sociological factors play a significant role in the commission of crime. These factors include social, economic, and cultural conditions that can contribute to an individual's likelihood of committing a crime.

For example, poverty and unemployment can increase the likelihood of crime, as individuals may turn to criminal activity as a means of survival or financial gain. Social inequality, discrimination, and marginalization can also contribute to crime, as individuals may feel excluded from society and may lack access to legitimate opportunities for success.

Additionally, cultural factors such as the presence of a criminal subculture or the glorification of criminal behavior in media can also influence an individual's likelihood of committing a crime. These sociological factors can interact and reinforce each other, creating a cycle of crime and social disadvantage.

Overall, sociological factors are an important aspect to consider when examining the commission of crime and developing strategies for crime prevention.
