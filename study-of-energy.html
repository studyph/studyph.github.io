7. cite the contribution of different scientists to the study of energy​


The word “energy” comes from the Greek enérgeia.

Developed by Aristotle (384 BC − 322 BC), enérgeia has no direct translation to English, although it is frequently described as “being at work”.

Although the term English “energy” acquired its current definition (meaning the quantitative property that must be transferred to an object to perform work or heat the object) in the 19th century, the ideas behind the concept began forming at the end of the 17th century, when the term was first used in English to refer to “power”.

By 1686, Gottfried Wilhelm von Leibniz (1646 − 1716), had developed concepts that correspond to our current understanding of kinetic and potential mechanical energy.

However, he didn’t use the term “energy”.

Thomas Young (1773 − 1829) first introduced the word “energy” to the field of physics in 1800, but the word did not gain popularity.

Thomas Young later established the wave nature of light through interference experiments.

15. It is the study of transformation of energy in all living things.


Bioenergetics

Answer:

The study of transformation of energy in all living things is called Bioenergetics. Living organisms survive or live off of energy transformations, by the means of direct intake of nutrients or by receiving it without the need of transmutation. Like, in plants the energy needed for their growth and photosynthesis is sunlight. These types of organisms are examples of Bioenergetics, like so:

Autotrophs- organisms that doesn’t need to break down the chemicals or nutrients received as it is directly consumed. Heterotrophs- these organisms can’t supply their needs of nutrients and chemicals, so they receive it from other resources or organisms.

Examples of Bioenergetics:

Catabolism- the breaking down of molecules into much smaller units Anabolism- construction of smaller units of molecules

Bioenergetics in humans is a study of energy transformation and how it spreads throughout the body for biological purposes such us the necessities of every organ for daily functions. For plants or autotrophs, bioenergetics is the means of acquiring energy also for the same purpose for growth and development.

18. it is the study of all forms of energy​


Answer:

C

Chemical Energy - is really a form of potential energy and is the energy stored in food, gasoline or chemical combinations.

Examples: Striking a match, combining vinegar and baking soda to form CO2 Gas, breaking light sticks releases chemical energy.

R

Radiant Energy - is a combination of heat and light energy. Light energy, like sound energy, travels out in all directions in waves.

Examples: A light bulb, the glowing coils on a toaster, the sun, and even headlights on cars.

E

Electrical Energy - Energy produced by electrons moving through a substance is known as electrical energy. We mostly see electric energy in batteries and from the outlets in our homes. Electrical energy lights our homes, run motors, and makers our TVs and radios work.

Examples: CD players, TVs and Video games.

A

Atomic Energy - is produced when you split atoms. A tremendous amount of energy is released when this happens.

Examples:Atomic bombs, nuclear power plants, nuclear submarines, and the sun.

M

Mechanical Energy - is moving energy. It is the form that we most see around us. All moving objects produce mechanical energy. The movements within machines is also mechanical energy.

Examples: People, a rolling bicycle, moving gears, and running cars.

✏️ANSWER✏️

The study of conversions between thermal energy and other forms of energy is called thermodynamics. The first law of thermodynamics states that energy is conserved. The second law of thermodynamics states that thermal energy can flow from colder objects to hotter objects only if work is done on the system.
