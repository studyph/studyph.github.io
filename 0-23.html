3. What is an equation whose roots are 5+√2 and 5-√2?​A. x²-10x+23=0B. x²+10x+23=0C. x²+10x-23=0D. x²-10x-23=0​


x² - 10x + 23 = 0.

Step-by-step explanation:

The roots are given by

[tex]x = 5 + \sqrt{2} \\ x = 5 - \sqrt{2} [/tex]

then set each equation to 0 by transposing 5 ± √2.

[tex]x - 5 + \sqrt{2} = 0 \\ x - 5 - \sqrt{2} = 0[/tex]

Since both of the trinomials equal 0, their product must also equal to 0. So, we set their product equal to 0.

[tex](x - 5 + \sqrt{2} )(x - 5 - \sqrt{2} ) = 0[/tex]

Then, distribute each term in the first group to the terms in the second group.

[tex] {x}^{2} - 5x - x \sqrt{2} - 5x + 25 + 5 \sqrt{2} + x \sqrt{2} - 5 \sqrt{2} - 2[/tex]

Combine like terms.

[tex] {x}^{2} - 10x + 23 = 0[/tex]

Therefore, the quadratic equation whose roots are 5 ± √2 is x² - 10x + 23 = 0.
