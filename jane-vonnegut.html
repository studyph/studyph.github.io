5. In a basketball game, Jane and Francine made 20 baskets. Jane scored 4 times as many baskets as francine. how many baskets did Jane score​


Answer:

Jane's score is 16.

Step-by-step explanation:

Let x be Francine's score and 4x be Jane's.

[tex]\\ \sf{} 4x + x = 20[/tex]

Find Francine's score first:

[tex]\\ \sf{} 4x + x = 20[/tex]

[tex]\\ \sf{} 5x = 20[/tex]

[tex]\\ \sf{} x = \frac{20}{5} [/tex]

[tex]\\ \large \boxed{ \sf{} x = 4} \\ \\ \\ [/tex]

Francine's score is 4.

Now that we've found Francine's score, let's find Jane's:

[tex]\\ \sf{} = 4x[/tex]

[tex]\\ \sf{} = 4(4)[/tex]

[tex]\\ \large \boxed{\sf{} = 16} \\ \\ [/tex]

Jane's score is 16.

Check to verify:

[tex]\\ \sf{} 16 + 4 = 20[/tex]

[tex]\\ \sf{} 20 = 20 \\ \\ \\ [/tex]

26. Tess is twice as old as jane and jane is three times older than bing


Bing is x. On the other hand, Jane's age is 3x while Tess's age is 6x which is twice as old as Jane's. This can be solved using operations on algebraic expressions.

Solution:

Let's use "x" to represent Bing's age. Then we can use the information given in the problem to determine the ages of Jane and Tess.

Jane is three times older than Bing:
            Jane's age = 3x

Tess is twice as old as Jane:
            Tess's age = 2 * Jane's age
                               = 2 * 3x
                               = 6x

If we already have Jane's and Tess's age, it would be easier for us to find what is Bong's age. Therefore, the ages of the three individuals are:

Bing's age = x

Jane's age = 3x

Tess's age = 6x

Application:

For example, if we know that Bing is 10 years old, then Jane is 3 times older than Bing, or 30 years old, and Tess is twice as old as Jane, or 60 years old. So the ages of the three individuals would be:

Bing's age = 10

Jane's age = 30

Tess's age = 60

We just need to substitute any number from the x to get the respective ages of the three individuals.
