29. When someone sees a blue hat, all the light is absorbed into the hat EXCEPT the blue; making what we see as the blue hat. true or false


TRUE

If we see the hat as blue, this only means that all the colors were absorbed by the hat except for the blue light, which in turn be reflected by the hat. The reflected blue light will enter the eyes and hit the cone of the eyes. Cone are light-sensitive cells located at the back of the retina of the eyes, which is responsible for color vision and signals the optic nerve that will relay the information to the cortex of the brain, which then process the information and perceived it as blue.  

Thus the statement above stating that “When someone sees a blue hat, all the light is absorbed into the hat EXCEPT the blue; making what we see as the blue hat” is correct.
