30. an 81cm2 square is divided into 9 smaller congruent squares what is the perimeter of one of these smaller squares


Answer:

If an 81 cm² square is divided into 9 congruent squares, then each smaller square must have an area of:

81 cm² ÷ 9 = 9 cm²

Let x be the length of one side of the smaller square. Then, the area of the smaller square can also be expressed as:

x² = 9 cm²

Taking the square root of both sides, we get:

x = 3 cm

Therefore, each smaller square has a perimeter of:

4x = 4(3 cm) = 12 cm

So the perimeter of one of these smaller squares is 12 cm.
