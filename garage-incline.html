1. A 1500-kg car is ascending the ramp of a garage inclined at 10° with constant speed when the force applied is P. determine the magnitude of force P if u=0.70



[tex]moving \: up \: a \: ramp \: at \: uniform \: speed : \\ P = F(friction) + F(parallel) \\ \\ F(parallel).component \: of \: car \: weight \: along \: the \: ramp \\ F(para) = mgsin10 \\F(para) = (1500kg)(9.8066 \frac{m}{ {s}^{2} } )sin10 \\ F(para) = 2554.3473 \: N \\ \\ F(perpendicular). \: component \: of \: car \: weight \: perpendicular \: to \: the \: ramp \\ F(perp) = mgcos10 \\ F(perp) = (1500kg)(9.8066 \frac{m}{ {s}^{2} } )cos10 \\ F(perp) = 14486.4236 \: N \\ \\ F(normal) = F(perpendicular) = 14486.4236 \: N \\ \\ F(friction) = μ(F(normal)) \\ F(friction) = 0.7(14486.4236 N ) \\ F(frict) = 10140.4965 \: N \\ \\ P = F(friction) + F(parallel) \\ P = 10140.4965N + 2554.3473N \\ P = 12694.8438 \: N[/tex]

9. A rectangular garage has a volume of 480 m^3, a length of 12 m and a width of 8 m. what is the height of the garage?


Volume

[tex] \normalsize\blue{\overline{\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad\quad \ \ \ }}[/tex]

Problem:

A rectangular garage has a volume of 480 m³, a length of 12 m and a width of 8 m. what is the height of the garage?

Answer:

[tex] \huge \underline{\boxed{\sf \red{5m}}}[/tex]

To find the missing dimension of a rectangular prism, you have to multiply the given dimensions then divide the product to the total volume.

Use this formula to find the missing dimension[tex]\swarrow[/tex]

[tex]\large\bold{ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \frac{Volume}{(Length)(Width)} = Height }[/tex]

Let's start solving.

[tex] \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \tt \large \frac{volume}{(length)(width)} = height \\ \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \tt \large \frac{ {480m}^{3} }{(12m)(8m)} = height \\ \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \tt \large \frac{ {480m}^{3} }{96m} = \: x \\ \\ \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \: \tt \large \green5 = \: x [/tex]

Therefore, 5 is the missing dimension or the value of the height.

Checking, we are multiplying all the dimensions together then if the product and the volume are the same, the answer is correct.

[tex]\large\tt{V = (L)(W)(H) }[/tex][tex]\large\tt{V = (12m)(8m)(5m) = 480^{3}}[/tex][tex]\large\tt{V = (96m)(5m) = 480^{3}}[/tex][tex]\large\tt{V = \green4\green8\green0^{3} = 480^{3}}[/tex]

So, our answer is correct.
