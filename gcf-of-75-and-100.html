How to find greatest common factor?
To find the greatest common factor of two numbers for example, first, determine the factors of each of the numbers, then see for the greatest number among all the factors that could divide both the two numbers.

To find the GCF of two numbers:

- List the prime factors of each number.

- Multiply those factors both numbers have in common.

If there are no common prime factors, the GCF is 1.

20. gcf and Lcm of 25 and 75​


Gcf of 25 and 75 is 25

For 25 and 75 those factors look like this:

Factors for 25: 1, 5, and 25

Factors for 75: 1, 3, 5, 15, 25, and 75

As you can see when you list out the factors of each number, 25 is the greatest number that 25 and 75 divides into.

Lcm of 25 and 75 is 75

Steps to find LCM

Find the prime factorization of 25

25 = 5 × 5

Find the prime factorization of 75

75 = 3 × 5 × 5

Multiply each factor the greater number of times it occurs in steps i) or ii) above to find the LCM:

LCM = 3 × 5 × 5

LCM = 75
