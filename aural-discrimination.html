28. identifies aurally and visually different instruments in 1 rondalla​


Answer:

A rondalla is a traditional Filipino musical ensemble composed of stringed instruments. Here are some of the most commonly used instruments in a typical rondalla and how they can be identified both aurally and visually:

1. Bandurria - A small, pear-shaped stringed instrument with 14 strings. It has a bright, plucky sound and can be identified by its distinctive tuning pegs and frets on the neck.

2. Laud - Similar to the bandurria, but larger and with more strings (typically 12 or 14). It has a rich, warm sound and can be identified by its long neck and large body.

3. Octavina - A small, four-stringed instrument that produces a higher-pitched sound than the bandurria or laud. It can be identified by its short neck and small body.

4. Guitar - A six-stringed instrument with a deep, resonant sound. It can be identified by its familiar shape and size.

5. Bass Guitar - A larger version of the guitar with four strings and a deeper, more booming sound. It can be identified by its size and shape, as well as the tuning pegs and frets on the neck.

Explanation:

When played together in a rondalla, these instruments create a rich, harmonious sound that is unique to Filipino music.
