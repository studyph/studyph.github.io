12. What is the prime factorization of 80? ​


Brainliest

#libreangmatuto

Answer:

2⁴x5

Step-by-step explanation:

A prime factorization is a operation of multiplied prime numbers that equal the factorized number. Prime numbers are infinite, but some of them are 2, 3, 5, 7, 11.

If have to try to get to 80 by multiplying values from this list. We can't get there by multiplying just 2, therefore we'll have to use other numbers. Doing 2x2x2x2x5 we can get 80. To simplify this, 2x2x2x2 is equal to 2⁴, therefore, the answer is 2⁴x5.

The rule for prime factoring is to get to the number we are factorizing by multiplying prime numbers, tryong to always use the smaller prime numbers.

The gcf of 50 and 80 can be obtained like this: The factors of 50 are 50, 25, 10, 5, 2, 1. The factors of 80 are 80, 40, 20, 16, 10, 8, 5, 4, 2, 1. The common factors of 50 and 80 are 10, 5, 2, 1, intersecting the two sets above.
