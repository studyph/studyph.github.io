3. Summary of The Sydney Opera House


The Sydney Opera House

Sydney Opera House is an opera house located in Port Jackson, New South Wales, Australia. It has a unique shape with the roof using a series of white sail-shaped shells. Because of its unique shape, it is awarded as one of the world’s most famous and distinctive buildings and a breakthrough in architecture. This breathtaking building was designed by Danish architect Jorn Utzon, but finished by an Australian architectural team led by Peter Hall.

On October 20th, 1973, Queen Elizabeth II formally opened the Sydney Opera house after Utzon was chosen as the winner of an international design competition in 1957. The structure and its surroundings take up the entirety of Bennelong Point on Sydney Harbour, between Farm Cove and Sydney Cove, close to the Sydney Harbour Bridge and the Royal Botanic Gardens. The structure has many performance spaces that collectively stage well over 1,500 acts each year, drawing more than 1.2 million spectators. Numerous performing artists, including three resident companies—Opera Australia, the Sydney Theatre Company, and the Sydney Symphony Orchestra—present performances.

Sydney Opera House is one of the most popular places in Australia, it is visited by people that reach more than eight million, and 350,00 visitors take a guided tour each year. It is managed and supervised under the Sydney Opera House Trust, an agency of the New South Wales State Government. On June 28th, 2007, because of its enchanting structure, the Sydney Opera House be appointed a UNESCO World Heritage Site. The Opera House was also a finalist for the list of the New 7 Wonders of the World.
