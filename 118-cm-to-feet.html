23. successive perfect square of √118​

Question:

What is the preceding and successive perfect square of √118

Answer:

a 9

b 16

Step-by-step explanation:

The preceding perfect square of √118 is the perfect square before √118.

On the other hand. the successive perfect square of √118 is the next perfect square of √118.

Let's approximate √118 first.

We know that 10 = √100 and 11 = √121.

Thus, the value of √118 must be between 10 and 11.

Now, we list all perfect square numbers until we reach the preceding and successive perfect square of √118.

1² = 1

2² = 4

3² = 9

4² = 16

5² = 25

6² = 36

and so on.

Notice that 9 and 16 are the perfect square before and after √118.

Therefore, 9 is the preceding perfect square of √118, while its successive perfect square is 16
