3. I Shall Return; by Claude McKay I shall return again; I shall return To laugh and love and watch with wonder-eyes At golden noon the forest fires burn, Wafting their blue-black smoke to sapphire skies. I shall return to loiter by the streams That bathe the brown blades of the bending grasses, And realize once more my thousand dreams Of waters rushing down the mountain passes. I shall return to hear the fiddle and fife Of village dances, dear delicious tunes That stir the hidden depths of native life, Stray melodies of dim remembered runes. I shall return, I shall return again, To ease my mind of long, long years of pain. 1. Describe the persona. What is he or she yearning for? 2. How does the poem describe the land of Africa? What does this imply about the feelings of the persona? 3. What figures of speech are present in the poem? What do they mean? 4. What is the overall theme and message of the poem?


Answer:

1. The persona in the poem is a person who is longing to return to Africa, their homeland. The persona misses the beauty and culture of Africa, and yearns to experience it again.

2. The poem describes Africa as a place of natural beauty, with forests, streams, and mountain passes. The persona fondly remembers the sounds of village dances and the music of the fiddle and fife. This implies that the persona has strong feelings of nostalgia and longing for their homeland.

3. The poem contains several figures of speech, including personification (the forest fires "burn"), metaphor (the "sapphire skies"), and imagery (the "brown blades of the bending grasses"). These figures of speech help to create a vivid and evocative picture of Africa in the reader's mind, and convey the persona's strong feelings of longing for their homeland.

4. The overall theme of the poem is the longing for home and the desire to return to one's roots. The persona speaks of their desire to experience the beauty and culture of Africa once again, and the poem suggests that this longing is driven by a sense of nostalgia and a desire to reconnect with one's heritage. The message of the poem is that our roots and our sense of belonging are important to us, and that we may yearn to return to the places that hold special meaning to us.
