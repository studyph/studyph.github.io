11. 1.The mean height of men aged 20 to 30 in a city is normally distributed with a mean of 67 inches and a variance of 16. Define the sampling distribution of the sample mean with a sample size of 400.In this example, the populations mean μ is equal to 67. Thus, μM is also equal to 67. For the standard error of the mean, it can be noticed that n=400 and σ=4. Solve for σM. ​


Answer:

The standard error of the mean (σM) is calculated as follows:

σM = σ / √n

= 4 / √400

= 4 / 20

= 0.2

So, the standard error of the mean for a sample size of 400 is 0.2 inches.

This means that if we repeatedly draw random samples of size 400 from the population of men aged 20 to 30 in the city and calculate the mean height for each sample, the means will be normally distributed with a mean of 67 inches and a standard deviation of 0.2 inches. This is the sampling distribution of the sample mean.
