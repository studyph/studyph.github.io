How many will store A and store B​


He can divide the remainder for store B and store C equally (evenly).

Shop A gets a share of 25 canvases, and store B gets 50 canvases.

Step-by-step explanation:

Be discovered:

Abigail has a total of 125 canvas.

Abigail plans to distribute to three stores.

Abigail gives 1/5 of what she has store A.

The rest is distributed to Shop B and Shop C equally.

Question:

Can she did the remaining equally to stores B and C?

How many will store A and store B.

Asked:

The first step, we find the number of canvases that are deposited in store A.

A = 1/5 x 125

A = 25 canvas.

The second step we find the rest of the canvas.

Remaining canvas = 125 - 25

Remaining canvas = 100 canvases

The final step is to find the number of canvases that are deposited in store B.

Because the amount deposited into store B and store C is the same, then we divide the remaining canvas by 2.

B = 100 : 2

B = 50 canvas.

He can divide the remainder for store B and store C equally (evenly).

Shop A gets a share of 25 canvases, and store B gets 50 canvases.
