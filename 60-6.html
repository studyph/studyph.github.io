27. Aling Maring, when asked about her age, replies, "I am six years older than twice the age of my youngest child". Express her age in algebraic equation if her age is 60. A. 2x + 6 = 60 B. 2a > 6 = 60 C. 6 - 2x = 60 D. 2a < 6 = 60


PROBLEM

Aling Maring, when asked about her age, she replied, "I am six years older than twice the age of my youngest child". Express her age in algebraic equation if her child's age is 60 years old.

ANSWER

The Algebraic expression is 2x + 6 = 60

SOLUTION

Let x be the age of Aling Maring's youngest child:

x = Aling Maring's Age

The first statement is "six years older", thus, we need to add 6 to the age of Aling Maring (x), we will get:

x + 6

However, it is stated "twice the age of my youngest child", so the age of Aling Maring (x) should be multiplied by 2 before adding 6 years. Hence, the expression would now be:

2x + 6

Lastly, the age of Aling Maring's youngest chils is 60. Hence, we will equate it to the obtained expression of Aling Maring's age (2x + 6)

∴ The equation for the given problem is 2x + 6 = 60

Note: The problem is a bit vague. So, I rephrased the question in a way that everyone might easily undersrand it.
