1. classify membranous and non membranous organelle of the cell​


Answer:

Main Difference – Membranous vs Nonmembranous Organelles

Most organelles like mitochondria, plastids, endoplasmic reticulum, and Golgi apparatus are fluid-filled structures. On that account, fluid-filled organelles are membranous. Nonmembranous organelles are ribosomes, cytoskeleton, nucleolus, and centrosome.

Here are the steps which lead to cell division (Mitosis).

The  cell decides to divide

The Ribosomes are released from the nucleus

Once outside the nucleus, they begin to make or synthesize proteins.

The Ribosomes float around the Cytoplasm or attach to the rough endoplasmic reticulum.

The proteins are then released by the Endoplasmic reticulum encapsulated within vesicles.

The proteins then head to the Golgi Apparatus and enter through its membrane.

The cells are then customized into whatever form is needed by the cell.

This entire process, adds the materials needed to reproduce another cell.

After the chromosomes are copied, appearing as what is known as the sister chromatids.

Then the centrioles move towards the opposite sides of the cell and proceed to pull the chromosomes apart.

The cell division is then complete.
