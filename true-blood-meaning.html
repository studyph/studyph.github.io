AFRICA, MY AFRICADiop started with the possessive adjective 'My' to indicate the identity he connects himself with within Africa. It's a patriotic opening salvo of belongingness and patriotism to one's race, country, and land. Diop used a lot of personifications as if he is talking to Africa as a real person. Like 'you', yours'. The tackles African challenges brought about by European colonization. It also sends a message to Africans that change and independence are possible. The poem's appeal stems from its candid depiction of African ailments and their cures. Mostly, filled with negative ideas that root back to history but also positive notes that describe the beauty of the continent.The words that describe African landscapes such as "Savannahs", "fields", and "riverbanks".The stanzas here are vivid representations of slavery and the dehumanization of African people at the hands of colonialists and imperialists. The most known punishment against black slaves is whipped. Whipping is a form of dehumanization which happens to horses and other animals. Horses, cows, and others are whipped so they will follow their masters. Diop describes the endless labor of Africans. Diop describes that the beaten and scarred back is not bent and broken. It has gotten stronger.In this poetry, black blood represents African nature because there is no black blood. So he demonstrates that, despite growing up in France, he is nonetheless cognizant of his African identity.
