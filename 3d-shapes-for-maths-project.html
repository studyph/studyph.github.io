11. How to solve the are of the 3d shape give examples


Answer:

To find the surface area of a 3D shape, you need to add up the areas of all of its faces. The formula for calculating the surface area of different 3D shapes varies depending on the shape. Here are some examples:

Cube: The surface area of a cube is given by 6 x s², where s is the length of one of its sides. For example, if the cube has a side length of 5 cm, its surface area would be 6 x 5² = 150 cm².

Rectangular prism: The surface area of a rectangular prism is given by 2lw + 2lh + 2wh, where l, w, and h are the length, width, and height of the prism, respectively. For example, if a rectangular prism has dimensions of 4 cm x 3 cm x 2 cm, its surface area would be 2(4x3) + 2(4x2) + 2(3x2) = 52 cm².

Sphere: The surface area of a sphere is given by 4πr², where r is the radius of the sphere. For example, if a sphere has a radius of 2 cm, its surface area would be 4π(2²) = 16π cm².

Cylinder: The surface area of a cylinder is given by 2πr² + 2πrh, where r is the radius of the base of the cylinder and h is its height. For example, if a cylinder has a radius of 3 cm and a height of 5 cm, its surface area would be 2π(3²) + 2π(3x5) = 94.25 cm².

These are just a few examples of how to calculate the surface area of 3D shapes. Remember to use the correct formula for the shape you are calculating, and make sure to double-check your work to avoid errors.

17. How to solve the are of the 3d shape


To find the area of a 3D shape, you need to first determine which shape you are dealing with. The formula for finding the area of a 3D shape varies depending on the shape. Here are some common 3D shapes and their respective area formulas:

Cube: The surface area of a cube is given by 6s², where s is the length of a side.

Rectangular prism: The surface area of a rectangular prism is given by 2lw + 2lh + 2wh, where l is the length, w is the width, and h is the height.

Sphere: The surface area of a sphere is given by 4πr², where r is the radius.

Cylinder: The surface area of a cylinder is given by 2πr² + 2πrh, where r is the radius and h is the height.

Once you have identified the shape and its formula, simply plug in the appropriate values and solve for the area.
