Mitosis is a type of cell division that results in two daughter cells each having the same number and kind of chromosomes(a threadlike structure of nucleic acids and protein found in the nucleus of most living cells, carrying genetic information in the form of genes) as the parent nucleus, typical of ordinary tissue growth.

Answer:

D

Explanation:

The cell cycle is a 4-stage process consisting of Gap 1 (G1), Synthesis, Gap 2 (G2) and mitosis. The stages in the cell cycle between one mitosis and the next, which include G1, S and G2, are known collectively as the interphase.


15. What are the stages of the mitosis?


The stages of mitosis are prophase, prometaphase, metaphase, anaphase, and telophase.

Prophase  - Mitosis begins with this stage. In this stafe, chromosomes recruit condensin and begin to undergo a condensation process that will continue until metaphase. Prometaphase - begins with the fragmentation of the nuclear envelope into many  vesicles that will eventually be divided between the future daughter cells. It is an essential step for spindle assembly. At the end of this stage, the  kinetochores on sister chromatids are connected by microtubules to opposite poles of the spindle. Metaphase - during this stage, the centromeres of all the cell's chromosomes line up at the equator of the spindle. Anaphase -  in this stage, abrupt separation of sister chromatids is present.Telophase - stage at which the chromosomes reach the poles. The nuclear membrane reforms, and chromosomes  decondense into their interphase conformations.

29. 1) What are the differences between mitosis and meiosis? 2) Give the similarities between mitosis and meiosis. 3) What are the dangers of mitosis? 4) What is the importance of meiosis?


Answer:

1. Mitosis is a type of cell division for somatic cells and for the asexual reproduction of unicellular eukaryotic cells. Meiosis is the type of cell division for the production of gametes in sexual reproduction.

2. Both mitosis and meiosis involve cell division. Both the processes occur in the M-phase of the cell cycle. In both cycles, the typical stages are prophase, metaphase, anaphase and telophase. In both cycles, synthesis of DNA takes place.

3. Mistakes during mitosis lead to the production of daughter cells with too many or too few chromosomes, a feature known as aneuploidy.

4. Meiosis is important because it ensures that all organisms produced via sexual reproduction contain the correct number of chromosomes.
