1. How to solve GRESA po??


Answer:

Problem solving using the GRESA method

G - Given

R - Required

E - Equation

S - Solution

A - Answer

Ex. Find the density of an object that weighs 250g and with a volume of 50 cm3.

G - mass = 250g

volume = 50 cm3

R - density

E - density = mass / volume

S - 250g/50 cm3 = 5g/cm3

A - The density of the object is 5g / cm3.

Here is another example:

What is the volume of a wooden block with a length of 9 cm, a width of 3 cm, and height of 5 cm?

G - length = 9 cm

width = 3 cm

height = 5 cm

R - volume

E - volume = length x width x height

S - 9 cm x 3 cm x 5 cm = 135 cm3

A - The volume of the block is 135 cm3.

a dancer lifts his partner above his head with an acceleration of 2.5 m/s². the dancer exerts a force a 200 Newton. what is the mass of the partner?

The formula for Force is  ( F = m × a ), and to solve for mass we can have the formula ( m = F / a )

Given Data:

a ( acceleration ) = 2.5 m/s²

F ( Force ) = 200 N or 200 kg·m/s²

m ( mass ) = ? unknown

Solution:

We can use the formula of Force ( F = m × a ), and to solve for mass we can have the formula ( m = F / a )

m = F / a

 = 200 kg·m/s² / 2.5 m/s²

 = 80 kg

26. INSTRUCTIONS: USE THE GRESA method to solve the following problems on ACCELERATION ​


Answer:

G - Given:

1.Initial velocity, u = 90 km/hr

Final velocity, v = 50 km/hr

Time taken, t = 2.5 hrs

2.Initial velocity, u = 0 m/s

Final velocity, v = 25 m/s

Time taken, t = 5 s

3.Initial velocity, u = 72 km/hr

Final velocity, v = 0 km/hr

Time taken, t = 2 hrs

4.A cyclist:

Initial velocity, u = 25 km/hr

Final velocity, v = 40 km/hr

Time taken, t = 1.5 hrs

An athlete:

Initial velocity, u = 0 km/hr

Final velocity, v = 30 km/hr

Time taken, t = 1.5 hrs

R - Required:

1.Acceleration of the car

2.Acceleration of the bus

3.Acceleration of the truck

4.Greater acceleration between the cyclist and athlete

E - Equation:

1.Acceleration, a = (v-u)/t

2.Acceleration, a = (v-u)/t

3.Acceleration, a = (v-u)/t

4.Acceleration, a = (v-u)/t

S - Solution:

1.a = (50-90)/(2.5) = -16 km/hr^2 (Note: Negative sign indicates deceleration)

2.a = (25-0)/(5) = 5 m/s^2

3.a = (0-72)/(2) = -36 km/hr^2 (Note: Negative sign indicates deceleration)

4.For the cyclist: a = (40-25)/(1.5) = 10 km/hr^2

For the athlete: a = (30-0)/(1.5) = 20 km/hr^2

The athlete has a greater acceleration.

A - Answer:

1.The acceleration of the car is -16 km/hr^2.

2.The acceleration of the bus is 5 m/s^2.

3.The acceleration of the truck is -36 km/hr^2.
4.The athlete has a greater acceleration than the cyclist.
