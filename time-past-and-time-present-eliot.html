Though the term was coined around 1840 by painter and poet Washington Allston, the notion of an objective correlative, or a set of objects, images, or situations combined to evoke a particular emotion, was later popularized by T.S. Eliot beginning with his article 'Hamlet and His Problems' in 1919.
