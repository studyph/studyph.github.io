1. The graph shows two sound waves. What is true? A. The red wave will be higher-pitched than the blue. B. The red wave will be lower-pitched than the blue. C. The red and blue waves will have the same pitch. D. None Of The Above


ANSWER:A. The red wave will be higher-pitch than the blue.

EXPLANATION:

↝The height of the wave is the amplitude. The larger the amplitude, the louder the sound. The blue wave is louder and the red wave is softer.

↝The speed at which the cycle repeats is the frequency. The higher the frequency, The higher-pitch the sound. In the tume it takes the blue wave to go through 2 cycles, the red wave has gone through 5 cycles, so the red wave has a higher frequency and a higher-pitch sound.

Charged particles that are accelerating, like time-varying electric currents, are what produce radio waves.

   A transmitter produces a radio wave, which a receiver then picks up. A radio transmitter and receiver may both transmit and receive energy into and from space using an antenna. Typically, transmitters and receivers are made to function across a specific band of frequencies.

Stages for radio wave transmission between a transmitter and a receiver:

Radio waves are emitted by the transmitter when electrons flow up and down it.The air is traversed by radio waves at the speed of light.Electrons inside a receiver vibrate as a result of the radio waves hitting it, reproducing the original signal. One strong transmitter and many receivers can engage in this process, which explains how hundreds or millions of individuals can simultaneously receive the same radio signal.

   The electromagnetic energy, a combination of electricity and magnetism, passes you by in waves similar to those on the ocean's surface. They are referred to as radio waves. Radio waves have a specific speed, length, and frequency just like ocean waves do. The wave's speed is only the speed at which it moves between two locations. The distance between one wave crest (peak) and the next is known as the wavelength, whereas the frequency refers to the number of waves that arrive each second. Seven waves arriving in a second are referred to as seven hertz because frequency is measured using this unit.

SOUND WAVES

Radar waves are produced by accelerating charged particles, similar to time-varying electric currents.

A sound wave is created by a transmitter and is subsequently captured by a receiver. An antenna can be used by a radio transmitter and receiver to send and receive energy into and from space, respectively. Typically, transmitters and receivers are designed to operate within a specified frequency range.

Transmission phases for sound waves between a transmitter and a receiver:

When electrons move up and down the transmitter, radio waves are released.Radio waves travel through the air at the speed of light.The original signal is reproduced by a receiver's own electrons vibrating as a result of the radio waves striking it. This approach describes how hundreds or millions of people can simultaneously receive the same radio signal from a single powerful transmitter and many receivers.

The only measure of a wave's speed is how quickly it travels between two points. The wavelength is the distance between one wave crest (peak) and the next, whereas the frequency is the quantity of waves that arrive each second. Since this unit is used to measure frequency, seven waves coming in a second are referred to as seven hertz.
