26. when rationalizing the denonminator of \large \sqrt{\frac{3}{5}} explain why both the numerator and denominator must be multiplied square root of 5?​


By radical properties, the square root of a fraction is equal to the square root of the numerator divided by the square root of the denominator.

[tex] \sqrt{ \frac{a}{b} } = \frac{ \sqrt{a} }{ \sqrt{b} } [/tex]

A fraction whose denominator has a square root is considered irrational, hence the term "rationalization." By rationalizing denominators, it reveals what the true quantity of the fraction is.

Say, for instance, the square root of the fraction ⅗.

[tex] \sqrt{ \frac{3}{5} } [/tex]

This is equal to √3/√5.

[tex] \sqrt{ \frac{3}{5} } = \frac{ \sqrt{3} }{ \sqrt{5} } [/tex]

Then, multiply the fraction by √5/√5.

[tex]( \frac{ \sqrt{3} }{ \sqrt{5} } )( \frac{ \sqrt{5} }{ \sqrt{5} } )[/tex]

This cancels the radical in the denominator.

[tex] \frac{ \sqrt{15} }{5} [/tex]

By rationalization, you're making a fraction rational by removing the irrational parts.
