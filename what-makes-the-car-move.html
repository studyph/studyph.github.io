25. A 200 kg amusement park car goes around a track. If it is moving at 8.9 m/s when it makes a turn around a curve of radius 300 m, what is the magnitude of the centripetal acceleration and centripetal force?


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The centripetal acceleration of an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{a_{c} = \frac{v^{2}}{r}}[/tex]

where:

ac = centripetal acceleration

v = velocity

r = radius

The centripetal force exerted on an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{F_{c} = \frac{mv^{2}}{r}}[/tex]

where:

Fc = centripetal force

m = mass

v = velocity

r = radius

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for centripetal acceleration

[tex]a_{c} = \dfrac{v^{2}}{r}[/tex]

[tex]a_{c} = \dfrac{(\text{8.9 m/s})^{2}}{\text{300 m}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{a_{c} = \text{0.264 m/}\text{s}^{2}}[/tex]

Therefore, the centripetal acceleration is 0.264 m/s².

Solving for centripetal force

[tex]F_{c} = \dfrac{mv^{2}}{r}[/tex]

[tex]F_{c} = \dfrac{(\text{200 kg})(\text{8.9 m/s})^{2}}{\text{300 m}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{F_{c} = \text{52.81 N}}[/tex]

Therefore, the centripetal force is 52.81 N.
