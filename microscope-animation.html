Answer:

I would think not. Eyesight strength corresponds to the size of the eye, because the pupil can let in more photons. The smaller an eye is, the less photons can enter it, and thus the worse the resolution would be.

The amount of photons hitting the bacteria don’t change, but the amount of those photons that can then enter the eye do. I’m not sure about focal distance however, and it’s likely that insects have a shorter focal distance than we do. So, they could probably focus more easily on bacteria that would be closer to them, that we would have a harder time seeing because we can’t physically focus on them when our eyes are that close.

29. jasmine examines a cell under the microscope to determine if it's a plant or an animal cell which structure in the cell would indicate that a cell is an animal cell​


How to determine the structure if it's plant or an animal cell

Despite the fact that all of living species are made up of cells with similar structures, the architectures of plant and animal cells differ.

The following are significant structural distinctions between the plant and an animal cell:

Animal cells lack a cell wall, but plant cells do. Plants rely on cell walls for stability and form.

Plant cells include chloroplasts, but animal cells do not. Chloroplasts allow plants to undergo photosynthesis and produce food.

Plant cells typically have one or more big vacuole(s), whereas animal cells, if present, have smaller vacuoles. Large vacuoles assist shape the plant and allow it to store water and food for later use. Because the storing function is less important in animal cells, the vacuoles are smaller.
