10. Helium is an unreactive noble gases that is used to inflate balloons a party balloon contains 3.33 x 1022 atoms of helium gas. What is the mass of helium in grams?​


Answer:

0.2214 g

Explanation:

Compute for the mass of 3.33 × 10²² atoms He.

mass = 3.33 × 10²² atoms × (1 mol / 6.022 × 10²³ atoms) × (4.003 g / 1 mol)

mass = 0.2214 g

19. A mixture of helium and neon gases is held in a container at 2.4 atmospheres. If the mixture contains twice as many helium atoms as neon atoms, what is the partial pressure of helium?


Answer:

4.8 atm

Explanation:

The partial pressure of one individual gas within the overall mixtures according to Dalton's Law of Partial Pressure is described as  :

Pi=Ptotal xi

PHe = Ptotal xHe

According to the problem, the mixture contains twice as many Helium atoms as Neon atoms, so x=2

*atm= atmosphere

Given:

Ptotal =2.4 atm

xHe= 2

Solution:

PHe = PtotalxHe

PHe =(2.4)*2

PHe = 4.8 atm


20. A tank is charged with a mixture of 1.0 x 103 mol of oxygen and 4.5 x 103 mol of helium. Calculate the mole fraction of each gas in the mixture.​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

• For O₂

[tex]\begin{aligned} X_{\text{O}_2} & = \frac{n_{\text{O}_2}}{n_{\text{O}_2} + n_{\text{He}}} \\ & = \frac{1.0 \times 10^3 \: \text{mol}}{1.0 \times 10^3 \: \text{mol} + 4.5 \times 10^3 \: \text{mol}} \\ & = \boxed{0.18} \end{aligned}[/tex]

• For He

[tex]\begin{aligned} X_{\text{He}} & = \frac{n_{\text{He}}}{n_{\text{O}_2} + n_{\text{He}}} \\ & = \frac{4.5 \times 10^3 \: \text{mol}}{1.0 \times 10^3 \: \text{mol} + 4.5 \times 10^3 \: \text{mol}} \\ & = \boxed{0.82} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the mole fractions of oxygen and helium are 0.18 and 0.82, respectively.
