30. according to Socrates the true self is the soul where virtue and wisdom rest. Why evil cannot be done to a good man?​


Answer:

Socrates believed that the true self is the soul, and that virtue and wisdom reside within it. He argued that doing evil is actually harmful to oneself, and that no one willingly chooses to do evil. Socrates believed that if a person truly understood what was good and virtuous, they would choose to act accordingly. Therefore, he believed that it was impossible for a good person to be harmed by the actions of others, as long as they remained true to their virtues and wisdom.

Socrates argued that a person's character and actions are reflections of their soul, and that one's soul is the most important aspect of their being. He believed that a person's soul is the source of their wisdom, virtue, and inner harmony, and that it is capable of guiding them towards what is truly good and just. In this sense, Socrates believed that the soul is the true self, and that it is the foundation of one's character and moral integrity.

Based on this perspective, Socrates argued that it was impossible for a good person to be harmed by the actions of others, as long as they remained true to their inner wisdom and virtues. Evil actions directed towards a good person may cause physical or emotional pain, but they cannot harm the person's soul or undermine their inner moral compass. Thus, Socrates believed that a person's inner goodness and virtue are ultimately invincible, and that they cannot be overcome by external forces or circumstances.
