3. John builds a cubical tank that has a side length of 200cm. what is the total capacity of the tank in cubic meters


✒️VOLUME

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \Large \: \rm Volume = 8 \: cu. \: meters [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

Convert the side length as meters from centimeters.

[tex] \rm 100 \: cm = 1 \: m [/tex]

[tex] 100 \times 2 \: cm = 1 \times 2 \: m [/tex]

[tex] \rm 200 \: cm = 2 \: m [/tex]

Thus, 200cm converted into meters is 2m. Find the volume of the cubical tank with side of 2m.

[tex] \begin{align} & \bold{Formula:} \\ & \quad \boxed{\rm Volume = (side)^3} \end{align} [/tex]

[tex] \rm Volume = (2 \:m)^3 [/tex]

[tex] \rm Volume = 8 \:m^3 [/tex]

Therefore, the total capacity of the cubical tank is 8 cu. meters

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

FORMULA:

VOLUME = s³

SOLUTION:

V = s³ = 200 × 1m/100 = 2m

V = (2m)³

= 8m³

the total capacity of cubical tank is 8 cu. meters

19. 4. What is the area of a rectangle it its length is 52cm and its perimeter is 200cm​


Answer:

2496 cm^2

Step-by-step explanation:

P = 2L + 2W

200cm = 2(52cm) + 2W

200cm = 104cm + 2W

200cm - 104 cm = 2W

96 cm = 2W

48cm = W

A = L x W

A = 52cm x 48 cm

A = 2496 cm^2
