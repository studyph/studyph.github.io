3. Why is focal length important when using the oil-immersion objective?


Focal length is the shortest with the oil immersion objective lens. Knowing focal length helps minimize the damage/scratch to the oil immersion lens or the slide.

The immersion oil must match the refractive indices of all wavelengths of the light spectrum with the specifications for an objective lens of the same design. This property of the oil is called the dispersion value.

Understanding oil-immersion

To further clarify the image that will be seen, between the object to be seen with the objective lens is given immersion oil. This immersion oil will increase the light that enters the objective lens after passing through the object so that the object will be seen more clearly.

The specific properties of the immersion oil are selected to match the properties of the objective lens that have been determined by the optical designer. Different manufacturers use different types of glass and optical formulations in their designs, and as a result, the specifications of the immersion oil also differ according to the characteristics of the lenses produced.

25. What is the function of immersion oil in the oil immersion objective? How does this improve the resolving power of a microscope?


What is the function of immersion oil in the oil immersion objective?

Immersion oil increases the resolving power of the microscope by replacing the air gap between the immersion objective lens and cover glass with a high refractive index medium and reducing light refraction.

How does this improve the resolving power of a microscope?

One way of increasing the optical resolving power of the microscope is to use immersion liquids between the front lens of the objective and the cover slip. Most objectives in the magnification range between 60x and 100x (and higher) are designed for use with immersion oil.
