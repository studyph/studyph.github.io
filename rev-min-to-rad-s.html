4. A wheel 80cm in diameter turns at 120 rev/min. What is the angular velocity of the wheel in rad/s?


The angular velocity of the wheel : 4π rad/s

Further explanation

The motion of objects passing through a circular path will have linear velocity and angular velocity  

The linear velocity of an object is the velocity that an object has when moving in a circular direction in the direction of its rotation  

The length of the path the object is traveling around the circle:  

C = 2πr  

Linear speeds can be formulated:  

 v = 2πr/T

r = radius (m)  

T = period (s)  

The speed of the rotating object is called Angular velocity. This value is part of circular motion.  

The angular velocity is denoted by ω including the vector quantity. The unit used is usually rpm (rotation per minute) or radians / sec  

Relationship between angular and linear velocity

ω = v/r

Because the question is the angular velocity, then we will only convert rpm (rev / min)  in rad / s

A wheel turns at 120 rev/min, so

ω = 120 rpm

[tex]\rm \omega=2\pi\times(RPM)/60\\\\\omega=2\pi\times 120/60\\\\\omega=4\pi~rad/s[/tex]

15. A wheel 12.14 ft in diameter is rotating at 89.76 rev/min. Find the distance (in ft) traveled by a point on the rim in 1 s.


Find the circumference of the wheel, it is the distance that the point on the rim travels in one revolution.

Circumference = Diameter × pi
Circumference = 12.14 ft × 3.14
Circumference = 38.1196 ft

Since the wheel rotates at 89.76 rev/min we must convert it to rev/sec. To do that, we must divide the number of revolution and the min by 60. The result is, 1.496 rev/sec.

Since the circumference is the distance that the point on the rim of the wheel travels per revolution, then we must multiply the Circumference by the number of revolution per sec, to get the distance travelled by the point on the rim per sec.

Circumference × rev/sec = distance
38.1196 ft × 1.496 = 57.02692160 ft ≈ 57.027 ft

19. A ferris wheel rotating initially at an angular speed of 0.16 rad/s accelerates to an angular speed of 0.7 rad/s. What is its angular acceleration if it moved to an angular position if 2.5 rad? ​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & \theta = \text{2.5 rad} \\ & \omega_0 = \text{0.16 rad/s} \\ & \omega = \text{0.7 rad/s} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the angular acceleration.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \alpha & = \frac{\omega^2 - \omega_0^2}{2\theta} \\ & = \frac{(\text{0.7 rad/s})^2 - (\text{0.16 rad/s})^2}{2(\text{2.5 rad})} \\ & = \text{0.09288 rad/s}^2 \\ & \approx \boxed{\text{0.093 rad/s}^2} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the angular acceleration of a ferris wheel is 0.093 rad/s².

27. A pulley of 5 cm radius on a motor is turning at 30 rev/s and slows down uniformly to 20 rev/s in 2 seconds. Calculate the angular acceleration of the motor. a. -10 rev/s^2 b. 5 rev/s^2 c. -5 rev/s^2 d. 10 rev/s^2


The angular acceleration of the motor : c. -5 rev/s

Further explanation

The speed of the rotating object is called angular velocity. This value is part of circular motion.

The angular velocity is denoted by w including the vector quantity. The unit used is usually rpm (rotation per minute) or radians/sec

Average angular velocity is the ratio of angular displacement to the time interval

[tex]\rm \omega = \dfrac {\Delta \theta} {\Delta t}[/tex]

Circular motion with constant acceleration (angular acceleration denoted by α) can be formulated:  

[tex]\rm \omega=\omega o+\alpha t\\\\\alpha=\dfrac{\omega-\omega o}{t}[/tex]

ωo = initial angular velocity  

ω = angular velocity after t  

A motor is turning at 30 rev/s and slows down uniformly to 20 rev/s in 2 seconds, so :

ωo = 30 rev/s

ω = 20 rev/s

t = 2 s

The angular acceleration :

[tex]\rm \alpha=\dfrac{20-30}{2}\\\\\alpha=\boxed{\bold{-5~rev/s}}[/tex]

29. A 0.12-m-radius grinding wheel takes 5.5 s to speed up from 2.0 rad/s to 11.0 rad/s. what is the wheel's average angular acceleration


SOLVING FOR AVERAGE ANGULAR ACCELERATION

Problem:

A 0.12-m-radius grinding wheel takes 5.5 s to speed up from 2.0 rad/s to 11.0 rad/s. what is the wheel's average angular acceleration.

Answer:

The average angular acceleration of the wheel is .

Explanation:

We can use the formula to solve for the required average angular acceleration of this given problem:

average angular acceleration = (final angular speed – initial angular speed)/2

By direct substitution:

ave. angular acc.= (11.0 – 2.0)/5.5

ave. angular acc. = 9.0/5.5

ave. angular acc. = 1.64 rad/s²

Hence, the average angular speed of the wheel which is 0.12 meters in radius is equal to 1.64 rad per square second.
