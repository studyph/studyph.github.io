5. Explain Formalism Strengths - Great for analyzing poetry - Easy to teach/learn - readily applied informally - well-known approach


Answer:

Formalism is a critical approach that emphasizes the form, structure, and technical features of a literary work, rather than its historical, biographical, or cultural context. Here are some of the strengths of the formalist approach:

Great for analyzing poetry: Formalism is particularly useful for analyzing poetry because it focuses on the technical aspects of the poem, such as rhyme, meter, and form. By analyzing these elements, formalists can uncover the poem's underlying structure and meaning.

Easy to teach/learn: Formalism is a relatively simple and straightforward approach that is easy to teach and learn. It requires minimal background knowledge or expertise in literary theory, making it accessible to students and scholars alike.

Readily applied informally: Formalist analysis can be easily applied to a wide range of literary works, including novels, short stories, and plays. Because it emphasizes the technical features of a work rather than its historical or biographical context, it can be used to analyze works from different time periods and cultural contexts.

Well-known approach: Formalism is a well-known and established approach in literary studies. It has been widely used and studied since the early 20th century and has contributed significantly to our understanding of literature as a formal art form. As a result, there is a vast body of literature and scholarship available on the topic, making it a valuable resource for students and scholars.
