16. Differentiate melodrama and tragedy.​


Answer:

melodrama is (archaic|uncountable) a kind of drama having a musical accompaniment to intensify the effect of certain scenes while tragedy is a drama or similar work, in which the main character is brought to ruin or otherwise suffers the extreme consequences of some tragic flaw or weakness of character.
