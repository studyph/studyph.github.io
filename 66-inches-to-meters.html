16. The perimeter of a garden is 66 meters and its area is 270 sq. meters. Find the dimensions of the garden. ​


The dimension of the rectangular garden is 15 m by 18 m.

Explanation:

Step 1: Formulate an equation using the formula for the perimeter of the rectangle.

The perimeter of a rectangle is given by the formula:

P = 2l + 2w

where: P - perimeter

l - length

w - width

Substitute the value of P which is 66 in the equation

66 = 2l + 2w

Simplify

33 = l + w

Equate to w

w = 33 - l

Step 2: Formulate an equation using the formula for the area of the rectangle.

The area of a rectangle is given by the equation:

A = l x w

where: A - area

l - length

w - width

Substitute the value of A which is 270 in the equation

270 = l x w

Equate to w

w = 270/l

Step 3: Compare the two equations and solve for length.

w = w

33 - l = 270/l

Simplify

33l - l² = 270

Equate to zero

l² - 33l + 270 = 0

Factor the equation

(l - 18)(l - 15) = 0

l1 = 18 | l2 = 15

Considering that length is the longest side of the rectangle, length = 18

Step 4: Solve for w using any of the two equations

w = 33 - l

w = 33 - 18

w = 15

The dimension of the rectangular garden is 15 m by 18 m.
