Meaning

Direct Speech

Meaning - Direct speech implies a direct discourse, that uses the actual words of the speaker to report it.

Alternative Name - Quoted speech

Point of view - Speaker

Usage - When we repeat the original words of a person

Quotation Marks - It uses quotation marks.

Reported Speech

Meaning - Indirect speech refers to indirect discourse that delineates what a person said, in own words.

Alternative Name - Indirect speech

Point of view - Listener

Usage - When we use our own words for reporting what other person says

Quotation Marks - It does not use quotation marks.

8. ano ang autism spectrum​


Answer:

Ang autism spectrum disorder (ASD) ay isang developmental disability dulot ng mga pagkakaiba sa utak na lumalabas sa pag-uugali, pakikipag-ugnayan, wika, at minsan, talino ng isang tao. Ito ay tinatawag na "spectrum disorder" dahil ang kondisyon ay maaaring maging banayad hanggang malubha, sabi ng ang US National Institute of Mental Health (NIH).

Answer & Explanation:

Ang Autism spectrum disorder (ASD) ay isang problema o karamdaman na nagsisimula nang maaga sa pagkabata at tumatagal sa buong buhay ng isang tao. Nakakaapekto ito sa kung paano kumilos at nakikipag-ugnayan ang isang tao sa iba, nakikipag-usap, at natututo. Kasama rito ang dating kilala bilang Asperger syndrome at laganap na mga karamdaman sa pag-unlad.

29. Write possible statements in the thought balloon for the following situations to show contrast in the speech context, speech style, and speech act. Do this in your answer sheet. 1. When you meet your friend vs. your teacher along the corridor. 2. When you introduce yourself to your classmate vs. in front of the class. 3. When you talk to yourself vs. the guidance coordinator about your problems. 4. When you deliver a speech to a friend vs. a large crowd. 5. When you explain to your sister vs. your parents why you can't graduate on time.​


Answer:

1. When you meet your friend along the corridor:

"Hey, what's up? Did you hear about the party this weekend?"

vs.

When you meet your teacher along the corridor:

"Good morning, Professor. I just wanted to let you know that I handed in my assignment on time."

2. When you introduce yourself to your classmate:

"Hi, I'm John. I'm new here. What's your name?"

vs.

When you introduce yourself in front of the class:

"Good morning, everyone. My name is John, and I'm excited to be joining this class."

3. When you talk to yourself about your problems:

"Why did I have to mess up on that test? I can't believe I got a C."

vs.

When you talk to the guidance coordinator about your problems:

"I'm really struggling with my coursework this semester. I was wondering if you had any suggestions for how I can improve my grades."

4. When you deliver a speech to a friend:

"So, I was thinking that we could start a study group to help each other prepare for exams."

vs.

When you deliver a speech to a large crowd:

"Ladies and gentlemen, it is my pleasure to stand before you today to talk about the importance of education in our society."

5. When you explain to your sister why you can't graduate on time:

"I'm just not doing well in my math class, and I don't think I can bring my grade up in time for graduation."

vs.

When you explain to your parents why you can't graduate on time:

"I understand how disappointed you must be, but I want to assure you that I am working as hard as I can to improve my grades. I just need a little more time to get back on track."
