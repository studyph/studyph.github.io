Answer:

Production is carried out in power stations (also called "power plants"). Electricity is most often generated at a power plant by electromechanical generators, primarily driven by heat engines fueled by combustion or nuclear fission but also by other means such as the kinetic energy of flowing water and wind.

Explanation:

Electricity generation is the process of generating electric power from sources of primary energy. For utilities in the electric power industry, it is the stage prior to its delivery (transmission, distribution, etc.) to end users or its storage (using, for example, the pumped-storage method).

9. What is the function of a substation?​


Answer:

The purpose of a substation is to 'step down' high voltage electricity from the transmission system to lower voltage electricity so it can be easily supplied to homes and businesses in the area through lower voltage distribution lines.

Which of the following is the correct path of electrical power that can be traced back to the source? a. power plant -- transmission substations -- distribution substation -- residences b. transmission substations -- power plants -- residences -- distribution substations c. residences -- distribution substations -- power plants -- transmission substations d. distribution substations -- transmission substations -- power plants -- residences"


Answer:

a. power plant -- transmission substations -- distribution substation -- residences

Explanation:

Power plant uses chemical energy which will be converted into thermal energy by combustion in Boiler. Thermal energy is converted to kinetic energy in which steams will flow to the turbine. Kinetic energy converted to mechanical energy by the steam produced to spin the turbine of the generator. Generator converts mechanical energy to electrical energy by spinning the magnet which results the creation of electricity.

Potential Energy (chemical energy) → thermal energy →  Kinetic Energy  → mechanical energy → electrical energy

If we power plant uses Hydroelectric power plant, it has stored a reservoir of water (potential energy) behind a dam which can be regulated to flow in the pipe (kinetic energy) to the turbine. As it flow to the turbine, the turbine rotates (mechanical energy) to power up the generator. The generator transform mechanical energy to electrical energy which will be distributed to the consumers.

power plant → transmission substations → distribution substation → residences

Potential Energy → Kinetic Energy → mechanical energy → electrical energy

The turbine’s rotation can be used to power something and convert its  mechanical work into a useful energy output like connecting the turbine to an  electric energy generator such as a dynamo working in reverse principle.  Connecting the turbine’s straw to the shaft of a dynamo enables the turbine to  rotate the motor of the dynamo within the magnetic field of permanent magnets  inside the dynamo. This relative movement between the motor’s coil of wire  and magnetic field induces magnetic forces that move charges and generate  electrical energy.

Electricity is a free energy. You will have an unlimited energy as long as there is a changing magnetic field which creates it. Changing magnetic fields creates changing electric fields and changing electric field produces current, this is the principle behind the creation of electric energy.  Electric power plant uses generator which is made of magnet connected to wire.
