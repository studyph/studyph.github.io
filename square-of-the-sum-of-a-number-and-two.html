22. . The sum of two numbers is 8. The sum of their squares is 40. Find the two numbers.​


✏️NUMBERS===============================

Problem: The sum of two numbers is 8. The sum of the their squares is 40. Find the two numbers.

Solution: Represent x and y as the two numbers respectively. Make two equations of the given statement.

[tex] \begin{cases}x + y = 8& \green{(eq. \: 1)} \\ {x}^{2} + {y}^{2} = 40 & \green{(eq. \: 2)} \end{cases}[/tex]

- Find the solution of x from the first equation in terms of y then substitute it to the second equation.

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 8 - y\\{x}^{2} + {y}^{2} = 40 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 8 - y\\(8 - y)^{2} + {y}^{2} = 40 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 8 - y\\64 - 16y + {y}^{2} + {y}^{2} = 40 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 8 - y\\64 - 16y + {2y}^{2} = 40 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 8 - y\\{2y}^{2} - 16y + 24 = 0 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 8 - y\\ {y}^{2} - 8y + 12 = 0 \end{cases}[/tex]

- Solve the quadratic equation in the second equation by factoring.

[tex] {y}^{2} - 8y + 12 = 0[/tex]

[tex](y - 6)(y - 2) = 0[/tex]

[tex]y - 6 = 0 \: \: \: \: ; \: \: \: \: y - 2 = 0[/tex]

[tex]y = 6 \: \: \: \: ; \: \: \: \: y = 2[/tex]

- We can use one of this roots to solve for the Eq. 1 (I use root 2 instead).

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 8 - y\\y = 2\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 8 - 2\\y = 2\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}x = 6\\y = 2\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The two numbers are:

[tex] \large \rm Numbers = \boxed{ \rm \green{ \: 6 \: and \: 2 \: }}[/tex]===============================
