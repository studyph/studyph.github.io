22. 5 important details about the idaw folk dance.​


Answer:

1. Meaning: The Idaw dance is a warrior dance performed by the Kalinga tribe. It depicts the bravery, strength, and agility of the warriors in battle.

2. Costumes: The dancers wear traditional Kalinga attire, which includes a bahag (loincloth) for men and a tapis (wrap-around skirt) for women. The men also wear a headgear made of animal horns and feathers, while the women wear beads and earrings.

3. Music: The dance is accompanied by the sound of gongs, which are played by a group of musicians. The rhythm and tempo of the music dictate the movements of the dancers.

4. Choreography: The dance involves intricate footwork and hand movements, which are designed to simulate the movements of warriors in battle. The dancers move in synchronized formations, often mimicking the movements of animals such as the eagle and the hawk.

5. Significance: The Idaw dance is an important cultural tradition of the Kalinga tribe, and it is often performed during festivals and other celebrations. It is also considered a form of cultural heritage that is passed down from generation to generation.
