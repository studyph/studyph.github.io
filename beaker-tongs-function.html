23. A 16-mL liquid sample is poured in an empty beaker. The empty beaker weighs 55 grams. When the sample is transferred to the beaker, the combined weight of the beaker and the liquid sample is now 75 g. Calculate the density of the liquid in g/mL. (HINT: Get the mass of the liquid sample by taking the difference between the weight of the empty beaker, and combined weight of beaker plus liquid sample)


Answer:

d = 1.25 g/ml or 1.25 g/m³

Step-by-step explanation:

Get the mass of the liquid sample

75g -55g =20 grams

mass of the liquid = 20grams

Use the formula

v = 16ml

d = M/V

d = 20grams/16ml

d = 1.25 g/ml or 1.25g/cm³

1g = 1 cm³
