12. WHAT IS THE MOLE FRACTION OF HCl in 20% aqueous HCl solution?​


Solution:

Step 1: Assuming that the mass of an aqueous HCl solution is 100 g, the mass of HCl and H₂O are

[tex]mass_{\text{HCl}} = 20 \: g[/tex]

[tex]mass_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = 100 \: g - 20 \: g = 80 \: g[/tex]

Step 2: Calculating the number of moles of the masses above

[tex]n_{\text{HCl}} = 20 \: g \times \frac{1 \: mol}{36.46 \: g} = 0.55 \: mol[/tex]

[tex]n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = 80 \: g \times \frac{1 \: mol}{18.02 \: g} = 4.44 \: mol[/tex]

Step 3: Calculating the total number of moles

[tex]n_{T} = n_{\text{HCl}} + n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{T} = 0.55 \: mol + 4.44 \: mol[/tex]

[tex]n_{T} = 4.99 \: mol[/tex]

Step 4: Calculating the mole fraction of HCl

[tex]X_{\text{HCl}} = \frac{n_{\text{HCl}}}{n_{T}}[/tex]

[tex]X_{\text{HCl}} = \frac{0.55 \: mol}{4.99 \: mol}[/tex]

[tex]\blue{X_{\text{HCl}} = 0.11}[/tex]

molar mass = 65.39 g/mol × 1

molar mass = 65.39 g/mol

Step 2: Determine the mole ratio needed.

mole ratio = 1 mol Zn : 2 mol HCl

Step 3: Calculate the number of moles of HCl reacted using the mole ratio.

[tex]\text{moles of HCl = 3.75 g Zn} × \frac{\text{1 mol Zn}}{\text{65.39 g Zn}} × \frac{\text{2 mol HCl}}{\text{1 mol Zn}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{moles of HCl = 0.1147 mol}}[/tex]

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of ZnCl₂.

molar mass = (65.39 g/mol × 1) + (35.45 g/mol × 2)

molar mass = 136.29 g/mol

Step 2: Determine the mole ratio needed.

mole ratio = 1 mol Zn : 1 mol ZnCl₂

Step 3: Calculate the mass of ZnCl₂ formed using the mole ratio.

[tex]\text{mass of ZnCl₂ = 5.00 g Zn} × \frac{\text{1 mol Zn}}{\text{65.39 g Zn}} × \frac{\text{1 mol ZnCl₂}}{\text{1 mol Zn}} × \frac{\text{136.29 g ZnCl₂}}{\text{1 mol ZnCl₂}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of ZnCl₂ = 10.42 g}}[/tex]

Solution: (d)

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of HCl and H₂.

For HCl

molar mass = (1.008 g/mol × 1) + (35.45 g/mol × 1)

molar mass = 36.458 g/mol

For H₂

molar mass = 1.008 g/mol × 2

molar mass = 2.016 g/mol

Step 2: Determine the mole ratio needed.

mole ratio = 2 mol HCl : 1 mol H₂

Step 3: Calculate the mass of H₂ produced using the mole ratio.

[tex]\text{mass of H₂ = 1.75 g HCl} × \frac{\text{1 mol HCl}}{\text{36.458 g HCl}} × \frac{\text{1 mol H₂}}{\text{2 mol HCl}} × \frac{\text{2.016 g H₂}}{\text{1 mol H₂}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of H₂ = 0.0484 g}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of Zn = 8.25 mol ZnCl₂} × \frac{\text{1 mol Zn}}{\text{1 mol ZnCl₂}}[/tex]

18. What volume of 4.50 m hcl can be made by mixing 5.65 m hcl with 250.0 ml of 3.55 m hcl?


[tex]\bold{Problem:}[/tex]

What volume of 4.50 M HCl can be made by mixing 5.65 M HCl with 250.0 mL of 3.55 M HCl?

[tex]\bold{Solution:}[/tex]

Here is the first way to solve this problem:

M₁ V₁ + M₂V₂ = M3V3

(3.55) (0.250) + (5.65) (x) = (4.50) (0.250 + X)

Where x is volume of 5.65 M HCl that is added

(0.250+ x) is total resultant volume

0.8875 +5.65x = 1.125 + 4.50 x 1.15x = 0.2375

X= 0.2065 L

Total amount of 4.50 M HCl is then (0.250

+ 0.2065) = 0.4565 L

Total amount = 456.5 mL

Here is the second way to solve this problem:

Since the amount of 5.65 M added is not asked for, there is no need to solve for it.

M₁ V₁ + M₂V₂ = M3V3

(3.55) (250) + (5.65) (x − 250) = (4.50) (x)

That way, x is the answer you want, the

final volume of the solution, rather than

x being the amount of 5.65 M solution

that is added.
