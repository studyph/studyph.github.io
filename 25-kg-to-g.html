8. calculate the vapor pressure of salt solution containing 20 g salt (NaCl) 1.5 kg of water at 25°​


Answer:

P solution = 23.6 mmHg

Explanation:

Note: The vapor pressure of water at 25°C is 23.8 mmHg.

Determining the van't Hoff factor

Assume that NaCl is 100% dissociated. In NaCl, there are 1 Na ion and 1 Cl ion.

i = 1 + 1 = 2

Calculating the number of moles of solute and solvent

n solute = 20 g × (1 mol / 58.443 g) = 0.34221 mol

n solvent = 1500 g × (1 mol / 18.015 g) = 83.26395 mol

Calculating the total number of moles

n T = n solute + n solvent

n T = 0.34221 mol + 83.26395 mol

n T = 83.60616 mol

Calculating the mole fraction of the solute

X solute = n solute / n T

X solute = 0.34221 mol / 83.60616 mol

X solute = 0.00409312

Calculating the vapor pressure lowering

∆P = (i)(X solute)(P solvent)

∆P = (2)(0.00409312)(23.8 mmHg)

∆P = 0.2 mmHg

Calculating the vapor pressure of the solution

P solution = P solvent - ∆P

P solution = 23.8 mmHg - 0.2 mmHg

P solution = 23.6 mmHg

12. You have ball with a volume of 25 cm3 and a mass of 20 g. What is its density in kg/L?​


Solution:

The density is the mass per volume. Let's convert the unit.

[tex]\rho=\dfrac{m}{V}= \dfrac{20\text{ g}\times\dfrac{1\text{ kg}}{1000\text{ g}} }{25\text{ cm}^3\times\dfrac{1 \text{ m}^3}{100^3\text{ cm}^3} \times\dfrac{1000\text{ L}}{1\text{ m}^3} } \\\\\boxed{\rho=0.8\text{ kg/L}}[/tex]

Note: 1 g/cm³ = 1 kg/L

Answer:

0.8kg/L

Explanation:

Given: Mass(m) = 20g

           Volume(v) = 25[tex]cm^{3}[/tex]

density is mass per unit volume: d = m/v

you have volume and mass given but you need to convert them

for mass:

1kg = 1000g

for volume:

1[tex]m^{3}[/tex] = 1000L

1m = 100cm

to convert:

m = 20g × [tex]\frac{1kg}{1000g}[/tex]

m = 0.02kg

v = [tex]25cm^{3}[/tex] × [tex](\frac{1m}{100cm}) ^{3}[/tex] × [tex]\frac{1000L}{1m^{3} }[/tex]

v = [tex]0.025L[/tex]

computing for density:

d = [tex]\frac{m}{v}[/tex]

d = [tex]\frac{0.02kg}{0.025L}[/tex]

d = [tex]0.8\frac{kg}{L}[/tex]

Answer:

8 kg = 8000 g

25 g = 0.025 kg

3 L = 30 dL

11 dL = 1.1 L

12 mg = 0.012 G

Solutions:

Convert kg into g:

Since 1 kg is equal to 1000 g, just multiply them into 8 so the results will be 8 kg = 8000 g.

Convert g into kg:

Since 1 g is equal to 0.001 kg, just multiply the gram into 25 and divide it to kilogram so the results will be 25 g = 0.025 kg.

Convert L into dL:

Since 1 L or (Liter) is equal to 10 dL or (deciliter), multiply it like this: 1 × 3 L and convert to dL: 10 x 30 dL so the results will be 3 L = 30dL.

Convert dL into L:

Since 10 dl is equal to 1 L, just add it to 1 and then you'll get the result.

Convert mg into G:

Since 1 mg is equal to 0.001 G, just multiply it to 12 and divide it to grams, then you'll get the result: 12 mg = 0.012 g.
