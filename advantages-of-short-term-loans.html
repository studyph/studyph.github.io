Answer:

Once upon a time, there was a young man named Jack who wanted to start his own business. He had a great idea for a small restaurant but didn't have enough money to open it. So, he decided to apply for a business loan from a local bank.

After submitting his business plan and financial statements, the bank approved his loan application. Jack was able to use the loan to rent a space, purchase equipment, and hire staff for his restaurant. He also used some of the loan to take a short course to improve his business skills.

As his restaurant started to gain popularity, Jack began to make a profit. He was able to pay back the loan on time, and even expand his business by opening a second location.

Sarah's loan application was approved and she was able to buy her dream car. She had to pay back the loan in monthly payments, but she was able to afford it comfortably.

In the end, Jack and Sarah were both able to achieve their financial goals thanks to the loans they had taken. Jack's business continued to thrive and Sarah was able to enjoy her new car.

They both learned that loans can be a valuable tool for achieving financial success, as long as they are used responsibly and paid back on time.

11. Define the following terminologies..1. Business Loan2. Consumer Loan3. Collateral4. Term of the Loan​


Answer:

A business loan is a loan specifically intended for business purposes. As with all loans, it involves the creation of a debt, which will be repaid with added interest.

A consumer loan is a loan given to consumers to finance specific types of expenditures. In other words, a consumer loan is any type of loan made to a consumer by a creditor.

Step-by-step explanation:

The term collateral refers to an asset that a lender accepts as security for a loan. Collateral may take the form of real estate or other kinds of assets, depending on the purpose of the loan.

A term loan is a loan from a bank for a specific amount that has a specified repayment schedule and either a fixed or floating interest rate.
