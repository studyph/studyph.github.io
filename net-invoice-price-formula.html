Another way of finding the net price is the complement method. The complement of your trade- discount rate is 100 percent minus the discount rate. This is sometimes called the net-price rate. You can multiply the list price by the complement to find the net price.

2. how to get/what's the formula for the discount nip rate, net invoice price, and discount? ​


Answer:

Convert the series of discounts to a single equivalent rate. To do so, we ﬁrst deduct the series of discounts individually from 100% and then multiply the resulting products by themselves to give us the NIP rate. If we multiply the NIP Rate by the list price, we get the net invoice price.

7. me.buena ordered. merchandise listed 15000.00.the invoiced showed the net price of 12,750.find the percent rate?​


Answer:

The percent discount is 15%.

Step-by-step explanation:

To find the discount rate or percent discount, we must first know how much is deducted (discount) from the original price to the net price.

Solving for amount deducted or discounted:

original price = 15,000net price = 12,750discount = 15,000 - 12,750discount = 2250

Now we must know how much percent of 15,000 is 2,250. This means we must know if 2,250 is what percent of 15,000.

Solving for the percent discount:

% discount = (2,250 / 15,000) * 100% discount=  0.15 * 100% discount  = 15%

To check if we got the correct answer, we multiply 0.15 or 15% to 15,000. If multiplying these values will give us 2,250, then our answer is correct.

Checking:

15,000 * 0.15 = 2,250

Therefore, the percent discount is 15%.

26. Mr.Buena ordered a merchandise listed 15,000.00. The invoiced showed the net price of 12,750. Find the percent rate?


Answer:

Mr.Buena ordered a merchandise listed 15,000.00. The invoiced showed the net price of 12,750. Find the percent rate.

The percent rate is 15%.

Step-by-step explanation:

What is asked?

Find the percent rate.

What are given?

original price - 15000

net price - 12750

Solution:

We have three steps to find the percent rate.

Find the discount price by subtracting the net price from the original price.Find the percent rate by dividing the discounted price by the original price.Multiply the answer by 100.

Let us start solving.

1. Finding Discount Price

original price - net price = ?

15000 - 12750 = 2250

2. Finding Percent Rate

discount rate / original price = ?

2250 / 15000 = 0.15

3. Multiply the answer by 100.

0.15 × 100 = 15%

(Just move the decimal point two times going to your right.)

Final Answer:

The percent rate is 15%.
