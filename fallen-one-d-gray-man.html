Answer:

The correct answer is:

c. Soiled or wet paper or wood

Compostable wastes are organic materials that can be broken down into nutrient-rich soil through natural decomposition processes. Examples of compostable wastes include old newspaper and paper cups, fallen branches and twigs, and dried leaves and weeds. However, soiled or wet paper or wood, such as paper or wood that has been contaminated with food or other non-organic materials, may not be suitable for composting as it can introduce contaminants and slow down the composting process. It is important to properly separate and prepare compostable wastes to ensure effective and safe composting.

13. THOMAS GRAY “Elegy Written in a Country Churchyard” 1.What is Gray’s attitude toward the people buried in the cemetery? Toward the rich? And toward the poor? 2.If you wandered into one of our cemeteries, would you see the same things Gray saw?


Answer:

In Thomas Gray's "Elegy Written in a Country Churchyard," the speaker's attitude towards the people buried in the cemetery is one of respect and reverence. The speaker seems to feel a deep sense of loss and sadness at the thought of all the people who have died and been buried in the cemetery, regardless of their social status or wealth. However, the speaker does express some disdain for the rich, who are described as being "vain" and "proud" and who have used their wealth and status to distance themselves from the common people. In contrast, the poor are depicted as being humble and hardworking, and the speaker seems to feel a sense of admiration and respect for them.If you were to wander into one of our modern-day cemeteries, you might not see the same things that Gray saw in his 18th-century rural English cemetery. For one thing, the physical appearance of cemeteries has changed a lot over the years, with many modern cemeteries featuring more elaborate headstones and monuments than those of the past. Additionally, the social and economic differences between the rich and the poor are likely to be less pronounced in a modern cemetery, as the poor are not generally buried in separate areas as they were in the past. However, it is still possible that you might see some of the same themes of loss, reverence, and respect in a modern cemetery that Gray explores in his poem.
