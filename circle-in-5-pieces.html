11. 3. Alex cuts circles with a diameter of 8 cm from a piece of paper with dimensions 40 cm by 24 cm. How many circles can he cut out of the paper? with a radius of 5 cm. can you cut from a rectangle with dimensions​


The radius of the circle is half of the diameter, so the radius is 8/2 = 4 cm.

The area of each circle is given by the formula A = πr^2, where r is the radius.

Substituting the value of r, we get:

A = π(4)^2

A = 16π

The area of the paper is 40 x 24 = 960 cm^2.

To find the number of circles that can be cut out of the paper, we need to divide the area of the paper by the area of each circle:

Number of circles = Area of paper / Area of each circle

Number of circles = 960 / 16π

Number of circles ≈ 60.42

Therefore, Alex can cut out approximately 60 circles from the paper.

Note: It is not possible to cut circles with a radius of 5 cm from a rectangle with dimensions 40 cm by 24 cm because the diameter of the circle (10 cm) is larger than the width of the rectangle (24 cm).
