Only at a time means, they seat on one part only.

_, _, _, _, _, _, _

Let say x, y, z are those three passengers including Jane's mother.

If they seated at x, y, z, _, _, _, _, → x, y, z, 4, 3, 2, 1 and x y z can be arranged at y z x and z y x and etc.

Thus, 3, 2, 1 for x, y, z passengers and remaining of 4, 3, 2, 1 for other passengers.

3 × 2 × 1 × 4 × 3 × 2 × 1 = 144

It is also equal to nPr = 3P3 for x, y, z passengers, and nPr = 4P4

nPr = 3P3 × 4P4

nPr = 144, ans.

24. A concert is held in a hall with a seating capacity of 800 seats. There are three types of seats: S seats, A seats, and B seats. There are 200 more A seats than S seats and 4 times as many B seats as S seats. The price per seat is 80 dollars for S seats, 70 dollars for A seats, and 50 dollars for B seats. If all seats are sold out, the total sales will be ______ dollars.


To properly analyze the problem,

Let x = S seats with 80 dollars per seat
y = A seats with 70 dollars per seat
z = B seats with 50 dollars per seat
T = seating capacity = 800 seats

Condition 1: There are 200 more A seats than S seats

y = x + 200

Condition 2: 4 times as many B seats as S seats

z = 4x

Solving for the number of S seats through substitution,

800 = x + (x+200) + 4x
800-200 = 6x
600 = 6x
x = 100 seats = S seats

Finding for the number of A seats, given Condition 1:

y = 100 + 200
y = 300 seats = A seats

Finding for the number of B seats, given Condition 2:

z = 4(100)
y = 400 seats = B seats

Now that you have all the number of seats for S, A, and B, respectively. You can now solve for the total number of seats sold:

Seats sold in Dollars = 100(80) + 300(70) + 400(50)
Seats sold in Dollars = $49,000

Therefore, the 800 seats were sold for $49,000.

26. a theater has 11 rows of seats.if there are 21 seats on the 1 row ,24 seats on the 2nd row,27 seats on the 3rd row and so on,how many seats are there in all?​


✒️ARITHMETIC SERIES

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{PROBLEM}:} [/tex]

A theater has 11 rows of seats.if there are 21 seats on the 1 row ,24 seats on the 2nd row,27 seats on the 3rd row and so on,how many seats are there in all?

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER}:} [/tex]

[tex] \qquad \LARGE \:\rm{396 \: seats} [/tex]

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

[tex] \large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION}:} [/tex]

» Each rows will form a sequence in which 3 seats is added on the next rows, that would we our common difference.

[tex] d = a_2 - a_1 = 24 - 21 = 3 [/tex]

[tex] d = a_3 - a_2 = 27 - 24 = 3 [/tex]

» The first term of the sequence is 21 since that is the first row. Find the sum of the first 11 terms of the sequence or the total number of seats in the theater.

[tex] \begin{align} & \bold{Formula:} \\ & \boxed{\rm S_n = \frac{\,n\,}{2} \big[ 2a_1 + d(n-1) \big]} \end{align} [/tex]

[tex] S_{11} = \frac{\,11\,}{2} \big[ 2(21) + 3(11 - 1) \big] [/tex]

[tex] S_{11} = \frac{\,11\,}{2} \big[ 2(21) + 3(10) \big] [/tex]

[tex] S_{11} = \frac{\,11\,}{2} \big[ 42 + 30 \big] [/tex]

[tex] S_{11} = \frac{\,11\,}{2} \big[ 72 \big] [/tex]

[tex] S_{11} = 11 \big[ 36 \big] [/tex]

[tex] S_{11} = 396 [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] There are a total number of 396 seats in the theater.
