2. A bus is parked 200 meters away from a building. The angle of depression of the bus from the top of the building is 55 degree. Find the height of the building.pa answer pls need ko asap ​​


Fundamental Concept

In a right triangle, the tangent of an angle can be solved as follows:

tangent θ  = opposite / adjacent

Derivative Equation

adjacent = opposite / tangent θ

Application

See attached photo for an illustration of the given problem.

In this case, the height of the building is the "adjacent" side to the angle.

So using the derivative equation, we can solve for the height of the building (represented by x) as follows:

x = 200 / tan 55°

x = 140.04 meters

16. An airplane takes off 200 meters in front of a 70-meter building. Is a 18.3° angle of elevation already enough for the plane to take off and not crash into the building? Assume that the airplane flies in a straight line and the angle of elevation remains constant until the airplane flies over the building. a. No, a higher angle of elevation is needed. b. Yes, a 18.3° angle of elevation is the minimimum the plane requires. c. No, a lower angle of elevation is needed. d. Yes, a 18.3° angle of elevation is more than enough.


b. Yes, a 18.3° angle of elevation is the minimimum the plane requires.

To see why, we can use trigonometry. Let x be the horizontal distance the plane travels before it clears the building. Then we have:

tan(18.3°) = 70 / (200 + x)

Solving for x, we get:

x = (70 / tan(18.3°)) - 200

Plugging in the given angle of elevation, we get:

x ≈ 179 meters

So as long as the plane travels at least 179 meters horizontally before it clears the building, it will not crash. Since the plane takes off 200 meters in front of the building, a 18.3° angle of elevation is sufficient.
