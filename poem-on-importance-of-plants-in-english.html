18. the importance of general statement and specific statement three stanza poem in english subject​


Answer:

In English subject, writing poetry is a common way to develop creative expression and language proficiency. When writing a poem, it is important to use both general and specific statements to convey the intended meaning effectively.

General statements are broad, universal ideas that can be understood by a wide audience. They provide the overall theme or tone of the poem and create a sense of familiarity for the reader. For example, a poem about love might start with a general statement such as "Love is a powerful force that can move mountains." This statement sets the tone for the rest of the poem and gives the reader an idea of what to expect.

On the other hand, specific statements are more detailed and precise. They provide specific images, emotions, and sensory experiences that bring the poem to life. They help to create a vivid picture in the reader's mind and evoke a stronger emotional response. For example, in the same love poem, a specific statement might be "Your gentle touch sends shivers down my spine." This statement is more personal and intimate, providing a specific image and emotional experience for the reader.

In a three-stanza poem, both general and specific statements are important to create a balanced and effective poem. The first stanza can start with a general statement that sets the theme or tone of the poem. The second stanza can contain specific statements that provide a more detailed description or image related to the theme. And the third stanza can bring the poem full circle by returning to a general statement that reinforces the overall message.

Using both general and specific statements in a poem can help to create a more dynamic and engaging piece of writing. It allows the writer to convey a universal message while also providing personal and specific details that make the poem more relatable and emotionally impactful. By balancing both types of statements, a poet can create a poem that resonates with readers and stays with them long after they have finished reading.
