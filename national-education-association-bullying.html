Answer:

Education:

Viewpoint 1: Education is crucial in preparing individuals for their future and giving them the necessary skills and knowledge to succeed in their personal and professional lives. It opens up opportunities and empowers people to make informed decisions and contribute positively to society.

Viewpoint 2: While education is important, the traditional education system may not be the best fit for everyone. Some people may learn better through experiential or vocational learning, and there is a need for more flexibility and creativity in the education system to accommodate diverse learning styles and interests.

Bullying:

Viewpoint 1: Bullying is a serious issue that can have long-lasting effects on the mental and emotional well-being of individuals. It is important to raise awareness, promote empathy and kindness, and implement strict anti-bullying policies in schools and communities to prevent and address bullying.

Viewpoint 2: While bullying is a problem, some people argue that it is a natural part of growing up and toughens individuals up for the challenges of the real world. They also believe that anti-bullying measures may go too far and stifle free speech or overprotect individuals from necessary challenges.

Vaping:

Viewpoint 1: Vaping can be harmful to health and has been linked to respiratory and cardiovascular problems. It is important to educate people on the risks of vaping and regulate the marketing and availability of vaping products, especially to youth.

Viewpoint 2: Some people believe that vaping is a less harmful alternative to smoking and can help individuals quit smoking. They also argue that individuals should have the freedom to make their own choices and that excessive regulation can be overly paternalistic.

Garbage:

Viewpoint 1: Proper waste management and reduction is crucial in protecting the environment and promoting sustainability. It is important to reduce waste at the source, promote recycling and composting, and properly dispose of hazardous waste to minimize the impact of garbage on our ecosystems.

Viewpoint 2: While waste reduction is important, some people argue that the burden should not solely be on individuals and that systemic change is necessary to address the root causes of waste production, such as overconsumption and planned obsolescence.

Earthquake:

Viewpoint 1: Earthquakes are natural disasters that can cause significant damage and loss of life. It is important to have proper earthquake preparedness and response measures in place, such as earthquake drills, building codes, and emergency supplies, to minimize the impact of earthquakes on communities.

Viewpoint 2: While earthquakes are a concern, some people argue that they are a natural part of the earth's cycles and cannot be prevented. They also believe that excessive focus on earthquakes can distract from other important issues, such as climate change.

24. Give the RRL of the topic "How to manage bullies and take action against bullying at educational institution"3 article/source​


Here are three sources related to managing bullies and taking action against bullying in educational institutions:

Twemlow, S. W., Fonagy, P., & Sacco, F. C. (2004). A developmental approach to mentalizing communities: II. The Peaceful Schools experiment. Bulletin of the Menninger Clinic, 68(4), 282-304.
This article focuses on the development of mentalizing communities as a way to prevent bullying in schools. The authors discuss the Peaceful Schools experiment, which aimed to reduce bullying by creating a supportive and nurturing school environment. The article also provides strategies for identifying and managing bullies in schools.

Olweus, D. (1993). Bullying at school: What we know and what we can do. Oxford, UK: Blackwell.
This book provides a comprehensive overview of bullying in schools and strategies for prevention and intervention. The author, Dan Olweus, is a pioneer in the field of bullying prevention and has conducted extensive research on the topic. The book covers topics such as the prevalence of bullying, the impact on victims and bullies, and effective prevention and intervention strategies.

Cross, D., Shaw, T., Hearn, L., Epstein, M., Monks, H., Lester, L., & Thomas, L. (2009). Australian Covert Bullying Prevalence Study (ACBPS). Child Family Community Australia, 25, 1-28.
This study focuses on the prevalence of covert bullying in Australian schools and provides recommendations for prevention and intervention. The authors discuss the need for a whole-school approach to bullying prevention, including education and training for teachers, parents, and students. The study also emphasizes the importance of taking action against bullying, including developing clear policies and procedures and providing support for victims.
