6. The EF of ascorbic acid is C₃H₄O₃. Based from an experimental data, the molecular mass of ascorbic acid is 180 g/mole. Determine the molecular formula of ascorbic acid.​


Solution:

Step 1: Represent a molecular formula.

[tex]\text{molecular formula} = (\text{C}_{3}\text{H}_{4}\text{O}_{3})_{n}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the empirical mass.

empirical mass = (12 g/mol × 3) + (1 g/mol × 4) + (16 g/mol × 3)

empirical mass = 88 g/mol

Step 3: Divide the molecular mass by the empirical mass.

[tex]n = \frac{\text{molecular mass}}{\text{empirical mass}}[/tex]

[tex]n = \frac{\text{180 g/mol}}{\text{88 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]n = 2[/tex]

Step 4: Multiply the subscripts by the value of n to obtain the molecular formula.

[tex]\text{molecular formula} = (\text{C}_{3}\text{H}_{4}\text{O}_{3})_{2}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molecular formula} = \text{C}_{6}\text{H}_{8}\text{O}_{6}}[/tex]

13. 10. Acetic acid, the acid present in vinegar, has the empirical formula of CH20. If theMolar mass of the acid is 60 g/mol, what is its molecular formula?​


Answer:

For acetic acid, the molar mass is 60.05 g/mol, and the molar mass of the empirical formula CH2O is 30.02 g/mol. The value of the integer n for acetic acid is therefore,

n=60.05g/mol30.02g/mol=2(4.5.2)

And the molecular formula is C2H4O2.

Note that n must be an integer and that your calculation should always yield a whole number (or very close to one).
