29. The sum of two numbers is 24. The second number is 6 more than the first number. What are the numbers.-16 and -8-6 and -186 and 188 and 16​


✏️NUMBERS

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{QUESTION:}}[/tex]

- The sum of two numbers is 24. The second number is 6 more than the first number. What are the two numbers?

A. -16 and -8B. -6 and -18C. 6 and 18D. 8 and 16

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{ANSWER:}}[/tex]

[tex]\qquad\LARGE\rm» \:\: \green{9\:and\:15}[/tex]

[tex]\red{••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••}[/tex]

[tex]\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTION:}}[/tex]

- Represent x and y as the first and second number respectively. Formulated equations of the given statement.

[tex]\begin{cases}x + y = 24& \red{(eq. \: 1)} \\y = 6 + x& \red{(eq. \: 2)} \end{cases}[/tex]

- Substitute y from the second equation to the first equation in terms of x.

[tex]\begin{cases}x + 6 + x = 24\\y = 6 + x\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{cases}2x + 6 = 24\\y = 6 + x\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{cases}2x = 24 - 6\\y = 6 + x\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{cases}2x=18\\y = 6 + x\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{cases} \begin{gathered} \frac{ \cancel2x}{ \cancel2} = \frac{18}{2} \end{gathered}\\y = 6 + x\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{cases}x = 9\\y = 6 + x\end{cases}[/tex]

- Thus, the first number is 9, then substitute it to the first equation to find y aka the second number.

[tex]\begin{cases}x = 9\\y = 6 + 9\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{cases}x = 9\\y = 15\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex]\therefore[/tex] The answer is not in the given options, the two numbers are 9 and 15.
