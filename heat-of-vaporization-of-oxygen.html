1. Calculate the heat of burning ethane (C2H6) in oxygen to give CO2 and water vapor?​


[tex]QUESTION:[/tex]

Calculate the heat of burning ethane (C2H6) in oxygen to give CO2 and water vapor?

[tex]ANSWER:[/tex]

The balanced chemical equation is

2C2H6+7O2= 4CO2+6H2O

Convert the 13.6grams of C2H6 to moles using the equation

moles= given mass/molar mass

The molar mass of C2H6=30g/mol

moles= 13.6/30=0.453mol

The molar ratio between C2H6 and O2 is 2:7

2= 0.453mol of C2H6

7= x moles of O2

Cross multiplying gives

2x=3.171

x= 3.171/2=1.5855mol of O2

Convert moles of O2 to grams of O2 using

moles= given mass/molar mass

1.5855= given mass/32 multiplying both sides by 32 gives

50.74 grams= mass of O2 used up

Mass of water vapour is found by first looking at the molar ratio between C2H6 and H2O which is 2:6 or 1:3

1= 0.453mol of C2H6

3= x moles of H2O

cross multiplying gives

x=1.359mol of water vapor

Converting moles of H2O to mass using the equation

moles= mass/molar mass

1.359=mass/18

multiplying both sides of the equation by 18 gives 24.5grams of water vapor.

Problem:

A student boils 9.43 grams of an unknown substances into vapor .if completely vaporizing the substance requiered 8,720 joules of energy ,what is the heat of vaporization?The heat of vaporation is joules/gram

Given:

m (grams) = 9.43

q (heat) = 8,720 joules

Hv (heat of vaporation) = ? unknown

Solution:

We can solve the problem using  

heat of vaporation = heat / mass (Hv = q/m)

Hv = q/m

     = 8,720 joules / 9.43 g

     = 924.708 joules/g

Answer:

Heat of Vaporization is 924.708 joules/g

20. what is latent heat of vaporization?


Latent heat of vaporization is a physical property of a substance. It is defined as the heat required to change one mole of liquid at its boiling point under standard atmospheric pressure. It is expressed as kg/mol or kJ/kg. When a material in liquid state is given energy, it changes its phase from liquid to vapor; the energy absorbed in this process is called heat of vaporization. The heat of vaporization of water is about 2,260 kJ/kg, which is equal to 40.8 kJ/mol.
