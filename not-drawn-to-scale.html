17. TrianglesABC and DEF shown below are congruent.(Note:figures not drawn to scale)​


Triangles ABC and DEF are congruent because they have corresponding sides in the same length and the angles at those points are equal, showing they have the same shape.

To prove this, we can use the Side-Angle-Side (SAS) theorem. According to SAS, if two sides and the included angle of one triangle are congruent to the corresponding parts of another triangle, then the two triangles are congruent.

In the case of triangles ABC and DEF, angles A and D, angles B and E, and angles C and F are equal, and each side of triangle ABC is congruent to the corresponding side in triangle DEF. This means ABC and DEF are congruent.

[tex] \Large\underline{\mathbb{SOLUTIONS}:} [/tex]

No. 1:

» The measure of the inscribed angle is one half the measure of its intercepted arc.

[tex]y = \frac{1}{2}(42\degree )[/tex]

[tex]y = 21 \degree [/tex]

» One circle theorem suggests that the angle is inscribed in a semicircle, then the angle forms a right angle.

[tex]x = 2(90\degree )[/tex]

[tex]x = 180\degree [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The value of x is 180 degrees while the value of y is 21 degrees.

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

No. 2:

» Arcs AB and AC are congruent. Thus, twice the measure of either of them plus the measure of arc BC is equal to 360°

[tex]2(m \overset{ \frown}{AC}) + m \overset{ \frown}{BC} = 360 \degree[/tex]

[tex]2(m \overset{ \frown}{AC}) + 90 \degree = 360 \degree[/tex]

[tex]2(m \overset{ \frown}{AC}) = 360 \degree - 90 \degree[/tex]

[tex]2(m \overset{ \frown}{AC}) = 270\degree[/tex]

[tex] \frac{2(m \overset{ \frown}{AC})}{2} = \frac{270\degree}{2} \\ [/tex]

[tex]m \overset{ \frown}{AC} = 135\degree[/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The measure of arc AC is 135 degrees.

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

No. 3:

» The measure of the intercepted arc is twice the measure of the inscribed angle.

[tex]x = 2(23\degree )[/tex]

[tex]x = 46 \degree [/tex]

[tex] \therefore [/tex] The value of x is 46 degrees.

[tex]••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••••[/tex]

» The measure of the intercepted arc is twice the measure of the inscribed angle.

[tex]m\overset{\frown}{RT} = 2(m\angle RST)[/tex]

Answer:

2. ∠x = 123°

  ∠y = 78°

  ∠z = 102°

3. ∠x = 70°

   ∠y = 64°

   ∠z = 105°

Step-by-step explanation:

1. 180 = 55 + 63 + ∠x

  ∠x = 180 - 55- 63

  ∠x = 62°

∠x and ∠y are supplementary angles:

  180 = ∠x + ∠y

  180 = 62 + ∠y

    ∠y = 180 - 62

    ∠y = 118°

2. ∠x and 57 are supplementary angles:

     180 = ∠x + 57

       ∠x = 180 - 57

       ∠x = 123°

   360 = ∠x + 116 + ∠y + 43

   360 = 123 + 116 + ∠y + 43

     ∠y = 360 - 123 -116 - 43

     ∠y = 78°

 ∠y and ∠z are supplementary angles:

      180 = ∠y + ∠z

       ∠z = 180 - ∠y

      ∠z = 180 - 78

       ∠z = 102°

3. the sum of interior angles of a pentagon is 540.

   540 =  ∠z + 116 + 112 + 97 + 110

   ∠z = 540 - 116 - 112 - 97 - 110

   ∠z = 105°

   ∠y and 116° are supplementary angles:

      180 = ∠y + 116

        ∠y = 180 - 116

        ∠y = 64°

   ∠x and 110° are supplementary angles:

     180 = ∠x + 110

       ∠x = 180 - 110

       ∠x = 70°
