1. Left Subclavian Artery​


Answer:

The left and right subclavian arteries are two major arteries in the thorax that lie beneath the clavicles. They receive blood flowing from the aortic arch, and once they pass the lateral border of the first rib, they become known as the axillary arteries.


2. left subclavian artery​


The left subclavian artery is the one below the clavicles, which are providing blood supply to the left arm and also have some branches supplying the neck and head.

13. 2. The only veins that carry oxygenated blood are the:A. subclavian veinsB. pulmonary veinsC. coronary veinsD. vena cavas​


Answer:

B. Pulmonary veins

Explanation:

The pulmonary veins carry oxygenated blood from the lungs into the left atrium where it is returned to systemic circulation. The aorta is the largest artery in the body. It carries oxygenated blood from the left ventricle of the heart into systemic circulation.

B. Pulmonary veins

The pulmonary veins carry oxygenated blood from the lungs into the left atrium where it is returned to systemic circulation. The aorta is the largest artery in the body. It carries oxygenated blood from the left ventricle of the heart into systemic circulation.

The lung veins sometimes referred to the pulmonary veins, are blood vessels that transfer freshly oxygenated blood from the lungs to the left atria of the heart.
