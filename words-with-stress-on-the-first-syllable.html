5. What is the difference between stressed and unstressed syllables? How to identify stressed syllables in words?​


What is the difference between stressed and unstressed syllables? How to identify stressed syllables in words?​

Explanation:

A stressed syllable is the part of a word that you say with greater emphasis than the other syllables. Alternatively, an unstressed syllable is a part of a word that you say with less emphasis than the stressed syllable(s). However, you don't emphasize or deemphasize syllables using volume; instead, you use pitch.

30. what all the words stressed on the first syllable​


Answer:

Words that have stress on the first syllable are considered nouns.

Examples of words with stress on the first syllable :

- Reset : To set again.

- Progress : Forward or onwards movement toward a destination.

- Subject : Something that is being discussed, described, or dealt with.

- Record : A piece of evidence from the past.

- Contrast - The state of being different from something else.
