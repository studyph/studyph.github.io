✏️TRIANGLE==============================

Question: The altitude of a triangle is 5cm less than its base. The area of the triangle is 42cm². Which of the following equations defines the area of the rectangle?

A. x (x+5) + 42 = 0B. ½ [x(x+5)] + 42 = 0C. x (x - 5) = 42D. ½ [x(x - 5)] = 42Answer: D. [tex] \small \frac{1}{2} \large \big[x(x-5)\big] = 42 \\ [/tex]

Reason: The altitude or the height is said to be 5cm less than the base. Thus, the altitude is 5cm shorter than the base.

[tex] h = b - 5 [/tex]

» The area of the triangle is 42cm². We can use the formula on how to find the area of the triangle then substitute to define its equation.

[tex] \begin{align} & \bold{Formula:} \\ & \boxed{A = \frac{1}{2} bh} \end{align} [/tex]

[tex] 42 = \frac{1}{2}\big[b(b-5)\big] \\ [/tex]

» Rearrange and change the variable [tex] b [/tex] into [tex] x [/tex] as it satisfies the given choices.

[tex] \frac{1}{2}\big[x(x-5)\big] = 42 \\ [/tex]==============================
