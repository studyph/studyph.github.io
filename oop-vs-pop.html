16. what is the difference between procedural programming and OOP? ​


Explanation:

Procedural programming and object-oriented programming (OOP) are two different programming paradigms that have distinct differences in terms of how they approach problem-solving and organization of code.

Procedural programming is a programming paradigm that emphasizes on procedures or routines, also known as functions or subroutines, to solve a problem. It involves breaking down a problem into smaller, manageable parts and then writing procedures to solve each part. The main focus is on the flow of control and the procedures that need to be executed to accomplish a specific task.

On the other hand, OOP is a programming paradigm that emphasizes on objects and their interactions to solve a problem. It involves breaking down a problem into objects and then creating classes and objects from those classes to represent the objects. The main focus is on the data (attributes) and the methods (functions) that operate on that data.

In summary, procedural programming is focused on procedures and the flow of control, while OOP is focused on objects and their interactions.
