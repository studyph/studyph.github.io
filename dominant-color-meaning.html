4. What is the meaning of dominance​


Answer:

Dominance, in genetics, greater influence by one of a pair of genes (alleles) that affect the same inherited character. If an individual pea plant with the alleles T and t (T = tallness, t = shortness) is the same height as a TT individual, the T allele (and the trait of tallness) is said to be completely dominant.

5. Is blue color dominant over red color Why or why not?​


Answer:

Pure hues are naturally dominant, though primary colors are more dominant than the others, because red, blue, and yellow literally can't be created by mixing other colors. Recessive colors, on the other hand, naturally fade into the background. They also easily take on the properties of any surrounding color.

11. The cool colors dominates the hue of?​


Answer:

COOL COLORS

Cool colors include green, blue, and purple, and variations of those three colors. Blue is the only primary color within the cool spectrum. Greens take on some of the attributes of yellow, and purple takes on some of the attributes of red. They are often more subdued than warm colors.

HUE

In color theory, hue is one of the main properties (called color appearance parameters) of a color, defined technically in the CIECAM02 model as "the degree to which a stimulus can be described as similar to or different from stimuli that are described as red, orange, yellow, green, blue, purple,"[1] which in certain theories of color vision are called unique hues.

17. in pea plants yellow pod color is recessive and green pod color is dominant a heterozygous plant produced offspring with a plant that is homozygous dominant for the trait


Answer:

Answer: There is 100% chance that the pea plant will have green pods.

Explanation-

Yellow pod color is recessive trait and green pod color is dominant trait. Let the allele for the yellow pod color be 'Y' and for the green pod color be 'y'.

Genotypes of the parents according to the question are-

Yy ( hetrozygous plant) and YY ( homozygous dominant plant)

When these parents are crossed, there is a 100% chance that the pea plant will have green pods. This is because the dominant allele Y is present in all the offsprings. This gives them a green pod color.

25. Identify which color is dominant


Answer:

Pure hues from the color wheel are innately dominant. Primary colors, though, are the most dominant (followed by secondary, then tertiary colors) because red, blue and yellow can't be created by mixing other colors. The eye also perceives dominant colors in the foreground of images and documents.

Answer:
