3. How are the limbs of a frog, whale, horse, lion, human, bat, and bird the same?


They are considered as Homologous Structures.

Limbs of a frog, whale, horse, lion, human, bat and bird are the same since they are homologous structures.

What are homologous structures?

These are body parts or organs from different organisms which are of the same characteristics. These are body parts with the same anatomical or structural features but not necessarily of the same function. This homology indicates that they are from a common ancestor.

As a conclusion, the above mentioned limbs are considered to be of the same ancestor that is why they are the same.

26. Carbon dioxide enters the blood at the a. capillaries of the abdominal organs b. capillaries of the head and forelimbs c. capillaries of the head, forelimbs, abdominal organs, and hind limbs d. capillaries of the lungs


Answer:

D. capillaries of the lungs

Explanation:

During gas exchange oxygen moves from the lungs to the bloodstream. At the same time carbon dioxide passes from the blood to the lungs. This happens in the lungs between the alveoli and a network of tiny blood vessels called capillaries, which are located in the walls of the alveoli.
