￼TESDA pursues the assessment and certification of the competencies of the middle-level skilled workers through Philippine TVET Competency Assessment and Certification System (PTCACS). 19. What are the control tests for belligerent occupation?


Answer:

Belligerent occupation refers to the military control and administration of territory that is not under the control of the occupying power's own government. The control tests for belligerent occupation are a set of criteria used to determine if a territory is considered to be under the effective control of an invading military force and whether the laws of war apply.

The control tests include:

The ability of the occupying power to maintain security and maintain public order in the occupied territory.

The ability of the occupying power to administer justice and to provide for the welfare of the population.

The ability of the occupying power to collect taxes and other revenues, and to provide basic public services.

The ability of the occupying power to maintain the infrastructure and economy of the occupied territory.

The degree of cooperation and consent of the local population to the occupation.

It's important to note that the international laws regarding belligerent occupation are not always clear, and legal disputes may arise in different situations, the above tests are used as a general guide.

y = 3 + 0.5*x
