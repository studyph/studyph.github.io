26. Technology is neutral and passive, and it does not compel man to enter. explain​


The statement that "technology is neutral and passive, and it does not compel man to enter" suggests that technology is not inherently good or bad, and it does not force people to use it or interact with it. Rather, technology is a tool or a set of tools that humans can choose to use or not use, depending on their needs, values, and preferences.

Technology is created by humans for specific purposes, such as to solve problems, improve efficiency, or enhance communication. However, the way technology is used and the impact it has on society and individuals depends on how people choose to use it. For example, social media platforms can be used to connect people, share information, and promote social causes, but they can also be used to spread misinformation, bully others, or incite violence.

In other words, technology itself is neutral and passive, but it can be used for good or bad purposes depending on the intentions and actions of the people who use it. It is up to individuals and society as a whole to determine how technology is used and what impact it has on our lives.
