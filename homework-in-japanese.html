3. what is japanese? describe the japanese?


Answer:

Japanese (日本語, Nihongo [ɲihoŋɡo] (About this soundlisten)) is an East Asian language spoken by about 128 million people, primarily in Japan, where it is the national language. It is a member of the Japonic (or Japanese-Ryukyuan) language family, and its ultimate derivation and relation to other languages is unclear. 14. TIMBRE-Description that reflects Japanese Culture.DYNAMICS-Description that reflects Japanese Culture.RHYTHM-Description that reflects Japanese Culture.MELODY-Description that reflects Japanese Culture.FORM-Description that reflects Japanese Culture.​


Answer:

TIMBRE

-Breath sounds of the shakuhachi bamboo flute

-Hard twang of the shamisen lute

DYNAMICS

-Studies in group dynamics in Japan, which started just after the Second World War, are reviewed. This review categorizes the studies into two classes: those conducted in the first generation (up to the 1970s) which still remain influential today, and those which represent the current major research trends in the area.

RHYTHM

-Flexible pulse, beatless or free rhythm (not tied to a steady beat)

-Powerful expression of feelings

-When beat is present it is in groups of 2, 4, or 8.

-Wide variety of tempos from very slow to very fast.

-Often music is associated with theater with the tempo accelerating as excitement and drama build up.

MELODY

-Melodies of folk songs differ greatly from the shakuhachi music

-Often short bits of melody repeated and varied

-Complete repetition of phrases at beginning and end commonly heard

-Changes in timbre more significant that changes in pitch or tempo in instrumental music

-Vocal music may include ornamentation

-Heterophonic texture - both or all parts basically play the same melody, but in slightly different versions.

FORM

-Based on rhythmic changes rather than melody

-Jo - introduction or slow beginning section

-Ha - "breaking apart" where tempo builds

-Kyû - "rushing" tempo reaches peak then slows to the end.

21. Mikey worked on her mathematics homework for ¾ hour and her science homework for ⅞ hour. How long did she spend in all doing homework?​


Mikey spent 1 ⅝ hour on doing all her homework.

Step-by-step explanation:

Fraction Word Problem

The problem above is asking for the total number of hours that Mikey spent on doing all her homework. To find the answer, just add the amount of hour she spent on her Mathematics homework and her Science homework.

Given:

amount of time on Mathematics homework - ¾ hour

amount of time on Science homework - ⅞ hour

Equation:

¾ + ⅞ = n

Solution:

To add dissimilar fractions, make them similar by finding their GCF. Then, divide the GCF by the denominator, times the numerator.

¾ + ⅞ (LCD: 8)

= ‌6⁄8‌ + ⅞

= 13⁄8‌ or 1 5⁄8‌

Final Answer:

Mikey spent 1 ⅝ hour on doing all her homework.
