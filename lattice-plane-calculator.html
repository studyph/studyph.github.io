6. Silver with a density of 10.51 g/cm^3 forms a face-centered cubic lattice. Use this information to calculate the radius of silver atom.​


Answer:

R = 14.44 nm

Explanation:

Calculating the mass of silver atom

m = 107.868 g/mol × (1 mol / 6.02214 × 10²³ atoms) × 4 atoms

m = 7.16476 × 10⁻²² g

Calculating the volume of silver unit cell

V = m / d

V = 7.16476 × 10⁻²² g / 10.51 g/cm³

V = 6.81709 × 10⁻²³ cm³

Calculating the radius of silver atom

V = 16√2 R³

R = ³√(V / 16√2)

R = ³√(6.81709 × 10⁻²³ cm³ / 16√2)

R = 14.44 nm

10. Barium metal crystallizes in a body centered cubic lattice. the unit cell edge length is 502 pm. calculate the atomic radius of ba.​


SOLUTION:

[tex]\begin{aligned} r & = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}a \\ & = \frac{\sqrt{3}}{4}(\text{502 pm}) \\ & = \boxed{\text{217 pm}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the atomic radius of barium is 217 pm.

17. An element crystallizes in a body-centered cubic lattice. The edge of the unit cell is 0.286 nm, and the density of the crystal is 7.92 g/cm3 . Calculate the atomic weight of the element.


Unit cell

A crystalline solid consists of repeating patterns (a crystal lattice) called a unit cell. There are seven kinds of unit cells, which differ in the lengths of the edges and the angles between them for example tetragonal, hexagonal, orthorhombic, triclinic, monoclinic, rhombohedral, and cubic. Each unit cell has six sides of a parallelogram. In the cubic unit cells, all sides have the same length and all angles are 90°. From the formula, the atomic weight of the element is 55.796 ≈ 55.8 g/mol.

Further explanation

There are 3 main structures of the unit cell:  

1. Body-Centered Cubic (BCC),  

2. Face Centered Cubic (FCC).  

3. Hexagonal Close Packed (HCP).  

If the cubic unit cell consists of 8 atoms, molecules, or ions located at the corners of the cube and also contains an identical component in the center of the cube, then it is BCC.

For all unit cells (except hexagonal), atoms on the faces contribute 1/2 atom to each unit cell, atoms on the edges contribute 1/4 atom, and atoms on the corners contribute 1/8 atom.

For example, metallic iron has a BCC unit cell, so there are 2 Fe atoms in each unit cell ( 8 x 1/8 the corners of the cube + 1 atom in the centre).

The density of a unit cell can be formulated:

[tex]\tt d=\dfrac{Z\times M}{a^3\times No}[/tex]

Z = atom per unit cell (2 for BCC lattice)

M = atomic weight

a = length of edge

No = Avogadro’s = 6.023 x 10²³

Given

The edge of the unit cell is 0.286 nm (a) = 2.86 x 10⁻⁸ m

density (d) = 7.92 g/cm³

Required

the atomic weight of the element.

Solution

[tex]\tt M=\dfrac{d\times a^3\times No}{Z}\\\\M=\dfrac{7.92\times (2.86\times 10^{-8})^3\times 6.023\times 10^{23}}{2}\\\\M=\boxed{\bold{55.796}}[/tex]

18. what is the lattice method


Example #1:

Multiply 42 and 35

 

Arrange 42 and 35 around a 2 × 2 grid

     

Draw the diagonals of the small squares

     

Multiply 3 by 4 to get 12 and put 12 in intersection of the first row and the first column

Notice that 3 is located in the first row and 4 in the first column. That is why the answer goes in the intersection.

 

By the same token, multiply 5 and 2 and put the answer in the intersection of second row and the second column

And so forth...

     

Then, going from right to left, add the numbers down the diagonals as indicated with the arrows.

The first diagonal has only 0. Bring zero down.

The second diagonal has 6, 1, 0. Add these numbers to get 7 and bring it down.

And so forth...

   

After the grid is completed, what you see in red is the answer that is 1470

Example #2:

Multiply 658 and 47

Arrange 657 and 47 around a 3 × 2 grid

   

Draw the diagonals of the small squares, find products, and put the answers in intersecting rows and columns

   

Then, going from right to left, add the numbers down the diagonals as shown before.

The first diagonal has only 6. Bring 6 down.

The second diagonal has 2, 5, and 5. Add these numbers to get 12. Bring 2 down and carry the 1 over to the next diagonal.

The third diagonal has 3, 0, 3, and 2. Add these numbers to get 8 and add 1 (your carry) to 8 to get 9.

and so forth...

   

After the grid is completed, what you see in red is the answer to the multiplication that is 30926

I understand that this may be your first encounter with the lattice method for multiplication. It may seem that it is tough. Just practice with other examples and you will be fine.
