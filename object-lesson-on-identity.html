A. Identical arrangement of objects

A permutation of non-distinct objects is a rearrangement of a set of objects that may contain duplicate elements. An identical arrangement of objects refers to arranging those objects in such a way that the order of the objects is the same, even though the objects themselves may be the same. So, the phrase "identical arrangement of objects" is the best description for a permutation of non-distinct objects.
