1. causes and effects of global warming in the Philippines​


Answer:

The Main cause of global warming in the Philippines is​ the emission of greenhouse gases, mostly carbon dioxide (CO2) and methane. Burning fossil fuels for energy use creates most of these emissions. Agriculture, steelmaking, cement production, and forest loss are additional sources of global warming in the Philippines.

The Effects of Global Warming in the Philippines is that it threatens people with food and water scarcity, increased flooding, extreme heat, more disease, and economic loss.It also create annual losses in GDP, changes in rainfall patterns and distribution, droughts, threats to biodiversity and food security, sea level rise, public health risks, and endangerment of vulnerable groups such as women and indigenous people.

Explanation:

Global warming is an aspect of climate change, referring to the long-term rise of the planet's temperatures. It is caused by increased concentrations of greenhouse gases in the atmosphere, mainly from human activities such as burning fossil fuels, and farming.
