2. Find the volume of a cuboid. Three different walls of the cuboid have areas: 96,48,32​


• Question:

Find the volume of a cuboid. Three different

walls of the cuboid have areas: 96,48,32

______________________________

• Solution:

Use Formula: V = a × b × c

The cuboid walls are rectangles or squares, so the formula for an area of each wall is P = a × b. To calculate the length of the sides solve the following system of equations:

[tex]\begin{cases}a \times b = 96 \\ a \times c = 48 \\ b \times c = 32 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{cases}a = \frac{96}{b} \\ a \times c = 48 \\ b \times c = 32 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{cases}\frac{96}{b} \times c = 48 \\ b \times c = 32 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{cases}c = 48 \times \frac{b}{96} \\ b \times c = 32\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{cases}b \times (48 \times \frac{b}{96}) = 32 \\ \frac{48b^2}{96} = 32 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex]\begin{cases}\frac{48b²}{96} = 32 \\ 48b² = 3072 \\ b = \sqrt{\frac{3072}{48}} = 8\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex]c = 48 \times \frac{b}{96} = 48 \times \frac{8}{96} = 4[/tex]

[tex]a = \frac{96}{b} = \frac{96}{8} = 12[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\underline{V = a \times b \times c = 12 \times 8 \times 4 = \underline{{\boxed{\red{384}}}}}}[/tex]

Answer:

Cuboid is a solid box whose every surface is a rectangle of same area or different areas.

A cuboid will have a length, breadth and height.

Hence we can conclude that volume is 3 dimensional. To measure the volumes we need to know the measure 3 sides.

Units of Volume

Volume of a cuboid = (length × breadth × height) cubic units.

= (l × b × h) cubic units.

(Since area = ℓ × b)

Volume of a cuboid = area of one surface × height cubic units

Let us look at the given cuboid.

The length of the cuboid = 5 cm

The breadth of the cuboid = 3 cm

The height of cuboid (thickness) = 2 cm

The number of 1 cm cubes in the given cuboid = 30 cubes = 5 × 3 × 2

We find that volume of the given cuboid with length 5 cm, breadth 3 cm and height 2 cm is 30 cu cm.

Therefore, volume of a cuboid = length × breadth × height

13. what are the ABCD area of a cuboid​


Answer:

The ABCD area of a cuboid is not a commonly used term in mathematics. However, a cuboid has six faces, and each face can be divided into two congruent rectangles. The area of each of these rectangles is given by the formula A = lw, where l is the length and w is the width of the rectangle. Therefore, the total area of the six rectangles that make up the faces of the cuboid is given by:

ABCD area = 2lw + 2lh + 2wh

where l, w, and h are the length, width, and height of the cuboid, respectively.

27. no of faces of cuboid​


Answer:

6

Step-by-step explanation:

By Euler's formula the numbers of faces F, of vertices V, and of edges E of any convex polyhedron are related by the formula F + V = E + 2. In the case of a cuboid this gives 6 + 8 = 12 + 2; that is, like a cube, a cuboid has 6 faces, 8 vertices, and 12 edges.
