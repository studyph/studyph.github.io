How to Find the Volume of a Sphere?

The volume of a sphere is the amount of space occupied by it. For a hollow sphere like a football, the volume can be viewed as the number of cubic units required to fill up the sphere.

To find the volume of a sphere, you only need to know the radius of the sphere.

The volume of a sphere is measured in cubic units, i.e., m3, cm3, in3, ft3, etc

Life on earth would be like if it is not a sphere:

No gravityThe position of the Earth with respect to the Sun becomes difficult to explainThe flight route will turn out to be weirdEarth will not rotate

EXPLANATION:

1. No gravity

In fact, it is the force of gravitational attraction that makes our planet spherical because it has been rotating since its inception. So, if the Earth were flat or triangular, gravity would probably never exist. If anything, it will have a strange way of working, namely pulling objects straight down to the bottom. Well, this is very contrary to the working principle of gravity and mass. In fact, the two things are highly correlated with each other.

2. The position of the Earth with respect to the Sun becomes difficult to explain

It is clear that the Earth moves around the Sun based on its orbit. In addition, the Earth also continues to rotate on its axis and this rotation causes day and night. There is also an equinox event, which is a phenomenon when the Sun is right on the Earth's equator. This will cause changes in the duration of day and night.

3. The flight route will turn out to be weird

Well, if the Earth wasn't round, the flight routes could be very strange. Maybe, the distance from the country at the end of the Earth will feel very far and tiring or it could be that the distance between the two is very short and doesn't take long. However, despite the various theories that exist, the flight routes in the world actually prove that the shape of the Earth is round.

4. Earth will not rotate

According to NASA, Earth rotates on its axis relative to the Sun every 24 hours at an inclination of 23.45 degrees from the plane of its orbit. This proves that the Earth is a spherical object that continues to rotate on its axis.
