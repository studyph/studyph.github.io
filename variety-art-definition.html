1. In your own words write down your own definition for the following principles of art. 1.balance2.emphasis3.harmony4.variety5.movement6.rhythm7.proportion:) ​


Answer:

1. BALANCE :

Balance in Art refers to the use of artistic elements such as line, texture, color, and form in the creation of artworks in a way that renders visual stability. Balance is one of the principles of organization of structural elements of art and design, along with unity, proportion, emphasis and rhythm.

2. EMPHASIS :

Emphasis is the principle of art that helps the audience put the story of a painting together in their own minds. Any object or area of emphasis is called a focal point. The focal point is meant to be the part of an artwork to which the viewer's eyes are first attracted. Artworks can have multiple focal points.

3. HARMONY :

Harmony is the principle of art that creates cohesiveness by stressing the similarities of separate but related parts. ... Specifically, harmony uses the elements of art (color, line, shape, form, value, space, texture) as a vehicle to create a sense of togetherness amongst otherwise separate parts.

4. VARIETY :

Variety is the principle of art that adds interest to an artwork. Variety works through juxtaposition and contrast. When an artist places different visual elements next to one another, he/she is using variety. Straight lines next to curvy lines add variety.

5. MOVEMENT :

Movement is the principle of art that an artist uses to guide a viewer's eye in, through, and out of a composition.

6. RHYTHM :

Rhythm is a principle of design that suggests movement or action. Rhythm is usually achieved through repetition of lines, shapes, colors, and more. It creates a visual tempo in artworks and provides a path for the viewer's eye to follow.

7. PROPORTION :

Proportion is the principle of art that refers to relative size. The principles of art differ from the elements of art. ... By manipulating proportion, the artist can make his/her subject seem strong, weak, funny, mysterious, etc. We can exaggerate proportions to emphasize a meaning or an element within the scene.

7. Rhythm- often the use of regular, evenly distributed elements

8. Variety- a sense of the difference between elements of an artwork – the opposite of unity, or harmony.
