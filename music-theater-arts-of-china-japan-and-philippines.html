Japanese theater:,

Kabuki (歌舞伎) is a traditional Japanese form of theater with roots tracing back to the Edo Period. It is recognized as one of Japan's three major classical theaters along with noh and bunraku, and has been named as a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage.

Chinese theater:,

Traditional Chinese theatre, often called Chinese opera, is musical in nature. Western forms like the spoken drama, western-style opera, and ballet did not arrive in China until the 20th century.

Indonesia theater:,

The two types of Indonesian theatre are known as Teater tradisional (Traditional Theatre) and Teater Modern Modern (Non-Traditional Theatre).

Thailand theatre:,

Thai National Theatre reflects the glory of Thai art. It is the important place established from the idea of King Vachiravudh (Rama VI) that Khon, Thai dance, and musical artists should perform their art to the audience. They therefore need a place or a theatre.
