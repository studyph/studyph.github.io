6. Differentiate true fossil, mold fossil, cast fossil and trace fossil.


True fossil
- actual plant or animal 
- animals or plants trapped in ice, amber, etc.
- soft tissues never decay

Mold fossil
- impression of the organism in rock
- when the organism is dissolved, this fossil is left

Cast fossil
- mold that is filled in and turned to rock
- shows the organism in 3D form

Trace fossil
- this fossil can happen when an animal walked in mud and then the mud hardened
- shows the evidence of an activity

Answer:

Explanation:

Body Fossils and Trace Fossils

The fossils of bones, teeth, and shells are called body fossils. Most dinosaur fossils are collections of body fossils. Trace fossils are rocks that have preserved evidence of biological activity. They are not fossilized remains, just the traces of organisms.

What are fossils?

Fossil is the rest of very old organic material of animal or plant origin, which has been preserved for thousands of years in the Earth's soil and subsoil.

Fossils can be classified into somatofossils and ichnofossils.

somatofossil

The living fossilized organism is called a somatofossil. Ex: leaves, bones and teeth.

ichnofossil

And the traces of living beings are called ichnofossil. Ex: footprints, tracks and feces.
