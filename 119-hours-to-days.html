9. After 119 days have elapse 1/21 of a radioisotope sample remains undecayed. What is the half-life of the radioisotope?


The half-life of the radioisotope : 27 days

Further explanation  

The atomic nucleus can experience decay into 2 particles or more due to the instability of its atomic nucleus.  

Usually radioactive elements have an unstable atomic nucleus.  

The main particles are emitted by radioactive elements ,so that they generally decay, are alpha (α), beta (β) and gamma (γ) particles  

General formulas used in decay:  

[tex]\large{\boxed{\bold{N_t=N_0(\dfrac{1}{2})^{T/t\frac{1}{2} }}}[/tex]

T = duration of decay  

t 1/2 = half-life  

N₀ = the number of initial radioactive atoms  

Nt = the number of radioactive atoms left after decaying during T time  

Given

T = 119 days

Nt = 1/21 No

Required

the half-life of the radioisotope

Solution

Input the value :

[tex]\tt \dfrac{1}{21}=\dfrac{1}{2}^{119/t1/2}\\\\log~\dfrac{1}{21}=\dfrac{119}{t1/2}~log~\dfrac{1}{2}\\\\-1.322=\dfrac{119}{t1/2}.-0.301\rightarrow t1/2=27.095\approx \boxed{\bold{27~days}}[/tex]
