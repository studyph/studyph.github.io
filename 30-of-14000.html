10. 45. the potential energy of a 40-kg cannon ball is 14000 j. how high was the cannon ball to have this much potential energy


Answer:

35.7 m

Explanation:

Given Data

• Mass of the cannonball, m = 40 kg

• Potential energy stored by cannonball, U = 14000 J

Finding the height (h) of the cannonball from the ground

With respect to the ground, the potential energy of the cannonball at a height h is expressed as:

U = m × g × h

14000 J = 40 kg × 9.8 m/s² × h

h = 14000 J

——————————

40 kg × 9.8 m/s²

≈ 35.7m

14. the potential energy of 40 kg cannonball is 14000 j how high was the canyon ball to have this much potential energymaayus nasagut po with solution ​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The height or position of an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{h = \frac{PE}{mg}}[/tex]

where:

h = height

PE = potential energy

g = acceleration due to gravity = 9.8 m/s²

h = height

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the height

[tex]h = \dfrac{PE}{mg}[/tex]

[tex]h = \dfrac{\text{14,000 J}}{(\text{40 kg})(\text{9.8 m/}\text{s}^{2})}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{h = \text{35.71 m}}[/tex]

Therefore, the cannon ball is at the height of 35.71 m.

23. Given that 14000 m of land is divided into three plots in the ratio 3:2:2.what is measurement of the largest plot?​


Answer:

Rules of dividing a quantity in three given ratios is explained below along with the different types of examples.

If a quantity K is divided into three parts in the ratio X : Y : Z, then

First part = X/(X + Y + Z) × K,

Second part = Y/(X + Y + Z) × K,

Third part = Z/(X + Y + Z) × K.

For example, suppose, we have to divide $ 1200 among X, Y, Z in the ratio 2 : 3 : 7. This means that if X gets 2 portions, then Y will get 3 portions and Z will get 7 portions. Thus, total portions = 2 + 3 + 7 = 12. So, we have to divide $ 1200 into 12 portions and then distribute the portions among X, Y, Z according to their share.

Thus, X will get 2/12 of $ 1200, i.e., 2/12 × 1200 = $ 200

Y will get 3/12 of $ 1200, i.e., 3/12 × 1200 = $ 300

Z will get 7/12 of $ 1200, i.e., 7/12 × 1200 = $ 700
