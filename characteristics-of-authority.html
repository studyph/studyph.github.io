9. _________connections show how the books share the same author, have similar characteristics, events, or setting. a text-to-lifeb text-to-author c text-to-song d. text-to-text​


BOOKS THAT SHARE THE SAME AUTHOR AND HAVE SIMILAR CHARACTERISTICS

ANSWER: D. Text-to-text

When anything in the text reminds the reader of something they've already read, it's called a text-to-text connection.

A text's purpose is merely the author's motivation for creating it. Writers can choose from a range of goals, which are typically divided into three categories: entertaining, informing, and persuading. Novels, stories, and poems are common examples of texts written to entertain.

Students who build connections while reading are more likely to comprehend the material. When students connect, they are more engaged in the reading experience because they are thinking. When students develop genuine connections to a text, they gain a better grasp of it.

Students can improve their comprehension abilities, increase critical material knowledge and vocabulary, and apply higher-order thinking skills by reading informational texts. In order for children to access challenging informational literature, scaffolding and the introduction of new reading methods may be required.
