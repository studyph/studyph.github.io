20. V. ASSESSMENT (Time Frame: 5 minutes)Differentiate the four types of speech delivery in terms of definition and give one example for each. Use the table below foryour answers.TypesDeilnikonExampleExtemporaneousImpromptuManuscriptMemorizedpasagot pag tama brainlist ko pag mali report ko​


Four Types of Speech Delivery

1. Extemporaneous

Delivering a speech without notes in a conversational manner is known as extemporaneous speaking. Most speeches require a format like this. Speaking from a script is referred to as manuscript speaking. It is helpful when a message needs to be communicated clearly.

As the speaker may use notes or cards to deliver his address, an extemporaneous speech is one that requires little preparation. Speakers must continue to communicate spontaneously and in their own language. Good structure is crucial for presentations in particular.

2. Impromptu

An impromptu speech is one that you give when you don't have any prior planning. Impromptu refers to a situation that has not been planned or organized. Delivering a message on the spur of the moment, such as when someone is requested to say a few words, is referred to as impromptu speaking. Improvised speaking includes, for instance, introducing oneself when you meet someone. A similar example of impromptu speaking is saying hello to a familiar face you see in the grocery store.

3. Manuscript

Iterating a written message verbatim is known as manuscript speaking. The speaker in a manuscript speech keeps his or her eyes on the written page, with the exception of when using visual aids. Reading from a manuscript has the benefit of providing accurate repetitions of the original words.

Few examples of manuscript are a speech delivered at an international convention, an address from the president or prime minister, an on-screen news report that was broadcast using a teleprompter, a fresh declaration from the pope.

4. Memorized

A speech that has been committed to memory in its entirety is called a memorized speech. Because the speaker is more intent on recalling the speech's text than on interacting with the audience, this style also damages relationships with them.

Reciting verbatim a written word that has been committed to memory by the speaker is known as speaking from memory. Naturally, while performing from a script, actors recite from memory.

A speech that is delivered from memory is one that has been meticulously prepared in advance and does not include any notes. Word-for-word memorization might be useful for speeches given on special occasions, such as a quick toast, a quick dedication, or a rapid eulogy.
