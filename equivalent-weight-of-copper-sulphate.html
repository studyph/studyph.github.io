Evaporation is used to separate a soluble solid from a liquid. For example, copper sulfate is soluble in water – its crystals dissolve in water to form copper sulfate solution. During evaporation, the water evaporates away leaving solid copper sulfate crystals behind.


2. chemical formula of Copper (II) sulphate?


Answer:

Copper(II) sulfate, also known as copper sulphate, are the inorganic compounds with the chemical formula CuSO4(H2O)x, where x can range from 0 to 5. The pentahydrate (x = 5) is the most common form. Older names for this compound include blue vitriol, bluestone, vitriol of copper, and Roman vitriol.

19. A 10 peso coin weight 8.7grams. It’s consist of 6% aluminum, 2% nickel and 92% copper. How many grams of copper are in 10 peso coin containing 92% copper by mass?


Percent Mass

There will be 8.004 g of copper in a 10 peso coin.

Explanation:

Percent concentration tells us how much of the component is present in a mixture. One way to describe the concentration is the percent mass of a component in a mixture. It is the ratio of the mass of the component in relation to the mass of the whole mixture and multiplied by 100%:

    Percent mass =  [tex]\frac{mass of a component}{mass of the mixture}[/tex] x 100%

However, if we are to determine the mass of the component given the percent mass, here is the formula:

    mass of component = [tex]\frac{percent mass}{100}[/tex] x mass of the mixture

Thus, in the given problem, we can solve for the mass of copper in 10 peso coin by substituting the values to the above formula

    mass of copper = [tex]\frac{92}{100}[/tex] x 8.7

                               = 0.92 x 8.7 g

                              = 8.004 g

23. A black, crystalline solid forms a purple vapor when it is heated. What is the nature of the solid? A. copper sulphate B. iodine C. phosphorus D. sulfur


A black, crystalline solid that forms a purple vapor when it is heated – the solid nature is Iodine.

What happens when Iodine is heated? Iodine is a black solid that transforms into a beautiful purple vapor when heated. The resulting purple vapor has a harsh odor. Heating iodine crystals undergo chemical change.

The chemical properties of Iodine are similar with halogens above it such as Fluorine, Chlorine and Bromine. These halogens are lighter than Iodine. Like the other halogens, iodine is an active element, but it is less active than Fluorine, Chlorine and Bromine.

The symbol of Iodine in the Periodic Table of Elements is "I" with an atomic number of 53. It belongs to Family Group Halogen.

The most important function of Iodine may be its ability to kill germs. It is present in antiseptics, commercial products that eliminate germs, and other medical products.

Here is the explanation of Iodine Sublimation: The process that occurs when the solid Iodine transforms into vapor when heated is called the process of sublimation. Sublimation occurs at very low temperatures (cold) and low pressures. An example that undergoes sublimation is dry ice. Unlike the ice that we normally know, dry ice does not melt into water. It just releases vapor.
