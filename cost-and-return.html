25. An investment opportunity costing 150,000 is expected to yield net cash flows of 45,000 annually for five years. the cost of capital is 10%. the book rate of return would be


BOOK RATE OF RETURN

If an investment opportunity costing 150,000 is expected to yield a net cash flow of 45,000 annually for five years while the cost of capital is 10%, the book rate of return would be 30%.

How to solve for the book rate of return?

First, we have to know which formula we are going to use after analyzing the given.We have the initial investment and the annual net profit.To compute the rate of return, we can simply divide the annual net profit by the initial investment, then multiply the answer by 100%.

Solving for RoR:

RoR = [tex]\frac{45,000}{150,000}[/tex] = 0.3 x 100% = 30%
