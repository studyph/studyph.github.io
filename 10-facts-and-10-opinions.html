4. The city was named after the Marikina River, which runs through it.

5. Marikina City is home to the Marikina Sports Center, a world-class sports facility used for various events and competitions.

6. The city is also known for its delicious food, particularly its famous street food like "kakanin" and "puto bumbong."

7. Marikina City has a rich history, dating back to the Spanish colonial period. Some of its historical landmarks include the Marikina Shoe Museum and the Our Lady of the Abandoned Parish.

8. The city has a well-developed public transportation system, with jeepneys, buses, and tricycles serving different parts of the city.

9. Marikina City is also home to several educational institutions, including the Marikina Polytechnic College and the Ateneo de Manila University - Marikina.

10. The city has a comprehensive disaster risk reduction and management program, which has helped it become one of the most disaster-resilient cities in the Philippines.
