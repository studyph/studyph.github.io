3. What are the nursing implications for each route of medication administration?​


Medicine use and safety

A drug is defined as any chemical that can affect living processes (Burchum and Rosenthal, 2016); this article focuses on those that are administered for therapeutic effect. To make it suitable for administration to a patient, a drug has to be formulated into a preparation that enables it to be taken or given – such as a tablet or injection. Once formulated, the drug becomes a medicine; however, the two terms tend to be used interchangeably. Ideally, any medicine should be effective, safe and selective in its mode of action.

Effectiveness refers to how the body responds to a medicine; if a medicine fails to lead to its intended result, there is no benefit to prescribing it (Burcham and Rosenthal, 2016). In the UK, the National Institute for Health and Care Excellence carries out technology appraisals on the use of new and existing medicines and treatments in the NHS; these use clinical evidence on effectiveness and economic evidence on whether the medicine/treatment represents value for money.

IMPLICATION OF THE "LOVE OF LABORED IS LOVE OF GOD

We remember your faith-based work, your love-based labor, and your faith-based persistence in our Lord Jesus Christ before our God and Father.

Work that is done without payment is referred to as a "labor of love." It's work that's driven by a sense of purpose, passion, or pleasure. "Remembering without stopping your... labor of love," says the King James Version of 1 Thessalonians 1:3, which is a literal translation of the ancient Greek, which is adequately reflected by the NIV's "labor spurred by love" translation.

What is the meaning of this phrase? The author of 1 Thessalonians, Paul, has traditionally been interpreted as clearly referring to Christian behaviors such as forgiving others or sharing the Gospel with non-believers. Limiting our "work" to such tasks, however, appears to be a bad idea. Furthermore, Paul later in 1 Thessalonians links love for others with an admonition to "work with your hands" since the Greek term translated here as "labor" denotes activity that needs substantial effort, what we'd call "hard work" (4:9-11). Manual labor, the job we do every day to sustain life, appears to be a part of the labor that arises from love.

22. ano ang implication meaning


Answer:

Ang implikasyon ay ang ugnayan sa pagitan ng dalawang mga pahayag ng isang pahayag na may pasubali (pahayag na may kondisyon) na A, na tinatawag na nauuna (antesedente) at B, na tinatawag na kinahinatnan o konsekwente (kinalabasan, bunga, resulta) kapag ang A ay nagpapahiwatig ng B.

HALIMBAWA NG IMPLIKASYON

Maraming taong nanood sa konsiyerto ni Alden.

Ang implikasyon ay sikat si Alden.

(Malamang maraming nanood sa konsiyerto dahil sikat si Alden.)

(Ipinapahiwatig ng dami ng tao ang kasikatan ni Alden.)

Ang implikasyon ay may kaugnayan sa mga salitang pagkakadamay, pagkakasangkot, pagsasangkot, pagdaramay, dalawit, dawit, at pagkakadawit.

Maaari rin itong maging kasingkahulugan ng salitang “epekto” o “resulta” sa pang-araw-araw na usapan.
