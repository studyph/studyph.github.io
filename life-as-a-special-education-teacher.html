21. B. A specialized service that helps a student with a disability - Related services are specialized services designed to help students with disabilities meet their individualized education program (IEP) goals.

22. A. Speech therapy - Speech therapy is an example of a related service. Other examples may include occupational therapy, physical therapy, counseling, or transportation.

23. B. The school - The school district is responsible for providing related services to students with disabilities as part of their IEP.

24. D. The act of including students with disabilities into general education as much as possible - Inclusion is the practice of ensuring that students with disabilities are included in the general education classroom and provided with the support they need to succeed.

25. B. A plan for students leaving high school - A transition plan is created for students with disabilities as they prepare to leave high school and enter post-secondary education or the workforce.

26. B. To help the student transition smoothly to the next school level or life after high school - The goal of a transition plan is to help the student with disabilities successfully navigate the transition from high school to post-secondary education, employment, or other adult activities.

27. C. The student’s parents and teachers - The transition plan is developed collaboratively by the student, family, educators and other professionals who have been involved in the student's education.

28. B. Goals for after high school - A transition plan should include a set of measurable post-secondary goals based on the student's preferences, interests, and strengths.

29. A. Yes - An IEP can still be in effect after a student graduates high school, until the student turns 22 years old or until the state's age of majority is reached.

30. B. To have all of the relevant paperwork and information in one place for easy access - An IEP binder is used to keep all documentation related to a student's IEP, such as progress reports, assessment results, and meeting notes, organized and easily accessible for review or reference.
