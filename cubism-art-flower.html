Answer:

Cubism is an early-20th-century avant-garde art movement that revolutionized European painting and sculpture, and inspired related movements in music, literature and architecture. In Cubist artwork, objects are analyzed, broken up and reassembled in an abstracted form—instead of depicting objects from a single viewpoint, the artist depicts the subject from a multitude of viewpoints to represent the subject in a greater context.[1] Cubism has been considered the most influential art movement of the 20th century.[2][3] The term is broadly used in association with a wide variety of art produced in Paris (Montmartre and Montparnasse) or near Paris (Puteaux) during the 1910s and throughout the 1920s.

12. describe the cubism, futurism, optical art, pop art ,installation art, performance art, ​

Optical Art - a style of visual art that uses optical illusions. Op art works are abstract, with many better known pieces created in black and white

Perform Art -it is created through actions perform by the artist or other participants

26. What is Impressionism, Expressionism, Surrealism, Cubism, and Abstact Expressionism in Arts​


Impressionism: Impressionism is a 19th century art movement characterized by relatively small, thin, yet visible brush strokes, open composition, emphasis on the accurate depiction of light in its changing qualities, ordinary subject matter, inclusion of movement as a crucial element of human perception and experience, and unusual visual angles.Expressionism: Expressionism is an early 20th century art movement that emphasized the expression of inner feelings and emotions rather than the accurate representation of objective reality. Expressionist works often employ distortion, exaggeration, primitivism, and fantasy to achieve emotional intensity.Surrealism: Surrealism is a 20th century avant-garde movement in art and literature that sought to release the creative potential of the unconscious mind, using techniques such as automatism, the rejection of rationalism, and the use of fantastic imagery.Cubism: Cubism is a 20th century avant-garde art movement characterized by the reduction of natural forms into basic geometric elements, such as circles, squares, and rectangles. Cubism also rejected traditional perspective, which resulted in an image that was flattened and fragmented.Abstract Expressionism: Abstract Expressionism is a mid-20th century modern art movement characterized by large, abstract canvases that are heavily influenced by the artist's emotions and inner turmoil. The resulting works often feature bold colors and vigorous brushstrokes. Abstract Expressionism is often seen as a reaction against the conventions of the art world.
