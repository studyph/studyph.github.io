21. what value is reflected in the poem?​


Answer:

Reflective poems contain some explicit or implicit generalisation about life, by the poet. Simply put, it is a mere reflection of the state of mind of the poet put forth in a poetic manner.

Here is an example of a reflective poem composed by me.

Finding meaning in what I do,

Is a real task. I wish I knew,

How to go about, or what to do,

Whom to talk with, I wish I knew.

The pointless thoughts ticking around,

A real meaning waiting to be found.

Is there any? My heart pounds,

Diverting my mind, I sleep sound.

It must mean something, I think.

After a sound sleep, in surprise, I blink.

I have to leave this worldly world,

In order to search for a meaning.

For it is only me, and I can do it.

I realise that the meaning I've been looking for,

Is not of the world,

But that of me.

And, all this while, it suddenly strikes,

Whatever I'm looking for, will be found

Nowhere in the outer world,

But within myself.

For it's actually no meaning

I've been looking for,

It is myself.

28. Poem with rhymes about claims of value​


Claims of value, oh so grand,
Are often made across the land.
People argue, they debate,
Which things are good or bad, or great.

Some say honesty is key,
Others think it's empathy.
Some say freedom is the goal,
Others say we need control.

But in the end, it's hard to say,
Which claims of value hold sway.
For every argument we hear,
There's another one that's near.

So let us listen, let us learn,
And let our minds be free to turn.
For claims of value, though they rhyme,
May not be true all of the time.
