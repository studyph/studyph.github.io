when you drag a box on the floor what happen to it?

Right so the system in the question is a box being dragged on a surface (which in this case isn’t completely smooth). Let’s assume that the surface is quite consistent. And let’s just talk about the part where the box is already moving, so we don’t have to talk about the friction that prevents it from moving in first place, known as static friction.

So the friction when an object is moving is called kinetic friction, and is calculated as mu•N where mu is a constant and N is normal reaction force. Normal force is equal to the weight of an object on a flat surface as long as the object doesn’t float away. So when an object is moving the frictional force it has Fr=mu•m•g (m is the mass and g is the gravitational acceleration). So the answer to your question the magnitude of the frictional force on a surface is determined by its mass and nothing affects the frictional force (not even speed or how hard you pull it).

20. a crate is dragged across an ice covered lake. the box accelerates at 0.08 m/S2 and is pulled by a 47 N force. what is the mass of the box?​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The mass of an object is given by

[tex]\boxed{m = \frac{F}{a}}[/tex]

where:

F = force

m = mass

a = acceleration

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the mass

[tex]m = \dfrac{F}{a}[/tex]

[tex]m = \dfrac{\text{47 N}}{\text{0.08 m/}\text{s}^{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{m = \text{587.5 kg}}[/tex]

Therefore, the mass of the box is 587.5 kg.

30. A box whose mass is 45kg is moved across a horizontal floor by dragging it with a rope attached to the front end with a force of 200N. If the rope makes an angle of 30° with the horizontal, how much work is done in dragging the box over a distance of 5.75m ?​


Answer:

W=8,912.5 J

Explanation:

Given:

m=45, kg=45N

f=200 N

d=5.75 m

cos=30 degrees

solution:

F=f cos

F=45Ncos30 degrees

F=1,350 N

Computation for total work:

W=F d

W=1,350N(5.75 m)

W=7,762.5 J

W= F d

W=200N(5.75 m)

W=1,150 J

total work:

W=7,762.5 J + 1,150 J

W=8,912.5 J
