1. how do you add a footer to a slide​


Answer:

Click INSERT > Header & Footer.

On the Slide tab, check Footer.

In the box below Footer, type the text that you want, such as the presentation title.

Check Date and time to add that to your slides.

Check Slide number to add that to your slides.

To stop the footer from appearing on the title slide, check Don't show on title slide.

Click Apply to All. Or, if you want the footer information only on the selected slide, click Apply instead of Apply to All.

SOURCE: Microsoft.com

3. What is the difference between google slide in ipad and google slide in desktop computers ?


The main difference between Google Slides on an iPad and Google Slides on a desktop computer is the level of functionality and the user interface.

Google Slides on a desktop computer generally has more features and options than the iPad version, also the user interface of Google Slides on an iPad is optimized for touch-based navigation, and is generally more streamlined than the desktop version.

The iPad version can be used offline but the desktop version does not have offline feature unless you install the google chrome extension to make it available offline.

The desktop version generally has more accessibility options like screen reader, keyboard shortcuts, high-contrast mode, etc. than the iPad version.

15. List of the Google Slide interface and write a short description each. Give atleast 5 Interfaces of Google Slide


Answer:

Title Slide: The Title Slide is the first slide in a Google Slides presentation, and it typically contains the presentation title, subtitle, and the name of the presenter.

Theme: The Theme interface in Google Slides allows you to customize the look and feel of your presentation. You can choose from a variety of pre-designed templates or create your own custom theme.

Slides Overview: The Slides Overview interface in Google Slides provides a bird's-eye view of all the slides in your presentation. You can use this interface to rearrange, delete, or duplicate slides.

Insert Menu: The Insert Menu interface in Google Slides provides access to a variety of options for adding content to your presentation. You can insert text boxes, images, videos, charts, tables, and more.

Speaker Notes: The Speaker Notes interface in Google Slides is where you can add notes to each slide that are not visible to the audience. These notes can be used to remind yourself of important talking points or to provide additional information that is not included on the slide itself.
