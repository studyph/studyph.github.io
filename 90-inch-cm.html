7. What is the perimeter of a 30-60-90 triangle whose shorter leg is 5 inches long? a. 5 3 cm c. 15 + 3 cm b. 15 + 5 3 cm d. 10 + 5 3 cm


The 30-60-90 triangle theorem states that the hypotenuse is twice the measure of the shorter leg. Furthermore, the sides of this type of triangle is in the ratio 1:2:[tex] \sqrt{3} [/tex]


Hence, since the shorter leg measures 5 inches, 

hypotenuse is equal to 5(2) = 10 inches, 

and the longer leg is 5[tex] \sqrt{3} [/tex]

Then the perimeter is 5+10+5[tex] \sqrt{3} [/tex] = 15+5[tex] \sqrt{3} [/tex] 

Hence, the answer is B. 15+5[tex] \sqrt{3} [/tex] inches

1. The circumference of a circle is given by the formula C = 2πr, where r is the radius and π is the constant pi, approximately equal to 3.14. Substituting r = 90 cm, we get:

C = 2π(90 cm)

C = 180π cm

C ≈ 565.48 cm

Therefore, the circumference of the circle is approximately 565.48 cm. The closest option is (a) 592.2 cm.

2. The circumference of a circle is given by the formula C = πd, where d is the diameter. Solving for d, we get:

d = C / π

Substituting C = 111.47 inches, we get:

d = 111.47 inches / π

d ≈ 35.5 inches

Therefore, the diameter of the circle is approximately 35.5 inches. The answer is (c) 35.5 in.

3. Similarly, we can solve for the radius of the circle using the formula C = 2πr:

C = 2πr

r = C / (2π)

Substituting C = 355.45 inches, we get:

r = 355.45 inches / (2π)

r ≈ 56.6 inches

Therefore, the radius of the circle is approximately 56.6 inches. The answer is (b) 56.6 in.

4. Again using the formula C = πd, we can solve for the diameter of the wheel in terms of its circumference:

C = πd

d = C / π

Substituting C = 301.4 cm, we get:

d = 301.4 cm / π

d ≈ 95.99 cm

Therefore, the diameter of the rim is approximately 95.99 cm. The answer is (c) 95.99 cm.
