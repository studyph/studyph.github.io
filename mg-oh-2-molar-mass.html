4. In an experiment to measure the molar mass of a gas, 250 cm3 of the gas was confined in a glass vessel. The pressure was 152 Torr at 298 K and, after correcting for buoyancy effects, the mass of the gas was 33.5 mg. What is the molar mass of the gas?​


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & P = \text{152 torr = 0.200 atm} \\ & V = \text{250 cm}^3 = \text{0.250 L} \\ & T = \text{298 K} \\ & mass = \text{33.5 mg = 0.0335 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of gas using ideal gas equation.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n & = \frac{PV}{\text{RT}} \\ & = \frac{(\text{0.200 atm})(\text{0.250 L})}{\left(0.082057 \: \dfrac{\text{L}\cdot\text{atm}}{\text{mol}\cdot\text{K}}\right)(\text{298 K})} \\ & = \text{0.0020447 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molar mass of the gas.

[tex]\begin{aligned} MM & = \frac{mass}{n} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.0335 g}}{\text{0.0020447 mol}} \\ & = \text{16.384 g/mol} \\ & \approx \boxed{\text{16.4 g/mol}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molar mass of the gas is 16.4 g/mol.

Ca = 40

O = 16

H = 1

In essence, the molar mass of Ca(OH)2 is;

Ca(OH)2 = 40 + (16+1) × 2

Ca(OH)2 = 74

Therefore;

% Ca = 40/74 × 100% = 54.05%

% O = 32/74 × 100% = 43.24%

14. What is the molarity of a 5L solution containing 60 g Mg(OH)2?​


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of solute.

molar mass = (24.31 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 2) + (1.008 g/mol × 2)

molar mass = 58.326 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 60 g Mg(OH)₂} × \frac{\text{1 mol Mg(OH)₂}}{\text{58.326 g Mg(OH)₂}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 1.0287 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (L)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{1.0287 mol}}{\text{5 L}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molarity = 0.206 M}}[/tex]

21. what is the molarity of a 5000 ml solution containing 60.0g Mg (OH)²? (Mg-24.31 g, O - 16.00 g and H - 1.008 g)​


First, we need to calculate the number of moles of Mg(OH)2 in the solution:

- Find the molar mass of Mg(OH)2:

- Mg: 1 x 24.31 g/mol = 24.31 g/mol

- 2 x O: 2 x 16.00 g/mol = 32.00 g/mol

- 2 x H: 2 x 1.008 g/mol = 2.016 g/mol

- Total molar mass: 24.31 + 32.00 + 2.016 = 58.326 g/mol

- Calculate the number of moles:

- 60.0 g Mg(OH)2 / 58.326 g/mol = 1.028 moles Mg(OH)2

Now, we can calculate the molarity of the solution:

- Molarity = moles of solute / liters of solution

- Liters of solution = 5000 ml / 1000 ml/L = 5.000 L

- Molarity = 1.028 moles / 5.000 L = 0.206 M

Therefore, the molarity of the solution is 0.206 M.
