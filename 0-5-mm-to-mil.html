15. A gas occupies a volume of 400cm3 at 0°C and 780 mm Hg. What volume (in liters) will it occupy at 80°C and 780 mm Hg?


Answer:The gas will occupy a volume of 0.470 liters at 80°C and 780 mm Hg.

Explanation:To solve this problem, we can use the combined gas law equation, which relates the pressure, volume, and temperature of a gas:

(P1 × V1) / T1 = (P2 × V2) / T2

Where:

P1 = initial pressure = 780 mm Hg

V1 = initial volume = 400 cm³ = 0.4 L

T1 = initial temperature = 0°C + 273.15 = 273.15 K

P2 = final pressure = 780 mm Hg

V2 = final volume = ?

T2 = final temperature = 80°C + 273.15 = 353.15 K

Plugging in the values:

(780 mm Hg × 0.4 L) / 273.15 K = (780 mm Hg × V2) / 353.15 K

Solving for V2:

V2 = [(780 mm Hg × 0.4 L) / 273.15 K] × (353.15 K / 780 mm Hg)

V2 = 0.470 L

Therefore, the gas will occupy a volume of 0.470 liters at 80°C and 780 mm Hg.
