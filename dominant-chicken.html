29. what do snapdragons, human height, and speckled chickens have common?A.Exhibits non-mendelian traitsB.Displays mendelian traitsC.produces dominant traitsD.Show multiple traits​


Answer:

A. Exhibits Non-Mendelian Traits

Explanation:

Codominance, incomplete dominance,(multiple alleles — not fitting for the three examples stated) and polygenic characteristics belong to what we call as Non-Mendelian inheritance. For instance, speckled chickens is a result from codominance, an inheritance pattern that expresses both alleles equally in the phenotypes of these heterozygotes. Snapdragons are good examples of incomplete dominance, an inheritance pattern in which their phenotypes are somewhere in between the phenotypes of both homozygous parents, causing a blend in the traits. Lastly, human height possesses polygenic characteristics, meaning, the characteristics are under the control of more than one gene that consists of two or more alleles.
