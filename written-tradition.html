5. Significance of Oral Tradition and Written Gospels. List down 3


Answer:

Oral Tradition (30-64 CE)  

- Messages that are transmitted orally from one generation to another.

- The messages may be passed down through speech or song and make take the form of folktales and fables, epic histories and narrations, proverbs or sayings and songs.  

- Make it possible for a society to pass knowledge across generations without heavy dependence on writing.  

- Used to teach children and adults about important aspects of their culture as well as help people make sense of the world.

Written Gospels (70 - 95 CE)

- The apostles' account of Jesus is gathered together and written down by the evangelists.

- It was done to gather essential information while the chief witnesses are alive.  

- Helps new Christians at that time and in the future to know Jesus and follow His teachings.

20. what is the difference between oral tradition and written gospels​

27. one contemporary and traditional composition written by a filipino​


Answer:

Contemporary composition: "Anak" by Freddie Aguilar

"Anak" is a classic Filipino song written by Freddie Aguilar in 1977. It tells the story of a parent's love and concern for their child who has gone astray. The song's message of love, forgiveness, and the importance of family resonates deeply with Filipinos, making it a timeless hit that continues to be popular to this day.

Traditional composition: "Bahay Kubo"

"Bahay Kubo" is a traditional Filipino folk song that describes a typical Filipino rural house made of indigenous materials such as bamboo and nipa. The song lists various vegetables and fruits that can be found in a typical Filipino garden, showcasing the country's agricultural richness and diversity. "Bahay Kubo" has been passed down through generations and remains a beloved piece of Filipino cultural heritage.
