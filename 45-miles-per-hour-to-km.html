30. 1. Nancy roller skates 45 km in 2 1/4 hours. What is her average speed in kilometers per hour? 2. Cindy rides her bike 82 km in 3 5/12 hours. What is her average speed in kilometers per hour? 3.Abigail rides her horse 25.5 miles in 4 hours. What is her average speed in miles per hour? 4. n airplane flies 3174 miles in 3 hours 50 minutes. What is its average speed in miles per hour? 5.Caleb rides his bike 3.5 miles in 35 minutes. What is his average speed in miles per minutes? 5.​


1. Nancy's average speed can be found by dividing the distance she traveled by the time it took her to travel that distance:

Average speed = distance ÷ time

In this case, the distance Nancy roller skated is 45 km and the time it took her is 2 1/4 hours. We can convert the mixed fraction to an improper fraction to make it easier to work with:

2 1/4 hours = (2 × 4 + 1) ÷ 4 = 9/4 hours

Now we can plug in the values to get:

Average speed = 45 km ÷ 9/4 hours

Average speed = 20 km/h

Therefore, Nancy's average speed is 20 kilometers per hour.

2. Cindy's average speed can be found in the same way as Nancy's:

Average speed = distance ÷ time

In this case, the distance Cindy rode is 82 km and the time it took her is 3 5/12 hours. We can convert the mixed fraction to an improper fraction:

3 5/12 hours = (3 × 12 + 5) ÷ 12 = 41/12 hours

Now we can plug in the values to get:

Average speed = 82 km ÷ 41/12 hours

Average speed = 24 km/h

Therefore, Cindy's average speed is 24 kilometers per hour.

3. Abigail's average speed can be found in the same way:

Average speed = distance ÷ time

In this case, the distance Abigail rode is 25.5 miles and the time it took her is 4 hours. We can plug in the values to get:

Average speed = 25.5 miles ÷ 4 hours

Average speed = 6.375 miles/h

Therefore, Abigail's average speed is 6.375 miles per hour.

4. To find the average speed of the airplane, we first need to convert the time it took to hours:

3 hours 50 minutes = 3 + 50/60 = 3.83 hours (approx)

Now we can use the same formula as before:

Average speed = distance ÷ time

In this case, the distance the airplane flew is 3174 miles and the time it took is 3.83 hours:

Average speed = 3174 miles ÷ 3.83 hours

Average speed = 828.65 miles/h

Therefore, the airplane's average speed is 828.65 miles per hour.

5. Caleb's average speed can be found by dividing the distance he rode by the time it took him:

Average speed = distance ÷ time

In this case, the distance Caleb rode is 3.5 miles and the time it took him is 35 minutes. We need to convert the time to hours:

35 minutes = 35/60 hours = 0.5833 hours (approx)

Now we can plug in the values to get:

Average speed = 3.5 miles ÷ 0.5833 hours

Average speed = 6 miles/min

Therefore, Caleb's average speed is 6 miles per minute.
