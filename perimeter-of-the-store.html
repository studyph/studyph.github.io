4. 1. Jaden drew a picture of a store with a triangular roof. If the sides of the roof measured 5 cm, 4 cm and 6 cm, find theperimeter of the roof.2. Innah is making a square table cloth. She puts a lace around it. How many centimetres of lace did she use if the side of the table cloth is 50 cm?3. A basketball court measures 600 cm wide and 700 cm long. Find the perimeter.4. Nuelle walks around the rectangular park in his school every morning. The park measures 42 m long and 35 m wide. How far is the distance covered by Nuelle if he walks around the park twice a day?5. A flower garden is 11 m long. Its width is 4 m shorter than its length. What is the distance around the garden?​


Answer:

To find the perimeter of the triangular roof, add up the lengths of all three sides: Perimeter = 5 cm + 4 cm + 6 cm = 15 cm

The lace goes around all four sides of the table cloth, so the length of the lace is equal to four times the length of one side of the square table cloth: Length of lace = 4 x 50 cm = 200 cm

The perimeter of the basketball court is the sum of the lengths of all four sides: Perimeter = 2(600 cm) + 2(700 cm) = 1200 cm + 1400 cm = 2600 cm

To find the distance covered by Nuelle, we need to find the perimeter of the rectangular park and then multiply it by two (since Nuelle walks around the park twice a day): Perimeter of park = 2(42 m + 35 m) = 2(77 m) = 154 m Distance covered by Nuelle = 2 x 154 m = 308 m

The width of the flower garden is 4 m shorter than its length, so we can represent the width as (length - 4). The perimeter of the garden is the sum of the lengths of all four sides: Perimeter = length + width + length + width Perimeter = 11 m + (11 m - 4 m) + 11 m + (11 m - 4 m) Perimeter = 44 m - 8 m Perimeter = 36 m

Therefore, the distance around the flower garden is 36 meters.
