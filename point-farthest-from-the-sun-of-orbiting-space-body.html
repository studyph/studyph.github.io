14. What do you call the point on earth’s orbit when the earth is at its farthest distance from the sun?


The term you use for the point of Earth's orbit when the Earth is at its farthest from the sun is...

Aphelion

Aphelion came from the Ancient Greek words 'helios' and 'apo'. Helios means sun and apo means far. The Earth is at its farthest from sun every early July and it is what we call aphelion. Its distance from the sun measures up to 4,800,000 km or 3 million miles. On the other hand, we call it perihelion when it is nearest to the sun and it happens every January. Aphelion and perihelion describe the two points in the Earth's orbit of the sun. Since the Earth's orbit is elliptical and not circular, there comes a time when the Earth's orbit is nearest or farthest to the sun.

In 2022, Earth is at aphelion on July 4 at 3:10am (EST). It will be 94, 509, 598 miles away from the sun.

In general, apocentre is the term used when a body's orbit is at farthest point from the body it orbits.
