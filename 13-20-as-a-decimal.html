16. Solve and identify if the decimal quotient is a terminating or repeating /nonterminating decimal. 1. 3➗20 2. 9➗40 3. 2➗11 4. 6 ➗13 5. 5➗16​ Solve and identify if the decimal quotient is a terminating or repeating /nonterminating decimal.


A decimal can be terminating or repeating/nonterminating. Let's identify the quotient of each division sentence.

3 ÷ 20 = 0.15 - terminating decimal9 ÷ 40 = 0.225 - terminating decimal2 ÷ 11 = 0.1818181818 - repeating/nonterminating decimal6 ÷ 13 = 0.4615384615 - repeating/nonterminating decimal5 ÷ 16 = 0.3125 - terminating decimal

Difference Between Terminating and Repeating/Nonterminating Decimal

Terminating Decimal - This kind of decimal refers to a number that has finite number of digits. It has an end.

Repeating / Nonterminating Decimal - This kind of decimal refers to a number that goes continuously, with a group of digits being repeated. It is endless.
