11. What are the five elements of a fable? Differentiate each from oneanother.​


Characterization

Characters are very few animated, inanimate or personified.

Setting

Place is anywhere and everywhere.

May be imaginary.

Time is anytime or no time, but real times.

Plot

Very simple, though interesting.

Thought provoking to didactic.

Theme

Moral or message implied or stated for societal or personal benefit.

Style

Casual every day.

Sometimes like a parent passing on wisdom to a daughter or son or wise person to a child.

Events are accurate, but don't necessarily real life.

Dialog, artifacts, feelings, are from the real world and described accurately.

Tone

Reflection of human strengths, frailities, weaknesses, or imperfections.

Reader is lead to new insights and/or understandings.

Point of View

Often third person narrative. Could be first.

13. Activity 7: What's the Message? Work with your seatmate. Discuss the questions below, then write your answers in the spaces provided. What is the moral of the fable "The Monkey, the Turtle, and the Crocodile"? What is the theme of the fable? What is the difference between the theme and the moral? 4. Explain through a sentence or two how the following elements contribute to the theme of the fable. Element How This Element Contributes to the Theme a. Setting b. Character C. Plot​


Answer:

The moral of the fable "The Monkey, the Turtle, and the Crocodile" is that deceit and betrayal will lead to dire consequences.The theme of the fable is that trust is important in relationships, and that it can be easily broken if one party is dishonest. The difference between the theme and the moral is that the theme is a more general idea or concept that can be applied to a broader range of situations, while the moral is a specific lesson that can be learned from the story.a. Setting: The setting of the fable, which takes place in a river, contributes to the theme by emphasizing the natural habitat of the characters and the dangers that come with it. The river symbolizes the unpredictable nature of life and the need for caution in all situations.

b. Characters: The characters in the fable contribute to the theme by illustrating the importance of trust in relationships. The monkey and the turtle demonstrate the value of loyalty and friendship, while the crocodile's deceitful actions show the consequences of betraying that trust.

c. Plot: The plot of the fable, which revolves around the crocodile's attempt to deceive the monkey, highlights the consequences of deceit and the importance of trust. The conflict between the characters serves to demonstrate the fragility of relationships and the need to be wary of those who might betray our trust.
