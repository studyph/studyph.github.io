13. Identify the principal muscles involved when: a. The frog leaps I b. The frog resumes a sitting position


Frog’s Muscle

The frog's calf muscle shortens as it prepares to leap. The calf muscle stops contracting after around 100 milliseconds, and the stretched tendon has received all of the energy. When a frog jumps, the tendon that wraps around the ankle bone releases its energy in a manner similar to a catapult or an archer's bow, forcing the ankle joint to extend very quickly and drive the frog ahead. The investigations revealed that the duration of the jump, from preparation to takeoff, is around a fifth of a second. Other frog species leap more quickly.

The muscles that are involved when the frog resumes sitting are the Gracilis minor muscles, which cover the posterior edge of the thigh. As a result, one of their functions is to aid in the extension of the thighs during the frog's return to sitting.
