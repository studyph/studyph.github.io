23. history of the fairytale cinderella​


Answer:

history of the fairytale cinderella

The story of Cinderella is one of the most beloved fairy tales of all time, and has been retold in various forms throughout history.

The earliest known version of the story dates back to ancient Greece, in the form of a tale called "Rhodopis". The story tells of a young Greek slave girl named Rhodopis who is taken to Egypt and eventually marries the Pharaoh. The story was later adapted by the Romans and retold in various forms throughout Europe.

The modern version of Cinderella that most people are familiar with is believed to have originated in France in the 17th century. French writer Charles Perrault published a version of the story in 1697 called "Cendrillon, ou la Petite Pantoufle de Verre" (Cinderella, or the Little Glass Slipper), which became one of the most popular fairy tales of all time. Perrault's version introduced many of the elements that are now considered essential to the story, including the Fairy Godmother, the glass slipper, and the pumpkin carriage.

The story was later adapted by the Brothers Grimm in their collection of fairy tales, published in Germany in the early 19th century. The Grimm Brothers' version of the story was darker and more violent than Perrault's, and included elements such as the stepsisters cutting off parts of their feet in order to fit into the glass slipper.

Since then, the story of Cinderella has been retold in countless adaptations, including stage plays, operas, films, and television shows. The enduring appeal of the story lies in its universal themes of transformation, perseverance, and the triumph of good over evil.
