17. 2) A small candy container is shaped like a cylinder. The manufacturer wraps a label around the curved surface of the container. Use the ruler on the Mathematics chart to measure the height and radius of the cylinder in centimeters. Which of the following is closest to the area of the label in square centimeters?​


Answer:

To calculate the area of the label on a small candy container shaped like a cylinder, we need to find the circumference of the cylinder first, and then multiply it by the height of the cylinder. The formula for the circumference of a cylinder is:

C = 2πr

where r is the radius of the cylinder and π (pi) is a constant approximately equal to 3.14.

Once we have the circumference of the cylinder, we can multiply it by the height of the cylinder to get the area of the label. The formula for the area of the label is:

A = C × h

where h is the height of the cylinder.

Without the actual measurements, it's challenging to provide an exact answer. However, assuming that the measurements are given, we can use the above formulas to calculate the area of the label.

For example, if the height of the cylinder is 10 cm and the radius is 5 cm, then the circumference of the cylinder is:

C = 2πr = 2 × 3.14 × 5 cm = 31.4 cm

Using the formula A = C × h, the area of the label is:

A = 31.4 cm × 10 cm = 314 cm^2

Therefore, the closest answer to the area of the label in square centimeters would be 314 cm^2.

21. Activity 4: Compare and ContrastDirections: On your Mathematics notebook, make a two-column chart Compare and contrastarithmetie and geometric sequenceArithmetic SequenceGeometric Sequence​


Answer:

In ARITHMETIC SEQUENCE, the next term is obtained by adding or subtracting the same specific value to the numbers. The value that is being subtracted or added is called common difference.

         Example:

              1, 3, 5, 7, 9, ...

Common difference = 2

9 - 7 = 2       7 + 2 = 9 (5th term)

7 - 5 = 2       5 + 2 = 7 (4th term)

5 - 3 = 2       3 + 2 = 5 (3rd term)

3 - 1 = 2        1 + 2 = 3 (2nd term)

In GEOMETRIC SEQUENCE, geometric sequence is formed by multiplying or dividing the same specific value to the numbers. The value that is being multiplied or divided is called common ratio.

        Example:

               2, 4, 8, 16, 32, ...

Common ratio: 2

4 / 2 = 2          2 x 2 = 4 (2nd term)

8 / 4 = 2          4 x 2 = 8 (3rd term)

16 / 8 = 2         8 x 2 = 16 (4th term)

32 / 16 = 2      16 x 2 = 32 (5th term)
