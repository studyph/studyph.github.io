24. A man thought of committing suicide and went on top of a tall building. He jumped, landing on somebody else, who died instantly. Is he criminally liable? Explain.


yes, criminal liability exists if one commits a crime while doing something dangerous like jumping from a height. One cannot commit crimes unless one does some illegal acts such as stealing property, killing another human being etc. So, even though this act may seem innocent or harmless, there still exist legal consequences for those actions. In case of the above situation, since the person committed a crime (i.e., murder) while doing something dangerous (i.e., jumping off a building), he should be held accountable for his action.

Another Answer:

The man is criminally liable for the death of the person he landed on. This is because, by jumping off the building, he willfully and knowingly put the other person in danger, which resulted in their death.
