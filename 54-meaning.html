6. Some of three means between 6 and 54


Answer:

90

Solution:

Find the difference first

[tex]a_n = a_1 + (n-1)d[/tex]

[tex]54 = 6 + (5-1)d[/tex]

[tex]54 = 6 + 4d[/tex]

Subtract 6 to both sides

[tex]48 = 4d[/tex]

Divide 4 to both sides

[tex]12 = d[/tex]

Since we already found the difference, we can now find the three missing term

[tex]a_2 = a_1 + d[/tex]

[tex]a_2 = 6 + 12 = 18[/tex]

[tex]a_3 = a_2+d[/tex]

[tex]a_3 = 18 + 12 = 30[/tex]

[tex]a_4 = a_3 + d[/tex]

[tex]a_4 = 30 + 12 = 42[/tex]

To get the some (sum), just add the terms.

18 + 30 + 42 = 90

15. geometric mean between 6 and 54 solution


QUESTION:

Geometric mean between 6 and 54.

SOLUTION:

Get the quotient of two geometric extremes, first.

[tex]54 ÷ 6 = 9[/tex]

Then, get the square root of it.

[tex] \sqrt{9} [/tex] = 3

Then, 3 is the common ratio, we can now find the missing mean.

[tex]6 × 3 = 18[/tex]

[tex]18 × 3 = 54[/tex] ✔︎

ANSWER:

Therefore, the geometric mean is 18.

27. arithmetic mean between 18 and 54


Given:

a₁ = 18

a₃ = 54


Common Difference:

d = (a₃-a₁) / (3-1)

d = (54 - 18) / 2

d = 36 / 2

d = 18


Arithmetic Mean:

a₂ = a₁ + d

a₂ = 18 + 18

a₂ = 36


Thus, the arithmetic mean between 18 and 54 is 36
