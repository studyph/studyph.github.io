1. objects that allow light all light to pass through them ​


Objects that allow all light to pass through them are called "transparent" materials. These materials have the property of transmitting light without absorbing or reflecting it, resulting in a clear and unobstructed view of objects behind them.

Examples of transparent materials include:

Glass

Water

Air

Plexiglass (acrylic)

Diamond

Clear plastic

Quartz

Some types of gel

Some types of crystals

Some types of gases, such as hydrogen or helium
