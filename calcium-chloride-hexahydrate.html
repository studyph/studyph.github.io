12. To prepare a 1l of 1000 ppm solution of calcium chloride, how many grams of calcium chloride dihydrate should be weighed? solution.


The weight of calcium chloride is 147.02g

Explanation:

     The salt combining calcium and chlorine is called calcium chloride. Both anhydrous (CaCl2) and hydrated versions of calcium chloride are sold commercially (CaCl2 H2O, CaCl2 2H2O, CaCl2 4H2O, CaCl2 6H2O). To make a 1 M calcium chloride stock solution, any of them can be utilized. White powder that is solid at room temperature is anhydrous calcium chloride. It must be stored in containers that are tightly sealed and airtight because it is highly hygroscopic.

      When dissolved in water, calcium chloride that is anhydrous as well as the lower hydrates produce a lot of heat (exothermic reaction). When dissolving them in water, exercise caution. Due to its high hygroscopicity, anhydrous CaCl2 is challenging to weigh precisely (quickly absorbs water molecules from air). Dihydrate and tetrahydrate forms of calcium chloride are more stable in this aspect.

Prepare the following:

Solutions and reagentsCalcium chloride dihydrate (molecular weight: 147.02; CaCl2.2H2O)distilled waterCylinder for measuringBeaker or conical flaskStirrer with a magnetCalcium Chloride, 1 M

Procedure:

Use a magnetic stirrer to dissolve 147.02 g of CaCl2.2H2O (molecular weight: 147.02) in 800 ml of deionized or Milli-Q water to make 1000 ml of a 1 M calcium chloride (CaCl2) solution.With deionized water, adjust the volume to 1000 ml. Mix it once more.Fill an autoclavable bottle with the medium. The solution should be autoclaved for 20 minutes at 15 lb/sq.in. (psi) from 121–124°C on the liquid cycle to sterilize it.

The solution can be kept at ambient temperature or in a frigid environment.
