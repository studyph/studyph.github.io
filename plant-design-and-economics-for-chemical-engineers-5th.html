9. a gardener buys a plant that is 12cm in height. Each week after that the plant grows 10cm. Note: The plant is 12cm high at the beginning of the 1st week a. what will be the height of the plant at the beginning of the 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, and 5th weeks,if it follows the same pattern?


Answer:

1st - 12 cm2nd - 22 cm3rd - 32 cm4th - 42 cm5th - 52 cm

Step-by-step explanation:

Number Pattern

Number pattern is a list of numbers that follow a certain sequence, pattern or specific rule.

Going back to the given problem above, we already know that the pattern rule is to add 10 to every number because each week after, the plant grows 10 cm.

Now we need to find the height of the plant at the beginning of the 1st, 2nd, 3rd, 4th, and 5th weeks.

Remember that the height of the plant at the beginning of the 1st week is 12 cm. To find the height at the beginning of every week, we need to add 10 cm.

1st week - 12 cm

2nd week - 12 cm + 10 cm = 22 cm

3rd week - 22 cm + 10 cm = 32 cm

4th week - 32 cm + 10 cm = 42 cm

5th week - 42 cm + 10 cm = 52 cm

20. 7/5th plus 4/5th equals?​


Answer:

[tex]2 \frac{1}{5} [/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

We know that 7/5 and 4/5 are like fractions or similar fractions so it means it is easy to add this two fractions, we just add the numerator and copy the denominator which is 5;

[tex]\frac{7}{5} + \frac{4}{5} = \frac{11}{5} [/tex]

Alright, we got our answer but we see that our answer is an improper fraction, so we need to convert it into a mixed number. We can use long division here, we need to divide 11 to 5. If you're done in dividing, you should get 2 the quotient and 1 the remainder. Now we're ready to convert 11/5 into a mixed number, our whole number for the mixed number is 2 the quotient, our numerator for the mixed number is 1 the remainder and our denominator is 5 from 11/5;

[tex]\frac{11}{5} = 2 \frac{1}{5} [/tex]

Therefore,

[tex]\frac{7}{5} + \frac{4}{5} = 2 \frac{1}{5} [/tex]
