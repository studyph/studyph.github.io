Puritan Migration

Puritans departed Britain for both economic and theological reasons, however religious persecution was the main driver. Early in the 17th century, England was experiencing a theological upheaval; the puritans and other religious nonconformists faced a hostile and perilous religious climate. This presented a challenge for the separatists because at the time, in Britain, the separation of the church from the state was regarded as treason.

Answer

The early American Literature was written by European explorers.

Explanation

The earliest records of American literature were from English-speaking Europeans explorers/settlers. To be more precise, they were from London, England.  These Englishmen established the Jamestown Colony which is located at present-day Williamsburg, Virginia, the Massachusetts Bay Colony in Boston and eleven more colonies in North America. Their works of literature consist of written journals, experiences, exploration, religion, and opportunities in the colony and the new world. Three well-known figures of early American Literature were John Smith, Nathaniel Ward, John Winthrop, and Anne Bradstreet.

John Smith is a mercenary, explorer, and is the president of Jamestown Colony. He wrote about the history of Virginia and his works were published in 1608 and 1624. J. Smith's works were one of America's colonial literature.

Nathaniel Ward and John Winthrop are what you call Puritans. The Puritans were English Protestants that wanted to "purify" the English church of Catholic teachings.  Their literature consists of Religious teaching related to Protestant Theology. Nathaniel Ward and John Winthrop resided at the Massachusetts Bay Colony.

Anne Bradstreet was a female English writer of poetry. Her literature works like, the 1650 Tenth Muse Lately Sprung Up in America was considered one of the early collections of American Poetry. However, it was published in England.

9. Which type was not part of puritan writing?


Puritan Writing

Preaching, poetry, and historical accounts make up the majority of puritan writing, although relatively little fiction was produced by Puritan authors. Puritans wrote in basic, uncomplicated language, much like their way of life. The Puritan Age was characterized by writing that was primarily uncomplicated and used straightforward language. There was little use of metaphorical constructs, and overly ornate or theatrical appeals were discouraged and the sermons and other presentations were made powerful by using biblical symbolism. The fundamental goal was to present the truth of God's presence in a way that was accessible to everyone.

Characteristics of puritan writing:

1. Genres - Early American Puritan literature frequently took the form of journals and diaries and was written in the first person.

2. Influence – Puritans had strong theological convictions that were founded on their interpretation of Christianity. The Puritans' daily life were heavily influenced by the Bible.

3. Plain style – With its straightforward writing style, Puritan writing imitates these cultural principles.

4. Purpose - Puritans used writing to achieve specific goals. Even the letters they sent to loved ones in Europe served a role beyond just keeping in touch and exchanging life updates.

10. Take on the role of a New England Puritan. Write a letter to a cousin in England explaining a typical day.


Answer:

Greetings Alfred,

Life in the new colony is good. It is wonderful that, as Puritans, we are free to worship as we please without threat of persecution. It is truly nice to worship and not be distracted by the unnecessary rituals and dress of Catholicism.

As you know, God has chosen those who will be saved and only patience and self-reflection will tell us whether or not we are chosen. In this new colony that we have established, it is much easier to listen to God and live a morally just life.

Here, religion touches on almost all aspects of life. Our government is comprised of a General Court made up of free men who make our laws. But everyone follows the laws of the Bible as well as those created by the Court. Our town hall meetings double as church services. In this way, God guides us through our decisions. Part of me does wish that women could be included in the politics of this colony, but I know that it is best to leave the voting to men. It is also unfortunate that, although we came here to escape religious intolerance, this new community is also intolerant to any form of worship other than strict Puritanism.

I, of course, am satisfied because I know that our way is morally right in God's eyes. However, I know some community members who feel that they would like to worship in other ways.

Signed,

Samuel Dunn Alcott

27th, July of 1631

21. The early colonial literature was personal. which type was not part of puritan writing


FICTION

Fiction is not part of Puritan Writing.

The vast majority of Puritan literature consists of sermons, poetry, and historical accounts, whereas relatively little fiction was written during this time.

Puritans embraced education and reading as means to a deeper understanding of the Bible. However, sermons, diaries, and poetry made up most of the work they contributed to the literary canon.

The writings of the Puritans reflect their religious convictions, such as the idea that all humans are sinful and deserve eternal condemnation if God does not save them.

Spirituality, self-reflection, and the path to salvation were important themes in the writings, with God as the focal point. Various writings attest to their earnest pursuit of God's will and adherence to a stringent moral code.

The Puritans' simple way of life was rooted in modesty and minimalism. Their religious practices and the Bible inform this orientation. Consequently, dressing or thinking in a striking manner irritated the Puritans. Allusions to these cultural norms can be seen in the spare language of the Puritans.
