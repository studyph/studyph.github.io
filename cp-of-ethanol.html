22. mixing 25g of water and 25g of ethanol compute for the mole fraction of ethanol​


Solution:

First, we calculate the number of moles of C₂H₅OH and H₂O. The number of moles of C₂H₅OH is

[tex]n_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}} = 25 \: g \: \text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH} \times \frac{1 \: mol \: \text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}}{46.068 \: g \: \text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}} = 0.54268 \: mol[/tex]

and the number of moles of H₂O is

[tex]n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = 25 \: g \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O} \times \frac{1 \: mol \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O}}{18.016 \: g \: \text{H}_{2}\text{O}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}} = 1.38766 \: mol[/tex]

Next, we calculate the total number of moles. We write

[tex]n_{T} = n_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}} + n_{\text{H}_{2}\text{O}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{T} = 0.54268 \: mol + 1.38766 \: mol[/tex]

[tex]n_{T} = 1.93034 \: mol[/tex]

Finally, we can calculate the mole fractions of C₂H₅OH. The mole fraction of C₂H₅OH is

[tex]X_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}} = \frac{n_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}}}{n_{T}}[/tex]

[tex]X_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}} = \frac{0.54268 \: mol}{1.93034 \: mol}[/tex]

[tex]\blue{X_{\text{C}_{2}\text{H}_{5}\text{OH}} = 0.28}[/tex]
