12. Write an explanation about the quote from - Christian Morgenstern “In every work of art, the artist himself is present” ​


Explanation:

The quote by Christian Morgenstern, "In every work of art, the artist himself is present," refers to the idea that a piece of art reflects the unique perspective, emotions, and experiences of the artist who created it. The artist's personal style, beliefs, and worldview are embedded in each stroke of the brush or line on the page. Regardless of the artist's intention, a work of art is a direct reflection of the person who created it.

This quote highlights the fact that no matter how abstract or ambiguous a piece of art might appear, it is a representation of the artist's personality and the intricate thought process that went into creating it. Sometimes an artist might intentionally include specific elements or symbols to convey a particular message or feeling. Still, even when this is not the case, the artwork subconsciously embodies the artist's emotional state and experiences at the time of creation.

Furthermore, Morgenstern's quote implies that art is not just a portrayal of the external world; it is also an extension of the artist's own thoughts and emotions. By examining the artwork and its style, we can gain insight into the artist's personality and worldview. For this reason, art is often considered an expression of the human condition and has the power to move and inspire individuals by creating an emotional connection.

In conclusion, Morgenstern's quote emphasizes the inseparable link between the artwork and the artist that creates it. It implies that in each work of art, there is a piece of the artist represented in it. Therefore, art is not just a visual medium but also a way of expressing oneself and leaving an imprint of one's personality in history.
