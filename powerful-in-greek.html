8. Which is the least among the noble motivations that prodded the Greeks to introduce the Olympic Game?a. love for sports and life b.military might and power c. leisure and entertainmentd. love of arts and culture​


Answer:

Which is the least among the noble motivations that prodded the Greeks to introduce the Olympic Game?

Answer: b. military might and power

Explanation:

Olympic games

The Olympic Games, conducted every fourth year between 776 B.C.E. and 395 C.E. in honor of Zeus, the greatest god of Greek mythology, drew spectators from all around Greece. Running, discus throwing, and the long jump were among the events that drew large crowds.

Olympia

Olympia's athletic activities were the oldest and most prominent of Greece's four major athletic festivals. The games began in 776 B.C.E. and were conducted every four years until 776 B.C.E., but they were most likely held much earlier. The hero Herakles is credited with inventing the running races at Olympia to commemorate the completion of one of his twelve labors, according to Greek mythology.

The Games were held in honor of Zeus, whose most important sanctuary was Olympia. A statue of Zeus was used to provide sacrifices and gifts, and athletes took oaths to follow the laws. The games were publicized by heralds who traveled to all of the main Greek cities throughout the Mediterranean, and conflicts were prohibited during the year leading up to the Games.

13. Give atleast 20 examples of gods in greek mythology (includes their power and where they live)​


Answer:

1. Zeus=

Domain/Place: Mount Olympus

Title: Olympian king and leader of the Olympic gods, god of lightning, thunder, and the heavens.

Power: He controlled weather, sky, thunder, lightning and storms. He can also use his power to transform to seduce mortal women, other goddesses, and nymphs.

2. Hera

Domain/Place: Mount Olympus

Title= wife of Zeus, Olympian queen of Olympus, women, and marriage.

Power: She had the ability to bless and curse marriages. She also called the protector of the Amazons, the group of warrior women.

3. Poseidon

Domain/Place: Ocean, Sea or Mount Olympus

Title: Olympian god of the sea, earth shaker, horses tamer

Power: He could cause violent earthquakes, volcano eruptions, hurricanes, violent big waves and more.

4. Hades=

Domain/Place: the Underworld

Title: god of the dead, king of the underworld and riches

Power: He controlled death and lives. He also possessed the cap of invisibility, which he used to kidnap Persephone, daughter of Demeter.

5. Ares

Domain/Place= Mount Olympus, Thrace, Macedonia, Thebes, Sparta, and Mani

Title: Olympian god of war, courage, and brutal warfare

Power= He had great power when it comes to war due to his powerful and smart strategies.

6. Apollo

Domain/Place= Mount Olympus, Delos (his sacred island)

Title= Twin brother of Artemis, Olympian god of the sun and light, god of music, truth and prophecy.

Power: He controlled the sun which can burn the earth, oceans, and sky if he wanted to. He gives oracles to all his followers.

7. Artemis

Domain/Place= Mount Olympus

Title= twin sister of Apollo, virgin goddess of the moon, hunt, wild animals, wilderness, and virginity

Power= She was strong and great with bow and arrows that no one can compare to her. Although she could heal mortals, she could also bring terrible diseases.

8. Athena=

Domain/Place: Mount Olympus

Title: goddess of wisdom and justice, handicrafts, and the strategic side of war

Power: She is known to be an independent, intelligent, and ruthless goddess in battles. She also had a shield called an aegis that depicted the head of Medusa, representing its great protective power.

9. Aphrodite

Domain/Place= Mount Olympus

Title= goddess of love and beauty, pleasure and procreation

Power: She was beautiful, and possessed a magic girdle called a cestus, that had the power of inspiring love.

10. Demeter

Domain/Place: Mount Olympus

Title= goddess of the harvest and agriculture

Power= She had power over all living things around the earth, nature, and seasons, especially crops and harvest. She also maintained the circle of life on earth.

11. Hephaestus

Domain/Place: Mount Olympus

Title: god of blacksmiths, craftsman, metals, fire and volcanoes

Power: He had strong power that can blow up anything, and he also created countless inventions.

12. Hermes

Domain/Place: Mount Olympus

Title: god of commerce, travel, boundaries, and messenger of the gods.

Power: He moves so fast, could deliver countless punches in seconds. He was the fastest among Greek gods. He used his power to charm the gods and heal mortals, and He is the guide to the Underworld and protector of thieves, shepherds, orators, road travelers, and merchants.

13. Dionysus

Domain/Place: Mount Olympus

Title= Olympian god of wine, pleasure, and madness

Power= He could turn a rock into water and water into wine with his thyrsos/magic staff or wand.

14. Persephone

Domain/Place: Earth and Underworld (6 months each place)

Title= goddess of the spring and vegetation, kidnapped queen of the underworld.

14. Nike

Domain/Place: Mount Olympus

Title= goddess of victory

Power: She can control victory in any battles and assumes the role of the divine charioteer, often accompanying Athena and Ares.

15. Hestia

Domain/Place: Not specified. She left Mount Olympus for a quieter place and gave her place to Dionysus as one of the 12 Olympians.

Title: virgin goddess of hearth, home and chastity

Power: She symbolized harmony in the family and city and the hearth fires dedicated to her burn for both day and night.

16. Iris

Place: Not specified.

Title: goddess of the rainbow and also a messenger for the gods to humanity.

Power: She can go to the Underworld to collect water from River Styx, for the swearing of solemn oaths.

17. Eris

Place: Not specified.

Title: goddess of chaos, discord, and strife.

Power: She has absolute authority over chaos, strife, and discord.

18. Nemesis

Place: Not Specified.

Title: goddess of balance, retribution, and vengeance

Power: She had absolute authority over luck, and also can create horrible curses as punishments.

19. Morpheus

Place: Not Specified.

Title: god of dream and sleep

Power: He had divine authority and control over dreams and can make people fall asleep and shape peoples' dreams.

20. Thanatos

Place: the Underworld

Title: god of death (peaceful)

Power: As the god of peaceful death, he is able to keep track of all souls in the Underworld. He keeps the balance between the living and the dead and stops the world from falling into chaos.
