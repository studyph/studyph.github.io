10. What is the mass of 5 moles of Fe2O3?


Given:

moles of Fe₂O₃ = 5 mol

Required:

mass of Fe₂O₃

Strategy:

The problem requires us to find the mass of 5 moles of Fe₂O₃. But how do we find it?

First, we will compute for the molar mass of Fe₂O₃ and then we will use dimensional analysis (factor-label method) to find the mass of Fe₂O₃.

Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of Fe₂O₃.

Based on the periodic table of elements, the atomic masses of Fe and O are 55.85 amu and 16.00 amu, respectively.

Note: The atomic mass is numerically equal to the molar mass.

The molar mass of Fe₂O₃ is

molar mass of Fe₂O₃ = (55.85 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 3)

molar mass of Fe₂O₃ = 159.7 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the mass of Fe₂O₃.

Using dimensional analysis, the mass of Fe₂O₃ is

[tex]\text{mass of Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3} = \text{5 mol Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3} \times \dfrac{\text{159.7 g Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3}}{\text{1 mol Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of Fe}_{2}\text{O}_{3} = \text{798.5 g}}[/tex]

Answer:

mass of Fe₂O₃ = 798.5 g

Hello!

In a 5.00 g sample of Fe2O3, compute the following and show your solution:

a) Molecular weight of Fe2O3

Fe = 2*(55.845 u) = 111.69 u

O = 3*(15.9994 u) = 47.9982 u

------------------------------------------------

Molecular weight of Fe2O3 = 111.69 + 47.9982

Molecular weight of Fe2O3 = 159.6882 g/mol ( ≈ 159.69 g/mol)

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{Molecular\:Weight\:of\:Fe_2O_3 \approx 159.69\:g/mol}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

b) No. of moles of Fe2O3

n (nº of moles) = ?

m (mass) = 5.00 g

MW (molar weight) = 159.69 g/mol

[tex]n = \dfrac{m}{MW}[/tex]

[tex]n = \dfrac{5.00\:\diagup\!\!\!\!\!g}{159.69\:\diagup\!\!\!\!\!g/mol}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{n \approx 0.031\:mol}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

c)  No. of particles (atoms) in mol

Knowing that the atom is formed by small particles and that by Avogadro's Law,  there are 6.022*10²³ atoms in one mole of any element, so we have:

1 mol ------------------ 6.022*10²³ atoms

0.031 mol ------------ y atoms

[tex]\dfrac{1}{0.031} = \dfrac{6.022*10^{23}}{y}[/tex]

multiply the means by the extremes

[tex]1*y = 0.031*6.022*10^{23}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{y = 1.86682*10^{22}\:atoms}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

d) No. of moles of Fe+3 ions

n (nº of moles) = ?

m (mass) = 5.00 g

MW (molar weight) = 55.8434 g/mol

[tex]n = \dfrac{m}{MW}[/tex]

[tex]n = \dfrac{5.00\:\diagup\!\!\!\!\!g}{55.8434\:\diagup\!\!\!\!\!g/mol}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{n \approx 0.00895\:mol}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

e) No. of ions of Fe+3

Ions are atoms that lose or gain electrons, knowing that the Cation (positive ion) is an atom that loses electrons and gets positively charged.

The iron has mass number 56 and atomic number 26 (or 26 electrons or 26 protons), so for Fe +3, it will be necessary to lose 3 electrons, so we have:

Z (atomic number) = P (protons) = e (electrons)

Z (atomic number) = 26

P (protons) = 26

e (electrons) = 26

*** But the charge +3 indicates that it lost 3 electrons, so now this ion has 23 electrons.

26 - 3 = 23 electrones

[tex]\boxed{\boxed{Fe^{+3}\:will\:have\:23\:electrons}}\end{array}}\qquad\checkmark[/tex]

22. What mass of Fe2O3 must be reacted to generate 324 g of Al2O3? Fe2O3(s) + Al(s) → Fe(s) + Al2O3(s)


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Write the balanced chemical equation.

Fe₂O₃(s) + 2Al(s) → 2Fe(s) + Al₂O₃(s)

Step 2: Calculate the mass of Fe₂O₃ reacted by using dimensional analysis.

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 1 mole of Fe₂O₃ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 1 mole of Al₂O₃.

The molar masses of Fe₂O₃ and Al₂O₃ are 159.687 g and 101.961 g, respectively.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3 & = \text{324 g} \: \text{Al}_2\text{O}_3 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Al}_2\text{O}_3}{\text{101.961 g} \: \text{Al}_2\text{O}_3} \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Al}_2\text{O}_3} \times \frac{\text{159.687 g} \: \text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Fe}_2\text{O}_3} \\ & = \boxed{\text{507 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, 507 g of Fe₂O₃ must be reacted to produce 324 g of Al₂O₃.
