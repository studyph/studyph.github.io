17. The force exerted on the smaller pistons of a hydraulic lift is 160 N. The areas of the smaller piston and the larger piston are 0.5 m2 and 1.0 m2, respectively. What is the output force of the larger piston?


Given:

[tex]F_{1} = \text{160 N}[/tex]

[tex]A_{1} = 0.5 \: \text{m}^{2}[/tex]

[tex]A_{2} = 1.0 \: \text{m}^{2}[/tex]

Required:

[tex]F_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

[tex]\frac{F_{1}}{A_{1}} = \frac{F_{2}}{A_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]F_{2} = F_{1} × \frac{A_{2}}{A_{1}}[/tex]

[tex]F_{2} = \text{160 N} × \frac{1.0 \: \text{m}^{2}}{0.5 \: \text{m}^{2}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{F_{2} = \text{320 N}}[/tex]

24. on a piston of 10cm diameter a force of 100N is uniformly applied. Find the pressure on the piston in kPa​


Answer:

P=12.732394854 kPa

Explanation:

Give:

d=10cm

F=100N

Find:

A=?

P=? in kPa

Formula:

A=(1/4)πd²

P=F/A

Solution:Find first the Area to proceed in finding the pressure.

A=(1/4)π(10 cm)²

A=(1/4)π(100 cm²)

A=78.53982 cm²×(1m/100cm)²

Cancel cm²

A=78.53982×(1m²/10,000)

A=0.007853982 m²

Now we can proceed in finding the pressure on the piston.

P=F/A

P=100N/0.007853982 m²

P=12,732.394854 N/m²

P=12.732394854 kPa
