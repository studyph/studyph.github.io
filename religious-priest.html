The standard word order for a sentence is subject + verb + object (SVO). Remember, the topic is the content of the sentence; so it comes first. The subject comes first in a sentence because it makes our meaning clear when we write and speak. Then the verb comes after the subject and the object after the verb; this is the most common word order. Otherwise a sentence is meaningless. And is a compound word that can be used in many ways. It can be used to add more information.

For example, my monster loves ice cream and ketchup. The word and can also be used to join two different clauses together. For example, my monster went upstairs and hid behind the toilet.
