2. What is the density of a plastic ball that has a volume of 6cm3 and a mass of 12?

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{PROBLEM:}} [/tex] What is the density of a plastic ball that has a volume of 6cm³ and a mass of 12?

[tex] \large \bold{\blue{SOLUTION:}} [/tex] Use the formula on how to find the density with the given mass and volume.

[tex] \boxed{ \rho = \frac{m}{V} } [/tex]

[tex] \rho = \frac{12g}{6cm^3} \\ [/tex]

[tex] \rho = 2g/cm^3 [/tex]

[tex] \large \therefore \underline{\boxed{\tt \purple{The \: density \: is \: 2g/cm^3}}} [/tex]

28. You have ball with a volume of 25 cm3 and a mass of 20 g. What is its density in kg/L?​


Solution:

The density is the mass per volume. Let's convert the unit.

[tex]\rho=\dfrac{m}{V}= \dfrac{20\text{ g}\times\dfrac{1\text{ kg}}{1000\text{ g}} }{25\text{ cm}^3\times\dfrac{1 \text{ m}^3}{100^3\text{ cm}^3} \times\dfrac{1000\text{ L}}{1\text{ m}^3} } \\\\\boxed{\rho=0.8\text{ kg/L}}[/tex]

Note: 1 g/cm³ = 1 kg/L

Answer:

0.8kg/L

Explanation:

Given: Mass(m) = 20g

           Volume(v) = 25[tex]cm^{3}[/tex]

density is mass per unit volume: d = m/v

you have volume and mass given but you need to convert them

for mass:

1kg = 1000g

for volume:

1[tex]m^{3}[/tex] = 1000L

1m = 100cm

to convert:

m = 20g × [tex]\frac{1kg}{1000g}[/tex]

m = 0.02kg

v = [tex]25cm^{3}[/tex] × [tex](\frac{1m}{100cm}) ^{3}[/tex] × [tex]\frac{1000L}{1m^{3} }[/tex]

v = [tex]0.025L[/tex]

computing for density:

d = [tex]\frac{m}{v}[/tex]

d = [tex]\frac{0.02kg}{0.025L}[/tex]

d = [tex]0.8\frac{kg}{L}[/tex]
