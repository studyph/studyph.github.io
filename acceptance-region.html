If the value falls in the rejection region, it means you have statistically significant results; You can reject the null hypothesis. If the p-value falls outside the rejection region, it means your results aren't enough to throw out the null hypothesis.

what is the importance of identifying the acceptance and rejection region?

Results from a statistical tests will fall into one of two regions: the rejection region— which will lead you to reject the null hypothesis, or the acceptance region, where you provisionally accept the null hypothesis.

what will the possible outcome be if we fail to correctly determine these regions?
