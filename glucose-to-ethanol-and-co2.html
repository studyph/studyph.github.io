1. Please answer seriously Fermentation is a complex chemical process of making wine by converting glucose into ethanol and carbon dioxide: C6H12O6 -> 2 C2H5OH + 2 CO2 a. Calculate the mass of ethanol (C2H5OH) produced if 200.0 grams of glucose reacts completely. b. Calculate the number of moles of carbon dioxide gas produced if 100.0 grams of glucose reacts. c. If 500.00 grams of ethanol were produced, how many grams of carbon dioxide would be formed? d. Calculate the number of moles glucose needed to produce 10.00 moles of ethanol. e. Calculate the mass in grams of glucose needed to produce 4.00 moles carbon dioxide.


Solution: (a)

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of substances needed.

For C₆H₁₂O₆

molar mass = (12.01 g/mol × 6) + (1.008 g/mol × 12) + (16.00 g/mol × 6)

molar mass = 180.156 g/mol

For C₂H₅OH

molar mass = (12.01 g/mol × 2) + (1.008 g/mol × 6) (16.00 g/mol × 1)

molar mass = 46.068 g/mol

Step 2: Determine the mole ratio needed.

mole ratio = 1 mol C₆H₁₂O₆ : 2 mol C₂H₅OH

Step 3: Calculate the mass of C₂H₅OH produced using the mole ratio.

[tex]\text{mass of C₂H₅OH = 200.0 g C₆H₁₂O₆} × \frac{\text{1 mol C₆H₁₂O₆}}{\text{180.156 g C₆H₁₂O₆}} × \frac{\text{2 mol C₂H₅OH}}{\text{1 mol C₆H₁₂O₆}} × \frac{\text{46.068 g C₂H₅OH}}{\text{1 mol C₂H₅OH}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of C₂H₅OH = 102.3 g}}[/tex]

Solution: (b)

Step 1: Determine the mole ratio needed.

mole ratio = 1 mol C₆H₁₂O₆ : 2 mol CO₂

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of CO₂ used using the mole ratio.

[tex]\text{moles of CO₂ = 100.0 g C₆H₁₂O₆} × \frac{\text{1 mol C₆H₁₂O₆}}{\text{180.156 g C₆H₁₂O₆}} × \frac{\text{2 mol CO₂}}{\text{1 mol C₆H₁₂O₆}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{moles of CO₂ = 1.11015 mol}}[/tex]

Solution: (c)

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of substances needed.

For CO₂

molar mass = (12.01 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 2)

molar mass = 44.01 g/mol

Step 2: Determine the mole ratio needed.

mole ratio = 2 mol CO₂ : 2 mol C₂H₅OH

Step 3: Calculate the mass of CO₂ formed using the mole ratio.

[tex]\text{mass of CO₂ = 500.00 g C₂H₅OH} × \frac{\text{1 mol C₂H₅OH}}{\text{46.068 g C₂H₅OH}} × \frac{\text{2 mol CO₂}}{\text{2 mol C₂H₅OH}} × \frac{\text{44.01 g CO₂}}{\text{1 mol CO₂}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of CO₂ = 477.66 g}}[/tex]

Solution: (d)

[tex]\text{moles of C₆H₁₂O₆ = 10.00 mol C₂H₅OH} × \frac{\text{1 mol C₆H₁₂O₆}}{\text{2 mol C₂H₅OH}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{moles of C₆H₁₂O₆ = 5.000 mol}}[/tex]

Solution: (e)

[tex]\text{mass of C₆H₁₂O₆ = 4.00 mol CO₂} × \frac{\text{1 mol C₆H₁₂O₆}}{\text{2 mol CO₂}} × \frac{\text{180.156 g C₆H₁₂O₆}}{\text{1 mol C₆H₁₂O₆}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of C₆H₁₂O₆ = 360. g}}[/tex]

10. What is the mass of ethanol that must be combusted to generate 20.4 L of CO2 at STP?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: Write the balanced chemical equation.

C₂H₅OH + 3O₂ → 2CO₂ + 3H₂O

Step 2: Use dimensional analysis to calculate the mass of ethanol combusted at STP.

Notes:

• Based on the balanced chemical equation, 1 mole of C₂H₅OH is stoichiometrically equivalent to 2 moles of CO₂.

• At STP, the volume of 1 mole of an ideal gas is 22.414 L.

• The molar mass of ethanol is 46.07 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{C}_2\text{H}_5\text{OH} & = 20.4 \: \cancel{\text{L}} \times \frac{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{CO}_2}}{22.414 \: \cancel{\text{L}}} \times \frac{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{C}_2\text{H}_5\text{OH}}}{2 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{CO}_2}} \times \frac{\text{46.07 g} \: \text{C}_2\text{H}_5\text{OH}}{1 \: \cancel{\text{mol} \: \text{C}_2\text{H}_5\text{OH}}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{21.0 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the mass of ethanol combusted is 21.0 g.

17. How many moles of glucose can the plant synthesize from the co2?


Answer:

Approximately 60 g of C6H12O6.

Explanation:

We have the balanced equation (without state symbols):

6H2O+6CO2→C6H12O6+6O2

So, we would need six moles of carbon dioxide to fully produce one mole of glucose.

Here, we got 88 g of carbon dioxide, and we need to convert it into moles.

Carbon dioxide has a molar mass of 44 g/mol. So here, there exist

88g44g/mol=2 mol

Since there are two moles of CO2, we can produce 26⋅1=13 moles of glucose (C6H12O6).

We need to find the mass of the glucose produced, so we multiply the number of moles of glucose by its molar mass.

Glucose has a molar mass of 180.156 g/mol. So here, the mass of glucose produced is

13mol⋅180.156 gmol≈60 g to the nearest whole number.

So, approximately 60 grams of glucose will be produced.

26. In the fermentation process, ethanol is produced from decomposition of glucose. C6H12O6 → 2C2H5OH + 2CO2. How many grams of ethanol is produced from the starting material of 987.1 g of glucose?

mass C₂H₅OH = 504.8 g

Calculating the molar mass of C₆H₁₂O₆ and C₂H₅OH

mass C₂H₅OH = 504.8 g
