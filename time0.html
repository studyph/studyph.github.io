25. What do graphs of distance vs time and distance vs time2 suggest?


Graphs of distance vs time shows velocity

Graphs of distance vs time² shows acceleration

Further explanation

Linear motion consists of 2: constant velocity motion with constant velocity and uniformly accelerated motion with constant acceleration

At constant velocity motion:

the speed of vo = v = constant

acceleration = a = 0

Δx = vt or x = xo + vt

An equation of constant velocity motion

[tex]\large {\boxed {\bold {S = v \times \: t}}}[/tex]

An equation of uniformly accelerated motion

[tex]\large {\boxed {\bold {x = xo + vo.t + \dfrac {1} {2} at ^ 2}}}[/tex]

V = vo + at

Vt² = vo² + 2a (x-xo)

x = distance on t

vo / vi = initial speed

vt / vf = speed on t / final speed

a = acceleration

In the displacement graph over time (d vs t) there are two possibilities that can occur :

1. The graph forms a straight line, heading up with a gradient m = tan θ. This corresponds to the position equation of constant velocity motion which is d = d0 + vt. This equation corresponds to the straight line equation y = mx + c with gradient m with y = d and x = t. So the gradient of the position versus time equation on this graph is m = tan θ = v

So the straight line on the graph shows the velocity of the object

2. The graph forms a parabolic because in the equation of position versus time in uniformly accelerated motion:

d = d0 + vot + 1/2 gt² is a quadratic function.

a parabola opens up if a> 0 (acceleration in the direction of velocity) and open down if a <0 (acceleration is opposite to velocity)
