10. LCM of 15, 45, 60, and 75.


Answer:

900

Step-by-step explanation:

LCM(15, 45, 60, 75) =

LCM(LCM(LCM(15, 45), 60), 75)

Working from the innermost parentheses outward:

LCM(15,45) = (15 × 45) / GCF(15,45)

= (15 × 45) / 15

= 675 / 15

= 45

LCM(45,60) = (45 × 60) / GCF(45,60)

= (45 × 60) / 15

= 2700 / 15

= 180

LCM(180,75) = (180 × 75) / GCF(180,75)

= (180 × 75) / 15

= 13500 / 15

= 900

Therefore,

LCM(15, 45, 60, 75) = 900

This is a proper fraction once the absolute value of the top number or numerator (60) is smaller than the absolute value of the bottom number or denomintor (75).

The fraction 60

75

can be reduced.

We will use the Greatest Common Factor (GCF) method to simplify it.

The GCF is the largest number between which two or more numbers can be divided evenly.

To find the GCF we list the the numerator and denominator factors (*) then we find the largest number that is repeated in both lists:

* The factors of 60 are 1,2,3,4,5,6,10,12,15,20,30,60;

* The factors of 75 are 1,3,5,15,25,75.

We can see that the GCD is 15 because it is the largest number by which 60 y 75 can be divided without leaving any residue.

To reduce this fraction, simply divide the numerator and denominator by 15 (the GCF).

So, 60

75

= 60÷15

75÷15

= 4

5

.

Thus, 60

75

is equivalent to 4

5

in the reduced form.

(*) The factors are numbers that multiply each other to get another number. For example, 2 and 3 are factors of 6, because by multiplying them you get 6.

The fraction 60

75

is equal to 60÷75 and can also be expressed as a decimal as 0.8 and expressed as a percentage as 80%.
