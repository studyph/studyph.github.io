The Little Black Boy

But soon with altered looks askance

They view his sable face and form,

When they perceive the scornful glance

Of the head boy, young Henry Orme.

Said he, ‘It does to me appear

To be a great disgrace and shame

Ladies and gentlemen,

Prejudice and bias are two things that have plagued our society for centuries. They come in different forms and are directed at people from various walks of life. We must acknowledge that we are all human beings and that we all deserve to be treated with respect and dignity, regardless of our race, religion, gender, or any other characteristic that makes us unique.

Prejudice and bias are often born out of ignorance and fear. People tend to fear what they do not understand, and this fear can easily lead to prejudice and bias. Unfortunately, this can have devastating consequences, not only for the individuals targeted by prejudice and bias but for society as a whole.

Prejudice and bias are often deeply rooted in our culture and history. They are perpetuated by stereotypes and discrimination, and they can be hard to break free from. However, it is important to remember that change is possible, and it starts with each and every one of us.

We must challenge our own prejudices and biases, and we must hold ourselves accountable for the impact that our actions and words have on others. We must educate ourselves about different cultures and perspectives, and we must actively seek to understand and appreciate our differences.

It is important to remember that we are all capable of prejudice and bias, and it is our responsibility to recognize it and work to overcome it. We must strive to create a society that is free from prejudice and bias, where everyone is valued and respected.

In conclusion, let us commit ourselves to fighting against prejudice and bias, to embracing diversity and inclusivity, and to creating a society that is built on respect, understanding, and compassion. Thank you.
