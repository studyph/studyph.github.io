9. comparison between ethnocentrism versus cultural relativism


Answer:

The perspective of ethnocentrism addresses foreign peoples from the standpoint of the superiority of the observer's culture, including values, religion, and symbols. Cultural relativism addresses other people in light of those people's culture.

Cultural relativism is the practice of assessing a culture by its own standards rather than viewing it through the lens of one's own culture. ... Xenocentrism is the opposite of ethnocentrism, and refers to the belief that another culture is superior to one's own.

19. Which ot the following statements is consistent with Boyle's Law concerning an ideal gas? A) At constant temperature and moles, a plot of pressure versus the inverse of volume is linear. B) At constant pressure and volume, a plot of temperature versus moles is linear. C) At constant temperature and pressure, a plot of moles versus volume is linear D) At constant temperature and moles, a plot of volume versus pressure is linear. E At constant pressure and moles, a plot of temperature versus volume is linear.


Answer:

A) At constant temperature and moles, a plot of pressure versus the inverse of volume is linear.

Explanation:

Boyle's Law states that the pressure of an ideal gas is inversely proportional to its volume at constant temperature and number of moles. This means that if the pressure of the gas increases, the volume of the gas decreases, and vice versa. A plot of pressure versus the inverse of volume will be linear at constant temperature and number of moles. The other choices do not accurately describe the relationship between the variables in Boyle's Law.
