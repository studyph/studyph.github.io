20. Adrian dissolved 10,0 g of sugar in 250 mL of water (the volume of cup of coffee). Calculatethe % by mass of sugar in the solution. The density of water is 1.00 g/mL.​


The % by mass of sugar in the solution : 3.85%

Further explanation  

The concentration of a substance can be expressed in several quantities such as moles, percent (%) weight/volume,), molarity, molality, parts per million (ppm) or mole fraction. The concentration shows the amount of solute in a unit of the amount of solvent.  

So the concentration of a solution can be expressed as a percent which is the ratio of solute to solution. Can be expressed in percent of mass (m / m) or volume percent (v / v)  

[tex]\tt \%mass=\dfrac{mass~solute}{mass~solution}\times 100\%[/tex]

Solution = Solute + Solvent  

Given

10,0 g of sugar in 250 mL of water

Required

the % by mass of sugar

Solution

mass of solute = mass of sugar = 10 g

mass of water :

= density x volume

= 1 g/ml x 250 ml

= 250 g

mass of solvent = mass of water = 250 g

mass of solution :

= 10 g + 250 g

= 260 g

% mass :

[tex]\tt =\dfrac{10}{260}\times 100\%\\\\=\boxed{\bold{3.85\%}}[/tex]
