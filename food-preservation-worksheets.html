Answer:

To determine the total number of canned goods from the worksheet about food donations, you will need to follow these steps:

Open the worksheet and locate the column that contains information about the canned goods.

Scan through the column and add up the number of canned goods that have been donated.

Once you have a total number, you can record it or use it as needed.

Alternatively, if the worksheet has a table or spreadsheet format, you can use a formula to calculate the total number of canned goods. For example, if the column containing the canned goods is labeled "Canned Goods" and the row numbers are labeled in column A, you can use the following formula in an empty cell: "=SUM(C2:C100)" (assuming that the canned goods are listed in rows 2 through 100). This formula will add up all the values in the "Canned Goods" column and display the total in the cell where the formula is entered.

24. Search for an example of a worksheet. Explain the different columns included in the worksheet.


Accounting Worksheet Columns Included:

-Unadjusted Trial Balance

-Adjusting Entries

-Adjusted Trial Balance

-Income Statement

-Balance Sheet

Explanations:

Unadjusted Trial Balance

A column contains all the company’s assets, liability, expenses, and revenue accounts, which are used in a concerned year.

Adjusting Entries

All the accounts of the company, which requires the passing of the adjustment entry will get listed in the adjustment column.

Adjusted Trial Balance

The adjusted trial balance will be prepared by combining the entries of the previous two columns, Unadjusted trial balance, and adjustments.

Income Statement

A column contains the values concerning the expenses and revenue accounts only. In this case, if the value of total revenue exceeds the expense column, then the difference will be the net income of the year of the company as it is earning more revenues in the year than what it is spending on its expenses.

Balance Sheet

A column contains the values with respect to the assets, liabilities, and owner’s capital only.
