6. what is an example of multimedia inside the classroom​


Answer:

Using this very broad definition of multimedia, multimedia in the classroom could include Power Point presentations that are created by the teacher, commercial software (such as multimedia encyclopedias) that is used for reference or instruction, or activities that directly engage the students in using multimedia to construct and convey knowledge. For the purposes of this course, we will focus on the final category -- engaging students in the use of multimedia to construct and convey knowledge.

Examples of multimedia, then, could include:

1. Students using concept-mapping software (such as Inspiration) to brainstorm

2. Students using a spreadsheet or graphing calculator to record data and produce charts

3. A small group of students creating a digital movie to demonstrate a procedure

4. A class website that displays student artwork

5. Students scanning their hands and importing the images into PowerPoint for a presentation about fingerprints

29. 3 examples of bias in classroom​


Answer:

Gender bias: Teachers may exhibit gender bias by treating boys and girls differently in the classroom. For example, they may call on boys more often than girls, or give boys more opportunities to participate in class discussions. This can create an unequal learning environment that can impact the academic achievement and confidence of female students.

Racial bias: Teachers may have implicit biases against students of certain races or ethnicities, which can affect how they interact with and evaluate those students. For example, a teacher may assume that a Black student is less intelligent or capable than a white student, leading them to provide less support or fewer opportunities for that student to succeed.

Socioeconomic bias: Teachers may unconsciously favor students from higher socioeconomic backgrounds over those from lower socioeconomic backgrounds. This can manifest in various ways, such as assuming that students from wealthier families are more intelligent or better behaved, or providing more opportunities and resources to those students than to their peers. This bias can have a significant impact on students' academic success and future prospects.
