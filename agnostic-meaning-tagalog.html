Hello :>

Definition of the following theism:

⇒ Monistic

Monism is the metaphysical view that all is of one essential essence, substance, or energy.

⇒ Monotheistic

Monotheism is the belief that there is only one deity, an all-supreme universally referred to as God.

⇒ Polytheistic

Polytheism is the belief in multiple deities, usually assembled into a pantheon of gods and goddesses, along with their own religious sects and rituals.

⇒ Agnostic

Agnosticism is the view or belief that the existence of God, of the divine or the supernatural, is unknown or unknowable.

⇒ Atheistic

A person who does not believe in the existence of a god or any gods

16. do you believe in gods when you are agnostic?


Good question, agnostic here.

Agnosticism. When you are agnostic, you believe that a God or gods' existence is not justifiable as humans can't provide enough evidence whether a God or gods truly exist or don't —that the basis for the existence or non-existence of a God or gods can never be known by humans.

Now answering that question, this is where it gets kinda complicated but...

Yes, you can still believe in a God or gods whilst being agnostic —and it's called Agnostic Theism or agnostotheism. It means you believe that a God or gods exist but still has this belief that the basis for His or their existence is unjustifiable. Meanwhile, you can also not believe in a God or gods and still possess the mindset of an agnostic —and it's called Agnostic Atheism, the opposite of agnostotheism.

18. What is the difference between agnostic and atheist? ​


Answer:

An atheist is someone who does not believe in God, whereas an agnostic does not believe it is possible to know for certain that God exists. It is possible to be both—an agnostic atheist does not believe but also believes we will never know whether a god exists.

30. What is the definition of agnostic?​


Answer:

Agnosticism is the view that the existence of God, the divine, or the supernatural is not known or knowable with any certainty. If the question is "Does God exist?", "yes" would imply theism, "no" would imply atheism, and "I'm not sure" would imply agnosticism—that God possibly can or cannot exist.
