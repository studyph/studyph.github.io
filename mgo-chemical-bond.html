THE FORMATION OF "MgO"

-The Mg loses two electrons to have an octet. And then, Oxygen gains two electrons to have an octet (again). The ionic bond between ions results from the electrostatic attraction of opposite charges. And now it results the final formula which is the magnesium oxide or for short MgO.

8. 1.How does ionic bonding take place between Sodium and chlorine to form (NaCl)? 2.How does ionic bonding take place between Magnesium and oxygen to form (MgO)?


Answer:

1.Because of the propensity of sodium to lose an electron and of chlorine to gain an electron, the elements are well suited to bond with one another. This transfer of electrons results in the formation of the ionic bond holding Na+ and Cl– together.

2.Another example of ionic bonding takes place between magnesium (Mg) and oxygen (O2) to form magnesium oxide (MgO). Since oxygen has a higher electronegativity, it attracts the two valence electrons from the magnesium atom and these electrons are transferred from the magnesium atom to the oxygen atom.

22. Why is it incorrect to draw a double bond in the Lewis diagram for MgO?​


Answer:

Magnesium (Mg) is able to bond with one oxygen (O) atom. The formula of the compound is MgO. You can see in the dot structure that the two atoms share four different electrons. When a bond is made with four electrons, it is called a double bond.
