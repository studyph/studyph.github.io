2. WHAT ARE THE CAUSES AND EFFECTS OFLANGUAGES DISORDERS?​


Answer:

Language disorders can have many possible causes, such as a brain injury or birth defect. A speech-language pathologist can help diagnose and treat a language disorder. Parents can help their child with language use and understanding through simple activities.

Explanation:

Answer:

What are language disorders in children?

Most infants or toddlers can understand what you’re saying well before they can clearly talk. As they get older and their communication skills develop, most children learn how to put their feelings into words.

But some children have language disorders. They may have:

Receptive language disorder. A child has trouble understanding words that he or she hears and reads.

Expressive language disorder. A child has trouble speaking with others and expressing thoughts and feelings.

A child will often have both disorders at the same time. Such disorders are often diagnosed in children between the ages of 3 and 5.


3. 1. Differentiate receptive and expressive language disorder.​


Answer:

Receptive language means the ability to understand information. It involves understanding the words, sentences and meaning of what others say or what is read.

Expressive language means being able to put thoughts into words and sentences, in a way that makes sense and is grammatically accurate.

7. Activity: Common Mental Disorder Complete the Table Below1. Major Depressire disorder 2. Bipolar Disorder 3. Schizophrenia 4. Post-traumatic Stress disorder need kona po ngayon.. please pakisagot po​


Brief Description:

1. Major Depressive Disorder - is a mental health condition characterized by persistent feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest in things that were once enjoyable. It can affect a person's daily life, relationships, and ability to function normally.

2. Bipolar Disorder - is a mental health condition that causes extreme mood swings, including episodes of mania and depression. A person with bipolar disorder may experience episodes of euphoria, elevated energy, and impulsive behavior during a manic episode, followed by periods of extreme sadness and hopelessness during a depressive episode.

3. Schizophrenia - is a chronic mental health condition that affects how a person thinks, feels, and behaves. It can cause symptoms such as delusions, hallucinations, disorganized speech and behavior, and a lack of motivation or emotion.

4. Post-traumatic Stress Disorder - is a mental health condition that can develop after experiencing or witnessing a traumatic event. It can cause symptoms such as flashbacks, nightmares, avoidance behavior, and heightened anxiety or arousal.

Common Symptoms:

1. Major Depressive Disorder - feelings of sadness, hopelessness, lack of energy or motivation, changes in appetite or sleep patterns, difficulty concentrating, and suicidal thoughts or tendencies.

2. Bipolar Disorder - manic episodes characterized by elevated mood, high energy levels, and impulsive behavior, followed by depressive episodes characterized by feelings of sadness, hopelessness, and loss of interest in activities.

3. Schizophrenia - delusions or hallucinations, disorganized speech or behavior, lack of motivation or emotion, and difficulty distinguishing reality from fantasy.

4. Post-traumatic Stress Disorder - flashbacks or nightmares, avoidance behavior or emotional numbness, heightened anxiety or arousal, and intrusive thoughts or memories of the traumatic event.
