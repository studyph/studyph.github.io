2. The base of an isosceles triangle is 18cm. If the perimeter is 62cm, find the length of each of the equal sides?


Answer:

The length of each equal sides is 22cm.

Step-by-step explanation:

Start with the formula: x + x + 18cm = 62cm since perimeter refers to the sum of the sides of a triangle and where x represents the equal sides of the isosceles right triangle.

Add x + x. Such that 2x + 18cm = 62cm.

Move 18cm to the other side of the equation. It will become 2x = 62cm + (-18cm).

Adding -18cm to 62cm will give 44cm. Thus, 2x = 44cm.

Lastly, divide both sides of the equation by 2 to get the value of x or the length of the side of the isosceles triangle. This will be 2x/2 = 44cm/2. Therefore, x = 22cm.

4. An arc of a circle measures 30°. If the radius of the circle is 5cm, what is the length of the arc?a. 2. 62cmb. 2. 3cmc. 1. 86cmd. 1.5 cm​


ARC LENGTH

First, we have to convert the given central angle which is in degrees to radians.

Multiply 30° by π/180. We can also just use a calculator to get the decimal form.

[tex]30( \frac{\pi}{180} ) = 0.524[/tex]

Second, let's use the formula to find the length of the arc which is:

[tex]s = rθ[/tex]where s is the length of the arc, r is the radius of the circle and θ is the central angle.

Lastly, let's substitute the values to the formula. Then simplify.

[tex]s = (5cm)(0.524)[/tex][tex]s = 2.62cm[/tex]

Therefore, the answer is a. 2.62cm.
