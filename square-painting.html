11. The amount of paint needed to paint a room is proportional to the area of the walls. If a room with 1000 square feet of walls space requires 2.5 gallons of paint, how many gallons of paint are needed for a room with 1750 square feet of wall space?​


Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

First, we provide the given information

1000 sq. ft. of wall space needs 2.5 gallons of paint

1750 sq. ft. of wall space requires x gallons of paint

We utilize the formula

[tex]\frac{a}{b} = \frac{w}{x}[/tex]

In this case,

a = 1000 sq. ft. of wall space

b = 2.5 gallons of paint

w = 1750 sq. ft. of wall space

x = ?

We solve for the value of x

[tex]\frac{1000}{2.5} = \frac{1750}{x}[/tex]

We cross-multiply

[tex]1000x = 2.5 * 1750[/tex]

Then, we retain the value of x and solve

[tex]\frac{1000x}{1000} = \frac{2.5*1750}{1000}[/tex]

[tex]x = \frac{2.5*1750}{1000}\\[/tex]

[tex]x = 4.375[/tex]

Therefore, it requires 4.375 gallons of paint to paint a 1750 sq. ft. of wall space.

24. A square painting canvas has a dimension of 11x - 3 units. What will be the area of the painting?


Answer:

The area of a square is calculated by multiplying the length of one side by itself. Since the painting canvas is a square with a side length of 11x - 3 units, the area can be calculated as:

Area = (side length)²

Area = (11x - 3)²

Expanding the expression using the binomial square formula, we get:

Area = (11x - 3)²

Area = (11x)² - 2(11x)(3) + 3²

Area = 121x² - 66x + 9

Therefore, the area of the painting is 121x² - 66x + 9 square units.
