19. how world system theory affect in the development of united kingdom? ​


Answer:

World Systems Theory is a theoretical framework developed by sociologist Immanuel Wallerstein in the 1970s that seeks to explain global inequalities in terms of the capitalist world economy. According to this theory, the world economy is divided into a core, semi-periphery, and periphery, with the core countries benefiting the most from the world system.

In the context of the United Kingdom, World Systems Theory could be applied to explain the country's historical development as a core country within the world economy. The UK's early industrialization and subsequent expansion of its empire allowed it to accumulate wealth and power, which it then used to maintain its dominant position within the world system.

However, World Systems Theory also highlights the exploitative nature of the world economy, as core countries benefit at the expense of the periphery. The UK's colonial history, for example, involved the extraction of resources and labor from its colonies to fuel its own industrialization and economic growth.

Overall, World Systems Theory can provide a useful framework for understanding the UK's historical development and its role within the global economy. However, it is important to note that the theory is not without its criticisms, and there are other factors beyond the world economy that have also influenced the UK's development.

WORLD SYSTEM THEORY

World-systems analysis is a well-established but poorly-defined critical research tradition in the social sciences. Its undisputed progenitor, Immanuel Wallerstein, steadfastly maintains that world-systems analysis is not a theory, yet it is widely referred to as such by commentators, critics, andpractitioners alike. The resolution to this conundrum is to identify the defining elements of world-systems analysis as a perspective for understanding human society, then to evaluate propositions based on these defining elements as theories that have been conceptualized from a world-systems perspective. In this paper five defining elements of world-systems analysis are identified that together support a central theorem that the core-periphery hierarchy of the modern world-system can best be understood in terms of state strength and cultural integration. A further conjecture is made that the successor world-system to the current capitalist world-economy is more likely to be an American world-empire than the socialist world-government cravedby many world-systems analysts.

WORLD SYSTEM THEORY AS A REGULAR CYLICAL RYTHMS

Wallerstein characterizes the world system as a set of mechanisms, which redistributes surplus value from the periphery to the core. In his terminology, the core is the developed, industrialized part of the world, and the periphery is the "underdeveloped", typically raw materials-exporting, poor part of the world; the market being the means by which the core exploits the periphery.

Apart from them, Wallerstein defines four temporal features of the world system. Cyclical rhythms represent the short-term fluctuation of economy, and secular trends mean deeper long run tendencies, such as general economic growth or decline.[1][2] The term contradiction means a general controversy in the system, usually concerning some short term versus long term tradeoffs. For example, the problem of underconsumption, wherein the driving down of wages increases the profit for capitalists in the short term, but in the long term, the decreasing of wages may have a crucially harmful effect by reducing the demand for the product. The last temporal feature is the crisis: a crisis occurs if a constellation of circumstances brings about the end of the system.

24. What is world system theory and dependency theory? how they differ each other?


Answer:

World system theory is an approach to understanding the global political economy. It views the world as a single economic system divided into core countries, semi-peripheral countries, and peripheral countries. Core countries are those with the most wealth and power, while peripheral countries are those with the least. Dependency theory is an economic theory that suggests that resources flow from a "periphery" of poor and underdeveloped states to a "core" of wealthy states, enriching the latter at the expense of the former. The two theories differ in their focus: world system theory focuses on how global economic systems are structured and how they interact, while dependency theory focuses on how resources flow from one country to another.

29. Creat an essay about dynamical systems theory?


Answer:

Dynamical systems theory is a branch of mathematics that studies the behavior of complex systems over time. It is used to model and analyze a wide variety of physical, biological, and social phenomena. The theory has been applied to many areas including economics, ecology, psychology, engineering, and robotics.

At its core, dynamical systems theory seeks to understand how different components interact with each other in order to produce an overall system behavior. This involves studying the relationships between variables such as energy levels or population sizes over time. By understanding these relationships it is possible to predict future states of the system based on current conditions. This can be used for forecasting or controlling complex processes such as climate change or economic growth.

The main tool used in dynamical systems theory is differential equations which are equations that describe how a system changes over time based on its initial conditions and external influences. These equations can be solved numerically using computers or analytically using mathematical techniques such as Laplace transforms or Fourier series expansions.

One important concept in dynamical systems theory is chaos which occurs when small changes in initial conditions lead to large differences in outcomes over time due to sensitive dependence on initial conditions (the butterfly effect). Chaos can lead to unpredictable behavior even though the underlying equations may be deterministic (not random). This unpredictability makes it difficult for scientists and engineers alike when trying to control complex processes like weather patterns or stock markets but also provides opportunities for creative solutions by exploiting chaotic dynamics instead of trying to suppress them completely.

In conclusion, dynamical systems theory provides powerful tools for understanding and predicting the behavior of complex physical, biological, and social phenomena over time by studying their interactions with each other through differential equations and chaos analysis techniques. It has been successfully applied across many disciplines from economics and ecology all the way through engineering and robotics providing valuable insights into our world around us
