Answer:

Step-by-step explanation:

Given: (0,8) and (-2,-6)

So ,

[tex]x_1=0[/tex][tex]x_2=-2[/tex][tex]y_1=8[/tex][tex]y_2=-6[/tex]

Using the formula:

[tex]\rm{X_{midpoint} = (x_2+x_1)/2}[/tex][tex]\rm{Y_{midpoint}= (y_2+y_1)/2}[/tex]

[tex] \\ [/tex]

For the x-coordinate:

[tex]\rm{X_{midpoint} = (x_2+x_1)/2}[/tex][tex]\rm{X_{midpoint} = (-2+0)/2}[/tex][tex]\rm{X_{midpoint} = -2/2}[/tex][tex]\rm{X_{midpoint}= -1}[/tex]

For the y-coordinate

[tex]\rm{Y_{midpoint}= (y_2+y_1)/2}[/tex][tex]\rm{Y_{midpoint} = (-6+8)/2}[/tex][tex]\rm{Y_{midpoint} = 2/2}[/tex][tex]\rm{Y_{midpoint}= 1}[/tex]

[tex] \\ [/tex]

Therefore , the midpoint is (-1,1)

ANSWERM = ( -1 , 1 )EXPLANATION

The midpoint for two points P and Q is a point M whose coordinates is the half-sum of the corresponding coordinates of the two points.

Thus,

[tex]M = (\frac{0 - 2}{2} , \frac{8 - 6}{2} ) = ( - 1,1) \\ [/tex]

11. Soil pH can vary over a significant range due to various causes. For example, the soil of areas with scarce rainfall, but with high concentrations of the soluble salt sodium carbonate (NaCO3), becomes basic due to the hydrolysis reaction of the carbonate ion, according to the equilibrium:CO-2*3(aq) + H2O(I) -- HCO-3(aq) + OH-(aq)These types of soils are too alkaline for agricultural purposes and must be remedied by the use of chemical additives. Suppose that, to remediate a sample of this type of soil, a technician had used virgin lime (CaO) as an additive. In that case, the remedy(A) was performed, because the basic character of virgin lime promotes the displacement of the described balance to the right, as a result of the pH elevation of the medium.(B) was performed, as the acidic character of quicklime promotes the displacement of the equilibrium described to the left, due to the reduction in pH of the medium.(C) was not performed, as the acidic character of quicklime promotes the displacement of the equilibrium to to the right, due to the reduction in pH of the medium.(D) was not performed, as the basic character of quicklime promotes the displacement of the described balance to the left, as a result of the pH elevation of the medium.(E) was not performed, as the neutral character of quicklime promotes the displacement of the described balance to the left, as a result of maintaining the pH of the medium.​


D.)It was not performed, because the basic character of quicklime promotes the displacement of the described balance to the left, due to the increase in pH of the medium.

The analysis of the chemical equilibrium presented brings us to the fact that the success of soil remediation is directly linked to the shift of the equilibrium to the right, that is, towards the consumption of carbonate ions.

In this way, the use of quicklime (CaO) as a remediation is not effective, because being a basic oxide, 'lime' shifts the balance to the left, a fact that is a consequence of the hydrolysis of the oxide, which generates hydroxide ions in the medium.
