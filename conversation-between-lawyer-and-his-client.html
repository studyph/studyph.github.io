Lawyer, advocate or attorney is a noun, subject. In practice it is also known as a Legal Consultant. Can mean a person who performs or provides advice (advice) and defense "represents" for other people who are related (clients) with the settlement of a legal case.

Duties of a lawyer:

Responsible for clients

A lawyer must understand that every suspect who is arrested, detained, prosecuted, or brought before a court must be considered innocent before the law

Before there is a court decision that has permanent legal force, and declares the suspect guilty, the lawyer is responsible for maintaining the principle of the presumption of innocence.

Become a client advisor and companion

The duty of a lawyer is not only limited to defending and acquitting the client from prosecution. A lawyer is obliged to provide legal consultation, represent, and assist clients from the beginning of the case, through the trial in court, until the case is finished.

Handling legal issues or cases

In handling cases, a lawyer must be able to analyze, define, create, explain, and apply legal concepts systematically. This ability is needed to resolve disputes between parties in conflicting legal matters.

Guarding the constitution and human rights

Lawyers are a profession that is close to the Law and the Constitution. The activities of lawyers must be in line with the constitution, including in enforcing regulations and fighting for human rights. If a lawyer does not fight for the Human Rights (HAM) of his client, various parties may act arbitrarily to their clients.

Spreading legal knowledge

As people who have extensive knowledge of law, lawyers have a social responsibility to spread legal knowledge to the wider community. This needs to be done so that more and more ordinary people are literate towards the law and more or less increase their knowledge of the law.

Update with various cases

Society is dynamic, innovation continues, change is an absolute thing, and changes in science are no exception. A lawyer must continue to learn because cases and legal science are also changing every day.

Provide legal assistance

Not everyone can afford the services of a lawyer, but in certain cases a lawyer can voluntarily provide free legal assistance to the community.

I would not report my client to the police because my job is to assist him to overcome his guilt by giving him room and freedom to express himself.

In my opinion, if I were a counselor providing counseling to a client and noticed that the client had unintentionally killed someone, I would not contact the police since my job is to help the client overcome his guilt by allowing him his own space and freedom to express himself. First and foremost, I would make sure I understood why it was done and how it happened. It is important to remember that as a counselor, you have a responsibility to support your clients correctly in situations where they need you most and want your understanding. Under no circumstances should you let a client down.

However, it would be a violation of our conversation with the customer because it should always be private and hidden if I ended up reporting him to the police. Therefore, the best course of action in this situation is to guarantee your client's confidence and secrecy and to be in a position to truly understand him without passing judgment.
