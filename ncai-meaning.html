Explanation:

Given:

[tex]mass_{solution} = 450 \: g[/tex]

[tex]\%(m/m) = 9\%[/tex]

Required:

[tex]mass_{solute}[/tex]

Solution:

Calculate the mass of solute (NaCl)

[tex]mass_{solute} = \frac{\%(m/m)}{100} \times mass_{solution}[/tex]

[tex]mass_{solute} = \frac{9\%}{100} \times 450 \: g[/tex]

[tex]\blue{mass_{solute} = 40.5 \: g}[/tex]

20. What is the procedure of on how you prepare of 0.500 table salt ( NaCI) solution diluted to a water. Note: M2= 0.100 M NaCI NaCI: 60g/mol


1. Measure out 60g of salt using a balance and put it in a volumetric flask (volume approximately 1000 mL)

2. Fill the volumetric flask up to the 1000 mL mark with room-temperature or purified water and mix to obtain a 0.500 M salt solution

3. Transfer an aliquot of the solution to a graduated cylinder (volume approximately 100 mL)

4. Transfer the remaining solution to a separatory funnel

5. Add a second volume of water (approximately 900 mL) to the separatory funnel

6. Shake the separatory funnel to mix the two solutions, and then let them sit

7. Drain the bottom layer into a clean flask, discarding any liquid remaining in the separatory funnel

8. Measure the volume of the diluted solution and calculate the molarity (M2= 0.100 M NaCI)

9. Label the flask with the date and concentration, store in a cool place.

To prepare a 0.500 M NaCl solution, you will need to follow these steps:

Calculate the mass of NaCl required to make 500 mL of a 0.500 M solution.

Molarity = moles of solute/liters of solution

0.500 M = x moles/0.500 L

x = 0.250 moles of NaCl

Mass of NaCl = moles x molar mass = 0.250 mol x 60 g/mol = 15.0 g

Weigh out 15.0 grams of NaCl using a digital balance and transfer it to a clean 500 mL volumetric flask.

Add distilled or deionized water to the flask until the volume reaches the 500 mL mark. Use a stirring rod to ensure that the NaCl dissolves completely.

Label the flask with the solution's concentration, the date, and any other relevant information.

To make a 0.100 M NaCl solution from this stock solution, you can dilute it with distilled water. For example, to make 100 mL of a 0.100 M NaCl solution, add 20 mL of the stock 0.500 M solution to 80 mL of distilled water.

Mix well, and your 0.100 M NaCl solution is ready to use.
