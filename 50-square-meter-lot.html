29. 6 C. Assessment/Application/Outputs 1. Directions: Analyze each problem and choose the letter of the correct answer. 1.A square lot was planted with santan plants all around. The side of the lot measured 10 meters. The plants were planted 2 meters apart, how many were planted in all? A.200 B.50 C. 20 D. 60 2.Rose uses 200 regular paces to walk around a square park. If one pace of Rose measures 40 cm, what is the measure of each side of the park? A. 1000 cm B. 3000 cm D. 4000 cm C. 2000 cm 3. The three sides of a triangle measure 24 cm, 34 cm, and 52 cm. What is the perimeter of the triangle? A. 58 cm B.86 cm C.76 cm D. 110 cm​


Answer:

1.To find the number of santan plants planted in the square lot, you can divide the perimeter of the square by the distance between each plant. The perimeter of a square is equal to four times the length of one side, which in this case is 10 meters.

The distance between each plant is 2 meters, so the number of plants is:

Number of plants = Perimeter of the square / Distance between plants

Number of plants = 4 x 10 m / 2 m

Number of plants = 40 / 2

Number of plants = 20

Therefore, the answer is (C) 20.

2.If Rose uses 200 regular paces to walk around a square park, and one pace measures 40 cm, then the total distance she walks is:

Total distance = 200 paces x 40 cm/pace

Total distance = 8000 cm

Since a square has four equal sides, the measure of each side of the park can be found by dividing the total distance by 4:

Measure of each side = Total distance / 4

Measure of each side = 8000 cm / 4

Measure of each side = 2000 cm

Therefore, the answer is (C) 2000 cm.

3.The perimeter of a triangle is the sum of the lengths of its sides. To find the perimeter of the triangle with sides measuring 24 cm, 34 cm, and 52 cm, you can add these values together:

Perimeter = 24 cm + 34 cm + 52 cm

Perimeter = 110 cm

Therefore, the answer is (D) 110 cm.
