11. In the second experiment of Mendel's work, you cross a dwarf garden pea with another dwarf pea. What will the first generation of peas look like? Choices: 25% dwarf 75% dwarf 50% dwarf all dwarf


In the second experiment of Mendel's work, where a dwarf garden pea is crossed with another dwarf pea, all the first-generation peas will be dwarf. This is because the dwarf trait is a homozygous recessive trait and both parent plants are homozygous for the dwarf allele. Therefore, all the offspring will inherit two copies of the dwarf allele, resulting in a dwarf phenotype. This is known as a monohybrid cross where only one trait is being studied.

13. Examples of Dwarf Planets​


EXAMPLES OF DWARF PLANETS

Like any other planets in the solar system and other interstellar bodies, a dwarf planet is an interstellar body and is somewhat round because of its own gravity. However, these planets does not have the mass and capability to clear out other smaller celestial bodies along its orbit around the Sun.

THE FIVE DWARF PLANETS

1.) Ceres - Named after the Roman goddess of Agriculture or Demeter in Greek Mythology, it is located between the orbit of Mars and Jupiter.

2.) Haumea - Located beyond the orbit of Neptune, or at the Kuiper Belt.

3.) Makemake - Located at the Kuiper Belt.

4.) Pluto - Named after the Roman god of the underworld or Hades in Greek Mythology. Located next to Neptune.

5.) Eris - Named after the Greek goddess of strife and discord, located beyond Pluto.

29. Heterozygous tall (Tt) and dwarf(tt) pea plant are crossed. The ratio in the offspring shall be A. All Tall B. All dwarf C. 3 Tall and 1 Dwarf D. 1 Tall and 1 Dwarf


Answer:

D. 1 tall and 1 dwarf

Explanation:

In a cross between heterozygous tall plants (Tt) and a homozygous dwarf plant (tt), the ratio in the offspring shall be 1 tall : 1 dwarf. This is because the heterozygous plant will make two types of gametes, one type having a T allele and the other having a t allele. Hence, the correct answer is ‘1 tall : 1 dwarf’
