11. how does the story of androcles and the lion begin?


A slave named Androcles once escaped from his master and fled to the forest. As he was wandering about there he came upon a lion lying down moaning and groaning. At first he turned to flee, but finding that the lion did not pursue him, he turned back and went up to him. As he came near, the lion put out his paw, which was all swollen and bleeding, and Androcles found that a huge thorn had got into it, and was causing all the pain. He pulled out the thorn and bound up the paw of the lion, who was soon able to rise and lick the hand of Androcles like a dog. Then the lion took Androcles to his cave, and every day used to bring him meat from which to live.
But shortly afterwards both Androcles and the lion were captured, and the slave was sentenced to be thrown to the lion, after the latter had been kept without food for several days.

The emperor and all his court came to see the spectacle, and Androcles was led out into the middle of the arena. Soon the lion was let loose from his den, and rushed bounding and roaring towards his victim. But as soon as he came near to Androcles he recognized his friend, and fawned upon him, and licked his hands like a friendly dog.

The emperor, surprised at this, summoned Androcles to him, who told him the whole story. Whereupon the slave was pardoned and freed, and the lion let loose to his native forest.




12. how to make the narrative about the story about Androcles and the lion​


Answer:

Once upon a time, there was a man named Androcles who lived in ancient Rome. He was a slave who had been sold to a cruel and heartless master. Androcles was treated poorly and was often punished for the smallest mistakes.

One day, Androcles decided to escape from his master's house and fled into the forest. As he was wandering through the dense woods, he suddenly heard a loud roar. He looked around and saw a huge lion approaching him.

Androcles was terrified and thought that the lion would surely attack and kill him. But to his surprise, the lion stopped in front of him and began to lick his feet. Androcles realized that the lion was not trying to harm him, but was actually in pain.

He saw that the lion had a large thorn stuck in its paw and was unable to remove it. Androcles, who had once been a doctor, carefully removed the thorn from the lion's paw. The lion roared in pain at first, but then licked Androcles' hand as a sign of gratitude.

From that day on, Androcles and the lion became friends. They would often spend time together in the forest, and the lion would even bring Androcles food to eat. However, their happiness was short-lived as Androcles was eventually caught and taken back to his master's house.

As punishment for his escape, Androcles was sentenced to be thrown into the Colosseum to be devoured by wild animals. On the day of his execution, Androcles was thrown into the arena, and to his amazement, the lion he had befriended appeared in front of him.

The lion recognized Androcles and ran towards him, licking his face and wagging his tail. The audience was stunned and moved by the sight of the lion's affection towards Androcles. The Emperor, who was watching the spectacle, was so impressed by the bond between the man and the lion that he ordered Androcles to be set free.

Androcles and the lion walked out of the Colosseum together, and the people cheered for them. From that day on, Androcles was a free man, and he and the lion continued to live together in the forest. Their friendship had conquered all obstacles, and they remained loyal to each other until the end of their days.
