If you increase the concentration of a reactant, there will be more of the chemical present. More reactant particles moving together allow more collisions to happen and so the reaction rate is increased. The higher the concentration of reactants, the faster the rate of a reaction will be.

3. Aluminum satellite dishes are exposed to oxygen in air and is prone to corrosion.However, aluminum reacts with oxygen to form a coating of aluminum oxideAIO3, which prevents any further corrosion. What is the mass of 7.25 mol ofaluminum oxide?​


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of Al₂O₃.

molar mass = (26.98 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 3)

molar mass = 101.96 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the mass of Al₂O₃.

[tex]\text{mass of Al₂O₃ = 7.25 mol Al₂O₃} × \frac{\text{101.96 g Al₂O₃}}{\text{1 mol Al₂O₃}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of Al₂O₃ = 739 g}}[/tex]

20. a metal that is resistant to corrosion​


Answer:

Aluminum

Aluminum alloys contain almost no iron and without iron, the metal can't actually rust, but it does oxidize. When the alloy is exposed to water, a film of aluminum oxide forms quickly on the surface. The hard oxide layer is quite resistant to further corrosion and protects the underlying metal.
