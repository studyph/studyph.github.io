24. 1. Define (magnet, magnetism, electromagnet) 2. What are the types of magnet? 3. Give 3 uses of magnet? 4. Draw, color and label the parts of electromagnet? Define each part.


Answer:

1.

- Magnet is an object that produces a magnetic field, which attracts certain materials like iron, nickel, and cobalt.

- Magnetism is the force of attraction or repulsion between magnetic materials.

- Electromagnet is a type of magnet that is created by an electric current flowing through a coil of wire.

2. The types of magnet are:

Permanent magnet: a magnet that retains its magnetism without external influence or electrical power.

Temporary magnet: a magnet that becomes magnetized in the presence of a magnetic field and loses its magnetism when the field is removed

.

Electromagnet: a magnet that is created by an electric current.

3. Some uses of magnet are:

Magnetic storage media like hard disk drives, tapes, and credit cards.

Magnetic separators in mining and recycling industries to separate ferrous materials from non-ferrous ones.

MRI (magnetic resonance imaging) machines in medical fields, which use magnetism to produce detailed images of internal organs and tissues.

4. (See image)

An electromagnet consists of a coil of wire (usually copper) wrapped around a magnetic core (e.g. iron or steel). When an electric current flows through the wire, it creates a magnetic field that magnetizes the core. The magnetic field produced by an electromagnet can be turned on and off by controlling the electric current flow.

Parts of an electromagnet:

Coil of wire: a conductor that carries the electric current, usually wound around a core to enhance the magnetic field.

Magnetic core: a ferromagnetic material that is used to concentrate and direct the magnetic field.

Power source: a battery or power supply that provides the electric current to the coil.

Answer:

1.A magnet is an object that produces a magnetic field. Magnetism is the force of attraction or repulsion between magnetic materials. An electromagnet is a type of magnet that produces a magnetic field by the flow of electric current.

2.There are four types of magnets:

Permanent magnets: They are made of materials such as iron, cobalt, and nickel, and can hold their magnetism for a long time.Temporary magnets: They can be magnetized when placed in a magnetic field, but lose their magnetism when the field is removed.Electromagnets: They are made by wrapping a wire around a core and passing an electric current through the wire, creating a magnetic field.Superconducting magnets: They are made of materials that have zero electrical resistance at low temperatures and can create very strong magnetic fields.

3.Three uses of magnet are:

Magnetic storage: Hard disk drives and magnetic tapes use magnets to store digital information.Medical imaging: Magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) uses strong magnets to create images of the body's internal structures.Electric generators: Electric generators use the motion of magnets to generate electricity.

4.the parts of an electromagnet.

An electromagnet has three main parts:

Wire: A coil of wire is wrapped around a core to create the magnetic field. When an electric current flows through the wire, it creates a magnetic field around the coil.Core: The core is usually made of iron or another magnetic material, and it enhances the magnetic field created by the coil of wire. It provides a path for the magnetic field to flow through, making the magnet stronger.Power source: An electromagnet needs a power source to create an electric current in the wire. This can be a battery, a power supply, or another source of electricity. When the power source is turned off, the magnetic field disappears.
