3. Assuming that the human body is 30% efficient in converting food to energy, and that a glass of milk has 600kJ, how many glasses (or how much of a glass) of milk do you need as energy to climb the flight of stairs?


To have enough energy to walk the flight of stairs, drink ten glasses of milk.

    The ratio of energy input to energy output is referred to as efficiency. A portion of the energy or heat that is supplied to a machine (such as a vehicle engine) is always lost, and only a small portion is really used to produce work. The more productive a machine is for a given input, the more output it generates.

Any efficiency that is determined using actual values will range between 0% and 100%. When efficiency is zero percent, all energy input is lost, and energy output is zero percent.A 100% efficiency, on the other hand, means that absolutely no energy is wasted.

    You cannot create energy, according to the fundamental tenet of energy conservation. Therefore, no machine's efficiency can ever be greater than 100%. But you'll probably read headlines claiming that LED lights or the efficiency of heat pumps can reach 300% or more.

Using the formula:

η=[tex]E_{out} /E_{in}[/tex] . 100%

where:

η is the efficiency [tex]E_{out}[/tex] is the energy output (in Joules)[tex]E_{in}[/tex] is the energy input 600kj multiplied by 30% efficiency is equal to 0.18kj where there are 42kcal in a glass of milktherefore there are 10 glass of milk in a 0.18kj.
