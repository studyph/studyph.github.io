THE LEAPING FROG: WORD PROBLEM

Problem:

Solve the word problem involving series and sequence. A frog wants to reach the end of a bamboo tree that has a height of a 78 meters. The frog leaps 4 meters during the day and slips 2 meters down during the night. How many days will it take the frog to reach the top of the bamboo?​

Answers with Step-by-step explanation:

In order to solve this word problem involving series and sequence, we can use our analytic and critical thinking skills to obtain the required data. We can also form a function in order to solve this puzzle. But we can also use a little bit of reverse engineering. As given in the problem, the frog leaps 4 meters during the day and slips 2 meters down during the night. This means that in a day, the frog can reach 2 meters. Since the top of the bamboo is 78 meters, this is the height that the frog needs to climb. The frog can keep doing this same routine for over 35 days.

On the 35th day, the frog has already leaped 70 meters.

On the 36th day, the frog has already leaped 72 meters.

On the 37th day, the frog has already leaped 74 meters.

On the daytime of 38th day, since the frog leaps constantly by 4 meters, he has already climbed the 78-meter tall bamboo. So the correct answer is 37 and a half days or we can just round this up to 38 days.

25. what are the effects of frog and insect, Ferns and Tree, Bee and Flower​


Answer:

Frog and Insect: Frogs are predators of insects and other small invertebrates, and their presence can help control populations of these organisms. Conversely, the loss of frogs from an ecosystem can lead to an increase in insect populations.Ferns and Trees: Ferns and trees can have a symbiotic relationship, where ferns can grow on the trunks and branches of trees, providing extra moisture and nutrients to the tree. Ferns also help to preserve soil moisture and prevent erosion.Bee and Flower: Bees are important pollinators for many flowers, and the relationship between bees and flowers is mutually beneficial. Flowers provide bees with nectar and pollen as a food source, while bees help to transfer pollen from flower to flower, allowing the flowers to reproduce and produce seeds. The loss of bee populations can have a negative impact on the reproduction of many plant species.

29. given animals:tarsiermosquitoes butanding Philippine eagle sea star butterfly frog​


1.

Tarsier - Females give birth to a single offspring, which is born fully furred and with its eyes open.

Mosquitoes - All mosquito eggs require some form of

water to hatch. Some species of female mosquitoes lay eggs directly on the water, while others lay eggs in small depressions where water can collect.

Butanding - The male ejaculates sperm from his penis into the female's vagina, or vent, sometimes while swimming. After fertilization, whale calves take between 1 year and 15 months to develop in the womb. The calf is born ready to swim and follows the mother on her marine migrations for at least the next 6 months.

Phillipine Eagle - The female matures sexually at five years of age and the male at seven. Like most eagles, the Philippine eagle is monogamous. Once paired, a couple remains together for the rest of their lives. If one dies, the remaining eagle often searches for a new mate to replace the one lost.

Sea Star - Males release sperm into the water and females release eggs. The fertilized eggs hatch into a larval form that lives as plankton, sometimes for months, before settling on the sea floor in its adult form.

Butterfly - First the basics; like humans, butterflies are either male or female. They mate, joining the tips of their abdomens, and the male passes sperm to the female in order to fertilize her eggs. The female then lays her eggs on plants or on the ground.

Frog - Typically, frogs lay eggs. This process usually occurs through external fertilization, where the female releases her eggs from her body into water. Then, the male releases his sperm to fertilize them.
