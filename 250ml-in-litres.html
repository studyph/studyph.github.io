26. In a vessel 36 litres of milk is mixed with 4 litres of water. From the vessel. 20 litres of this mixture is taken out and 12 litres of milk is added to it. What is the resultant respective ratio between the quantities of milk and water in the vessel ?


Answer:

The resultant respective ratio between the quantities of milk and water in the vessel will be 30:2.

Step-by-step explanation:

First, we need to start of with the initial mixture which had 36 litres of milk and 4 litres of water. The ratio of milk to water will be 36:4. Also we need to note that the total amount of mixture in the vessel will now be 40 litres.

If we take away 20 litres of the mixture from the vessel, the ratio of milk to water of that amount of mixture will be 18:2.

If we now add 12 litres of milk to that mixture, will now have a 32 litres mixture of milk and water having the ratio of 30:2.
