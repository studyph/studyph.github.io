Step-by-step explanation:

We are given the measure of the whole segment which is FM=3n+1 and the measure of half of segment which is MG=6-2n.

To get the other half we only subtract the measure of the whole (FM) to the measure of the half that is given (MG).

FM-MG=FG

3n+1-6-2n=FG

16. △ABC is a right triangle, AD⊥BC, EF//AD, FG⊥AC, DE⊥AC.  If EF=5 and EG =3, find FG​


Find FG​

The answer is FG = 4. Using the Pytha gorean Theorem in a right triangle, we can solve for the missing side FG if we know the other two sides EG and EF. By substituting the values given, we get FG = 4 units.

In right triangle ABC, AD is the altitude from A to BC, so we can apply the Pytha gorean Theorem to get:

AC² = AB² - BC²

Because triangle ABC is a right triangle, we get:AC² = AB² - BC² = AB² - (AC - AB)² = 2AB² - AC²Solving for AB, we get:AB = √(AC²/2)

Since DE is perpendicular to AC, triangle ADE is similar to triangle ABC, so we have:

DE/AD = AC/ABSolving for AD, we get:AD = (DE × AB)/AC

Since EF is parallel to AD, triangle AEF is similar to triangle ADE, so we have:

EFAD = AE/DE

Solving for AE, we get:AE = (EF × DE)/AD = (5 × DE)/(DE × AB/AC) = 5AC/(2√2)

Since FG is perpendicular to AC, triangle AFG is similar to triangle ABC, so we have:

FG/AC = AB/BC

Solving for FG, we get:FG = (AB × AC)/BC = (AC²/2)/(AC/BC) = BC/2To find BC, we can use the Pytha gorean Theorem again, since triangle ABD is also a right triangle:

BD² = AB² - AD² = AB² - (DE × AB/AC)²

Solving for BD, we get:BD = AB × √(1 - DE²/AC²) = AC/√2

Finally, the Pythagorean Theorem can be used to calculate BC:

BC² = AC² - BD² = AC² - (AC²/2) = AC²/2So:BC = AC/√2

And:

FG = BC/2 = (AC/√2)/2 = AC/(2√2)

• Therefore, if EF = 5 and EG = 3, we can use the Pytha gorean Theorem to find AC

E G² + G F² = E F²3² + F G² = 5²F G² = 16F G = 4

29. 5. How about the book placed in the table?a. Fn and Fsb. Fg and inc. Ft and ind. Ft and Fg​


Answer:

b. Fg and in

Explanation:

Hint: Consider the forces that can be present between the book and table. While considering the forces between the book and the table, also keep in mind the state of motion (state of rest or state of motion) of the book.

Complete step by step answer:

The force which is present between an object and a surface when the object is just only in contact or sliding over the surface is known as the frictional force.This frictional force depends on smoothness and roughness of the surface.There are two types of frictional forces which are static frictional force and kinetic frictional force.The static frictional force is present between the object and the surface when the object is just in the contact with the surface in a steady state.The kinetic frictional force is present between the object and the surface when the object is sliding over the surface.The book placed on the table is a rest and hence is in the contact with surface of the table.There must be some force holding the book at its position. Otherwise, the book has slipped from the surface of the table.Since the book is at rest and not moving on the surface of the table hence, there is the static frictional force between the book and the surface of the table keeping the book at its position until some force greater than this static frictional force is applied on it.

Note:If the book slides on the surface of the table then there is kinetic frictional force between the book and the surface of the table.Always remember that frictional force act in the opposite direction of the motion and there are mainly two types of friction.One is static friction which act when object is in rest position while other is kinetic friction which act when object is in motion.
