11. A pail holds about __________ of water. A.10mL B.10L C.10kg D.10g


Answer:

A pail holds about 10 L of water.

Step-by-step explanation:

Measuring the Volume of Liquid

What is volume?

In measuring the volume of a liquid, volume refers to capacity and the units used in measuring liquid.

The standard unit in the metric (SI) system for the unit of liquid volume is liter (L). Another commonly used unit of liquid volume is milliliter (mL), where 1000 mL = 1 L.

When do we use liters and milliliters?

Milliliters

Milliliters are often written as ml. It is used in measuring small amount of liquid.

Liters

Liters often written as L. It is used in measuring big amount of liquid.

Going back to the question above, "A pail holds about __ of water".

What is the answer?

10 kg and 10 g are not our options because kg and g are units used in measuring the weight of objects.

10 mL and 10 L are our options now.

We cannot choose 10 mL as our answer since the container used is pail and pail can hold big amount of water. It can hold more than 10 mL.

Therefore, 10 L is the answer as liter is used for big amount of water.

14. PERCENT by VOLUME VOLUME OF SOLUTE 10ml VOLUME OF SOLVENT 50ml what is the percent by volume?


✏️ Volume Percent

[tex] {\Large{\overline{\underline{\sf{\hookrightarrow Answer:}}}}} [/tex]

The volume percent is 16.67%.Solution:

Given that:

the volume of the solute is 10 mLthe volume of the solvent is 50 mL

Solve:

[tex] {\boxed{\sf{volume\:percent =\frac{volume\:of\:solute}{volume\:of\:solution} \times 100 \% }}} [/tex]

The total volume of the solution is the sum of both the volume of solvent and the volume of solute. Thus,

[tex] \sf{volume\:percent =\frac{10\:mL}{50\:mL + 10\:mL} \times 100 \% } [/tex]

[tex] \sf{volume\:percent =\frac{10\:mL}{60\:mL} \times 100 \% } [/tex]

[tex] \sf{volume\:percent = 0.1667 \times 100 \% } [/tex]

[tex] {\sf \therefore volume\:percent = {\boxed{\green{\sf{16.67 \%}}}}} [/tex]

Explanation:

% Volume Concentration = volume of solute / total volume of solution x 100%

% Volume Concentration = volume of solute / volume of solute + volume of solution x 100%

Given:

Volume of solute = 10mL

Volume of solution = 100mL

% Volume Concentration = 10mL / 10 mL + 100 mL x 100%

% Volume Concentration = 10mL / 110mL x 100%

% Volume Concentration = 0.909 x 100%
