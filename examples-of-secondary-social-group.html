4. Ano ang kahulugan ng social group? Primary at secondary group?


A social group is a collection of people who interact with each other and share similar characteristics and a sense of unity. A social category is a collection of people who do not interact but who share similar characteristics.

A primary group is typically a small social group (small-scale society) whose members share close, personal, enduring relationships. These groups are marked by members' concern for one another, in shared activities and culture. Examples include family, childhood friends, and highly influential social groups.

Secondary groups are another type of social group. They have the opposite characteristics of primary groups. They can be small or large and are mostly impersonal and usually short term. These groups are typically found at work and school.

14. Define the following- Primary group- Secondary group- In group and out group- Reference group- Social structure​


Answer:

Primary group: A primary group is a small social group consisting of individuals who share close, personal and enduring relationships characterized by face-to-face interactions, intimacy, and a strong sense of belonging. Examples of primary groups include families, close friends, and small peer groups.

Secondary group: A secondary group is a larger, impersonal group that is often formed for a specific purpose or goal, and whose relationships are generally more formal and instrumental than those found in primary groups. Examples of secondary groups include workplaces, political parties, and professional associations.

In-group and out-group: In-group and out-group refer to the social groups to which individuals belong (in-group) or do not belong (out-group). Members of an in-group share a common identity, values, and beliefs, and often view members of the out-group as different or even inferior. This can lead to biases and discrimination against the out-group.

Reference group: A reference group is a group of people that individuals use as a standard for evaluating their own attitudes, beliefs, and behaviors. These groups may be aspirational (such as a high-status social group) or normative (such as a group that reflects the values and norms of one's culture or subculture).

Social structure: Social structure refers to the patterns of relationships and social arrangements that shape social interactions and hierarchies in a given society. This includes social institutions such as families, schools, and political systems, as well as social norms and values that guide behavior and expectations for individuals within those institutions. Social structure can influence a wide range of outcomes, from individual behavior and decision-making to economic and political systems.
