20. what are differences between social science from natural sciences and humanities​


Answer:

Natural sciences use the scientific method in order to study the world around us. Physics, Chemistry, Biology, Geology etc.

“Social sciences” is a bit of a misnomer. What it refers to is disciplines that study human interactions: Sociology, Economics etc. But they do not qualify as sciences since they generally do NOT use the Scientific Method.

For information, the Scientific Method involves:

1) Creation of a hypothesis

2) Use the hypothesis to make a prediction that can be experimentally tested

3) Perform the experiment to see if the prediction is correct

Social science is perhaps referred to as such due to the involvement of mathematics, specifically statistics in their studies.
