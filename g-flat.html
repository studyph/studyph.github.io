9. It is a group of flats or sharps placed at the beginning of the staff which is written after the G Clef sign.


Answer:

The key signature comes right after the clef symbol on the staff. It may have either some sharp symbols on particular lines or spaces, or some flat symbols, again on particular lines or spaces. If there are no flats or sharps listed after the clef symbol, then the key signature is “all notes are natural”.

14. The G MAJOR scale is the simplest key because it has no sharp or flat?


Answer:

NO

Explanation:

G MAJOR

The G Major is not the simplest key because it has one sharp. It is the first major key in the family of sharp keys to receive the first sharp in its key signature. The sharp in G major is written on the fifth line of the treble staff and third line of the base staff. The G major scale is composed of pitch names G, A, B, C, D, E, F#. The F# is sharp; its pitch is raised one-half step.

The simplest major scale that has no sharp or flat is the C major scale and not the G major scale. C major scale is composed of pitch names C, D, E, F, G, A, B.
