1. b) A 2.5g sample of groundwater was found to contain 5.4 ug of Zn2+. What isthe concentration of Zn2+ in parts per million?​


Parts per Million by Mass (ppm)Discussion:

Parts per million (ppm) by mass is the ratio of the mass of a solute to the mass of the solution, multiplied by 1 million or 10⁶. The formulas used in solving percent by mass are as follows:

[tex]\boxed{\text{parts per million by mass} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solution}} × 10^{6}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{parts per million by mass} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solute + mass of solvent}} × 10^{6}}[/tex]

The parts per million by mass is a unitless number because it is a ratio of two similar quantities.

[tex]\\[/tex]

Problem:

A 2.5 g sample of groundwater was found to contain 5.4 μg of Zn²⁺. What is the concentration of Zn²⁺ in parts per million?

Given:

[tex]\text{mass of solute = 5.4 μg} = 5.4 × 10^{-6} \: \text{g}[/tex]

mass of solution = 2.5 g

solute: Zn²⁺

solution: groundwater

Required:

concentration (in ppm)

Solution:

[tex]\text{concentration} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solution}} × 10^{6}[/tex]

[tex]\text{concentration} = \frac{5.4 × 10^{-6} \: \text{g}}{\text{2.5 g}} × 10^{6}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{concentration = 2.16 ppm}}[/tex]

10. Write the expressions for Kc for the following reactions. In each case indicate whether the reaction is homogeneous or heterogeneous. (a) I2 ⇌ 2I (b) 2SO2 + O2 ⇌ 2SO3 (c) CO2 + C ⇌ 2CO (d) H2O + CO ⇌ CO2 + H2 (e) Fe2+ + Zn ⇌ Zn2+ + Fe


Start off by checking if there are solids, and liquids in the reaction. If so, such molecules must be omitted in the expression.

Let's start with the simple reaction:

[tex]aA+bB \leftrightharpoons cC+dD[/tex]

[tex]K=\frac{[C]^c[D]^d}{[A]^a[B]^b}[/tex]

So the answer in your question will be:

[tex]K=\frac{[I]^2}{[I_2]} \\K=\frac{[SO_3]^2}{[O_2][SO_2]^2}\\K=\frac{[CO]^2}{[CO_2][C]} \\K=\frac{[H_2][CO_2]}{[CO]}\\K=\frac{[Fe][Zn_2]}{[Zn][Fe_2]}[/tex]

Usually water is omitted since it is liquid but if it is in gaseous state consider it in the equation for Kp.

16. zn(s)+2Ag→zn2+(aq)+2Ag(s)​


Explanation:

This is a chemical equation that describes a single replacement reaction between zinc (Zn) and silver (Ag) ions.

In this equation, zinc metal (Zn(s)) reacts with silver ions (Ag+) in an aqueous solution to form zinc ions (Zn2+) and solid silver (Ag(s)).

The zinc atom is being oxidized as it loses electrons to form Zn2+ ions and the silver ions are being reduced as they gain electrons to form solid silver atoms.

The left side of the equation represents the reactants, zinc metal and silver ions, and the right side represents the products, zinc ions and solid silver.

It is also important to notice that the number of atoms is conserved on both sides of the equation, this is called the law of conservation of mass.
