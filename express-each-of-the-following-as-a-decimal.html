4. Supposed that the z is the test statistics for hypothesis testing, calculate the value of the z for each of the following. Express your answer in 2 decimal places. µ = 77, σ = 6, n = 109, x̄ = 78.2


Answer:

The value of z for the given details in the problem is 2.088.

Further Explanation

In doing researches, especially if it's Quantitative Researches, the usage of statistical treatment or tools is very important. Since the statistical tool helps the researcher to achieve one of the research's goals. This is where the Hypothesis Test comes in. Hypothesis Tests is a process of decision making by drawing a conclusion from the population on the basis of the sample size.

Steps In Doing Hypothesis Testing

1. Statement of the Hypotheses

Null HypothesisAlternative Hypothesis

2. Finding the Critical Value

3. Computing the Test Value.

4. The decision to Reject or Accept the Null Hypothesis

5. The implication of the decision in Step 4

One of the Hypothesis Tests that has been employed by the researchers is Z - Test for a Mean. This is used to draw a conclusion from the population on the basis of the sample size. This can be used when the sample size is normally distributed and the standard deviation of the population ([tex]\sigma[/tex]) is known or given.

The given situation refers to Step 3 of the Hypothesis Testing. The formula that should be used in the given details in the problem is [tex]z=\frac{\bar{x}-\mu}{\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}}[/tex],

where [tex]\bar{x}[/tex] is the sample mean, [tex]\mu\\[/tex] is the population mean, [tex]\sigma\\[/tex] is the population standard deviation, and [tex]n[/tex] is the sample size.

To solve for the value of [tex]z[/tex], substitute the values of [tex]\bar{x}=78.2[/tex], [tex]\mu=77[/tex], [tex]\sigma=6[/tex], and [tex]n=109[/tex] into the formula, then simplify the right side using a scientific calculator.

Solution:

                                      [tex]\begin{aligned}z&=\frac{\bar{x}-\mu}{\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}}\\z&=\frac{78.2-77}{\frac{6}{\sqrt{109}}}\\z&=\frac{1.2}{\frac{6}{\sqrt{109}}}\\z&=1.2\times{\frac{\sqrt{109}}{6}}\\z&=\frac{1.2\sqrt{109}}{6}\\z&=2.088\end{aligned}[/tex]

This clearly shows that the value of [tex]z[/tex] is [tex]2.088[/tex].

7. Solve each of the following problems. Then, express your answer as a fraction and as a decimal. 1. There are 25 students in a class. Twelve of them have come late. What fraction of the class has come early?​


Answer:

To find the fraction of the class that has come early, we need to subtract the number of students who came late (12) from the total number of students (25) to get the number of students who came early:

Number of students who came early = Total number of students - Number of students who came late

Number of students who came early = 25 - 12

Number of students who came early = 13

So, 13 out of 25 students came early.

To express this as a fraction, we can write:

Fraction of the class that came early = 13/25

To express this as a decimal, we can divide 13 by 25 using a calculator or long division:

13 ÷ 25 = 0.52

Therefore, the fraction of the class that came early is 13/25, which is equivalent to 0.52 as a decimal.

Step-by-step explanation:

The problem states that there are 25 students in a class, and 12 of them came late. To find the fraction of the class that came early, we need to subtract the number of students who came late from the total number of students to find the number of students who came early.

We can subtract the number of students who came late (12) from the total number of students (25) to find that 13 students came early. The fraction of the class that came early is 13/25 because there are 13 students who came early out of a total of 25 students in the class.

To express 13/25 as a decimal, we divide 13 by 25, which gives us 0.52. Therefore, the fraction of the class that came early is 13/25, which is equivalent to 0.52 as a decimal.

12. Supposed that the z is the test statistics for hypothesis testing, calculate the value of the z for each of the following. Express your answer in 2 decimal places. µ = 20.8, σ = 4, n = 99, x̄ = 21.2


Answer:

The value of z is 0.9949874371 or 0.99.

To solve for the actual standard score or z, use the central limit theorem. The central limit theorem states that if either the sample size is at least 30 and/or the population is normally distributed, then the standard score for the sample mean is

                                   [tex]\[z=\frac{\bar{x}-\mu}{\frac{\sigma}{\sqrt{n}}}\]\\[/tex]

To verify the formula for the standard score, access the link https://brainly.ph/question/2160720.

where, z is the standard score for the sample mean, \bar{x} is the sample mean, [tex]\mu[/tex] is the population mean, [tex]\sigma[/tex] is the population standard deviation, and n is the sample size.

To know when to apply the central limit theorem, access the link https://brainly.ph/question/2134599.

Use these steps to solve for z:

1. Substitute the value of [tex]\mu = 20.8\\[/tex], [tex]\bar{x}=21.2[/tex], [tex]\sigma = 4[/tex] and [tex]n=99[/tex] into the equation.

2. Simplify the expression.

3. Round it off to the nearest hundredths.

                                    [tex]\begin{aligned}z&=\frac{21.2-20.8}{\frac{4}{\sqrt{99}}}\\&=\frac{0.4}{0.4020151261}\\&=0.9949874371\\&\approx{0.99}\end{aligned}\\[/tex]

The formula is taken from computing the test value of a hypothesis test.

The  steps in traditional method of doing hypothesis tests are as follows:

1. State the hypotheses and identify the claim.

2. Determine the critical value using the appropriate distribution table.

3. Compute the test value.

4. Decision making (accept or reject the null hypothesis)

5. State the conclusion.

The third step above determines the test value of a particular hypothesis test.

17. Supposed that the z is the test statistics for hypothesis testing, calculate the value of the z for each of the following. Express your answer in 2 decimal places. µ = 31.2, σ = 3, n = 66, x̄ = 32


In general, z-test is used to compare how far, in standard deviation, the given data point is away from the population mean. It is essential in testing a hypothesis. However, we have to satisfy a number of conditions before we use this statistical calculation. In general, these are:

1.) The sample data should be randomly selected and should represent the population.
2.) Sample size should be greater than 30, otherwise use t-test.
3.) The data should be normally distributed and should be independent form each other.

Take a look at this example when z-test is appropriate to use as test statistics:

You recently discovered a new treatment for skin allergy and you want to test if it's more effective and cost-efficient than the most of the proven treatments. Then, you conduct a clinical trial in your community.

Data required for z-test vary, but in case you will need your sample’s size and mean and the population’s mean and standard deviation. One of the formulas of z-test is:

[tex]z = \frac{sample mean - population mean}{ (\frac{population variance}{\sqrt{n} })} [/tex]
[tex]z= \frac{32 - 31.2}{ (\frac{3}{\sqrt{66} })} [/tex]
[tex]z = 2.17[/tex]

The answer is 2.17! You need to further compare it against a critical value, but it isn't necessary since you don't have level of significance (α) as a given.
