18. why do humans have fat spread all over the body, while frogs have fat bodies found only in one area


Frogs and humans have different body anatomy. One must have noticed that there are no fats on the skin on the limbs of the frog because the frog skin also serves on its respiration since frogs are amphibians, they can adapt on their environment whether they are on land or in water. Having fats on their skin and muscles on their limbs will lessen the area for respiration and may even block the pathway of the biological process.  

The frogs’ fat bodies (yellowish, fingerlike structured fats) or adipose tissues are located in one area and that is in its body cavity. It is usually found near its gonads (reproductive organs) and intestines. The purpose of the fat bodies are for their hibernation and estivation, reproduction, and metamorphosis. As aforementioned, frogs can adapt the environment’s temperature but they cannot regulate their own body temperature. Hence, fat bodies are important in their hibernation and estivation since it insulates heat and is also a source of energy during those activities that contributes to the formation of the genetic material (since the process needs energy and during estivation and hibernation, frogs stay in place to survive and no immediate food is available) and the genetic material is needed for reproduction. For the metamorphosis, fat bodies serves as insulators on the laid eggs and serves as the source of nutrients for the tadpoles. The fat bodies also help them float in water because of the density, buoyancy, and molecular composition of fats. These also serve as protection of the visceral organs and shock absorber. Fat bodies are larger in females than in males because females have larger reproductive organs since eggs take up most of the body cavity space.  

Humans, on the other hand, have fats all over the body as the alternative source of energy, serves as protection, and for insulation for the cold temperatures. With that, body temperatures can be regulated. In humans, the fats are not called fat bodies since the term only applies to the amphibians. Still, they are made up of almost the same components.
