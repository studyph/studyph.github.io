1. A gas has a volume of 460 mL at 500 mm Hg. What will be the volume at 1.2 atm? (252.2 mL)


Given:

[tex]P_{1} = \text{500 mm Hg}[/tex]

[tex]V_{1} = \text{80 mL}[/tex]

[tex]P_{2} = \text{1.2 atm} × \frac{\text{760 mm Hg}}{\text{1 atm}} = \text{912 mm Hg}[/tex]

Unknown:

[tex]V_{2}[/tex]

Solution:

Using Boyle's Law, the final volume of a gas can be written as

[tex]P_{1}V_{1} = P_{2}V_{2}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = V_{1} × \frac{P_{1}}{P_{2}}[/tex]

[tex]V_{2} = \text{460 mL} × \frac{\text{500 mm Hg}}{\text{912 mm Hg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{V_{2} = \text{252.2 mL}}[/tex]

9. A sample of sulfur dioxide occupies a volume of 652 mL at 40oC and 720 mm Hg. What volume will the sulfur dioxide occupy at 760 mm Hg and zero degrees Celsius?


Explanation:

This is a combined gas law. Therefore, the equation is  [tex]\frac{P_{1} V_{1} }{T_{1} }[/tex]= [tex]\frac{P_{2}V_{2} }{T_{2} }[/tex]

And your given is:

P1 = 720 mmHg

V1 = 652 mL (convert to liters) -> 652 mL / 1000 = 0.652L

T1 = 40 degrees Celsius (convert to Kelvin) -> 40 + 273.15 = 313.15 K

P2 = 760 mmHg

V2 = ???

T2 = 0 degrees Celsius (convert to Kelvin) -> 0 + 273.15 = 273.15 K

Now, let's derive the formula so that V2 is isolated so that it's easier to solve.

[tex](\frac{P_{1} V_{1} }{T_{1} })(\frac{T_{2} }{P_{2} })[/tex] = [tex]\frac{P_{2}V_{2} }{T_{2} } (\frac{T_{2}}{P_{2}} )[/tex]

V2 = [tex]\frac{P_{1} V_{1}T_{2} }{T_{1} P_{2} }[/tex]

Then, substitute all the given values

V2 = (720mmHg * 0.652L * 273.15K) / (760mmHg *313.15K)

and calculate

V2 = 0.539 L is the volume :)

~ (round to the lowest significant number) 0.5L

17. Find the volume of a gas at standard pressure if Its volume at 780 mm Hg is 80 ml? (HINT : standard pressure = 760 mmHg)


Good Day.

Problem:

Find the volume of a gas at standard pressure if Its volume at 780 mmHg is 80 ml?

Note: standard pressure is equal to 1 atm = 760 mmHg

Given Data:

V1 (initital volume) = 80 ml              V2 (final volume)  =  ? unknown

P1 (initial pressure)= 780 mmHg     P2 (final pressure)  = 760 mmHg

Solution:

This problem can be solve using Boyle's Law which state that volume is inversely proportional to pressure when the amount and temperature of gas are constant. Formula of Boyle's Law is P1V1 = P2V2

To solve for the V2, we can use V2 = P1V1 / P2

V2 = P1V1 / P2

      = 780 mmHg  × 80 ml  / 760 mmHg     (cancel the unit mmHg)

      = 62400 ml / 760 mmHg

      = 82.105 ml

Answer:

The volume is 82.105 ml at standard pressure

22. A gas sample has an initial volume of 55.2 ml, an initial temperature of 35.0oc and an initial pressure of 735 mm hg. the volume is decreased to 48.8 ml and the temperature is increased to 72.5oc. what is the final pressure


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

To convert the temperature from degree Celsius to kelvin, add 273.15 to the temperature expressed in degree Celsius.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_1 & = \text{735 mm Hg} \\ V_1 & = \text{55.2 mL} \\ T_1 & = 35.0^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{308.15 K} \\ V_2 & = \text{48.8 mL} \\ T_2 & = 72.5^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{345.65 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final pressure using combined gas law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_2 & = \frac{P_1V_1T_2}{V_2T_1} \\ & = \frac{(\text{735 mm Hg})(\text{55.2 mL})(\text{345.65 K})}{(\text{48.8 mL})(\text{308.15 K})} \\ & = \boxed{\text{933 mm Hg}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the final pressure is 933 mm Hg.

27. A sample of gas occupies a volume of 450.0 mL at 740 mm Hg and 16°C. Determine the volume of this sample at 760 mm Hg and 37°C.


Problem:

A sample of gas occupies a volume of 450.0 mL at 740 mm Hg and 16°C. Determine the volume of this sample at 760 mm Hg and 37°C.

Note:

To solve problem in gas law, temperature must be in kelvin unit. We have to convert degree celsius to kelvin by the formula ( Kelving = °C  +273.15).

Kelvin = 16°C + 273.15 = 289.15 K

Kelvin = 37°C + 273.15 = 310.15 K

Given Data:

P₁ (initial pressure) = 740 mmHg         P₂ (final pressure) = 760 mmHg

T₁ (initial temperature ) = 289.15 K      T₂ (final temperature)  = 310.15 K

V₁ (initial volume)  =  450 ml                 V₂ (final volume) = ? unknown

We can use the formula  V₂  =  P₁ × T₂ × V₁  / P₂T₁

V₂  =  P₁ × T₂ × V₁  / P₂T₁

     = 740 mmHg  × 310.15 K × 450 ml  / 760 mmHg ×  289.15 K

     = 103, 279, 950 ml / 219,754

     = 469.98 ml

Answer:

Final volume is equal to  469.98 ml

Further explanation  

Some of the laws regarding gas, can apply to ideal gas (volume expansion does not occur when the gas is heated), among others  

Boyle's law at constant T, P = 1 / V  Charles's law, at constant P, V = T  Avogadro's law, at constant P and T, V = n  

So that the three laws can be combined into a single gas equation, the ideal gas equation  

 [tex]\rm PV=nRT[/tex]

 In Boyle's law, it is stated that  

In a closed system, the absolute pressure by an ideal gas is inversely proportional to the volume it occupies if the temperature is constant  

 So it can be stated  

 [tex]\rm P_1V_1=P_2V_2[/tex]

A gas has a volume of 460 mL at 500.mm Hg.

So :

V₁ = 460 ml

P₁ = 500 mmHg , convert to atm :

[tex]\rm P_1=\dfrac{500~mmHg}{760~mmHg}\times 1~atm\\\\P_1=0.658~atm[/tex]

Then the volume(V₂) at 1.2 atm (P₂) :

[tex]\rm V_2=\dfrac{P_1\times V_!}{P_2}\\\\V_2=\dfrac{0.658~atm\times 460~ml}{1.2}\\\\V_2=\boxed{\bold{252.23~ml}}[/tex]

30. a gas has a volume of 1234 ml at 37 C and 620 mm Hg pressure. Calculate its volume at STP


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

Note that at STP, the temperature and pressure of an ideal gas are 273 K and 760 mmHg, respectively.

[tex]\begin{aligned} P_1 & = \text{620 mmHg} \\ V_1 & = \text{1234 mL} \\ T_1 & = 37^{\circ}\text{C} = \text{310 K} \\ P_2 & = \text{760 mmHg} \\ T_2 & = \text{273 K} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the final volume using combined gas law.

[tex]\begin{aligned} V_2 & = \frac{V_1P_1T_2}{P_2T_1} \\ & = \frac{(\text{1234 mL})(\text{620 mmHg})(\text{273 K})}{(\text{760 mmHg})(\text{310 K})} \\ & = \boxed{\text{886.5 mL}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the volume of the gas at STP is 886.5 mL.
