FURTHER EXPLANATION

1. Mathematics is exhibited only through numbers.

Answer: DISAGREE

Reason:

Mathematics is more than just numbers. It is more about reasoning, making logical inferences and generalizations, and seeing patterns in the natural world, both visible and invisible.

2. Mathematics can progress even without numbers,

Answer: AGREE

Reason:

You have already studied mathematics without numbers if you did any geometric proofs in high school. You don't need computer calculations to know that two triangles are equal when their sides are equal (you can identify them with an explicit motion), or to prove that a line intersecting two parallel lines yields equal angles.

3. Every phenomenon, whether scientific or social, can be explained by

mathematics.

Answer: DISAGREE

Reason:

There may be many situations in which the scenario cannot be mathematically defined. Similarly, it is not possible to depict all social phenomena.

4. Patterns that occur in nature are only for art appreciation and not for

mathematical explorations.

Answer: AGREE

Reason:

Natural patterns are only for art appreciation, not for mathematical exploration. Mathematics is not intended for everyone to learn. Pattern-based production does not always produce precise results. Mathematical models are error-prone simulations of patterns.

5. Natural occurrences are random events and cannot be modeled by

certain designs.

Answer: AGREE

Reason:  

Natural occurrences are unpredictable and cannot be predicted by certain designs. Mathematical models are never completely accurate. In mathematics, theorems are absolute truths that can be proven over time. There is no aspect of human or social life that does not involve some form of mathematical reasoning.

6. Mathematical models are never accurate,

Answer: DISAGREE

Reason:

Mathematical models are accurate. These models are accurate as it will never be changed. The result that will be gathered mathematically can't be changed unlike when we use qualitative models. Mathematical models refer to quantitative models which is more accurate than qualitative models.
