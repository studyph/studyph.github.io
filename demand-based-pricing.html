Inflation is the rate of increase in prices over a given period of time. Inflation is typically a broad measure, such as the overall increase in prices or the increase in the cost of living in a country.

What increases when price decreases?

If the price goes up, the quantity demanded goes down (but demand itself stays the same). If the price decreases, quantity demanded increases. This is the Law of Demand.

28. In a 1/2 sheet of paper answer the following. Given the demand schedule below, determine the elasticity in the different situations. Show your solution: 3 point for the solution, 1 point for the type of elasticity, 1 point for the conclusion (conclusion should be base on the situation) Price Quantity Demanded Situation I 11 III 1. Situation I and II 2. Situation II and III 35​


Answer:

Elasticity = (% Change in Quantity Demanded) / (% Change in Price)

Situation I:

Price = 50, Quantity Demanded = 30

When the price increases to 60, the quantity demanded decreases to 35.

% Change in Quantity Demanded = ((35-30)/30) x 100% = 16.67%

% Change in Price = ((60-50)/50) x 100% = 20%

Elasticity = 16.67% / 20% = 0.83

Note: The demand is inelastic in situation I, which means that the percentage change in quantity demanded is less than the percentage change in price.

Situation II:

Price = 60, Quantity Demanded = 35

When the price increases to 70, the quantity demanded remains constant at 35.

% Change in Quantity Demanded = 0%

% Change in Price = ((70-60)/60) x 100% = 16.67%

Elasticity = 0% / 16.67% = 0

note: The demand is perfectly inelastic in situation II, which means that the percentage change in quantity demanded is zero when there is a change in price.

Situation III:

Price = 70, Quantity Demanded = 35

When the price decreases to 60, the quantity demanded remains constant at 35.

% Change in Quantity Demanded = 0%

% Change in Price = ((60-70)/70) x 100% = -14.29%

Elasticity = 0% / -14.29% = 0

note: The demand is perfectly inelastic in situation III, which means that the percentage change in quantity demanded is zero when there is a change in price.


Based on the calculated elasticity values, we can conclude that situation I has an inelastic demand, while situations II and III have perfectly inelastic demands. This means that in situation I, an increase in price would lead to a less than proportionate decrease in quantity demanded, whereas in situations II and III, any change in price would not result in a change in the quantity demanded.
