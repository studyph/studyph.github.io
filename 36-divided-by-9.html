Let's assume R varies directly as square of P.

R = kP²           (original equation)

Ex: R = 3, P = 2

3 = k(2)²

3 = 4k

Multiplying R by 9

R x 9 = kP²

9R = kP²    (The P would be larger than the P in the original equation)

Ex: R = 3, P = 2

3 x 9= kP²

27 = k(2)²

27 = 4k

Dividing R by 36

R/36 = kP²

R/36 = kP²    (The P would be smaller than the P in the original equation)

Ex: R = 3, P = 2

3 / 36= kP²
