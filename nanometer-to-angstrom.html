3. what is an angstrom?​


Answer:

Unit of length

Explanation:

The angstrom or ångström is a metric unit of length equal to 10⁻¹⁰ m; that is, one ten-billionth of a metre, a hundred-millionth of a centimetre, 0.1 nanometre, or 100 picometres. Its symbol is Å, a letter of the Swedish alphabet. The unit is named after the Swedish physicist Anders Jonas Ångström.

11. One nanometer is equal to 10-7 centimeter. If the diameter of a virus is 20 nanometers, what is its diameter in centimeter?​


Step-by-step explanation:

The answer would be 0.000002 centimeters.

Also, to convert from scientific notation to a regular mathematical number, you need to move the decimal place to the left depending on the amount of movements of the exponent. As a result, one nanometer = 0.0000001.

So, since 1 nanometer = 0.0000001, and the problem is asking what is the diameter of the virus when converting it from 20 nanometers to centimeters, we need to multiply:

0.0000001 x 20, which would result to 0.000002.
