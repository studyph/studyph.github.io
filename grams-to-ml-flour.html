2. If a recipe requires 3 cups of flour to bake the cake, how many grams of flour is need assuming that its density is 0.5g/mL? (Show and upload the picture of your solution below)Use the assumption:1 cup flour = 236.59 mL*​


To convert cups to milliliters, we can use the conversion factor provided:

1 cup flour = 236.59 mL

To find the mass of flour needed, we can use the density provided:

density of flour = 0.5 g/mL

We can use the formula:

mass = volume x density

where the volume is in milliliters and the density is in grams per milliliter.

So for 3 cups of flour, the volume in milliliters is:

3 cups x 236.59 mL/cup = 709.77 mL

And the mass of flour in grams is:

mass = volume x density = 709.77 mL x 0.5 g/mL = 354.89 g

Therefore, 354.89 grams of flour is needed to bake the cake.

22. Mother mixes 125 grams of softened butter, 125 grams of sugar, 2 eggs ( about 150 grams ) and 125 grams of flour to make 45 serving of sponge cake. What is the ratio of the amount of butter to the amount of sugar to the amount of flour?


Answer:

To find the ratio of the amount of butter to the amount of sugar to the amount of flour, we first need to add the amount of butter, sugar, and flour to find the total amount of these ingredients: 125 grams + 125 grams + 125 grams = 375 grams

We then divide each ingredient by the total amount to find the ratio of each ingredient to the others:

Butter: 125 grams / 375 grams = 1/3

Sugar: 125 grams / 375 grams = 1/3

Flour: 125 grams / 375 grams = 1/3

Therefore, the ratio of the amount of butter to the amount of sugar to the amount of flour is 1:1:1, or 1/3:1/3:1/3.

26. 450 gram of flour with 1 L of water


Answer:

percent concentration = 31.03%

Explanation:

Calculating the mass of the solvent

mass solvent = 1 L × (1000 g / 1 L)

mass solvent = 1000 g

Note: The density of water is 1 g/m L or 1000 g/L

Calculating the mass of the solution

mass solution = mass solute + mass solvent

mass solution = 450 g + 1000 g

mass solution = 1450 g

Calculating the percent concentration

percent concentration = (mass solute / mass solution) × 100

percent concentration = (450 g / 1450 g) × 100

percent concentration = 31.03%
