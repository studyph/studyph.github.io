13. Mario likes to wear colored shirts. He has 18 shirts in the closet. Five of these are blue, six are in different shades of red, and the rest are of mixed or different colors. What is the probability that he will wear a blue or a red shirt?​


Answer:

There are a total of 18 shirts in Mario's closet. Out of these, 5 are blue and 6 are red.

The probability of Mario wearing a blue or a red shirt is the sum of the probabilities of him wearing a blue shirt and a red shirt.

P(wearing blue or red shirt) = P(wearing blue shirt) + P(wearing red shirt)

P(wearing blue shirt) = number of blue shirts / total number of shirts = 5/18

P(wearing red shirt) = number of red shirts / total number of shirts = 6/18

P(wearing blue or red shirt) = 5/18 + 6/18 = 11/18

Therefore, the probability of Mario wearing a blue or a red shirt is 11/18.

24. 2.) Rhian likes to wear colored shirts. She has 15 shirts in the closet. 5 of these are blue, 4 are different shades of red, and the rest are of different colors. What is the probability that she will wear a blue or a red shirt?​


There are a total of 15 shirts in Rhian's closet. Out of these, 5 are blue and 4 are different shades of red. Therefore, the number of shirts that are either blue or red is:

5 + 4 = 9

So, there are 9 shirts that Rhian can wear that are either blue or red. The probability of her wearing a blue or a red shirt can be calculated by dividing the number of shirts that are either blue or red by the total number of shirts:

Probability of wearing a blue or a red shirt = number of blue or red shirts / total number of shirts

Probability of wearing a blue or a red shirt = 9 / 15

Probability of wearing a blue or a red shirt = 0.6

Therefore, the probability that Rhian will wear a blue or a red shirt is 0.6 or 60%.

28. rose like to waer colored shirts. she has 15 shirts in the closet. five of these are blue, four are in different shades of red and the rest are of different colors. what is the probability that she will wear blue or red shirt?


Answer:

Rose has a total of 15 shirts in her closet. Out of these, 5 are blue and 4 are different shades of red. Therefore, the number of shirts that are either blue or red is:

Number of blue shirts + Number of red shirts = 5 + 4 = 9

The probability of Rose wearing a blue or red shirt is the ratio of the number of favorable outcomes (blue or red shirts) to the total number of possible outcomes (all shirts in her closet):

P(blue or red) = Number of blue or red shirts / Total number of shirts

P(blue or red) = 9 / 15

P(blue or red) = 0.6 or 60%

Therefore, the probability of Rose wearing a blue or red shirt is 0.6 or 60%.
