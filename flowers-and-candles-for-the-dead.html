30. Do all flowers have to be "dead headed" to encourage new blooms?i will make u brainliest if you answer the right thing not the nonsense ​


Answer:

The simple answer is no

Explanation:

You plant flowering shrubs in order to see those lovely blossoms open. In time, the blossoms fade and die. In many cases, you help the plant to produce more flowers by trimming off dead and wilted blossoms. This is called deadheading. Deadheading is a simple enough procedure. You simply pinch or snip off the wilting flower’s stem, making the cut just above the next leaf nodes. This allows the plant to invest its energy in producing more flowers rather than helping seeds mature. Many plants flower better when you deadhead faded blossoms.
