The centripetal force of a 15,190-N car accelerating at 3.00 m/s2 is 145,660 N. This is calculated by F = m x a/r, where m is the mass of the car (15,190 N), a is the acceleration (3.00 m/s2), and r is the radius of the circle (9.8 m/s2). Therefore, F = 15,190 x 3.00/9.8 = 145,660 N.

6. A 2000 lb elevator is lifted upward with an acceleration of 4 ft/s2. What is the tension in the supporting cable?


Answer:

The tension in the supporting cable is 2248.45 lbs

Explanation:

Tension is a pulling force to move an object. Its unit is in Newtons (N) or pounds (lbs).

(Note: a free body diagram was illustrated below)

Applying Newton's Law of Motion to the problem, we use the formula

a. ∑Fy = ma

b. W = mg

where

Fy      is the sum of all vertical forces, unit is in Newtons (N) or

         pounds (lbs)

         Remember: Upward direction = positive

                             Downward direction = negative

m       is the mass of the object, unit is in kg

a        is the acceleration of the object, unit is in ft/[tex]s^2[/tex]

W       is the weight force, unit is in Newtons or pounds

g        is the acceleration due to gravity 32.2 ft/ [tex]s^2[/tex]

Given information

W = 2000 lbs

a = 4 ft/[tex]s^2[/tex]

T = ?

Solving the problem

1. Use the weight force formula to derive the formula for the mass

W = mg

m =  [tex]\frac{W}{g}[/tex]

2. Use Newton's law formula to solve for the tension

∑Fy = ma

Substitute the formula for m

∑Fy = [tex]\frac{W}{g} (a)[/tex]

T - W =  [tex]\frac{W}{g} (a)[/tex]

T = W +  [tex]\frac{W}{g} (a)[/tex]

T = 2000 +  [tex]\frac{2000}{32.2} (4)[/tex]

T = 2248.45 lbs

17. A car starts from rest and has a constant acceleration of 3 ft/s2 . what is the average velocity during the first 10s of motion


AVERAGE VELOCITY

Using the formula v = at, where a is acceleration and t is time, the car's velocity after 10 seconds of motion is v = 3 ft/s2 x 10 s = 30 ft/s. Since the car started from rest, the average velocity during the first 10 seconds is half of the final velocity, or 15 ft/s. Therefore, the average velocity during the first 10 seconds of motion is 15 ft/s.

THE AVERGAE VELOCITY DURING FIRST 10S OF MOTION

To solve this problem, we can use the formula v = at, where v is velocity, a is acceleration, and t is time. The problem tells us that the car has a constant acceleration of 3 ft/s^2 and starts from rest. We want to find the average velocity during the first 10 seconds of motion.

First, we can use v = at to find the car's velocity after 10 seconds of motion. Plugging in the given values, we get:

v = 3 ft/s^2 x 10 s = 30 ft/s

This means that after 10 seconds, the car's velocity is 30 ft/s.

Next, we need to find the average velocity during the first 10 seconds of motion. Since the car started from rest, its initial velocity was zero. The average velocity during the first 10 seconds is the average of the initial velocity (zero) and the final velocity (30 ft/s). This is given by:

average velocity = (initial velocity + final velocity) / 2

= (0 + 30 ft/s) / 2

= 15 ft/s

Therefore, the average velocity during the first 10 seconds of motion is 15 ft/s.

22. The weight of a body is 100 lb. Determine. the rate of acceleration [in both feet per second per second (ft/s2) and meters per second per second (m/s2)] if a net force of 50lb is applied to the body.​


Answer:

The acceleration of the body can be calculated using Newton's Second Law of Motion, which states that the acceleration of an object is directly proportional to the net force acting on it and inversely proportional to its mass.

The formula for calculating acceleration is:

a = F_net / m

where a = acceleration F_net = net force acting on the body m = mass of the body

In this case, the weight of the body is given as 100 lb. We can convert this to mass using the formula:

m = W / g

where W = weight of the body g = acceleration due to gravity (32.2 ft/s^2 or 9.81 m/s^2)

For the given weight of 100 lb, the mass can be calculated as:

m = 100 lb / 32.2 ft/s^2 = 3.11 slugs (where "slug" is the unit of mass in the English system)

Now we can calculate the acceleration using the given net force of 50 lb:

a = F_net / m a = 50 lb / 3.11 slugs a ≈ 16.08 ft/s^2

Therefore, the rate of acceleration in feet per second per second (ft/s^2) is approximately 16.08 ft/s^2.

To convert this to meters per second per second (m/s^2), we can use the conversion factor 1 ft/s^2 = 0.3048 m/s^2:

a = 16.08 ft/s^2 × 0.3048 m/s^2/ft/s^2 a ≈ 4.90 m/s^2

Therefore, the rate of acceleration in meters per second per second (m/s^2) is approximately 4.90 m/s^2.

We can use Newton's Second Law of Motion (F = ma) to calculate the acceleration (a) of an object given its net force (F) and mass (m). First, we convert the weight of the object into mass, which we can do by dividing the weight by the acceleration due to gravity. Then, we calculate the acceleration using the formula a = F/m. In this case, if a body with a weight of 100 lb experiences a net force of 50 lb, the rate of acceleration is approximately 16.08 ft/s2 (or 7.29 m/s2 using SI units).
