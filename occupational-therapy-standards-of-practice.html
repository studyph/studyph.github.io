Answer:

Occupational standard protocols are important to practice in the workplace for several reasons:

Safety: These protocols are designed to ensure the safety of employees and employers. By following these protocols, you can reduce the risk of accidents and injuries in the workplace.

Consistency: Occupational standards help to create consistency in the workplace. By having established protocols and procedures, everyone knows what is expected of them and how to perform their job responsibilities.

Compliance: In some industries, there are legal requirements that mandate specific occupational standard protocols. Failing to follow these protocols can result in legal and financial consequences for the employer.

Quality: Occupational standard protocols are often developed based on best practices in the industry. By following these protocols, you can help to ensure that the work being performed is of a high quality.

Overall, practicing occupational standard protocols in the workplace is important for creating a safe and consistent work environment, maintaining compliance with legal requirements, and producing high-quality work.

9. why is the importance of occupational safety and hazard standards​


Answer:

Occupational safety and hazard standards are essential to provide a safe work environment and prevent accidents, injuries, and illnesses. They ensure that employers are taking the necessary steps to protect their employees from potential hazards and risks on the job. The standards set out requirements for the safe design, construction, and use of equipment, tools, workstations, and materials. They also provide guidance to employers on how to identify, assess, and control risks in the workplace. By following the standards, employers can create a safe working environment and promote a culture of safety throughout the organization. This can lead to improved employee morale, productivity, and reduced liability costs. Additionally, occupational safety and hazard standards are essential in providing a safe work environment that is compliant with relevant laws and regulations. This helps employers to avoid costly penalties and potential legal action.

RULE 1070

OCCUPATIONAL HEALTH AND ENVIRONMENTAL CONTROL

1071: General Provisions

(1) This rule establishes threshold limit values for toxic and carcinogenic substances and physical agents, which may be present in the atmosphere of the work environment. Threshold

Limit Values refer to airborne concentration of substances and represent conditions under

which it is believed that nearly all workers may be repeatedly exposed daily without adverse

 effect.

(2)   The Regional Office, on the advice of the Director, may issue a special rule establishing thresh-

 hold limit values for toxic substances not found in the table and such rule shall remain in effect

until a permanent standard is issued by the Secretary.

(3) The Secretary shall periodically review or update the Standards on threshold limit values, per-

missible noise exposure levels, illumination levels, human carcinogens, temperature and

and humidity and other technical standards upon recommendation of a technical committee

 in the Bureau of Working Conditions. The member of the technical committee shall either

be a physician, engineer, chemist or nurse who has completed at least an occupational health/

occupational safety and health-training course required by this Standards, and who has been

an occupational health/occupational safety and health practitioner for not less than three

(3) years. Other members of the technical committee shall be drawn from the labor and employers’ sectors. The technical committee shall be convened by the Director of the Bureau

 of Working Conditions as the need for review of the abovementioned technical standards

 arises. The Standards formulated by the technical committee shall become effective upon

announcement by the Secretary of Labor and Employment.
