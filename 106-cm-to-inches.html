Given:

One side of a triangle is five times another side.

The third side of the triangle is 16 inches.

The perimeter of the triangle is 106 inches.

Required:

The lengths of the other two sides of the triangle.

Equation:

Let x be the length of the shorter side.

Then, the longer side is 5x.

Using the formula for the perimeter of a triangle:

Perimeter = sum of all sides

106 = x + 5x + 16

Solution:

Simplifying the equation:

106 = 6x + 16

90 = 6x

x = 15

Therefore, the shorter side is 15 inches and the longer side is 5 times that, or 75 inches.
