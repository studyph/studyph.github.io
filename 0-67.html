23. What is the center and the radius of the circle with a general equation: 4x^2 + 4y^2 - 16x + 40y + 67 = 0? ​


Step-by-step explanation:

Remember if we have an equation of a circle in a form, (x -h)^2 +(y -k)^2 = r^2, then the center of the circle is (h,k) with a radius r how ever the given equation is in general so we'll need to rewrite the equation in a form, (x -h)^2 +(y -k)^2 = r^2.

[tex]4 {x}^{2} + 4 {y}^{2} - 16x + 40y + 67 = 0 \\ 4 {x}^{2} - 16x + 4 {y}^{2} + 40y = - 67 \\ 4( {x}^{2} - 4x) + 4( {y}^{2} + 10y) = - 67 \\ 4( {x}^{2} - 4x + 4) + 4(y^2 + 10y + 25) = - 67 + 16 + 100 \\ 4 {(x - 2)}^{2} + 4{(y + 5)}^{2} = 49 \\ {(x - 2)}^{2} + {(y + 5)}^{2} = \frac{49}{4} [/tex]

Answer:

The center of the circle is (2,-5) with radius 7/2.

If you are confused why the center is (2,-5) and not (-2,5) and the radius is 7/2 and not 49/4, Try pluging both centers and radii in this form of equation, (x -h)^2 +(y -k)^2 = r^2, and see which equation matches the equation (x -2)^2 +(y +5)^2 = 49/4

center(h,k) with radius r:

[tex](x -h)^2 +(y -k)^2 = r^2[/tex]

center(2,-5) with radius 7/2:

[tex](x -2)^2 +(y -(-5))^2 = (\frac{7}{2})^2 \\ (x -2)^2 +(y +5)^2 = \frac{49}{4}[/tex]

center(-2,5) with radius 49/4:

[tex](x -(-2))^2 +(y -5)^2 = (\frac{49}{4})^2 \\ (x +2)^2 +(y -5)^2 = \frac{2401}{16}[/tex]
