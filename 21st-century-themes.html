9. The Masque of the red death by Edgar Allan Poe What is the 21st century theme and why?​


Answer:

The 21st century theme of The Masque of the Red Death is that of inequality and social injustice. This is seen in the story when Prince Prospero attempts to insulate himself from the plague that is ravaging the land by throwing a lavish masquerade ball in his castle, attended by only the wealthy and privileged. This theme resonates in the 21st century as there is still a large amount of social and economic inequality present in the world, with some people having access to resources and opportunities that others do not.

The 'Red Death' had long devastated the country. No pestilence had ever been so fatal, or so hideous.''

This quote is from ''The Masque of the Red Death'' by Edgar Allen Poe, which is a story about how some wealthy folks thought they could cheat the spread of a horrible disease, and therefore, death.

In the story, Prince Prospero invites 1,000 of his closest friends to stay with him in his castle while the terrible disease runs rampant across his country. After about six months, the prince decides to throw an unusual masked ball to entertain them.

However, the party, which takes place in a suite of seven color-themed rooms, doesn't go as planned. A strange and horrifying masked figure with the face of a corpse suddenly appears at midnight and starts to stalk around the rooms. The prince confronts the figure, but when he gets close to it, Prospero abruptly falls to the floor and dies. All of the other guests in the castle face the same fate when they come in contact with the figure, who Poe reveals as the Red Death.

24. what the themes and motifs of american colonization in 21st century literature of the Philippines and the world?​


Answer:

Philippine literary production during the American Period in the Philippines was spurred by two significant developments in education and culture. One is the introduction of free public instruction for all children of school age and two, the use of English as medium of instruction in all levels of education in public schools.

Free public education made knowledge and information accessible to a greater number of Filipinos. Those who availed of this education through college were able to improve their social status and joined a good number of educated masses who became part of the country’s middle class.

The use of English as medium of instruction introduced Filipinos to Anglo-American modes of thought, culture and life ways that would be embedded not only in the literature produced but also in the psyche of the country’s educated class. It was this educated class that would be the wellspring of a vibrant Philippine Literature in English.

27. how do women writers and reach the themes and subjects of 21st century literature​


Explanation:

Ideas and notions concerning gender have become some of the most important literary themes of the early 21st century. Female and male authors have enthusiastically thrown themselves into a bona fide discovery of the possibilities offered by social gender and biological sex in terms of new ways of life, self-esteem, self-knowledge, and insights in a global world. Gender themes are employed in investigations of ideas and notions concerning the human condition, grieving, loss, attachments to the past, desire, and madness.
