19. A 10-lb object is suspended by a string from an overhead support.? A horizontal force of 5.8 pounds is applied on the object. The measure of the angle which the string makes with the horizontal is???


Answer:

φ=60°

Step by step explanation:

Before the horizontal force F is applied:

The gravitational force keeps the string stretched. The string reacts to the gravitational force W by pulling the object upwards with the same amount of force : [tex]T=W[/tex]

After the horizontal force is applied:

The string tilts to one side while the tension T of the string changes direction and finally stops moving when it reaches a static equilibrium with F.

This statement can be written in algebraic form:

Vertically:

∑[tex]F_{y}=0[/tex] ⇔[tex]T_{y}-W=0[/tex] ⇔Tsin(φ) =mgC ⇔ T=mgC/sin(φ)

where C=1/32.17, for the imperial system of units to be coherent.

Horizontally:

∑[tex]F_{x}=0[/tex] ⇔ [tex]F-T_{x}=0[/tex] ⇔ F=Tcos(φ)

This is a system of equations, we will solve it by eliminating T.

Substitute T from the previous equation:

F=mgC cos(φ)/sin(φ) ⇔ sin(φ)/cos(φ)=mgC/F ⇔

tan(φ)=[tex]\frac{10 *32.17*\frac{1}{32.17} }{5.8} =\frac{10}{5.8} =1.72[/tex]

⇔ φ≅60°

In the attached picture you can see the tension T being split into its components on the horizontal and the vertical axis.

Problem: What is the PE of 250 kg object suspended 5m above earth's surface?

Given values:

Mass = 250 kgHeight = 5 mGravity = 9.8 m/s²

[tex]\bold{Formula : } \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \qquad \\ \boxed{ \sf \: PE = mgh \: }[/tex]

Substitute the given values into the above formula and solve for PE:

[tex]\sf:\implies{PE = mgh}[/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies{PE = (250 \: kg)(9.8 \:m/s^2)(5 \: m)}[/tex]

[tex]\sf:\implies{PE = 12, 250 \: J}\\[/tex]

28. A spring (k = 200 N/m) is suspended with its upper endsupported from a ceiling. With the spring hanging in itsequilibrium configuration. an object (mass = 2.0 kg) is attachedto the lower end and released from rest. What is the speed of theobject after it has fallen 4.0 cm?​


Answer:

The speed of the object is 0.57 m/s.

Explanation:

Formula to be use

1. Hooke's law formula for spring

F = kx                    equation 1  

where

F     is the force, unit is in Newtons (N)

k     is the force or spring constant, unit is in Newton per meter (N/m)

x     is the distance of elongation or compression of the spring

2. 2nd Newton's law of motion

   F = ma                 equation 2

where

F     is the force

m    is the mass, unit is in kg

a     is the acceleration, unit is in m/[tex]s^2[/tex]

3. Constant acceleration formula

   [tex]v_{2} ^2=v_{1} ^2+2ax[/tex]       equation 3

where

[tex]v_{2}[/tex]     is the final velocity, unit is in m/s

[tex]v_{1}[/tex]     is the initial velocity, unit is in m/s

[tex]a[/tex]      is the acceleration

[tex]x[/tex]      is the distance traveled when an object was attached at

       other end of the spring or the elongation of the spring        

For the given information

k = 200 N/m

m = 2.0 kg

x = 4.0 cm

Solving the problem

1. Convert 4 cm ⇒ meter

x = 4.0 cm x [tex]\frac{1m}{100cm}[/tex] = 0.04 m

2. Use Hooke's law formula to solve for force

F = kx

F = (200 N/m)(0.04 m)

F = 8 N

3. Use the formula for 2nd Newton's law of motion and constant acceleration equation

a. Using equation 2, solve for the acceleration

   F = ma

Substitute the given information

  8 N = (2kg) a

   a = [tex]\frac{8N}{2kg}[/tex]

   a = 4 m/[tex]s^2[/tex]

b. Using equation 3, solve for the speed of the object

   [tex]v_{2} ^2=v_{1} ^2+2ax[/tex]

Substitute the given information

[tex]v_{2} ^2=0+2(4 m/s^2)(0.04m)[/tex]      [tex]v_{1}[/tex] (initial speed) is zero because the

                                             object was released from rest,

                                             meaning zero (0) to a

                                             its final velocity, [tex]v_{2}[/tex] (which we are

                                             looking for)                                          

[tex]v_{2} ^2=0.32[/tex]

[tex]v_{2} =\sqrt{0.32} =0.57 m/s[/tex]

Therefore, the speed of the 2 kg object hanged from one end of a spring is 0.57 m/s.
