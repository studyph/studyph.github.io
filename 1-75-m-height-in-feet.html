Find the area of a trapezoid with bases of 9 centimeters and 7 centimeters, and a height of 3 centimeters. 5. The area of a trapezoid is 75 square inches and the bases are 17 inches and 33 inches. Find the height. 6. Find the area of a parallelogram with a base of 7 inches and a height of 10 inches. 7. The area of a parallelogram is 24 square centimeters and the base is 6 centimeters. Find the height.


Answer:

NUMBER 1

Area of a Rectangle = length x width

Conversion: 1 ft = 12 inches

Classroom: L=20ft W=30ft

Area (classroom) = 20ft x30ft = 600 sq. ft. (7,200 sq. inches)

Floor Tile: L= 24 inches W=36 inches | Convert to ft. : L= 2ft W= 3ft

Area (Tile) : 2ft (24 inches) x 3ft (36 inches) = 6ft (72 inches)

Solution for how many tiles needed:

Area of Classroom (Floor area) ÷ Area of each Floor tile

= 600 sq ft ÷ 6 ft = 100 tiles (total number of tiles)

100 tiles x 6 ft (area per tile ) = 600 ft (total floor area)

Hence, per tile is 6 ft and the total floor area is 600 sq ft, therefore, 100 tiles are needed.

NUMBER 2

Area of a room =  24 sq ft. | LxW = 6ft x 4ft = 24 sq. ft.

Therefore, the dimensions of the room is L= 6ft. x W= 4ft.

NUMBER 3

Length of each side (square) = 275m

Total area = 275m x 275 m = 75625 sq. m

Cost of leveling the field = 5 pesos

Solution: 75625 sq. m x  5 pesos = 378125 pesos per sq m

NUMBER 4

Area of trapezoid = (b1 + b2) x h ÷ 2

b1 = 7 cm  b2 = 9 cm  h= 3 cm

Solution: A = (7+9) 3 ÷ 2 = 24 sq m.

NUMBER 5

Area of trapezoid = (b1 + b2) x h ÷ 2

b1 = 17 inches  b2 = 33 inches A= 75 sq inches  h = ?

Solution: h= 75/(17+33) x 2 = 3 inches

Answer: H= 3 inches

NUMBER 6

Area of parallelogram = base x height

Given: b= 7 inches  h= 10 inches

Solution: A= 7 inches x 10 inches = 70 sq inches  

NUMBER 7

Area of parallelogram = base x height

Given: b = 6 cm   A= 24 sq cm

Solution: 24 cm ÷ 6 cm = 4 cm (height)

Checking: 6 cm x 4 cm = 24 sq cm | Therefore, the missing height is 4 cm
