A rabbit's ears contain an extensive network of blood vessels that provide a large surface area for heat exchange. These vessels swell (vasodilation) when the rabbit is hot, and contract (vasoconstriction) when it is cool, so much so that they are barely visible in cold weather.

10. There are two buses. The Rabbit heads north on the expressway at 45 kph. Exactly 12 minutes after, the Panther follow at a steady speed of 54 kph. How long does it take the Panther to overtake the Rabbit?​


Answer:

First, let's convert the time that the Rabbit has been traveling into hours.

12 minutes = 12/60 = 0.2 hours

So, when the Panther starts, the Rabbit has already traveled for 0.2 hours at 45 kph, covering a distance of:

distance = speed x time

distance = 45 kph x 0.2 hours

distance = 9 km

Now, let's denote the time it takes for the Panther to catch up to the Rabbit as t. During this time, the Rabbit will have traveled for an additional t hours, covering a distance of:

distance = speed x time

distance = 45 kph x (0.2 + t) hours

distance = 9 + 45t km

At the same time, the Panther will be traveling at 54 kph for t hours, covering a distance of:

distance = speed x time

distance = 54 kph x t hours

distance = 54t km

When the Panther catches up to the Rabbit, the two buses will have traveled the same distance. So we can set the two expressions for distance equal to each other and solve for t:

9 + 45t = 54t

Subtracting 45t from both sides gives:

9 = 9t

Dividing both sides by 9 gives:

t = 1 hour

Therefore, it will take the Panther 1 hour to catch up to the Rabbit.

25. 1) ln rabbits black coat (B) is dominant over brown (b)and straight hair (H) is dominant to curly (h). Cross 2hybrid rabbits and give the phenotypic ratio for thefirst possible generation of offepring.​


Answer:

9:3:3:1 for black coat straight hair : black coat curly hair : brown coat straight hair : brown coat curly hair.

Explanation:

A hybrid rabbit would have a genotype of BbHh. Crossing 2 hybrids together would mean solving for the the first generation offspring of BbHh x BbHh. To illustrate this we need to make a 4x4 Punnett Square in which all possible gene combinations for each parent is listed against a row or column. To list these down, remember FOIL, wherein for 2 pairs of alleles (Bb x Hh), we simply combine the Firsts (BH), the Outers (Bh), the Inners (bH), and the Lasts (bh). Do this for both parents and list them down, each parent on a row or column. Then, for each of the 16 cells, we fill out the result of crossing the corresponding row with the column. We thus have a Punnett Square that looks like this:

           BH       Bh        bH         bh

BH    | BBHH  BBHh   BbHH   BbHh

Bh     | BBHh   BBhh   BbHh    Bbhh

bH   |  BbHH   BbHh  bbHH   bbHh

bh     |  BbHh   Bbhh   bbHh    bbhh

We can now count the number of rabbits for each phenotype.

Black coat straight hair (BBHH, BbHH, BBHh, BbHh) = 9

Black coat curly hair (BBhh, Bbhh) = 3

Brown coat straight hair (bbHH, bbHh) = 3

Brown coat curly hair (bbhh) = 1

Our phenotypic ratio therefore is 9:3:3:1 for black coat straight hair: black coat curly hair: brown coat straight hair: brown coat curly hair.
