20. What are the difference between mental Attributes and Functional Attributes?​


Answer:

Mental attributes and functional attributes are two different types of characteristics used to describe products, services, or experiences.

Mental attributes are intangible qualities that are associated with how a product, service, or experience makes someone feel. These attributes are related to psychological or emotional factors, such as enjoyment, pleasure, excitement, or satisfaction. For example, a mental attribute of a high-end luxury car might be the feeling of prestige or status that comes with owning and driving the car.

Functional attributes, on the other hand, are tangible characteristics that are associated with the physical features and capabilities of a product, service, or experience. These attributes are related to the practical or utilitarian aspects, such as performance, durability, speed, or ease of use. For example, a functional attribute of a high-end luxury car might be its advanced safety features or its powerful engine.

In summary, mental attributes are related to how something makes a person feel, while functional attributes are related to the practical or physical aspects of the thing itself. Both types of attributes can be important in influencing people's perceptions and preferences, and businesses often use a combination of mental and functional attributes to market and differentiate their products and services.
