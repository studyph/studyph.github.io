21. What are the characteristics of a Linear model of communication?Select one:a. Persuasion not mutual understanding, psychological effect over social effects, complex, unidirectionalb. simple, multidirectional, psychological effect over social effects, persuasion not mutual understandingc. simple, unidirectional, psychological effect over social effects, mutual understanding not persuadingd. unilinear, simple, persuasion is not mutual, social effects over psychological effects


The characteristics of a Linear model of communication is :

c. simple, unidirectional, psychological effect over social effects, mutual understanding not persuaded.

Explanation:

Linear communication is a process of delivering messages from a communicator to the communicant directly or indirectly using various communication media. Where this communication only occurs in one direction without reciprocity of the message conveyed by the communicator. Linear communication pattern, namely one-way view of communication. Where the communicator provides a stimulus and the communicant gives the expected response or response, without holding selection and interpretation.

As an example of this linear communication we often encounter in daily activities, for example, communication on television, radio and various writings in the mass media. All of these things are linear aka one-way communication, because we as communicants do not give reciprocity. The linear communication model is a very simple communication model. This model describes communication that takes place in one direction.

Message flow is described as direct from the sender of the message to the recipient of the message, the communicator to the communicant. In the linear communication model, there is no concept of feedback. The recipient of the message is passive in receiving the message.

Linear communication models include:

• Aristotle's Model of Communication,

• Lasswell Communication Model,

• Berlo SMCR Communication Model,

• Shannon and Weaver's Communication Model.
