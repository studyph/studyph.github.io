16. A CLOSED CONICAL INVERTED TANK HAS A BASE DIAMETER OF 3M AND AN ALTITUDE OF 4M, IT IS FILLED WITH WATER TO A DEPTH OF 1.3m a. compute the volume of water b. if the tank is upright, its base is lying on the ground, compute the new depth of water. c. compute the lateral surface area not occupied by water when in upright position


[tex]\mathbb{FORMULA:}[/tex]

Volume of a cone:

[tex]\mathsf{V=\dfrac{1}{3}\pi r^2h}[/tex]

Lateral surface area of a cone:

[tex]\mathsf{LSA=\pi r \sqrt{h^2+r^2}}[/tex]

where:

V = volume of the cone

LSA = lateral surface area of the cone

r = radius of the base

h = height of the cone

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

Refer to the figure

[tex]\\[/tex]

a.) Volume of water (cone is inverted):

From the figure, the volume of water is:

[tex]\mathsf{V=\dfrac{1}{3}\pi x^2 (1.3)}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

To determine x (radius of the cone at the depth 1.3 m), we can use similar triangles.

Using similar triangles (ratio and proportion)

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{x}{1.3}=\dfrac{1.5}{4}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{x=\dfrac{1.5}{4}(1.3)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{x=\dfrac{39}{40}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Substitute the value of x,

[tex]\mathsf{V=\dfrac{1}{3}\pi \left(\dfrac{39}{80}\right)^2(1.3)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{V=\dfrac{6591\pi}{64000} \ m^3}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathcal{ANOTHER \ SOLUTION:}[/tex]

Using similar solids:

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{V_{water}}{V_{cone}}=\left(\dfrac{h_{water}}{h_{cone}}\right)^3}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{V_{water}}{\dfrac{1}{3}\pi(1.5)^2(4)}=\left(\dfrac{1.5}{4}\right)^3}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{V_{water}}{3\pi}=\dfrac{2197}{64000}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{V_{water}=\dfrac{3\pi(2197)}{64000}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{V_{water}=\dfrac{6591\pi}{64000} \ m^3}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

b.) Depth of water (cone is upright):

From the figure, the height of water when the cone is upright is:

[tex]\mathsf{h=4-y}[/tex]

When the cone upright, the volume of water will remain the same.

From the figure, the volume of water is equal to the volume of the cone minus the volume of air. (Note: You can also use volume of a frustum of a cone).

[tex]\mathsf{V_{water}=V_{cone}-V_{air}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{V_{water}=\dfrac{6591\pi}{64000}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{V_{cone} = 3\pi}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

From the figure, the volume of air is:

[tex]\mathsf{V_{air}=\dfrac{1}{3}\pi x^2y}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Since we want to determine the depth of water, we need to determine the value of x in terms of y (height of air). To do that, We can use similar triangles.

Using similar triangles:

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{x}{y}=\dfrac{1.5}{4}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{x=\dfrac{3}{8}y}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Substitute the value of x,

[tex]\mathsf{V_{air}=\dfrac{1}{3}\pi \left(\dfrac{3}{8}y\right)^2(y)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{V_{air}=\dfrac{3\pi}{64}y^3}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Substitute the volume of air,

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{6591\pi}{64000}=3\pi-\dfrac{3\pi}{64}y^3}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{3}{64}y^3=3-\dfrac{6591}{64000}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{3}{64}y^3=\dfrac{185409}{64000}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y^3=\dfrac{185409(64)}{3(64000)}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y=\sqrt[3]{\dfrac{185409(64)}{3(64000)}}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y=3.9537 \ m}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Substitute y,

[tex]\mathsf{h=4-3.9537}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{h=0.0463 \ m}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathcal{ANOTHER \ SOLUTION:}[/tex]

Using similar solids:

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{V_{air}}{V_{cone}}=\left(\dfrac{h_{air}}{h_{cone}}\right)^3}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{3\pi-\dfrac{6591\pi}{64000}}{3\pi}=\dfrac{y^3}{4^3}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{61803}{64000}=\dfrac{y^3}{64}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y=\sqrt[3]{\dfrac{64(61803)}{64000}}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{y=3.9537 \ m}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{h=4-3.9537}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{h=0.0463 \ m}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

c. lateral surface area of air (cone is upright):

From the figure, the lateral surface area of the air is:

[tex]\mathsf{LSA_{air}=\pi x \sqrt{x^2+y^2}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

From b, y = 3.9537 and x = (3/8)y

[tex]\mathsf{x=\dfrac{3}{8}y}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{x=\dfrac{3}{8}(3.9537)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{x=1.4826 \ m}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Substitute x,

[tex]\mathsf{LSA_{air}=\pi (1.4826)\sqrt{(1.4826)^2+(3.9537)^2}}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{LSA_{air}=19.6674 \ m^2}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathbb{ANSWER:}[/tex]

a.) Volume of water (cone is inverted):

[tex]\boxed{\mathsf{V=\dfrac{6591 \pi}{64000} \ \approx \ 0.3235 \ m^3}}[/tex]

b. Height of water (cone is upright):

[tex]\boxed{\mathsf{h=0.0463 \ m}}[/tex]

c. Lateral surface area of air (cone is upright):

[tex]\boxed{\mathsf{LSA_{air}=19.6674 \ m^2}}[/tex]
