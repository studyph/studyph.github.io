7. Balance the following chemical equationsa. Al + O2 → Al2O3b. KCIO3 → KCI + O2C. H3PO4 + Mg(OH)2 → Mg3(PO4)2 + H2O​


Answer:

4 Al + 3 O2 → 2 Al2O3 2 KCIO3 → 2 KCI + 3 O2 2 H3PO4 + 3 Mg(OH)2 → Mg3(PO4)2 + 6 H2O

Explanation:

I cannot explain all of these, however. Instead, I will be giving some tips when balancing chemical equations. Consider the following reaction:

Al + O2 → Al2O3

Focus first on the compound and find those atoms that have similarities in each side. Also, recall the Least Common Multiple you have learnt in Math. Solving, you can see that there is an oxygen atom on each side, so as the aluminium atom. You can use trial and error method:

Al2O3 becomes 2 Al2O3.

Hence, there are 6 oxygen atoms. To balance the equation, you must have the same number of moles of reactants and products. That is,

O2 becomes 3 O2

Thus, there are 6 oxygen atoms on the reactant side. Same method will apply on the aluminium atom. Reminder: Don't use fractions when balancing equations.

15. What percent composition of phosphorus is in Zn3(PO4)2?​


The percent composition of phosphorus in Zn₃(PO₄)₂ : 16.04%

Further explanation  

Proust states the Comparative Law that compounds are formed from elements with the same Mass Comparison so that compounds have a constant composition of elements  

The empirical formula is the mole ratio of compounds forming elements  

In the same compound, although from different sources and formed by different processes, it will still have the same composition/comparison  

[tex]\tt \%~A~in~A_xB_y=\dfrac{x.Ar~A}{MW~A_xB_y}\times 100\%[/tex]

Given

Compound Zn₃(PO₄)₂

Required

The percent composition of phosphorus

Solution

Molar mass Zn₃(PO₄)₂ = 386.1 g/mol

Molar mass P = 30.97 g/mol

There are 2 P atoms in the Zn₃(PO₄)₂ compound, so :

%P in Zn₃(PO₄)₂ :

= (2 x 30,97)/ 386.1 g/mol x 100%

=  16.04%

30. Molar mass of Mg33(PO4) 2​


Answer:

To calculate the molar mass of Mg33(PO4)2, we need to add the atomic masses of each element present in the compound, multiplied by their respective subscripts.

Mg33(PO4)2 can be broken down into:

33 magnesium (Mg) atoms

2 phosphate (PO4) groups

To find the atomic masses of the elements, we can refer to the periodic table.

The atomic mass of Mg is 24.31 g/mol.

The atomic mass of P is 30.97 g/mol, and there are 2 P atoms in each phosphate group.

The atomic mass of O is 15.99 g/mol, and there are 8 O atoms in each phosphate group.

Now we can calculate the molar mass:

Mg33(PO4)2 = (33 x 24.31 g/mol) + (2 x (2 x 30.97 g/mol + 8 x 15.99 g/mol))

= 801.56 g/mol

Therefore, the molar mass of Mg33(PO4)2 is approximately 801.56 g/mol.
