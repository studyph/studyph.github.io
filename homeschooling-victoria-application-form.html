Answer:

"Manguna Ka" is it traditional filipino him that was originally composed in the early 1990s by a Filipino church musician named Fr. Francisco Santiago. The hymn was written in the tagalog language and is often sound in catholic churches during mass and other religious ceremonies.

The hymn "Manguna ka" also known by its full name title "Manguna ka, o Birheng Maria." It is a popular him that is widely recognized and beloved in the Philippines, often song during the month of May, which is dedicated to the Virgin Mary.

The music of "Manguna Ka" is in the form of a hymn, with a simple melody of a four-four time signature. The harmonies are mostly diatonic, or within the key, with occasional chromaticism. The hymn has a moderate tempo and is typically stung in a solemn and reverent manner.

Overall, "Manguna Ka" is a significant part of Filipino musical and religious heritage, representing the deep catholic faith and culture identity of the Filipino people. Its enduring popularity and continued use in religious services reflect its lasting impact on Filipino musical tradition.
