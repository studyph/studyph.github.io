9. A stone is dropped into a deep well,heard to hit the water 3.41s after being dropped.Determine the depth of the well


Assuming 340 m/s for the speed of sound (which varies with temperature), depth is 52 meters. 

The 3.41 seconds is the sum of two times. 

1. time for the rock to fall, and that is 
d = ½gt² or  
t = √(2d/g)  

2. time for the sound to return up the well, and that is 
t = d/340  

so the equation is 
√(2d/9.8) + d/340 = 3.41 
solve for d  
answer = 52 m

Explanation:

time (t) = 2.6 s

acceleration (a) (earth's grav. force) =  -9.8 m/s^2

initial velocity (i) = 0 m/s

d = ?

d = (i ⋅ t) + (0.5 ⋅ a ⋅ t)^2

d = (0 m/s) (2.6 s) + 0.5 (-9.8 m/s^2) (2.6 s)^2

d = 0 m/s + 0.5 (-9.8 m/s^2) (6.76 s^2)

d = -33.124m  (round off)

d = -33.1m

13. a stone is dropped into a deep well and is heard to hit the water 3.41s after being dropped determine the depth of the well​


Problem:

A stone is dropped into a deep well and is heard to hit the water 3.41s after being dropped determine the depth of the well​ .

Given Data:

a (acceleration g) = 9.8 m/s²

Vi (initial velocity) = 0 m/s  (drop from rest)

t (time)  =  3.41 s

d (distance) = unknown

Solution:

We can use the formula d = 1/2at² since Vi (initial velocity) is 0 m/s.

d = 1/2at²

   = 1/2 × 9.8 m/s² × (3.41 s)²

   = 4.9 m/s² × 11.6281 s²

   = 56.98 m

Answer:

The depth of the well is 56.98 m

The stones accelerates at 9.8 m/s². This means that in every second, the stones increases its velocity by 9.8 m/s. Acceleration due to gravity is constant in certain places like here on Earth. Gravity on the moon differ from Earth and influence free falling object differently.
