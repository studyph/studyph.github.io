6. Find suplementary angles if one angle is 50 degrees less than the other angle.


First angle: x
Angle's supplement: x -50
Sum of the measures of supplementary angles = 180°

Add:
x + x - 50 = 180
2x - 50 = 180
2x = 180 + 50
2x = 230
2x/2 = 230/2
x = 115 

First angle: x = 115°
Angle's supplement: x - 50 = 115 - 50 = 65°

The supplementary angles have measurements of 115° and 65°.

Check:
115° + 65° = 180°
180° = 180°

11. an angle is 50 degrees what is its complement


Answer:

y = 40°

Step-by-step explanation:

hi!

An angle and its complement add up to 90 degrees. So, we can form an equation with this information:

[tex]\sf{x+y=90}[/tex]

Either x or y will be given in the problem. NOTE that x and y are completely interchangeable, meaning that it doesn't matter which number you pick for x or y.

So let's find y, since we're given x.

[tex]\sf{50+y=90}[/tex]        {Subtract 50 from Both Sides}[tex]\sf{y=90-50}[/tex]        {Simplify}[tex]\sf{y=40^o}[/tex]

Hence, the answer is y = 40°.

Answer:

82 Degrees

The sum of the interior angles of a triangle is always 180 degrees.

Let x be the measure of the third angle.

We know that the sum of the measures of the two given angles is:

48 + 50 = 98 degrees

To find the measure of the third angle, we can use the formula for the sum of the interior angles of a triangle:

x + 48 + 50 = 180

Combining like terms, we get:

x + 98 = 180

Subtracting 98 from both sides, we get:

x = 82

Therefore, the measure of the third angle is 82 degrees.
