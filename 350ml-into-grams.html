14. The volume of a gas is increased from 150mL to 350mL by heating it if original temperature of the gas was 25°C, what will be it's final temperature?​


Answer:

Use gas equation :

V1/T1 = V2/T2 ( pressure is constant

V1 = 350.0 mL

V2 = 150 mL

T2 = 25.0°C = 298.15 K

substitute

350 mL / T1 = 150 mL / 298.15 K

T1 = 350 mL * 298.15 K / 150 mL

T1 = 695.68 K

T1 = 695.68 - 273.15 = 420 °C

20. What is the percent by volume of a solution that contains 350mL of isopropyl ( C3H8O) and 150 mL of water?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & V_{\text{solute}} = \text{350 mL} \\ & V_{\text{solvent}} = \text{150 mL} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the percent by volume.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \%(v/v) & = \frac{V_{\text{solute}}}{V_{\text{solute}} + V_{\text{solvent}}} \times 100\% \\ & = \frac{\text{350 mL}}{\text{350 mL + 150 mL}} \times 100\% \\ & = \boxed{70\%} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the percent by volume of a solution is 70%.

To calculate the final volume, we can use the formula:

Vf = V¹ + V

Given that Vf = 3500 ml and V¹ = 350 ml, we can solve for V:

V = Vf - V¹

V = 3500 ml - 350 ml

V = 3150 ml

So, the final volume is 3150 ml.
