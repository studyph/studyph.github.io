12. description of lack of facilities and teacher shortage in public schools​


There are a number of issues that can affect the quality of education in public schools, including a lack of facilities and a shortage of teachers.

Lack of facilities: Public schools may lack the necessary facilities to provide a high-quality education to students. This can include a lack of modern classrooms, science labs, and other educational resources. This can make it difficult for students to learn effectively, as they may not have access to the same resources as their peers in private schools or wealthier public schools.

Teacher shortage: Another issue that can affect the quality of education in public schools is a shortage of teachers. This can occur when there are not enough qualified teachers available to meet the needs of the students in a particular school or district. This can result in larger class sizes, which can make it difficult for teachers to give individual attention to students, and may also lead to a lack of specialized instruction in certain subjects.

Both of these issues can have negative impacts on student learning and achievement, and addressing them is an important part of improving the quality of education in public schools.
