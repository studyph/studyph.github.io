6. 2 paragraph critique using the aspects of feminism approach about action coalitio​


Explanation:

The modern day humanist movement has embraced feminism, but like various types of humanism, the tension between the various waves of feminism is ever present. Established feminist movements within the United States have primarily fallen into four different time periods. The different movements—often termed first wave, second wave, third wave, and fourth wave feminism—share similar goals but different characteristics of action.

These various waves of feminism are interwoven into women’s rights, civil rights, and social justice movements. What has especially characterized these movements is the championing of equality under the law and within our socioeconomic structures. That said, the differences and the existing tension among these various waves outlines an important question: What kind of equality does feminism seek? The waves of feminism are not a linear progression and consensus of progress, even though they roughly follow a linear timeline. Instead, they are intense changes of perspective among different generations of women.

13. best explanation about feminism ​


Answer:

Feminism is a social, political, and cultural movement that aims to achieve equality between men and women. It seeks to challenge and change the societal norms and cultural practices that have historically oppressed and discriminated against women.

Feminism is not about promoting women over men, but rather about promoting gender equality and dismantling the patriarchal structures that have given men more power and privilege in society. Feminism recognizes that gender inequality is not just a women's issue, but a societal issue that affects everyone.

Feminism has evolved over time and has different branches or schools of thought. Some of the common themes in feminist theory include:

1. The belief that gender is a social construct, rather than a biological fact.

2. The recognition of intersectionality, which acknowledges that gender intersects with other social identities such as race, class, sexuality, and ability.

3. The critique of patriarchy, which is a system of social, economic, and political power that privileges men over women.

4. The call for women's rights, including the right to vote, work, and have control over their own bodies.

5. The promotion of women's empowerment, which involves giving women the tools and resources to assert their own agency and make choices for themselves.

Overall, feminism is a movement that seeks to create a more just and equitable society for all genders. It challenges the status quo and works towards creating a world where everyone is treated with dignity and respect, regardless of their gender.


14. formalism aproach and feminism aproach about no racism


Formalism and feminism are two different approaches to analyzing social issues, including the issue of racism.

Formalism is an approach that emphasizes the importance of following established rules and procedures in order to maintain social order and stability. In the context of addressing racism, formalism would focus on enforcing anti-discrimination laws and ensuring that individuals and institutions adhere to established policies and procedures that prohibit discrimination based on race.

Feminism, on the other hand, is an approach that seeks to uncover and challenge the underlying power structures that perpetuate inequality and oppression, including racism. Feminist theory recognizes that race and gender are intersecting forms of oppression, and that racism affects women and girls in unique ways. Thus, a feminist approach to addressing racism would focus on understanding the ways in which racism intersects with other forms of oppression and working to dismantle the underlying power structures that perpetuate discrimination.

Both formalism and feminism can be useful in addressing issues of racism, as they offer different perspectives and strategies for addressing the problem. However, it is important to recognize that no single approach can fully address the complex and multifaceted issue of racism. A comprehensive approach that takes into account a variety of perspectives and strategies is likely to be most effective.

27. make a feminism essay about the story of why women wash dishes​


Answer:

The practice of women washing dishes can be traced back to the early 20th century when the concept of the "ideal" household was first popularized. According to this ideal, the man was the breadwinner and the woman was responsible for taking care of the home and family. This division of labor was based on the assumption that women were naturally suited to domestic work and that it was their primary role in society.

However, this ideal was not rooted in reality. In truth, women have always been an integral part of the workforce, including in industries such as agriculture, manufacturing, and service. The notion that women were only fit for domestic work was a product of social conditioning, reinforced by cultural norms and expectations.

As a result, women were expected to perform a variety of household chores, including cooking, cleaning, and laundry. But the most emblematic of these chores was washing dishes. This task was seen as the epitome of domesticity and was often used as a benchmark for measuring a woman's worth as a wife and mother.

Despite the fact that washing dishes is a task that can be performed by anyone regardless of gender, it was almost exclusively reserved for women. This gendered division of labor created a situation in which women were expected to perform domestic work regardless of their other responsibilities or ambitions.

This expectation had far-reaching consequences for women's lives. Women who wanted to pursue careers or other interests were often discouraged from doing so because it conflicted with their domestic duties. Women who did manage to balance both often faced discrimination and criticism from society for neglecting their "proper" role as a homemaker.

In recent years, there has been a growing awareness of the need to challenge traditional gender roles and expectations. The feminist movement has played a critical role in this shift, advocating for the equal distribution of domestic labor and challenging the idea that women's primary role is to take care of the home and family.

As a result of these efforts, there has been a gradual shift in attitudes towards domestic work. Men are now more likely to participate in household chores, including dishwashing. However, there is still a long way to go before we achieve true equality in the domestic sphere.

In conclusion, the story of why women wash dishes is a powerful illustration of how gender stereotypes and expectations can perpetuate inequality in the domestic sphere. It highlights the need to challenge traditional gender roles and to strive for a more equitable distribution of domestic labor. Only by doing so can we create a society that values the contributions of all its members, regardless of gender.
