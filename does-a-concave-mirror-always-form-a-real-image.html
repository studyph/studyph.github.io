29. how does the image formed in a plane mirror? is it concave or convex?​


Answer:

In a plane mirror, the image is formed by the reflection of light off the mirror's surface. A plane mirror is a flat mirror with a smooth, even surface that is perpendicular to the direction of the incident light. When light strikes the surface of a plane mirror, it is reflected back in a direction that is opposite to the incident direction, according to the law of reflection.

The image formed in a plane mirror is a virtual image, which means that it appears to be located behind the mirror. A virtual image is not a real, physical object, but rather an optical illusion that is created by the reflection of light.

A plane mirror does not change the shape of the image, so the image appears in the same shape as the object. For example, if the object is a square, the image will also be a square. If the object is a circle, the image will also be a circle.

A plane mirror is neither concave nor convex. It is a flat mirror with a uniform curvature of zero. Convex mirrors are curved outward, while concave mirrors are curved inward. Plane mirrors produce images that are the same size as the objects and are upright, while convex mirrors produce images that are smaller than the objects and are upright, and concave mirrors produce images that are larger than the objects and can be either upright or inverted.
