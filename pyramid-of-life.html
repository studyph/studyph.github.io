This pyramid illustrates the hierarchical organization of living things, starting with the largest and most inclusive category at the top (the biosphere) and progressing down to the smallest and most specific category at the bottom (cells).

The biosphere refers to the entire portion of Earth that is inhabited by living organisms. The ecosystem is a smaller, interconnected system within the biosphere, consisting of all the living and nonliving components that interact in a specific environment. A community is a group of different populations of organisms living and interacting within the same ecosystem. A population is a group of individuals of the same species living in the same area. An organism is a single living being, such as a plant or animal.

An organ system is a group of organs that work together to perform a specific function in an organism. Organs are structures within an organism that perform specific functions, such as the heart or liver. Tissues are groups of similar cells that work together to perform a specific function within an organ. Cells are the basic units of life, the smallest units of an organism that are capable of functioning independently.
