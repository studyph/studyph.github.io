Arthropod

animal phylum

WRITTEN BY

Robert D. Barnes

Professor of Biology, Gettysburg College, Pennsylvania, 1963–93. Author of Invertebrate Zoology.

Last Updated: Oct 20, 2020 See Article History

Alternative Title: Arthropoda

Arthropod, (phylum Arthropoda), any member of the phylum Arthropoda, the largest phylum in the animal kingdom, which includes such familiar forms as lobsters, crabs, spiders, mites, insects, centipedes, and millipedes. About 84 percent of all known species of animals are members of this phylum. Arthropods are represented in every habitat on Earth and show a great variety of adaptations. Several types live in aquatic environments, and others reside in terrestrial ones; some groups are even adapted for flight.

21. Phylum platyhelminthes scientific classification


ANSWER: Flatworms

The Phylum Platyhelminthes contains all the "flatworms."

These are invertebrates that are unsegmented, have bilateral symmetry, no body cavity (acoelomates), and one opening for both ingestion and waste removal. Examples include planarians, flukes, and tapeworms. Many members of this phylum are parasitic.
