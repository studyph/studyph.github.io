WHAT IS THE HOLY EUCHARIST?

Eucharist, also called Holy Communion or Lord’s Supper, in Christianity, ritual commemoration of Jesus’ Last Supper with his disciples. The Eucharist (from the Greek eucharistia for “thanksgiving”) is the central act of Christian worship and is practiced by most Christian churches in some form. Along with baptism it is one of the two sacraments most clearly found in the New Testament

The story of the institution of the Eucharist by Jesus on the night before his Crucifixion is reported in the Synoptic Gospels (Matthew 26:26–28; Mark 14:22–24; and Luke 22:17–20) and in the First Letter of Paul to the Corinthians (I Corinthians 11:23–25). According to the Gospel accounts, Jesus established the practice at the Last Supper, a traditional Passover seder, when he blessed the bread, which he said was his body, and shared it with his disciples. He then shared a cup of wine with his disciples and told them that “this is the blood of my covenant, which is poured out for many.” According to St. Luke, Jesus called on his followers to repeat the ceremony in his memory, and the letters of the Apostle Paul and the Acts of the Apostles in the New Testament demonstrate that early Christians believed that they were to continue the celebration as an anticipation in this life of the joys of the banquet that was to come in the kingdom of God.

23. Why is it important to acknowledge the importance of the Holy Eucharist?


Answer:

The Holy Eucharist is a sacrament of the Catholic Church, which is considered by Catholics to be the Body and Blood of Jesus Christ. It is an essential component of the Mass, which is the highest form of worship in the Catholic Church. Here are some reasons why acknowledging the importance of the Holy Eucharist is crucial for Catholics:

1. It is a source of grace: Catholics believe that receiving the Holy Eucharist grants them spiritual nourishment, strength, and grace. It helps them cultivate a closer relationship with Jesus Christ and live a more virtuous life.

2. It is a symbol of unity: The Holy Eucharist represents the body of Christ being shared among his followers. Catholics come together to participate in the Mass and share in the body and blood of Christ, which signifies their unity and oneness as a community.

3. It is a reminder of Christ's sacrifice: The Holy Eucharist is a memorial of Christ's sacrifice on the cross. By receiving the Holy Eucharist, Catholics are reminded of Christ's love and sacrifice, and they are called to live a life of service and sacrifice in their daily lives.

4. It is a celebration of the resurrection: Catholics believe that the Holy Eucharist represents the risen Christ. By participating in the celebration of the Holy Eucharist, Catholics remember and celebrate the resurrection of Christ and their hope for eternal life.

In summary, for Catholics, acknowledging the importance of the Holy Eucharist is essential because it provides spiritual nourishment, unites the community, reminds them of Christ's sacrifice, and celebrates the resurrection.
