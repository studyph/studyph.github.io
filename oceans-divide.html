4. An ocean trench occurs when ------- A. Oceanic crust subducts below continental crust B. Plates divide and magma rises up C. we dig a whole in the ocean D. when plates slide past each other


Answer:

Oceanic trenches are prominent long, narrow topographic depressions of the ocean floor. They are typically 50 to 100 kilometers (30 to 60 mi) wide and 3 to 4 km (1.9 to 2.5 mi) below the level of the surrounding oceanic floor, but can be thousands of kilometers in length. There are about 50,000 kilometers (31,000 mi) of oceanic trenches worldwide, mostly around the Pacific Ocean, but also in the eastern Indian Ocean and a few other locations. The greatest ocean depth measured is in the Challenger Deep of the Mariana Trench, at a depth of 11,034 m (36,201 ft) below sea level.

13. The open-ocean zone is further divided into which zones? Check all that apply.


The open-ocean zone is further divided into which zones? Check all that apply.

A series of zones separate the vast and complex  open ocean, each distinguished by varying physical and biological properties.  The open ocean ecosystem (or ocean zone) typically begins at the outer edge  of the continental shelf, ranging from the surface to the deepest ocean depths.  This region's water climate, collectively called the pelagic zone, is  further subdivided into the epipelagic, mesopelagic, bathypelagic, and benthopelagic regions.  Each of these zones is delineated by both the  depth and the relative sunlight penetration through the water. In the epipelagic zone (0–200 meters) (0-657 feet), penetrating sunlight enables relatively high  phytoplankton photosynthetic activity (generally microscopic algae), supplying other species with energy and food sources.  Therefore, this "lighted" area is also  protected by, and called the photic zone.  Deeper water organisms will rely primarily on energy sources produced  from the photographic zone above under the epipelagic (or photographic zone)
