3. Direction: Draw a circle with a diameter of 8 cm. Then, cut it and label its parts.​


I can provide you with instructions on how to draw a circle with a diameter of 8 cm and label its parts.

Instructions:

Using a ruler, draw a straight line that measures 8 cm. This will serve as the diameter of the circle.
Using a compass, place the pointed end on one end of the diameter line and the pencil end on the other end. Draw a circle around the diameter line.
Once the circle is drawn, use a pair of scissors to carefully cut out the circle along the edge.
Label the parts of the circle:
The diameter is the line that passes through the center of the circle and connects two points on the circumference.
The circumference is the outer boundary of the circle.
The radius is the distance from the center of the circle to any point on the circumference.
The center is the point in the middle of the circle where the diameter intersects.
The chord is any line segment that connects two points on the circumference.
