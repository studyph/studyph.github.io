23. what effect did the unity of the early christians have on the spread and growth of the church


Answer:

The unity of early Christians played a significant role in the spread and growth of the church. The early Christians were united in their beliefs and practices, which helped to create a sense of community and belonging among them. This sense of unity and shared purpose made it easier for them to evangelize and attract new members to the faith. Additionally, the early Christians were known for their acts of charity and community service, which further helped to attract new converts and build a positive reputation for the church. Overall, the unity of the early Christians helped to create a strong foundation for the continued growth and expansion of the Christian faith.
