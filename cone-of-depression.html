21. What is the meaning of depressions? and what are the types of depressions?


What is depression?

Depression - is marked especially by sadness, inactivity, difficulty in thinking and concentration, a significant increase or decrease in appetite and time spent sleeping, feelings of dejection and hopelessness, and sometimes suicidal tendencies.

○ Different Types of Depression ○

▪ Major depression

— it has severe symptoms lasting six months or more and has a profound effect on the patient's life.

▪ Bipolar disorder

— it's also known as manic depression . Those with this disorder may experience intense joy and deep sadness.

▪ Dysthymia

— although not as severe as major depression , has depressive symptoms that make it difficult for the patient to move normally. Others may also experience intense grief.

▪ Postpartum depression

— it is a debilitating mood disorder that affects many postpartum mothers.

▪ Seasonal affective disorder or SAD

— is more likely to occur due to lack of sunlight during the fall and winter. It usually disappears in spring and summer.

27. Types of volcanoes DescriptionShield coneCinder coneComposite Cone/Stratovolcanoes​


Answer:

SHIELD CONE

Flow after flow pours out in all directions from a central summit vent, or group of vents, building a broad, gently sloping cone of flat, domical shape, with a profile much like that of a warrior's shield.

CINDER CONE

A cinder cone is a steep conical hill of loose pyroclastic fragments, such as volcanic clinkers, volcanic ash, or cinder that has been built around a volcanic vent. The pyroclastic fragments are formed by explosive eruptions or lava fountains from a single, typically cylindrical, vent.

COMPOSITE CONE/ STRATOVOLCANOES

Stratovolcanoes have relatively steep sides and are more cone-shaped than shield volcanoes. They are formed from viscous, sticky lava that does not flow easily. The lava therefore builds up around the vent forming a volcano with steep sides.

Cinder Cone

Shield Cone

Composite Cone

-They have a convex and perfect shape and most of them have alternating eruptions. Most of them have a magma type of basalt, andesite, dacite, and rhyolite and a lava type of acidic lava, which is very viscous (sticky).
