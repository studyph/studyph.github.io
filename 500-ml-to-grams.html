16. How many grams of H3PO4 are contained in 500 ml of 0.500M solution of this acid


Solution:

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of solute (H₃PO₄).

molar mass = (1.008 g/mol × 3) + (30.97 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 4)

molar mass = 97.994 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\text{molarity} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{volume of solution (in L)}}[/tex]

moles of solute = molarity × volume of solution (in L)

moles of solute = 0.500 M × 0.500 L

moles of solute = 0.250 mol

Step 3: Calculate the mass of solute.

[tex]\text{mass of solute = 0.250 mol H₃PO₄} × \frac{\text{97.994 g H₃PO₄}}{\text{1 mol H₃PO₄}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of solute = 24.5 g}}[/tex]

19. What is the molarity of a solution that contains 18.7 grams of kcl in 500 ml (mw 74.5)?


SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & mass_{\text{solute}} = \text{18.7 g} \\ & MW_{\text{solute}} = \text{74.5 g/mol} \\ & V_{\text{solution}} = \text{500 mL = 0.500 L} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{mass_{\text{solute}}}{MW_{\text{solute}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{18.7 g}}{\text{74.5 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.251 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molarity of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} M & = \frac{n_{\text{solute}}}{V_{\text{solution}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.251 mol}}{\text{0.500 L}} \\ & = \text{0.502 mol/L} \\ & = \boxed{0.502 \: M} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molarity of the solution is 0.502 M.

22. How many grams of KNO3 will be required to prepare 500 mL of 1.20 M KNO3 ?​


To calculate the number of grams of KNO3 required to prepare 500 mL of 1.20 M KNO3 solution, we need to use the formula:

Molarity (M) = moles of solute / volume of solution in liters

We can rearrange this formula to find the number of moles of solute needed:

moles of solute = Molarity (M) x volume of solution in liters

We know the volume of solution (500 mL = 0.5 L) and the desired molarity (1.20 M), so we can plug these values into the formula:

moles of KNO3 = 1.20 M x 0.5 L

moles of KNO3 = 0.60 moles

Now that we know the number of moles of KNO3 needed, we can use its molar mass to convert moles to grams:

mass of KNO3 = moles of KNO3 x molar mass of KNO3

The molar mass of KNO3 is 101.1 g/mol. Therefore:

mass of KNO3 = 0.60 moles x 101.1 g/mol

mass of KNO3 = 60.66 g

So, 60.66 grams of KNO3 will be required to prepare 500 mL of 1.20 M KNO3 solution.
