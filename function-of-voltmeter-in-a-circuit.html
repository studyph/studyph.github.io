4. what is voltmeter? what is the function of this?​


What is voltmeter? What is the function of this?​

What is a voltmeter? A voltmeter is an tool that measures the difference in electric potential between two locations in an electric circuit. It is connected in a parallel manner. It has a high resistance and draws very little current from the circuit. Analog voltmeters use a galvanometer and a series resistor to move a pointer around a scale in proportion to the voltage detected. Amplifier-based meters can detect voltages as low as a few microvolts. An analog-to-digital converter is used in digital voltmeters to display voltage numerically. Voltmeters come in a variety of forms, some of which are powered separately (for example, by a battery) and others which are powered by the voltage source being measured. Generators and other stationary devices are monitored using instruments permanently positioned in a panel. Standard test devices used in electrical and electronics work include portable instruments that can measure current and resistance using a multimeter. Any measurement that can be converted to a voltage, such as pressure, temperature, flow, or level in a chemical process plant, can be displayed on a properly calibrated meter.
