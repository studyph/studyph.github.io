Biotechnology is the use of living organisms or enzymes from living organisms to produce or modify products for specific practical purposes.

11. why the application of genetic engineering agriculture and medicine is considered as biotechnology minimum 10 sentence​


Genetic engineering involves the manipulation of genetic material to create desired traits in living organisms, which is a key aspect of biotechnology.

Agriculture and medicine are two major fields where genetic engineering is widely applied, making them important areas of biotechnology.

In agriculture, genetic engineering is used to create crops that are resistant to pests, diseases, and environmental stressors, resulting in increased yields and improved food security.

Genetic engineering is also used in agriculture to create crops that have enhanced nutritional value or longer shelf life, which can benefit both farmers and consumers.

In medicine, genetic engineering is used to develop new treatments for genetic disorders, such as cystic fibrosis and sickle cell anemia.

Genetic engineering is also used to create new vaccines and therapies for diseases like cancer and HIV/AIDS.

Biotechnology companies are heavily involved in the development and commercialization of these new treatments, making biotechnology an important part of the healthcare industry.

Genetic engineering is also used to create genetically modified organisms (GMOs), which are organisms that have been modified in a laboratory to have specific traits.

GMOs are widely used in agriculture and have been a topic of controversy due to concerns about their safety and potential impact on the environment.

Overall, genetic engineering has revolutionized agriculture and medicine, making biotechnology a critical field that has the potential to improve human health and well-being, as well as address global challenges such as food security and climate change.

Explain how understanding evolution is important to medicine, agriculture, and maintaining the diversity of organisms on earth.

Understanding evolution is important There are many examples of this in the world of medicine. 15. What are the progress in agriculture, engineering and medicine by the use of natures resources.?


Answer:

Explanation:

Humans have had a profoundly adverse impact on the environment. Failure to conduct activities like manufacturing, transportation, large-scale fishing, agriculture and waste disposal in moderation impacts the land, air and water. While the full range of long-term consequences of human intervention on the environment is uncertain, some consequences like climate change are already apparent.

TL;DR (Too Long; Didn't Read)

The human impact on the environment is substantial and adverse. These include land degradation (deforestation), air pollution, water contamination and climate change.

Land Degradation

There are many examples of human failure to use land resources sustainably. Deforestation occurs when humans clear forests to use the land either for agriculture or for habitation. Consequently, forest cover dwindles significantly, leading to soil erosion and extinction of plant species. Land animals also decline in numbers and even face extinction due to human expansion that encroaches on their natural habitat and limits their ability to spread geographically.

Air Pollution

One of the biggest environmental impacts of human activities is air quality. The transportation sector contributes heavily to air pollution because most forms of transportation, including cars, planes and ocean vessels, use fossil fuels. When burned, fossil fuels release carbon dioxide and other greenhouse gases into the environment.

In addition, the manufacturing industry grows exponentially with the expansion of the human population. Manufacturing plants emit carbons and sulfurs that do not occur naturally in the environment, causing an imbalance in the quality and composition of air. Some air pollutants deplete the ozone layer and expose the Earth to dangerous radiation from the sun.

Water Contamination

Human intervention in the environment also jeopardizes the supply and flow of clean drinking water. Activities like waste disposal from residential, commercial and industrial areas, oil spills and runoff from agriculture all contaminate bodies of water. The direct deposit of pollutants into lakes, rivers, seas and streams and indirect runoff of hazardous substances during the rainy seasons both impact water sources. Another environmental issue impacting water systems is overfishing, which causes a reduction in diversity of marine life.

Climate Change

Human activities in the environment interfere with the planet's natural balance, making the Earth’s climate less stable and predictable. Climate change brings abnormal occurrences such as unprecedented flooding; increased numbers of storms, hurricanes and typhoons; fiercer brush fires; and most notably tsunamis, which are uncommon in the Earth’s recent history. Phenomena such as rising sea levels, unseasonably high temperatures and drought hint toward an environment that cannot take much more negative human impact.
