7. Applied EcoA. Briefly explain how you can apply economics in your daily life in terms of the following: 1. Use of daily or weekly allowances 2. Time Management 3. Chosen School and course​


Answer:

Use of daily or weekly allowances: Economics can help you make better decisions with your daily or weekly allowances by understanding the concept of opportunity cost. Opportunity cost refers to the cost of the next best alternative forgone when making a decision. For example, if you have a daily allowance of $10 and you want to buy a coffee that costs $5, you need to consider what else you could do with that $5, such as saving it or spending it on something else you need. By considering opportunity cost, you can make better decisions that align with your priorities and goals.

Time Management: Time is a scarce resource, and economics can help you manage it effectively. One way to do this is by using the concept of marginal analysis, which involves weighing the costs and benefits of each additional hour you spend on a particular task. For example, if you are studying for an exam, you need to decide whether to spend an extra hour reviewing your notes or taking a break to relax. By considering the marginal costs and benefits of each option, you can make a more informed decision about how to allocate your time.

Chosen School and course: Economics can also help you make better decisions about your education, such as choosing a school and course of study. By considering the costs and benefits of each option, you can make a decision that aligns with your goals and preferences. For example, if you are deciding between two schools, you need to consider the tuition costs, the quality of education, and the potential return on investment in terms of future job prospects. By weighing these factors, you can make a more informed decision about which school to attend and what course to study.
