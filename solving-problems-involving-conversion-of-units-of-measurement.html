Answer:

1. In the Summer Olympic Games, athletes compete in races of the following lengths: 100 meters. If Usain Bolt were to run in all these races, how many kilometers would he run? meters, 200 meters, 400 meters, 800 meters, 1500 meters, 5000 meters and 10,000

Solution:

Just from the problem, the runner was to run in all these races: 100 meters, 200 meters, 400 meters, 800 meters, 1,500 meters, 5,000 meters, 10,000 meters. { Given }

So, he would run 100 + 200 + 400 + 800 + 1,500 + 5,000 + 10,000

= 18000m = 18000 × 0.001km = 18km { Substitution, 1m=0.001km }

2. Jerry weighs in at 85 kg. He is 80 dag heavier than his opponent. How much does his opponent weigh?

Solution

{ 1 dag = 10g }

80 dag = 800g = 0.8kg

His opponent weighs (85 + 0.8)

= 85.8kg

Hence, the opponent's weight is 85.8kg

3. A two-liter bottle contains 87 centiliters of oil and 4.1 deciliters of water. How much more liquid is needed to fill the bottle?

Solution

1 liter = 10 deciliters = 100 centiliters

2 liters = 200 centiliters

4.1 deciliters = 41 centiliters

[tex]200-87-41=72[/tex] centiliters.

Thus, there are 72 centiliters liquid that is needed to fill the bottle

{ The transformation of the unit }

4. John rode 2 kilometers on his bike. His sister Sally rode 3000 meters on her bike. Who rode the farthest and how much farther did they ride (answer in km)?

Solution

3000 meters = 3 kilometers

2km < 3km { Unit conversion }

So, Sally rode the farthest.

According to the question, John rode 2km and Sally rode 3km

5. Jessa is measuring two line segments. The first line segment is 30 cm long. The second line segment is 500 mm long. How long are the two line segments together? (answer in cm)

Solution

The first line segment is 30cm long, the second line segment is 500mm long

500mm = 50cm (1cm = 10mm, mutual conversion)

30 + 50 = 80 (cm)

So the two line segments are 80cm long together.

Answer:

1. To find out how many kilometers Usain Bolt would run in all these races, we need to convert all the distances from meters to kilometers and then add them up:

•100 meters = 0.1 kilometers

•200 meters = 0.2 kilometers

•400 meters = 0.4 kilometers

•800 meters = 0.8 kilometers

•1500 meters = 1.5 kilometers

•5000 meters = 5 kilometers

•10,000 meters = 10 kilometers

Adding all these up, we get:

0.1 + 0.2 + 0.4 + 0.8 + 1.5 + 5 + 10 = 18 kilometers

Therefore, Usain Bolt would run 18 kilometers in all these races.

2. Jerry weighs 85 kg, and he is 80 dag heavier than his opponent. To find out how much his opponent weighs, we need to convert the given weight difference from dag to kg, and then subtract it from Jerry's weight:

•80 dag = 8 kg (1 dag = 0.1 kg)

Subtracting 8 kg from Jerry's weight, we get:

85 kg - 8 kg = 77 kg

Therefore, Jerry's opponent weighs 77 kg.

3. A two-liter bottle contains 87 centiliters of oil and 4.1 deciliters of water. To find out how much more liquid is needed to fill the bottle, we need to convert the given volumes to liters and then add them up:

•87 centiliters = 0.87 liters (1 centiliter = 0.01 liter)

•4.1 deciliters = 0.41 liters (1 deciliter = 0.1 liter)

Adding these up, we get:

0.87 + 0.41 = 1.28 liters

Since the bottle can hold 2 liters of liquid, we need to subtract the current amount from the capacity to find out how much more is needed:

2 - 1.28 = 0.72 liters

Therefore, 0.72 liters more liquid is needed to fill the bottle.

4. John rode 2 kilometers on his bike, and Sally rode 3000 meters on her bike. To find out who rode the farthest and how much farther they rode, we need to convert Sally's distance from meters to kilometers and then compare the two distances:

•3000 meters = 3 kilometers (1 kilometer = 1000 meters)

Comparing the two distances, we see that Sally rode farther than John by:

3 km - 2 km = 1 km

Therefore, Sally rode 1 kilometer farther than John.

5. Jessa is measuring two line segments. The first line segment is 30 cm long, and the second line segment is 500 mm long. To find out how long the two line segments are together, we need to convert the second segment from millimeters to centimeters and then add the two lengths:

• 500 mm = 50 cm (1 centimeter = 10 millimeters)

Adding the two lengths, we get:

30 cm + 50 cm = 80 cm

Therefore, the two line segments are 80 cm long together.
