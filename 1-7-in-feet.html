5. 1. The width of a rectangular garden is 2x - 7 feet and the length is 3x + 8 feet. Find the perimeter of the rectangular garden.​


The perimeter of a rectangle is the sum of the lengths of all its sides. In this case, the rectangular garden has two equal length sides and two equal width sides.

So, the perimeter of the rectangular garden is:

P = 2(l + w)

where P is the perimeter, l is the length, and w is the width.

Substituting the given values, we have:

P = 2(3x + 8 + 2x - 7)

Simplifying the expression inside the parentheses:

P = 2(5x + 1)

Multiplying by 2:

P = 10x + 2

Therefore, the perimeter of the rectangular garden is 10x + 2 feet.
