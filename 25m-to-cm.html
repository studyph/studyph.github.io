20. 6. What is the area of a square whose side is 5m+2 ? A. 25m² + 4 B. 25m² + 10 m + 4 C. 25m² - 10m +4 D. 25m² + 20 m +4​


Answer:

The answer is D. 25m² + 20m + 4.


Step-by-step explanation:

The area of a square is equal to the side length squared. Therefore, the area of a square whose side is 5m + 2 can be found by squaring 5m + 2.

(5m + 2)^2 = (5m + 2)(5m + 2) [using the formula for squaring a binomial]

= 25m^2 + 10m + 10m + 4 [using FOIL method]

= 25m^2 + 20m + 4
