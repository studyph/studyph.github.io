13. how to read using ruler with cm, mm, and inches.​


Answer:

Reading a ruler with centimeters (cm), millimeters (mm), and inches can be tricky, but with some practice, it can be easy to do. Here are the steps to read a ruler with cm, mm, and inches:

Identify the units: The first step is to determine which units the ruler is marked with. A ruler with cm and mm markings will have longer and shorter lines. A ruler with inches will have longer lines marked with numbers, and shorter lines marked with fractions.

Locate the zero mark: The zero mark on the ruler is where the measurement starts. It is usually at the edge of the ruler.

Read the cm or inches: To read cm or inches, look at the number next to the line that the object ends at. For example, if the end of the object is at the 3 cm mark, the length of the object is 3 cm. If the end of the object is at the 2 inch mark, the length of the object is 2 inches.

Read the mm: To read mm, look at the number on the ruler that the object lines up with. If the object ends at the 3.5 cm mark, look at the line between 3 cm and 4 cm to see the mm measurement. In this case, the object would be 35 mm long.

Estimate: If the object ends between two lines on the ruler, estimate the fraction of the measurement. For example, if the object ends between the 3 cm and 4 cm mark, estimate the fraction of a cm that the object covers. If the object ends between the 1/2 inch and 3/4 inch mark, estimate the fraction of an inch that the object covers.

Practice reading rulers with cm, mm, and inches to improve your skills. With practice, it will become easier to read measurements accurately.
