2. give the definitions of the six principles of design​


Answer:

The six principles of design are:

Advertisement

Balance: How visual elements are distributed in relation to an axis

Pattern: How visual elements repeat to create consistency

Rhythm: How visual elements repeat or altgive the definitions of the six principles of designernate to create interest

Emphasis: How visual elements work together to create a focal point

Contrast: How visual elements oppose each other to highlight differences

Unity: How visual elements create an overall sense of cohesion

Explanation:

To understand how these principles are applied with maximum impact, look to the masters. Throughout history, the world’s greatest artists have used the six principles of design to captivate viewers.

For example, take a look at "The School of Athens,” a fresco by Raphael.

Advertisement

Here, Raphael uses the design principle of balance. Despite not being perfectly symmetrical, all the elements are arranged so that neither the left nor the right side of the work has dominance over the other. We see this when we highlight the major elements (red), in relation to a vertical axis (black):

Jordan_SchoolofAthens_Figure3.jpg.png

On either side of the vertical axis, the arches present mirror images of each other, columns are evenly distributed, a marble sculpture appears near the outer edges, and the many figures in the bottom half of the work occupy roughly the same size and shape of space. Using balance, Raphael depicts a large number of elements with a wide variety of colors, shapes, and sizes, without seeming cluttered or disorderly.

9. Identify the six web design principles and briefly explain the importance of each indesigning your website,1.2.3.4.5.6.help please​


The six principles of web design are:

User-centered design: This principle involves designing the website with the user's needs and goals in mind. The website should be easy to navigate and use, and should provide the user with the information they are looking for in a clear and concise way.

Accessibility: This principle involves making the website accessible to people with disabilities, such as those who are blind or have low vision, deaf or hard of hearing, or who have mobility impairments. This is important because it ensures that everyone can access and use the website, regardless of their abilities.

Responsive design: This principle involves designing the website to be flexible and adaptable, so that it can be easily viewed and used on different devices, such as smartphones, tablets, and desktop computers. This is important because it ensures that the website can be accessed and used by a wide range of people, regardless of the device they are using.

Aesthetics: This principle involves making the website visually appealing and engaging, through the use of color, typography, images, and other design elements. This is important because it helps to create a positive user experience, and can help to attract and retain visitors to the website.

Usability: This principle involves making the website easy to use and navigate, through the use of clear and intuitive navigation, clear labeling, and other user-friendly design elements. This is important because it helps to ensure that users can find the information they are looking for quickly and easily, and can help to reduce frustration and improve the overall user experience.

Content: This principle involves creating high-quality, engaging, and relevant content for the website, and presenting it in a way that is easy to read and understand. This is important because it helps to provide value to the user, and can help to establish the website as a reliable and trustworthy source of information.
