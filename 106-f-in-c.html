20. A photon has a (f) frequency of 2.68 x 106 Hz. calculate its energy.PA ANSWER PLS​


Answer:

1.78 • 10–²⁷J

Explanation:

As you know, the energy of a photon is directly proportional to its frequency.

This relationship between energy and frequency is established by the Planck - Einstein equation, which looks like this

E=h⋅ν ,where

E - the energy of the photon

h - Planck's constant, equal to 6.626 ⋅ 10−³⁴J s

ν - the frequency of the photon

Notice that the frequency of the photon is given to you in hertz, Hz . As you know, one hertz, which denotes one cycle per second, is equivalent to

1 Hz = 1 s − ¹

Plug in the value you have and solve for E to get

E = 6.626 ⋅ 10 − ³⁴ Js ⋅ 2.68 ⋅ 10⁶ s−¹

E = 1.7758 ⋅ 10−²⁷J

Rounded to three sig figs, the answer will be

E = 1.78 ⋅ 10−²⁷J (final answer)

25. 11. What is the frequency of an EM wave with a wavelength of 280 meters? A. 1.07 x 106 Hz. C. 1.07 x 106 /s B. 1.07 x 106 kHz D. 1.06 x 10-6 m​


[tex]\tt{\huge{\blue{Explanation:}}}[/tex]

The frequency of an electromagnetic wave (EM) is given by

[tex]\boxed{f = \frac{c}{\lambda}}[/tex]

where:

λ = wavelength

c = speed of light in vacuum = 3 × 10⁸ m/s

f = frequency

[tex]\tt{\huge{\red{Solution:}}}[/tex]

Solving for the frequency

[tex]f = \dfrac{c}{\lambda}[/tex]

[tex]f = \dfrac{3 \times 10^8 \: \text{m/s}}{\text{280 m}}[/tex]

f = 1.07 × 10⁶ /s

[tex]\boxed{f = 1.07 \times 10^6 \: \text{Hz}}[/tex]

Therefore, the answer is A. 1.07 × 10⁶ Hz
