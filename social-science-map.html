Sociological Science

The academic discipline of social science is interested in society and the interpersonal interactions among members of a community, and it typically heavily utilizes empirical methodologies. Among their sub-fields are anthropology, economics, political science, psychology, and sociology.

History

The interaction between continuity and change is history's primary concern.

Anthropology

Understanding what humans are, how they evolved, and how they differ from one another is the goal of anthropology.

Economics

Economics is a social science that studies how products and services are produced, distributed, and consumed. It examines the decisions that people, organizations, governments, and entire countries make when allocating resources.

Sociology

Since sociologists concentrate more emphasis on how external groups influence an individual's conduct, they focus primarily on the collective aspects of human behavior.

Geography

Geography aims to comprehend where things are found and why they are there, how objects that are nearby or far away affect one another over time, and why locations and the people who inhabit them evolve and grow in specific ways.

Demography

The field of social sciences known as demography is dedicated to the study of human populations, including their composition, movement, and interactions with the environment, as well as social and economic change.

The study of politics

Politics at the municipal, state, national, and international levels are the main topics of study in political science. We are committed to advancing modes of inquiry that support citizenship as well as understandings of the institutions, behaviors, and relationships that make up public life.

This area of social science uses a variety of lenses made up of a body of concepts, theories, frameworks, and approaches to help us understand and look into the intricate variables that influence and develop human civilization.
