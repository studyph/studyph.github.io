10. nelson mandela summarize speech


Nelson Mandela was a key leader in the struggle against apartheid, South Africa's official policy of segregation and white supremacy. In 1963 South African authorities discovered incriminating documents and illegal weapons at the headquarters of Umkhonto we Sizwe, a group Mandela had helped found. Umkhonto had ties to the African National Congress (ANC), a group dedicated to eliminating apartheid. The ANC's policy had long been to use peaceful methods to try to resist apartheid. Umkhonto was founded by activists who were willing to use violent methods.

The authorities arrested Mandela and several other men and charged them with multiple offenses including sabotage, treason, and conspiring to overthrow the South African government. Facing the death penalty, Mandela gave this speech at his trial. In the speech, he admits to his illegal acts and makes a moral argument for his actions. His argument turns on the idea that the legal and nonviolent means that black South Africans had used to struggle for years against the oppressive apartheid system had been ineffective. A more militant approach, including forms of violence, had become necessary in Mandela's view.

21. Talambuhay Ni Nelson Mandela


Nelson Mandela: Bayani ng Africa 

Ang tinutukoy na kalayaan ni Nelson Mandela sa kanyang talumpati ay ang kalayan ng kanilang bansa. Kalayaan sa pansariling pamamahala o demokrasya. Maituturing na isang malaking tagumpay para sa mga mamamayan ng isang bansa ang mapagkalooban ng kalayaang political, kalayaang pulili ng lider  kalayaang hindi matutumbasan ng kahit anong material na bagay. Bahagi rin ng kalayaang pinupunto ng kanyang talumpati ay ang pagiging malaya sa tinatawag na diskriminasyon , ibig sabihin may pantay- pantay na kalayaan ang bawat isang mamamayan ng Timog Africa, itim man o Puti ang kulay ng balat.

Ang mga mamamayan ng Timog Africa ay pinagkaitan ng pansariling kalayaan nakakulong ang mga ito sa rehas ng diskriminasyon at limitasyon. Kaya naman masasabing isang malaking tagumpay ang nakamit nila.


Walang katumbas na bagay ang maihahambing sa halaga ng kalayaang tinutukoy ni Mandela,  kaya naman masasabi natin na ito ay buong tapang at buong bangis nilang ipinaglaban. Itinuturing ni Mandela na ang pagkamit ng kalayaang ito ay ang simula ng paghilom ng mga sugat na iniwan ng masalimuot na nakaraan .

Ibig sabihin ay panahon na para ang mamamayan ng Timog Africa ay tumayo sa sarili nilang mga paa upang bumuo ng panibagong bayan na kung saan ay malaya ang lahat  sa kumpleto, makatarungan, at panghabambuhay na kapayapaan. Kapayapaan na maihahalintulad mo sa malayang paglalakd sa daan na walang takot sa bawat puso, karapatan sa pagkakaroon ng dignidad – isang bansang may kapayapaang pansarili at pambansa.

Hindi nagging madali ang tinahak na landas ng mga mamamayan ng Timog Africa upang makamit ang tinutukoy niyang kasarinlan, isang masalimuot at nakatatakot na landas ang kanilang nalagpasan upang makamit ang inaasam na kalayaan. Ang mga pangarap nilang tila walang katuparan naging makatotohanan at kanila ng nararanasan.

Bahagi rin ng talumpati, ang pangangako ni Mandela sa mga kababayan na pangalagaang mabuti ang natamasang kasarinlan.  Malaking bahagi ng talumpating ito, ay ang taos pusong pasasalamat ni ni Mandela at ng buong mamamayan ng Timog Africa sa mga nagging bahagi ng kanilang pagtahak patungo sa inaasam na kalayaan.

Pinasasalamatan ang pagpunta ng bawat isa  upang angkinin, kasama ang mamamayan ng aming bansa, ang tagumpay para sa katarungan, para sa kapayapaan, at para sa dignidad. At ang pagpapakita ng pag-asa sa patuloy na suporta sakanilang laban na ipagpapatuloy ang pagharap sa mga pagsubok sa pagbuo ng kapayapaan, kaunlaran, pagkakapantay-pantay ng mga lalaki at babae, walang diskriminasyon sa lahi, at demokrasya.

25. Reflection in Nelson mandela​


Answer:

Mandela's life and story are testament to the spirit of courage and endurance in the face of geopolitical imperative. They are also, however, a cautionary tale of the limits of human agency and the power of geography.

Explanation:

Nelson Mandela's life and death provide a moment to reflect on the geopolitics of a continent in which the post-apartheid leader will always be revered as a relentless champion of democracy, human rights and the unfinished struggle for independence. He was the scion of a royal family, a lawyer, activist, guerrilla leader, a prisoner on the infamous Robben Island and eventually a president who sought to create a South African identity that transcended race. Mandela's lifelong struggle was one against the imperatives of geopolitics.

In many ways, Mandela, who died at 95 years of age, was exceptional among Africa's rulers. He defied the self-enrichment that has characterized many of the continent's leaders since independence, from the Republic of the Congo's (formerly Zaire's) Mobutu Sese Seko to Angola's Jose Eduardo dos Santos. Self-enrichment is rife within the African National Congress, and though Mandela certainly used that to his benefit, he became only modestly wealthy. He also fought the reflex so common in other regions of post-colonial Africa by stepping down from a single term in office in 1999. Even those leaders who have come close to his stature on the world stage — Ghana's Kwame Nkrumah or Senegal's Leopold Senghor, for example — surrendered power only when forced to do so. Among Mandela's many legacies is his successful transition from guerrilla commander to true president.

In particular, he acknowledged and sought to effectively manage the geopolitical limitations of South Africa. It is a country awash in riches, but the markets for virtually all its products lay beyond its shores — in the West for its diamonds, gold and other mineral resources, and increasingly in the East for its coal, oil and natural gas. But the country was starved for capital when Mandela took over. It badly needed foreign buyers for its industry, mining sector and transportation infrastructure. Under Mandela's administration, South Africa re-opened to the world and saw the recovery of significant and diverse foreign investment.

Mandela did not nationalize industries or drive away foreign investors, despite some populist expectations to the contrary. Mandela understood that South Africa was emerging from pariah status and that its economy was isolated and subject to international sanctions. Nationalizing the South African economy would have driven away exactly what the country needed: fresh economic confidence. Industry needed rehabilitation, and to achieve the economic goals of the impoverished black majority, Mandela needed to assure domestic and foreign investors that their positions would be forever protected. Mandela and his administration took great pains to assure these economic provisions.

Mandela knew that he had the political mandate to reshape his country. But he also understood the country's geopolitical limitations. He did not try to erase the colonial institutions that preceded his rule but instead sought to entrench systems that would enable good governance. He did not nationalize industries or drive out foreign investment but instead trod a careful path that has helped South Africa to grow and evolve, if not yet fully thrive, as a multiracial democracy. For all his popularity, Mandela knew that his country could not afford to be a pariah and that foreign investment would be vital to its economic success.


26. Nelson Mandela title of speech ​


Answer:

"I Am Prepared to Die" is the name given to the three-hour speech given by Nelson Mandela on 20 April 1964 from the dock of the defendant at the Rivonia Trial. The speech is so titled because it ends with the words "it is an ideal for which I am prepared to die".
