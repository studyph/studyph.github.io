1. If the planar drawing of the graph has 15 edges and 8 vertices, how many faces does the graph have? ​


G has a total of 9 vertices.

Further explanation:

When can you make a graph where none of the lines cross? We say the graph is planar if this is possible (since you can draw it on the plane). When a planar graph is drawn without the edges crossing, the edges and vertices of the graph divide the plane into regions. Each area will be called a "face." Geometry is another part of math where you may have heard the words "vertex," "edge," and "face."

Euler's Formula

For many solid shapes:

Number of Faces + the Number of Vertices - the Number of Edges = 2

This can be written as:

F + V − E = 2

It is given that E = 15 and F = 8 therefore Face can be calculated by plugging these values into the above equation

V - 15 + 8 = 2

V + 8   =  17      ( adding 15 on both sides)  

V = 9
