3. what is the meaning of The Evil Eye? how you will know if you were affected of The Evil Eye? (i'm taking jotting down what The Evil Eye is about)


Answer:

The evil eye is a human look believed to cause harm to someone or something. The supernatural harm may come in the form of a minor misfortune, or more serious disease, injury — even death. Folklorist Alan Dundes, in his edited volume "The Evil Eye: A Casebook" (University of Wisconsin Press, 1992) notes that "the victim's good fortune, good health, or good looks — or unguarded comments about them — invite or provoke an attack by someone with the evil eye ... Symptoms of illness caused by the evil eye include loss of appetite, excessive yawning, hiccups, vomiting and fever. If the object attacked is a cow, its milk may dry up; if a plant or fruit tree, it may suddenly wither and die."

25. Explain the statement," most evils of this world can be traced into the advent of profit motive. ON THE THREE EVILS STORY


Explanation:

The statement "most evils of this world can be traced to the advent of profit motive" suggests that the pursuit of profit is a major cause of many of the negative aspects of our world. The profit motive refers to the desire to make money or to gain a financial advantage, often at the expense of other values such as ethics, social responsibility, or the environment.

The argument behind this statement is that many of the world's problems, such as inequality, environmental degradation, and exploitation of workers, can be traced back to the pursuit of profit. When companies or individuals prioritize profit over other values, they may engage in unethical or harmful practices in order to maximize their financial gain.

For example, companies may exploit workers by paying low wages or providing poor working conditions in order to maximize profits. They may also engage in environmental degradation, such as pollution or deforestation, if it is profitable for them to do so. Similarly, the pursuit of profit can lead to economic inequality and exploitation, as those who are able to generate more profit accumulate more wealth and power, often at the expense of others.

While profit can be a driving force for innovation and progress, the argument here is that the pursuit of profit must be balanced with consideration for other values such as social responsibility, ethics, and the environment in order to avoid the negative consequences associated with the profit motive.

27. what is the author's conception of good and evil in the story love and time​


Answer:

In the story, the author presents two characters, Love and Time, who have different conceptions of good and evil. Love represents the positive and benevolent force in the world, while Time represents the negative and destructive force. Love sees the good in people and tries to make the world a better place, while Time sees the flaws and imperfections in everything and brings about change through destruction.

The author suggests that good and evil are not absolute concepts, but rather depend on one's perspective and point of view. Love and Time have opposing views of the world, and their actions reflect their different beliefs about what is good and what is evil. Love sees the beauty and potential in everything, while Time sees only the inevitable decay and destruction.

Ultimately, the story suggests that good and evil are complex and nuanced concepts that are shaped by our experiences and beliefs. The author does not present a clear-cut definition of good and evil, but rather encourages readers to think critically about these concepts and their implications in the world
