How does infection affects a person's white blood cells WBC count?

When you get sick, your body makes more white blood cells to fight the bacteria, viruses, or other foreign substances causing your illness. This increases your white blood count. Other diseases can cause your body to make fewer white blood cells than you need. This lowers your white blood count.

8. Which of the following does not belong to the group? a. erythrocytes b. blood c. platelets d. antigen e. leukocytes


Antigen does not belong to the group of blood composition.

Blood Components and Their Functions

Well, here are the components of blood and their functions that you need to know:

1. Plasma

The liquid component of blood is called plasma. Blood plasma consists of a mixture of water, sugar, fat, protein, and salt. The main job of plasma is to transport blood cells throughout the body along with nutrients, waste products, antibodies, clotting proteins, chemical messengers such as hormones, and proteins that help maintain the body's fluid balance.

2. Erythrocytes (Red Blood Cells)

Erythrocytes, or red blood cells, are the most abundant cells in the blood. ed blood cells account for 40-45 percent of blood volume.They vary in shape, but most red blood cells are shaped like a biconcave disc with a flattened center, resembling a bowl.

The production of red blood cells is controlled by erythropoietin, a hormone produced by the kidneys. The formation of red blood cells starts with immature cells in the bone marrow. It takes about seven days for red blood cells to mature before being released into the bloodstream. Red blood cells last only 120 days on average.

3. White Blood Cells (Leukocytes).

Although the number is much less than red blood cells, leukocytes have a very large role in fighting infection. he number of leukocytes in the body accounts for about 1% of total blood volume.The most common type of white blood cell is the neutrophil, which is an "immediate response" cell and accounts for 55-70 percent of the total white blood cell count.

4. Platelets

Unlike red and white blood cells, platelets are not actually cells but smaller cell fragments. Platelets help in the process of blood clotting, or coagulation, by gathering at the site of injury.

9. whats the platelets what's the platelets​


Answer:

What's the platelets?

A tiny, disc-shaped piece of cell that is found in the blood and spleen. Platelets are pieces of very large cells in the bone marrow called megakaryocytes. They help form blood clots to slow or stop bleeding and to help wounds heal. Having too many or too few platelets or having platelets that don’t work as they should can cause problems. Checking the number of platelets in the blood may help diagnose certain diseases or conditions. Also called thrombocyte.
