15. Ano ang tunkulin na ginagampanan ng Securities and Exchange Commission


Answer:

The Securities and Exchange Commission (Filipino: Komisyon sa mga Panagot at Palitan, commonly known as SEC) is the agency of the Government of the Philippines responsible for regulating the securities industry in the Philippines. In addition to its regulatory functions, the SEC also maintains the country's company register.

An agency within Department of Finance, the SEC is temporarily headquartered at the Philippine International Convention Center in Pasay City, Metro Manila. It will due to transfer to a new site in Bonifacio Global City, Taguig City.

23. in forming partnership, what requirements must be submitted with the Security and Exchange Commission? ​


The requirements for forming a partnership with the Securities and Exchange Commission (SEC) will depend on the type of partnership you are forming and the jurisdiction in which you are located. In general, partnerships are not required to register with the SEC. However, if the partnership is engaged in certain types of securities activities, such as buying and selling securities for clients or managing investment portfolios, it may be required to register as an investment adviser with the SEC.

To register as an investment adviser with the SEC, a partnership must submit a Form ADV, which is a registration form that provides information about the partnership, its business practices, and the investment advisory services it provides. The Form ADV consists of two parts: Part 1 and Part 2. Part 1 contains information about the partnership's business, ownership, and any disciplinary history, while Part 2 provides information about the investment advisory services offered by the partnership.

In addition to the Form ADV, the partnership may also be required to submit other documents and information to the SEC, such as audited financial statements and proof of compliance with certain regulatory requirements.

It's important to note that the requirements for forming a partnership and registering with the SEC can be complex and vary depending on the specific circumstances. It's recommended that you consult with a qualified attorney or financial advisor who can provide guidance on the specific requirements for your partnership.
