5. Find the average acceleration 9 a northbound subway train that slows down from Rims to 9.6 m/s in 0.85.​


Answer:

To find the average acceleration of the northbound subway train, we can use the formula:

average acceleration = (final velocity - initial velocity) / time

where the final velocity is 0 m/s (since the train comes to a stop), the initial velocity is 9 m/s (since the train starts at a speed of 9 m/s), and the time is 0.85 s.

average acceleration = (0 m/s - 9 m/s) / 0.85 s

average acceleration = -10.59 m/s^2

Therefore, the average acceleration of the northbound subway train is -10.59 m/s^2, which indicates that the train is slowing down.

10. A 5.00 × 10− subway train is brought to a stop from a speed of 0.500 / in 0.400 by a large spring bumper at the end of its track. What is the spring constant k of the spring?


Answer:

This can be calculated with the law of conservation of energy. The kinetic energy Ek of the train is completely transformed to potential energy of the spring Ep which stops the train:

Ek=Ep

(1/2)*m*v²=(1/2)*k*x², where m is mass of the train, v is the velocity of the train, k is the force constant of the spring and x is the path the train went while being stopped and also the amount of length the spring compressed.

We solve for k:

(1/2)*m*v²=(1/2)*k*x², 1/2 cancel out:

m*v²=k*x², we divide both sides by x² and get k:

k=(m*v²)/x²={(5*10^5)*(0.5²)}/(0.4²)=781250 N/m

So the force constant of the spring is: k=781250 N/m.

A subway train can be considered continuous if it satisfies certain conditions of smoothness and connectivity. In calculus, continuity is defined as the property that a function or object has when there are no sudden jumps or discontinuities in its behavior.

For a subway train to be continuous, it must have a smooth and unbroken trajectory along its path, with no sudden changes in speed, direction, or acceleration. This requires that the train's motion is well-controlled and that it is subject to a set of physical laws that ensure its smooth operation.

One important condition for the continuity of a subway train is that it must be able to maintain a constant velocity or speed, with no sudden changes or interruptions. This requires that the train's engine and braking systems are well-designed and properly maintained, so that they can operate smoothly and respond quickly to changes in speed or direction.

Another important condition for the continuity of a subway train is that it must be connected, both physically and electrically. This means that all of the components of the train, including the cars, motors, and control systems, must be properly linked together and communicating effectively, so that the train can operate as a single, unified entity.

Overall, the continuity of a subway train is a complex and multi-faceted property that depends on many different factors, including the design of the train, the condition of its components, and the skill and experience of the operators and maintenance personnel who keep it running smoothly.
