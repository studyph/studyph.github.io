1. how would you differentiate sinusoidal and non sinusoidal oscillators.​


Answer:

A cosine wave is sinusoidal. It has the same form but it has been phase-shifted one-half π radians. A non-sinusoidal waveform is typically a periodic oscillation but is neither of these. Some examples are triangle waves, rectangle waves, square waves, trapezoid waves and saw tooth waves.

8. Explain frequency and wavelength using sinusoidal waves.​


Explanation:

The wavelength of a wave is the distance between any two corresponding points on adjacent waves. ... Standing waves

The wavelength of a sinusoidal standing wave is equal to the square of the distance between its nodes, which are stationary locations with no motion. Three standing waves are shown in a box in the upper illustration.

Since speed is known to be precisely proportional to both wavelength and frequency, it follows that if either one rises, the other would as well.

FORMULA: speed=wavelength×frequency

According to our calculations, a whole sine wave's average value is zero. The average value of a complete sinusoidal wave is zero because the amount of current in the positive and negative cycles is equal and flows in the opposite directions, neutralizing the impact.

Key Concept: A vector known as a phasor in the complex plane can be used to describe a sinusoidal signal. A phasor F=Aej, which is a vector in the complex plane with length A and an angle measured in the anticlockwise direction, may be used to represent a sinusoidal signal with the equation f(t)=Acos(t+) by a sinusoidal signal.
