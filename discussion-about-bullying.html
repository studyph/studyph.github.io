1. problem to be studied of discussed about cyber-bullying​


Answer:

Inconsistent findings and inflated claims regarding incidence, growth through time, and impacts characterize cyberbullying research. To create a useful and coherent body of knowledge, it is necessary to reach some level of agreement on the phenomenon's definition as a concept and to measure cyberbullying in a "bullying context," which will help to ensure that findings on cyberbullying are not mixed up with findings on general cyberaggression or cyberharassment. We propose that cyberbullying be classified as a subclass or specialized kind of bullying, alongside verbal, physical, and indirect/relational forms of bullying.

8. 1. Discuss the different strategies used when dealing with bullying/bullies.​


Answer:

1. Teach Kids to Stand Up for Themselves-

One of the best ways to prevent bullying in schools is to teach kids how to stand up for themselves. This means teaching them how to react if a bully ever confronts them. Kids need to know that they have the right to defend themselves and that it’s okay to be assertive.

2. Teach Them How to Make Friends-

It’s also important to teach kids how to make friends. This can help them avoid being targeted by bullies in the first place. When kids have a strong social support system, they’re less likely to be targeted by bullies.

3. Help Them Develop a Positive Self-Image-

Another essential way to prevent bullying in schools is to help kids develop a positive self-image. Kids who feel good about themselves are less likely to be targets of bullying in schools. Parents need to encourage their children and help them feel good about themselves. One way to do this is to praise their accomplishments, no matter how small. Another way is to help them find activities that they’re passionate about and make them feel good about themselves. When kids have a positive self-image, they’re less likely to be targeted by bullies.

4. Teach Them How to Respond to Bullying in schools-

Another essential way to prevent bullying in schools is to teach kids how to respond to it. If your child is being bullied, they need to know what to do. The first step is to tell someone they trust, like a parent or teacher. Then, they can develop a plan to stand up to the bully or avoid them altogether. If your child witnesses bullying in schools, they can also play a role in stopping it. They can stand up for the victim, talk to a trusted adult, or even tell the bully to stop. Bystanders play a critical role in bullying prevention.

5. Teach your child how to deal with cyberbullying-

Cyberbullying is a type of bullying that occurs online or through electronic devices. It can be very damaging and scary for kids, and it’s essential to teach them how to deal with it. If your child is being cyberbullied, they should tell someone they trust immediately.

15. Theory about bullying?theory about bullying and studies?theory about bullying and learning?theory about the relationship of bullying and academic performance?


One theory about bullying is that it is a learned behavior that is reinforced by positive outcomes such as increased social status or control over others.

Studies have shown that children who witness or experience bullying may develop mental health issues such as anxiety, depression, and PTSD. They also have shown that children who bully others may have a lack of empathy, difficulty regulating their emotions, and a desire for power and control.

Another theory about bullying and learning is that it can negatively impact a student's ability to focus and succeed academically. Students who are bullied may have difficulty paying attention in class, completing assignments, and achieving their academic goals.

Research also suggests that there is a relationship between bullying and academic performance. Students who are bullied may have lower grades, test scores, and graduation rates than those who are not bullied. Additionally, students who bully others may also have lower academic performance due to disciplinary actions or the negative effects of bullying on their own mental health.
