1. The formula for determining the number of games and byes for single elimination is 2^n - 1, where n is the number of teams. For double elimination, it is 2^(n+1) - 2, and for round-robin, it is n*(n-1)/2.

2. The advantages of single elimination are that it is simple and quick, with only one team emerging as the champion. However, it does not allow for redemption or second chances for teams that lose early on. Double elimination provides a second chance for teams that lose in the first round, but it may require more time and resources to complete. Round-robin ensures that each team plays against all other teams, which can be fairer, but it may require more time and can be more complex to schedule.

3. As a sports manager, I would use single elimination for shorter tournaments with fewer teams where time and resources are limited. I would use double elimination for larger tournaments where more teams are involved and a second chance is desirable. Round-robin would be appropriate for smaller tournaments with more time available and where fairness and equity are priorities.

12. 3. If there are 6 teams in a volleyball tournament during the School Intramural Day and each team must play every other team in the elimination round. How many elimination games will there be? b. 12 d. 18a. 9c. 15​


Answer:

d. 18

Step-by-step explanation:

The number of ways to choose 2 teams out of 6 is given by the combination formula:

C(6,2) = 6! / (2! * (6-2)!) = 15

So there are 15 possible games between the 6 teams in the elimination round.

However, each game involves 2 teams, so we must divide the total number of games by 2 to get the number of unique games:

15 / 2 = 7.5

Since we can't have half a game, we round up to the nearest whole number to get the final answer:

There will be 8 elimination games
