The SIMPLE PRESENT TENSE is the grammar used to express everyday habits, including routine activities and general statements of fact.

If you want to express something, a situation that has always happened or used to happen, both in the past, present and future, then this tense is what you should use.

In the simple present tense there is usually an adverb related to time as a complement that the activity is something that is often/always, but can also be rarely/never done.

Negative Sentence

I/You/They/We + Do not + VerbShe/He/It + Does not + Verb (s/es)

PRESENT CONTINUOUS/PRESENT PROGRESSIVE TENSE is a grammar that is used to express an activity that is happening/taking place at the moment.

The activity is happening or in progress (hence it is also called the present progressive) when you speak of it.

Activities started in the past, are currently in progress, and may still take place in the future or it is unknown when they will end.

Negative Sentence

Subject + To be (am/is/are) + Not + Verb (ing)

I + Am not + Verb (ing)You/They/We + Are + Not + Verb (ing)She/He/It + Is + Not + Verb (ing)

The negative form of the two structures above can also be shortened to: don't, doesn't, aren't, and isn't.
