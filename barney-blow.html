Jay Barney developed the resource based view of the firm, which is a strategic management theory designed to explain why some firms perform better than others even when they occupy a very similar business environment.

The resource-based view seeks to explain why some firms perform better than others by looking to the firms’ resources. This contrasts with earlier perspectives, such as Porter’s five forces, which focus on the external environment as sources of threats and opportunities.

The core idea behind the resource-based view is that competitive advantage comes from a firm’s effective use of tangible and intangible resources or assets. 19. Verify that the following arguments are valid and classify according to the type of valid argument form. (10 points) a) If it rains, I will sleep. I did not sleep. Therefore, it did not rain. b) If it’s a reptile, then it’s cold-blooded. Barney is not cold-blooded. Therefore, Barney is not a reptile.


Answer:

a) This argument is not valid. The conclusion that it did not rain does not necessarily follow from the premise that "If it rains, I will sleep" and the fact that "I did not sleep." There are other possible reasons why the speaker did not sleep, such as being too busy or having insomnia, that are unrelated to whether it rained or not. This argument is an example of the fallacy of denying the consequent.

b) This argument is valid. The premise "If it’s a reptile, then it’s cold-blooded" establishes a necessary condition for being a reptile. The fact that "Barney is not cold-blooded" negates this necessary condition, which allows us to conclude that "Barney is not a reptile." This argument is an example of the valid deductive argument form known as modus tollens.
