Answer:

1. Digital divide

The digital divide is the unequal access to digital technology, including smartphones, tablets, laptops, and the internet. The digital divide's effects can be measured based on an individual's amount of time spent on the internet and skill of conducting activity online.

2. Internet addiction is when a person has a compulsive need to spend a great deal of time on the Internet, to the point where other areas of life (such as relationships, work or health) are allowed to suffer.

3. Cyberbullying

Cyberbullying or cyberharassment is a form of bullying or harassment using electronic means. Cyberbullying and cyberharassment are also known as online bullying. It has become increasingly common, especially among teenagers, as the digital sphere has expanded and technology has advanced.

4. What Is the Digital Divide? The digital divide in education is the gap between those with sufficient knowledge of and access to technology and those without, according to the ACT Center for Equity in Learning. To examine the divide requires looking at who can connect to what and how they do so.

What I know about basketball:

Basketball is a team sport that is played on a rectangular court with two hoops at either end.

The objective of the game is to shoot the ball into the opposing team's hoop and score points.

Each team has five players on the court at a time, and the game is divided into four quarters.

There are various positions in basketball, including point guard, shooting guard, small forward, power forward, and center.

Basketball has a rich history, with the NBA being one of the most popular professional sports leagues in the world.

What I want to know about basketball:

What are some common strategies used in basketball?

How are players evaluated and scouted for college and professional teams?

What are some common injuries and how can they be prevented?

How has the game evolved over time, and what impact have rule changes had on the sport?

What I have learned about basketball:

There are many different strategies that teams can use to succeed, such as the pick and roll, fast break, and zone defense.

Players are evaluated based on a variety of factors, including skill level, physical attributes, and mental toughness.

Common basketball injuries include ankle sprains, knee injuries, and concussions, and they can often be prevented through proper conditioning and training.

The game has evolved significantly over time, with changes to rules and equipment leading to faster, more high-scoring games.

What I know about baseball:

Baseball is a bat-and-ball game played between two teams of nine players each.

The objective of the game is to score runs by hitting the ball and running around a series of four bases.

There are two main leagues in professional baseball: the American League and the National League.

Baseball has a rich history, with many legendary players and teams.

What I want to know about baseball:

What are some common strategies used in baseball, such as pitching and batting techniques?

How are players evaluated and scouted for college and professional teams?

What impact have rule changes had on the sport?

How has baseball's popularity changed over time, and what factors have contributed to these changes?

What I have learned about baseball:

Pitching and batting techniques can vary greatly depending on the player and situation, with different types of pitches and swings being used to gain an advantage.

Players are evaluated based on a variety of factors, including physical abilities, mental toughness, and statistics.

Rule changes have had a significant impact on the game over time, with changes to the strike zone, designated hitter rule, and instant replay all affecting gameplay.

Baseball's popularity has fluctuated over time, with factors such as media coverage, fan demographics, and league expansion all playing a role.
