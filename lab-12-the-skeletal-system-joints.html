11. PARTS OF SKELETAL, DESCRIPTION SYSTEM 1. Breastbone 2. Cartilage 3. Ligaments 4. Tendons 5. Joints​


1. Breastbone

The sternum (or breastbone) is a flat bone located at the anterior aspect of the thorax. It lies in the midline of the chest and has a 'T' shape. As part of the bony thoracic wall, the sternum helps protect the internal thoracic viscera – such as the heart, lungs and oesophagus.

2. Cartilage

This smooth and flexible substance covers the tips of your bones where they meet. It enables bones to move without friction (rubbing against each other).

3. Ligaments

A ligament is a dense, white band of fibrous elastic tissue. Ligaments connect the ends of bones together in order to form a joint. These help to limit joint dislocation and restrict improper hyperextension and hyperflexion. Also made of fibrous tissue are bursae.

4. Tendons

A tendon is a tough, flexible band made of fibrous connective tissue, and functions to connect muscle to bone.

5. Joints

Joints are where two bones meet. They make the skeleton flexible — without them, movement would be impossible. Joints allow our bodies to move in many ways.
