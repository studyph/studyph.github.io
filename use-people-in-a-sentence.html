4. What is the gerund used in this sentence? Explain its use. Take time getting used to refusing people’s requests.


A gerund is a word that is written like a verb as it ends with -ing but is functioning as a noun.

Given Sentence:

Take time getting used to refusing people’s requests.

In order to identify the gerund used in a sentence, let's get the words that ends with -ing. In the given sentence, there are two, which are getting and refusing. In this sentence, getting is verb and refusing is part of the phrase refusing people's requests that acts as the subject. Therefore, the gerund in this sentence is refusing.

30. How can use of proper sentence make people good communicators?


The usage of proper sentences is essential in conversations because it prevents misinterpretation and other such problems. No one wants to speak with a person you can't fully understand. Simple grammar or pronunciation mistakes can change the meaning of a sentence entirely. Therefore, if you use proper, grammatically correct sentences, you make people understand more of what you're saying. People will be attracted to that kind of "proffessional" way of speech, which will make you pretty popular as well.
