2. Khibe Beructie B. Tyes Lesson 4 Basic Geometric Terms and Lines in a Plane Most Essential Learning Competencies Recognize and draw a point, line, line T​


Answer:

Sure, I'd be happy to help you with that!

A point is a basic geometric element that has no size or dimension, and it is represented by a dot. It is usually named using a capital letter.

A line is a straight path that extends infinitely in both directions. It is represented by a straight line with arrowheads at each end. It is named using any two points on the line, with a line symbol placed above the letters representing the points. For example, a line passing through points A and B would be written as "line AB."

A line segment is a part of a line that has two endpoints. It is represented by a straight line with a segment symbol above the letters representing the endpoints. For example, a line segment between points A and B would be written as "segment AB."

A ray is a part of a line that has one endpoint and extends infinitely in one direction. It is represented by a straight line with an endpoint at one end and an arrowhead at the other end. It is named using its endpoint and another point on the ray. For example, a ray with endpoint A and passing through point B would be written as "ray AB."

A perpendicular line is a line that intersects another line at a 90-degree angle. It is represented by a small square at the intersection point. It is named using the letters representing the points where the two lines intersect. For example, a perpendicular line passing through points A and B would be written as "perpendicular to line AB at point C."

WorkDefinition: The product of force and displacement in the direction of the forceCallout in the illustration: The block being lifted off the ground by a forceLink to energy: Work is a means of transferring energy to or from an object

2. Energy

Definition: The ability to do workCallout in the illustration: The raised block that has gained gravitational potential energyLink to work: Energy is transferred to or from an object by doing work on it

3. Kinetic Energy

Definition: The energy an object possesses due to its motionCallout in the illustration: The moving block that has kinetic energyLink to potential energy: Kinetic energy can be converted into potential energy and vice versa

4. Potential Energy

Definition: The energy an object possesses due to its position or configurationCallout in the illustration: The raised block that has gravitational potential energyLink to kinetic energy: Potential energy can be converted into kinetic energy and vice versa

5. Law of Conservation of Energy

Definition: Energy cannot be created or destroyed, only transferred or transformed from one form to anotherCallout in the illustration: The total energy of the system remains constant despite the transfer between kinetic and potential energyLink to work and energy: Work done on a system can change its energy, but the total energy of the system remains constant according to the law of conservation of energy.

These concepts are connected by the idea that energy can be transferred between objects or transformed from one form to another, and that the total amount of energy in a closed system remains constant. Work is a means of transferring energy, while kinetic and potential energy are two forms of energy that can be converted into each other. The law of conservation of energy states that the total amount of energy in a system remains constant, even though it may be transformed from one form to another.
