6. Definition of health and safety in the workplace


Workplace health and safety–also referred to as occupational health and safety–refers to the right of every employee, regardless of industry, to carry out his daily work in a safe environment. There are various laws and legislations that dictate what employers must do to facilitate this, to minimize accidents, injuries and fatalities.

2.in your personal point of view how important is preventive maintenance and safety in the workplace?cite some example.​

Preventive maintenance entails taking the appropriate precautions and actions to avoid accidents or equipment failures before they occur.

Preventive maintenance includes things like doing regular company and equipment inspections, cleaning and lubricating important equipment, and keeping your firm's grounds tidy.

The purpose of preventative maintenance is to avoid equipment failure and reduce the likelihood of accidents before they happen.

Finally, taking some safeguards to guarantee that your business faces little risk allows you and your staff to focus on improving what is currently working rather than trying to fix what is broken.
