21. What is the force (in newton) between a positive charge of 6 microcoulombs and a negative charge of 3 x 10^-4 coulomb if they are 0.5m apart?


Answer:

The force between two charges is given by Coulomb's law:

F = k * q1 * q2 / r^2

where F is the force, k is the Coulomb's constant (9 × 10^9 N·m²/C²), q1 and q2 are the magnitudes of the charges in coulombs, and r is the distance between the charges in meters.

In this case, we have:

q1 = 6 μC = 6 × 10^-6 C (positive charge)

q2 = -3 × 10^-4 C (negative charge)

r = 0.5 m

Substituting these values into the formula, we get:

F = (9 × 10^9 N·m²/C²) * (6 × 10^-6 C) * (-3 × 10^-4 C) / (0.5 m)^2

F = -2.16 × 10^-2 N

Note that the negative sign indicates that the force is attractive (i.e., the charges are of opposite signs). Therefore, the force between the two charges is 2.16 × 10^-2 newtons, or approximately 0.022 N.
