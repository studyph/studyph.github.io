3. analyze the musical elements of the 20th century music​


The musical elements of the 20th century is composed of the following:

Tone Color

In this time, there was less emphasis on the blended sound, individual tone color is clearly heard and percussion played the major role in music.

Harmony

During the 20th century, Polychord was created. Polychord means that there are two chords that are played in the same time. There was also the use of tone cluster, use of Polytonality and use of 2 keys – Bitonality.

Rhythm

In the 20th century, this is one of the most striking elements. Rhythm is used to generate power, drive and excitement. The beats are grouped irregularly and the accented beat comes at an equal time intervals.

Melody

In this century, the melody was based on a wide variety of scales and freely used in all 12 chromatic tones without a tonal center.

The Elements of Music in the 20th century

*Rhythm

*Dynamics

*Melody

*Harmony

*Tone color

*Texture

*Form

RHYTHM

Rhythm -is the element of "TIME" in music. When you tap your foot to the music, you

are "keeping the beat" or following the structural rhythmic pulse of the music.

DYNAMICS

All musical aspects relating to the relative loudness (or quietness) of music fall under

the general element of DYNAMICS.

MELODY

Melody is the LINEAR/HORIZONTAL presentation of pitch (the word used to

describe the highness or lowness of a musical sound).

HARMONY

Harmony is the VERTICALIZATION of pitch. Often, harmony is thought of as the

art of combining pitches into chords (several notes played simultaneously as a

"block").

TEXTURE

Texture refers to the number of individual musical lines (melodies) and the

relationship these lines have to one another.

FORM

The large-scale form of a musical composition can be projected via any

combination of the musical elements previously studied.
