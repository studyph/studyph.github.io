11. Character(s): Who are the characters in the story of A Day in the Country?


A Day in the Country by: Anton Pavlovich ChekhovCharacters:TerentyDanilkaFyoklaSilanty Silitch

Terenty is a homeless cobbler. An old man who is tall with a thin, pock -marked face, very long legs, and bare feet, dressed in a woman's tattered jacket. He took care of Fyokla and Danilka and looks after them as his own children. He might appear careless and aloof in the story but he always find ways to help the kids, see them in the evening while they were asleep, and prepares bread for them to eat in the morning.

Danilka is the older brother of Fyokla but his age is way too young to take care of his sibling Fyokla. He is only 8 and the only thing he could do is to play along while searching for food. This must be the reason why his hand was trapped in the hole of the tree while trying to get the egg of the cuckoo.

Fyokla the little girl seem to be more mature than his brother. At the age of 6 she realized how important his brother is to her that she run for help just to save him. In her heart lies a feeling of yearning for home. She kept company of Terenty and Danilka though she is exhausted already.

Silanty Silitch is the sacristan who is also Terenty's bosom friend.
