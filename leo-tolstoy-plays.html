Critique about "The Two Brothers" by Leo Tolstoy

In his short novella "The Two Brothers," Leo Tolstoy describes the lives of two brothers, one of them is a wealthy landowner and the other a destitute peasant.

The value of familial ties is discussed along with issues of greed and envy in the novel.

The language of "The Two Brothers" by Tolstoy is generally interesting and well-written, with vivid depictions of the two brothers' divergent lifestyles. The narrative skillfully highlights the value of forgiveness and reconciliation as well as the damaging effects of greed and envy on family connections.

The younger brother's hatred and rage result in disastrous outcomes as the narrative develops to a satisfying conclusion.

The narrative does, however, contain certain flaws. For starters, the characters are not very well-developed, making it challenging for the reader to understand their intentions and sentiments. The characters seem to be more like archetypes, expressing specific features or characteristics, rather than completely fleshed-out persons.

Additionally, because Tolstoy is notorious for being didactic in his writing, some readers could find the moral lesson of the narrative to be a little heavy-handed.

Despite its weaknesses, "The Two Brothers" is nonetheless a well-written and thought-provoking novel that gives useful insights into human nature and the value of family connections.

It may be particularly relevant for readers who are interested in examining themes of avarice, envy, and forgiveness, and who admire works of classic literature.

19. write a critical analysis of the story two brothers by Leo Tolstoypahelp​


Russian short story analysis

"Two Brothers" is a short story written by the famous Russian writer Leo Tolstoy. The story explores themes such as family, forgiveness, and the consequences of one's actions.

The story revolves around two brothers, Ivan and Dmitry, who were once close but became estranged due to a disagreement over the ownership of a piece of land. Ivan, the elder brother, is wealthy and successful, while Dmitry is poor and struggles to make a living. The two brothers have not spoken to each other for years, but one day, Dmitry falls ill, and Ivan comes to visit him. While Ivan is there, he realizes the true extent of his brother's suffering and comes to understand the pain that he caused his brother by taking the land that Dmitry believed was rightfully his.

The central theme of the story is forgiveness. Tolstoy shows that forgiveness can be a difficult and painful process but is ultimately necessary for healing and reconciliation. Ivan's visit to Dmitry shows that he has come to realize the error of his ways and is willing to ask for forgiveness. Dmitry, on the other hand, is initially hesitant to forgive his brother, but eventually does so after reflecting on the past and understanding the reasons behind Ivan's actions.

Another significant theme in the story is the consequences of one's actions. Ivan's greed and desire for wealth caused him to take the land that his brother believed was rightfully his. This action led to the estrangement between the two brothers and caused Dmitry a great deal of pain and suffering. However, Ivan's realization of his mistake and his willingness to seek forgiveness shows that it is possible to make amends for past wrongs.

Tolstoy's writing style in "Two Brothers" is straightforward and accessible, making the story easy to follow and understand. He uses simple language and straightforward dialogue to convey the emotions and thoughts of the characters, creating a sense of authenticity and realism in the story.

Overall, "Two Brothers" is a powerful story about forgiveness, reconciliation, and the consequences of one's actions. It teaches us that forgiveness is necessary for healing and that it is never too late to make amends for past wrongs. Tolstoy's simple yet powerful writing style makes the story accessible to a wide audience, and its themes are universal and relevant to people of all backgrounds and cultures.

23. Literary Critique about "God Sees the Truth, But Waits" by Leo Tolstoy.​


GOD SEES THE TRUTH, BUT WAITS

God Sees the Truth, But Waits is a short story that was written and published by a Russian Author Leo Tolstoy in 1872. Leo Tolstoy was one of the greatest authors of all time and won many Nobel Peace prizes in literature in the early 20th century, with also the commandment of another of one of his greatest works, which is “War and Peace”.  

The short story starts with the protagonist Ivan Dmitrich Aksionov ahd he is a handsome and an outgoing guy because he was full of vices back then until he had a wife. One summer day he planned to go to Nizhny Fair and he continued his journey despite his wifes warning that he must not go to that place because of a bad dream. He went to his destination, met a merchant along his way soon to be an acquaintance and slept together in an inn. A day after while he was walking, two soldiers arrested him because he was accused of killing the merchant.

The title “God Sees the Truth, But Waits”, tell’s that proving your innocence is easier said than to be done because sometimes the law really dictates someone even without justifications but at the same time it evokes the feeling to the readers that at the end of the day, whether someone would be proven guilty or no guilty, God is the only one who knows the truth and our actions would always judge us if in case we know the event on the first place, will wait for us to speak the right works. It encourages us to be religious and faith is the most powerful weapon for it makes even the worst of all situations get well. Despite all strong beliefs by God, Aksinov was still arrested without concrete evidence. Soon enough he discovered the real killer on the time he was in prison which was Makar Seymonich.  

He was angry that time and that is because of the broken justice system that he had faced. Being painfully profounded in a cell and knowing that your relatives would die after your release is one of life’s disappointments. It was believed that only the richest and influential people can manage to taste less of the punishments of the law, it shows the relationship of people in which they have different social statuses, particularly upper and lower class people.

The plot itself has one of the most unusual parts where the protagonist has a very strong sense of resilience. He forgave Makar Semyonich because of what he had done. Forgiving is a humane thing to do as all of us people aren’t perfect and we make bad choices all throughout our lives but forgiving somebody because of a heinous crime symbolizes that people who have a strong belief in God would always forgive. It teaches a philosophical question and they have to discover what reality is (R. Kuli, 2020). Our future is very inevitable, if that is really your fate when it is good or bad we should always accept it. It is part of God’s Plan assuming Aksionov actions are a part of it. Until his death, he still believed and prayed to God during his prison sentence.

To conclude, Tolstoy's work did a good job on all parts, especially it is direct and it shows how life in Russia was before back then there was almost no independence of all countries and human rights were still disputed. Although it expresses a very nice and straight forward lesson, the lessons must not be done in this type of context as the modern era comes, forgiving may also be one of the worst decisions that man ever has to make. The reality is forgiving someone is very contradicting to justice and all sinners must learn the hard way.
