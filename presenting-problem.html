11. reflection presenting the statement of the problem​


Answer:

The five key elements of a problem statement are the topic, the research problem, justification for the importance of the problem, deficiencies in knowledge about the problem, and audiences that may benefit. The topic is the general subject area of the study. The research problem is the specific issue or concern that needs studying. Justification for the importance of the problem is where the author provides evidence from the literature review on why the problem needs to be studied. Deficiencies in knowledge about the problem could be lack of knowledge about the problem, it could be conflicting evidence about the problem, it could be a need to validate a study, or it could be a need to improve current practice. Audiences that may benefit from the topic could include researchers, healthcare workers, patients, or anyone else who may benefit from this research.

It is important for researchers to justify a problem in a study in order for readers to understand why the specific research and outcomes are important. If they can’t justify the importance of the problem, then nobody is going to care or change their ways.
