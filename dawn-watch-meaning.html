15. The dawn of neutrophilia pertains to the:


Answer:

secondary granules

Explanation:

At the "dawn" of neutrophilia, these secondary granules are most obvious in the golgi area. As the cell matures closer to a metamyelocyte, they fill the entire cytoplasm. While the cytoplasm shifts to producing secondary granules it also looses the prominence of its primary granules.
