1. what is the definition of budgeting?


Answer:

noun

a statement of the financial position of an administration (as of a nation) for a definite period of time based on estimates of expenditures during the period and proposals for financing them

verb

to put or allow for in a statement or plan coordinating resources and expenditures : to put or allow for in a budget

adjective

suitable for someone whose is following a plan for coordinating resources and expenditures : suitable for one on a budget (see BUDGET

In 2004, Emerald Avenue in Ortigas Center, where the old World Bank office was located, started to wind down after 9 PM. Finding a place to buy a midnight snack whenever I did overtime was hard. It was also hard to find a taxi after work.

Today, even at 3 AM, the street is bustling with 24-hour restaurants, coffee shops, and convenience stores, hundreds of BPO (Business Process Outsourcing) employees taking their break, and a line of taxis waiting to bring these new middle class earners home. 24. 2. Give two (2) sources of income and give the definition of each one. 3. Give two (2) components of a family budget that you prioritize at home and discuss how does your family allocate budget for those.


Answer:

(1). Two sources of income are earned income and passive income.

•Earned income is money that is earned through employment or self-employment, such as wages, salaries, and tips.

•Passive income is money that is earned through investments or other activities that require little to no ongoing effort, such as rental income, dividends, and interest.

(2).Two components of a family budget that may be prioritized are housing and food.

•Housing expenses, such as mortgage or rent payments, property taxes, and insurance, may be a top priority for a family. Families often allocate a significant portion of their budget towards these expenses.

•Food expenses, such as groceries and dining out, may also be a priority for a family. Families may allocate a certain amount of their budget each month for these expenses, and may also look for ways to save money on food, such as by planning meals in advance or shopping at discount stores.
