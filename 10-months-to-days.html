1. 10 days as part of a month?​


Answer:

5/14 ; 10/29 ; 1/3 ; 10/31

Step-by-step explanation:

In this question, we are asked to express 10 days as a fraction if we divide it by the length of a month.

The count of days per month varies. Because of this, we have 4 scenarios depending on which month we pick.

A. The month has 28 days.

This happens during the month February of a non-leap year.

10 becomes the numerator and 28 becomes the denominator. Thus, we have the initial fraction 10/28. We need to reduce it to its simplest form, so we need to find their Greatest Common Factor (GCF) first.

The factors of 10 are 1, 2, 5, and 10. The factors of 28 are 1, 2, 4, 7, 14, and 28. Looking at this list, we can observe that the GCF of 10 and 28 is 2.

We now need to divide 10 and 28 by 2. Doing so, the final fraction will be 5/14.

B. The month has 29 days.

This happens during the month February of a leap year.

Doing the same process, we have the fraction 10/29. Since these two are already relatively prime, we can no longer simplify the fraction further.

C. The month has 30 days.

This happens during the months April, June, September, and November.

Again doing the same process, we will get the fraction 10/30. Since they are not relatively prime, we need to reduce it again into its simplest form.

The factors of 10 are 1, 2, 5, and 10. The factors of 30 are 1, 2, 3, 5, 6, 10, 15, and 30. Looking at these lists, the GCF is 10.

Using this, we get the final fraction 1/3.

D. The month has 31 days.

This happens during the months January, March, May, July, August, October, and December.

Again doing the same process, we have the fraction 10/31. Since they are already relatively prime, we cannot simplify further.

Therefore, there are 4 possible answers to this question: 5/14 ; 10/29 ; 1/3 ; 10/31.
