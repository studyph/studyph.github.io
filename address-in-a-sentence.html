In Japan or Sweden they can expect to live more than 80 years; in Brazil, 72 years; India, 63 years; and in one of several African countries, fewer than 50 years. 29. 3-5 sentences knowledge how you can address the intercultural communication​


Answer:

1. Repeat or confirm what you think was being said.

2. Pay attention to nonverbal communication.

3. Speak slowly and clearly.

Explanation:

1. It can be helpful to go back and re-assure them that you have explained what the objective of the conversation was.

2. Pay attention to nonverbal cues such as intonation, eye contact, and posture. Certain cultures avoid strong eye contact when speaking so don't try to force it if you are not used to doing so.

3. Speaking slowly and clearly is often interpreted as being confident. This will help you avoid mistakes and seeming nervous. Also, taking time to think before you speak can help you to avoid embarrassing sounding offs or mispronouncing word-spelt words.
