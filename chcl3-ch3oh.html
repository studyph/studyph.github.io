2. 7. A solution is made containing 14.6 g of CH3OH in 184 g of H2O. Calculate (a) the mole fraction of CH3OH, (b) the mass percent of CH3OH, (c) the molality of CH3OH.


(a) SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & mass_{\text{solute}} = \text{14.6 g} \\ & MM_{\text{solute}} = \text{32.042 g/mol} \\ & mass_{\text{solvent}} = \text{184 g} \\ & MM_{\text{solvent}} = \text{18.015 g/mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of each component.

• For solute (CH₃OH)

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{mass_{\text{solute}}}{MM_{\text{solute}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{14.6 g}}{\text{32.042 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.45565 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

• For solvent (water)

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_{\text{solvent}} & = \frac{mass_{\text{solvent}}}{MM_{\text{solvent}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{184 g}}{\text{18.015 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{10.214 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the mole fraction of solute.

[tex]\begin{aligned} X_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{n_{\text{solute}}}{n_{\text{solute}} + n_{\text{solvent}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.45565 mol}}{\text{0.45565 mol + 10.214 mol}} \\ & = \boxed{0.0427} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the mole fraction of CH₃OH is 0.0427.

------------------------------------------------------------

(b) SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & mass_{\text{solute}} = \text{14.6 g} \\ & mass_{\text{solvent}} = \text{184 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the mass percent of solute (CH₃OH)

[tex]\begin{aligned} \%(m/m) & = \frac{mass_{\text{solute}}}{mass_{\text{solute}} + mass_{\text{solvent}}} \times 100\% \\ & = \frac{\text{14.6 g}}{\text{14.6 g + 184 g}} \times 100\% \\ & = \boxed{7.35\%} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the mass percent of CH₃OH is 7.35%.

------------------------------------------------------------

(c) SOLUTION:

Step 1: List the given values.

[tex]\begin{aligned} & mass_{\text{solute}} = \text{14.6 g} \\ & MM_{\text{solute}} = \text{32.042 g/mol} \\ & mass_{\text{solvent}} = \text{184 g = 0.184 kg} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of solute.

[tex]\begin{aligned} n_{\text{solute}} & = \frac{mass_{\text{solute}}}{MM_{\text{solute}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{14.6 g}}{\text{32.042 g/mol}} \\ & = \text{0.45565 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the molality of solution.

[tex]\begin{aligned} m & = \frac{n_{\text{solute}}}{mass_{\text{solvent}}} \\ & = \frac{\text{0.45565 mol}}{\text{0.184 kg}} \\ & = \text{2.48 mol/kg} \\ & = \boxed{2.48 \: m} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the molality of the solution is 2.48 m.

9. 2. A solution is made containing 14.6 g CH2OH in 184 g H20. Calculate(a) the mole fraction of CH3OH.(b) the mass percent of CH3OH,(c) the molality of CH3OH.​


Given:

mass of solute = 14.6 g

mass of solvent = 184 g

solute: CH₃OH

solvent: H₂O

Required:

mole fraction of solute

mass percent

molality

Solution: (a)

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of each component.

For solute

molar mass of solute = (12.01 g/mol × 1) + (1.008 g/mol × 4) + (16.00 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of solute = 32.042 g/mol

For solvent

molar mass of solvent = (1.008 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of solvent = 18.016 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of each component.

For solute

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{molar mass of solute}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute} = \frac{\text{14.6 g}}{\text{32.042 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solute = 0.45565196 mol}[/tex]

For solvent

[tex]\text{moles of solvent} = \frac{\text{mass of solvent}}{\text{molar mass of solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solvent} = \frac{\text{184 g}}{\text{18.016 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{moles of solvent = 10.2131439 mol}[/tex]

Step 3: Calculate the mole fraction of solute.

[tex]\text{mole fraction of solute} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{moles of solute + moles of solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{mole fraction of solute} = \frac{\text{0.45565196 mol}}{\text{0.45565196 mol + 10.2131439 mol}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mole fraction of solute} = 0.0427}[/tex]

Solution: (b)

Step 1: Calculate the mass percent of solute by direct substitution.

[tex]\text{mass percent} = \frac{\text{mass of solute}}{\text{mass of solute + mass of solvent}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\text{mass percent} = \frac{\text{14.6 g}}{\text{14.6 g + 184 g}} × 100[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass percent} = 7.35\%}[/tex]

Solution: (c)

Step 1: Calculate the molality of solution by using direct substitution.

Since the moles of solute is already obtained in Step 2 for solving the mole fraction of solute, which is 0.45565196 mol, and the mass of solvent is already given, which is 184 g or 0.184 kg, the molality of the solution is

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{moles of solute}}{\text{mass of solvent (kg)}}[/tex]

[tex]\text{molality} = \frac{\text{0.45565196 mol}}{\text{0.184 kg}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{molality} = 2.48 \: m}[/tex]

10. Chloroform, CHCl3, is a colorless, sweet- smelling liquid that was once used as anesthetic in surgery. CHCl3 may react with chlorine gas, Cl2, to produce carbon tetrachloride, CCl4, and hydrochloric acid, HCl. Compute for the percent yield of the reaction if 36.8 g of CCl4 was recovered when 30.0g of CHCl3 is allowed to react with excess Cl2.


Answer:

percent yield = 95.1%

Explanation:

Write the balanced chemical equation.

CHCl3 + Cl2 → CCl4 + HCl

Compute the theoretical yield.

theoretical yield = 30.0 g CHCl3 × (1 mol CHCl3 / 119.38 g CHCl3) × (1 mol CCl4 / 1 mol CHCl3) × (153.82 g CCl4 / 1 mol CCl4)

theoretical yield = 38.7 g

Solve for the percent yield.

percent yield = (actual yield / theoretical yield) × 100

percent yield = (36.8 g / 38.7 g) × 100

percent yield = 95.1%

18. A solution contains 128 g of CH3OH and 108 g of water.What is the mole of fraction of CH3OH in the solution?​


Answer:

0.400

Explanation:

Step 1: Compute the molecular weights of CH3OH and H2O.

MW CH3OH = 12.01 + 4(1.008) + 16.00 = 32.04 g/mol

MW H2O = 2(1.008) + 16.00 = 18.02 g/mol

Step 2: Compute the number of moles of each compound.

n CH3OH = 128 g / 32.04 g/mol = 4.00 mol

n H2O = 108 g / 18.02 g/mol = 5.99 mol

Step 3: Solve for the mole fraction of CH3OH.

X CH3OH = n CH3OH / (n CH3OH + n H2O)

X CH3OH = 4.00 mol / (4.00 mol + 5.99 mol)

X CH3OH = 0.400

19. . A solution contains 128 g of CH3OH and 108 g of water. What is the mole fraction of CH3OH in the solution?​


Strategy:

The mole fraction is a dimensionless quantity that expresses the ratio of the number of moles of one component to the number of moles of all components present. In general, the mole fraction of component [tex]i[/tex] in a mixture or solution is given by

[tex]X_{i} = \frac{n_{i}}{n_{\text{T}}}[/tex]

where [tex]n_{i}[/tex] and [tex]n_{\text{T}}[/tex] are the number of moles of component [tex]i[/tex] and the total number of moles present, respectively.

Pointers to Remember:

Mole fraction is dimensionless, meaning, it has no unit of measurement.Mole fraction is always less than one.The sum of mole fractions of all components is always equal to one.If the given quantity is mass, convert it to moles using the molar mass of the component of interest.If the given quantity is percent by mass, always assume that the mass of the solution is 100 g. Then, determine the mass of each component.

[tex]\\[/tex]

The solute is CH3OH and the solvent is H2O.

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of each component.

For solute

[tex]\text{molar mass}_{solute} = \text{32.042 g/mol}[/tex]

For solvent

[tex]\text{molar mass}_{solvent} =[/tex](1.008 g/mol × 2) + (16.00 g/mol × 1)

[tex]\text{molar mass}_{solvent} = \text{18.016 g/mol}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of each component.

For solute

[tex]n_{solute} = \frac{\text{mass}_{solute}}{\text{molar mass}_{solute}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{solute} = \frac{\text{128 g}}{\text{32.042 g/mol}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{solute} = \text{3.994757 mol}[/tex]

For solvent

[tex]n_{solvent} = \frac{\text{mass}_{solvent}}{\text{molar mass}_{solvent}}[/tex]

[tex]n_{solvent} = \frac{\text{108 g}}{\text{18.016 g/mol}}[/tex]

Step 4: Calculate the mole fraction of solute.

[tex]X_{solute} = \frac{n_{solute}}{n_{solute} \: + \: n_{solvent}}[/tex]
