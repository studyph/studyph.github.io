15. In a chemical reaction, Iron (II) chloride is mixed with sodium with sodium phosphate forming iron (II) phosphate and sodium chloride.3 FeCl2 + 2 Na3PO4 -> Fe3 (PO4)2 + 6NaClIf 23 grams of iron (II) chloride reacts with 41 grams of sodium phosphate,a. What is the limiting reagant?b. How much sodium chloride can be formed?c. How much of the excess reagent remains when this reaction has gone to completion?​


a. SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the number of moles of NaCl formed by each reactant.

• Using FeCl₂

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 3 moles of FeCl₂ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 6 moles of NaCl.

The molar mass of FeCl₂ is 126.745 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of NaCl} & = \text{23 g} \: \text{FeCl}_2 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{FeCl}_2}{\text{126.745 g} \: \text{FeCl}_2} \times \frac{\text{6 mol NaCl}}{\text{3 mol} \: \text{FeCl}_2} \\ & = \text{0.362933 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

• Using Na₃PO₄

Based on the balanced chemical equation, 2 moles of Na₃PO₄ is stoichiometrically equivalent to 6 moles of NaCl.

The molar mass of Na₃PO₄ is 163.94 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{moles of NaCl} & = \text{41 g} \: \text{Na}_3\text{PO}_4 \times \frac{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Na}_3\text{PO}_4}{\text{163.94 g} \: \text{Na}_3\text{PO}_4} \times \frac{\text{6 mol NaCl}}{\text{2 mol} \: \text{Na}_3\text{PO}_4} \\ & = \text{0.750274 mol} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 3: Determine the limiting reagent.

Since FeCl₂ produced less amount of NaCl than Na₃PO₄,

[tex]\boxed{\text{FeCl}_2 \: \text{is the limiting reagent.}}[/tex]

------------------------------------------------------------

b. SOLUTION:

The mass of a product that can be formed is dictated by the limiting reagent. In this case, we will start at the number of moles of NaCl formed from the limiting reagent (FeCl₂) which is equal to 0.362933 mol.

The molar mass of NaCl is 58.44 g/mol.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of NaCl} & = \text{0.362933 mol NaCl} \times \frac{\text{58.44 g NaCl}}{\text{1 mol NaCl}} \\ & = \boxed{\text{21 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the mass of NaCl that can be formed is 21 g.

------------------------------------------------------------

c. SOLUTION:

Step 1: Calculate the mass of excess reagent reacted.

We start from the number of moles of NaCl formed by the limiting reagent (FeCl₂) which is equal to 0.362933 mol.

In this case, the excess reagent is Na₃PO₄.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{Na}_3\text{PO}_4 \: \text{reacted} & = \text{0.362933 mol NaCl} \times \frac{\text{2 mol} \: \text{Na}_3\text{PO}_4}{\text{6 mol NaCl}} \times \frac{\text{163.94 g} \: \text{Na}_3\text{PO}_4}{\text{1 mol} \: \text{Na}_3\text{PO}_4} \\ & = \text{20 g} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Step 2: Calculate the mass of excess reagent remaining at the end of reaction.

[tex]\begin{aligned} \text{mass of} \: \text{Na}_3\text{PO}_4 \: \text{remaining} & = \text{initial mass of} \: \text{Na}_3\text{PO}_4 - \text{mass of} \: \text{Na}_3\text{PO}_4 \: \text{reacted} \\ & = \text{41 g} - \text{20 g} \\ & = \boxed{\text{21 g}} \end{aligned}[/tex]

Hence, the mass of excess reagent remaining at the end of reaction is 21 g.

18. If 23 grams of iron(II) chloride reacts with 41 grams of sodium phosphate, what is the limiting reactant? How much sodium chloride can be formed?Balanced Eqn: 3FeCl2 + 2 Na3PO4= Fe3(PO4) +6NaCl​


Given:

mass of FeCl₂ = 23 g

mass of Na₃PO₄ = 41 g

balanced chemical equation: 3FeCl₂ + 2Na₃PO₄ → Fe₃(PO₄)₂ + 6NaCl

Required:

limiting reactant

mass of NaCl

Solution: (limiting reactant)

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of each reactant.

For FeCl₂

molar mass of FeCl₂ = (55.85 g/mol × 1) + (35.45 g/mol × 2)

molar mass of FeCl₂ = 126.75 g/mol

For Na₃PO₄

molar mass of Na₃PO₄ = (22.99 g/mol × 3) + (30.97 g/mol × 1) + (16.00 g/mol × 4)

molar mass of Na₃PO₄ = 163.94 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the number of moles of NaCl produced by each reactant.

Using FeCl₂

Since [tex]\text{3 mol FeCl}_{2} \bumpeq \text{6 mol NaCl}[/tex], the number of moles of NaCl formed is

[tex]\text{moles of NaCl} = \text{23 g FeCl}_{2} \times \frac{\text{1 mol FeCl}_{2}}{\text{126.75 g FeCl}_{2}} \times \frac{\text{6 mol NaCl}}{\text{3 mol FeCl}_{2}}[/tex]

moles of NaCl = 0.3629191 mol

Using Na₃PO₄

Since [tex]\text{2 mol Na}_{3}\text{PO}_{4} \bumpeq \text{6 mol NaCl}[/tex], the number of moles of NaCl formed is

[tex]\text{moles of NaCl} = \text{41 g Na}_{3}\text{PO}_{4} \times \frac{\text{1 mol Na}_{3}\text{PO}_{4}}{\text{163.94 g Na}_{3}\text{PO}_{4}} \times \frac{\text{6 mol NaCl}}{\text{2 mol Na}_{3}\text{PO}_{4}}[/tex]

moles of NaCl = 0.750 mol

Step 3: Determine the limiting reactant.

Since FeCl₂ produces less number of moles of NaCl

[tex]\boxed{\text{FeCl}_{2} \: \text{is the limiting reactant.}}[/tex]

Solution: (mass of NaCl)

Step 1: Calculate the molar mass of NaCl.

molar mass of NaCl = (22.99 g/mol × 1) + (35.45 g/mol × 1)

molar mass of NaCl = 58.44 g/mol

Step 2: Calculate the mass of NaCl formed.

Since FeCl₂ is the limiting reactant, the mass of NaCl formed is

[tex]\text{mass of NaCl} = \text{0.3629191 mol NaCl} \times \frac{\text{58.44 g NaCl}}{\text{1 mol NaCl}}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\text{mass of NaCl = 21 g}}[/tex]

20. what occurs when a phosphate- phosphate bond in an ATP molecule is broken?​


Answer:

When one phosphate group is removed by breaking a phosphoanhydride bond in a process called hydrolysis, energy is released, and ATP is converted to adenosine diphosphate (ADP). Likewise, energy is also released when a phosphate is removed from ADP to form adenosine monophosphate (AMP).
