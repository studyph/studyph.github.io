15. A.Poured 1 tablespoon of salt to 1 glass of water and stir.B.Mixed 3 stick of coffee powder in 1 small cup of water and stir.C.Add 5 tablespoon of sugar in cup of water and stir.It was too sweet​


Answer:

Homogeneous Mixture (Clear appearance/Unchanged)Homogeneous Mixture (Water becomes dark due to coffee's color)Homogeneous Mixture (Water becomes mildly brown)

Explanation:

There are two main types of mixtures: homogeneous mixtures and heterogeneous mixtures.

A homogeneous mixture is a mixture in which the composition is uniform throughout the mixture. The saltwater described above or at question number 1 is homogeneous because the dissolved salt is evenly distributed throughout the entire saltwater sample. The same concept applies to number 3. Black coffee that doesn't have undissolved materials in question number 2 is a homogeneous mixture because it's the same throughout. The substances are distributed entirely so you cannot distinguish one part from another.

A heterogeneous mixture is a mixture in which the composition is not uniform throughout the mixture. Some examples include salad, oil in water, mixed fruits, ice in soda, cereal in milk.
