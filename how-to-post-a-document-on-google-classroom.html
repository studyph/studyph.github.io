1. Positive aspects of globalization:

a) Increased access to goods and services: Globalization has resulted in greater availability and accessibility of goods and services from different parts of the world. For example, you can find products from different countries in supermarkets and online stores.

b) Increased cultural exchange: Globalization has led to greater cultural exchange between countries, resulting in a better understanding and appreciation of different customs, traditions, and ways of life. For example, you can learn about different cultures by traveling to different countries or through online platforms.

c) Increased economic growth: Globalization has resulted in increased economic growth in many countries, particularly in developing countries. This has led to more job opportunities, higher standards of living, and better access to education and healthcare.

2. Negative aspects of globalization:

a) Unequal distribution of wealth: Globalization has resulted in the concentration of wealth in the hands of a few individuals and corporations, while many people in developing countries continue to live in poverty. This has led to greater income inequality and social unrest in some countries.

b) Environmental degradation: Globalization has led to increased industrialization and consumption, resulting in environmental degradation and pollution. For example, the production and transportation of goods and services have led to increased carbon emissions, contributing to climate change.

c) Cultural homogenization: Globalization has also led to the spread of Western culture and values, resulting in the loss of cultural diversity and identity in some countries. For example, the spread of fast food chains and Western fashion trends has led to the erosion of local cuisine and traditional clothing.

6. write the down(4) parts of google classroom and their function?​


Answer:

Creating a class

When you open Google Classroom, the first thing you'll need to do is create a class. Click the Plus icon in the top-right corner, then select Create class.

￼

This will bring up a dialog box asking if you're planning on using Google Classroom at a school with students. Google Classroom requires schools to use G Suite for Education if their teachers plan to use it in their actual classrooms. This provides teachers and students with more privacy and security measures. If you're using Google Classroom for your own personal use, you won't need to worry about this.

￼

You'll then need to enter the name of your class. There's also the option to enter a Section, Subject, and Room if you're using Google Classroom in a school and want to include this information. When you're done, click Create.

￼

Navigating Google Classroom

Once you've created a class, you'll be taken to that class' page.

Menu

In the Menu, you can access other classes, your calendar, to-do lists, and more.

￼

Stream Tab

The Stream tab is kind of like your class' homepage. You'll find all of your posts here, as well as upcoming work.

￼

Classwork Tab

The Classwork tab is where you'll create, assign, and grade all of the work for your class.

￼

People Tab

In the People tab, you can view and communicate with the students and teachers in your class.

￼

Grades Tab

In the Grades tab, you can see each student's overall grade as well as their grades for individual assignments.

￼

How to collaborate using google drive?​

Google Drive is one of the effective tools for collaboration tasks or work.
