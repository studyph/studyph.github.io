1. Two couples act on the frame. If the resultant couple moment is to be zero, determine the distance d between the 40lb couple forces. ​


Answer: d = 5.54 ft

Step-by-step explanation:

+Clockwise Summation of M = 0

= (40Cos30°)(d) = 34.64lb

= -60lb(4/5)(4) = -192lb

d = -192/34.64

d = 5.54ft (distance between 40lb couple forces)


2. Two couples act on the frame. If the resultant couple moment is to be zero, determine the distance d between the 40lb couple forces. ​


[tex]\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

Couple moment produced by the 60 lb couple forces:

[tex]\mathsf{M_c_1=60(\dfrac{4}{5})(4)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{M_c_1=192 \: \: lb.ft} \: \: \textsf{(cw)}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Couple moment produced by the 40 lb couple forces:

[tex]\mathsf{M_c_2=40cos(30^\circ)(d)} \: \: \textsf{(ccw)}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

The resultant couple moment is given by the equation:

[tex]\mathsf{M_c=M_c_1+M_c_2}[/tex]

Substituting Mc1 and Mc2, we get (cw positive):

[tex]\mathsf{M_c=-40cos(30^\circ)(d)+192}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

Since the resultant couple moment is zero,

[tex]\mathsf{M_c_1=M_c_2}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{40cos(30^\circ)(d)=192}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{d=\dfrac{192}{40cos(30^\circ)}}[/tex]

[tex]\\[/tex]

[tex]\mathbb{ANSWER:}[/tex]

The distance d, is equal to:

[tex]\large{\boxed{\mathsf{d=5.543 \: \: ft}}}[/tex]

30. Given load of P 47 and the length L 52. Calculate the couple moment at point O (in kip.ft).​


[tex]\mathbb{SOLUTION:}[/tex]

Assumption: The moment at point O is counterclockwise.

[tex]\mathsf{{\Sigma}M_O=0;} \: \: \textsf{(clockwise: positive, in lb.ft)}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{\dfrac{1}{2}(47)(52)[\dfrac{2}{3}(52)]-\dfrac{1}{2}(47)(52)[52+\dfrac{1}{3}(52)]-M_O=0}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{-42362.667-M_O=0}[/tex]

[tex]\mathsf{M_O=-42362.667}[/tex]

Since Mo is negative, our assumption is wrong. The direction of Mo is clockwise.

[tex]\mathsf{M_O=42,362.667 \: \: lb.ft}[/tex]

[tex]\mathbb{ANSWER:}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\mathsf{M_O=42.363 \: \: k.ft \: \: (clockwise)}}[/tex]
