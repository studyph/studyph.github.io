30. The length of a rectangle is 2cm longer than twice the width. If the perimeter of the rectangle is 34cm. Find its dimensions.​


✏️RECTANGLE===============================

Problem: The length of a rectangle is 2cm longer than twice the width. If the perimeter of the rectangle is 34cm. Find its dimensions.

Solution: Represent l and w as the length and width of the rectangle respectively. Make two equations of the given statement.

[tex] \begin{cases}l = 2w + 2& \green{(eq. \: 1)} \\ 34 =2(l + w) & \green{(eq. \: 2)}\end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}l = 2w + 2 \\ 17 = l + w \end{cases}[/tex]

- Substitute l from the first equation to the second equation in terms of w.

[tex] \begin{cases}l = 2w + 2 \\ 17 =(2w + 2) + w \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}l = 2w + 2 \\ 17 =2w + 2 + w \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}l = 2w + 2 \\ 17 =3w + 2 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}l = 2w + 2 \\ 17 - 2 =3w \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}l = 2w + 2 \\ 15 =3w \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}l = 2w + 2 \\ \begin{gathered} \frac{15}{3} = \frac{ \cancel{3}w}{ \cancel3} \end{gathered} \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}l = 2w + 2 \\ w = 5 \end{cases}[/tex]

- Thus, the width of the rectangle is 5 cm, Substitute it to the first equation to find the length of the rectangle.

[tex] \begin{cases}l = 2(5) + 2 \\ w = 5 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}l = 10 + 2 \\ w = 5 \end{cases}[/tex]

[tex] \begin{cases}l = 12 \\ w = 5 \end{cases}[/tex]

- Therefore, the dimensions of the rectangle are:

[tex] \large \rm Length = \boxed{ \rm \green{ \: 12 \:cm. \: }}[/tex]

[tex] \large \rm Width = \boxed{ \rm \green{ \: 7 \:cm. \: }}[/tex]===============================
