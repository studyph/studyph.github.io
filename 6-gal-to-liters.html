19. Calculate the volume in liters of sample of alcohol having a mass of 3.5 lb if the density of alcohol is 6.6 lb/gal?​


Answer:

Solving for Volume

The volume of the sample is 2.0 L

Explanation:

To answer the question it requires several processes. Also, the processes will involve the skill of formula transposition and conversion of the unit. The first step will be solving for volume using the given quantities. We need to do formula transposition to derive the formula of volume. After computation, we expect that the volume will be in gallons. Thus, the next step is to convert the unit, gallons to liters.

To give a background about the problem, density is the amount of material in a given volume. Mathematically, it can be expressed using the formula density = mass/volume. To solve for the volume, we do a formula transposition that will result in the formula, volume = mass/density. Substituting the values to the formula, it will be:

volume = [tex]\frac{3.5lb}{6.6 lb/gal}[/tex]

The answer will be 0.53 gallons. However, it is specified in the problem that the volume should be expressed in liters. Thus, there is a need to convert gallons to liters.

It is important to note that when converting units, we have to make use of the conversion factor and multiply this to the original or given value. The conversion factors can be expressed in fraction form. The technique is to cancel units. Thus, the fraction form of conversion should be expressed in a manner that the unit of the original or given unit is found at the denominator. This is the unit that we would want to cancel. Going back to the problem, since the original or given quantity is in gallons, we have to make sure that this unit can be found in our denominator of the conversion factor.

The next question will be how many liters are there in one gallon. This will tell us the conversion factor to use. There are 3.785 liters in 1 gallon so we now have our conversion unit which is [tex]\frac{3.785gal}{1L\\}[/tex].

Now that we have the conversion factor, we can now convert 0.53 gallons to liter. To convert we do the following:

   L= 0.53 gallons x [tex]\frac{3.785 L}{1gal}[/tex]

How did we get this formula? To recall, 0.53 gallons is the answer we get earlier. The fraction is the conversion unit. We multiply this conversion unit so that we can cancel out gallons, leaving us with liters as the unit. Then, substituting the formula will give us 2.00605L. Since the given quantities are expressed in 2 significant figures, we will also express the answer in two significant figures.
