26. A random sample of 60 students is chosen from a large population whose diastolic blood pressures have a standard deviation of 5 mm Hg. If the 60 students gave a mean pressure of 85 mm Hg, compute the 88.12% confidence interval of the mean of the diastolic pressures of all students. ​


To compute the confidence interval, we can use the formula:

CI = x̄ ± z*(σ/√n)

where:

x̄ = sample mean = 85 mm Hg

z = z-score corresponding to the level of confidence, which can be found using a standard normal distribution table or calculator. For 88.12% confidence level, z = 1.51 (approximately).

σ = population standard deviation = 5 mm Hg

n = sample size = 60

Substituting the values, we get:

CI = 85 ± 1.51*(5/√60)

= 85 ± 1.29

Therefore, the 88.12% confidence interval for the mean diastolic pressures of all students is (83.71, 86.29) mm Hg.
