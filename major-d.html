Key that has a note with a sharp before it

d. F Major

Sharp (from French) or diesis (from Greek)[a] implies "upper in pitch" in music. Sharp, in musical notation, is defined as "one semitone (half step) higher in pitch." Sharp is the polar opposite of flat, which is a pitch reduction.

In key signatures or as an accident, a sharp symbol,, is employed. The music below, for example, contains a key signature with three sharps (marking either A major or F minor, the relative minor) and a sharp accidental on the note A.

B, for example, sounds the same as, or is enharmonically comparable to, C natural (C), and E is enharmonically equivalent to F in twelve-tone equal temperament. Enharmonic equivalences do not exist in other tuning systems in general.
