6. Whatcapitalsubject togains arecapital gains taxation?​


Capital gains are generally included in taxable income, but in most cases, are taxed at a lower rate. A capital gain is realized when a capital asset is sold or exchanged at a price higher than its basis. Basis is an asset's purchase price, plus commissions and the cost of improvements less depreciation.

30. Gaining ratio can be calculated using the gains of the remaining partners and the gains are the difference between the new ratio and the old ratio.​


Step 1 OF 1

Calculation of Gaining Ratio: X: Y: Z

Old ratio[tex] = \frac{1}{2} : \frac{3}{10} : \frac{1}{5} [/tex]

[tex] = \frac{5:3:2}{10} [/tex]

New ratio[tex] = 3:2[/tex]

Gaining Ration = New Ratio - Old Ratio

Y'S Gain [tex] = \frac{3}{5} - \frac{3}{10} = \frac{3}{10} [/tex]

Z' Gain [tex] = \frac{2}{5} - \frac{2}{10} = \frac{2}{10} [/tex]

Gaining Ratio [tex] = 3:2[/tex]

FINAL ANSWER

The gaining ratio of the remaining partners is 3:2.
