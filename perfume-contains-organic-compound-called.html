The store receives a total of 1,600 milliliters of perfume in this delivery.

To find out, we can start by calculating how much perfume is in each case:

10 bottles/case x 80 milliliters/bottle = 800 milliliters/case

Since there are 2 cases in the delivery, we can multiply the amount of perfume per case by the number of cases:

2 cases x 800 milliliters/case = 1,600 milliliters

1. What is his expected profit on the boxes of perfumes?

Given: 10 boxes of perfumes

           ₱12,000 - cost of each box

           12 bottles per box

           ₱1,500 - selling price per bottle of perfume

Find the cost of perfume per bottle.

Cost of Perfume per Bottle = ₱12,000 ÷ 12

Cost of Perfume per Bottle = ₱1,000

Therefore, each bottle of perfume costs ₱1,000.

If Rodrigo plans to sell the perfumes at ₱1,500, find his expected profit.

Expected Profit = ₱1,500 x 12 x 10

Expected Profit = ₱18,000 x 10

Expected Profit = ₱180,000

Therefore, Rodrigo's expected profit is ₱180,000.

2. How much does Rodrigo earned profit?

Given: ₱180,000 - expected profit

           ₱12,000 - costs of each box of perfume

           10 boxes of perfumes

Find Rodrigo's earned profit.

Earned Profit = Expected Profit - Expenses

Earned Profit = ₱180,000  - (₱12,000 x 10)

Earned Profit =₱180,000 -  ₱120,000

Earned Profit = ₱60,000

Therefore, Rodrigo had earned ₱60,000.

23. Mrs. Eldon buys two No bottles bottles of perfume; one contains 48.5 ml and the other contains 150.5 ml. How much more perfume is in the larger of the two bottles?


Answer: there is 102 ml more perfume in the larger bottle than in the smaller bottle.

Step-by-step explanation:

To find out how much more perfume is in the larger of the two bottles, we need to subtract the volume of the smaller bottle from the volume of the larger bottle.

Volume of larger bottle - Volume of smaller bottle = Difference

150.5 ml - 48.5 ml = Difference

102 ml = Difference

Therefore, there is 102 ml more perfume in the larger bottle than in the smaller bottle.
