6. What is conduction convection and radiation and examples?​


Answer:

Conduction, convection, and radiation are the three types of heat transfer mechanisms. They are described as follows:

1. Conduction: When heat flows from one object to another through direct contact, it is called conduction. In other words, the heat is transferred from one molecule to another through a medium. Example: A metal spoon left in a cup of hot tea becomes hot from the tea, which is transferring heat via conduction.

2. Convection: When heat is transferred through the bulk movement of a fluid (gas or liquid), it is called convection. In other words, the heated fluid moves from one place to another and transfers heat. Example: A pot of boiling water heats the surrounding air, which rises and circulates the heat through convection currents.

3. Radiation: When heat is transferred through electromagnetic waves, it is called radiation. This type of heat transfer does not require a physical medium for transmission. Example: The sun heats the earth by electromagnetic radiation.

*InformationWhat is Conduction?the process by which heat or electricity is directly transmitted through a substance when there is a difference of temperature or of electrical potential between adjoining regions, without movement of the material.

What is Convection?the movement caused within a fluid by the tendency of hotter and therefore less dense material to rise, and colder, denser material to sink under the influence of gravity, which consequently results in transfer of heat.

What is Radiation?Radiation is energy that comes from a source and travels through space at the speed of light. This energy has an electric field and a magnetic field associated with it, and has wave-like properties. You could also call radiation “electromagnetic waves”.
