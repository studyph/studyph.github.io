Answer:

To find the Beginning Capital/Inventory of the owner on December 31, 2020, you can use the following formula:

Beginning Capital/Inventory = Capital (January 31, 2021) + Net Income - Drawing

Plugging in the given values, we get:

Beginning Capital/Inventory = P20,000 + P18,000 - P56,000

Simplifying, we get:

Beginning Capital/Inventory = P20,000 + P18,000 - P56,000 = P-18,000

So the Beginning Capital/Inventory of the owner on December 31, 2020 is P-18,000.

This means that the owner has a negative net worth of P18,000 on December 31, 2020.

27. Year-end net assets would be overstated and current expenses would be understated as a result of failure to record which of the following adjusting entries?


Adjusting Journal Entries

To fix mistakes in the revenue and expense accounts on the income statement, negative adjusting entries are used. If the credit balance of a revenue account is overstated, the negative adjustment is a debit entry. If the debit balance of a revenue account is understated, the negative adjustment is a credit entry.

Adjustment of an Unearned AccountThe reversal of a prepaid adjustment is this adjustment. In other words, if we paid our insurance premium. When the time comes and State Farm earns the money, it moves it from the liability account, unearned income, to a regular income account by debiting the liability account, unearned income, and crediting a Insurance Income or other revenue account.

Additional information:

Because supplies are reported as an asset, this adjustment is required. They must be expensed as they are used up because the cost has already passed.

This correction is required because costs that are paid in advance like insurance are recorded as a debit to an asset account. The expense must be added as the cost used up or expired over time. An expense such as an insurance expense is to be debited, and the asset account is to be credited.

When a pay period falls between two fiscal years, this adjustment is required. Wages earned but unpaid through the end of the fiscal year must be accrued at the end of the year. The book does not provide examples of how to do the same for related payroll taxes, but you must.
