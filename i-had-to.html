1. __________ he would be there, I would have never agreed to come. I had known Had I known Had known I Known I had


The most correct answer is Had I known.

Judging from the structure and context of the sentence above, it is included in conditional sentence type 3.

What is Conditional Sentence Type 3?

This sentence expresses an event that was expected to occur in the past but did not happen.

Each conditional sentence consists of two clauses, namely:

1. Main Clause

2. IF Clause (Secondary Sentence)

Here is a pattern to form a conditional type 3:

If + past perfect, + subject + would have/wouldn't have + past participle

Or

Subject + would have/wouldn't have + past participle + if + past perfect

The main clause can be placed in front of or behind the IF clause.

This difference in location does not affect the meaning.

Examples :

1. If you had remembered to invite your ex-boyfriend, he would have attended.

2. If she had asked you for forgiveness, would you have forgiven her?

From the question above, the complete sentence can be obtained: If I had known he would be there, I would have never agreed to come.

 In this sentence, the word "If I had known" can be replaced with the word "had I known". Both have almost the same meaning. This sentence pattern has exactly the same meaning as "I wish I knew that...". Only the words are different.

This expression is used when you talk about something you didn't know in the past but wanted to know at the time. Because of had the subject known this information, the subject's previous actions would have been different.

So the answer to the missing sentence in this question is "had I known," or it could be "if I had known."

So what exactly is the function of a conditional sentence?

1. To expect something to happen in the future

2. To rely on something that is no longer possible.

3. To suppose something has happened in the past,
