2. Calculate the molar solubility of lead(II) bromide (PbBr2). For lead(II) bromide, Ksp = 4.67 x 10^-6.


PbBr₂ ⇒ Pb²⁺ + 2Br⁻

s               s        2s

Ksp PbBr₂ = [Pb²⁺][Br⁻]²

Ksp = [s][2s]²

Ksp = 4s³

4.67 x 10⁻⁶ = 4s³

s = 0.011

14. What quantity of electricity is needed to produce 4.25 g of lead in the electrolysis of molten lead (II) bromide?


Answer:

electric charge = 3.96 × 10³ C

Explanation:

Solve for the quantity of electricity.

electric charge = 4.25 g Pb × (1 mol Pb / 207.22 g Pb) × (2 mol e / 1 mol Pb) × (96485 C / 1 mol e)

electric charge = 3.96 × 10³ C
