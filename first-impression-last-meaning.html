21. 10. It is a type of bias that follows the saying, “First impressions last forever.” *​


The statement described is the definition of anchoring bias

Bias is one type of many cognitive faulty that can befall anybody, and this happens because human being is not that perfect in regards of how they think. However, there can always be an improvement, and who would have known that one could benefit from it. One instance of biases is anchoring bias:

Anchoring bias happens when an initial piece of information that we get affects us in a great manner. This can be a judgment about a certain person, or a tendency to rely on that information too much. The more extreme version of anchoring bias is placing a suspicion of somebody, when their first impression seems bad in our eyes (same example as the question asked).

This bias can be harmful in both in short term and long term, so whenever one realizes that he might be trapped in anchoring bias, it is better to withdraw any further assumption and forget that first information.

Remembering types of biases and their definition can be hard most of the time. However, as for anchoring bias, it is way easier to remember. One reason is that it contains the word "anchor". Everyone knows what anchor is, and when it is thrown into the sea, it immediately hinder a ship's movement.

This is an appropriate analogy to the definition itself: first throw or first information will have a tremendous effect, hence the ship being stopped, or a person whose judgment is clouded thanks to a bad first impression. Yet, there is also an example of benefit of anchoring bias, it is when we can conclude or make use of small number of information that will manage to help us in our affairs.

22. in your own opinion, does first impression really lasts? why or why not?


Answer:

“The first impression is an idea, feeling or opinion about a person formed without conscious thought or on the basis of little evidence.”

We deeply believe that it is our character and personality that counts, but the reality is that people make judgements about who we are, based on the first impression.It is not always the most accurate representation. And because it is formed in very short time and based on a very little information, it is always related to the other persons' personal experience. Hence it is someone’s perception of you and not a real image. The accuracy of the image increases with the time. With every event or meeting with that person, we gather more information and that brings more clarity to whom he or she is.

The first impression is also about the initial feeling people have meeting you. How you make them feel is more important than what you are saying. Your appearance together with your body language and the tone of your voice create the atmosphere of the meeting. You can make others feel comfortable and relaxed or stressed and pressured. Think of how people respond to you.

From the scientific point of view, our brain is a thousand years old structure. Thanks to the increased use of our brain capability, we evolved as homo sapiens. But many reactions were inherited from our ancestors, like the one that allows our brain to recognize another human as a threat or friend in a few milliseconds. It has been trained for a thousand years to judge the situation and react very quickly based on little information.

The first impression is not the same for various people. They heavily depend on their expectations, prejudices, beliefs and stereotypes. We all have a tendency to interpret situations, events and objects in our own unique way and the distortion of opinion is the result of personal experience. In the same way we differ in interpreting another person’s appearance and behaviour. It is not always a bad thing, it makes life interesting and meeting people an exciting experience.

What we need to remember is that to every person their own opinion looks real, true and solid. While it is only an illusion, their perception is their reality.
