3. A grocer combined walnuts that cost Php800 per kilo and cashew nuts that cost Php550 per kilo. Find the amount of walnuts and the amount of cashew nuts used to make the 50 kilo mixture costing Php710 per kilo. Representation: Working Equation: Solution: Answer:


Answer:

Representation:

Let x be the amount of walnuts used in the mixture in kilograms.

Let y be the amount of cashew nuts used in the mixture in kilograms.

Working Equation:

The total weight of the mixture is 50 kilograms, so we have:

x + y = 50

To find the amount of walnuts and cashew nuts needed to make a 50 kilo mixture costing Php710 per kilo, we need to use the fact that the cost of the mixture is equal to the weighted average of the costs of the individual nuts. The weighted average formula is:

(weight of item 1 x cost of item 1) + (weight of item 2 x cost of item 2) = total cost

In our case, the weighted average equation is:

(x * 800) + (y * 550) = 50 * 710

Solution:

Using the first equation, we can solve for one of the variables in terms of the other:

x + y = 50

y = 50 - x

Substituting y = 50 - x into the second equation, we get:

(x * 800) + ((50 - x) * 550) = 50 * 710

Simplifying and solving for x, we get:

800x + 27500 - 550x = 35500

250x = 8000

x = 32

Therefore, the grocer used 32 kg of walnuts and 18 kg of cashew nuts to make the 50 kg mixture costing Php710 per kilo.

Answer:

32 kg of walnuts and 18 kg of cashew nuts were used to make the 50 kilo mixture costing Php710 per kilo.

8. A grocer combined walnuts that cost 16$ per kilo and cashew nuts that cost 11$ per kilo. Find the amount of walnuts and the amount of cashew nuts used to make the 50 kilo mixture costing 14.2$ per kilo. Representation: Working Equation: Solution: Answer:


Answer:

Let x be the amount of walnuts in kilos and y be the amount of cashew nuts in kilos.

Since the mixture is 50 kilos and costs 14.2$ per kilo, the total cost of the mixture is:

50 * 14.2 = 710$

The cost of the walnuts is 16$ per kilo, so the cost of x kilos of walnuts is:

16x$

The cost of the cashew nuts is 11$ per kilo, so the cost of y kilos of cashew nuts is:

11y$

The total cost of the mixture is the sum of the costs of the walnuts and cashew nuts:

16x + 11y = 710

We also know that the amount of the mixture that is walnuts and the amount that is cashew nuts adds up to 50 kilos:

x + y = 50

We can use substitution to solve for one of the variables. Solving for x in the second equation, we get:

x = 50 - y

Substituting this into the first equation, we get:

16(50 - y) + 11y = 710

Expanding and simplifying:

800 - 16y + 11y = 710

-5y = -90

y = 18

So there are 18 kilos of cashew nuts in the mixture. Substituting this back into the second equation to solve for x, we get:

x = 50 - y = 50 - 18 = 32

So there are 32 kilos of walnuts in the mixture.

Therefore, the grocer combined 32 kilos of walnuts and 18 kilos of cashew nuts to make the 50 kilo mixture costing 14.2$ per kilo.
