Quota samplingE. ClusterI. AvailabilityB. SnowballF. PurposiveJ. SystematicC. VoluntaryG. StratifiedK. ProbabilityD. StatisticsH. Simple Random​


Answer:

1. Going to different areas to obtain a sample from varied set of people.

Answer: STATISTICS

2. Offering varied sampling techniques whose validity depends much on statistics.

Answer: PROBABILITY

3. Interviewing people buying fish at the market place.

Answer: AVAILABILITY

4. Selecting respondents from each of these sections: A, B, C, and D

Answer: CLUSTER

5. Making sections A, B, C, and D as your respondents

Answer: STRATIFIED

6. Choosing from your class the native speakers of English as the subjects in your study entitled: The Extent of the Grammatical Competence of UST Freshmen Students

Answer: PURPOSIVE

7. Putting all the names of population members in a box and draw from the box the total number of the sample.

Answer: SNOWBALL

8. Selecting the exact number of samples possessing comparative features or traits with the population.

Answer: QUOTA SAMPLING

9. Accommodating extra-willing people to act as the respondents

Answer: VOLUNTARY

10. Taking every 15th person in the sampling frame as the chosen respondent.

Answer: SIMPLE RANDOM
