27. What is the formula for calculating speed and the formula for calculating velocity?


Explanation:

Velocity (v) is a vector quantity that measures displacement (or change in position, Δs) over the change in time (Δt), represented by the equation v = Δs/Δt. Speed (or rate, r) is a scalar quantity that measures the distance traveled (d) over the change in time (Δt), represented by the equation r = d/Δt.

Answer:

The formula for calculating speed is distance / time.

The formula for calculating velocity is displacement /  time.

Explanation:

In order to get the speed, we simply divide the given distance by the time.

For velocity, since it involves two different distances, we need to get the difference for both distances.

To get the displacement, simply subtract both given distances.

Divide the displacement and time to get the velocity.
