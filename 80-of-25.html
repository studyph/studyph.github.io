17. if 25% of 50% of 80 is 10 then 1/4 of 5/10 of 80 is​


The result from

[tex]\rm \dfrac{1}{4}\:of\:\dfrac{5}{10}\:of\:80\:=\boxed{\bold{10}}[/tex]

Further explanation

Fraction numbers are known as numerators and denominators

On a number line, fractions are expressed in points that lie between two integers

The general form of fractions is

[tex]\large {\boxed {\bold {\dfrac {a} {b}}}[/tex]

a = numerator

b = denominator

There are 3 forms of fraction

1. proper fraction 2. improper fraction 3. mixed fraction

There are several steps to multiply fractions

1. Multiply the numerators 2. Multiply the denominators 3. Simplify the fraction

The percent is a number that shows a comparison of the number 100 or can be called per hundred (symbol%)

Fractions either proper or mixed (including of numerators and denominators) or decimal forms can be converted into percentages and vice versa

From the problem there are two math sentences  :

1. 25% of 50% of 80 is 10

we translate this sentence into Algebraic expressions:

[tex]\rm \dfrac{25}{100}\times \dfrac{50}{100}\times 80=10[/tex]

2. 1/4 of 5/10 of 80

we translate this sentence into Algebraic expressions:

[tex]\rm \dfrac{1}{4}\times \dfrac{5}{10}\times 80[/tex]

To find the value of the second math sentence, then we try to compare its value with the first math sentence. To be comparable, then the first mathematical sentence we change in the form of fractions  (Simple fractional form)

[tex]\rm \dfrac{25}{100}=\dfrac{1}{4}\\\\\dfrac{50}{100}=\dfrac{5}{10}[/tex]

From this fraction, it can be seen that the first and second sentences are identical, so the results are the same as the results of the first sentence : 10
