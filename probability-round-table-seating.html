3. in how many ways can 10 people be seated in a round table if 3 persons want to sit next to one another?(probability)​


There are 3! ways for the 3 persons to sit next to one another. Consider them as one group or one entity and count them with the remaining 7 persons. Now, we can arrange the 8 entities (consisting of that 3 persons as one entity and the remaining 7 persons) around the table in (8 - 1)! = 7!. So the total number of ways is 7!3! = 30,240.

P = (n - 1)!      "Cyclical permutation"

Thus, for 7 people.
P = (7 - 1)! = 720 ways

the remaining 8 persons can sit around the second round table in

{(8 – 1)!} = (7!) = 5040 ways for each sitting arrangement around the first round table.

So,

the answer is (6435*720*5040).

8. in how many ways can 6 children be seated at a round table if the eldest will be seated on the right side of the youngest ​


Combinatorial problem in mathematics, specifically a permutation problem

The answer is 24. 4 x 3 x 2 x 1 = 24. There are 24  ways to arrange the 6 children around the table, given that the eldest child will be seated on the right side of the youngest. There are four chairs left, and the first youngster can sit in any of them. The second child can then sit in any of the remaining three seats, the third child in any of the remaining two seats, and the final child in the last available seat.

Explanation of the problemWe can fix their table places if the eldest child is sitting to the right of the smallest child. Without going into too much detail, we may infer that the smallest child will sit at the "top" of the table, and the eldest will sit to their right. The task is reduced to putting the remaining four children in the four chairs to the left of the youngest child.

25. if there are 12 people in a party in how many ways can they be seated in a round table with only 5 seats​


If there are 12 people in a party and only 5 seats available in a round table, the number of ways they can be seated can be calculated using the permutation formula for circular arrangements:

P(12,5) = (12-1)!/(12-5)! = 11!/7! = 11 x 10 x 9 x 8 x 7 = 55,440

Therefore, there are 55,440 ways to seat 12 people in a round table with only 5 seats available.
