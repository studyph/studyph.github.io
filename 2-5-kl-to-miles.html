27. If x = 2 miles and y = 5 miles. What is the magnitude of the resultant vector? ​


The magnitude of the resultant vector is 5.39 or [tex]\sqrt{29}[/tex] miles if x is equal to 2 miles and y is equal to 5 miles.

We can retrieve the answer by using the distance formula or the Pythagorean theorem. We can apply the formula: [tex]a^{2} = b^{2} + c^{2}[/tex]  the resultant is the hypotenuse of the two distances or sides of the triangle in this case.

Let [tex]a^{2}[/tex] be the resultant R

[tex]a^{2}[/tex] = [tex]2 ^{2} + 5^{2}[/tex] = [tex]\sqrt{29}[/tex]

Note: The values of b and c can be any of the two remaining sides as long as a is always the longest side.

If [tex]a^{2}[/tex] is R, then R= 5.39 miles
