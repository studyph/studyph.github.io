28. use the distance and midpoint formula to find the length and midpoint of the segment to the indicate midpoints (8,6)and(-1,3)​


QUESTION:

Use the distance and midpoint formula to find the length and midpoint of the segment to the indicate midpoints (8, 6) and (-1, 3).

ANSWER:

To get the distance between the two given points, let us use the formula:

[tex]\sf D= \sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2+(y_2-y_1)^2}[/tex]

wherein,

D refers to distance[tex]\sf (x_1,y_1)[/tex] is (8, 6)[tex]\sf (x_2,y_2)[/tex] is (-1, 3)

[tex]\sf D= \sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2+(y_2-y_1)^2}[/tex]

[tex]\sf D= \sqrt{(-1-8)^2+(3-6)^2}[/tex]

[tex]\sf D= \sqrt{(-9)^2+(-3)^2}[/tex]

[tex]\sf D= \sqrt{81+9}[/tex]

[tex]\sf D= \sqrt{90}[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\sf D= 9.49} [/tex]

Remember that distance is always positive.

The distance between the two given points is approximately 9.49.

To get the midpoint of the two given points, you can use the midpoint formula.

[tex]\sf M=(\frac{x_1+x_2}{2}, \frac{y_1+y_2}{2})[/tex]

[tex]\sf M=(\frac{8 + ( - 1)}{2}, \frac{6+3}{2})[/tex]

[tex]\sf M=(\frac{8 - 1}{2}, \frac{6+3}{2})[/tex]

[tex]\sf M=(\frac{7}{2}, \frac{9}{2})[/tex]

[tex]\boxed{\sf M=(3.5, 4.5)} [/tex]

The midpoint is (3.5, 4.5).
