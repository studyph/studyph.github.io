14. has a diameter with endpoints (-1,4) and (4,2)​


Answer:

[tex](x - \frac{3}{2} ) ^{2} + (y - 3)^{2} = \frac{29}{4} [/tex]

Step-by-step explanation:

In this solution, I will assume that the problem is expecting us to give the equation of the circle that has a diameter with the length of a line segment connecting the points (-1, 4) and (4, 2).

To find the equation of the circle, we need to find two essential things. These are the circle's center and the circle's radius.

Let's find the center first. The midpoint of a line segment can be found by finding the arithmetic mean of each coordinate.

( (-1 + 4)/2, (4+2)/2 ) = (3/2, 3)

Now that we have the center of the circle, let's find the radius. But, to find the radius, we need to find the diameter first. We can use the Pythagorean Theorem to find the diameter's (hypotenuse) length.

The change in x is 5 units, and the change in y is 2 units. These are the legs of the right triangle.

c^2 = 25 + 4

c = ± sqrt(29)

Since there can never be negative lengths, we take the positive root.

c = sqrt(29)

Since this is the diameter, the radius is sqrt(29) / 2.

The standard form for the circle's equation is (x-h)^2 + (y-k)^2 = r^2. We have the values h = 3/2, k = 3, and r = sqrt(29) / 2, so we can just substitute.

(x-3/2)^2 + (y-3)^2 = [sqrt(29) / 2]^2

(x-3/2)^2 + (y-3)^2 = 29/4

21. what is the length of the radius of the circle,if the endpoint of the diameter are (5,2) and (5,8)​


The length of the radius of the circle is 3 units.

Let [tex]d[/tex] be the length of the  diameter.

Given that the endpoints of the diameter are (5, 2) and (5, 8), so the diameter is the line segment between points (5, 2) and (5, 8); and its length can be calculated by using the distance formula:

[tex]\boxed{d=\sqrt{(x_2-x_1)^2+(y_2-y_1)^2}}[/tex]

Apply the endpoints:

[tex](5,2) \rightarrow (x_1,y_1) \quad (5,8)\rightarrow (x_2,y_2)[/tex]

Substitute the values to the formula:

[tex]d=\sqrt{(5-5)^2+(8-2)^2}[/tex]

[tex]d=\sqrt{0^2+6^2}[/tex]

[tex]d=\sqrt{36}=6[/tex]

Note that radius is half the length of the diameter, thus the radius is:

[tex]\displaystyle r=\frac{d}{2}=\frac{6}{2}=\boxed{3}[/tex]

Therefore, the length of the radius is 3 units.
