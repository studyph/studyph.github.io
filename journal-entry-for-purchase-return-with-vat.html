7. mystery different between journal and entries and journal adjusting entries?​


Adjusting entries are changes to journal entries you've already recorded. Specifically, they make sure that the numbers you have recorded match up to the correct accounting periods. Journal entries track how money moves—how it enters your business, leaves it, and moves between different accounts


8. purchase merchandise 50000 on account from abc co.,terms 1/15; n/30 per JV no. 602.What is the journal entry?​


Answer:

The journal entry for this transaction would be as follows:

Debit: Accounts Payable (ABC Co.) - $50,000

Credit: Merchandise Inventory - $50,000

This journal entry reflects that the company has purchased merchandise from ABC Co. on account, with terms of 1/15; n/30, and that the transaction is recorded under Journal Voucher (JV) no. 602. The debit to Accounts Payable (ABC Co.) increases the company's liability to ABC Co. for the amount of the purchase, and the credit to Merchandise Inventory increases the company's assets by the value of the goods received.

1/15; n/30 is a common trade credit term, it means that the company has a discount of 1/15 for paying the invoice within 15 days and will pay the net amount (remaining after discount) after 30 days.

Please note that this entry is an example and the actual journal entry may vary depending on the company's accounting policies and chart of accounts.

26. what is the difference between simple journal entry and compound journal entry?​


Answer:

Compound journal entry is an accounting entry which affects three or more account heads. A simple journal entry has just two rows i.e. one debit and one credit, whereas a compound journal entry has three or more rows.
