6. identify the characters in the story "the tale of the woodcutter and the tiger" what roles do the characters play in the tale? what are the characteristics of these characters that you admire/don't admire


The characters in the Korean folklore "The Tale of the Woodcutter and the Tiger" are the Woodcutter, [Mamma] Tiger, Mother and the Three Baby Tigers.

The Woodcutter is a common villager who suddenly encounters a tiger. In his fear of being eaten by the tiger, he lied and made up a story that eventually made the tiger change his mind and spare his life instead.

Characteristics: Smart yet deceitful. Despite the life-threatening experience he had, he managed to think of a way to escape the danger. But although witty, lying to others is still a wrong thing to do because his actions affected the life and beliefs of the innocent baby tigers.

The [Mamma] Tiger is the wild animal whom the Woodcutter encountered. He thought that the Woodcutter's story was true and that he is his brother and they have a mother who has been looking for him for years. In the story, the Tiger begets three baby tigers.

Characteristics: Strong yet soft-hearted. The tiger is a ferocious beast and yet when told about [her] mother, her heart melted and she drew away from her true nature. She further portrays righteousness and ideals of respect as she passed on an important trait to her children: honoring their ancestors.

The Mother is the literal mother of the Woodcutter who already passed away. He is visited by his son, the Woodcutter, during Jesa, a Korean tradition where families and individuals pay honor to their ancestors in remembrance of their death anniversary.

Characteristics: Patient and loving. Her son remembered him to be someone who always prepared dinner (food) for them and patiently waited for their lost brother.

The Three Baby Tigers are the babies of the [Mamma] Tiger whom the Woodcutter has encountered. Despite the loss of their Mamma, these three baby tigers remembered the tradition their mother has taught them to do and was faithful in bringing offerings to their thought-to-be ancestor/grandmother, the mother of the Woodcutter.

Characteristics: Obedient and Respectful. Although unaware of the truth, these three baby tigers willfully obeyed the tradition their [Mamma] Tiger passed on them. They have never met their thought-to-be ancestor/grandmother and yet they knew the importance of paying honor and respect to her by visiting her tomb and bringing offerings to her.

8. what is the rising action, falling action of the tale of genji?​


"The Tale of Genji" is a classic work of Japanese literature that was written by Murasaki Shikibu in the 11th century. As a long and complex narrative, it contains multiple rising and falling actions throughout its many chapters. However, I can provide a general overview of the rising and falling actions of the tale:

The rising action of "The Tale of Genji" is generally considered to be the first 17 chapters, which focus on the life of Genji, the son of an emperor, and his romantic relationships with various women. During this section of the book, Genji experiences many successes and failures in love, and his relationships with the women in his life become increasingly complex and intertwined.

The falling action of the tale is generally considered to begin around Chapter 18, when Genji's son Kaoru becomes the focus of the narrative. The later chapters of the book explore Kaoru's relationships and experiences, as well as the lives of other characters who are connected to Genji's family. The falling action of the tale is marked by a sense of melancholy and nostalgia, as the characters reflect on the passing of time and the impermanence of life.

Overall, it is important to note that "The Tale of Genji" is a complex work with many different narrative threads and themes. The rising and falling actions of the tale are not necessarily straightforward or easily defined, and different readers may interpret the structure of the book in different ways.


9. What role do other Asian societies play in the novel of the tale of genji?​


The Tale of Genji, thought by many to be the first novel in the history of world literature, was written by a woman, Murasaki Shikibu, in the eleventh century. Lady Murasaki lived during the Heian Period (794-1185), an era remarkable for the poetry, diaries, and fiction produced by court ladies. Their sensitivity to nature and the art of love set the tone for the art and literature of their time. While the men wrote in an awkward, scholarly form of Chinese, the women developed a Japanese script more suitable to the Japanese language. In succeeding periods of warfare, popular tales of the bravery of the samurai warriors would all but obliterate court ladies' writings. Even in such tales, however, the warrior would stop in the midst of battle to recite a poem, play his flute, and sigh over the falling of a blossom. The tastes of the women of Japan were never again to dominate the arts, but the sensibilities of the Heian period continued to exert an influence over Japanese literature in later years.
