[tex]Formulas:

\\ A_{rec} =LW \\

P_{rec}=2L+2W \\ \\

Equation 1: \\ A_{rec} =LW \\ W = \frac{A_{rec}}{L}[/tex]

Substitute Equation 1 to the formula of the Perimeter

[tex]P_{rec} = 2L + 2W \\ P_{rec} = 2L + 2( \frac{A_{rec}} {L}) \\ 38 m = 2L + 2( \frac{78m}{L}) \\ (38m = 2L + \frac{156}{L})L[/tex]

multiply both sides by L to cancel the L in the denominator

[tex]38L = 2 {L}^{2} + 156 \\ (2 {L}^{2} - 38L + 156 = 0 ) \div 2 \\ {L}^{2} - 19L + 78 = 0 \\ L_{1} = 13m \\ L_{2} = 6m[/tex]

14. geometric problems:the area of a rectangular field is 78 sq meters and its perimeter is 38 meters.


We let the length be l and the width be w

The area of the rectangle is computed as lw so:
78 = lw

The perimeter is computed as 2(l+w) so:
38 = 2(l+w)
19 = l + w

Method 1: Guess and Check
We look for two numbers with a product of 78 and a sum of 19.
78 + 1 = 79  X
39 + 2 = 41  X
13 + 6 = 19  √

Therefore the dimensions of the rectangle are 13 by 6 m.

Method 2: Quadratic Formula and Vieta's Formula
By Vieta's formula:
αβ = 78 = c
α + β = 19 = -b
*take note that we let a=1

The quadratic equation would be:
t² - 19t + 78

Then we do the quadratic formula
t = 19 ± √(19² - 4(78)) = 19 ± √49 = 19 ± 7 = 13 or 6
                2                      2              2

78/13 = 6       and              78/6 = 13
Therefore the dimensions are 13 by 6 m.
