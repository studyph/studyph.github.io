5. A swimming pool is 35 m long and 20 m wide and field to an average depth of 1.5 m. How many kg. of water can it contain?


Problem:

A swimming pool is 35 m long and 20 m wide and field to an average depth of 1.5 m. How many kg. of water can it contain?

In this problem we need to find to volume first of the swimming pool using the formula V = length × width × height. After solving the volume of the swimming pool, we will use the density of water in kg/m³ to find it mass in kg.

Given data:

Length = 35 m

Width   = 20 m

Height  = 1.5 m

Volume = ? unknown

Solution:

V   = length × width × height

    =  35 m × 20 m  ×  1.5 m

    = 1050 m³

This means that the swimming pool can hold 1050 m³ volume of water. Using this data, we can solve for the mass using the density of water              997 kg/m³.

Solving for kg of water:  mass = density ×  volume

Density  =  997 kg/m³

mass    =   ?

Volume  = 1050 m³

Solution:

m = d × v

   = 997 kg/m³  ×  1050 m³

   = 1,046,850 kg

Answer?:

1,046,850 kg mass of water

29. 1. The length of the garden is 8 m longer than its width and the area is 20 m? How long is the garden?​


Answer:

the answer is 160 m long

Answer:

Length = 10 m

Step-by-step explanation:

Given:

length = 8 m longer than its width = 8 + w

Area = 20 m²

Let:

l = length (Lowercase L)

w = width

A = Area

SOLUTION:

Formula for Area of a rectangle:

l × w = A

if l = 8 + w ; A = 20 m²

(8 + w)(w) = 20 m²

8w + w² = 20

w² + 8w = 20

By Completing the Square:

w² + 8w = 20

w² + 8w +(8/(2×1))² = 20 +(8/(2×1))²

w² + 8w +(8/2)² = 20 + (8/2)²

w² + 8w +(4)² = 20 + (4)²

w² + 8w + 16 = 20 + 16

(w + 4)² = 36

√(w + 4)² = ±√36

w + 4 = ±6

Solving for Roots:

w + 4 = 6

w + 4 - 4 = 6 - 4

w = 6 - 4

w = 2 m

w + 4 = -6

w + 4 - 4 = -6 - 4

w = -6 - 4

w = -10 m

Since we are talking about dimensions, neglect the negative root. Substitute the positive root to the original formula of Area.

l × w = A

l × (2m) = 20 m²

(l × 2m)/2m = 20 m²/2 m

l = 20 m²/2 m

l = 10 m

The length of the garden is 10 m.
